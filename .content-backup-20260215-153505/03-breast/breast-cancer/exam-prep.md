# 📋 Breast Cancer — Exam Prep Mode

---

## 🎯 One-Liners

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Most common cancer in women | Breast cancer |
| Most common type of breast cancer | Invasive ductal carcinoma (70-80%) |
| Best prognosis subtype | Luminal A (ER+, PR+, HER2-) |
| Worst prognosis subtype | Triple negative (ER-, PR-, HER2-) |
| Triple assessment components | Clinical exam + Imaging + Pathology |
| Screening mammography age | 40-50 years (varies by guidelines) |
| Peau d'orange indicates | Lymphatic obstruction (locally advanced) |
| BRCA1/2 mutations increase risk of | Breast AND ovarian cancer |
| Drug for ER+ breast cancer | Tamoxifen (premenopausal), Aromatase inhibitors (postmenopausal) |
| Drug for HER2+ breast cancer | Trastuzumab (Herceptin) |

---

## 📝 Types of Breast Cancer

| Type | Frequency | Features |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Invasive ductal (IDC)** | 70-80% | Most common, hard mass |
| **Invasive lobular (ILC)** | 10-15% | Bilateral, diffuse |
| **DCIS** | Pre-invasive | Non-invasive, microcalcifications |
| **LCIS** | Pre-invasive | Risk marker, bilateral risk |
| **Inflammatory** | 1-5% | Peau d'orange, poor prognosis |
| **Paget's disease** | Rare | Nipple eczema, underlying cancer |

---

## 🔬 Investigations

| Investigation | Purpose |
|---------------|---------|
| **Mammography** | Screening & diagnosis (>40 yrs) |
| **USG breast** | <40 yrs, dense breasts, cysts vs solid |
| **Core biopsy** | Histology, receptor status |
| **MRI breast** | High-risk screening, extent assessment |
| **CT chest/abdomen** | Metastasis workup |
| **Bone scan** | Bone metastasis |
| **PET-CT** | Staging, recurrence |

---

## ⚠️ Red Flags for Malignancy

- Hard, irregular, fixed mass
- Skin dimpling/tethering
- Peau d'orange
- Nipple retraction (new)
- Bloody nipple discharge
- Axillary lymphadenopathy
- Paget's disease of nipple

---

## 💊 Treatment Algorithm

```
EARLY BREAST CANCER (Stage I-II)
├── Surgery: BCS + RT or Mastectomy
├── SLNB → ALND if positive
├── Adjuvant chemo if indicated
├── Hormonal therapy if ER+
└── Trastuzumab if HER2+

LOCALLY ADVANCED (Stage III)
├── Neoadjuvant chemotherapy
├── Surgery
├── Adjuvant RT + Chemo
└── Hormonal/Targeted therapy

METASTATIC (Stage IV)
├── Palliative intent
├── Systemic therapy (hormonal/chemo/targeted)
└── Local treatment for symptoms
```

---

## 📐 Important Concepts

**BCS (Breast Conserving Surgery) criteria:**
- Single tumor
- Tumor <4-5 cm
- No skin/chest wall involvement
- Patient willing for RT
- No contraindication to RT

**Contraindications to BCS:**
- Multicentric disease
- Large tumor:breast ratio
- Prior RT to chest
- Inflammatory breast cancer
- Patient preference
