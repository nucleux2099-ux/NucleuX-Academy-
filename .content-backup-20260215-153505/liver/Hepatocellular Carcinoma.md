# Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)

> **Source**: Blumgart Ch 82-85, AASLD Guidelines
> **Topic System**: [[Liver Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> HCC develops in cirrhosis (~80%), diagnosed by imaging (APHE + washout, no biopsy needed in cirrhotic), staged by BCLC (tumor burden + liver function + performance status), with treatment ranging from resection/transplant (early) to systemic therapy (advanced).

---

## Epidemiology

- **Incidence:** 6th most common cancer worldwide
- **Mortality:** 3rd most common cause of cancer death globally
- **Male:Female ratio:** 2-3:1
- **Cirrhosis:** Present in ~80% of HCC cases

### Etiology by Region
| Region | Alcohol | HBV | HCV | NAFLD |
|--------|---------|-----|-----|-------|
| Western | 32% | 13% | 44% | 10% |
| East Asia | 32% | 41% | 9% | 18% |
| North America | 37% | 9% | 31% | 23% |

> 💡 **NAFLD is the fastest growing cause of HCC in the US**

---

## Surveillance

### Who Should Be Screened?
| Population | Annual HCC Risk |
|------------|-----------------|
| Cirrhosis (any etiology) | 1-8% |
| HBV (without cirrhosis) | Variable |
| Family history of HCC | Increased |

### Protocol
- **Ultrasound + AFP** every 6 months
- Combined sensitivity: 63%

---

## Diagnosis (Imaging-Based)

### In Patients with Cirrhosis or Chronic HBV

**Diagnostic Hallmark:**
- **Arterial phase hyperenhancement (APHE)** + 
- **Washout** on portal venous or delayed phase

> 💡 **No biopsy needed** if imaging shows typical features in cirrhotic patient

### LI-RADS Classification
| Category | Definition | Action |
|----------|------------|--------|
| LR-1 | Definitely benign | No follow-up |
| LR-3 | Intermediate | Close follow-up |
| LR-5 | Definitely HCC | Treat as HCC |
| LR-M | Probably malignant, not specific for HCC | Biopsy |

---

## BCLC Staging System

| Stage | Tumor Burden | Liver Function | PS | Treatment | Survival |
|-------|--------------|----------------|-----|-----------|----------|
| **0 (Very Early)** | Single ≤2 cm | Preserved (CP A) | 0 | Ablation/Resection | >5 years |
| **A (Early)** | Single OR ≤3 nodules ≤3 cm | Preserved | 0 | Resection/Ablation/LT | >5 years |
| **B (Intermediate)** | Multinodular | Preserved | 0 | TACE/TARE | >2.5 years |
| **C (Advanced)** | Portal invasion/extrahepatic | Preserved | 1-2 | Systemic therapy | >2 years |
| **D (Terminal)** | Any | End-stage (CP C) | 3-4 | Best supportive care | ~3 months |

## Milan Criteria (Transplant Eligibility)

- **Single tumor ≤5 cm** OR
- **Up to 3 tumors, each ≤3 cm**
- No macrovascular invasion
- No extrahepatic disease

> 💡 **Within Milan = 4-year survival 75%, recurrence <15%**

---

## Treatment Options by Stage

| Treatment | Indication |
|-----------|------------|
| **Resection** | BCLC 0/A, preserved liver function, no portal HTN |
| **Ablation (RFA/MWA)** | Single ≤3 cm, or bridge to transplant |
| **Liver Transplant** | Within Milan, cirrhosis limiting resection |
| **TACE** | BCLC B, multinodular, preserved function |
| **Systemic** | BCLC C (atezolizumab + bevacizumab, sorafenib, lenvatinib) |

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

> 💎 **Pearl 1:** HCC in cirrhotics can be diagnosed by imaging alone – APHE + washout = HCC

> 💎 **Pearl 2:** AFP >400 ng/mL is highly suggestive but NOT diagnostic alone

> 💎 **Pearl 3:** BCLC is the standard staging system – incorporates tumor, liver function, and performance

> 💎 **Pearl 4:** Milan criteria define transplant eligibility (single ≤5cm OR ≤3 nodules ≤3cm)

> 💎 **Pearl 5:** NAFLD is the fastest-growing cause of HCC in Western countries
