# Fundoplication

> **Source**: Shackelford Ch 8-10 - *GERD and Antireflux Surgery*, Ch 19 - *Achalasia*
> **Topic System**: [[Esophagus Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot
> **Tier**: 4 (Surgical Technique)

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> Fundoplication wraps the gastric fundus around the distal esophagus to augment the LES—Nissen (360°) is most effective but requires normal motility; Toupet (270° posterior) or Dor (180° anterior) are partial wraps used with dysmotility or after Heller myotomy.

---

## Indications

### Primary Indications (GERD)

| Indication | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Failed medical therapy** | Persistent symptoms despite optimal PPI |
| **Patient preference** | Desire to avoid lifelong medication |
| **Complications of GERD** | Stricture, Barrett's (controversial), aspiration |
| **Large hiatal hernia** | Symptomatic Type II, III, IV |
| **Medication intolerance** | Side effects from PPI |
| **Volume regurgitation** | Not controlled by acid suppression |

### After Myotomy (Achalasia)

| Setting | Fundoplication Type |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Heller myotomy** | Dor (anterior) or Toupet (posterior) |
| **POEM** | Generally NOT done (no access) |
| **Failed POEM with GERD** | May need late fundoplication |

## Types of Fundoplication

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Nissen (360°) | Toupet (270°) | Dor (180°) |
|---------|---------------|---------------|------------|
| **Wrap degree** | Complete | Partial posterior | Partial anterior |
| **Efficacy** | Highest | Good | Moderate |
| **Dysphagia risk** | Higher | Lower | Lowest |
| **For normal motility** | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| **For poor motility** | No | **Preferred** | Alternative |
| **After Heller** | No | **Option** | **Preferred** |
| **Gas-bloat risk** | Higher | Lower | Lowest |

### Step-by-Step Technique

#### Port Placement

```
    LAPAROSCOPIC PORT PLACEMENT
    ═══════════════════════════

              Camera (10mm)
                  ●
                  │
         ●───────┼───────●
      Surgeon    │     Surgeon
       Right     │      Left
       (5mm)     │     (5mm)
                 │
         ●───────┼───────●
      Liver     │     Assistant
      retractor │      (5mm)
       (5mm)    │
```

#### Key Steps

| Step | Action | Key Points |
|------|--------|------------|
| **1** | Liver retraction | Nathanson retractor or flexible retractor |
| **2** | Divide gastrohepatic ligament | Preserve hepatic branch of vagus if present |
| **3** | Identify right crus | Start crural dissection |
| **4** | Mobilize esophagus | Create window behind esophagus |
| **5** | Identify left crus | Complete posterior dissection |
| **6** | Divide short gastrics | Mobilize fundus for tension-free wrap |
| **7** | Crural closure | Posterior closure with permanent suture |
| **8** | Create wrap | "Floppy" Nissen - 2-3 cm, loose |
| **9** | Secure wrap | 2-3 sutures incorporating esophageal wall |

### The "Floppy" Nissen Principles

> [!warning] Critical Technical Points
> 1. **Short wrap**: 2-2.5 cm (NOT 4-5 cm)
> 2. **Tension-free**: Divide short gastrics
> 3. **Fundus-to-fundus**: Use FUNDUS, not body of stomach
> 4. **Loose**: 52-60 Fr bougie should pass easily
> 5. **Over esophagus**: Not stomach (avoid slipped Nissen)

```
    "SHOE-SHINE" TEST
    ═════════════════

    The fundus should easily slide
    back and forth behind esophagus
    like a shoe-shine motion

        ←─── Fundus ───→
             ╱    ╲
            │ Esoph │
             ╲    ╱
    If tight → improper mobilization
```

## Toupet Fundoplication (270° Posterior)

### Indications

- **Impaired esophageal motility** (contraction amplitude <30 mmHg)
- **After Heller myotomy** (alternative to Dor)
- **Redo fundoplication** (after failed Nissen)
- **Patient preference** (lower dysphagia)

### Technique

```
    TOUPET (270° POSTERIOR)
    ═══════════════════════

    Cross-sectional view:

         Anterior
            │
            ▼
        ┌───────┐
        │░░░░░░░│
        │░░███░░│ ← Esophagus
        │░░███░░│
        │       │
        └───────┘
            │
            ▼
       Posterior

    View from front:

              Esophagus
                 │
            ┌────┴────┐

              │

    • Each limb sutured to crura AND esophagus
    • Anterior esophagus remains exposed
    • 270° coverage posteriorly
```

## Dor Fundoplication (180° Anterior)

### Primary Indication

**After Heller myotomy** - covers and protects the myotomy site

### Advantages After Heller

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Protects myotomy** | Covers exposed mucosa |
| **Buttresses repair** | If mucosal injury occurred |
| **Easier technically** | No posterior dissection needed |
| **Maintains myotomy** | Less likely to "close" the myotomy |

## Complications

### Early Complications

| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Dysphagia** | 10-50% early | Usually resolves; dilation if persists |
| **Wrap disruption** | <1% | Reoperation |
| **Vagal injury** | 1-2% | Supportive; gastroparesis if bilateral |
| **Splenic injury** | <1% | Splenectomy if uncontrolled |
| **Esophageal perforation** | <1% | Repair, drain |

### Late Complications

| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Recurrent GERD** | 5-10% | PPI, consider redo |
| **Persistent dysphagia** | 3-5% | Dilation, redo with partial wrap |
| **Gas-bloat syndrome** | 5-10% | Dietary modification, may improve |
| **Slipped Nissen** | 2-5% | Redo fundoplication |
| **Para-esophageal hernia** | 1-2% | Reoperation |

## Fundoplication After Myotomy

### Why Partial Wrap?

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Already compromised LES** | Myotomy disrupts sphincter |
| **Risk of dysphagia** | Complete wrap + disrupted peristalsis = obstruction |
| **Match to motility** | Achalasia = aperistalsis |

### Dor vs Toupet After Heller

| Feature | Dor (Anterior) | Toupet (Posterior) |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Myotomy protection** | Yes | No |
| **Anti-reflux** | Moderate | Better |
| **Technical ease** | Easier | Requires posterior dissection |
| **Preference** | Many surgeons prefer | Equal outcomes in studies |

> [!tip] Clinical Pearl
> **Most surgeons prefer Dor after Heller** because:
> 1. Covers and protects the myotomy
> 2. Buttresses any mucosal injury
> 3. No need for posterior dissection (already done for myotomy)
> 4. Adequate reflux control in most patients

## Quick Recall Questions

1. Q: What is the key difference between Dor and Toupet fundoplication?
   A: Dor is 180° anterior (covers front of esophagus), Toupet is 270° posterior (wraps around back)

2. Q: Why is a partial fundoplication preferred after Heller myotomy?
   A: The LES is already disrupted by myotomy, and complete wrap + aperistalsis = high dysphagia risk

3. Q: What are the 5 principles of a "floppy" Nissen?
   A: Short (2-2.5 cm), tension-free (divide short gastrics), fundus-to-fundus (not body), loose (52-60 Fr bougie), over esophagus (not stomach)

4. Q: What is gas-bloat syndrome?
   A: Inability to belch/vomit after fundoplication due to effective one-way valve; presents with bloating, early satiety
