## Card Set

### Card 1: Classification Standard
**Q: What is the current standard classification for esophageal motility disorders and when was it updated?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Chicago Classification version 4.0 (2021)**

Key features:
- Hierarchical approach starting with IRP
- Three main categories:
  1. Disorders of EGJ outflow (IRP ≥15)
  2. Major disorders of peristalsis
  3. Minor disorders of peristalsis
- Supine swallows as primary, with provocative tests for confirmation

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 2: IRP Definition
**Q: What is the Integrated Relaxation Pressure (IRP) and what is the cutoff value?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Definition:** Mean LES pressure during the 4-second window of maximal relaxation following a swallow

**Normal value:** <15 mmHg

**Clinical significance:**
- IRP ≥15 mmHg = EGJ outflow obstruction
- Most important single parameter in Chicago Classification
- Elevated IRP is the entry point for achalasia diagnosis

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 3: DCI Parameter
**Q: What is DCI (Distal Contractile Integral)? What are the normal and abnormal values?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Definition:** Amplitude × Duration × Length of distal esophageal contraction
- Measured in mmHg.cm.s

**Values:**
| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| <100 | Failed contraction |
| 100-450 | Weak (ineffective) |
| 450-8000 | Normal |
| >8000 | Hypercontractile (Jackhammer) |

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 4: Distal Latency
**Q: What is Distal Latency (DL) and what is its clinical significance?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Definition:** Time from UES relaxation to the Contractile Deceleration Point (CDP)

**Normal value:** >4.5 seconds

**Clinical significance:**
- DL <4.5 seconds = Premature (spastic) contraction
- Key parameter for diagnosing:
  - Distal Esophageal Spasm (DES)
  - Type III Achalasia
- Distinguishes "timing problem" from "strength problem"

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 5: Achalasia Definition
**Q: What are the manometric criteria for diagnosing achalasia?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Two required criteria:**

1. **IRP ≥15 mmHg** (EGJ outflow obstruction)
2. **100% failed peristalsis** (no normal propagating contractions)

Both criteria must be present to diagnose achalasia. If some peristalsis is preserved with elevated IRP, it is classified as EGJOO, not achalasia.

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 6: Achalasia Subtypes
**Q: Describe the three subtypes of achalasia and their key manometric features.**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

| Subtype | Key Feature | % of Cases |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Type I** | No esophageal pressurization (classic/absent) | 32% |
| **Type II** | Panesophageal pressurization ≥20% swallows | 50% |
| **Type III** | Premature/spastic contractions (DL <4.5s) ≥20% swallows | 18% |

All share: IRP ≥15 + 100% failed normal peristalsis

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 7: Treatment Response by Subtype
**Q: Which achalasia subtype has the best treatment response and which has the worst?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Best response: Type II Achalasia**
- ~90-95% success rate with PD/LHM/POEM
- Panesophageal pressurization indicates preserved muscle function
- 50% of all achalasia cases

**Worst response: Type III Achalasia**
- 60-70% success rate
- Spastic contractions harder to treat
- POEM preferred (allows extended myotomy)
- 18% of all achalasia cases

Memory: "Type **II** = **Too** good, Type **III** = **Troublesome**"

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 8: EGJOO
**Q: What is EGJOO? How does it differ from achalasia?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**EGJOO = EGJ Outflow Obstruction**

**Criteria:**
- IRP ≥15 mmHg
- NOT meeting criteria for achalasia
- Some preserved peristalsis

**Key difference from achalasia:**
- Achalasia has 100% failed peristalsis
- EGJOO has some normal contractions preserved

**Important:** EGJOO requires additional workup! May be:
- Early achalasia
- Mechanical obstruction (tumor, stricture)
- Medication effect (opioids)
- Artifact

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 9: Distal Esophageal Spasm
**Q: What are the diagnostic criteria for Distal Esophageal Spasm (DES)?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**DES Criteria:**
1. **Normal IRP** (<15 mmHg) - KEY distinguishing feature
2. **≥20% premature contractions** (DL <4.5 seconds)
3. Some normal peristalsis may be present

**Clinical features:**
- Chest pain prominent
- Dysphagia intermittent
- "Corkscrew esophagus" on barium swallow

**vs. Type III Achalasia:**
- Type III has IRP ≥15 (elevated)
- DES has IRP <15 (normal)

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 10: Jackhammer Esophagus
**Q: What defines Hypercontractile (Jackhammer) Esophagus?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Criteria:**
- ≥20% swallows with **DCI >8000 mmHg.cm.s**
- Normal IRP (<15 mmHg)

**Clinical features:**
- Severe chest pain
- Dysphagia
- Can affect any esophageal segment

**Note:** Old term "Nutcracker esophagus" referred only to high amplitude - Jackhammer uses DCI (amplitude × duration × length) which is more comprehensive

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 11: Absent Contractility
**Q: How do you differentiate Absent Contractility from Type I Achalasia?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

| Feature | Absent Contractility | Type I Achalasia |
|---------|---------------------|------------------|
| **IRP** | <15 mmHg (NORMAL) | ≥15 mmHg (elevated) |
| Peristalsis | 100% failed | 100% failed |
| LES function | Normal relaxation | Impaired relaxation |

**Key:** IRP distinguishes them!
- Same failed peristalsis
- Different LES behavior

**Causes of Absent Contractility:**
- Scleroderma/systemic sclerosis
- Severe GERD
- Post-surgical states

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 12: IEM Definition
**Q: What is Ineffective Esophageal Motility (IEM) and is it clinically significant?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Definition:** >70% ineffective swallows (failed or weak with DCI <450)

**Clinical significance:**
- Often **NOT clinically significant**
- May be secondary to GERD (impaired clearance)
- May be associated with systemic diseases

**Management:**
- Treat underlying cause (especially GERD)
- No specific intervention usually needed
- Consider in context of symptoms

**Note:** IEM is a minor disorder - don't overtreat!

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 13: Pseudoachalasia
**Q: What is pseudoachalasia and how do you differentiate it from primary achalasia?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Pseudoachalasia:** Secondary achalasia-like pattern due to mechanical obstruction (usually malignancy)

**Red flags for pseudoachalasia:**
| Feature | Primary Achalasia | Pseudoachalasia |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Age | Any (peak 30-60) | Usually >55 |
| Duration | Gradual (months-years) | Rapid (weeks-months) |
| Weight loss | Mild-moderate | Severe (>15 lbs) |

**Workup:**
- CT chest/abdomen
- EUS
- Look for mass at EGJ

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

### Card 14: HRM Protocol
**Q: What is the standard HRM protocol according to Chicago Classification v4.0?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Standard Protocol:**
1. Patient fasting ≥6 hours
2. Catheter placed transnasally
3. **10 supine water swallows** (5 mL each)
4. Wait 20-30 seconds between swallows

**Adjunctive tests (for confirmation):**
- Multiple Rapid Swallow (MRS) - 5 swallows in <10 sec
- Solid swallow (bread/cracker)
- Rapid Drink Challenge (RDC) - 200 mL water
- Upright swallows

**Change in v4.0:** Provocative tests now recommended for confirming diagnoses

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 15: Diagnostic Algorithm
**Q: What is the first-line test for evaluating dysphagia, and when is HRM indicated?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**First-line: EGD (Upper endoscopy)**
- Rules out structural lesions
- Biopsies for eosinophilic esophagitis
- Can see dilated esophagus in achalasia

**HRM indicated when:**
- EGD is normal or non-diagnostic
- Suspected motility disorder
- Pre-operative evaluation for antireflux surgery
- Post-fundoplication dysphagia

**Sequence:**
```
Dysphagia → EGD → Normal → HRM → Diagnosis
```

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

### Card 16: Barium Findings
**Q: What are the characteristic barium swallow findings in achalasia and DES?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Achalasia:**
- "Bird's beak" or "rat-tail" appearance at EGJ
- Dilated esophageal body
- Retained food/contrast
- Delayed emptying

**DES:**
- "Corkscrew" or "rosary bead" esophagus
- Non-progressive tertiary contractions
- Intermittent narrowing
- Generally normal emptying

**Note:** HRM is gold standard, but barium is useful for:
- Morphology assessment
- Pre-operative planning
- Follow-up (Timed Barium Esophagogram)

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 17: TBE Utility
**Q: What is a Timed Barium Esophagogram (TBE) and when is it used?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Definition:** Serial X-rays after barium ingestion at 1 and 5 minutes

**Interpretation:**
- Normal: Complete emptying by 5 minutes
- Abnormal: >5 cm barium column at 5 minutes

**Uses:**
1. **Pre-treatment baseline** in achalasia
2. **Post-treatment follow-up** - objective measure of improvement
3. **Comparing treatments** - standard endpoint in trials

**Advantage:** Objective, quantifiable, reproducible measure of esophageal emptying

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 18: EndoFLIP
**Q: What is EndoFLIP and what does it measure?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**EndoFLIP = Endoscopic Functional Lumen Imaging Probe**

**What it measures:**
- EGJ distensibility (EGJ-DI)
- Cross-sectional area at various pressures
- Real-time compliance assessment

**Normal EGJ-DI:** >2.8 mm²/mmHg

**Clinical uses:**
1. Confirm EGJOO when HRM equivocal
2. Intra-operative assessment during myotomy
3. Post-fundoplication evaluation
4. Predict treatment response

**Advantage:** Measures actual EGJ opening, not just pressure

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

### Card 19: Medical Treatment
**Q: What medical treatments are available for spastic esophageal disorders (DES/Jackhammer)?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Step-wise approach:**

| Step | Drug Class | Examples | Mechanism |
|------|------------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | CCB | Nifedipine 10-30mg | Smooth muscle relaxation |
| 1 | Nitrates | Isosorbide 5-10mg | NO-mediated relaxation |
| 2 | TCA | Amitriptyline 10-25mg | Pain modulation |
| 3 | PDE5-I | Sildenafil | Smooth muscle relaxation |

**For achalasia (bridge/poor surgical candidates):**
- Botulinum toxin 100 units into LES
- Temporary effect (6-12 months)

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

### Card 20: Treatment Options for Achalasia
**Q: What are the main treatment options for achalasia and their relative efficacy?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Options:**

| Treatment | Success Rate | Durability |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Pneumatic Dilation (PD) | 85-90% | May need repeat |
| LHM + Dor | 90% | Long-term |
| POEM | 90-95% | Long-term |
| Botox | 70-80% | 6-12 months |

**Selection:**
- **Type I/II:** PD, LHM, or POEM (equivalent)
- **Type III:** POEM preferred (extended myotomy)
- **High-risk:** Botox (temporary)
- **Young patients:** Consider PD or POEM (avoid surgery)
- **Previous treatment failure:** POEM

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 21: POEM Advantages
**Q: What are the advantages of POEM for treating achalasia?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Advantages of POEM:**

1. **Extended myotomy** - can reach proximal esophagus (Type III)
2. **No external incisions** - endoscopic approach
3. **Shorter hospital stay** - typically 1-2 days
4. **Anterior or posterior** - choice of approach
5. **Repeat procedure possible** - if needed
6. **Effective for failed LHM**

**Disadvantage:**
- Higher GERD rates (no fundoplication)
- 40-50% develop reflux esophagitis
- May need long-term PPI

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 22: Opioid Effect
**Q: How can opioids affect esophageal manometry findings?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Opioid effects on HRM:**
- Can cause **elevated IRP** (EGJ obstruction pattern)
- May mimic EGJOO
- Increases LES tone
- Can cause spastic contractions

**Clinical importance:**
- Always check medication list!
- Recent opioid use = unreliable HRM
- May need to repeat off opioids
- Common cause of "false" EGJOO

**Recommendation:** Hold opioids 24-48 hours before HRM if possible

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

---

### Card 23: Scleroderma Esophagus
**Q: What is the classic manometric pattern in scleroderma/systemic sclerosis?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Classic pattern:**
1. **Absent contractility** in distal 2/3 (smooth muscle)
2. **Low LES pressure** (hypotensive LES)
3. Normal proximal contractions (striated muscle preserved)

**Clinical consequence:**
- Severe GERD (no barrier + no clearance)
- Dysphagia
- Barrett's esophagus risk
- Stricture formation

**Key features:**
- IRP is LOW (unlike achalasia)
- 100% failed distal contractions
- Proximal striated muscle intact

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High

### Card 24: Version Changes
**Q: What are the key changes from Chicago Classification v3.0 to v4.0?**

<details>
<summary>Reveal Answer</summary>

**Key changes in v4.0 (2021):**

| Aspect | v3.0 | v4.0 |
|--------|------|------|
| Position | Supine only | Supine + upright option |
| IRP cutoff | Variable by system | Standardized ≥15 mmHg |
| EGJOO | Definitive diagnosis | Requires confirmation |
| Fragmented | Break >3cm | Break must be >5 cm |
| Provocative tests | Optional | Recommended for confirmation |

**Philosophy:** v4.0 emphasizes confirming findings before treatment

</details>

Confidence: ⬜ Low | ⬜ Medium | ⬜ High
