# Pancreatic Adenocarcinoma (PDAC)

> **Source**: Blumgart Ch 60-63, NCCN Guidelines
> **Topic System**: [[Pancreas Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> PDAC has worst prognosis of solid tumors (11.5% 5-year survival), with resectability determined by arterial/venous involvement, KRAS mutated in >90%, and neoadjuvant therapy now preferred for borderline resectable disease.

---

## Epidemiology

- **Incidence:** 62,610 new cases/year (US) - 3.2% of all cancers
- **Mortality:** 49,830 deaths/year - 4th leading cause of cancer death
- **5-year survival:** 11.5% overall (worst of all solid tumors)
  - Localized: 44.3%
  - Regional: 16.2%
  - Distant: 3%
- **Presentation:** 51% with distant disease at diagnosis

## Molecular Pathology

### Somatic Mutations
| Gene | Frequency | Significance |
|------|-----------|--------------|
| **KRAS** | >90% | Most common; G12D (44%), G12V (31%) |
| **TP53** | 70% | Cell cycle dysregulation |
| **SMAD4** | 50% | TGF-β pathway |
| **CDKN2A** | 40% | p16 inactivation |

> 💡 **ASCO/NCCN:** Recommend germline AND somatic testing for ALL PDAC patients

## TNM Staging (AJCC 8th Edition)

### Key Changes in 8th Edition
- T-stage now based on **SIZE** (not extrapancreatic extension)
- N-stage based on **NUMBER** of nodes

| Stage | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **T1** | ≤2 cm |
| **T2** | >2 cm and ≤4 cm |
| **T3** | >4 cm |
| **T4** | Involves celiac axis or SMA (unresectable) |

| N Stage | Nodes |
|---------|-------|
| N0 | 0 positive |
| N1 | 1-3 positive |
| N2 | ≥4 positive |

## Resectability Classification

| Category | Arterial | Venous |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Resectable** | No contact with CA, SMA, CHA | No contact OR ≤180° SMV/PV without irregularity |
| **Borderline Resectable** | Head: CHA contact, SMA ≤180° | >180° SMV/PV; OR ≤180° with contour irregularity |
| **Locally Advanced** | Head: SMA/CA >180° | Unreconstructible SMV/PV |

## Diagnostic Workup

### Essential Imaging
1. **Pancreas protocol CT** (thin cuts, dual phase, 3D reconstructions)
2. **CT chest** - Lung metastases
3. **EUS-FNA** - Tissue diagnosis
4. **MRI/MRCP** - Biliary anatomy

### Tumor Markers
| Marker | Use |
|--------|-----|
| **CA 19-9** | Baseline, response monitoring (falsely elevated if jaundiced) |

> **Note:** 10% of patients are Lewis antigen negative (nonproducers)

## Pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple)

### Seven Major Steps
1. Isolate infrapancreatic SMV
2. Extensive Kocher maneuver
3. Portal dissection (hepatic artery, cholecystectomy, divide bile duct)
4. Transect stomach/duodenum
5. Divide proximal jejunum and ligament of Treitz
6. Transect pancreatic neck, resect uncinate from SMA
7. Reconstruction (PJ → HJ → GJ)

### Anastomoses
1. **Pancreaticojejunostomy** (or pancreaticogastrostomy)
2. **Hepaticojejunostomy**
3. **Gastrojejunostomy** (or duodenojejunostomy if pylorus-preserving)

---

## Treatment by Stage

| Stage | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Resectable** | Surgery → Adjuvant chemo (mFOLFIRINOX × 6 mo) |
| **Borderline Resectable** | Neoadjuvant chemo/CRT → Restaging → Surgery if possible |
| **Locally Advanced** | Chemo (FOLFIRINOX) → Reassess |
| **Metastatic** | Palliative chemo, symptom management |

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

> 💎 **Pearl 1:** Painless jaundice + weight loss = pancreatic head cancer until proven otherwise

> 💎 **Pearl 2:** CA 19-9 is falsely elevated with obstruction – interpret carefully

> 💎 **Pearl 3:** Resectability = vascular involvement (SMA, celiac axis, SMV/PV)

> 💎 **Pearl 4:** 8th edition staging: SIZE matters (T stage), NODE COUNT matters (N stage)

> 💎 **Pearl 5:** All PDAC patients need germline testing (5-19% have germline mutations)
