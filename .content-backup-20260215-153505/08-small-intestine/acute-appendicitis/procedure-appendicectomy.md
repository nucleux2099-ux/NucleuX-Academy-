# 🔧 Appendicectomy — Procedural Notes

## Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Procedure** | Removal of the vermiform appendix |
| **Indication** | Acute appendicitis, interval appendicectomy, incidental |
| **Approaches** | Open (Gridiron/Lanz) or Laparoscopic |
| **Anesthesia** | General anesthesia |
| **Duration** | 30-60 minutes |

---

## 🔵 Open Appendicectomy

### Indications for Open Approach
- Limited laparoscopic expertise
- Previous extensive abdominal surgery
- Generalized peritonitis (some surgeons prefer)
- Resource-limited settings

### Incisions

| Incision | Description | Advantage |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Gridiron (McBurney's)** | Oblique incision perpendicular to line joining ASIS-umbilicus at McBurney's point | Muscle-splitting, less damage |
| **Lanz** | Transverse incision in skin crease at McBurney's point | Better cosmesis |
| **Rutherford-Morrison** | Extended Gridiron for difficult cases | Better exposure |

### Steps (Gridiron Approach)

1. **Position:** Supine
2. **Incision:** 
   - Mark McBurney's point
   - Oblique skin incision (3-5 cm)
   
3. **Dissection through layers:**
   ```
   Skin
     ↓
   Subcutaneous fat
     ↓
   External oblique aponeurosis (split along fibers)
     ↓
   Internal oblique muscle (split along fibers)
     ↓
   Transversus abdominis (split along fibers)
     ↓
   Transversalis fascia
     ↓
   Peritoneum
   ```

4. **Identify caecum** → Follow taeniae coli to appendix base

5. **Mobilize appendix:**
   - Divide mesoappendix with appendicular artery
   - Ligate artery (may need to do in segments if inflamed)

6. **Appendix removal:**
   - Clamp base
   - Ligate with absorbable suture (2-0 Vicryl)
   - Divide appendix
   - Cauterize/ligate stump

7. **Stump management options:**
   - Simple ligation (most common now)
   - Purse-string burial (traditional)
   - Z-stitch burial

8. **Closure:** Layer by layer, skin with subcuticular or interrupted

### Intraoperative Findings to Note
- Appendix position and condition
- Perforation site (if any)
- Pus amount and character
- Faecolith presence
- Caecum and terminal ileum condition
- Ovaries and tubes (in females)

## 🟢 Laparoscopic Appendicectomy

### Advantages over Open
- Less wound infection
- Less postoperative pain
- Faster recovery
- Better cosmesis
- Diagnostic capability (if diagnosis uncertain)
- Better visualization (obese patients)

### Contraindications (Relative)
- Hemodynamic instability
- Severe cardiopulmonary disease
- Pregnancy (relative - actually safe)
- Previous extensive surgery

### Port Placement

**Standard 3-port technique:**
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│           UMBILICUS                 │
│              ⬤ 10mm (Camera)        │
│                                     │
│    ⬤ 5mm                   ⬤ 5mm   │
│  (LIF - working)      (Suprapubic) │
│                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Alternative positions:**
- Single incision laparoscopic surgery (SILS)
- 2-port technique (experienced surgeons)

### Steps

1. **Pneumoperitoneum:** 
   - Veress needle or open (Hasson) technique
   - Pressure: 12-14 mmHg

2. **Port insertion:**
   - 10mm umbilical (camera)
   - 5mm suprapubic (grasper)
   - 5mm LIF (working port)

3. **Identification:**
   - Visualize appendix
   - Assess inflammation, position, adhesions

4. **Mesoappendix division:**
   - Window in mesoappendix at base
   - Divide with:
     - Bipolar cautery
     - Harmonic scalpel
     - Clips (appendicular artery)
     - Endoloop

5. **Appendix base:**
   - Endoloop × 2 (proximal)
   - Endoloop × 1 (distal) or clip
   - Divide between
   - Alternative: Endostapler (for inflamed base)

6. **Specimen retrieval:**
   - Through 10mm port
   - Use endobag if perforated/gangrenous

7. **Irrigation:** Saline wash if contaminated

8. **Port closure:** Fascia for 10mm, skin for all

## ⚠️ Intraoperative Complications

| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| **Bleeding** | Pressure, clip, convert if uncontrolled |
| **Caecal injury** | Primary repair, consider conversion |
| **Appendix base necrosis** | Stapler, partial caecectomy |
| **Unable to locate appendix** | Systematic search, convert |
| **Incidental Meckel's** | Remove if symptomatic/young patient |

---

## 📝 Post-operative Care

### Uncomplicated
- Diet: Clear fluids → regular diet as tolerated
- Mobilization: Same day
- Analgesia: Paracetamol ± NSAIDs
- Antibiotics: Single pre-op dose sufficient
- Discharge: 24-48 hours

### Complicated (Perforation/Abscess)
- NPO until bowel function returns
- IV antibiotics 5-7 days
- Monitor for abscess (fever, rising WBC)
- Longer hospital stay

---

## 🏥 Complications

### Early
- Wound infection (most common)
- Intra-abdominal abscess
- Bleeding
- Ileus

### Late
- Adhesive intestinal obstruction
- Incisional hernia
- Stump appendicitis (rare)
- Fecal fistula (caecal injury)

---

## 📊 Evidence-Based Points

| Topic | Evidence |
|-------|----------|
| Lap vs Open | Lap: less infection, faster recovery, similar efficacy |
| Antibiotics | Pre-op prophylaxis reduces wound infection |
| Stump management | Simple ligation = burial (no difference in complications) |
| Negative appendicectomy | Acceptable rate <15-20% |
| Drainage | Not routinely recommended |
