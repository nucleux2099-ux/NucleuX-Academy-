# 🔍 Deep Vein Thrombosis — Explorer Mode

> **The Big Picture:** Clot in deep veins. Virchow's triad explains it all. Danger is PE. Prevention is better than cure. Anticoagulation is mainstay.

## 💡 Key Atomic Concepts

### Atom 1: Wells Score
> Clinical prediction rule for DVT probability
> ≤0 = Low risk (DVT unlikely)
> 1-2 = Moderate risk
> ≥3 = High risk (DVT likely)

### Atom 2: D-Dimer
> High sensitivity, low specificity
> Good for RULING OUT DVT (if negative + low Wells)
> Positive D-dimer needs imaging confirmation

### Atom 3: Post-Thrombotic Syndrome
> Chronic sequela of DVT
> Valve destruction → Chronic venous hypertension
> Pain, swelling, skin changes, ulceration
> Prevented by adequate anticoagulation and compression

## 📊 Clinical Features

| Feature | Finding |
|---------|---------|
| **Pain** | Calf tenderness |
| **Swelling** | Unilateral leg edema (>3cm difference) |
| **Homan's sign** | Pain on dorsiflexion (unreliable) |
| **Skin** | Warm, erythema |
| **Veins** | Dilated superficial veins |
| **Phlegmasia** | Massive DVT → Blue/white painful leg |
