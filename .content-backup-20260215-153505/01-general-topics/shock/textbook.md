## Definition

Shock is a state of inadequate tissue perfusion resulting in cellular hypoxia and dysfunction. It represents a failure of the circulatory system to meet the metabolic demands of tissues. Without prompt recognition and treatment, shock progresses to irreversible organ damage and death.

📚 *Bailey & Love, 28th Ed, Ch 2, p.13*

---

## Pathophysiology

### Normal Oxygen Delivery

Oxygen delivery (DO₂) depends on:
- Cardiac output (CO) = Heart Rate × Stroke Volume
- Arterial oxygen content = (Hb × 1.34 × SaO₂) + (0.003 × PaO₂)

**DO₂ = CO × Arterial O₂ Content**

Normal DO₂ ≈ 1000 mL O₂/min

### Cellular Response to Hypoxia

When oxygen delivery fails:
1. **Aerobic metabolism fails** → ATP production drops
2. **Anaerobic glycolysis** → Lactate production
3. **Cell membrane dysfunction** → Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase failure
4. **Cellular swelling** → Organelle damage
5. **Lysosomal rupture** → Autodigestion
6. **Cell death** → Organ failure

📚 *Sabiston, 21st Ed, Ch 4, p.63*

## Classification of Shock

### 1. Hypovolemic Shock

**Mechanism:** Decreased circulating volume → Decreased preload → Decreased cardiac output

**Causes:**
- **Hemorrhagic:** Trauma, GI bleeding, ruptured aneurysm, postoperative
- **Non-hemorrhagic:** Dehydration, burns, pancreatitis, third-spacing

**Hemodynamic profile:**
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| CVP/PCWP | ↓ |
| Cardiac Output | ↓ |
| SVR | ↑ (compensatory vasoconstriction) |
| SvO₂ | ↓ (increased extraction) |

### 2. Cardiogenic Shock

**Mechanism:** Pump failure → Decreased cardiac output despite adequate preload

**Causes:**
- Acute myocardial infarction (most common)
- Arrhythmias
- Acute valvular dysfunction
- Myocarditis
- Cardiomyopathy

**Hemodynamic profile:**
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| CVP/PCWP | ↑ (backup of blood) |
| Cardiac Output | ↓ |
| SVR | ↑ |
| SvO₂ | ↓ |

### 3. Distributive Shock

**Mechanism:** Massive vasodilation → Maldistribution of blood flow

**Types:**
- **Septic shock:** Most common in ICU; cytokine-mediated vasodilation
- **Anaphylactic shock:** IgE-mediated histamine release
- **Neurogenic shock:** Loss of sympathetic tone (spinal cord injury)
- **Adrenal crisis:** Cortisol deficiency

**Hemodynamic profile (warm septic shock):**
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| CVP/PCWP | ↓ or Normal |
| Cardiac Output | ↑ or Normal |
| SVR | ↓ (KEY DIFFERENTIATOR) |
| SvO₂ | ↑ (cells can't extract O₂) |

### 4. Obstructive Shock

**Mechanism:** Mechanical obstruction to blood flow

**Causes:**
- Pulmonary embolism
- Cardiac tamponade
- Tension pneumothorax
- Constrictive pericarditis

**Hemodynamic profile:**
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| CVP | ↑ |
| Cardiac Output | ↓ |
| SVR | ↑ |
| SvO₂ | ↓ |

📚 *Schwartz, 11th Ed, Ch 5, p.95-100*

## Hemorrhagic Shock (ATLS Classification)

The American College of Surgeons ATLS classification is essential knowledge:

| Parameter | Class I | Class II | Class III | Class IV |
|-----------|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Blood loss (mL)** | <750 | 750-1500 | 1500-2000 | >2000 |
| **Blood loss (%)** | <15% | 15-30% | 30-40% | >40% |
| **Heart rate** | <100 | 100-120 | 120-140 | >140 |
| **Blood pressure** | Normal | Normal | ↓ | ↓↓ |
| **Pulse pressure** | Normal | ↓ | ↓↓ | ↓↓↓ |
| **Respiratory rate** | 14-20 | 20-30 | 30-40 | >40 |
| **Urine output (mL/hr)** | >30 | 20-30 | 5-15 | Negligible |
| **Mental status** | Slightly anxious | Mildly anxious | Anxious, confused | Confused, lethargic |
| **Fluid replacement** | Crystalloid | Crystalloid | Crystalloid + Blood | Crystalloid + Blood |

**Key teaching point:** Blood pressure is maintained until Class III (>30% loss) due to compensatory mechanisms. Tachycardia and narrowed pulse pressure are earlier signs.

📚 *ATLS Manual, 10th Ed, Ch 3*

## Clinical Recognition

### Compensated Shock
- Tachycardia
- Peripheral vasoconstriction (cool extremities)
- Tachypnea
- Oliguria
- Anxiety
- Blood pressure maintained

### Decompensated Shock
- Hypotension (SBP <90 or MAP <65)
- Severe tachycardia
- Altered consciousness
- Anuria
- Metabolic acidosis
- Mottled skin

### Irreversible Shock
- Multi-organ failure
- Refractory hypotension
- Severe acidosis (pH <7.2)
- High mortality despite treatment

## Diagnostic Markers

### Shock Index
**Formula:** HR ÷ SBP

| Value | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| 0.5-0.7 | Normal |
| 0.7-1.0 | Mild shock |
| 1.0-1.5 | Moderate shock |
| >1.5 | Severe shock |

Shock Index is more sensitive than BP alone for detecting occult shock.

### Lactate
- Normal: <2 mmol/L
- Elevated lactate indicates anaerobic metabolism
- Serial lactate (lactate clearance) predicts outcomes
- Target: >10% clearance per 2-4 hours

### Base Deficit
- Reflects metabolic acidosis from anaerobic metabolism
- >6 mEq/L suggests significant shock

### ScvO₂ (Central Venous O₂ Saturation)
- Normal: 65-70%
- <65%: Inadequate DO₂ or increased extraction
- >70% in sepsis: Cells unable to extract oxygen

📚 *Sabiston, 21st Ed, Ch 4, p.68*

## Management

### General Principles
1. **Recognize early** — Don't wait for hypotension
2. **Identify type** — Treat underlying cause
3. **Resuscitate** — Restore tissue perfusion
4. **Monitor response** — Use endpoints, not just BP

### Initial Resuscitation

**Airway and Breathing:**
- Ensure airway patency
- High-flow oxygen
- Consider intubation if GCS <8 or respiratory failure

**Circulation:**
- Large-bore IV access (×2)
- Crystalloid bolus: 1-2 L (or 30 mL/kg) in sepsis
- Assess response (HR, BP, urine output, lactate)
- Blood products if hemorrhagic (1:1:1 ratio in massive transfusion)

### Vasopressors and Inotropes

| Drug | Receptor | Primary Effect | Indication |
|------|----------|----------------|------------|
| **Norepinephrine** | α1 >> β1 | Vasoconstriction | Septic shock (1st line) |
| **Epinephrine** | α + β | Vasoconstriction + Inotrope | Anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest |
| **Vasopressin** | V1 | Vasoconstriction | Add-on in septic shock |
| **Dopamine** | D > β > α | Dose-dependent | 2nd line (more arrhythmias) |
| **Dobutamine** | β1 > β2 | Inotrope | Cardiogenic shock |
| **Phenylephrine** | α1 | Vasoconstriction | Neurogenic shock |

📚 *Surviving Sepsis Campaign Guidelines 2021*

## Specific Management

### Septic Shock (Hour-1 Bundle)
1. Measure lactate
2. Blood cultures before antibiotics
3. Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
4. 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4
5. Vasopressors (norepinephrine) if hypotension persists

### Hemorrhagic Shock
1. Control hemorrhage (surgery/intervention)
2. Permissive hypotension (SBP 80-90) until control achieved
3. Balanced resuscitation (avoid crystalloid overload)
4. Massive transfusion protocol if needed (1:1:1 PRBC:FFP:Platelets)
5. Tranexamic acid within 3 hours

### Cardiogenic Shock
1. Treat underlying cause (PCI for STEMI)
2. Avoid excessive fluids
3. Inotropes (dobutamine)
4. Consider mechanical support (IABP, ECMO)

### Anaphylactic Shock
1. **Epinephrine IM** (0.3-0.5 mg) — FIRST LINE
2. IV fluids
3. Antihistamines
4. Steroids (prevent biphasic reaction)
5. Remove trigger

### Neurogenic Shock
1. Differentiate from hemorrhagic (warm, bradycardic)
2. Vasopressors (not just fluids)
3. Atropine for symptomatic bradycardia

📚 *Schwartz, 11th Ed, Ch 5, p.110-118*

## Monitoring and Endpoints

### Clinical Endpoints
- MAP ≥65 mmHg
- Urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr
- Mental status improvement
- Capillary refill <3 seconds

### Laboratory Endpoints
- Lactate clearance >10% per 2 hours
- Base deficit normalizing
- ScvO₂ >70%

### Advanced Monitoring
- Arterial line (continuous BP)
- Central venous catheter (CVP, ScvO₂)
- Pulmonary artery catheter (rarely used now)
- Echocardiography (cardiac function)
- Point-of-care ultrasound (volume status)

---

## Key Points Summary

1. Shock = inadequate tissue perfusion
2. Four types: Hypovolemic, Cardiogenic, Distributive, Obstructive
3. Tachycardia precedes hypotension — recognize early
4. Shock Index (HR/SBP) >1.0 indicates significant shock
5. Lactate >2 mmol/L indicates tissue hypoperfusion
6. Crystalloids are first-line fluids
7. Norepinephrine = first-line vasopressor in septic shock
8. Epinephrine = drug of choice in anaphylaxis
9. Distributive shock is the only type with LOW SVR
10. Hour-1 bundle is critical in sepsis
