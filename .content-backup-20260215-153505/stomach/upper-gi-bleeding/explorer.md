# Upper GI Bleeding

> **Source**: Sleisenger Ch 20, Ch 92
> **Topic System**: [[Stomach Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> UGIB presents with hematemesis/melena, stratified by Glasgow-Blatchford (pre-endoscopy) and Rockall (post-endoscopy), managed with resuscitation (transfuse if Hgb <7), PPI infusion, and EGD within 24h (or emergent if unstable/variceal).

---

## Definition & Epidemiology

**Upper GI Bleeding (UGIB)** = Bleeding from esophagus, stomach, or duodenum (proximal to ligament of Treitz)

- **Hospitalization rate:** ~500,000/year (USA)
- **Mortality:** 5-10%
- **Self-limited:** 80% stop spontaneously
- **Rebleeding:** 20% continue/rebleed → mortality 30-40%

## Clinical Presentation

| Term | Definition | Implication |
|------|------------|-------------|
| **Hematemesis** | Vomiting blood (fresh/coffee-ground) | Definite UGIB |
| **Melena** | Black, tarry stool | Usually UGIB (50-100mL blood) |
| **Hematochezia** | Bright red blood per rectum | Usually LGIB, but can be brisk UGIB |

### "Severe GI Bleeding" Criteria
Any ONE of:
- Shock or orthostatic hypotension
- Hematocrit drop ≥6%
- Transfusion requirement ≥2 units pRBC

---

## Risk Stratification

### Pre-Endoscopy: Glasgow-Blatchford Score (GBS)

| Variable | Score Range |
|----------|-------------|
| BUN | 0-6 points |
| Hemoglobin | 0-6 points |
| SBP | 0-3 points |
| Heart rate ≥100 | 1 point |
| Melena | 1 point |
| Syncope | 2 points |
| Hepatic disease | 2 points |
| Cardiac failure | 2 points |

> 💡 **GBS = 0 → Safe for outpatient management**

### Post-Endoscopy: Rockall Score
- Uses clinical AND endoscopic findings
- Predicts rebleeding and mortality

---

## Forrest Classification (Ulcer Bleeding)

| Class | Appearance | Rebleed Risk | Management |
|-------|------------|--------------|------------|
| **Ia** | Spurting | 90% | Endoscopic therapy |
| **Ib** | Oozing | 50% | Endoscopic therapy |
| **IIa** | Visible vessel | 40% | Endoscopic therapy |
| **IIb** | Adherent clot | 20-30% | Remove clot, treat lesion |
| **IIc** | Flat spot | 10% | PPI, no therapy |
| **III** | Clean base | 5% | Early discharge |

## Transfusion Thresholds

| Threshold | Population |
|-----------|------------|
| Hgb <7 g/dL | Most patients (restrictive strategy) |
| Hgb <8-9 g/dL | Active ischemia, significant comorbidities |

> 💡 **TRIGGER trial:** Restrictive transfusion reduces mortality in UGIB

---

## PPI Therapy

### Pre-Endoscopy
- **IV PPI bolus** (80 mg) reduces high-risk stigmata at EGD
- Does NOT reduce rebleeding or mortality without endoscopy

### Post-Endoscopy (High-Risk Ulcers)
- IV PPI infusion × 72 hours
- Reduces rebleeding vs. intermittent dosing

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

> 💎 **Pearl 1:** GBS = 0 can be managed as outpatient (very low risk)

> 💎 **Pearl 2:** Transfuse at Hgb <7 (restrictive) – liberal transfusion increases mortality

> 💎 **Pearl 3:** Octreotide early if variceal bleeding suspected (even before EGD)

> 💎 **Pearl 4:** High-risk ulcer stigmata (Forrest Ia, Ib, IIa) need endoscopic therapy

> 💎 **Pearl 5:** Dual therapy (injection + clips/cautery) superior to monotherapy
