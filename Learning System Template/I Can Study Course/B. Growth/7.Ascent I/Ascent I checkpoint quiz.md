## Learning notes

### Be Like Water

Waterfalling is an issue we commonly see in learners who are strongly influenced by _framing bias_. It indicates a pattern of mapping where the connections are “top-down” with very little connectivity between ideas that exist on the same “layer” of the map hierarchy. Waterfalling can be avoided by frequently thinking about how concepts relate to each other, even after they have been added into chunks. It’s also important to consider how chunks are related to other chunks.

It can be tempting to simply confirm that the only relationships are those we have already committed and thought about. However, high-level learners take the time to consider the relationships they have _not_ yet thought about, as these could influence their chunk structure and, therefore, the quality of their memory and understanding.

## Learning notes

### Antisocial Chunks

Lonely chunks refers to when chunks are created but contain no content. It is essentially a storage box with nothing inside it. This commonly occurs when topics are prechunked based on framing bias and not corrected by the inquiry process. If chunks are formed correctly based on identifying similarities between ideas and then looking for ways to group them, there should never be a chunk that is empty. This is because the chunk is defined by the potential contents in the first place.

Lonely chunks are acceptable when hypothesizing potential groups. However, this should be corrected while studying more as we realise this chunk is unnecessary. Keeping lonely chunks is detrimental to memory as it adds additional elements to our map and increases our cognitive load unnecessarily.

## Learning notes

### Relationships Vs Chunking

Lower-order inquiry is when the questions asked promote only isolated thinking. This is detrimental to learning as this kind of thinking does not create strong memory or understanding. Lower-order inquiry can be avoided by following the question quality guidelines which keep questions broad, open and multi-relational.
