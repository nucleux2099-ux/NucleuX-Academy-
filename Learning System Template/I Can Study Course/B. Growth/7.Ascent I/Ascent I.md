# Ascent I — Aim

> [!info] Stage focus
> Build the Aim step of the Bear Hunter System. Precision questioning, importance-based chunking, and cognitive load management form the conceptual scaffold for deeper encoding.

## How to work this stage

- Start every study session with Aim questions: “Why is this important?” and “How is it related to ___?”
- Draft tentative chunk structures and revisit them after each green-light pass.
- Capture confusion in Kolb reflections—it signals productive load.

### Practice checklist
- Minimum three Aim sessions logged with chunk sketches
- Kolb reflection on at least one Aim attempt
- Questions catalogue maintained (use it when moving into Shoot)

### Ready to move on when…
1. You can explain the rationale behind your chunk hypotheses.
2. Aim questions consistently uncover relationships rather than facts.
3. You’ve documented common Aim pitfalls and how you correct them.
