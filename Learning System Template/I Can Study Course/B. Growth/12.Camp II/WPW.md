# WPW
## Teaching to learn

It is not good to be dependent on a good teacher. By developing the skills to be your own best teacher, you will always be able to teach yourself!

This is where the Whole-Part-Whole (WPW) technique comes in. This technique is highly multi-order, covering all levels of retrieval in Bloom's revised taxonomy.

![Bloom's revised taxonomy](https://learning.icanstudy.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fprod.icanstudy.com%2F%2Fsites%2Fdefault%2Ffiles%2Finline-images%2FBloom%2527s%2520simplified%2520version_1.png&w=3840&q=75)

Bloom's revised taxonomy (2001)

This multi-level coverage is what makes the technique so powerful. However, a significant amount of practice is required to perform this skill correctly. Even those who have a perfect level of competence with the methods in this program can take months to master WPW.

## High-volume retrieval practice (HVRP)

The WPW reteaching strategy is an example of **high volume retrieval practice (HVRP)**.

This is an excellent approach during the early to mid stages of revision as it allows you to catch mistakes and target weaknesses quickly. It covers the topic very comprehensively, including relationships and specific details. Another example of HVRP is brain dumps. However, we have found this method to be less effective at testing relationships and conceptual understanding.

_We recommend using HVRP as part of your spaced retrieval. This will help prepare you to use your knowledge for exams, essays and problem-solving._

### Implementing HVRP

Schedule HVRP for your **end-of-week** or **2-week** revision session. This way, your subsequent revision sessions will be more targeted. Below is an example of what your learning schedule might look like.

|   |   |
|---|---|
|**Timing**|**Activity**|
|Before class/lecture (e.g., the weekend before or even weeks in advance)|Prestudy with inquiry-based learning via the BHS, creating at least a basic mind map up to layers 1 or 2 (if not layer 3) of learning.|
|During class/lecture|Consolidate layers 1, 2 and 3. <br><br>For subjects like Maths or Physics, use this time to practice applying techniques and using the information to solve problems.|
|Same day after class|Consolidate and add layer 4. <br><br>Skin your mind maps and create flashcards (and potentially test questions) for yourself.|
|Mid-week (only necessary if you are studying a high volume of material every week)|HVRP using WPW|
|End of the week|HVRP using WPW. If you already did this mid-week, do it for the new content you’ve covered since then.<br><br>For additional revision, answer the test questions you created (or share them with your study group).|
|Approximately 3 to 4 weeks later, and then again before the test or exam (if you have time)|HVRP via brain dump (linear or mind map) and practice papers.|

You should consistently complete your flashcards daily using discrete pockets of available time via microlearning. Flashcards should not need their own scheduled session.
