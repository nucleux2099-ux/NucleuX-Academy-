## Decisional delays

# What are decisional delays?

Decisional delays are the time it takes to decide what to do next. Cutting this time out may potentially save you hours a day. Decisional delays also interrupt your _flow state._

Decisional delays are harmful because...

- They are barriers to maintaining flow states. This has potentially game-changing impacts on overall productivity as each break in flow requires an average of 15 minutes to re-enter flow. This can easily contribute to over an hour of delays daily.
- They use limited cognitive resources on planning that can make you unnecessarily tired and unfocused.
- They provide minimal positive value. The extra processing time does not confer any meaningful advantage.

# Identifying decisional delays

Follow these instructions to start becoming aware of how decisional delays affect you.

1. Use a time-tracking app and track how you spend your time every day for around one week.
2. Every time you break out of a deep work/flow state, end the task time tracking and restart it once you are re-entering a focused session.
3. Compare the time you tracked to the schedule you set.
4. Identify the amount of time that was spent between tasks that your schedule did not plan for and the number of flow breaks that occurred. Each flow break can create 10 to 15 minutes of cognitive disruption and "lost time".
5. Using these numbers, you can estimate how much time and disruption is caused by decisional delays.

Note that simply tracking your time and becoming aware of decisional delays is an important first step towards solving them.

# How to mitigate decisional delays

1. Experiment with your schedule and task flow to batch planning and prioritisation at the beginning or end of each day to avoid the need for mid-day planning.
2. Block distractions from focus zones so new decisions do not enter your radar while you are working.
3. Create very clear and specific instructions for yourself during planning sessions so there is no doubt or need for decision-making when you are in execution mode.
