# Loss aversion
## "Good enough"

After seeing all of the skills and strategies in this course, you may feel it’s too _difficult_ or _time-consuming_ to implement and practise them.

Often, learners have expressed that their current methods are “good enough”. However, many people have commented to us in our community, through emails, reviews, and in-person, that they regretted this months or years later.

### The small pond

People often compare themselves with those immediately around them. They feel satisfied as long as they outperform the people they can see. However, this is misleading if you are having to compete against others outside of your immediate visibility.

**Ask yourself:** Am I comparing myself to _others_, or am I comparing myself to my _standards_?

### Key principle

Always seek to set realistic and appropriate standards and use them for comparison. Comparing yourself to others to understand your performance is risky, unreliable, and unhealthy.

## Short-term pain, long-term gain

What is “good enough” now may not be good enough for the future. People have a misconception that if things become more difficult, they can solve it by just trying harder. However, this often fails because everyone else also tries harder!

**Ask yourself:** Am I preparing myself for future challenges?

### Key principle

Always seek to use and familiarise yourself with methods and approaches that will help you both in the short _and_ long term. If your current method is heavily effort and motivation-dependent, this leaves very little room for error or improvement.

## Loss aversion bias

Loss aversion is a phenomenon whereby people always prefer **not to lose** something they already have, even if it means forgoing an opportunity to **gain** something else. Because of loss aversion, you may tend to think your current process is “good enough” and hesitate to make changes.

**Ask yourself:** Am I investing in gaining skills and assets that will benefit me for life, or am I avoiding the opportunity to gain tomorrow out of fear of losing today?

### Key principle

Become aware of when you make decisions out of fear and avoidance. This can indicate that your decisions are being made with too much emotional mind and not enough rational mind!

#### Further reading on loss aversion

Loss aversion is a phenomenon that has been shown repeatedly in multiple replicated [studies](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2020/05/200518144913.htm). There is also some newer [evidence](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.02723/full) that suggests that the effects of loss aversion can be subtle and unpredictable.


## Risk management

Part of overcoming the “good enough” mentality is systematically sorting through our “risks”. Sometimes, the risks we _perceive_ in a decision or action may not be the full scope of the risks.

|Perceived risk|Hidden risk of this behaviour|
|---|---|
|**Risk of time wasted compared to alternatives.**<br><br>E.g. "I don't have the time to practice these techniques when there is so much already I'm behind on!"|Risk of continuing to fall behind as current methods caused us to fall behind in the first place.|
|**Risk of investing time and effort into making unnecessary changes.**<br><br>E.g. "I'll be ok with what I'm doing now. It's worked fine so far!"|Risk of losing out on long-term opportunities due to inadequate skills.<br><br>Risk of being unable to overcome future challenges if the difficulty increases.|
|**Risk of learning new skills incorrectly or getting things wrong.**|Risk of never learning new skills due to avoiding experimentation.|
