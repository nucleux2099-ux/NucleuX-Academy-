# Scheduling better – prioritising
## Tasks over time

Prioritising maximises the value of your time. Just 15 minutes spent prioritising can save you days. This is why task prioritisation is more important than time management.

### Not all time is spent equal.

Time spent on something that gets you to your goals is more important than time spent on something less meaningful.

No matter how great your schedule is, it won’t help you reach your goals if it is not _aligned_ with those goals. To achieve your goals as efficiently as possible, you must prioritise the _content_ of your schedule.

![[Image 26.jpg|A graph showing minimal progress towards goal when many tasks are not prioritised.]]

When we have full schedules, but the tasks are not well-prioritised, we do not improve our position or progress towards our goal effectively.

Don't confuse being busy with being productive. Productive usage of time means that your time is spent on things that get you closer to your goals (whatever those goals are).

![[Image 27.jpg|When most of our tasks are prioritised, we make much faster progress towards our goal without being any busier!]]

## A simple priority check

At the end of every day, identify the one or two highest priority items that put the future you in the best position. Remember that urgent doesn't always mean important!

**Once you have identified these, block them into your schedule. All of your other tasks should be centred around these blocks.**

### Helpful questions for prioritising well

Ask yourself these questions to identify high priority items to block out in your schedule

1. What are the one or two most important things for me to complete?
2. What (if done) would provide me the best value or put me in the best position in the future?
3. What skill, attribute, or resource do I need that would be life-changing (even if it isn't urgent right now)?
