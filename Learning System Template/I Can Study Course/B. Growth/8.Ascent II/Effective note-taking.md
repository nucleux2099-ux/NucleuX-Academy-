# Effective note-taking
## Evidence-based note-taking

The science around note-taking is complicated. Here are some of the key principles of note-taking that we know from reviewing the latest research.

Key principles of note-taking

1. The method we use to take notes matters less than how we are thinking as we take notes.
2. Good note-taking can help our brain think by reducing unhelpful and extraneous cognitive load.
3. Bad note-taking can make us avoid thinking, which reduces the effectiveness of learning.
4. Wordier notes are usually less processed and therefore less effective. Typing can exacerbate this as it is usually easier to create high volumes of notes compared to freehand writing.

In the sublessons below, we will explore some of these principles in much greater depth to see how you can refine your note-taking technique.
