# Beginner’s mindmap guide
## Art of mapping

By now, you've probably realised that creating mind maps is a surprisingly technical skill.

Below, we'll summarise some of the biggest steps as a general refresher of the process. Then we will explore some additional examples and dissections.

### Creating a good mindmap: An overview

1. Identify the biggest chunks. These will be hypothetical at first. There are usually 3 to 7 for a single topic.
2. Arrange the chunks and most important contained concepts so that the relationship and flow between them is expressed.
3. Identify sub-concepts and sub-chunks and add them to the map. Always think about how each new chunk or concept fits into the big picture.
4. Add more details until the topic is complete. Information that is highly specific and not related to other parts on the map can be added to memorisation techniques such as flashcards.

General tips

1. When arranging your first chunks, leave big spaces between them so there is room for sub-chunks and other concepts.
2. Make the main flow of relationships between the largest chunks visually very clear. This is the "backbone" of the topic.
3. Remember to relate each new item to the bigger picture as you go.
4. Constantly refine and revise your structure. Do not overly commit to your first chunk structure. Everything is just a hypothesis!

### Missing something?

If you spot a part of the process that you were neglected or skipping, attempt a mindmap following the guidelines above before moving on.
