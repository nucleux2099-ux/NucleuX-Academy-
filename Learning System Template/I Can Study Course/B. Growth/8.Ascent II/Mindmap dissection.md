# Mindmap dissection
## Learning happens in the brain 🧠

Simply completing a mindmap does not make your learning effective. If misused, mind maps can be just as bad as other types of notes. What's important is to use the right cognitive process so that mindmapping helps your brain think.

You know you are mindmapping correctly when you...

1. Find it easier to think of new relationships and chunks by seeing what you have already written down
2. Are able to reflect on how each individual piece connects to the big picture
3. Find that it feels messy and confusing at first, but over time as the mindmap becomes more organised and accurate, your knowledge also feels more organised and accurate
4. Continuously refine and update your mindmap (which indicates that you are constantly thinking of the big picture, holding yourself to a high standard, and keeping structures hypothetical)
5. Constantly engage in higher-order thinking processes (analysing by finding similarities and differences and evaluating by prioritising and making value judgments)
