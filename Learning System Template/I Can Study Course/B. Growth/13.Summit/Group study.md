# Group study
## Group study

Group study can be a valuable tool to motivate one another, revise, and prepare for assessments. However, it may be easier to procrastinate and distract each other if it is unstructured. There are three major roles that you should appoint to people in your group.

Three major roles

- Leader: Directs the flow of discussion and probes for deeper exploration
- Timekeeper: Ensures discussions are focused and relevant, without wasted time
- Fact-checker: Asks challenging questions and forces people to justify or prove their answers or statements.

Expand the accordions below for a detailed breakdown of each role.

### Leader

Leaders steer and manage the group’s attention. Their role is to ensure the discussions are aligned with the agenda and learning objectives. Leaders encourage active participation and look for more perspectives and deeper discussions. They push people to the limits of their knowledge comfort zone and help the group face cognitive difficulty.

_Leaders are kept in balance by the time-keeper who helps prevent the discussion from taking too long and the fact-checker who ensures the accuracy of the discussion is high._

### Timekeeper

Timekeepers keep up the pace and pressure. They monitor whether the group is running over time for a particular topic, which may risk being unable to cover other points of equal or greater importance. 

Timekeepers also keep track of the main points during discussion and summarise what has been discussed so far if the group gets stuck and needs to consolidate thoughts. They should also pay attention to the energy levels and focus of the group and suggest timed breaks or increase the time pressure during teaching or discussion to bring the group’s focus up to optimum.

### Fact-checker

Fact-checkers bring challenge, inquiry and accuracy to the group. They are responsible for paying close attention to what other members are saying and finding loopholes, inaccuracies or probing and challenging questions that they may not have thought of before. 

They should ask questions such as “Why is that important?” or “Why does that happen?” or "So what?". Fact-checkers must actively look for perspectives that have been missed and connections that have not been made. They should have Google, resources, and an AI at the ready to constantly look up the validity of statements and see if challenging questions make sense to ask the group.

_The fact-checker role is one of the most difficult but also one of the most important. This role is normally given to the person with the greatest expertise and higher-order thinking skills. However, in all situations, roles should be swapped around between members so each person can have the opportunity to fill each role._


If there are more than three people in the group, increase the number of fact-checkers. If only two people are in the group, remove the leader role.

Switch roles regularly. You may wish to switch roles after two or three hours, or after each session.

## Tip

If someone is disengaged from learning and prevents efficient progress, remove them from the group.
