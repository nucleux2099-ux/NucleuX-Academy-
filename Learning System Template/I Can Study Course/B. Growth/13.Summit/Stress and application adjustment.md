# Stress and application adjustment
## Exam execution

Two major factors influence our ability to perform under pressure.

### 1. Psychological state

Stress and pressure can affect your cognitive performance and your ability to retrieve knowledge, especially at higher orders. Some people refer to getting "tunnel vision" when they are under pressure where they become blind to connections they could normally easily form while calm.

On the night before and the day of a high-pressure assessment, the main focus should be on keeping calm, relaxed, and confident. Last-minute revision and outside-the-room cramming are likely to detriment your performance more due to the negative impact on stress while providing a very low chance of covering material that makes a meaningful difference to your result.

#### Tip

Focus training, mindfulness meditation, and Wim Hof breathing cycles can all help to create alertness and calm before a high-stakes assessment.

### 2. Application adjustment

Application adjustment is about practising your ability to use knowledge in many different ways in preparation for assessments. The methods introduced in the [interleaving table](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/interleaving-table) will protect you against most question styles to a high degree. However, in the later stages of learning, the objective may be to find those extra few marks that are much less predictable.

**Finding gaps in the late stages of learning is difficult because:**

1. There are generally very few gaps at this stage.
2. There are different types of gaps: _known_ and _unknown_. Known gaps are detected when we have difficulties in recall or knowledge application. Unknown gaps are things we never knew in the first place. With unknown gaps, we might feel confident in our ability to answer, based on what we know but have missed an entire perspective that we never considered.

#### Breaching questions

**Breaching questions** is a method that can be helpful for efficiently identifying both known and unknown knowledge gaps, even if they are rare. They are called _breaching_ questions because they are designed to "breach" through plateaus in learning. Breaching questions is an advanced method because it requires a high level of cognitive load tolerance and the ability to do a significant level of non-linear mapping mentally.

##### Instructions

1. Find the learning objectives (LO) of the subject and list them out.
2. For each LO, list all the keywords you can think of that are related to this.
3. As you list out each keyword, mentally map out how they are all related to each other.
4. When you do this, pay attention to any difficulties in mentally mapping. This may indicate a known knowledge gap.
5. After completing this for each LO of the topic, look through the keywords and see if there are any you have missed. A keyword you know belongs in the topic but has not been listed and mentally mapped could indicate a known or unknown gap.
6. Look for keywords that exist in the list/map for one LO but not another. Explore whether these keywords could apply to the other LOs. This forces us to try and find perspectives where this keyword is relevant, helping to reveal unknown gaps.
