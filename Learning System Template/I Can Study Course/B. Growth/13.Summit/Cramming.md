# Cramming
## What is cramming?

Cramming is taking a massive block of knowledge and processing it in a short span of time (usually before an exam). **Cramming is very useful if done correctly.** 

### How most people cram

People usually cram by just grabbing a heap of content and learning how they normally would. They spend long hours compressed into just a few days or weeks. Unfortunately, cramming can be risky and stressful if using techniques with low retention or are inefficient at building higher levels of knowledge mastery.

_This might have been you in the past._ 

### Cramming the right way

An efficient cramming system should abide by the following principles.

Principles of efficient cramming

- It must allow you to skip content strategically to make the most of your available learning time.
- It must allow a retention rate and mastery depth that is sufficient for the best possible outcome.
- It must be efficient enough to not sacrifice sleep (and therefore sleep-dependent memory consolidation or cognitive performance declines due to sleep deprivation)

The system we will use for cramming is called the ‘**Multipass System**’.

This is an advanced system because it requires you to already have excellent _order control_, be an inquiry-based learner, and be able to write effective notes (using VPReFRE).

#### Cram early

One of the easiest and most helpful changes you can make to cramming is to cram _early_. Instead of leaving it at the last minute, complete the course/curriculum as early as possible. This means you can spend the remaining time with whatever else you'd like to do, stress-free and guilt-free. All you have to do is keep yourself topped up and find gaps using occasional SIR to sail through to your assessments.

**Many of our Summit-level learners can complete a typical one-year curriculum in just a couple of months of focused work.**
