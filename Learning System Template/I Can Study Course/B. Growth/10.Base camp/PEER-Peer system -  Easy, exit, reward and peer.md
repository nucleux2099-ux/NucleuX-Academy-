# PEER-Peer system -  Easy, exit, reward and peer
## Easy

We make things ‘easy’ by establishing **minimum viable goals (MVG)**, which we’ve discussed before.

### Tip

Students often make their MVGs too difficult! If procrastination is a problem, even standing up or sitting at your desk can be considered a good MVG.

## Exit

‘Exit’ means that at each MVG, you tell your brain you can quit anytime. This keeps your brain focused on the MVGs so that the final goal won’t scare you away.

## Reward

Set a reward for whenever you attain a goal. Make sure your reward requires a minimum amount of work; try to make this time as long as possible. The closer you can get to your final goal before rewarding yourself, the better. It’s up to you to decide what is ‘good enough’ for a reward.

### Tip

Do not let the reward tempt you prematurely. Consider physically locking away the reward, perhaps with the help of others. Often, people rely on willpower alone to stay away from their reward. We can see how this is prone to failure.

## Peer

**Peer** is the last step. It creates another level of accountability using someone else (e.g., family, friends, or new friends from the iCanStudy community). _We covered this when discussing the burnt ships strategy._

Once the behaviours have become habits, this step will no longer be necessary. The best thing about this system is that you will need to use it less and less as the behaviours all become fully automatic.
