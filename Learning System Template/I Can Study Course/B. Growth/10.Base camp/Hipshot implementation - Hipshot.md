# Hipshot implementation - Hipshot
## Boosting your BHS

With your high level of BHS mastery, we can start applying BHS with more complicated routines and schedules. Expand the accordions below to explore some examples of how BHS (and Hipshot) can be utilised to gain enormous time efficiency advantages.

At this stage of your technique, you should aim to adjust your schedules and routines to learn with some of the more advanced implementation strategies.

### BHS implementation variations

#### Standard BHS implementation

This standard implementation uses all the normal BHS techniques daily. It is a good starter schedule to get used to replacing normal study with BHS. 

**Characteristic features of the standard BHS implementation:**

- Aim always happens during prestudy and covers at least one week's worth of material.
- Shoot happens before (if viable), during, and after main learning events
- Skin always happens after the main learning event. 

**Time efficiency:** 5/10 

**Difficulty:** Low

**Instructions:**

1. Perform Aim step for one week’s worth of material at the end of the previous week (e.g. on Sunday)
2. Do a shoot for only the most basic and important concepts/relationships if you have time after the Aim step. If you don’t have time, do this in the morning of, or at any time before learning it in your _main learning event_.
3. Continue the Aim-Shoot cycle during the _main learning event_, finishing your Shoot step.
4. If you have time, do the Skin step at the end of the day or, alternatively, at the end of the week for the entire week’s worth. 

You can also do a Skin for each day’s material and continually add to the GRINDEmap with each new day’s content, then re-Skin the entire week again. Both methods will be effective. What’s more important is that you do what you have time for and can do consistently. Some busier weeks may require you to alternate.


#### Accelerated BHS implementation

This accelerated implementation is an intermediate schedule to get used to working independently from the slower pace and restrictions of classes and lectures.

**Characteristic features of the accelerated BHS implementation:**

- The Aim always covers an entire topic at a time.
- You are working at a different pace and order than your peers.
- Aim and Shoot always occur before main learning events.
- Skin occurs at the end of the week for an entire topic at once.

**Time efficiency:** 7/10 

**Difficulty:** Moderate

**Instructions:**

1. Spend one weekend Aiming and Shooting for around 60-70% of the content for the entire next topic you will study for a single subject. This gives you a significant lead and reduces the pressure of trying to "keep up".
2. Every weekend, do the same process for a different subject, allowing you to stay weeks ahead of the formal curriculum pace for most subjects at all times.
3. During the week, complete the Shoot for the remaining 30-40% of content before, during and after main learning events, much like how you might in the standard BHS. The goal is to finish both Aim and Shoot for the entire topic for a subject within one week of the formal timing.
4. Perform Skin at the end of the week.

Attending classes or lectures after you have already self-studied the material is useful as a recheck, re-skin and easy multi-order retrieval session. 

**Tips:**

- Use materials or resources at your disposal for self-study, including Google.
- Don’t worry about covering 100% of the curriculum – you have plenty of chances to pick up things you missed as you will be so far ahead.
- Continue a weekly + monthly interleaved retrieval (appropriate to knowledge type and order) to find gaps, maintain retention, and ensure comprehensive mastery
- Continue lower-order retrieval methods for layer 4 details throughout. This should not take much time if you have effective encoding methods.
- You should still have plenty of time available each week to focus on other subjects as needed for tests, assignments or unexpected difficulties.


#### Ultra-accelerated BHS implementation

This ultra-accelerated implementation is for students adept at using the standard and accelerated BHS with a high mastery over the core technique. It creates total academic independence from curriculum pacing, creating the most free time and efficiency.

**Characteristic features of the ultra-accelerated BHS implementation:**

- Aim, Shoot and Skin all happen before main learning events using self-study material
- The time during curriculum weeks is used for interleaved retrieval and getting further ahead or more skills development.
- Entire topics are studied in a single session as the norm.

**Time efficiency:** 10/10 

**Difficulty:** High

**Instructions:**

The instructions are brief because it relies on simply cramming through as much as possible, as fast as possible, without sacrificing the quality of learning or retention. The objective is to be so far ahead of formal curriculum pacing that most pressures and challenges are eradicated.

This combination is essentially impossible for most learners who have not had substantial cognitive training.

1. On weekends, breaks or other times without classes or lectures, Aim, Shoot and Skin for the entire subject's topic. 
2. Continue to do this for your subjects every weekend, aiming to be at first a few weeks ahead for one subject, then a few weeks ahead for every subject by the next week, then several weeks ahead for all subjects by the week after. 
3. Continue with spaced interleaved retrieval and prioritise gap-finding and perfecting your knowledge mastery for the remaining time.

**Tips:**

- Because your speed of self-study should be much higher than the curriculum pace, you should be able to complete the entire next topic for a subject in approximately one day or even less.
- Don’t worry about getting 100% coverage. Instead, aim for 90% coverage to 90% retention. You have plenty of time to cover the gaps later. 
- Attending classes or lectures after you have already self-studied the material is useful as a recheck and easy multi-order retrieval session.
