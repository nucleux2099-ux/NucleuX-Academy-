# Fixed mindset vs. growth mindset
## Nurturing growth

Those with a fixed mindset see their ability level and intelligence as fixed and unchangeable. To overcome a fixed mindset, we must:

1. **Take action** - one of the easiest ways to change our thoughts, feelings, and beliefs, is through action. it is a misconception that we have to _feel_ like taking action first. We can take action despite not feeling motivated to.
2. **Reflect effectively** - use Kolb's to turn mistakes into learning.
3. **Focus on the chain performance** (see below)

### Chain of performance

One useful model to help with your marginal gains is to identify what determines a desirable outcome. Simply wanting an outcome does not create it. By analysing the chain of performance, we can understand which aspects hold us back the most. Our efforts can become more targeted and effective.

#### What determines outcomes?

1. Outcomes and performance (which come from...)
2. Skills and processes (which come from...)
3. Attributes and behaviours (which come from...)
4. Mindsets, beliefs, and identities.

##### Examples of skills and processes

- Encoding and retrieval skills
- Time management and prioritisation skills
- Self-reflection and problem-solving skills
- Focus and attention management skills
- Emotional self-regulation skills

You can see how deficiencies in any of these skills could cause significant problems with outcomes and performance.
##### Examples of attributes and behaviours

- Open-mindedness
- Willingness to experiment
- Willingness to face discomfort
- Diligence and commitment
- Patience
- Humility
- Self-compassion

When these attributes (or the behaviours naturally associated with these attributes) are absent, it can cause significant challenges to developing skills. For example, imagine trying to develop the skills in this program without patience or open-mindedness!

##### Examples of mindsets, beliefs, and identities

- Fear of failure
- Beliefs about intelligence and abilities
- Beliefs about talent and capabilities
- Identity as a learner
- Identity as someone who tries their best
- Identity as someone who always gets back up

Mindsets, beliefs, and identities influence our attributes and behaviours.
