# Neuroticism -  Measurement
## Measuring the process

When planning the process, you need to include metrics. When will you see development, and how much? 

With metrics, you’ll know you are doing things correctly because the numbers will show if you are achieving the right processes, learning the right skills and keeping a productive mindset.

### Feedback Analysis Loop

The **Feedback Analysis Loop (FAL)** compares your expectations with what happened in reality. 

- If your expectations _match_ reality, you know there’s no problem with your processes. 
- If your expectations _don’t match_ reality, you need to adjust your processes as they are unlikely to help you learn the skills and reach your goals.

The FAL is another framework we can apply to Kolb’s experiential learning cycle. Using FALs gives us concrete, manageable information, making reflecting and finding abstractions easier.

#### Note

FALs can be modified for any need (e.g., studying, sports, personal development). You can add extra steps or criteria depending on what you want to measure.

The only components that need to stay are:

- expectations,
- reality, and 
- a comparison between the two. 

If your FAL has these three elements, you can modify it however suits you!
