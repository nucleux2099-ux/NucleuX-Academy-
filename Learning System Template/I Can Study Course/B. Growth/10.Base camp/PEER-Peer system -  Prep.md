## PEER peer system – prep

# P for Prep

We use the anatomy of a habit to create a robust system for conquering neuroticism: the **PEER-Peer system**. This involves planting _good_ cues that help you study and removing _bad_ cues that make you procrastinate. Make sure the bad cues are removed to the point that you would be too lazy to procrastinate!

# If at first you don't succeed...

According to _nudge theory_ and the broader field of behavioural science, changing your environmental cues is one of the most powerful influencers of behaviour. If your first cue optimisation is unsuccessful at creating the desired behaviour, optimise and optimise again!

**It is rare for cue optimisation to be ineffective when repeatedly optimised.**

## Nudge theory: Environment is more important than willpower

Lots of research shows that the environment influences our behaviour more than willpower. This is called **nudge theory** – a branch of behavioural analysis science that explores how modifying our environment nudges us to engage in good or bad default behaviours**.**


Successful, productive people use [nudge theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nudge_theory), while people who constantly burn out or rely on motivation only use willpower.

Read [this article](https://jamesclear.com/design-default) by James Clear, the author of Atomic Habits, on how we can optimise our environment for decision-making.
