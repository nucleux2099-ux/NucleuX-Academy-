# Priority 0+1 system
## Using Priority 0+1

### Instructions

1. Look at your goals and identify your top priority. You can use reverse goal setting principles to identify this.
2. Block out time to accomplish your top priority.
3. Fill the rest of your timetable according to your lower priorities.
4. In the case of unforeseen events, shuffle lower-priority items to protect the highest priority tasks.

## Eisenhower Matrix

To determine your priorities, use the [Eisenhower Matrix](https://www.eisenhower.me/eisenhower-matrix/), which categorises tasks by importance and urgency.

![[Image 56.jpg|Modified Eisenhower matrix with instructions]]

Modified Eisenhower matrix showing priorities and instructions for each quadrant.

We recommend prioritising and scheduling your tasks **the day before**. On the day, keep a list of tasks that pop up and throw them into the Eisenhower matrix to prioritise and schedule for the next day.

Key principles for prioritisation

- Decide if tasks are important or not based on the consequence of failure; not an intuitive feeling of importance which is often based on urgency.
- Tasks with a low consequence of failure (that are acceptable and manageable) are not important, even if it "feels" important.
- Avoid scheduling urgent tasks first as this can lead to urgency trapping where time is constantly spent on urgent tasks and your future self is never more equipped to handle them or remove workload at the root cause.

## Priority 0

Priority 0 refers to your **values and goals**. We recommend stepping back and reassessing these **once every 6 months**. Regularly reassessing your Priority 0 is important because it ensures that your Priority 1 tasks are aligned with your goals. It doesn’t take long and will save you a lot of time by keeping you on the right track.

## Common questions

### What are some examples of urgency trapping?

A common example of urgency trapping among students is filling all their time with urgent homework and never having time to work on study skills. If academic challenges increase faster than your skills, the gap between skills and challenges widens. This means students falling into this trap can eventually fail to reach academic goals as by the time it's felt as urgent, it is already too late.

This doesn’t mean you must schedule urgent tasks later in the day. It simply means you should schedule important and non-urgent tasks into your day _first_ and fit in the urgent tasks around them.

**For example,** you could schedule 20 minutes every evening for meditation, which is important but non-urgent. You can then fill the afternoon with other tasks. This way, your urgent tasks will not preclude your important task (meditation).

### How do you identify Priority 0?

Priority 0 comes to core values and long-term vision. Values are your fundamental beliefs that dictate how you behave and act. Long-term visions are open and abstract ideas of the type of person we want to be and the type of life we want to live.

### What is the difference between a long-term vision and a long-term goal?

Your long-term vision is _not_ a SMART goal. Setting goals that are too long-term can create tunnel vision. Over time, our personality and priorities are guaranteed to change. While some things remain with you for your entire life, it’s naïve to believe that we know enough about ourselves now to confidently think that it will not change for the next 10 years. 

This is especially true before the age of 30 in modern society when you are transitioning into adulthood and experiencing many things for the first time. These new experiences are some of the most transformative moments that shape who you will be for the rest of your life. Instead of setting “goals” that are very specific, we can instead set visions. 

Visions are a more open and abstract idea of the type of person we want to be and the type of life we want to live. This ensures that we have a broad checklist that we can use to calibrate our medium-term goals, making sure we are always heading in the right direction but not being so narrow that we close off other opportunities.

**For example:**

A long-term vision might be to have financial freedom, working at an intellectually stimulating job but with enough free time to explore multiple hobbies and interests. We want to be the type of person who is patient, calm, confident and capable of adapting and overcoming challenges enjoyably and easily. 

This means that medium-term goals that do not take us closer to this long-term vision are unlikely to be good decisions. For example, getting tutoring to help us get better grades does not give us the skills to overcome challenges independently, nor does it increase our time management to give us the skills to have enough free time for hobbies and interests.

### How often does Priority 0 change?

**Always evaluate your long-term vision open-mindedly. It can be tempting and comfortable to believe that our long-term vision never changes, but this is unlikely.** 

We are a living, dynamic organism that constantly takes in information about the world and ourselves. We are always learning more about our thoughts, emotions, values, and experiences, shaping our personality and priorities. It is normal for your long-term vision to change, often in unexpected and sometimes radically different ways. Instead of going into this exercise assuming that your thoughts are unchanged, deliberately look for reasons, thoughts or emotions that indicate that what you want long-term _has_ changed. 

This helps us overcome the uncontrollable biases that can blind us from seeing our thoughts objectively. You can read more about cognitive biases [here](https://www.verywellmind.com/what-is-acognitive-bias-2794963). You can evaluate your own cognitive biases [here](https://yourbias.is/).

**We recommend reviewing your Priority 0 every 6 months.**

### How often does Priority 1 change?

This priority 1 step should be repeated once every day or week, depending on how variable your schedule is. Busier people with more things going on in their lives will need to do this daily.

### How can I be confident to delete tasks?

If the task is truly important, it will return to your list. Unless you have ample free time and a willingness to spend it on these tasks instead of relaxation, sleep, or more non-urgent, important tasks, it is not advisable to spend time on them.
