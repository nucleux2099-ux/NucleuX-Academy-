# PEER-Peer summary
## Summary checklist

Expand the accordions below to see our checklist to ensure you are completing the PEER-Peer system effectively.

### Prep

1. Identify cues that trigger unwanted behaviours such as procrastination. Aim to identify at least three or more.
2. Remove and lock away these cues however possible. Consider using site-blocking apps like SiteBlock. We want these “bad cues” to become INVISIBLE in our daily lives.
3. Identify cues that trigger desirable behaviours. Aim to identify at least three or more. 
4. Plant these cues so that they are **unavoidable** in your daily life. We want these cues to almost intrude into our vision or path. Like a boulder rolling downhill to roll to the bottom inevitably, our environment should be crafted so that we _inevitably_ roll towards our desired behaviour or action.

### Easy

1. Write your final desired goal (for example, studying for 3 hours). 
2. Write down all the steps required to get there. Make the first few steps as easy as possible to make it ridiculously easy to transition into your behaviour.

Conversely, we want to make our undesirable behaviour close to impossible. **Try to write down the steps required to start your undesirable behaviour to see how easy it might be!**

**For example**, procrastinating on social media might be as simple as taking out your phone and opening the app or tapping the button if you’re already on your phone. We can see how much more willpower it takes to do one type of behaviour (beneficial or productive) than another (harmful or time-wasting). This is partly because companies invest millions to make it as easy for us to get distracted as possible! They have crafted environments for us so we must take back control.


### Exit

1. Remind yourself that you can quit after every MVG. The only decision you need to make is whether you do the next MVG or quit.

### Reward

1. Identify two or three actions, behaviours, or activities you enjoy. Choose things that you can access and do readily. Consider how addictive your reward will be and if that affects your next scheduled task or event. 
    - For example, if you have no other focus blocks planned after your reward break, it doesn't matter so much. Remember that a sleep and wind-down routine is also a scheduled task! 
2. Make this reward inaccessible without doing the desired behaviour. 
    - For example, blocking Netflix outside of specific hours of the day or using an accountability partner (e.g. a parent) to show that you’ve completed something before getting them to unlock apps or websites.
### Peer

1. Find one or two accountability partners. Find a partner in the iCanStudy community if you need people on the same wavelength as you!
2. Establish and agree to consequences. 
3. Ensure the consequences cause you to feel at least a moderate sense of dread and fear. If the consequence isn’t significant, your laziness or procrastination will win.
4. Write down your agreed consequence to make it tangible and real. This symbolises that you are taking it seriously.

## Tips

Find the balance. If the consequence is too extreme, you will likely lie to avoid the punishment. Find a consequence that is significant and undesirable yet not so extreme that you would avoid it at all costs. For example, giving someone $50 might be appropriate, while giving someone $500 is too extreme. Doing a dare in front of your class might be appropriate while doing a dare in front of your entire school during assembly might be too extreme. 

_Be responsible and do not act rashly._
## Tips

- You can use incognito mode to stop getting personalised recommendations and subscriptions on YouTube that make it too easy for you to get side-tracked. 
- You can also block specific words from appearing in the URL instead of blocking entire websites. For example, if you need YouTube for studying, use incognito to prevent easy access to your recommended content and subscriptions. 

This forces you to search for videos manually. When you search for something on Youtube, the search term appears in the website URL; therefore, if you block those search words in your site blocker, your site-blocking app will allow you to search for “good” content while blocking the site every time the distracting search term pops up in the URL!

**You can also enable these in the parental control options in most router settings.**
