# OFF-rest timing
## Limitations of the Pomodoro technique

The Pomodoro technique is widely known and adopted but has disadvantages. After all, it did originate from a literal tomato cooking timer, not research!

![[03 Spaces/Learning System/attachments/Image 30.png|A timer the shape of a tomato]]

The origin of the Pomodoro technique!

### Unoptimised focus time

The first disadvantage is that your brain’s ability to focus will **vary depending on the situation**. If you’re focusing well (often called being in _flow_), you don’t want to cut off precious focus time prematurely, nor do you want to extend unfocused time if you can’t stay focused for the full 25 minutes.

### Unoptimised rest time

The second disadvantage is that it doesn’t explain how to use the “rest” time. Having only five minutes to rest may be insufficient.

## OFF-rest timing

Many people do not find the traditional Pomodoro technique sustainable and effective in the long term. As a result, most experienced and productive people do not use it at all. Instead, it is better to personalise the Pomodoro technique and optimise it for yourself. One variant that is commonly recommended instead is the [Flowmodoro technique](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-dkYI2OECZo), which more closely resembles our guidelines and variation.

Ultimately, the important thing is that your method works for you. In our experience, OFF-rest timing is the most flexible and adaptable variant we have encountered, with compatibility in diverse settings.

OFF-rest timing is short for **Optimised-For-Focus rest timing**. It aims to keep your efficiency and focus high for as many cycles as possible throughout the day.

### Using OFF-Rest timing

**Use OFF-rest timing for extended work periods where you will have multiple cycles of work and rest.**

The type of rest you take depends on the circumstances, but you still want to be as productive as possible during your breaks. 

#### Analogy

Imagine moving a heavy boulder. The hardest part is getting it rolling in the first place. It takes very little energy and effort to keep it moving if you keep pushing it a little bit whenever it slows down. But if you wait until the boulder has completely stopped before pushing again, you must spend much more energy to get it back in motion.

#### Determining the type of break

1. If you are relatively fresh, take a topical or miscellaneous break.
2. If you are low on energy, take an active relaxation break (we suggest doing focus training or mindful meditation).
3. Regardless, use a timer to measure how long you stay focused, and ensure that your rest time is one-third of your focused time.

#### Not all rest is equal

You should rest _as soon as_ your brain is starting to become dull. This way, you can refocus and feel restored much more quickly. If we wait until we are noticeably tired before resting, refocusing and restoring our energy will take much longer, making us less productive overall.

**You may also use the Wim-Hof breathing method during breaks to rapidly re-energise if you have less than 10 minutes. A tutorial on this helpful technique is** [**here**](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tybOi4hjZFQ)**.**
