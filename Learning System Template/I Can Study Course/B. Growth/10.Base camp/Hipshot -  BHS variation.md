# Hipshot -  BHS variation
## Building on mastery

Now that we have close to unconscious competence of the traditional Bear Hunter System with Aim-Shoot cycling, we will build upon it with an advanced variation we call **Hipshot**.

### Hipshot

The Hipshot variation will switch quickly between Aim and Shoot. It is essentially a faster method of Aim-Shoot cycling that exploits the high cognitive load tolerance you have trained and your ability to form relational networks rapidly.

#### Instructions

1. Collect keywords as normal
2. Perform the Aim step mentally to identify a hypothetical logic layer.
3. Immediately Shoot on your mental model of the logic layer to validate or correct it.
4. As you gain clarity about the validity and intuitiveness of the logic layer, commit it to a GRINDE map.
5. Repeat this process from step 2 until you work through each of your layers.
6. Perform the Skin step once you are satisfied with your initial map and feel ready for refinement.

**Correct usage of Hipshot allows you to save time by:**

- Drastically reducing the time for Aim
- Reducing the number of iterations before arriving at a map to Skin

While Hipshot increases speed and efficiency, it is vital that quality and accuracy are not impacted. Hipshot is only possible with close to unconscious competence with the traditional Aim-Shoot cycle. If you attempt Hipshot and struggle, please return to the traditional BHS.

#### Note

Like Aim-Shoot cycling itself, Hipshot often occurs as a natural byproduct of unconscious competence developing with Aim and Shoot individually.
