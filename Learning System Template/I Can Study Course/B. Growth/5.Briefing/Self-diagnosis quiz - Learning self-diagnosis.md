# Self-diagnosis quiz - Learning self-diagnosis

## Question 1

### Answer

Regarding effective learning, select the most correct statement:

**A learning system depends on both efficient encoding and adequate retrieval.**

**Methods for learning (either during encoding or retrieval) can be considered effective when they promote certain types of difficulty and effort.**

**Processing information efficiently while learning involves thinking about how new information fits into the big picture.**


---

### Learning notes

Effective learning methods help to build knowledge by finding relationships and constructing a big picture understanding. While thinking of relationships are crucial, it is what we do with these relationships that matters. 

This process of comparing, grouping, simplifying, and trying to judge which relationships are important and how they relate to each other creates cognitive load. When cognitive load is caused by these processes, it is desirable as it leads to efficient learning.



## Question 2
### Answer

Regarding SIR, select the most correct statement:

**Efficient spacing means testing ourselves with retrieval frequently enough that we do not forget too much, but with enough of a gap that we are challenging ourselves.**

**Retrieval practice is ideally performed through a combination of natural work applications and dedicated learning sessions.**

**Different types of knowledge and topics require different interleaving methods.**


---

### Learning notes

Effective and efficient use of SIR means our spacing intervals challenge us, while helping us stay on top of our knowledge decay. While it may be technically more beneficial to challenge ourselves every day, this is not a practically efficient use of time.

Hopefully, we are able to apply our knowledge through our daily work. However, for many students, this is not possible. While work applications in real-life settings provide context, relevance, and direction for consolidating knowledge, dedicated sessions allow for targeted review of retrieval types you are weaker on.

Knowledge can be broadly divided into different orders of learning and different types of knowledge. Unlike encoding methods which tend to transfer fairly universally for different knowledge types (i.e. you can use a few efficient encoding methods to build good memory and depth of understanding for most topics), interleaving methods tend to be much more focused. Therefore, a good interleaving system involves creating the right combination of methods for each topic that address the type of knowledge and orders of mastery you need.

We recommend reviewing the [interleaving table](https://icanstudy.com/learning-support/interleaving-table) for more information.


## Question 3

### Answer

Regarding skill improvement, select the most correct statement:

**The fastest and safest way to improve skills is to make frequent mistakes, early on with frequent critical reflection.**

**Kolb's leads to positive marginal gains when experiments are reflected on in new cycles. Without this, marginal gains may not positively compound.**

**During conscious competence of skill development, it may not be possible to determine if we are making progress with just a few attempts.**


---

### Learning notes

Mistakes are crucial for skill development, however we must pair it with good reflective technique to turn mistakes into valuable learning. Mistakes without reflection do not provide learning. Similarly, experiments that are not reflected on do not lead to compounding improvements. Experimenting without reflection can cause fluctuation in skill, rather than consistent improvement.

During conscious competence, one of the first signs of progress is that consistency improves, followed by a reduction in time taken and effort required. However, consistency and accuracy can only be measured across multiple attempts. When there are not enough attempts, it is not possible to get a sense of accuracy. This is another reason why practice is important.
