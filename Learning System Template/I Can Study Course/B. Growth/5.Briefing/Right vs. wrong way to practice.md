# Right vs. wrong way to practice
## Practice does _not_ always make perfect.

In fact, practice can even be harmful! Practice only makes perfect if you are practising _correctly_. 

Many people practise the wrong way. This causes slower rates of skill development and improvement, wasted time and effort, and unnecessary mistakes. While mistakes are crucial for learning, we must have the _skills_ to learn from them. Learning from mistakes is a success. Those who struggle to learn from mistakes also struggle to prevent the same mistakes from occurring repeatedly.

![[Image 10.jpg|Practice can be harmful if they lead to mistakes with serious consequences]]

If we don't learn from our mistakes, practice is meaningless.

### So, how do we practise the right way?

[Kolb’s Experiential Learning Cycle](https://www.simplypsychology.org/learning-kolb.html) is a useful tool for making practice more productive. The stages are as follows:

1. Experience
2. Reflection
3. Abstraction
4. Experimentation

#### Important note

What we teach is a modification of Kolb's experiential learning cycle. Please avoid learning this technique from other sources, as the way we teach Kolb’s is different to how it is typically presented. Our modifications arise from years of experience teaching this framework and observing the subtle practical limitations of the original cycle.

When we learn how to reflect efficiently, we create a safety net for our mistakes. Instead of leading to undesirable consequences, they are transformed into valuable and exciting learning opportunities!

![[Image 11.jpg|Reflection is what turns practice into progress]]

Kolb's allows mistakes to lead to learning instead of undesirable consequences.

##### References

Learning and adapting from mistakes is about knowing what you are doing (monitoring) and changing your methods. The following research shows that people are generally very bad at making meaningful adjustments to their methods, either due to a lack of self-awareness or a lack of ability to make useful changes.
