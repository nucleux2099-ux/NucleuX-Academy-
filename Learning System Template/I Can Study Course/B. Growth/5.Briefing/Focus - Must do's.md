# Focus - Must do's
## Elements of focus

To focus is to concentrate deeply on a task and shut out distractions. It enables you to achieve more within a given time than someone who lacks focus. Here are some points to help you create and maintain focus:

1. **Sleep:** You must protect your sleep. In general, aim for at least 8 to 9 hours of sleep. Sleep has dramatic carry-over effects on focus, energy, mood, and [sleep-dependent memory consolidation](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fnhum.2018.00018/full) (a critical learning phenomenon).
2. **Remove distractions:** Before you start a focused work session, spend time removing all possible distractions. Remember to use your distraction cheat sheet to note down everything that distracts you, and make sure to remove all of those things at the beginning of your next study session.
3. **Breaks:** Have breaks more often, but keep them short (i.e. high frequency, low duration). This is because our body recovers faster when it is less exhausted. Frequent, shorter breaks allow for longer overall focus throughout the day – so it’s better to have a 20 to 30-minute study session and a 5-minute break than to study for 4 hours and have a 1-hour break. To keep the length of your break under control, use a timer set to 5, 10 or 15-minute intervals.
