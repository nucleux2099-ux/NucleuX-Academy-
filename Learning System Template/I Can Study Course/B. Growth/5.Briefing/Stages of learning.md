# Stages of learning
Key principles

- Marginal gains is a way of thinking about progress that helps us create the optimal path for success.
- Kolb’s helps us do the practice in a way that helps marginal gains compound positively so that we are always moving in the right direction.
- Marginal gains depend on the stage of competence. Most gains involve exploring the types of mistakes before even the first success!

## The progression of skill

As our skills develop, we follow the [Hierarchy of Competence](https://dev.to/kathryngrayson/understanding-the-hierarchy-of-competence-8mb#:~:text=The%20Hierarchy%20of%20Competence%20is,we%20learn%20a%20new%20skill.).

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Unconscious incompetence|We have no knowledge of a new skill and are unaware that we are bad at it.|
|Conscious incompetence|We perform and review the skill incorrectly; however, we are aware that we are not good at it.|
|Conscious competence|We can correctly analyse and apply the skill, but it requires active effort.|
|Unconscious competence|We can perform the skills intuitively with minimal thought and effort.|

### Kolb's meets marginal gains

**Kolb's** is the engine of improvement. Using Kolb's helps us learn from each experience so that we improve and learn from each mistake. Being good at Kolb's gives you **confidence** to experiment because you know you can "succeed" no matter what, either by doing the skill correctly or by gaining valuable learning from the attempt.

**Marginal gains** is the road we travel. It's a perspective on development and progress. It helps us to reframe progress in a way that is safer, more effective, and more motivating. However, it does not tell us **how** to gain the 1% improvement. Kolb's fills this role.
