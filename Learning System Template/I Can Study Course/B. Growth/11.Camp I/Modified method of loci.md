# Modified method of loci
## About the modified method of loci

The **modified method of loci** builds upon the original method of loci (more commonly known as the Memory Palace) to help you remember large strings of arbitrary information.

### Instructions

1. Create a story or "scene" with different elements for each piece of information.
2. Make elements absurd. Mundane and boring elements are not memorable.
3. Keep elements spatially distinct. Don't have too many things happening in the same "location".
4. Make elements interdependent. Create relationships between elements to help with retention.
5. Draw out your "scene" in your mindmap canvas to serve as a memory anchor. This replaces the normal "visualisations" you have been using.

Your drawing doesn’t have to look nice; it just needs to consolidate the information in your brain. Even students with zero artistic ability can successfully use this technique.

Usage notes for this technique

- As a lower-order memorisation method, it is not effective or efficient for information that has causal/functional/conceptual relationships with other pieces of information.
- This method excels with lists of information (ordered or unordered).
- It can be used for small lists of just a few items, or longer lists of 30+.
- This method should not replace higher-order encoding approaches if a higher-order relational network is viable.
