# Exam technique
## Exam execution guidelines

Follow these guidelines to ensure consistent performance during assessments.

1. Get good sleep (especially before exams). Sleep is essential for exam execution due to sleep-dependent memory consolidation. The recommended amount is at least 9 hours in bed, per night!
2. Use priority-checking to double-check answers you are unsure about while maintaining an optimum pace for other questions.
3. Think like an examiner to evaluate your knowledge and consider how you can use it to solve challenging problems, especially in ways you are not confident about. View the interleaving table for methods.
4. Use slow, deep breathing to activate the parasympathetic response, mitigate anxiety spirals and eventually train yourself out from anxiety pathways that may have become a habit.

# Common questions
## I only get nervous in high-pressure exams. What should I do?

If you’re already doing most of these things, you can use **graduated exposure therapy**. This is when you put yourself in increasingly similar scenarios to what triggers your nervousness. For exams, that means practising answering questions in more pressured settings before your actual exam. This can be done by adding strict time pressures, using exam conditions, and involving friends to increase competition.

## I'm too busy and stressed before exams to remember these tips. What should I do?

Make a pre-exam checklist and mark on your schedule when to start going through the checklist. This could be weeks before your exam. We all forget things when we're under pressure. The trick is to use the methods of self-management we have learned so you create safety nets for your brain.
