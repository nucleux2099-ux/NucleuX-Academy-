# In-class technique

## In-class technique

# In-class guidelines

Maximise learning and reduce overwhelm from classes, lectures and workshops by following these guidelines.

1. Always prestudy. Prestudy improves your retention, engagement and enjoyment in class.
2. Focus your prestudy on the big-picture concepts, not details.
3. Always paraphrase and process information before writing notes.
4. Don't avoid feeling challenged. Good learning feels uncomfortable because your brain is actively working.
5. Write non-linear notes. Mindmaps and spatial representations are much more effective than linear notes.
6. Limit your word-count. The more you write, the less you're processing. Learning happens in the brain.

# Additional learning

Check out our article on [how to prestudy](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/prestudy-guide) for more guidance on this critical skill.

# Alert: This is a high-yield lesson

In our experience, learners see dramatic improvements in learning quality and overall learning experience by applying the techniques from this lesson. Likewise, we commonly find significant issues for learners who do not practise or apply the methods from this lesson sufficiently.

# Common questions

## I don't have time to do any prestudy. What should I do?

Prestudy is more valuable than reviewing previous material. This is because effective prestudy reduces how much you forget and prevents you from becoming continually overwhelmed.

**If the bucket leaks, it's more important to plug the holes than to keep filling it**

## How do I know if I am doing prestudy correctly?

Prestudying should help you feel:

- Less overwhelmed
- More able to consolidate and make sense of information from class or lectures
- Generally aware of the main ideas that will be covered
- Able to ask deeper or more interconnected questions (if asking questions is possible)

If you do not feel this, ensure your technique is correct by reading [this guide](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/prestudy-guide).

## I've never tried prestudying before. How long will it take to do it correctly?

With daily practice, learners can develop sufficient skill after around 1 to 2 weeks. However, this range is only approximate. Each person has their own pace, and you should focus on using your own perception of skill to guide your progress.

Read our article on [how to know when to move on](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/lesson-progress-speed) for more guidance.

## I struggle to think and process before writing notes. What should I do?

This can indicate a weakness in deep processing. Don't worry. It will get easier with time! You can train this by delaying your note-taking for as long as possible (until you feel like you will forget). Over time, try to extend how long you can delay note-taking.

**Tip:** You will find it easier to delay note-taking when you constantly relate information to the big picture and try to simplify and group new information together.
