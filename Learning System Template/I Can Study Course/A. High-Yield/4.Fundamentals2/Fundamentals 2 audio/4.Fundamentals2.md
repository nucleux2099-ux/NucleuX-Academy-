# 🧠 **Fundamentals 2**

## 🚀 This stage equips you with high-yield techniques for mastering learning systems that most people take a lifetime to develop.

---

- Perfect for urgent learning needs (e.g., exams).
- Mastery of these techniques is sufficient for **top-level achievement**.

# 🏔️ **Exam Technique**

## 📜 **Guidelines**
- Prioritise **good sleep** (9 hours per night).  
- Use **priority-checking** to review uncertain answers.  
- **Think like an examiner** to anticipate curveball questions.  
- Practise **slow, deep breathing** to manage anxiety.

---

# 🏫 **In-Class Technique**

## 🔑 **Key Guidelines**
1. **Prestudy**: Focus on big-picture concepts before class.
2. **Paraphrase**: Process and summarise before note-taking.
3. **Non-linear notes**: Use mindmaps or visual structures.
4. **Embrace discomfort**: Learning should challenge you.

> 💡 **High-Yield Tip**  
> Effective prestudy reduces forgetting and prevents overwhelm.  
> _"Plug the holes before refilling the bucket."_

---

# 📝 **Note-Taking**

## 🧠 **Key Guidelines**
- Manipulate knowledge in a **different format** (e.g., visuals, spatial diagrams).  
- **Limit word-count**: Use keywords, symbols, and mindmaps.  
- Avoid **over-visualisation**: Use images sparingly for key concepts.

### 🖼️ **Tips for Visualisations**
- Use images to create **memory anchors**.  
- Add **keywords** under images to clarify meaning.  
- Avoid **hieroglyphics**: Repeatedly replacing words with simple images is ineffective.

---

# 📋 **Notes and Processing**

## 🛠️ **Technique Overview**
1. **Collecting**: Write concise notes with keywords and symbols.  
2. **Processing**: Simplify and group concepts, creating a web of relationships.

### 🧩 **Key Questions**
- How can I group this information?  
- What does it relate to?  
- How does it fit into the big picture?

> 💡 **Key Principle**  
> The **evaluate level** of Bloom’s taxonomy improves retention and deep learning.

---

# 🕒 **Study Scheduling**

## 📆 **Guidelines**
- Follow a **1 day, 1 week, 1 month** revision schedule.  
- Prioritise **prestudy** over review.  
- Protect time for core encoding and retrieval sessions.

### 🗓️ **Example Schedule**
| **Day/Time**        | **Activity**                  | **Focus**                                          |
|----------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Sunday anytime       | Prestudy                     | Big-picture concepts, relationships (no details). |
| Weekday evenings     | Revise daily material        | Detailed understanding; no memorisation yet.      |
| End of the week      | Revise weekly material       | Use interleaved retrieval and start memorisation. |
| Pockets of time      | Flashcards                   | Microlearning on-the-go.                          |
| End of the month     | Monthly review               | Consolidate with interleaved retrieval.           |
| 2–3 weeks pre-exam   | Intensive retrieval practice | Practice papers, peer quizzing, targeted review.  |

---

# 🔄 **Revision**

## 🧩 **Test, Target, Teach**
1. **Test**: Create and answer self-made and premade questions.
2. **Target**: Focus on weak areas first.
3. **Teach**: Explain concepts to others, starting with lay terms.

---

# 🔢 **Rote-Learning**

## 📜 **Guidelines**
- Combine visualisation with **spaced repetition** (e.g., flashcards).  
- Use **active recall** instead of recognition.  
- Avoid batch learning; practise throughout the day.  

> 💡 **Remember**  
> Rote-learning is necessary for **10–30% of material**, depending on the level.

---

# ✅ **Fundamentals Checkpoint**

## 📋 **Checklist**
- Experimented with changing methods and learned from mistakes.  
- Improved tracking of **marginal gains**.  
- Experienced managing discomfort while learning.  
- Using **non-linear notes** with relationships and groups.  
- Established a **spacing schedule** and applied interleaving.  

## 🧠 **Core Techniques to Practise**
- Non-linear note-taking.  
- Delayed note-taking.  
- Grouping and categorising.

---
