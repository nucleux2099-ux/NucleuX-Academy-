## Practice vs. theory time

We previously introduced the idea of the **theory-practice cycle**.

![[Image 8.jpg|What it really takes to learn new skills]]

The theory-practice cycle occurs when theory is learned in relation to relevant experiences.

We'll now explore this in more depth so that you can adjust your plan to ensure the maximum chance of success in learning new methods. As you can see in the diagram below, most of your learning will happen offline, as you practise theory that you learn online via tasks and techniques. 

![[Image 9.jpg|The theory practice cycle shows that most time is spent offline, and it replaces your current methods with new ones.]]

A simplified diagram of the theory-practice cycle.

Importantly, most of your "practice time" will be time you are **already spending** learning. By replacing our current methods with new ones, we can fit lots of practice without spending much "extra" time.

|Correct way to practise|Incorrect way to practise|
|---|---|
|Study similar hours as before, but **replace** current techniques with new ones learned from this program.|Study similar hours as before using current techniques, then spend **extra time** to practise new techniques.|

### Optimising your theory-practice balance

Depending on how much time you commit to learning and studying, you will need to adjust the amount of time you have for theory.

#### Key principle

**To prevent overload, never exceed a 5:1 ratio of practice to theory.**

For example, if you spend 25 hours studying per week, then you should spend at most 5 hours on learning theory. Even this may be excessive. Learners have been able to achieve remarkable progress with as high as a 40:1 ratio of practice to theory time.

#### Additional information

You may wish to read our article on [how long to spend on each lesson or stage](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/lesson-progress-speed) for further guidance.

#### Note about the growth phase

Once you enter the growth phase, your progress will slow down. This is because we are entering into a progressive sequence of cognitive retraining.

_This process cannot be rushed and requires rock-solid foundations. Our method of retraining is a world-first to maximise your memory and fundamentally change how you process information!_
