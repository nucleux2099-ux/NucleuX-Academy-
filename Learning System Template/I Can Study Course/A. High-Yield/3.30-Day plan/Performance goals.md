# Performance goals
Key principles

- Performance goals are process and attribute-based.
- Performance goals give you crucial early feedback, which provides opportunities for self-correction and adjustment.
- Consider these attributes: time management, focus and procrastination, learning efficiency, learning consistency, and stress management.
- Pick just a few of the most important attributes to develop in the short term. Avoid overloading yourself and thus failing at all of them.

## Performance goals for learning efficiency

Check out [this article](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/learning-efficiency) from the Learning Support page for more guidance on how to think about setting goals for learning efficiency.

# Beware your brain (again)

As usual, it appears our brain is trying to sabotage us again! The human brain is notoriously bad at assessing our own abilities and skills. 

Beware of thinking, **“This doesn’t apply to me”** and **“I’m different”**. Being open-minded carries no risk while helping to prevent us from making unnecessary mistakes and wasting time.

# Note

This is especially common among high achievers, who **often can safely** ignore advice aimed at the general public without consequence. 

However, this also means that those who ignore advice have the highest chance of running into issues. As a result, we have found that most learners who struggle with learning the methods we teach in this program are actually high achievers who rushed the fundamentals!
