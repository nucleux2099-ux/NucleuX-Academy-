# _Fundamentals

> [!info] Stage focus
> Build the core learning loop: Spaced-Interleaved Retrieval, mindset reframes, and marginal gains tracking. These habits carry through every advanced technique later on.

## How to work this stage

- Alternate short theory blocks with immediate SIR practice reps (teach-back, question remixing, mind maps).
- Start a marginal gains ledger so improvements stay visible (and motivating).
- Expect discomfort—mindset lessons challenge long-held beliefs. Capture reflections with Kolb cycles.

### Lesson map
- [[Spacing, interleaving and retrieval]] — anchor the SIR method and retrieval scheduling
- [[The problem with studying]] — dismantle counterproductive beliefs about effort and results
- [[Marginal gains]] — design compounding improvements instead of one-off wins
- [[Recommended tasks - Fundamentals - SIR]] — guided exercises to cement the loop
- [[Fundamentals checkpoint]] — self-audit before moving forward

### Practice checklist
- Daily SIR reps logged (aim for at least three variations per concept)
- Weekly marginal gains update with one Kolb reflection
- Fundamentals checkpoint completed with honest ratings

### Ready to move on when…
1. SIR feels automatic: you can plan spaced reviews without guidance.
2. Marginal gains tracker shows at least two recorded improvements.
3. Mindset reframes (desirable difficulty, experimentation) are noted in reflections.
