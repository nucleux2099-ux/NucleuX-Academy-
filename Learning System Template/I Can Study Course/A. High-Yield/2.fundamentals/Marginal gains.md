# Marginal gains
Key points

1. Consistency is better than sudden bursts of effort
2. Marginal gains are not only easier and more sustainable, they also produce a greater total effect
3. Marginal gains stack and compound, which is why starting imperfectly now is better than starting perfectly later and missing out on compound gains

**In our experience**, it is extremely rare for learners to think about progress in marginal gains (especially among our younger high school learners). Adopting this method of thinking is a sign of intelligence and maturity, as it indicates the person:

- Appreciates that skills take time to develop
- Understands that time spent on trying to make "large strides" instead of marginal gains is often wasted and ineffective
- Recognises that the worst case scenario is not that they develop the skill slowly, but they may fail to develop it at all. 

## Key principle

Marginal gains create safety of success. Therefore, progressing through "large strides" and "big jumps" is unwise because it sacrifices safety for a temporary (and unlikely) boost.
