# Recommended tasks Marginal gains
## Taking the first steps

Viewing progress through marginal gains is about more than understanding the concept.

It needs to become a habit. Most people do not think about progress via marginal gains "naturally". We commonly see learners who use marginal gains perfectly when setting goals but forget about them when viewing their mistakes. 

Their old thinking habits make them continue to think, "_I'm not improving even though I'm trying so hard_". Despite knowing that they _should_ think about marginal gains, they fail to catch themselves when they do not.

### Key principle

Habits operate so fast and subconsciously that we can be aware of how we must think and act but still struggle to stop ourselves from doing the same thing we are used to. **Retraining this takes time, diligence, and self-awareness.**

Therefore, it's vital that we try to retrain our **habit** of thinking about progress through the lens of marginal gains. Complete the following recommended tasks to help start this retraining process.

### Recommended tasks

Fundamentals

Identify your marginal gains for learning efficiently

Follow the five steps outlined in our "Tracking marginal gains" guide [here](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-support/tracking-marginal-gains) to identify your 1% gains for learning efficiency. Complete this task once you have compounded your 1% gains at least three times. This means you have:

1. Identified a marginal gain
2. Tried to reach it
3. Reflected on the experience
4. Identified the next marginal gain with our 5-step framework
5. Repeated this process twice

Remember that marginal gains in the early stages often involve **identifying gaps in knowledge**, **learning more** about what action to take, and **building basic self-awareness**.
