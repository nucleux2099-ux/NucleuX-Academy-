# Tracking marginal gains
## Tracking your marginal gains

Tracking marginal gains is about **seeing progress.** 

![[03 Spaces/Medical Hub/🏥 Clinical Rotations/Clinical skills/Clinical cases/Attachments/Image 5.jpg|When you can see marginal gains, it is motivating. When you do not track them, they are hidden.]]

Failure to track marginal gains means a failure to see progress. This is one of the most common causes of demotivation in the early stages of skill development.

### You can't trust your brain

The human brain is terrible at tracking small changes that occur gradually. Therefore, most people will have absolutely no intuitive sense of whether they are making marginal gains during the early stages. Tracking it actively is one of the only ways to visualise and "feel" the progress.

Viewing progress through marginal gains is what creates a **positive motivation cycle**.

![[03 Spaces/Medical Hub/🏥 Clinical Rotations/Clinical skills/Clinical cases/Attachments/Image 6.jpg|Positive motivation cycle due to marginal gains]]

Without marginal gains, the outcomes become invisible, and we enter a **demotivation spiral**. Over time, this can lead to burn out as our efforts seem to be in vain.

![[Image 7.jpg|Demotivation spiral without marginal gains leads to confusion and burnout]]
