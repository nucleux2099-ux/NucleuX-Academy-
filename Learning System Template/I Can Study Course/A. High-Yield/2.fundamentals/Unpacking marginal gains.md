## Unpacking marginal gains

The concept of marginal gains is widespread since James Clear published his international bestseller, Atomic Habits.

Utilising marginal gains is a powerful tool to have in your toolkit. It is a secret weapon of some of the world’s most successful and high-performing individuals. Mastering marginal gains at a young age is a competitive advantage that few people can beat.

However, it's essential to recognise the different types of gains. This is a practical nuance to the concept that we rarely see discussed.

### Types of "gains"

In the graph below, you can see that there are three different coloured lines. The green top line represents positive gains. This is what we want! The grey line in the middle represents fluctuating gains. The red line at the bottom represents negative gains.

![[03 Spaces/Medical Hub/🏥 Clinical Rotations/Clinical skills/Clinical cases/Attachments/Image 4.jpg|3 types of marginal gains represented. One positive, one fluctuating, one negative.]]

There are three types of marginal gains. Positive, fluctuating, and negative.

The table below outlines the nature of these three types of gains.

|Type of gain|How this gain occurs|Root problem|
|---|---|---|
|**Positive**<br><br>Desirable progress towards goals|Gains compound positively. Each 1% gain builds on the previous one. This could be building on previous knowledge, skills, and processes.|No problems!|
|**Fluctuating**<br><br>Causes a lack of progress, demotivation, and burnout.|There is 1% change, but each 1% change does not build on the previous one. As a result, the gains do not compound. Skills and processes fluctuate between getting better and worse.|This is a problem with the entire approach to progress. Occurs if you think about marginal gains _inconsistently_.|
|**Negative**<br><br>Causes progress away from goals. Worsens position.|Gains compound negatively. Each 1% gain builds on the previous one. However, they worsen skills, knowledge, and processes.|This is a problem with a lack of reliable information, calibration, and/or feedback on progress.|

#### How to ensure you get positive marginal gains

Follow the guidelines below to make sure your gains take you closer to your goal.

1. Think about marginal gains consistently. Train yourself to view progress through this lens by default.
2. Whenever you make mistakes, feed them back in as reflections for your next cycle of experiments.
3. Avoid randomly experimenting on different changes every time.
4. Get feedback on your work and progress regularly.
5. Ensure you are getting advice from reliable, qualified sources of expertise.
