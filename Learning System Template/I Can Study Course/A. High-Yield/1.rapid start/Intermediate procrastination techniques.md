## Procrastination

# Dealing with procrastination

Many people deal with procrastination with unsustainable solutions. People attribute their procrastination to a lack of motivation. However, this leads them to become trapped in a cycle of fluctuating motivation, which leads to burnout over time.

# There's no such thing as procrastination

When we give procrastination a name, it can prevent us from tackling the issue effectively. Procrastination can occur due to many different reasons. Each trigger and response can have different solutions. To end procrastination, we must be specific in what behaviour we are trying to change and what causes this behaviour.

![[03 Spaces/Medical Hub/🏥 Clinical Rotations/Clinical skills/Clinical cases/Attachments/Image 1.jpg|Multiple factors affect whether intentions lead to the desirable behaviour.]]

When the motivation needed is high, we are less likely to be able to complete the task consistently.

# The nature of motivation

Motivation always fluctuates because many variables influence it. Your plans must allow you to achieve your goals even on a bad day with low motivation.

# Willpower-dependent vs willpower-enhanced

You can see how the solution to address one factor can be dramatically different to another. It is also unrealistic to assume all of these factors can be perfectly controlled on a daily basis. Instead, we must rely on habits and environments to move us into auto-pilot. By doing so, we can become willpower enhanced, rather than willpower dependent.

| Willpower dependent                               | Willpower enhanced                                                                    |
| ------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Relies on motivation and willpower to take action | Relies on habits and environment to make taking action frictionless                   |
| Productivity depends on motivation                | Productivity depends on habits                                                        |
| Inconsistent                                      | Highly consistent                                                                     |
| Leads to burnout                                  | Prevents burnout                                                                      |
| Low reserve for when unexpected challenges arise. | Able to utilise willpower when challenges increase to push through and overcome them. |
