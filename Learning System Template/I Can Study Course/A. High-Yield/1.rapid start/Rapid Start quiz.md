## Question 1.1

Gurpreet is studying engineering and finds himself spending most of his time working on assignments last-minute, despite being a diligent student and starting on assignments early. He isn't very efficient with programming, and he makes lots of errors. It also takes him a long time to figure out how to approach a project correctly. Because he has constant imminent deadlines, he always feels like he is behind and trying to catch up.

### Answer

What is the issue that most accurately describes Gurpreet's situation?

His schedule is too packed and unrealistic

**He has urgency trapping**

He runs out of time because he procrastinates too much

His growth is limited because he avoids experimentation

---

### Learning notes

Gurpreet suffers from urgency trapping, which involves prioritising urgent but not necessarily important tasks over important but non-urgent ones, leading to a perpetual cycle of urgency.

He would likely be able to tackle his assignments more quickly and efficiently if he spent time to improve his programming skills and knowledge. By not actively prioritising time in his schedule to work on this, he will constantly struggle to keep up, even though he is working very hard. Over time, he may be completely unable to keep up as challenges get harder.

**In essence, he lacks the skills to excel but does not invest time to develop these skills.**

## Question 1.2

Gurpreet is studying engineering and finds himself spending most of his time working on assignments last-minute, despite being a diligent student and starting on assignments early. He isn't very efficient with programming, and he makes lots of errors. It also takes him a long time to figure out how to approach a project correctly. Because he has constant imminent deadlines, he always feels like he is behind and trying to catch up.

### Answer

Gurpreet decides to dedicate an extra hour at the end of every day to work on his programming skills. What issue is Gurpreet likely to now face?

He is likely to procrastinate more because he is tired

He is going to overload himself because he is practising too much

**He will struggle to follow this new schedule because it is too packed**

There are no likely issues because he is investing in overcoming skill deficiencies.

---

### Learning notes

While Gurpreet should be applauded for taking the time and effort to work on his skills, it will be difficult for him with such a packed schedule.

Gurpreet is already falling behind and using all his time on assignments. It will be difficult for him to consistently commit to an extra hour for skill development every day. Because skills take time to development, a lack of consistency means he will probably be unable to develop his skills enough for it to make a difference. This can lead to frustration and demotivation.

**Gurpreet needs to set aside time to work on his skills by taking time away from something else. This can be difficult, but it's necessary for long-term improvement.**

## Question 2

Priya, a 21-year-old university student, had always been a diligent learner, consistently earning top grades in her courses. She was known for her meticulous note-taking and precise study routines. However, recently, she found herself hesitant to try new learning methods, even though she was aware that her current approach was becoming less effective.

One day, Priya's friend suggested they join a study group to prepare for an upcoming exam. Priya was initially intrigued but soon began to feel anxious. She worried that trying a new approach, such as collaborative learning, might lead to a time-consuming adjustment period, which could be problematic considering her busy schedule. Additionally, she was concerned about how well she'd adapt to a different study environment, as she felt that her current solo approach was tried-and-tested. These might lead to mistakes or errors in her understanding, tarnishing her reputation as a top student.

### Answer

What is Priya's biggest barrier to improving her learning efficiency?

Her time management is not flexible enough

She has urgency trapping as she is unwilling to spend time on non-urgent skills development

Her concerns are correct. Priya needs better decision-making skills to realise that this new approach is not worth trying.

**Priya's fear of failure prevents her from experimenting and growing**

---

### Learning notes

While Priya's concerns are understandable, there are clear signs of a fixed mindset and fear of failure. Although she justifies these concerns through a lens of time investment and adjustment periods, these are fundamentally "what if" scenarios.

She has already recognised that her current approaches are becoming less effective. By avoiding attempting, exploring, and experimenting with alternative methods because it might create errors and "tarnish her reputation" (i.e. threaten her identity), she is avoiding the possibility of growth and improvement.

**Because fear of failure directly prevents all growth, this is Priya's largest barrier.**

## Question 3.1

Sourabh is a 25-year-old medical student. He has recently been watching lots of videos on how to learn more effectively on YouTube. He finds these videos insightful and has watched hours of videos and tutorials. Despite watching so many videos, he still feels uncertain about how to change his learning methods. He asks questions on every video to try and gain more certainty. Although the videos make sense, he is concerned about what would happen if the techniques aren't effective. He is especially concerned that they won't be very effective for his pharmacology paper, where he has to learn about many detailed drug doses and medication names. After several days of asking questions, Sourabh still does not feel confident to change his methods.

### Answer

What is the biggest barrier for Sourabh to improve?

**The amount of information and certainty he wants before experimenting is too high.**

He likely does not know how to ask meaningful questions, which prevents him from gaining certainty.

He has not spent enough time asking the right questions to gain confidence. He needs to ask more questions.

He is not watching enough videos. Sourabh should explore other resources to find better explanations.

---

### Learning notes

Sourabh suffers from **information over experimentation**. This is when a person asks many questions to gain certainty before trying something new. These questions often concern "what if" situations when something goes wrong. Information over experimentation is a fixed mindset symptom where the person tries to reduce the risk of failure.

Unfortunately, it is impossible to reduce the risk of mistakes to zero. This leads people to ask so many questions that they never practice. Because they can never reach certainty, they can get frustrated and even blame others for not giving them the certainty they want.

![Bar chart showing that too much information is detrimental](https://learning.icanstudy.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fprod.icanstudy.com%2F%2Fsites%2Fdefault%2Ffiles%2Finline-images%2FWhat%2520we%2520need%2520for%2520certainty%2520vs%2520clarity_0.jpeg&w=3840&q=75)

View the full article on information vs. experimentation [here](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-resources/information-over-experimentation).

**Asking questions is an important part of learning. Questions about new methods are helpful when they help you gain clarity over the process. Questions that ask about "what if" scenarios to reduce the risk of mistakes that may never occur are not helpful.**

This is called the _inverse-U value of information_. You can read more about this in our article [[What if I make a mistake I feel uncertain. What should I do | here.]]
## Question 3.2

Sourabh is a 25-year-old medical student. He has recently been watching lots of videos on how to learn more effectively on YouTube. He finds these videos insightful and has watched hours of videos and tutorials. Despite watching so many videos, he still feels uncertain about how to change his learning methods. He asks questions on every video to try and gain more certainty. Although the videos make sense, he is concerned about what would happen if the techniques aren't effective. He is especially concerned that they won't be very effective for his pharmacology paper, where he has to learn about many detailed drug doses and medication names. After several days of asking questions, Sourabh still does not feel confident to change his methods.

### Answer

Choose the statement that reflects the best path of action for Sourabh to improve.

**First, he must recognise that he is asking "what if" questions to avoid making mistakes.**

**Second, he must experiment with these new methods to identify mistakes and issues.**

**Third, he must reflect and refine the method, while testing the effectiveness frequently.**



---

### Learning notes

The most significant barrier to Sourabh is that he probably does not recognise how harmful his "what if" questions are! For Sourabh, it feels natural and logical that he must ask questions to gain certainty. Without certainty, how can he attempt?

This is a fixed mindset symptom. Recognising when questions become detrimental is an important first step. Without this, it will be difficult for him to catch himself on his growth-restricting habits.

After this, he must experiment with the method, understanding that he will likely make mistakes. Giving up on new methods too quickly will prevent him from learning any new skill that cannot be mastered in a single attempt (i.e. most skills).

Finally, he must refine his processes through reflection and experimentation. Waiting too long before testing the effectiveness creates unnecessary risks and consequences of failure.

# Key principle

Gaining awareness of an issue is the most important first step for those with a strong fear of failure and growth-restricting thinking habits.
