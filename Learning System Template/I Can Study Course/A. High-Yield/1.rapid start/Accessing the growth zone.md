# Accessing the growth zone

## Accessing the growth zone

Key principles

- Beware of the reversion response to fear
- Challenging goals require additional skills
- Developing skills require navigation through the fear zone
- Beware of the misinterpreted-effort hypothesis
- Aim to develop a growth mindset with the strategies at the end of this lesson

# The journey to learn new skills

Many people spend too long on becoming 100% certain about the theory before attempting to use skills and gain real experience. This is detrimental to learning and can cause a common issue of [information over experimentation](https://learning.icanstudy.com/learning-resources/information-over-experimentation).

![What we think it takes to learn new skills (pie chart) 75% theory and 25% practice](https://learning.icanstudy.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fprod.icanstudy.com%2F%2Fsites%2Fdefault%2Ffiles%2Finline-images%2FWhat%2520we%2520think%2520it%2520takes%2520to%2520learn%2520new%2520skills_0.jpeg&w=3840&q=75)

An oversimplified view of what many people think learning new skills involves.

In reality, practice is essential to give relevance to theory. Our questions become more targeted, and we can enter the **theory-practice cycle**, which is the engine for skill development. When the theory-practice cycle is delayed or infrequent, skill development is slow and ineffective.

![The theory practice cycle for learning new skills.](https://learning.icanstudy.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fprod.icanstudy.com%2F%2Fsites%2Fdefault%2Ffiles%2Finline-images%2FWhat%2520it%2520really%2520takes%2520to%2520learn%2520new%2520skills_0.jpeg&w=3840&q=75)
