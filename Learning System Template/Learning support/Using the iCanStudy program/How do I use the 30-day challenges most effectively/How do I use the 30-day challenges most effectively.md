# How do I use the 30-day challenges most effectively
**How do I use the 30-day challenges most effectively? How do they fit in the core program? Can I do more than one?**

**Using the ****iCanStudy**** program**

**Overview**

The 30-day challenges are created to give you the experiences and insights you need to learn new skills optimally. They aim to give you a concentrated exposure to the types of organic experiences you may normally encounter in months or years of unguided experimentation and learning. After the 30-day challenges, you should expect to have deeper awareness and metacognition of each dimension, as well as a range of relevant experiences that make the core program easier to work through.

**Frequently asked questions**

Do I have to complete them?

Every challenge is completely optional. Progression through the core program without the 30-day challenges is absolutely viable and reasonable if you do not have time.

What if I do not have 30 days before my cohort starts?

The diagnostic report recommends prioritising certain dimensions. If you feel like those dimensions are very limiting, we recommend completing the 30-day challenge before the core program or progressing through the core program more slowly than you normally would.

If you feel that you are confident to improve your dimensions straight away, you can ignore the 30-day challenges and start with the core program only.

The challenges exist so people have an option to get an early start on skill development in a way that does not require much guidance and support, before their cohort commences.

Can I do multiple challenges at the same time?

Yes, you can complete multiple challenges simultaneously. However, we recommend starting on the dimension that is most limiting for you, based on the archetype calculated from your diagnostic assessment.

Can I do challenges later, while going through the core program?

Yes, you can complete the challenges anytime you feel like you are plateauing through the core program or that your ability to consolidate new techniques from the core program is slow. These situations may indicate that you lack the relevant experiences and insights to learn the core program skills, and you may benefit from exposure to these experiences through the challenges.

How should I progress through the high-yield phase if I am also doing the challenges?

If you feel overwhelmed going through the core program and challenges simultaneously, we recommend focusing on only one. If you find the core program techniques relatively straightforward to implement and practice, prioritise moving through the core program until you face difficulties.

If you find the core programs overwhelming and difficult to integrate into your system, prioritise the 30-day challenges and move back to the core program when you have completed the relevant challenge.

If you feel that you can do both simultaneously, we recommend progressing through the core program at half the pace recommended in the stage overview descriptions.
