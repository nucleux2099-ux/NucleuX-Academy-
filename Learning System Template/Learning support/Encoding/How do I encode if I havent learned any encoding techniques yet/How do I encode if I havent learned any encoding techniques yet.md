# How do I encode if I haven't learned any encoding techniques yet
**How do I encode if I haven't learned any encoding techniques yet?**

**Encoding**



**Early encoding practice**

The first set of encoding techniques is taught in Fundamentals 2. Before this point, there are two methods of improving your encoding ability:

Try your best with what you know

Attempt the 30-day self-regulation or deep processing challenges

**Method 1: Try your best**

While this may not seem like a reliable solution, simply trying your best with the methods you already are familiar with is one of the best things you can do in the early parts of the program.

The reason encoding techniques are not taught directly until Fundamentals 2 is because correct encoding can be challenging and is generally harder to learn to perform accurately than interleaved retrieval. After just a few days of practice with interleaved retrieval, most learners can see immediate improvements in memory and depth of understanding. These improvements continue for months as learners deepen their interleaving skills.

However, with only a few days of practice with *encoding*, some learners will still be confused and find it difficult to assess their level of improvement.

**Gaining competence with interleaved retrieval first is beneficial because:**

It creates a stronger learning system with regular spacing.

It generates familiarity with higher-order learning as many interleaving strategies force higher-order learning even without deliberate encoding techniques. This makes it easier to learn advanced encoding techniques later.

It produces a greater benefit in a shorter period of time. Even with terrible encoding skills, good interleaving offers an immediate improvement.

**Dangers of technique overload**

Furthermore, working on both encoding and interleaved retrieval simultaneously is likely to cause technique overload, leading to the learner failing to gain competence in either.

For these reasons, we recommend that learners simply use their existing methods of encoding and focus on learning through interleave retrieval first.

**Method 2: Attempt the 30-day challenges**

For those who have the time and feel extremely limited by their current encoding methods *despite attempting interleaved retrieval already*, the 30-day self-regulation and deep processing challenges are found on the  page.

Both challenges introduce basic principles of encoding that can help create a stronger foundation to build on in later lessons. While these challenges do not teach a step-by-step encoding system, following the instructions should substantially improve overall confidence with information processing so that it is no longer a rate limiter to continuing your interleaved retrieval.
