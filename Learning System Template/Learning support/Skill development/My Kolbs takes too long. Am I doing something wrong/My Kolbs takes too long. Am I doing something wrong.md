# My Kolbs takes too long. Am I doing something wrong
**My Kolb's takes too long. Am I doing something wrong?**

**Kolb's as a self-limiting process**

**Progression note**

This article is relevant for those who have learned Kolb's in Briefing.

Kolb's relies on our self-awareness and metacognition during all the steps. When we first start using Kolb's, it can be confusing as our level of introspection and insight may be limited. The table below outlines some common limitations, how they feel, and how they are resolved over time.

| Experience | Likely cause | Solution |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Reflecting is tedious or confusing. | Limited self-awareness and insight into meta-learning make it difficult for learners to know what is meaningful enough to reflect on. | Those with low metacognition around learning must develop this at high priority as it is a limitation for all forms of learning skill development. This will improve as more insight develops around which cognitive and metacognitive events are meaningful. This can take 4 to 12 weeks. |
| Kolb's sessions take too long. | Limited self-awareness, metacognition, or critical reflection skills can make Kolb's time-consuming as there is no clear focus. | Those with adequate self-awareness, metacognition, and critical reflection skills should be able to complete a high-quality Kolb's session within 30 minutes. Because Kolb's is sequential, limitations in an earlier step affect the quality of all later steps. Therefore, it is appropriate to limit sessions to 30 minutes and not complete all steps until your efficiency and speed naturally improve as your self-awareness develops with repeated sessions. |
