# How to perform a skills audit
**How to perform a skills audit**

**What is a Skill Audit?**

**Audit (/ˈ****ɔːdɪt****/): ***a systematic review or assessment of something.*

A skills audit is a **structured **way to pinpoint issues in your learning system and make **targeted improvements**. It involves **reviewing your techniques against guidelines** to identify gaps and allow for improvements.

Regular skill audits help to keep your learning system flexible and effective.

**Note**

The first reference to skills audit is at the **Briefing **stage. Further references to the Skills Audit exist at the Final Checkpoints of **Ascent 2 **and **Base Camp**.

*If you have not reached these stages, don't feel overwhelmed! You can still use these instructions to assess your performance.*

**When do I need a skill audit?**

Skill audits are recommended in the following situations.

After rushing or selective learning

Learning skills too quickly (i.e. without adequate practice) or skipping techniques (e.g. because you thought they were not relevant) significantly increases your chances of introducing technique mistakes or deficient systems of learning and self-management. 

When this happens, it is difficult, even for experienced coaches, to identify the root cause of problems. Skill audits help to carefully identify any errors in your technique or system.

Resuming after a long break

A long break can cause you to forget or lose skills because you haven't practised them. This is called "**technique decline**." A skill audit can help you find areas where you're weaker and give you a plan to improve.

Progressing to new stages

In some instances, progressing to the next stage of the program requires mastery of the current and prior stages**.** This may be because the next stages build directly on assumed foundational skills, such as when moving from Fundamentals 2 to Briefing or from Technique Training to Ascent 1.

A skill audit helps ensure you're ready for new techniques and helps you maintain technique proficiency.

Feeling stuck or lack of progress

When you feel your skills are plateauing and you are unsure why you are not improving, a skill audit helps identify specific (sometimes subtle) mistakes in your technique that can be optimised.

**How do I perform a skills audit correctly?**

A **growth mindset** is crucial to perform a skills audit. In the journey of learning, it is more important to recognise when we are mistaken than to assume we are correct.

Keep the following points in mind during your skills audit:

**Keep an open mind:** Approach the audit with the sincere intention to understand and improve your skills. Be open-minded to finding areas to work on, even if you have spent lots of time on these areas already.

**Be honest with yourself:** Accuracy in your self-assessment is crucial. The goal of a skills audit is to gain clarity over where you might need to improve. A 'bad' rating is not a negative reflection on your past efforts but rather a sign of commitment to continuous improvement.

**Take your time:** Assess each skill meticulously. A skill audit can take hours if you are advanced through the program. In some instances, you may need to apply a skill and practice it to gain enough self-awareness to assess yourself accurately.

**Keep it regular:** We recommend performing a skill audit at least every 2 to 3 months. This may be more frequent if you are learning new techniques quickly.

**What are the steps?**

**When you perform a skills audit, take the ****LEEP****!**

Step 1 - Learning flow map

Create a flow map to review each step of your learning process. Use the *Gear Checklist *in the **Briefing **Stage.

Check if your learning flow includes techniques from all completed stages.

Step 2 - Evaluate

Check your techniques against the guidelines in the course. These guidelines can be:

Checkpoint videos

Milestone Checklists

Meta-Checklists

General objectives at the start of each stage.

End-of-Stage Quizzes

Look for gaps or problems from rushing or missing parts of the techniques.

Rate yourself in each technique on a scale of 1 to 5, where 1 means poor and 5 means excellent.

Be as objective and accurate as possible for the best outcome.

Step 3 - Error correction

Fix the gaps by adjusting your techniques against target metrics and stage checkpoints.

Apply the techniques with a focus on consistency and practice. We recommend only focusing 1 or 2 aspects at a time to prevent overload.

Seek feedback via:

Self-evaluation

Discord community

Office Hours

Step 4 - Perfect practice

Use frameworks like Marginal Gains Tracking and Kolb's Cycle to develop your skills. Reflect on mistakes and errors through systematic experimentation using both frameworks to evaluate your progress.

Remember that reflection is a **cyclical process**. Fixing a technique or mistake may require multiple experiments. This is especially true if you have rushed or experienced technique decline.

If you are in this position, focus on holding a growth mindset. Adopt a gradual approach to bridge the gaps.

**Two methods of approaching skill audits**

You can perform a skill audit in two ways:

**Skill screen:** This is a faster and simpler approach that is useful when performed regularly. It helps identify major gaps or remind yourself of the correct technique.

**Comprehensive audit:** This is a slower but more detailed approach that is recommended for when a skill screen has not been effective at identifying gaps or the issues are more long-standing.

| Skill screen (faster) | Comprehensive audit (detailed) |
| --- | --- |
| Review previous checkpoint videos and major lessons introducing new techniques. Rate your competency, accuracy, and consistency on a lesson-by-lesson basis using a scale of 1 to 5. | Review every lesson, checkpoint, and end-of-stage quiz from the beginning of the course. Rate your competency, accuracy, and consistency on a lesson-by-lesson basis using a scale of 1 to 5. |
| Identify the key aspects of the techniques that need high competence. | Look for discrepancies between theoretical understanding and the practical application of each lesson along with key aspects of techniques |
| Focus on areas where you score less than 4 out of 5. | Focus on areas where you score less than 4 out of 5 or less than 5 out of 5 for foundational techniques. |
