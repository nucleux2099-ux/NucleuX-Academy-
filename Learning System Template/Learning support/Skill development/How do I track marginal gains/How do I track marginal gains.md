# How do I track marginal gains
**How do I track marginal gains?**

**Progression note**

This article is relevant for learners who have completed the *Tracking marginal gains* lesson in *Fundamentals 1.*

**How to track your gains**

**Step 1: Anchor the goal**

**Identify a meaningful, challenging goal you want to achieve in the medium term (between nine months and three years from now).**

**Step 2: Dissect the goal**

**What knowledge, attributes, skills, processes, or resources do I need to achieve this goal?**

**Think about:**

**Mental health**

**Focus and concentration**

**Time management and prioritisation skills**

**Studying skills**

**Ask yourself, "How sure am I?"**

**This is an opportunity to identify if you truly know what is needed to achieve a goal, or if your ideas are based on unreliable sources or assumptions.**

**Step 3: Evaluate your target level**

**For each factor you identified in step 2, rate (out of 10) what level you need to reach to achieve your goal. Then, describe what this rating means to you so that it is clear and explicit.**

**For example:**

**You may need 7/10 time management and 8/10 studying skills to reach your goal of scoring in the top 5% of your cohort at the end of the year. Then, describe what 7/10 time management and 8/10 studying skills mean to you.**

**Step 4: Evaluate your current level**

**For each factor, rate (out of 10) your current level and justify your rating. We can often overestimate or underestimate our abilities, so justify your rating as objectively as possible.**

**Step 5: Identify the 1%**

**How could you go from where you are now to becoming 1% closer to your target level.**

**Tips:**

**Do not try to improve on more than 2 skills or attributes at the same time. This causes overload and will hinder your ability to improve.**

**Marginal gains usually progress from knowledge to actions to behaviours, habits, and positions. **

**In the early stages, you first need to acquire knowledge on what action you need to take to improve your skills, processes, or attributes. **

**Next, you must take action to commence the process of improvement. **

**Over time, your actions change your behaviours and habits or your position. Your position is how much control you have over achieving your goal. When you are in a better position, you can reduce risks more.**

**When you review your marginal gains again in the future, you often only need to repeat these final two steps (Step 4: Evaluate your current level and Step 5: Identify the 1%) as the first three steps will be similar.**

**Key principle**

**At any point in the above steps, you may find that you do not know the answer. **

**For example:**

**You may not be sure what skills you need to achieve your goal during step 2**

**You may not know how good you need to be in step 3**

**You may not know how to begin improving some attributes or skills in step 5**

**When you're unsure, gaining more information is the most important marginal gain for you.**

**You cannot take action before knowing what action to take. Identifying uncertainties and gaining more information is often the best first marginal gain for any difficult goal.**

**The uncertainty of marginal gains**

**Often, the early stages of progress (or "gain") are confusing because there are so many things that must be worked on. Over time, as we work on each part, one step at a time, it becomes easier and clearer to see what our next best step is. In the beginning, the best step is sometimes just to take a step at all!**

**Therefore, since the focus is on a ****marginal**** gain, you do not need a comprehensive list of every knowledge, attribute, skill, process or resource, but simply the ones that seem clearly necessary as the next step.**

**Examples**

**Below is an example of marginal gains for a learner.**

**Anchoring the goal**

**I want to achieve at least 90% in my end-of-year exam.**

**Dissect the goal**

**To achieve this, I need:**

**Adequate mental health to stay focused, energised, and cognitively effective. To achieve this, I need:**

**More knowledge about how to maintain mental health**

**More self-awareness about my own mental health and what causes it to fluctuate**

**Techniques to improve my mental health**

**Practice and experience using these techniques to manage my mental health**

**Good focus and concentration, so I can study effectively and not waste time. To achieve this, I need:**

**Fewer distractions**

**To achieve this, I need to understand what my distractions are and processes/skills to reduce distractions**

**Ability to enter into deep flow more easily**

**I need to know what deep flow is and how to trigger it**

**I need techniques and processes to help me do this**

**I need practice and experience using these processes**

**Excellent time management and prioritisation to stay on top of my workload and tasks to cover the high content volume before the exam. To achieve this, I need:**

**Better time management methods. This means:**

**More flexible and sustainable**

**More consistent**

**To achieve these, I need more knowledge on how to have better time management and effective methods I can use**

**Better task prioritisation so I spend my time wisely. To achieve this, I need:**

**Knowledge of how to prioritise**

**Self-awareness of mistakes I make with prioritising**

**Tools and processes to prioritise effectively**

**Practice and experience with these processes**

**Excellent learning skills. To achieve this, I need:**

**High-quality encoding skills to learn deeply and reduce how much I forget. To achieve this, I need:**

**Knowledge of what makes good encoding**

**Techniques to help me encode at the level I need to**

**Practice and experience to use these techniques proficiently**

**High-quality retrieval practice to find gaps and maintain my memory and performance. To achieve this, I need:**

**Knowledge of what makes good retrieval practice**

**Techniques to help me retrieve adequately**

**Practice and experience to use these techniques proficiently**

**To achieve any of the above, I also need:**

**Sufficient feedback on my techniques to know that I am doing it accurately**

**Frequent reflection and self-feedback to identify mistakes and correct them**

**A growth mindset to experiment and make mistakes without being paralysed by uncertainty and asking "what if" questions that stop me from just trying things.**

** ****Evaluating target level**

Mental health: 7/10 - I need it to be good enough to keep me optimum and performing well but it does not have to be good enough that I am invulnerable to all stress.

Focus and concentration: 8/10 - I have lots to do and limited time. I must use my available time effectively and produce meaningful work in blocks I have set out. I do not need to be so perfect that I am able to focus and concentrate in any setting, since I will mostly be studying in controlled environments.

Time and task management: 7/10 - I do not have a very busy schedule and I don't have many other commitments. My time and task management needs to be good enough that I spend my time working on the things that will help me achieve my goal, but I have enough room for error that it does not have to be perfect.

Learning skills: 9/10 - This exam is very difficult and competitive. To achieve at least 90%, my learning skills will need to be very good, with deep mastery of all the core parts of my system.

Feedback: 8/10 - I will need lots of feedback to improve my techniques as I suspect I will have many difficulties improving, based on my past experience.

Self-reflection: 8/10 - I have never tried to work on these skills actively before and I know that I struggle with self-reflection, including tasks like identifying marginal gains. To improve quickly, I must be able to identify problems and self-correct very quickly to improve as much as I need to, even when external feedback is slower.

Growth mindset: 8/10 - I have some fixed mindset tendencies, and to improve, I must experiment actively and try new techniques even when I am unsure how to do it correctly. This makes me feel uncertain, but I must be able to do this more comfortably to improve as quickly as I need.

**Evaluating current level**

Mental health: 4/10 - I can handle some stress but I feel like I get overwhelmed easily. I have some knowledge and processes to help manage mental health, but not much.

Focus and concentration: 3/10 - I get distracted and procrastinate very easily. Sometimes I am able to have deep focused work, but it is inconsistent and unreliable. I do not have much knowledge and experience with managing my focus effectively.

Time and task management: 6/10 - I mostly do what I need to do, except I sometimes find it difficult to complete all my tasks and adapt my schedule when things don't go to plan. I have some knowledge on what I can do but have not practised using any techniques.

Learning skills: 5/10 - My academic performance is highly variable, and I average 70% on tests. I sometimes do very well (90%+) but have never felt confident to get a high mark. I spend lots of time studying, but this doesn't translate into predictable results very often. I have some knowledge of what I can do, and I have learned some techniques, but I have not practised them much, and I am unsure what to start with. 

Feedback: 2/10 - I rarely ask for feedback and do not feel comfortable asking for feedback. I know where and how to ask for feedback but I have very little experience with engaging in it.

Self-reflection: 2/10 - I struggle with self-reflection and feel confused by the process. I do not know how to start.

Growth mindset: 3/10 - I feel uncertain about trying new things and tend to ask lots of questions before giving things a go to gain more certainty. As a result, I delay experimentation for days (or even weeks) until I feel certain enough. I know what it means to have a growth mindset, but I do not know what I can do to change this, and I have limited experience in trying to develop a growth mindset.

**Finding the 1%**

**In this example below, the rationale for how the 1% was identified is explained in italics for your education. You are not expected to write down the parts in italics in your own marginal gains tracking.**

Mental health: Learn about what affects mental health and some processes to improve it.

*Rationale: In Step 4, we wrote, "I have some knowledge and processes to help manage mental health, but not much". Therefore, the next step should be to improve knowledge and gain more clarity.*

Focus and concentration: Learn about what affects focus and some processes to improve it.

*Rationale: In Step 4, we wrote, "I do not have much knowledge and experience with managing my focus effectively". Therefore, the next step should be to improve knowledge and gain more clarity.*

Time and task management: Practise and apply some techniques I know for improving time management and see how it goes.

*Rationale: In Step 4, we wrote, "I have some knowledge on what I can do but have not practised using any techniques". Therefore, the next step should be to apply what we have learned to get more information on barriers, mistakes, or successes.*

Learning skills: Apply one or two of the learning techniques I know of and see how it goes.

*Rationale: In Step 4, we wrote, "I have some knowledge of what I can do, and I have learned some techniques, but I have not practised them much, and I am unsure what to start with". Therefore, the next step should be to apply what we have learned to get more information on barriers, mistakes, or successes.*

Feedback: Ask for feedback on my attempted work at least once.

*Rationale: In Step 4, we wrote, "I know where and how to ask for feedback, but I have very little experience with engaging in it". Therefore, the next step should be to gain some experience to get more information on barriers, mistakes, or successes.*

Self-reflection: Engage in 20 minutes of self-reflection every Wednesday and Saturday by reviewing step 4 and step 5 of my marginal gains tracking.

*Rationale: In Step 4, we wrote, "I... feel confused by the process. I do not know how to start.". Therefore, the next step should be to improve self-awareness and familiarity with the general process of self-reflection. Once there is general familiarity, we can work on improving quality. Ironically, "I do not know how to start" is often a sign that we need to just start with our best attempt and improve after an initial attempt instead of simply thinking about how to start, which can be an endless and futile process.*

Growth mindset: Try to use a technique I am not sure about and see how it goes. Find some mistakes and barriers that I did not know about now (as per conscious competence model).

*Rationale: In Step 4, we identified that we delay experimentation and attempt to seek certainty instead. This is a sign of a fixed mindset because it is not possible to gain certainty on a new technique before making some mistakes and attempting it first. Therefore, the best next step is to just attempt something new and identify mistakes, helping us move along the journey of unconscious incompetence.*

**There are currently many marginal gains I can pursue at this point. I am likely to become overwhelmed if I attempt more than three simultaneously. Therefore, I will pursue the gains around focus and concentration, growth mindset, and learning skills first because these seem to be the most important or limiting for me currently. I can pursue other gains later if I evaluate them to become of a greater priority.**

**General rule**

If we know enough to attempt something and start making some mistakes, the next step is usually to experiment and learn from each attempt through self-reflection and analysis. 

If we do not know enough even to attempt it and make a mistake, the next step is usually to learn more about the concept or technique so that we can make our first attempt.
