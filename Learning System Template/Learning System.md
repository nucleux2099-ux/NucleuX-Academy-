## Precision Learning System: Phased Approach

### Phase 1: Rapid Start (Stabilising the Enablers)

This foundational phase focuses on self-management and mindset—clearing the operative field of obstacles before proceeding to complex cognitive work.

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Self-Management**|**Time and Task Prioritization:** Eliminate urgency trapping by focusing on important tasks. The intention without execution is meaningless.|
|**Focus and Distraction Control**|**BEDS-M Framework:** Use this system to improve focus, including dedicated **Breaks**, eliminating distractions in the **Environment**, prioritizing **Deep Sleep**, **Setting Intentions** (specific and achievable), and **Ritualization/Mindfulness**. Advanced learners prioritize creating an optimized zone of focus.|
|**Practice vs. Theory**|Establish the **Practice-to-Theory Ratio** (recommendation of 5:1). Skill development requires applying theory to relevant experience.|

### Phase 2: Fundamentals (High-Yield Core Techniques)

This stage equips the learner with sufficient mastery for top-level achievement in most academic settings and provides the fundamental equipment needed for the Growth Phase.

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Encoding Transition**|**Notes and Processing:** A transitional technique to break the habit of passive, linear note-taking. Engage in deep processing by asking questions and simplifying concepts into groups (chunks) and relationships, leveraging the _evaluate_ level of Bloom’s taxonomy.|
|**Retention and Retrieval**|**Spaced, Interleaved Retrieval (SIR):** A pillar technique to combat the forgetting curve. **Spacing** involves expanding gaps between review sessions. **Interleaving** means hitting concepts from multiple angles (e.g., teaching an imaginary student, drawing mind maps, modifying questions).|
|**Revision Strategy**|**Test, Target, Teach (T-T-T):** Test your knowledge, **Target** areas of weakness, and **Teach** the content. When teaching, explain concepts in lay terms _before_ introducing technical terminology (reverse explanation).|
|**Rote Learning**|**Digital Flashcards:** Use digital flashcards (like Anki with the Leitner system) only for Layers 3 and 4 (important and arbitrary details). Use them frequently in **microlearning** sessions, not large batches.|

### Phase 3: Growth Phase - Technique Training

This stage introduces cognitive retraining, focusing on building conceptual structures (the "web" of knowledge) rather than accumulating isolated facts.

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Conceptual Structure**|**Memory is Reconstructed:** Memory relies on the structural web of interconnected nodes (concepts and relationships), not pulling information from a static box. **Conceptual Learning** builds strong structures, whereas **Rote Learning** builds weak ones.|
|**Inquiry-Based Learning**|**Traffic Light System (TLS):** A drill to train your brain to prioritize curiosity and purpose. **Red Light** (Stop) involves creating questions (What am I curious about? Why do I need to know this?). **Green Light** (Go) means actively seeking answers to _your_ questions, not passively consuming everything.|
|**Learning Direction**|**Order Control:** Take control of the learning order. The "best" order is dictated by your existing knowledge and curiosity, not the linear presentation in a textbook.|
|**Self-Correction**|**Kolb’s Experiential Cycle:** A pillar technique for skill development (Experience, Reflection, Abstraction, Experiment). Use this cycle regularly to track **marginal gains** and learn from mistakes.|

### Phase 4: Growth Phase - Ascent I (Bear Hunter System: Aim)

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Bear Hunter System (BHS)**|The system for encoding new knowledge: **Aim** → **Shoot** → **Skin**.|
|**Aim Step (Precision Questioning)**|Lead with "Why is this important?" and "How is this related to ___?" to force evaluative thinking.|
|**Structure Formation**|**Importance-Based Chunking:** Group information based on _why_ it is relevant.|
|**Cognitive Load Management**|Expect confusion; it signals desirable difficulty that yields better encoding.|

### Phase 5: Growth Phase - Ascent II (Bear Hunter System: Shoot)

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Shoot Step (Deep Processing)**|Fill the Aim scaffold by manipulating ideas.|
|**Effective Note-Taking**|**VPReFRE Criteria:** Notes must be Visual, Processed, Relational, Freehand, Reflective, Efficient.|
|**Knowledge Organization**|**Layers of Learning:** Logic, Concepts, Important Details, Arbitrary Details.|
|**Note-Taking Structure**|Use mind maps, concept graphs, arrows, spatial layout to reduce cognitive overload.|

### Phase 6: Growth Phase - Ascent III (Bear Hunter System: Skin)

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Skin Step (Refinement)**|Consolidate, integrate, and refine chunk structure; treat as interleaving.|
|**Advanced Mapping**|**GRINDE Maps:** Grouped, Reflective, Interconnected, Nuanced, Directional, Emphasised.|
|**Chunk Optimization**|**Intuitive Chunking** and the **2-4 Rule** challenge framing bias.|

### Phase 7: Base Camp & Camp (Consolidation and Advanced Retrieval)

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**BHS Implementation**|**Hipshot Variation:** Standard/Accelerated/Ultra to stay ahead of syllabus.|
|**Advanced Retrieval**|**Whole-Part-Whole (WPW) Technique:** Multi-order retrieval practice.|
|**Self-Management Upgrade**|Focus training (OFF-Rest), prioritisation (Priority 0+1).|

### Phase 8: Summit (Hyper-Efficiency and Performance)

|Core Components|Key Techniques & Rationale|
|:--|:--|
|**Advanced Cramming**|**Multipass System:** Swiss-cheese style layered passes.|
|**Performance Optimization**|Exam execution under pressure and stress.|

## Mindsets to Keep Front and Centre

1. **Metacognition is king.** Track thoughts and emotions so techniques stay aligned with desired outcomes.
2. **Desirable difficulty signals progress.** If it feels easy, you’re not encoding deeply.
3. **Feedback keeps you honest.** Use coaches, peers, and checklists to surface blind spots.

Whenever you feel tempted to rush, revisit this roadmap, note your current phase, and recommit to the next focused upgrade.
