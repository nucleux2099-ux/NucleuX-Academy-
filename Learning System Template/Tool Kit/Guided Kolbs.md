### Guidelines for experience
> [!tip]  Your experience should be:  
>
>1. **Process-focused** - avoid reflecting on outcomes (e.g. test results) as this is not a process. Think about the problematic part of the result, such as the process leading to the result, or your reaction to the result.
>2. **Specific** - avoid reflecting on many events and activities as this will make it difficult to produce a targeted and focused reflection. To reflect on multiple skills and experiences, you should complete multiple separate cycles to keep each cycle focused.
>3. **Recent** - reflecting on experience that happened too long ago makes it easier to forget important parts of the process and increases the chances of error.
>4. **Concise** - the experience is usually only one sentence as we will elaborate on it in the next steps.

## Step 2: Reflection

> [!input]- **List and describe the sequence of events, in chronological order***  
> **✎**   

> [!input]- **How did you feel about the experience?***  
> _Be specific and detailed._  
> **✎**   

> [!input]- **Which aspects (if any) felt especially difficult? Which felt like they went well?***  
> **✎**   

> [!input]- **How did you respond to challenges and difficulties during this process?**  
> _Skip if none._  
> **✎**   

> [!input]- **What were the triggers to you feeling the way you did?**  
> **✎**   

> [!input]- **Why do you think you acted the way you did during this experience?***  
> **✎**   

### Difficulties with reflection
> [!info]  
> If deep reflection is hard, focus on improving self-awareness and give yourself up to **30 minutes**. Quality reflection accelerates later steps.

---

## Step 3: Abstraction

### Guidelines for abstraction
> [!tip]  
> Analyse your reflection for **root causes**, not symptoms. If you struggle:  
> - **Reflection too brief** – add more “data points”.  
> - **Self-awareness low** – observe more next time.  
> - **Unfamiliar with analysis** – practise and you’ll improve quickly.  

> [!input]- **What habits, beliefs, and tendencies explain why you acted the way you did?***  
> **✎**   

> [!input]- **Do you act or respond in similar ways in other parts of your life?**  
> **✎**

### Guidelines for experiment
