# 📋 Blood Transfusion — Exam Prep Mode

## 🎯 One-Liners
| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Universal donor | O negative |
| Universal recipient | AB positive |
| Storage temp for RBCs | 2-6°C |
| Shelf life of PRBC | 35-42 days (with additives) |
| Shelf life of platelets | 5 days (room temp with agitation) |
| FFP contains | All clotting factors |
| 1 unit PRBC raises Hb by | ~1 g/dL |
| Massive transfusion ratio | 1:1:1 (PRBC:FFP:Platelets) |
| Most common transfusion reaction | Febrile non-hemolytic |
| Most dangerous reaction | Acute hemolytic (ABO mismatch) |

## 📊 Blood Products
| Product | Content | Storage | Indication |
|---------|---------|---------|------------|
| PRBC | RBCs, Hct 55-80% | 2-6°C | Anemia |
| Platelets | Platelets | 20-24°C | Thrombocytopenia |
| FFP | All factors | -18°C | Coagulopathy |
| Cryoprecipitate | Fibrinogen, VIII, vWF | -18°C | Fibrinogen deficiency |

## ⚠️ Transfusion Reactions
| Type | Timing | Features | Treatment |
|------|--------|----------|-----------|
| Febrile | During/soon after | Fever, chills | Stop, antipyretics |
| Allergic | During | Urticaria, itching | Antihistamines |
| Acute hemolytic | During | Fever, back pain, shock | STOP immediately |
| TRALI | Within 6 hrs | Pulmonary edema, hypoxia | Supportive |
| TACO | During/after | Pulmonary edema, HTN | Diuretics |

## 💊 Massive Transfusion Complications
- Hypothermia (use warmer)
- Hypocalcemia (citrate toxicity)
- Hyperkalemia
- Coagulopathy (dilutional)
- Acidosis
