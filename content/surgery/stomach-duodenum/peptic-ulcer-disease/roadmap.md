## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Peptic Ulcer Disease:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Stomach anatomy & blood supply | — | Understanding gastric and duodenal arterial supply explains which vessels bleed in different ulcer locations |
| Anatomy | Duodenal anatomy | AN47.4 | D1 is the most common ulcer site; posterior D1 ulcers erode into the gastroduodenal artery |
| Physiology | Acid-pepsin aggression vs mucosal defense | — | The balance between aggressive (acid, pepsin, NSAID, H. pylori) and defensive (mucus, bicarbonate, blood flow) factors determines ulcer formation |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Explain the pathogenesis — role of H. pylori, NSAIDs, acid secretion, and mucosal defense mechanisms
- Manage emergency complications — bleeding (endoscopic therapy, angioembolization), perforation (omental patch)
- Describe elective surgical options — highly selective vagotomy, truncal vagotomy + drainage — and their indications

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |

| Pathology | PUD pathology & complications | PA24.1 | Ulcer complications: hemorrhage, perforation, penetration, and gastric outlet obstruction |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Upper GI Bleeding** — PUD accounts for ~50% of UGIB
2. **Pyloric Stenosis** — Chronic duodenal ulceration causes fibrotic pyloric stenosis
3. **Postgastrectomy Syndromes** — Complications of ulcer surgery — dumping, afferent loop, alkaline reflux

### Prerequisites

- **Gastric Anatomy & Physiology** — Acid secretion physiology and gastric anatomy are the foundation

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

PUD integrates physiology (acid secretion), microbiology (H. pylori), pharmacology (PPIs, eradication), pathology (ulcer types, complications), and surgery (emergency repair, elective procedures). The H. pylori revolution fundamentally changed surgical practice — making vagotomy almost obsolete and demonstrating how microbiological discovery can transform surgical disease.
