## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Upper GI Bleeding:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Gastric & duodenal arterial supply | — | Knowing the gastroduodenal, left gastric, and splenic arteries helps identify bleeding sources and plan intervention |
| Physiology | Hemostasis & coagulation | — | Understanding the coagulation cascade guides resuscitation and correction of coagulopathy in acute bleeding |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply the Glasgow-Blatchford score for risk stratification and admission decisions
- Use the Forrest classification to guide endoscopic treatment and predict rebleeding risk
- Manage the algorithm — resuscitation, PPI, endoscopy, and when to involve surgery/IR

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pathology | PUD as cause of UGIB | — | Peptic ulcers are the most common cause — Forrest classification grades the bleeding stigmata and predicts rebleeding |
| Pharmacology | IV PPI therapy | — | High-dose IV PPI (80mg bolus + 8mg/hr infusion) stabilizes clot by maintaining gastric pH >6 |
| Radiology | CT angiography, endoscopy | — | Upper GI endoscopy within 24h is both diagnostic and therapeutic — hemoclips, thermal coagulation, injection |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Peptic Ulcer Disease** — PUD management follows from controlling acute bleeding
2. **Portal Hypertension** — Variceal bleeding is the second most common cause of UGIB

### Prerequisites

- **Gastric Anatomy & Physiology** — Understanding gastric vasculature is essential for managing UGIB

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Upper GI bleeding integrates anatomy (arterial supply), physiology (hemostasis, shock compensation), pharmacology (IV PPIs, tranexamic acid), radiology (CT angiography), and endoscopy/surgery. The multi-disciplinary acute care pathway — gastroenterology, radiology, surgery, ICU — makes UGIB a prototype of team-based emergency management.
