## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Peptic Ulcer Disease:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Stomach & duodenal anatomy | — | Gastric ulcers occur along the lesser curve; duodenal ulcers occur in D1 — anatomical location determines surgical approach |
| Anatomy | Duodenal anatomy (D1 — ulcer site) | AN47.4 | D1 posterior wall ulcers erode into the gastroduodenal artery — most dangerous site for hemorrhage |
| Physiology | Acid secretion & mucosal defense | — | PUD results from imbalance between aggressive factors (acid, pepsin, H. pylori) and defensive factors (mucus, bicarbonate, blood flow) |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Differentiate gastric from duodenal ulcers — pathogenesis, location, acid secretion patterns
- Manage complications — hemorrhage (Forrest classification), perforation (Graham patch), stenosis
- Apply H. pylori eradication protocols and understand the evidence for test-and-treat strategy

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pathology | Peptic ulcer pathology | PA24.1 | Chronic peptic ulcers show fibrosis at base, undermined edges, and endarteritis obliterans in surrounding vessels |
| Microbiology | H. pylori microbiology & diagnosis | — | H. pylori causes 70-90% of duodenal ulcers — diagnosis by urease test, histology, stool antigen, or urea breath test |
| Pharmacology | PPIs, H2 blockers, antacids | — | PPIs are the mainstay — irreversibly inhibit H+/K+ ATPase on parietal cells |
| Radiology | Endoscopy, CT for perforation | — | OGD is diagnostic and therapeutic (Forrest classification for bleeding ulcers); CT for perforation |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Upper GI Bleeding** — PUD is the most common cause of upper GI bleeding
2. **Gastric Cancer** — Chronic gastric ulcers have malignant potential — all must be biopsied

### Prerequisites

- **Gastric Anatomy & Physiology** — Understanding gastric acid secretion and mucosal defense is fundamental

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Peptic ulcer disease beautifully integrates physiology (acid-mucosal balance), microbiology (H. pylori revolution), pharmacology (PPIs, eradication therapy), pathology (ulcer healing, malignant transformation), and surgery (hemorrhage control, perforation repair). The discovery of H. pylori transformed PUD from a surgical to a medical disease — one of medicine's greatest paradigm shifts.
