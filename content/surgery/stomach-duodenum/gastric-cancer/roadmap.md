## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Gastric Cancer:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Stomach lymphatic drainage (D1/D2) | — | D2 lymphadenectomy follows the celiac axis branches — understanding gastric lymphatic stations is essential for oncological surgery |
| Pathology | Lauren classification (intestinal vs diffuse) | — | Intestinal type (well-differentiated, glandular) has better prognosis; diffuse type (signet ring, linitis plastica) is aggressive |
| Pathology | Intestinal metaplasia sequence | — | Correa cascade: gastritis → atrophy → intestinal metaplasia → dysplasia → cancer — explains screening in endemic areas |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Stage gastric cancer using TNM and plan the extent of resection (subtotal vs total gastrectomy)
- Describe D1 vs D2 lymphadenectomy and the evidence supporting D2 dissection
- Apply multimodal treatment — perioperative FLOT, adjuvant chemoradiation, or neoadjuvant approaches

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pharmacology | Perioperative chemotherapy (FLOT) | — | FLOT (5-FU, leucovorin, oxaliplatin, docetaxel) is the current standard for resectable gastric cancer |
| Microbiology | H. pylori as Group I carcinogen | — | H. pylori eradication reduces gastric cancer risk — WHO classifies it as a definite carcinogen |
| Radiology | CT staging, EUS for T-staging | — | EUS is the most accurate modality for T-staging; CT for N and M staging |

### Prerequisites

- **Gastric Anatomy & Physiology** — Gastric anatomy, especially lymphatic drainage, is foundational
- **Peptic Ulcer Disease** — Chronic PUD and H. pylori increase gastric cancer risk

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Gastric cancer integrates anatomy (D2 lymphadenectomy stations), pathology (Lauren classification, HER2 status), microbiology (H. pylori carcinogenesis), pharmacology (FLOT, trastuzumab for HER2+), and radiology (EUS/CT staging). Japanese contributions to D2 lymphadenectomy and Korean screening programs show how cultural context shapes surgical practice.
