# Gastric Cancer

> **Source**: Shackelford Ch 49-51, NCCN Guidelines
> **Topic System**: [[Stomach Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> Gastric cancer is classified by Lauren (intestinal vs diffuse), staged by AJCC 8th edition (T=depth, N=node count, ≥16 nodes required), with staging laparoscopy for T1b+, and treatment ranging from endoscopic resection (early) to perioperative chemo + surgery (locally advanced).

---

## Epidemiology

- **Incidence:** 26,500 new cases/year (US); ~1 million globally
- **5-year survival:** 32% overall
- **Male:Female ratio:** 2:1
- **Median age:** 68 years

### Risk Factors
| Acquired | Genetic Syndromes |
|----------|-------------------|
| H. pylori (strongest) | HDGC (CDH1) - 70-80% risk |
| Smoking, alcohol | Lynch syndrome |
| High-salt diet | FAP |
| Pernicious anemia | Peutz-Jeghers |

---

## Lauren Classification

| Type | Features | Prognosis |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Intestinal** | Gland-forming, well-differentiated, distal stomach, older patients | Better |
| **Diffuse** | Signet ring cells, younger patients, proximal, linitis plastica | Worse |
| **Mixed** | Features of both | Intermediate |

> 💡 **Trend:** Intestinal type decreasing, diffuse/proximal increasing in Western countries

---

## TNM Staging (AJCC 8th Edition)

### T Classification
| Stage | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| T1a | Lamina propria/muscularis mucosae |
| T1b | Submucosa |
| T2 | Muscularis propria |
| T3 | Subserosa |
| T4a | Perforates serosa |
| T4b | Adjacent structures |

### N Classification
| Stage | Nodes Involved |
|-------|----------------|
| N0 | 0 |
| N1 | 1-2 |
| N2 | 3-6 |
| N3a | 7-15 |
| N3b | ≥16 |

> **Minimum lymph nodes:** ≥16 for adequate staging; ≥30 optimal

## Clinical Signs (Late Presentation)

| Sign | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Sister Mary Joseph nodule** | Periumbilical metastasis |
| **Virchow's node** | Left supraclavicular LN |
| **Blumer's shelf** | Rectal shelf on DRE |
| **Krukenberg tumor** | Ovarian metastasis |
| **Irish's node** | Left axillary LN |

---

## Diagnostic Workup

1. **EGD with biopsy** - ≥6-8 biopsies
2. **CT chest/abdomen/pelvis** - Staging
3. **EUS** - T and N staging (if no bulky disease)
4. **Staging laparoscopy** - For T1b+ or N+ (yield ~30% upstage)
5. **Peritoneal cytology** - Positive = M1 disease

---

## Treatment by Stage

| Stage | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **T1a, well-diff** | Endoscopic resection (EMR/ESD) |
| **T1b-T2 N0** | Surgery (subtotal/total gastrectomy + D2) |
| **T3+ or N+** | Perioperative chemo (FLOT) + Surgery |
| **Metastatic** | Systemic therapy, palliation |

### Surgical Principles
- **Margins:** ≥4 cm (intestinal), ≥6 cm (diffuse)
- **Lymphadenectomy:** D2 (standard), ≥16 nodes
- **Extent:** Total vs subtotal based on location

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

> 💎 **Pearl 1:** H. pylori is the strongest risk factor for gastric cancer

> 💎 **Pearl 2:** Lauren classification guides prognosis: intestinal better than diffuse

> 💎 **Pearl 3:** Staging laparoscopy upstages ~30% of patients (prevents unnecessary laparotomy)

> 💎 **Pearl 4:** Minimum 16 lymph nodes for adequate staging

> 💎 **Pearl 5:** FLOT (perioperative chemo) is standard for locally advanced disease

> 💎 **Pearl 6:** Know the eponymous signs: Virchow, Sister Mary Joseph, Krukenberg, Blumer, Irish
