## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Breast Cancer:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Axillary lymph node levels (I, II, III) | — | Axillary dissection levels determine staging and guide surgical extent — Level I/II clearance vs sentinel node biopsy |
| Pathology | IDC, ILC, DCIS — breast cancer types | — | Histological subtype dictates surgical approach (wide excision vs mastectomy) and prognosis |
| Pathology | BRCA1/2, HER2, ER/PR | — | Molecular profiling drives targeted therapy decisions — trastuzumab for HER2+, tamoxifen for ER+ |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Stage breast cancer using TNM and determine operability
- Compare breast-conserving surgery vs mastectomy — indications and contraindications
- Plan multimodal treatment based on molecular subtype (Luminal A/B, HER2+, Triple-negative)

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pharmacology | AC-T, trastuzumab, tamoxifen | — | Systemic therapy selection based on molecular subtype is critical for multimodal treatment |

| Radiology | Mammography, MRI breast, PET-CT staging | — | BI-RADS classification guides biopsy decisions; MRI for high-risk screening |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Breast Reconstruction** — Post-mastectomy reconstruction options follow logically from cancer surgery
2. **Neck Dissection** — Locally advanced breast cancer may involve supraclavicular nodes

### Prerequisites

- **Benign Breast Disease** — Understanding ANDI classification helps differentiate benign from malignant
- **Breast Anatomy & Examination** — Clinical examination skills are the starting point for all breast pathology

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Breast cancer is a paradigm of multidisciplinary oncology — integrating pathology (histology, molecular typing), pharmacology (chemotherapy, hormonal therapy, targeted therapy), radiology (screening, staging), and surgery. Understanding the biology drives the surgery, not the other way around.
