## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Femoral Hernia:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Femoral canal — boundaries, femoral ring | AN15.1 | The femoral ring is bounded by the inguinal ligament, lacunar ligament, pectineal ligament, and femoral vein — the rigid boundaries explain high strangulation risk |
| Anatomy | Inguinal ligament & lacunar ligament | — | The lacunar ligament forms the medial boundary — it must be incised carefully during repair to avoid aberrant obturator artery |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Distinguish femoral hernia from inguinal hernia by anatomical landmarks — relation to the pubic tubercle
- Explain why femoral hernias have the highest strangulation risk — rigid femoral ring
- Describe surgical approaches — Lockwood (low/crural), Lotheissen (high/inguinal), McEvedy (high/vertical)

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Femoral hernia is a condition where anatomy dictates urgency — the rigid femoral ring makes strangulation much more likely than inguinal hernia. Understanding the anatomy (femoral canal boundaries, aberrant obturator artery) is essential for safe repair and avoiding vascular injury during emergency surgery.
