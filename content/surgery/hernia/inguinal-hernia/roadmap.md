## 📐 Foundations

| Anatomy | Spermatic cord — hernia relations | — | Indirect hernias lie within the spermatic cord coverings; understanding cord contents prevents injury during repair |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Differentiate direct from indirect inguinal hernia by anatomy (Hesselbach triangle, deep ring)
- Compare open (Lichtenstein) vs laparoscopic (TEP, TAPP) repair — indications and outcomes
- Recognize and manage complications — strangulation, sliding hernia, Richter hernia, Littre hernia

### Prerequisites

- **Inguinal Canal Anatomy** — Detailed inguinal canal anatomy is prerequisite knowledge

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Inguinal hernia is the most common general surgery condition — mastering it requires integrating inguinal canal anatomy (walls, rings, Hesselbach triangle), surgical technique (tension-free mesh repair), and clinical examination (cough impulse, reduction, relation to pubic tubercle). The evolution from tissue repair to mesh repair demonstrates how evidence changes surgical practice.
