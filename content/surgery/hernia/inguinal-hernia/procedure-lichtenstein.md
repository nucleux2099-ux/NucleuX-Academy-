# 🔧 Lichtenstein Tension-Free Mesh Repair — Procedural Notes

## Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Procedure** | Open inguinal hernia repair with mesh |
| **Indication** | Primary inguinal hernia (direct or indirect) |
| **Anesthesia** | Local/Regional/General |
| **Duration** | 30-45 minutes |
| **Recurrence** | <1% |

---

## 🔷 Preoperative

### Patient Preparation
- Informed consent (pain, recurrence, infection, testicular atrophy)
- Hair removal (if excessive)
- Mark operative side
- Prophylactic antibiotics (single dose cefazolin)
- Empty bladder

### Position
- Supine
- Arms by side

---

## 🔷 Surgical Steps

### 1. Incision
```
    ASIS ●─────────────────● Pubic tubercle
           \               /
            \    2 cm     /
             ╲───────────╱  ← Incision 2 cm above
                             inguinal ligament
```
- 5-6 cm incision parallel to inguinal ligament
- 2 cm above inguinal ligament
- Centered over deep ring (midpoint of inguinal ligament)

### 2. Dissection Through Layers
```
Skin
  ↓
Scarpa's fascia
  ↓
External oblique aponeurosis → OPEN along fibers
  ↓
Inguinal canal exposed
```

### 3. Identify Structures
- Identify and protect ilioinguinal nerve
- Identify and protect iliohypogastric nerve
- Identify spermatic cord

### 4. Create Cord Mobilization
- Elevate cord with finger/tape
- Identify cord structures
- Identify hernia sac

### 5. Deal with Hernia Sac

**Indirect Hernia:**
- Separate sac from cord structures (gentle dissection)
- Reduce sac into abdomen OR
- Ligate and excise sac if large

**Direct Hernia:**
- Invaginate/reduce back through floor
- Usually no need to open sac

### 6. Mesh Placement (The Key Step)
```
                Mesh (7 × 15 cm or tailored)
    ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                      │
    │    ●───────────────────────●         │
    │    │                       │         │ ← Cut slit for cord
    │    │      MESH             │         │
    │    │                       ╲         │
    │    ●───────────────────────●╲        │
    │              Slit →         ╲        │
    │           for cord           ╲       │
    └──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Fixation Points:**
1. **Medial:** Overlap pubic tubercle by 2 cm, suture to rectus sheath
2. **Inferior:** Continuous suture along inguinal ligament
3. **Superior:** Loose sutures to conjoint tendon/internal oblique
4. **Lateral:** Tails cross behind cord, suture together

### 7. Closure
- Approximate external oblique over cord
- Scarpa's fascia
- Skin (subcuticular or interrupted)

---

## 🔷 Key Technical Points

### Critical Principles
✓ **Tension-free** — Mesh must lie flat, not tight
✓ **Adequate overlap** — 2 cm beyond pubic tubercle medially
✓ **Cord in slit** — Not compressed
✓ **Protect nerves** — Identify ilioinguinal and iliohypogastric

### Common Errors
✗ Inadequate medial overlap
✗ Mesh placed under tension
✗ Nerve injury (chronic pain)
✗ Cord compression

---

## 🔷 Postoperative Care

### Immediate
- Day surgery possible
- Mobilize same day
- Ice pack to reduce swelling
- Scrotal support if needed

### Discharge Instructions
- Avoid heavy lifting for 4-6 weeks
- Light activities within 1 week
- Driving after 1 week
- Return to work: 1-2 weeks (desk job), 4-6 weeks (manual labor)

---

## 🔷 Complications

| Complication | Incidence | Prevention/Management |
|--------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Chronic pain** | 5-10% | Careful nerve identification |
| **Seroma** | 5-8% | Usually resolves; aspirate if large |
| **Hematoma** | 2-5% | Meticulous hemostasis |
| **Wound infection** | 1-2% | Prophylactic antibiotics |
| **Recurrence** | <1% | Adequate mesh overlap |
| **Testicular atrophy** | <0.5% | Preserve testicular vessels |
| **Mesh infection** | <1% | May require mesh removal |

---

## 🔷 Laparoscopic Alternatives

### TEP (Totally Extraperitoneal)
- Preperitoneal space dissection
- Does NOT enter peritoneal cavity
- Preferred for bilateral/recurrent

### TAPP (Transabdominal Preperitoneal)
- Enters peritoneal cavity first
- Mesh placed preperitoneally
- Better visualization

### Comparison

| Feature | Lichtenstein | TEP | TAPP |
|---------|--------------|-----|------|
| Learning curve | Easy | Steep | Moderate |
| Anesthesia | Local possible | General | General |
| Cost | Lower | Higher | Higher |
| Chronic pain | Higher | Lower | Lower |
| Recovery | 2-4 weeks | 1-2 weeks | 1-2 weeks |
| Bilateral repair | Two incisions | One setup | One setup |
