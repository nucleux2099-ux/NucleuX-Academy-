## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Identify risk factors for incisional hernia — wound infection, obesity, emergency surgery, vertical incision
- Classify by EHS classification and plan repair — primary suture vs mesh (onlay, sublay, IPOM)
- Describe component separation techniques (Ramirez, TAR) for large abdominal wall defects

### Prerequisites

- **Wound Healing** — Impaired wound healing is the primary cause of incisional hernia — infection, obesity, malnutrition, steroid use

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Incisional hernia integrates wound healing biology (why fascial closure fails), abdominal wall anatomy (fascial planes for mesh placement), and surgical technique (component separation, mesh selection). It represents the long-term consequence of impaired healing and is a growing challenge with increasing laparotomy and obesity rates.
