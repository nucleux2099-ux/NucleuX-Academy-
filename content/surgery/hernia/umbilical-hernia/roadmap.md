## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Differentiate infantile (resolves by age 5) from adult umbilical hernia (progressive, needs repair)
- Identify risk factors — obesity, ascites, multiple pregnancies, chronic cough
- Plan repair — Mayo's repair (suture) for small defects; mesh repair for larger defects

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Umbilical hernia connects embryology (patent umbilical ring), abdominal wall anatomy (linea alba weakness), and clinical decision-making (observation in children vs repair in adults). In cirrhotics with ascites, umbilical hernia repair carries special considerations — addressing both the hernia and the ascites.
