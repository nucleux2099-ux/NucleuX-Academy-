## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Colorectal Cancer:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Colonic blood supply (SMA/IMA) | — | Oncological resections follow vascular pedicles — understanding SMA/IMA anatomy determines resection extent |
| Anatomy | IMA & marginal artery | — | The marginal artery of Drummond provides collateral flow — critical for anastomotic safety |
| Anatomy | Rectal anatomy & mesorectal fascia | AN47.10 | TME plane for rectal cancer depends on understanding the mesorectal fascia and CRM |
| Pathology | Adenoma-carcinoma sequence | — | Most CRCs arise through the adenoma-carcinoma pathway over 10-15 years — basis for screening |
| Pathology | APC, KRAS, p53 pathway | — | Molecular progression model guides targeted therapy and predicts response to anti-EGFR agents |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Explain the adenoma-carcinoma sequence and its implications for screening colonoscopy
- Apply TNM staging and plan appropriate surgical resection based on tumor location
- Understand the role of molecular profiling (MSI, RAS, BRAF) in treatment decisions

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pharmacology | FOLFOX, FOLFIRI, anti-EGFR | — | Chemotherapy regimen selection depends on sidedness, RAS status, and MSI status |
| Radiology | CT colonography, MRI pelvis (rectal) | — | MRI pelvis is mandatory for rectal cancer staging — assesses CRM and extramural vascular invasion |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Rectal Cancer** — Rectal cancer management differs significantly from colon cancer — neoadjuvant therapy, TME, organ preservation
2. **Colon Cancer** — Specific operative planning for colon cancer resections
3. **Colostomy Management** — Stoma care is essential knowledge for any CRC surgeon

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Colorectal cancer exemplifies how anatomy (vascular pedicle surgery), pathology (molecular subtypes), pharmacology (targeted therapy), and radiology (MRI staging) converge into precision oncology. The evolution from purely anatomical staging to molecular classification is reshaping surgical decision-making.
