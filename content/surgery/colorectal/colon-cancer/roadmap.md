## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Colon Cancer:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Right colon — ileocolic, right colic arteries | — | Right hemicolectomy requires ligation of ileocolic and right colic vessels at their SMA origin |
| Anatomy | Left colon — left colic, sigmoid arteries | — | Left-sided resections depend on IMA territory; marginal artery adequacy determines safe resection margins |
| Pathology | Adenoma-carcinoma sequence | — | Understanding the molecular pathway (APC→KRAS→p53) explains screening rationale and polyp surveillance |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Plan the appropriate colonic resection based on tumor location and vascular anatomy
- Apply TNM staging to determine adjuvant therapy needs
- Manage emergency presentations — obstruction, perforation, bleeding

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pharmacology | Adjuvant FOLFOX | — | Stage III colon cancer benefits from adjuvant chemotherapy — FOLFOX is standard of care |
| Radiology | CT staging, colonoscopy | — | CT chest-abdomen-pelvis for staging; colonoscopy for tissue diagnosis and synchronous lesion detection |

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Colon cancer integrates surgical anatomy (vascular pedicle-based resections), pathology (adenoma-carcinoma sequence, MSI status), pharmacology (adjuvant FOLFOX, immunotherapy for MSI-H), and radiology (staging CT, colonoscopy). The shift to molecular classification (CMS subtypes) is transforming treatment paradigms.
