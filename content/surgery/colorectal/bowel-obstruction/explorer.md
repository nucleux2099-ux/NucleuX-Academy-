# Bowel Obstruction

> **Source**: Sleisenger Ch 123-124, Maingot Ch 29
> **Topic System**: [[Colon Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> Bowel obstruction presents with colicky pain, vomiting, distention, and obstipation; distinguish simple vs. strangulated (surgical emergency), and adhesive SBO may trial conservative management (NG, NPO) for 48-72h if no signs of strangulation.

---

## Classification

| Classification | Categories |
|----------------|------------|
| **Location** | Small bowel (SBO) vs. Large bowel (LBO) |
| **Degree** | Complete vs. Partial |
| **Mechanism** | Simple vs. Strangulated |
| **Cause** | Mechanical vs. Functional (ileus) |

### Critical Distinction

| Type | Definition | Urgency |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Simple** | Luminal obstruction without vascular compromise | May trial conservative |
| **Strangulated** | Obstruction WITH vascular compromise | SURGICAL EMERGENCY |
| **Closed-loop** | Segment obstructed at both ends | High risk for rapid strangulation |

---

## Common Causes

| Setting | SBO | LBO |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Developed countries** | Adhesions (70%), Hernias (10%), Malignancy (10%) | Colorectal cancer (60%), Volvulus (15%), Diverticulitis (10%) |
| **Developing countries** | Hernias (most common) | Sigmoid volvulus |
| **Pediatric** | Intussusception, Malrotation | Hirschsprung's |

## Pathophysiology

```
OBSTRUCTION
     │
     ▼
Proximal bowel distention (swallowed air + succus)
     │
     ▼
↑ Intraluminal pressure → ↓ Venous outflow
     │
     ▼
Mucosal injury (4-6h) → Focal necrosis (8-12h)
     │
     ▼
Bacterial overgrowth + Bacterial translocation
     │
     ▼
SIRS, Sepsis, Perforation
```

> 💡 **Historical Pearl:** Wangensteen (1942) proved swallowed air is the primary source of distention – basis for NG decompression!

## Clinical Features

### Classic Tetrad of SBO
1. **Colicky abdominal pain** (paroxysmal → constant if strangulating)
2. **Vomiting** (bilious early, feculent late)
3. **Abdominal distention**
4. **Constipation → Obstipation**

### SBO vs LBO Presentation

| Feature | Proximal SBO | Distal SBO/LBO |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **Vomiting** | Early, profuse, bilious | Late, feculent |
| **Distention** | Minimal | Marked |
| **Pain** | Periumbilical | Lower abdomen |

---

## Signs of Strangulation (Surgical Emergency)

| Clinical Sign | Significance |
|---------------|--------------|
| **Constant, severe pain** | No longer colicky |
| **Peritonitis** | Rebound, guarding |
| **Fever** | Late sign |
| **Tachycardia** | Early sign |
| **Leukocytosis** | WBC >15,000 |
| **Acidosis** | Elevated lactate |
| **Bloody NG aspirate** | Mucosal ischemia |

> 💡 **Key:** Transition from colicky to constant pain = concerning for strangulation

---

## Imaging

### X-ray Findings
| Finding | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| Dilated small bowel (>3 cm) | SBO |
| Air-fluid levels | Obstruction |
| "String of pearls" | Complete SBO |
| Paucity of distal gas | Complete obstruction |
| Free air | Perforation |

### CT Abdomen (Gold Standard)
- **Sensitivity:** 90-95%
- **Transition point:** Where dilated meets decompressed bowel
- **Closed-loop:** C-shaped or U-shaped bowel segment
- **Strangulation:** Mesenteric edema, poor bowel wall enhancement

## Gastrografin Challenge

- **Indication:** Adhesive SBO without strangulation
- **Protocol:** 100 mL via NG, X-ray at 8-24h
- **Resolution:** Contrast in colon = will resolve
- **Therapeutic:** Hyperosmolar, reduces edema

---

## Key Clinical Pearls

> 💎 **Pearl 1:** Adhesions = #1 cause of SBO in developed countries (history of surgery)

> 💎 **Pearl 2:** Transition from colicky to constant pain = strangulation until proven otherwise

> 💎 **Pearl 3:** CT is gold standard – shows transition point, closed-loop, strangulation signs

> 💎 **Pearl 4:** Gastrografin is both diagnostic AND therapeutic in adhesive SBO

> 💎 **Pearl 5:** Don't miss incarcerated hernia – examine groin in EVERY obstruction!

> 💎 **Pearl 6:** LBO with competent ileocecal valve = closed-loop obstruction (emergency)
