## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Differentiate mechanical from paralytic ileus using clinical and radiological features
- Identify the common causes of SBO (adhesions, hernias) vs LBO (cancer, volvulus)
- Recognize strangulation features requiring emergency surgery

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Intestinal Obstruction** — Detailed management of small bowel obstruction including adhesions and hernias
2. **Volvulus** — Large bowel volvulus is a specific cause requiring urgent decompression

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Bowel obstruction bridges anatomy (bowel segments, blood supply), physiology (motility, fluid shifts), pathology (ischemia, necrosis), and radiology (plain film, CT). The ability to distinguish simple from strangulated obstruction is a life-saving surgical decision.
