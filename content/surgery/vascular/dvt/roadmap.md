## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Deep Vein Thrombosis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Deep venous system of lower limb | — | Understanding the deep veins (femoral, popliteal, iliac) and their anatomy explains DVT location patterns and PE risk |
| Pathology | Virchow triad & thromboembolism | PA11.1 | Stasis, endothelial injury, hypercoagulability — the three factors driving venous thrombosis |
| Physiology | Coagulation cascade | — | The intrinsic and extrinsic pathways converge at factor Xa — understanding this explains anticoagulant mechanisms |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply the Wells score to assess clinical probability and guide investigation
- Describe the anticoagulation protocol — initial heparin/LMWH, transition to oral anticoagulants (DOACs vs warfarin)
- Identify indications for IVC filter, thrombolysis, and surgical thrombectomy

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Radiology | Venous duplex USG, D-dimer | — | Compression USG is the first-line investigation — non-compressible vein confirms DVT; D-dimer has high NPV |
| Pathology | Thrombophilia workup | — | Factor V Leiden, protein C/S deficiency, antiphospholipid syndrome — evaluate in young patients or recurrent DVT |

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

DVT integrates vascular anatomy (deep venous system), pathology (Virchow triad, thrombophilia), pharmacology (anticoagulants — heparin, warfarin, DOACs), and radiology (compression USG). The prevention of DVT in surgical patients (thromboprophylaxis) is one of the most impactful patient safety measures — connecting basic science to clinical practice.
