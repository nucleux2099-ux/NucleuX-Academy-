## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Varicose Veins:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Saphenous veins — GSV, SSV anatomy | — | The GSV runs from medial malleolus to saphenofemoral junction; SSV drains into the popliteal vein — essential for surgical planning |
| Anatomy | Saphenofemoral junction | — | SFJ incompetence is the most common cause — understanding this anatomy guides stripping, EVLA, and foam sclerotherapy |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe the CEAP classification for chronic venous disease (C0-C6)
- Perform clinical tests — Trendelenburg, Perthes, tap test — and their significance
- Compare treatment options — compression, sclerotherapy, EVLA/RFA, stripping — based on anatomy

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Venous Disease** — Chronic venous insufficiency is the progression of untreated varicose veins
2. **DVT** — DVT must be excluded as a cause of secondary varicose veins

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Varicose veins integrate venous anatomy (saphenous systems, perforators), physiology (venous valve function, muscle pump mechanism), pathology (chronic venous insufficiency, lipodermatosclerosis), and surgery (endovenous vs open techniques). The evolution from surgical stripping to endovenous ablation demonstrates how understanding vascular anatomy enables minimally invasive alternatives.
