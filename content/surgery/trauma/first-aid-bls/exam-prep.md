# First Aid & BLS — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU17.1 **[PG]**, SU17.2 **[PG]**, SU17.3 **[UG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **CAB** sequence (not ABC) since AHA 2010
- Compression rate: **100-120/min**, depth: **5-6 cm**, ratio **30:2**
- **Shockable rhythms**: VF, pulseless VT → defibrillation
- **Non-shockable**: Asystole, PEA → CPR + epinephrine
- **Epinephrine** dose: **1 mg IV every 3-5 min** (both shockable and non-shockable)
- **Amiodarone**: 300 mg IV bolus for refractory VF/pVT, then 150 mg
- **Defibrillation energy**: Biphasic 120-200 J; Monophasic 360 J
- **Synchronized cardioversion** for unstable tachycardia WITH pulse
- Infant CPR: 2-finger technique (1 rescuer), 2 thumb-encircling (2 rescuer), ratio **30:2** (1 rescuer) or **15:2** (2 rescuers)
- **Heimlich manoeuvre**: NOT for infants — use back blows + chest thrusts

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**Hs and Ts (Reversible causes of cardiac arrest):**
- **H**ypovolemia, **H**ypoxia, **H**ydrogen ion (acidosis), **H**ypo/hyperkalemia, **H**ypothermia
- **T**ension pneumothorax, **T**amponade, **T**oxins, **T**hrombosis (coronary/pulmonary)

**START Triage — "30-2-Can Do":**
- Respirations >**30** → Immediate
- Capillary refill >**2**s → Immediate
- **Can**'t follow commands → Immediate

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **First step in BLS** → Check scene safety, then check responsiveness
2. **Drug of choice in all cardiac arrest** → Epinephrine 1 mg IV
3. **When to stop CPR** → ROSC, physician decision, rescuer exhaustion, or DNR
4. **Precordial thump** → Only if witnessed arrest, no defibrillator, do within 10 sec
5. **Compression-only CPR** → Recommended for untrained bystanders
6. **Recovery position** → Unconscious patient who IS breathing
7. **Laryngeal mask airway (LMA)** → Supraglottic device, does NOT protect against aspiration
8. **Cricothyroidotomy** → Surgical airway when intubation fails ("can't intubate, can't oxygenate")

## ⚡ One-Liners

| Fact | Answer |
|------|--------|
| Most common initial rhythm in adult cardiac arrest (out-of-hospital) | VF |
| Most common rhythm in in-hospital arrest | PEA |
| Best predictor of ROSC | End-tidal CO₂ (>10 mmHg) |
| Post-ROSC target temperature management | 32-36°C for 24h |
| Glasgow Coma Scale range | 3-15 |
| Triage officer's role | Classify only, NOT treat |
