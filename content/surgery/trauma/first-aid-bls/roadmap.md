## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into First Aid & BLS:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Basic hemodynamics for resuscitation | — | Understanding the relationship between chest compressions and cardiac output guides effective CPR technique |
| Anatomy | Airway anatomy for BLS | — | Head tilt-chin lift and jaw thrust maneuvers are based on upper airway anatomy — displacing the tongue from the posterior pharynx |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Perform high-quality CPR — rate (100-120/min), depth (5-6cm), full recoil, minimal interruptions
- Apply the chain of survival — early recognition, early CPR, early defibrillation, post-arrest care
- Demonstrate basic airway maneuvers and recovery position

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

First aid and BLS integrate cardiovascular physiology (cardiac output during CPR), respiratory physiology (ventilation), and neuroanatomy (airway management). These are the most time-critical skills in medicine — the foundation upon which all advanced resuscitation is built. Every medical professional must master BLS regardless of specialty.
