# Chest Trauma — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU17.9 **[PG]**, SU17.10 **[PG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **Tension pneumothorax**: Clinical diagnosis → needle decompression 2nd ICS MCL → then chest drain
- **Beck's triad** (tamponade): Muffled heart sounds + Hypotension + Distended neck veins
- **Massive haemothorax**: >**1500 mL** initial OR >**200 mL/h** for 2-4h → thoracotomy
- **Flail chest**: ≥**3 ribs** fractured in ≥**2 places**. Underlying **pulmonary contusion** causes hypoxia (not the flail)
- **Open pneumothorax**: Wound >⅔ tracheal diameter → 3-sided dressing (flutter valve effect)
- **Diaphragmatic rupture**: Left > Right (80:20). CXR shows bowel in thorax, NG tube coiling in chest
- **Aortic disruption**: 90% at **isthmus** (just distal to left subclavian). CXR: **widened mediastinum** >8cm
- **Pulmonary contusion**: Most common potentially lethal chest injury. Develops over 24-48h on CXR
- **Chest drain position**: **5th ICS, anterior axillary line**, safe triangle, above the rib
- **Commotio cordis**: VF from blunt chest impact during cardiac vulnerable period (repolarization)

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**Lethal Six — "ATOMFC":**
- **A**irway obstruction
- **T**ension pneumothorax
- **O**pen pneumothorax
- **M**assive haemothorax
- **F**lail chest
- **C**ardiac tamponade

**Thoracotomy indications — "1500 & 200":**
- **1500** mL initial drainage
- **200** mL/hour ongoing (for 2-4h)

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **Tension pneumothorax vs tamponade** → Both have hypotension + JVD. Tension: hyperresonant, tracheal deviation. Tamponade: muffled sounds, no tracheal deviation
2. **eFAST detects** → Pericardial fluid + pneumothorax + intra-abdominal fluid
3. **Tracheobronchial injury sign** → Persistent large air leak after chest drain, fallen lung sign
4. **Oesophageal injury gold standard** → Contrast swallow (Gastrografin then barium)
5. **Rib 1-2 fracture** → High-energy trauma, suspect great vessel injury
6. **Rib 9-12 fracture** → Suspect liver (right) or spleen (left) injury
7. **Sucking chest wound physiology** → Mediastinal flutter → decreased venous return
8. **Pericardiocentesis landmark** → Left xiphocostal angle, aim toward left shoulder at 45°
