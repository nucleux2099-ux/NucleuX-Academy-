## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify soft tissue injuries — abrasion, laceration, contusion, crush, avulsion, degloving
- Apply wound management principles — debridement, irrigation, primary vs delayed closure
- Determine tetanus prophylaxis requirements based on wound type and immunization history

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Soft tissue injuries connect trauma principles, wound biology (healing phases), microbiology (infection prevention, tetanus), and surgical technique (debridement, closure methods). The decision between primary closure and delayed closure depends on understanding wound contamination, blood supply, and healing biology.
