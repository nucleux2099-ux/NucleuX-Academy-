# Soft Tissue Injuries — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU17.7 **[PG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **Laceration vs Incised**: Laceration has irregular edges + tissue bridging; incised has clean edges
- **Crush syndrome**: Rhabdomyolysis → **myoglobinuria** (dark urine) → AKI. CK >5x normal
- **Compartment syndrome**: Pressure >30 mmHg → **fasciotomy** (emergency, do NOT elevate — worsens ischaemia)
- **Earliest sign of compartment syndrome**: **Pain on passive stretch** of involved muscles
- **Golden period**: 6 hours for wound closure (8h in highly vascular areas like face/scalp)
- **Tetanus-prone wound**: >6h old, >1cm deep, contaminated, crush/burn, devitalized tissue
- **Tetanus prophylaxis**: TT + TIG for tetanus-prone wound in unimmunized patient
- **Degloving injury**: Skin separated from deep fascia — assess capillary bleeding to determine viability
- **Volkmann's contracture**: Forearm compartment syndrome → flexion of wrist and fingers, extension of MCP
- **Rhabdomyolysis triad**: Myalgia + weakness + dark urine

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**6 Ps of Compartment Syndrome:**
- **P**ain (out of proportion)
- **P**ressure (tense compartment)
- **P**araesthesia
- **P**aralysis (late)
- **P**allor (late)
- **P**ulselessness (very late — don't wait!)

**Crush Syndrome Management — "FAST":**
- **F**luids (aggressive NS before release)
- **A**lkalinize urine (NaHCO₃)
- **S**erum K⁺ management
- **T**reat AKI (dialysis if needed)

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **Most reliable sign of compartment syndrome** → Pain on passive stretch
2. **Compartment syndrome — DO NOT** → Elevate limb above heart level (reduces perfusion pressure)
3. **Most common compartment involved** → Anterior compartment of leg
4. **Myoglobin filtered by kidney at** → >1.5 mg/dL (urine turns reddish-brown)
5. **Negative blood on urine dipstick with dark urine** → Rules out myoglobinuria (dipstick detects heme)
6. **Delayed primary closure** → Day 4-7, wound left open initially, closed once clean
7. **Skin graft types**: Split-thickness (SSG) — epidermis + partial dermis; Full-thickness (FTSG) — epidermis + full dermis
8. **SSG donor site heals by** → Re-epithelialization from adnexal structures
