# Head Injury — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU17.4 **[UG]**, SU17.6 **[PG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **EDH**: Middle meningeal artery (85%), biconvex on CT, **lucid interval**, does NOT cross suture lines
- **SDH**: Bridging veins, crescent-shaped on CT, crosses sutures
- **Acute SDH** = hyperdense; **Subacute** (3d-3wk) = isodense; **Chronic** (>3wk) = hypodense
- **GCS motor component** is the **best predictor** of outcome
- **Cushing's triad** (raised ICP): Hypertension + Bradycardia + Irregular respiration
- **Monroe-Kellie doctrine**: Fixed cranial volume = brain (80%) + blood (10%) + CSF (10%)
- **CPP = MAP − ICP** (target CPP >60 mmHg)
- **Base of skull fracture signs**: Battle's sign, Raccoon eyes, CSF rhinorrhoea/otorrhoea, haemotympanum
- **Ring test/Halo sign**: CSF mixed with blood forms double ring on filter paper
- **Mannitol**: Osmotic diuretic, contraindicated in hypotension/renal failure
- **Diffuse axonal injury (DAI)**: Rotational deceleration, CT often normal, MRI shows petechial hemorrhages in corpus callosum and brainstem

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**GCS — "Eye 4, Verbal 5, Motor 6":**
- **E**ye: 4-Spontaneous, 3-Voice, 2-Pain, 1-None
- **V**erbal: 5-Oriented, 4-Confused, 3-Words, 2-Sounds, 1-None
- **M**otor: 6-Obeys, 5-Localizes, 4-Withdrawal, 3-Flexion, 2-Extension, 1-None

**Base of skull fracture — "BORED":**
- **B**attle's sign
- **O**torrhoea (CSF)
- **R**accoon eyes
- **E**cchymosis (periorbital)
- **D**eafness (CN VII/VIII)

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **Most common cause of EDH** → Temporal bone fracture → Middle meningeal artery
2. **Talk and die** → Classic EDH with lucid interval
3. **Most common traumatic intracranial lesion** → Cerebral contusion
4. **Chronic SDH CT appearance** → Hypodense crescent, treated by **burr hole drainage**
5. **Pupil dilation (unilateral)** → Ipsilateral CN III compression (uncal herniation)
6. **Kernohan's notch phenomenon** → Ipsilateral hemiplegia (false localizing sign)
7. **CT indication in mild head injury** → GCS <15 at 2h, suspected skull fracture, vomiting ≥2, age >65, coagulopathy, amnesia >30 min
8. **Brain death**: GCS 3, absent brainstem reflexes, no respiratory effort on apnea test
