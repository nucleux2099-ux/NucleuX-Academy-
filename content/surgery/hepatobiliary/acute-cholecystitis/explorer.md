# Acute Cholecystitis — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Acute gallbladder inflammation, usually due to cystic duct obstruction, requiring timed source control decisions.

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Sepsis or gangrenous/perforation suspicion
- Persistent pain/fever with worsening inflammatory markers
- High-risk physiology in elderly/comorbid patients
- Empyema or pericholecystic collection signs

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Clinical pattern + RUQ findings + inflammatory response
2. Ultrasound first-line for wall thickening, stones, sonographic Murphy sign
3. Severity grading to guide early surgery vs temporizing strategy
4. Cross-sectional imaging when complications suspected

## Management ladder
1. Resuscitation + analgesia + antibiotics + risk optimization
2. Early laparoscopic cholecystectomy in suitable candidates
3. Percutaneous cholecystostomy as bridge in selected high-risk unstable patients
4. Interval definitive surgery planning after initial control when needed

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite first-line protocolized care.
- Inability to achieve source control in expected timeline.
- Structural complexity requiring higher-center HPB/endoscopy support.
- Need for ICU/oncology/MDT integrated management.
