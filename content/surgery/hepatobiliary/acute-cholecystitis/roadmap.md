## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Acute Cholecystitis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Calot triangle — cystic artery | — | Safe cholecystectomy requires identifying the critical view of safety in Calot's triangle — cystic artery and cystic duct clearly seen |
| Pathology | Acute cholecystitis pathology | — | Stone impaction → gallbladder distension → mucosal injury → secondary infection — understanding the sequence guides timing of surgery |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply the Tokyo Guidelines severity grading (Grade I-III) for acute cholecystitis
- Achieve the critical view of safety during laparoscopic cholecystectomy
- Determine timing of cholecystectomy — early (<72h) vs interval vs emergency

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Radiology | USG: Murphy sign, pericholecystic fluid | — | Point-of-care USG showing gallbladder wall thickening (>3mm), pericholecystic fluid confirms diagnosis |
| Microbiology | E. coli, Klebsiella in biliary sepsis | — | Gram-negative organisms dominate biliary infections — guides empirical antibiotic selection (piperacillin-tazobactam) |

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Acute cholecystitis integrates anatomy (Calot's triangle, critical view of safety), pathology (inflammatory cascade), microbiology (biliary pathogens), radiology (USG diagnosis), and surgery (laparoscopic cholecystectomy). The Tokyo Guidelines represent how clinical grading systems translate basic science into management algorithms.
