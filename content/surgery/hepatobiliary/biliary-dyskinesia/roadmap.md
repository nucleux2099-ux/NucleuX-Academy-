## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Define biliary dyskinesia and its diagnostic criteria (Rome IV, HIDA scan EF <35%)
- Differentiate acalculous biliary pain from functional dyspepsia and other causes
- Evaluate the evidence for cholecystectomy in biliary dyskinesia — patient selection predicts outcomes

### Prerequisites

- **Cholelithiasis** — Biliary dyskinesia is considered when typical biliary symptoms occur without gallstones

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Biliary dyskinesia bridges the gap between functional GI disorders and surgical pathology — integrating GI physiology (gallbladder motility, sphincter of Oddi function), radiology (HIDA scan), and evidence-based surgery (carefully selected cholecystectomy). It's a reminder that not all surgical diseases have visible pathology.
