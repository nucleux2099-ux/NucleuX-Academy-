# Cholangitis — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Acute biliary infection with obstruction; severity can progress rapidly to sepsis and organ dysfunction.

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Hypotension or lactate rise
- Altered mentation/septic encephalopathy
- Oliguria/renal dysfunction
- Persistent fever+jaundice despite antibiotics

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Clinical syndrome framing (Charcot/Reynolds context)
2. Labs: cholestatic pattern + inflammatory markers + organ dysfunction panel
3. Ultrasound first, then MRCP/EUS/CT as needed for obstruction mapping
4. Severity grading to determine decompression urgency

## Management ladder
1. Immediate sepsis bundle + source-oriented antibiotics
2. Early biliary decompression (usually ERCP) in moderate/severe disease
3. Escalate to PTBD/surgical drainage if endoscopic route not feasible
4. Definitive stone/stricture/cause management after stabilization

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite first-line protocolized care.
- Inability to achieve source control in expected timeline.
- Structural complexity requiring higher-center HPB/endoscopy support.
- Need for ICU/oncology/MDT integrated management.
