## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Portal Hypertension:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Portal venous system & portosystemic anastomoses | — | The 4 sites of portosystemic anastomosis (esophageal, rectal, umbilical, retroperitoneal) explain the clinical manifestations |
| Anatomy | Sinusoidal architecture | — | Hepatic sinusoidal structure determines whether portal hypertension is pre-sinusoidal, sinusoidal, or post-sinusoidal |
| Physiology | Hepatic sinusoidal pressure | — | Portal pressure gradient >10 mmHg is clinically significant; >12 mmHg causes variceal bleeding |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify portal hypertension by site — pre-hepatic, hepatic (pre/sinusoidal/post), post-hepatic
- Manage acute variceal bleeding — resuscitation, terlipressin, EVL, Sengstaken tube, TIPS
- Apply primary and secondary prophylaxis strategies for variceal hemorrhage

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pathology | Cirrhosis causing portal HTN | — | Cirrhosis is the most common cause — architectural distortion increases sinusoidal resistance |
| Pharmacology | Beta-blockers (propranolol) for variceal prophylaxis | — | Non-selective beta-blockers reduce portal pressure by decreasing cardiac output and splanchnic flow |
| Radiology | Doppler USG portal vein, CT: varices, splenomegaly | — | Doppler measures portal vein velocity and direction — hepatofugal flow indicates advanced disease |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Upper GI Bleeding** — Variceal bleeding is a life-threatening complication requiring emergency management
2. **Spleen — Surgical Conditions** — Splenomegaly and hypersplenism from portal hypertension may need splenectomy

### Prerequisites

- **Liver Anatomy** — Understanding hepatic sinusoidal and portal venous anatomy is essential

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Portal hypertension connects hepatic anatomy (portal vein, portosystemic anastomoses), liver pathology (cirrhosis), pharmacology (beta-blockers, terlipressin, octreotide), and interventional radiology (TIPS). Understanding the hemodynamics explains every clinical feature — varices, ascites, splenomegaly, hepatic encephalopathy.
