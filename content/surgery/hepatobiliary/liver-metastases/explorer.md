# Liver Metastases — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Secondary liver deposits (commonly colorectal) where biology + distribution + reserve determine resectability and survival benefit.

## Curriculum Position
- **Track:** MCh Surgical Gastroenterology
- **Primary curriculum source:** `MCh-SGE-NMC-Curriculum-2021.polished.md`
- **Competency lens:** resectability logic + escalation safety + MDT integration

## First-principles map (ATOM)
1. **Mechanism:** disease biology and organ failure drivers
2. **Pattern:** syndrome/stage presentation archetype
3. **Signal:** investigations that change pathway assignment
4. **Action:** first definitive lane selection
5. **Escalation:** when to shift to advanced or rescue pathway

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Rapidly progressive bilobar disease
- Insufficient future liver remnant risk
- Uncontrolled extrahepatic disease
- Poor response/intolerance to systemic therapy

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Primary tumor biology + staging integration
2. Triphasic imaging + lesion burden mapping
3. Resectability assessment with FLR planning
4. Oncology-HPB MDT sequencing decision

## Management ladder
1. Resectable pathway with oncologic principles
2. Borderline disease: conversion strategy with systemic therapy
3. Ablation/combined approaches for selected patterns
4. Unresectable disease: systemic/palliative pathway with reassessment loops

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite protocolized initial care.
- Change in resectability/operability class on interval reassessment.
- Need for advanced procedural/critical-care support.
- Mandatory MDT takeover for complex sequencing decisions.
