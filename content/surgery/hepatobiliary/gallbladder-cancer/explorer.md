# Gallbladder Cancer — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Aggressive HPB malignancy where stage-specific resectability and oncologic strategy determine survival outcomes.

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Obstructive jaundice with mass
- Rapid weight loss with advanced local disease signs
- Vascular/biliary involvement suggesting unresectability
- Metastatic suspicion at presentation

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Imaging staging pathway (US/CT/MRI ± MRCP) with resectability intent
2. Define T-stage depth and liver/duct/vascular extension
3. Rule out distant disease before radical plan
4. Histology strategy based on resectability context and MDT plan

## Management ladder
1. Stage-wise oncologic pathway: curative resection vs palliative intent
2. Extent of surgery based on depth/spread and nodal status
3. Biliary drainage and symptom control in advanced disease
4. Mandatory MDT integration (HPB surgery, oncology, radiology, pathology)

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite first-line protocolized care.
- Inability to achieve source control in expected timeline.
- Structural complexity requiring higher-center HPB/endoscopy support.
- Need for ICU/oncology/MDT integrated management.
