## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Hepatocellular Carcinoma:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Couinaud segments for resection planning | — | Liver resection follows Couinaud segmental anatomy — each segment has independent portal pedicle and hepatic vein drainage |
| Pathology | Chronic hepatitis & cirrhosis → HCC | — | 90% of HCC occurs in cirrhotic livers — understanding the cirrhosis-HCC pathway is fundamental |
| Pathology | Alcoholic cirrhosis → HCC | — | Alcohol-related liver disease is a major risk factor in India — cirrhosis of any cause increases HCC risk |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply BCLC staging to determine treatment strategy — resection, transplant, ablation, TACE, systemic
- Interpret LI-RADS criteria for non-invasive HCC diagnosis on CT/MRI
- Evaluate resectability using future liver remnant volume and portal hypertension assessment

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Microbiology | HBV as HCC risk factor | — | Hepatitis B can cause HCC even without cirrhosis — the only liver cancer with a directly carcinogenic virus |
| Radiology | CT: arterial enhancement & venous washout (LI-RADS) | — | LI-RADS 5 (arterial hyperenhancement + washout) is diagnostic without biopsy — unique in oncology |
| Pharmacology | Sorafenib, atezolizumab+bevacizumab | — | Systemic therapy for unresectable HCC has transformed outcomes — immunotherapy is the new first-line |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Liver Transplant Indications** — Milan criteria determine transplant eligibility for HCC in cirrhosis
2. **Liver Metastases** — Differentiate primary HCC from metastatic liver disease

### Prerequisites

- **Liver Anatomy** — Couinaud segmental anatomy is the foundation of liver surgery
- **Portal Hypertension** — Portal hypertension from cirrhosis complicates HCC management

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

HCC integrates hepatic anatomy (Couinaud segments), pathology (chronic liver disease, viral hepatitis), microbiology (HBV, HCV), radiology (LI-RADS), pharmacology (immunotherapy), and surgery (resection, transplant). It's the only major cancer diagnosed radiologically without biopsy — making LI-RADS knowledge essential.
