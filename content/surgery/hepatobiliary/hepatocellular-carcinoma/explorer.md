# Hepatocellular Carcinoma — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Primary liver malignancy usually on chronic liver disease background, requiring stage-linked curative vs non-curative pathway decisions.

## Curriculum Position
- **Track:** MCh Surgical Gastroenterology
- **Primary curriculum source:** `MCh-SGE-NMC-Curriculum-2021.polished.md`
- **Competency lens:** resectability logic + escalation safety + MDT integration

## First-principles map (ATOM)
1. **Mechanism:** disease biology and organ failure drivers
2. **Pattern:** syndrome/stage presentation archetype
3. **Signal:** investigations that change pathway assignment
4. **Action:** first definitive lane selection
5. **Escalation:** when to shift to advanced or rescue pathway

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Jaundice/decompensation with tumor burden
- Macrovascular invasion suspicion
- Extrahepatic spread suspicion
- Rapid liver function decline limiting curative options

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Dynamic imaging-based diagnosis in appropriate context
2. Assess liver reserve + performance status with tumor stage
3. Portal invasion/metastatic workup before curative planning
4. MDT decision for resection vs transplant vs locoregional/systemic

## Management ladder
1. Map strategy to stage + liver reserve + PS
2. Curative lane: resection/transplant/ablation where eligible
3. Non-curative lane: locoregional/systemic sequencing
4. Palliative/supportive optimization with symptom burden control

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite protocolized initial care.
- Change in resectability/operability class on interval reassessment.
- Need for advanced procedural/critical-care support.
- Mandatory MDT takeover for complex sequencing decisions.
