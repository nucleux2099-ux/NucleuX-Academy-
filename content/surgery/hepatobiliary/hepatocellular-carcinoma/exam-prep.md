# Hepatocellular Carcinoma — Exam Prep

## 60-second viva frame
1. Definition + stage/resectability class
2. Differential and emergency risk line
3. Investigation and staging sequence
4. First-line definitive lane
5. Escalation trigger + rescue fallback

## High-yield one-liners
- Exam-safe definition
- Most dangerous miss
- Best first staging/action test by scenario
- Timing principle for intervention
- Follow-up/prognostic line

## Must-know tables to fill
- Etiology/biology risk profile
- Stage/resectability/severity criteria
- Investigation order
- Management lanes by stage/physiology
- Complications and rescue options

## Traps to avoid
- Over-focusing on anatomy while ignoring physiology.
- Delaying source control in unstable patients.
- Misclassifying borderline/resectable categories.
- Ignoring MDT requirement in complex HPB/oncology cases.

## Short-note prompts
- Pathophysiology and natural history
- Unstable patient decision algorithm
- Role of surgery/endoscopy/radiology/oncology
- Outcome optimization and surveillance

## Rapid recall drill
- Q: immediate red-flag action?
- Q: threshold for emergent OR/ICU/MDT?
- Q: what changes lane from curative to non-curative?
