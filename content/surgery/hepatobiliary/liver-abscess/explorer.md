# Liver Abscess — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Localized suppurative liver infection (pyogenic/amoebic/other) requiring etiology-specific antimicrobials ± drainage.

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Sepsis/shock physiology
- Large or multiloculated abscess with failure on antibiotics
- Impending rupture features
- Persistent fever despite targeted therapy

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Clinical triad context (fever, pain, hepatomegaly) not mandatory
2. Labs + blood cultures + liver panel
3. US/CT for size, location, complexity, and drainage planning
4. Differentiate pyogenic vs amoebic vs mixed possibilities early

## Management ladder
1. Early empiric antimicrobials then narrow by culture/clinical response
2. Percutaneous aspiration/catheter drainage by size and complexity criteria
3. Monitor response with clinical + lab + interval imaging checkpoints
4. Investigate underlying source (biliary/portal/cryptogenic) to prevent recurrence

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite first-line protocolized care.
- Inability to achieve source control in expected timeline.
- Structural complexity requiring higher-center HPB/endoscopy support.
- Need for ICU/oncology/MDT integrated management.
