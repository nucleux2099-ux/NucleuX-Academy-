## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Spleen — Surgical Conditions:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Splenic artery & vein anatomy | — | The splenic artery arises from the celiac trunk; its tortuous course along the superior pancreatic border is surgically important |
| Physiology | Splenic immune function | — | The spleen filters encapsulated bacteria — post-splenectomy patients are at lifelong risk of OPSI (overwhelming post-splenectomy infection) |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- List the indications for splenectomy — traumatic, hematological, and oncological
- Describe the principles of OPSI prevention — vaccination, antibiotic prophylaxis, patient education
- Manage splenic trauma — AAST grading, non-operative management criteria, splenic artery embolization

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Microbiology | Post-splenectomy vaccination (pneumococcal, meningococcal) | — | Vaccination 2 weeks before elective splenectomy is mandatory — pneumococcus is the most dangerous organism |
| Pathology | Hereditary spherocytosis — splenectomy indication | — | The spleen destroys spherocytes — splenectomy is curative for symptomatic hereditary spherocytosis |

## ➡️ What's Next

*This is an advanced/terminal topic — review and consolidate your knowledge.*

### Prerequisites

- **Portal Hypertension** — Congestive splenomegaly from portal hypertension is a common indication for splenic surgery

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Splenic surgery integrates anatomy (splenic hilum, short gastric vessels), immunology (splenic filtration, OPSI risk), hematology (indications for splenectomy), and trauma surgery (grading and non-operative management). The shift toward splenic preservation in trauma reflects the growing appreciation of splenic immune function.
