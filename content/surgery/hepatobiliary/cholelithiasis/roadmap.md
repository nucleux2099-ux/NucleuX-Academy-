## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Cholelithiasis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Biliary anatomy — Calot triangle | — | Understanding Calot's triangle is essential for safe cholecystectomy — the commonest elective abdominal surgery |
| Physiology | Bile composition & cholesterol metabolism | — | Gallstones form when bile becomes supersaturated with cholesterol or bilirubin — the lipid triad theory |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify gallstones by composition (cholesterol, pigment, mixed) and their risk factors
- Describe the natural history of gallstones — most are asymptomatic; identify indications for surgery
- Perform safe laparoscopic cholecystectomy — critical view of safety, Strasberg criteria

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Radiology | USG: gallstones, wall thickening | — | Transabdominal USG is 95%+ sensitive for gallstones — first-line investigation |
| Pathology | Chronic cholecystitis pathology | — | Recurrent inflammation leads to fibrotic, contracted gallbladder — Rokitansky-Aschoff sinuses are pathognomonic |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Acute Cholecystitis** — Stone impaction in cystic duct causes acute cholecystitis
2. **Cholecystitis** — Chronic cholecystitis from recurrent stone impaction
3. **Choledocholithiasis** — Stones migrate from gallbladder to CBD
4. **Gallbladder Cancer** — Porcelain gallbladder and large stones are risk factors

### Prerequisites

- **Liver Anatomy** — Biliary tree anatomy and bile physiology provide the foundation

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Cholelithiasis is the gateway to all biliary surgery — connecting hepatic physiology (bile composition), pathology (stone formation, chronic inflammation), radiology (USG diagnosis), and surgery (laparoscopic cholecystectomy). Understanding the pathogenesis of gallstone formation explains why certain populations (4F: female, forty, fertile, fat) are at risk.
