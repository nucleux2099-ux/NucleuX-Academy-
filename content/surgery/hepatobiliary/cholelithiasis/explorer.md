# 🔍 Cholelithiasis & Cholecystitis — Explorer Mode

> **The Big Picture:** Gallstones are incredibly common (10-15% population). Most are silent. When they cause symptoms, cholecystectomy is the definitive treatment. Lap chole is one of the most performed surgeries worldwide.

## 🎨 Types of Gallstones

| Type | Composition | Frequency | Risk Factors |
|------|-------------|-----------|--------------|
| **Cholesterol** | >50% cholesterol | 75-80% | 4F: Fat, Female, Forty, Fertile |
| **Pigment (Black)** | Calcium bilirubinate | 15-20% | Hemolysis, cirrhosis |
| **Pigment (Brown)** | Calcium bilirubinate + bacteria | 5% | Biliary infection, stasis |
| **Mixed** | Cholesterol + pigment | Common | Various |

## 💡 Key Atomic Concepts

### Atom 1: The 4 F's (Cholesterol Stones)
> **F**at, **F**emale, **F**orty, **F**ertile — Classic risk factors for cholesterol gallstones. Estrogen increases cholesterol secretion into bile.

### Atom 2: Murphy's Sign
> Deep inspiration during RUQ palpation causes the inflamed gallbladder to descend onto the examiner's fingers, causing pain and inspiratory arrest. Pathognomonic for acute cholecystitis.

### Atom 3: Charcot's Triad → Reynolds' Pentad
> **Charcot's:** Fever + Jaundice + RUQ pain = Cholangitis
> **Reynolds':** Add confusion + shock = Severe/suppurative cholangitis (EMERGENCY)

```
BILIARY COLIC                    ACUTE CHOLECYSTITIS
──────────────                   ────────────────────
• Episodic RUQ pain              • Persistent RUQ pain (>6 hrs)
• After fatty meals              • Fever
• Lasts 30 min-6 hrs             • Murphy's sign POSITIVE
• Resolves spontaneously         • Elevated WBC
• No fever                       • Tender, palpable gallbladder
• No Murphy's sign               • May have mild LFT elevation
```
