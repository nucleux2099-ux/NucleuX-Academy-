# Choledocholithiasis — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Presence of stones in common bile duct causing biliary obstruction, cholestasis, pain, pancreatitis, or cholangitis.

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Cholangitis physiology
- Progressive jaundice with sepsis signs
- Pancreatitis with persistent obstruction
- Failed biliary drainage

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Risk stratify probability (low/intermediate/high) before invasive testing
2. LFT trend + cholestatic markers
3. US then EUS/MRCP for confirmation in intermediate-risk group
4. ERCP reserved as therapeutic first strategy in high-probability cases

## Management ladder
1. Stabilize and treat infection if present
2. Therapeutic ERCP + clearance strategy when indicated
3. Plan cholecystectomy timing after duct clearance in gallstone disease
4. Use alternative drainage/surgical pathway if ERCP fails/unavailable

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite first-line protocolized care.
- Inability to achieve source control in expected timeline.
- Structural complexity requiring higher-center HPB/endoscopy support.
- Need for ICU/oncology/MDT integrated management.
