## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Choledocholithiasis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | CBD anatomy, ampulla of Vater | — | CBD stones impact at the ampulla — understanding the anatomy explains jaundice, pancreatitis, and cholangitis |
| Anatomy | Duodenal relations of CBD | — | The intrapancreatic portion of the CBD is where stones commonly impact |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply the ASGE risk stratification for choledocholithiasis (low, intermediate, high risk)
- Select the appropriate investigation — MRCP for intermediate risk, direct ERCP for high risk
- Plan management — ERCP with sphincterotomy, laparoscopic CBD exploration, or combined approach

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Radiology | MRCP, EUS for CBD stones | — | MRCP is non-invasive and highly sensitive; EUS can detect small stones ERCP misses |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Obstructive Jaundice** — CBD stones are the most common benign cause of obstructive jaundice
2. **Cholangitis** — Infected bile proximal to an obstructing stone causes ascending cholangitis

### Prerequisites

- **Cholelithiasis** — CBD stones usually originate from the gallbladder — secondary stones

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Choledocholithiasis integrates hepatobiliary anatomy (CBD, ampulla), radiology (MRCP, EUS, ERCP), and surgical decision-making (ERCP + LC vs single-stage laparoscopic approach). The risk stratification approach exemplifies evidence-based surgical practice — avoiding unnecessary ERCP in low-risk patients.
