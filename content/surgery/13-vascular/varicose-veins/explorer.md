# 🔍 Varicose Veins — Explorer Mode

> **The Big Picture:** Dilated, tortuous superficial veins. Valve incompetence is the key. Most are primary (no identifiable cause). Treatment ranges from conservative to surgical ablation.

---

## 🧬 Core Concept

```
         NORMAL VENOUS RETURN
                  │
         Valves prevent reflux
         Deep → Superficial protected
                  │
                  ▼ VALVE FAILURE
                  │
         VENOUS HYPERTENSION
                  │
         Vein wall dilation
         More valve failure (vicious cycle)
                  │
                  ▼
         VARICOSE VEINS
                  │
    ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
    ▼                           ▼
 SYMPTOMS                  COMPLICATIONS
 • Aching                  • Skin changes
 • Heaviness               • Ulceration
 • Cosmetic                • Bleeding
                           • Thrombophlebitis
```

---

## 💡 Key Atomic Concepts

### Atom 1: Trendelenburg Test
> Elevate leg → Empty veins → Apply tourniquet at SFJ → Stand patient
> Veins fill from ABOVE tourniquet = SFJ incompetence
> Veins fill from BELOW = perforator incompetence

### Atom 2: CEAP Classification
> **C**linical (C0-C6), **E**tiological (Primary/Secondary), **A**natomical (Superficial/Deep/Perforator), **P**athophysiological (Reflux/Obstruction)

### Atom 3: Sapheno-Femoral Junction
> The key point in GSV incompetence. Located 4 cm below and lateral to pubic tubercle. Flush ligation here is essential.

---

## 📊 Treatment Options

| CEAP | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| C1 (spider veins) | Sclerotherapy |
| C2 (varicose) | EVLA, RFA, Surgery |
| C3-4 (edema, skin changes) | Compression + Ablation |
| C5-6 (healed/active ulcer) | Compression + Treat reflux |
