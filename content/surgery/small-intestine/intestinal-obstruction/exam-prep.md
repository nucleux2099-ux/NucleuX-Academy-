# 📋 Intestinal Obstruction — Exam Prep Mode

---

## 🎯 One-Liners

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Most common cause of SBO | Adhesions (previous surgery) |
| Most common cause of LBO | Colorectal cancer |
| Classic triad of intestinal obstruction | Colicky pain + Vomiting + Constipation + Distension |
| Absolute constipation means | No flatus AND no feces |
| Rule of 3-6-9 | SB >3cm, LB >6cm, Cecum >9cm = dangerous |
| Closed loop obstruction | Two points blocked → High strangulation risk |
| Most useful initial investigation | Plain X-ray abdomen (erect + supine) |
| Coffee bean sign seen in | Sigmoid volvulus |
| Bird's beak sign seen in | Sigmoid volvulus (on contrast enema) |
| Step ladder pattern seen in | Small bowel obstruction |

---

## 📝 Clinical Features

**Cardinal symptoms:**
1. Colicky abdominal pain
2. Vomiting (early in SBO, late in LBO)
3. Abdominal distension
4. Absolute constipation (complete obstruction)

**Signs suggesting STRANGULATION:**
- Continuous (not colicky) pain
- Fever
- Tachycardia
- Localized tenderness/peritonism
- Absent bowel sounds
- Blood in rectum
- Elevated lactate

---

## 🔬 Investigations

| Investigation | Findings |
|---------------|----------|
| **X-ray erect** | Air-fluid levels (>5 = significant) |
| **X-ray supine** | Dilated loops, valvulae/haustra |
| **CT abdomen** | Transition point, cause, strangulation signs |
| **Bloods** | WBC↑, Lactate↑, K+ deranged |
| **Contrast enema** | LBO cause, volvulus reduction |

---

## 📊 SBO vs LBO Comparison

| Feature | SBO | LBO |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Vomiting | Early, bilious | Late, feculent |
| Distension | Central, moderate | Peripheral, marked |
| Pain | Umbilical | Lower abdominal |
| X-ray loops | Central, <3cm normal | Peripheral, >6cm |
| Valvulae | Complete across | Haustra incomplete |
| Cause | Adhesions | Cancer |

## 💊 Management Algorithm

```
SUSPECTED OBSTRUCTION
         │
         ▼
    RESUSCITATE
    (IV fluids, NGT, catheter, analgesia)
         │
         ▼
    INVESTIGATE
    (X-ray → CT if needed)
         │
    ┌────┴────┐
    ▼         ▼
 SIMPLE    COMPLICATED
    │      (Strangulation/
    │       Closed loop)
    │         │
    ▼         ▼
CONSERVATIVE  EMERGENCY
(Drip & suck)  SURGERY
48-72 hrs
    │
    ▼
Resolving? → Continue
Not resolving? → Surgery
```

---

## ⚠️ Indications for Emergency Surgery

- Signs of strangulation
- Closed loop obstruction
- Complete LBO with competent ileocecal valve
- Cecum >12 cm
- Failure of conservative management (48-72 hrs)
- Peritonitis
