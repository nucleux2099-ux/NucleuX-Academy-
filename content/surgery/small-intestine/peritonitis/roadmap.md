## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Peritonitis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Peritoneum & peritoneal cavity | — | The peritoneum is a vast serous membrane — its rich blood supply and large surface area amplify the inflammatory response |
| Pathology | Peritoneal inflammatory response | — | Peritonitis triggers massive cytokine release, third-spacing, and sepsis — understanding this guides aggressive resuscitation |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify peritonitis — primary (SBP), secondary (perforation), tertiary (persistent nosocomial)
- Apply the Mannheim Peritonitis Index for prognosis and surgical planning
- Describe surgical principles — source control (repair, resection, diversion), lavage, damage control

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |

### Prerequisites

- **Acute Appendicitis** — Perforated appendicitis is a common cause of peritonitis
- **Peptic Ulcer Disease** — Perforated peptic ulcer causes chemical then bacterial peritonitis

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Peritonitis is the intra-abdominal emergency where pathology (inflammation, sepsis), microbiology (polymicrobial infection), surgery (source control), and critical care (resuscitation, organ support) converge. The concept of source control — removing the cause of contamination — is the fundamental surgical principle that makes peritonitis treatable.
