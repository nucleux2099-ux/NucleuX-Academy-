## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Acute Appendicitis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Appendix anatomy — McBurney's point, appendicular artery | AN47.5 | McBurney's point localizes the base of the appendix; the appendicular artery (branch of ileocolic) must be ligated during appendicectomy |
| Anatomy | Anterior abdominal wall landmarks | — | Surface anatomy landmarks guide the Lanz/McBurney incision and laparoscopic port placement |
| Pathology | Acute suppurative inflammation | — | Progression from mucosal ulceration → transmural inflammation → perforation → peritonitis explains clinical escalation |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply Alvarado score and clinical features to diagnose acute appendicitis
- Manage the spectrum — early uncomplicated, perforated, appendiceal mass, appendiceal abscess
- Describe laparoscopic and open appendicectomy technique and post-operative complications

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Acute appendicitis is the most common surgical emergency — integrating anatomy (McBurney's point, variable positions), pathology (inflammation-perforation sequence), microbiology (empirical antibiotics), and radiology (USG/CT diagnosis). The debate between surgery vs antibiotics for uncomplicated appendicitis reflects the evolution of evidence-based surgical practice.
