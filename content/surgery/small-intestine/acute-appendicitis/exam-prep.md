# 📋 Acute Appendicitis — Exam Prep Mode

> **High-yield facts for NEET-PG, INICET, and university exams**

---

## 🎯 One-Liners (Most Asked)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Most common surgical emergency | Acute appendicitis |
| Most common position of appendix | Retrocaecal (65%) |
| Blood supply of appendix | Appendicular artery (branch of ileocolic) |
| McBurney's point location | Junction of lateral 1/3 & medial 2/3 of line from ASIS to umbilicus |
| Pathognomonic symptom | Pain migration (periumbilical → RIF) |
| Most reliable sign | Tenderness at McBurney's point |
| When NOT to operate | Appendicular mass >48 hrs (Ochsner-Sherren regimen) |
| Gold standard investigation | CT abdomen (sensitivity >95%) |
| Preferred surgery | Laparoscopic appendicectomy |
| Antibiotic of choice | Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole |

---

## 📝 MANTRELS Mnemonic (Alvarado Score)

| Letter | Symptom/Sign | Points |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| **M** | Migration of pain to RIF | 1 |
| **A** | Anorexia | 1 |
| **N** | Nausea/Vomiting | 1 |
| **T** | Tenderness in RIF | 2 |
| **R** | Rebound tenderness | 1 |
| **E** | Elevated temperature (>37.3°C) | 1 |
| **L** | Leukocytosis (>10,000) | 2 |
| **S** | Shift to left (>75% neutrophils) | 1 |

**Interpretation:**
- 1-4: Appendicitis unlikely
- 5-6: Possible, observe/CT
- **7-10: Probable, operate**

## 🔬 Classical Signs (Exam Favorites)

| Sign | Description | Indicates |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **McBurney's sign** | Tenderness at McBurney's point | Acute appendicitis |
| **Rovsing's sign** | RIF pain on LIF palpation | Peritoneal irritation |
| **Psoas sign** | Pain on right hip extension | Retrocaecal appendix |
| **Obturator sign** | Pain on internal rotation of flexed hip | Pelvic appendix |
| **Dunphy's sign** | Increased pain on coughing | Peritonitis |
| **Pointing sign** | Patient points to exact location | Localised peritonitis |
| **Sherren's triangle** | Hyperesthesia in RIF | Appendicitis |

## ⚠️ Complications (PGAP)

- **P**erforation (most common, 20-30%)
- **G**angrene
- **A**bscess (pelvic, subphrenic)
- **P**ylephlebitis (portal vein thrombosis) - rare but deadly

### Post-operative Complications
- Wound infection (most common)
- Intra-abdominal abscess
- Fecal fistula (caecal injury)
- Adhesive obstruction

---

## 🔄 Special Scenarios

### Appendicular Mass
- **Definition:** Inflamed appendix + omentum + loops of bowel
- **Timing:** Forms after 48-72 hours
- **Management:** Ochsner-Sherren regimen → Interval appendicectomy (6-8 weeks)

### Appendicular Abscess
- **Management:** USG/CT-guided drainage → Interval appendicectomy

### Appendicitis in Pregnancy
- **Most common surgical emergency in pregnancy**
- **Position:** Appendix displaced upward by uterus
- **Investigation:** USG first, MRI if inconclusive (avoid CT)
- **Surgery:** Laparoscopic safe in all trimesters

### Appendicitis in Elderly
- **Atypical presentation common**
- **Higher perforation rate (>50%)**
- **Higher mortality**

## 💊 Antibiotic Protocol

| Situation | Regimen |
|-----------|---------|
| Uncomplicated | Single dose pre-op (Ceftriaxone 1g) |
| Complicated (perforation/abscess) | Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole × 5-7 days |
| Penicillin allergy | Ciprofloxacin + Metronidazole |
