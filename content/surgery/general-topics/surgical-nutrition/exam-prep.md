| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Prevalence of malnutrition in surgical patients? | **30–50%** |

## 📝 Mnemonics

### REFEEDING — Refeeding Syndrome Features
- **R**apid insulin surge
- **E**lectrolyte shifts (PO₄³⁻, K⁺, Mg²⁺ intracellularly)
- **F**luid overload
- **E**dema
- **E**ncephalopathy (Wernicke's — give thiamine!)
- **D**eath from cardiac arrhythmia
- **I**ntake was nil for >5 days
- **N**utrition started too fast
- **G**lucose administration triggers it

### FEED — When to Use Enteral Nutrition
- **F**unctional GI tract
- **E**arly (within 24–48h)
- **E**conomical and safe
- **D**efends gut barrier

## 🔬 Quick Comparisons

| Feature | Enteral Nutrition | Parenteral Nutrition |
|---------|------------------|---------------------|
| Gut barrier | Maintained | Atrophies |
| Cost | Low | 5–10× higher |
| Infection risk | Lower | Higher (line sepsis) |
| Metabolic complications | Fewer | More (hyperglycaemia, liver) |
| Immune function | Preserved | Impaired |
| Preferred when | Gut works | Gut non-functional |

| Marker | Half-life | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Albumin | 20 days | Chronic status / prognosis |
| Transferrin | 8 days | Intermediate monitoring |
| Pre-albumin | 2 days | Short-term response to feeding |
| RBP | 12 hours | Ultra-short (rarely used) |

## ⚠️ Examiner Traps & Common Mistakes

1. **Trap:** Low albumin post-op = malnutrition → give albumin
   **Reality:** Albumin is a **negative acute-phase protein**. Falls due to inflammation. Do NOT infuse albumin for nutritional purposes.

2. **Trap:** Acute pancreatitis needs bowel rest + TPN
   **Reality:** **Enteral nutrition (nasojejunal)** is preferred. EN reduces infections and mortality compared to TPN.

3. **Trap:** Start full-calorie feeds immediately in malnourished patients
   **Reality:** Risk of **refeeding syndrome**. Start at 10–20 kcal/kg/day, advance over 3–5 days. Give **thiamine before feeds**.

4. **Trap:** Normal Saline is the best resuscitation fluid
   **Reality:** RL/balanced crystalloids are preferred. NS in large volumes causes **hyperchloraemic metabolic acidosis** (SMART trial 2018).

## 🏆 Key Classifications

### Types of Enteral Feeds
1. **Polymeric** — Intact protein (standard patients)
2. **Semi-elemental** — Peptides + MCT (impaired absorption)
3. **Elemental** — Free amino acids (severe malabsorption)
4. **Disease-specific** — Diabetic, renal, hepatic formulas
5. **Immunonutrition** — Arginine, omega-3, glutamine (perioperative)

### Access Routes
- **Short-term (<4 weeks):** Nasogastric or nasojejunal tube
- **Long-term (>4 weeks):** PEG (percutaneous endoscopic gastrostomy) or feeding jejunostomy
