## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Surgical Nutrition:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Carbohydrate absorption | — | Enteral nutrition requires functional absorption — understanding nutrient absorption guides feeding route selection |
| Physiology | Protein absorption & metabolism | — | Protein requirements increase dramatically after surgery (catabolic state) — essential for wound healing |
| Physiology | Pancreatic enzyme secretion | — | Pancreatic insufficiency after surgery/disease causes malabsorption — requires enzyme replacement |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Assess nutritional status using clinical (SGA, albumin, prealbumin) and anthropometric parameters
- Compare enteral vs parenteral nutrition — indications, complications, and evidence for early enteral feeding
- Calculate caloric and protein requirements for surgical patients (Harris-Benedict, stress factors)

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pharmacology | Pancreatic enzyme supplements (PERT) | — | Patients with pancreatic insufficiency need PERT — understanding the physiology explains the dose titration |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Perioperative Management** — Nutritional optimization is key to reducing post-operative complications
2. **Postgastrectomy Syndromes** — Nutritional deficiencies are major long-term complications after gastrectomy

### Prerequisites

- **Fluid & Electrolyte Balance** — Fluid management precedes nutritional support in the surgical patient

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Surgical nutrition connects GI physiology (absorption, gut barrier function), metabolic biochemistry (catabolism, nitrogen balance), pharmacology (TPN, micronutrient supplementation), and clinical surgery (enhanced recovery protocols). The paradigm shift toward early enteral feeding reflects understanding that the gut has immunological and barrier functions beyond mere digestion.
