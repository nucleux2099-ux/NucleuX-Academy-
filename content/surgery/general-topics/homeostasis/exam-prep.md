# Homeostasis & Metabolic Response — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU1.1 (Perioperative care), SU1.3 (Homeostasis)
> **High-yield facts for NEET-PG, INICET, and university exams**

---

## 🎯 One-Liners (Most Asked)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Who described ebb and flow phases? | **Sir David Cuthbertson** (1932) |
| What is the ebb phase? | Hypometabolic phase (first 24h) — ↓ metabolic rate, ↓ CO, ↓ temp |
| What is the flow phase? | Hypermetabolic/catabolic phase (day 2–7+) — ↑ metabolic rate, catabolism |
| Master stress hormone of surgery? | **Cortisol** — peaks at 4–6 hours post-surgery |
| Why does post-op hyperglycaemia occur? | ↑ Cortisol + ↑ catecholamines + **insulin resistance** (TNF-α, IL-6) |
| Post-op hyponatraemia mechanism? | **Dilutional** — excess ADH secretion → water retention |
| Treatment of post-op hyponatraemia? | **Fluid restriction** (NOT saline) |
| Why does albumin fall post-op? | Negative acute-phase protein + dilution + capillary leak |
| Best short-term nutritional marker? | **Pre-albumin (Transthyretin)** — t½ = 2 days |
| Primary cytokine driving CRP? | **IL-6** |
| What is SIRS-CARS balance? | Pro-inflammatory (SIRS) vs anti-inflammatory (CARS) — imbalance → organ failure or sepsis |
| What blocks afferent signals to hypothalamus? | **Epidural anaesthesia** |
| ERAS carbohydrate loading timing? | Maltodextrin drink **2 hours** before surgery |
| Target blood glucose in ICU? | **140–180 mg/dL** |
| Negative nitrogen balance cause? | Skeletal muscle proteolysis for gluconeogenesis |

## 📝 Mnemonics

### CUTHBERTSON — Metabolic Response Phases
- **C**atabolic flow phase follows ebb
- **U**rine output drops (ADH)
- **T**emperature drops in ebb phase
- **H**yperglycaemia (stress response)
- **B**reakdown of protein (negative N₂ balance)
- **E**nergy from fat (FFAs primary fuel in flow phase)
- **R**etention of Na⁺ and H₂O (aldosterone + ADH)
- **T**achycardia (sympathoadrenal)
- **S**IRS response activated
- **O**liguria early post-op
- **N**euroendocrine axis activated (HPA + sympathoadrenal)

### STRESS — Hormones Elevated After Surgery
- **S**ympathetic catecholamines
- **T**hyroxine (minor role)
- **R**enin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone
- **E**pinephrine & norepinephrine
- **S**omatotropin (GH)
- **S**teroid (Cortisol + ADH)

## 🔬 Quick Comparisons

| Feature | Ebb Phase | Flow Phase |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Duration | 0–24 hours | Days 2–7+ |
| Metabolic rate | ↓ Decreased | ↑ Increased |
| Cardiac output | ↓ | ↑ |
| O₂ consumption | ↓ | ↑ |
| Body temperature | ↓ | ↑ (may be febrile) |
| Dominant process | Shock resuscitation | Catabolism → Anabolism |

| Feature | Acute-Phase Proteins (Positive) | Negative Acute-Phase Proteins |
|---------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Examples | CRP, Fibrinogen, Ferritin, Haptoglobin | Albumin, Transferrin, Pre-albumin |
| Direction post-surgery | ↑↑ | ↓↓ |
| Clinical use | Inflammation monitoring | Nutritional assessment (pre-albumin best) |

## ⚠️ Examiner Traps & Common Mistakes

1. **Trap:** Post-op hyponatraemia = give normal saline
   **Reality:** It's **dilutional** (excess ADH). Treat with **fluid restriction**.

2. **Trap:** Low albumin post-op = malnutrition
   **Reality:** Albumin is a **negative acute-phase protein**. Falls acutely due to inflammation, NOT nutritional deficiency. Use **pre-albumin** for nutritional monitoring.

3. **Trap:** Post-op day 1 fever = infection
   **Reality:** Early fever (day 1–2) is usually **cytokine-mediated** (IL-1, IL-6). Infection-related fever typically appears from day 3 onwards.

4. **Trap:** Tight glucose control (<110 mg/dL) is best
   **Reality:** NICE-SUGAR trial showed target **140–180 mg/dL** is optimal. Too-tight control → dangerous **hypoglycaemia**.

## 🏆 Key Classifications

### Cuthbertson's Phases of Metabolic Response
- **Ebb phase** → Hypometabolic (first 24h)
- **Flow phase** → Catabolic (day 2–7) → Anabolic (day 7+)

### Moore's Four Phases of Surgical Convalescence
1. **Adrenergic-corticoid phase** (injury, day 0–3)
2. **Corticoid withdrawal phase** (turning point, day 3–7)
3. **Anabolic phase** (muscular strength, day 7–weeks)
4. **Fat gain phase** (full recovery, weeks–months)
