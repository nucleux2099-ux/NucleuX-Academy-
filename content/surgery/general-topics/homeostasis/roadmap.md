## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Homeostasis:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Homeostasis & feedback mechanisms | PY1.1 | Negative feedback loops maintain internal environment — surgical stress disrupts these, requiring corrective intervention |
| Physiology | Thermoregulation | — | Maintaining core temperature during surgery prevents coagulopathy and wound complications |
| Physiology | Neuroendocrine stress response | — | Surgery triggers the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis — cortisol, catecholamines, ADH surges affect fluid balance |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe the neuroendocrine response to surgical stress — HPA axis activation, catecholamine surge
- Explain feedback mechanisms that maintain temperature, pH, glucose, and fluid balance
- Identify how surgical interventions disrupt and restore homeostasis

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Homeostasis provides the conceptual framework for all of surgical physiology — from why we monitor vitals (detecting homeostatic failure) to why we resuscitate (restoring homeostasis). The surgical stress response connects endocrine physiology, fluid balance, and metabolic regulation into a unified picture of the body's response to injury.
