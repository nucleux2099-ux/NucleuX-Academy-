# 📋 Shock — Exam Prep Mode

> **High-yield facts for NEET-PG, INICET, and university exams**

## 🎯 One-Liners (Most Asked)

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Definition of shock | Inadequate tissue perfusion/oxygenation |
| Most common type of shock in surgical patients | Hypovolemic (hemorrhagic) |
| Most common type of shock in ICU | Septic shock |
| First sign of hemorrhagic shock | Tachycardia (NOT hypotension!) |
| Blood pressure falls in which class | Class III (>30% blood loss) |
| Shock Index formula | Heart Rate ÷ Systolic BP |
| Normal Shock Index | 0.5-0.7 |
| Shock Index indicating significant shock | >1.0 |
| Best marker of tissue perfusion | Lactate |
| Urine output goal in resuscitation | 0.5 mL/kg/hr (adults) |
| Fluid of choice in resuscitation | Crystalloids (RL or NS) |
| Vasopressor of choice in septic shock | Norepinephrine |

## 📊 Classification of Shock

| Type | Problem | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Hypovolemic** | ↓ Volume | Hemorrhage, burns, dehydration |
| **Cardiogenic** | ↓ Pump | MI, arrhythmia, valve failure |
| **Distributive** | ↓ SVR | Sepsis, anaphylaxis, neurogenic |
| **Obstructive** | Obstruction to flow | PE, tamponade, tension pneumothorax |

## 🩸 ATLS Hemorrhage Classification (MUST KNOW!)

| Parameter | Class I | Class II | Class III | Class IV |
|-----------|---------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Blood loss (mL)** | <750 | 750-1500 | 1500-2000 | >2000 |
| **Blood loss (%)** | <15% | 15-30% | 30-40% | >40% |
| **Heart rate** | <100 | 100-120 | 120-140 | >140 |
| **Blood pressure** | Normal | Normal | ↓ | ↓↓ |
| **Pulse pressure** | Normal | ↓ | ↓↓ | ↓↓↓ |
| **RR** | 14-20 | 20-30 | 30-40 | >40 |
| **Urine output** | >30 | 20-30 | 5-15 | Minimal |
| **Mental status** | Anxious | Mildly anxious | Confused | Confused/Lethargic |
| **Fluid** | Crystalloid | Crystalloid | Crystal + Blood | Crystal + Blood |

## 📝 Hemodynamic Profiles

| Type | CVP | CO | SVR | SvO2 |
|------|-----|----|----|------|
| **Hypovolemic** | ↓ | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ |
| **Cardiogenic** | ↑ | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ |
| **Distributive** | ↓/N | ↑/N | ↓ | ↑ |
| **Obstructive** | ↑ | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ |

**Memory trick:** Only DISTRIBUTIVE has LOW SVR (vasodilation)

## 💊 Vasopressors and Inotropes

| Drug | Receptors | Effect | Use |
|------|-----------|--------|-----|
| **Norepinephrine** | α1 >> β1 | Vasoconstriction + inotrope | Septic shock (1st line) |
| **Epinephrine** | α + β | Vasoconstriction + inotrope | Anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest |
| **Dopamine** | D > β > α | Dose-dependent | 2nd line (arrhythmogenic) |
| **Vasopressin** | V1 | Vasoconstriction | Add-on in septic shock |
| **Dobutamine** | β1 > β2 | Inotrope (↑CO) | Cardiogenic shock |

## 🔬 Lactate Interpretation

| Lactate (mmol/L) | Interpretation |
|------------------|----------------|
| <2 | Normal |
| 2-4 | Mild hypoperfusion |
| >4 | Significant hypoperfusion |
| >8 | Severe shock, high mortality |

**Key concept:** Lactate CLEARANCE (decreasing lactate) predicts survival better than single value

---

## ⚡ Septic Shock — Quick Points

**Definition (Sepsis-3):**
- Sepsis + Vasopressor requirement to maintain MAP ≥65
- AND Lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation

**Hour-1 Bundle:**
- Measure lactate
- Blood cultures before antibiotics
- Broad-spectrum antibiotics within 1 hour
- 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension
- Vasopressors if hypotension persists

---

## ⚠️ Special Types

| Type | Cause | Treatment |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Neurogenic** | Spinal cord injury | Vasopressors (NOT fluids) |
| **Anaphylactic** | Allergic reaction | Epinephrine IM + fluids |
| **Adrenal crisis** | Cortisol deficiency | Hydrocortisone + fluids |
| **Tension pneumothorax** | Air accumulation | Needle decompression → Chest tube |
| **Cardiac tamponade** | Pericardial fluid | Pericardiocentesis |

## 📐 Important Formulas

```
Shock Index = HR / SBP
   Normal: 0.5-0.7
   >1.0 = Significant shock

MAP = DBP + (SBP - DBP)/3
   Target in shock: ≥65 mmHg

Parkland Formula (Burns):
   4 mL × Weight (kg) × %TBSA
   Half in first 8 hours
   Half in next 16 hours
   
Blood Volume:
   Adult male: 70 mL/kg
   Adult female: 65 mL/kg
```

## 📝 Quick Revise Before Exam

- Tachycardia = FIRST sign (BP drops LATE)
- Shock Index >1.0 = significant shock
- Lactate >2 = tissue hypoperfusion
- Crystalloid = fluid of choice (RL or NS)
- Norepinephrine = 1st line vasopressor in septic shock
- Epinephrine = anaphylaxis
- Distributive shock = LOW SVR (only type)
- Class III = BP starts to fall (>30% loss)
- Hour-1 bundle = sepsis treatment standard
