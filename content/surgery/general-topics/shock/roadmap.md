## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Shock:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Cardiac output & determinants | PY5.3 | Shock = inadequate tissue perfusion; understanding CO = HR × SV explains why shock occurs and how to treat it |
| Physiology | Blood pressure regulation | PY5.6 | MAP = CO × SVR — this equation explains the hemodynamic patterns of different shock types |
| Physiology | Baroreceptor compensatory reflex | — | The baroreceptor reflex produces tachycardia and vasoconstriction in early shock — explains compensated shock |
| Physiology | Microcirculation & tissue perfusion | — | Shock ultimately damages tissues at the microcirculatory level — explaining multi-organ failure |
| Physiology | Body fluid compartments & shifts | PY1.5 | Fluid redistribution in shock determines resuscitation strategy — crystalloid vs colloid vs blood |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify shock types (hypovolemic, cardiogenic, distributive, obstructive) by hemodynamic parameters
- Apply ATLS hemorrhagic shock classification (Class I-IV) and guide resuscitation
- Identify endpoints of resuscitation — lactate clearance, urine output, base deficit correction

### Extensions

- **Physiology:** Renal response to shock — The kidney is an early indicator of perfusion — oliguria signals inadequate resuscitation
- **Physiology:** Oxygen delivery in shock — DO₂ = CO × CaO₂ — understanding oxygen delivery guides transfusion triggers and ventilator settings

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Fluid & Electrolyte Balance** — Fluid resuscitation is the first treatment for hypovolemic shock
2. **Blood Transfusion** — Hemorrhagic shock requires blood product replacement
3. **Polytrauma Assessment** — Shock assessment is integral to ATLS primary survey

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Shock is the quintessential surgical emergency — integrating cardiovascular physiology (CO, SVR, MAP), pathology (end-organ damage), pharmacology (vasopressors, inotropes), and clinical skills (ATLS protocols). Every surgical specialty encounters shock, making it the most cross-cutting emergency topic.
