# 📋 Burns — Exam Prep Mode

## 🎯 One-Liners
| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Rule of 9s (adult arm) | 9% |
| Rule of 9s (adult leg) | 18% |
| Rule of 9s (adult trunk) | 18% anterior + 18% posterior |
| Parkland formula | 4 mL × weight (kg) × %TBSA |
| Fluid in first 8 hours | Half of total |
| Fluid choice | Crystalloid (Ringer's lactate) |
| Major burn definition | >20% TBSA (adults), >10% (children/elderly) |
| Inhalational injury suspect if | Enclosed space, singed nasal hairs, carbonaceous sputum |
| Escharotomy indication | Circumferential burn with compromised circulation |
| Early excision timing | Within 72 hours |

## 📊 Depth Classification
| Degree | Depth | Appearance | Healing |
|--------|-------|------------|---------|
| Superficial (1st) | Epidermis | Red, painful | 3-5 days |
| Partial (2nd) | Dermis | Blistered, wet | 2-3 weeks |
| Full (3rd) | Full thickness | Waxy, painless | Needs grafting |

## 💊 Resuscitation
**Parkland Formula:**
- 4 mL × Weight (kg) × %TBSA
- Half in first 8 hours (from time of burn)
- Half in next 16 hours
- Titrate to urine output (0.5-1 mL/kg/hr)

📚 **Sources:** Bailey & Love Ch 31 | ABA Guidelines
