## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Burns:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Skin anatomy & appendages | AN17.1 | Burn depth classification (superficial, partial, full thickness) correlates directly with which skin layers are destroyed |
| Physiology | Fluid shifts in burns | — | Burns cause massive fluid shifts from intravascular to interstitial space — basis for Parkland formula |
| Physiology | Pain pathways (burn depth assessment) | — | Intact pain sensation indicates dermal nerve endings are viable — deep burns are paradoxically painless |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify burn depth and calculate TBSA using the Rule of Nines and Lund-Browder chart
- Calculate fluid resuscitation using the Parkland formula and titrate to urine output
- Manage burn wound — escharotomy indications, topical agents, skin grafting

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pathology | Thermal cell injury patterns | — | Jackson's zones (coagulation, stasis, hyperemia) guide burn wound management decisions |
| Pathology | Burn shock (hypovolemic) | — | Burns >20% TBSA cause systemic capillary leak — understanding this drives aggressive resuscitation |
| Microbiology | Burn wound infections — Pseudomonas | — | Pseudomonas aeruginosa is the most feared burn wound pathogen — causes blue-green pus, sepsis |
| Pharmacology | Silver sulfadiazine, topical agents | — | Topical antimicrobials prevent colonization and invasion of burn wounds |

### Extensions

- **Pathology:** Marjolin ulcer in burn scars — Chronic burn scars can undergo malignant transformation to SCC after decades — requires lifetime surveillance

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Wound Healing** — Burns heal through the same phases — understanding healing guides skin graft timing
2. **Fluid & Electrolyte Balance** — Parkland formula and resuscitation targets are critical for burn management

### Prerequisites

- **Fluid & Electrolyte Balance** — Fluid resuscitation principles must be understood before burns management
- **Shock** — Burns cause hypovolemic shock — requires aggressive resuscitation

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Burns management integrates anatomy (skin layers for depth assessment), physiology (fluid shifts, thermoregulation loss), microbiology (wound infection prevention), pharmacology (topical agents, analgesics), and surgery (escharotomy, grafting). It's a prototype of multi-system injury requiring holistic surgical management.
