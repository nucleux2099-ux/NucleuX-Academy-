## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Organ Transplantation:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Adaptive immunity & HLA system | — | HLA matching determines graft survival — understanding MHC molecules explains rejection mechanisms |
| Microbiology | T-cell mediated rejection | — | T-cells recognize foreign HLA antigens — the primary mechanism of acute cellular rejection |
| Pathology | Chronic rejection pathology | — | Chronic rejection involves antibody-mediated vascular damage — leads to graft arteriosclerosis and fibrosis |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify types of graft rejection — hyperacute, acute cellular, acute antibody-mediated, chronic
- Describe immunosuppression protocols and their side effects
- Understand brain death criteria and organ allocation principles

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pharmacology | Immunosuppressants (cyclosporine, tacrolimus) | PH4.6 | Triple immunosuppression (CNI + antimetabolite + steroid) prevents rejection but increases infection and malignancy risk |
| Microbiology | Opportunistic infections post-transplant | — | CMV, Pneumocystis, fungal infections are major post-transplant threats — prophylaxis is essential |

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Organ transplantation is the ultimate integration of immunology (HLA matching, rejection), pharmacology (immunosuppressants), microbiology (opportunistic infections), and surgical technique. It transforms understanding of the immune system from theoretical to life-saving practical application.
