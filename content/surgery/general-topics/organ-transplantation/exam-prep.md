# Immunosuppressive Therapy — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU13.2 **[UG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **Tacrolimus** (FK506): Binds **FKBP-12** → inhibits calcineurin → blocks IL-2. 100x more potent than cyclosporine
- **Cyclosporine**: Binds **cyclophilin** → inhibits calcineurin. Side effects: nephrotoxicity, HTN, gingival hyperplasia, hirsutism
- **MMF**: Inhibits **IMPDH** → blocks de novo purine synthesis → selective for lymphocytes (lack salvage pathway)
- **Azathioprine**: Prodrug of **6-MP**. NEVER combine with **allopurinol** (fatal myelosuppression)
- **Sirolimus**: Binds FKBP-12 but inhibits **mTOR** (not calcineurin). Used in **drug-eluting stents** too
- **Basiliximab**: Anti-**CD25** (IL-2 receptor α-chain) — chimeric monoclonal antibody for induction
- **OKT3 (Muromonab)**: Anti-**CD3** — causes cytokine release syndrome; mostly historical now
- **ATG**: Polyclonal antibody causing T-cell depletion — used for induction and steroid-resistant rejection
- **PTLD**: EBV-driven, treat by reducing immunosuppression + Rituximab

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**Cyclosporine side effects — "GNASH HH":**
- **G**ingival hyperplasia
- **N**ephrotoxicity
- **A**naphylaxis
- **S**eizures
- **H**TN, **H**irsutism, **H**epatotoxicity

**Tacrolimus vs Cyclosporine — "Tacro = Too much sugar":**
- Tacrolimus → **Diabetes** (more), **Neurotoxicity** (more)
- Cyclosporine → **Hirsutism**, **Gingival hyperplasia** (more)

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **Most commonly used CNI today** → Tacrolimus
2. **Drug causing hemolytic uremic syndrome** → Cyclosporine/Tacrolimus (both)
3. **Immunosuppressant safe in pregnancy** → Azathioprine (relatively)
4. **Drug requiring trough level monitoring** → Tacrolimus (target 5-15 ng/mL)
5. **Steroid-resistant acute rejection treatment** → ATG or OKT3
6. **Most common infection post-transplant (1-6 months)** → CMV
7. **Pneumocystis prophylaxis** → TMP-SMX for 6-12 months post-transplant
8. **Drug-eluting stent coating** → Sirolimus or Everolimus
