## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Fluid & Electrolyte Balance:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Body fluid compartments (ICF/ECF) | PY1.5 | Total body water distribution determines how IV fluids distribute — isotonic stays in ECF, dextrose distributes to TBW |
| Physiology | GFR & renal handling of electrolytes | PY7.2 | The kidney regulates sodium, potassium, and water balance — renal physiology explains electrolyte disorders |
| Physiology | Acid-base balance & ABG interpretation | PY7.6 | Surgical patients frequently develop acid-base disturbances — ABG interpretation is essential |
| Physiology | Osmosis & membrane transport | PY1.3 | Osmotic principles explain fluid movement between compartments and guide IV fluid selection |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Calculate daily maintenance fluid and electrolyte requirements for surgical patients
- Diagnose and correct common electrolyte disorders — hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypocalcemia
- Interpret ABG and classify acid-base disorders (metabolic acidosis in bowel obstruction, alkalosis in pyloric stenosis)

### Extensions

- **Physiology:** Urine concentration mechanisms — Concentrating ability assessment helps evaluate renal function in surgical patients
- **Physiology:** Aldosterone in electrolyte balance — Post-surgical aldosterone surge causes sodium retention and potassium loss — explains post-op electrolyte patterns

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Shock** — Fluid resuscitation is the first step in shock management
2. **Surgical Nutrition** — Nutritional requirements and electrolyte needs go hand in hand
3. **Perioperative Management** — IV fluid management is core to perioperative care

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Fluid and electrolyte balance is the physiological bedrock of surgical practice — every operation disturbs fluid homeostasis. Integrating renal physiology (GFR, tubular handling), pharmacology (diuretics, IV fluids), and pathology (third-spacing, shock) enables rational fluid management that prevents post-operative morbidity.
