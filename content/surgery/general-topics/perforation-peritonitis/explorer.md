# Perforation Peritonitis — Explorer

## Clinical definition
GI perforation with peritoneal contamination causing systemic inflammatory response; requires rapid source control and sepsis management.

5. **Escalation:** when to shift to advanced or rescue pathway

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Shock physiology
- Diffuse peritonitis with guarding/rigidity
- Rising lactate/organ dysfunction
- Delayed presentation with septic complications

## Diagnostic ladder
1. Clinical diagnosis first in unstable patient
2. Erect chest/abdominal imaging for free air where feasible
3. CT for source localization in stable patients
4. Risk stratification for damage-control vs definitive surgery

## Management ladder
1. Sepsis bundle + broad antibiotics + aggressive resuscitation
2. Urgent source control (repair/resection/diversion as indicated)
3. Damage-control strategy in physiologically exhausted patients
4. Post-op ICU support, leak surveillance, and complication rescue

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite protocolized initial care.
- Change in resectability/operability class on interval reassessment.
- Need for advanced procedural/critical-care support.
- Mandatory MDT takeover for complex sequencing decisions.
