## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Blood Transfusion:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | ABO & Rh blood group systems | PY2.5 | Mismatched transfusion causes acute hemolytic reaction — understanding ABO antigens prevents fatal errors |
| Physiology | Platelet physiology & hemostasis | PY2.4 | Component therapy decisions require understanding of primary and secondary hemostasis |
| Pathology | Coagulation cascade disorders | PA20.1 | Coagulation factor deficiencies guide specific component replacement — FFP, cryoprecipitate |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe blood group systems (ABO, Rh) and cross-matching procedures
- Classify and manage transfusion reactions — immediate and delayed
- Apply massive transfusion protocol (1:1:1 ratio) and understand its rationale

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pathology | Transfusion reactions — hemolysis | — | Recognizing and managing transfusion reactions (febrile, allergic, hemolytic, TRALI) is essential |
| Microbiology | Transfusion-transmitted HBV | — | Screening for HBV, HCV, HIV is mandatory — understanding window period limitations |
| Microbiology | Transfusion-transmitted infections | — | Blood-borne infections remain a risk despite screening — CMV, malaria, bacteria |

### Extensions

- **Pharmacology:** Immunosuppression in massive transfusion — Massive transfusion causes immunosuppression and dilutional coagulopathy — understanding guides MTP protocols

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Shock** — Blood loss leading to hemorrhagic shock — transfusion is the definitive treatment
2. **Perioperative Management** — Transfusion decisions are core perioperative skills
3. **Damage Control Surgery** — Massive transfusion protocol is integral to trauma resuscitation

### Prerequisites

- **Fluid & Electrolyte Balance** — IV fluid management precedes blood product therapy

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Blood transfusion integrates physiology (blood groups, hemostasis), pathology (coagulation disorders, transfusion reactions), microbiology (transfusion-transmitted infections), and clinical surgery (massive transfusion protocols, component therapy). It's one of the most cross-disciplinary topics in surgical practice.
