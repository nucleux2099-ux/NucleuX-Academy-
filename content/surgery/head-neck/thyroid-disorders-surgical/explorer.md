# Thyroid Disorders — Surgical Perspective

> *NucleuX Academy — Surgery > Head & Neck*
> *Sources: Sabiston 22nd Ed Ch.37, Bailey & Love 28th Ed Ch.50*

---

## 1. Introduction

**[UG]** The **thyroid gland** is the largest endocrine gland, located in the anterior neck (C5-T1 level). Surgical management is required for compressive goitres, thyrotoxicosis unresponsive to medical therapy, suspicious nodules, and confirmed malignancy.

---

## 2. Surgical Anatomy Essentials

- **Blood supply**: Superior thyroid artery (from ECA), Inferior thyroid artery (from thyrocervical trunk), Thyroidea ima (inconstant, from aorta/brachiocephalic)
- **Recurrent laryngeal nerve (RLN)**: Runs in the **tracheo-oesophageal groove** — most important structure to protect during surgery
- **Superior laryngeal nerve (external branch)**: Runs with superior thyroid artery — injury causes loss of high-pitched voice
- **Parathyroid glands**: 2 superior (from 4th pharyngeal pouch), 2 inferior (from 3rd pouch — more variable position)
- **Berry's ligament**: Posterior suspensory ligament — RLN is at risk at this point

---

## 3. Clinical Examination of Thyroid

**[PG]** A thyroid swelling **moves with deglutition** (attached to pretracheal fascia). A **thyroglossal cyst moves with tongue protrusion** (attached to foramen caecum via thyroglossal duct).

**Key findings to assess**: Size, nodularity, consistency, tenderness, retrosternal extension, tracheal deviation, cervical lymphadenopathy, voice change, Pemberton's sign.

---

## 4. Investigation of Thyroid Nodule

**[UG]** **FNAC (Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology)** is the **single most important investigation** for a thyroid nodule.

**Bethesda Classification:**
| Category | Diagnosis | Management |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| I | Non-diagnostic | Repeat FNAC |
| II | Benign | Follow-up |
| III | Atypia of undetermined significance | Repeat FNAC or lobectomy |
| IV | Follicular neoplasm | **Lobectomy** (cannot distinguish adenoma vs carcinoma on cytology) |
| V | Suspicious for malignancy | Lobectomy or total thyroidectomy |
| VI | Malignant | Total thyroidectomy |

---

## 5. Indications for Thyroid Surgery

- Compressive symptoms (dysphagia, stridor)
- Retrosternal goitre
- Failed medical therapy for **Graves' disease**
- Suspicious/malignant nodule (Bethesda IV-VI)
- Cosmetic concerns

## 7. Complications of Thyroid Surgery

| Complication | Cause | Prevention |
|-------------|-------|------------|
| **RLN injury** | Traction/transection | Identify nerve, use NIM |
| **Hypoparathyroidism** | Parathyroid devascularization | Identify & preserve glands |
| **Thyroid storm** | Unprepared thyrotoxic patient | Pre-op Lugol's iodine + beta-blockers |
| **Haematoma** | Reactionary bleeding → airway compromise | Meticulous haemostasis, bedside clip cutter |
