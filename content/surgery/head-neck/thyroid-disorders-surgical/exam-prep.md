# Thyroid Disorders (Surgical) — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU22.3 **[PG]**, SU22.6 **[UG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **FNAC** is the most important investigation for thyroid nodule (Bethesda I-VI)
- **Bethesda IV** (follicular neoplasm): Lobectomy needed — capsular/vascular invasion can't be assessed on FNAC
- **RLN injury**: Unilateral → hoarseness (vocal cord in paramedian); Bilateral → **stridor/airway obstruction** (both cords midline)
- **External branch of SLN** ("nerve of Galli-Curci"): Injury → inability to produce high-pitched sounds
- **Berry's ligament**: RLN most vulnerable here during dissection
- **Ligament of Berry**: Posterior, attaches thyroid to trachea (rings 2-3)
- **Thyroid storm**: Fever, tachycardia, delirium → treat with **beta-blockers + PTU + Lugol's iodine + steroids + cooling**
- **Graves' disease surgery**: Subtotal or total thyroidectomy after rendering euthyroid
- **Pre-op preparation for Graves'**: Carbimazole 4-6 weeks → add Lugol's iodine 10 days pre-op (reduces vascularity)
- **Pemberton's sign**: Facial plethora and JVD on raising arms → retrosternal goitre

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**Thyroid Storm Treatment — "Block THAT Storm":**
- **B**eta-blockers (Propranolol)
- **T**hionamides (PTU — also blocks peripheral T4→T3)
- **H**ydrocortisone (blocks conversion + treats adrenal insufficiency)
- **A**nti-pyretics (NOT aspirin — displaces T4 from TBG)
- **T**reat precipitant

**Complications of Thyroidectomy — "RRHHH":**
- **R**LN injury
- **R**eactive haemorrhage
- **H**ypoparathyroidism (hypocalcemia)
- **H**ypothyroidism
- **H**orner's syndrome (rare)

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **Cold nodule on scan** → Higher malignancy risk → FNAC mandatory
2. **Hot nodule** → Functioning, rarely malignant → treat thyrotoxicosis
3. **Chvostek's sign** → Facial nerve tap → facial twitch (hypocalcemia)
4. **Trousseau's sign** → BP cuff inflation → carpopedal spasm (hypocalcemia)
5. **Post-thyroidectomy stridor** → Open wound at bedside first → then intubate
6. **Post-thyroidectomy hypocalcemia timing** → 24-48 hours (monitor Ca²⁺)
7. **Kocher's incision** → 2 cm above sternoclavicular joint, in skin crease
8. **Delphian node** → Pre-laryngeal node, if involved suggests thyroid malignancy
