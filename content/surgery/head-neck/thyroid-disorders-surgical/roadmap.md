## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe the surgical anatomy of the thyroid — RLN, superior laryngeal nerve, parathyroid glands, Berry ligament
- Plan the extent of surgery — hemithyroidectomy vs total thyroidectomy — based on disease indication
- Manage post-thyroidectomy complications — bleeding (airway emergency), RLN injury, hypocalcemia

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Thyroid surgery epitomizes the importance of anatomy in surgical practice — the RLN, parathyroids, and superior laryngeal nerve must be identified and preserved. It integrates endocrine physiology (hormonal management), pathology (FNAC, frozen section), and clinical skills (voice assessment, calcium monitoring). A 'simple' thyroidectomy requires mastery of neck anatomy.
