## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Perform a systematic thyroid examination — inspection, palpation from behind, auscultation for bruit
- Elicit movement with swallowing (thyroid) vs tongue protrusion (thyroglossal cyst) to differentiate
- Assess thyroid status clinically — signs of thyrotoxicosis (tremor, tachycardia, lid lag) and hypothyroidism

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Thyroid examination is a foundational clinical skill that connects anatomy (surface landmarks, fascial attachments), physiology (thyroid functional status assessment), and clinical medicine (examination technique, clinical reasoning). It's often the first surgical examination taught and is high-yield for clinical exams.
