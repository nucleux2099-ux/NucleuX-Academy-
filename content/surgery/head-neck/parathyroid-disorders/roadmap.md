## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Differentiate primary, secondary, and tertiary hyperparathyroidism — biochemistry and management
- Interpret calcium, PTH, phosphate, and vitamin D levels to diagnose parathyroid disorders
- Plan surgical management — focused parathyroidectomy vs bilateral exploration, intraoperative PTH monitoring

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Parathyroid disorders integrate endocrine physiology (calcium-PTH axis), renal physiology (phosphate handling, vitamin D activation), pathology (adenoma vs hyperplasia), radiology (localization studies), and surgery (minimally invasive parathyroidectomy). The elegant use of intraoperative PTH monitoring shows how biochemistry directly guides surgical decision-making.
