# Parathyroid Disorders — Exam Prep

> **NMC Codes:** SU23.2 **[UG]**, SU23.3 **[PG]**

---

## 🎯 High-Yield Facts

- **Primary HPT**: 85% solitary **adenoma**, 10% hyperplasia, <5% carcinoma
- **Most common cause of hypercalcemia in outpatients** → Primary HPT
- **Most common cause of hypercalcemia in inpatients** → Malignancy (PTHrP)
- **Sestamibi scan**: Adenoma shows **persistent uptake** (delayed washout) vs thyroid tissue
- **Subperiosteal resorption** on X-ray (radial side, middle phalanx of index finger) — pathognomonic of HPT
- **Brown tumour**: Osteoclastic lesion filled with haemosiderin — NOT a neoplasm
- **Salt and pepper skull**: Multiple lytic lesions in primary HPT
- **Secondary HPT in CKD**: Low Ca²⁺ → High PTH → High phosphate → treat with **phosphate binders + calcitriol**
- **Tertiary HPT**: Autonomous, persists after renal transplant → needs parathyroidectomy
- **Miami criterion**: >50% PTH drop at 10 min post-excision confirms successful surgery
- **MEN 1** (3 Ps): Parathyroid hyperplasia + Pituitary + Pancreatic tumours
- **MEN 2A**: Medullary thyroid Ca + Phaeochromocytoma + Parathyroid hyperplasia

## 🧠 Mnemonics

**Primary HPT — "Bones, Stones, Abdominal Groans, Psychic Moans":**
- **Bones**: Osteitis fibrosa cystica, brown tumours
- **Stones**: Renal calculi
- **Groans**: Constipation, pancreatitis, PUD
- **Moans**: Depression, confusion

**Hypercalcemia ECG — "Short QT":**
- Shortened **QT interval** (opposite of hypocalcemia which prolongs QT)

## 📝 MCQ-Style Key Points

1. **Investigation of choice for localization** → **Sestamibi scan** (⁹⁹mTc-MIBI)
2. **PTH acts on** → Bone and Kidney (NOT directly on intestine — indirect via Vit D)
3. **Superior parathyroids develop from** → 4th pharyngeal pouch (more constant position)
4. **Inferior parathyroids develop from** → 3rd pharyngeal pouch (with thymus — more variable, may be ectopic)
5. **Ectopic parathyroid locations** → Thymus (most common ectopic), carotid sheath, mediastinum
6. **Parathyroid carcinoma clue** → Ca >14 mg/dL + very high PTH + palpable mass
7. **Familial hypocalciuric hypercalcemia (FHH)** → Ca/Cr clearance ratio <0.01. Benign, NO surgery
8. **Hungry bone syndrome** → Post-parathyroidectomy hypocalcemia (Ca²⁺/PO₄³⁻ drop, bones "hungry")
