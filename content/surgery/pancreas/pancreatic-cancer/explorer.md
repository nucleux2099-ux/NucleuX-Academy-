# Pancreatic Cancer — Explorer

## Clinical definition
Aggressive pancreatic ductal malignancy requiring early staging to define resectable, borderline, or unresectable pathway.

## Curriculum Position
- **Track:** MCh Surgical Gastroenterology

## First-principles map (ATOM)
1. **Mechanism:** disease biology and organ failure drivers
2. **Pattern:** syndrome/stage presentation archetype
3. **Signal:** investigations that change pathway assignment
4. **Action:** first definitive lane selection
5. **Escalation:** when to shift to advanced or rescue pathway

## Red flags (must not miss)
- Obstructive jaundice with cholangitis
- Severe weight loss/cachexia + poor PS
- Vascular encasement/metastatic spread signs
- Uncontrolled pain or gastric outlet obstruction

## Diagnostic ladder
1. High-quality pancreas protocol imaging for staging
2. CA19-9 and baseline fitness/liver profile context
3. Resectability class assignment before treatment start
4. Histology strategy guided by operability and oncology plan

## Management ladder
1. Resectable: surgery-first or protocolized perioperative strategy
2. Borderline: neoadjuvant-first with response reassessment
3. Unresectable/metastatic: systemic + symptom-directed palliation
4. Biliary/gastric outlet/pain interventions by symptom burden

## Decision-to-escalate checkpoints
- Failing physiology despite protocolized initial care.
- Change in resectability/operability class on interval reassessment.
- Need for advanced procedural/critical-care support.
- Mandatory MDT takeover for complex sequencing decisions.
