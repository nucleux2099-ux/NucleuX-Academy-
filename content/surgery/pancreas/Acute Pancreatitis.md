# Acute Pancreatitis

> **Source**: Sleisenger Ch 58, Blumgart Ch 54-56
> **Topic System**: [[Pancreas Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> Acute pancreatitis requires 2 of 3 criteria (characteristic pain, lipase >3×, imaging), managed with aggressive LR resuscitation, early enteral nutrition, and delayed intervention for necrosis (wait 4 weeks unless infected).

---

## Diagnostic Criteria (Revised Atlanta 2012)

**Requires 2 of 3:**
1. Characteristic abdominal pain (acute epigastric/LUQ, radiating to back)
2. Serum amylase or lipase >3× upper limit of normal
3. Imaging consistent with pancreatitis (CT, MRI, or US)

## Etiology: "I GET SMASHED"

| Letter | Cause | Frequency |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| **G** | Gallstones | 40-60% |
| **E** | Ethanol | 25-35% |
| **I** | Idiopathic | 10-15% |
| **T** | Trauma/ERCP | 5% |
| **S** | Steroids (drugs) | <1% |
| **M** | Mumps (infections) | Rare |
| **A** | Autoimmune | Rare |
| **S** | Scorpion stings, Hyperlipidemia | 1-4% |
| **H** | Hypercalcemia, Hypothermia | Rare |
| **E** | ERCP | 5% |
| **D** | Drugs | <1% |

> 💡 **Pearl:** ALT >3× ULN has 95% PPV for gallstone etiology

## Severity Classification (Revised Atlanta 2012)

| Severity | Definition | Frequency | Mortality |
|----------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Mild** | No organ failure, no local/systemic complications | 80% | <1% |
| **Moderately Severe** | Transient organ failure (<48h) OR local complications | 15% | <5% |
| **Severe** | Persistent organ failure (>48h) | 5% | 30-50% |

---

## BISAP Score (At Admission)

Score 1 point each:
- **B**UN >25 mg/dL
- **I**mpaired mental status
- **S**IRS (≥2 criteria)
- **A**ge >60 years
- **P**leural effusion

**BISAP ≥3 = High risk for mortality (5-20%)**

## Local Complications (Revised Atlanta)

| Complication | Timing | Contents | Wall |
|--------------|--------|----------|------|
| **Acute peripancreatic fluid collection** | <4 weeks | Fluid only | No |
| **Pancreatic pseudocyst** | >4 weeks | Fluid only | Yes |
| **Acute necrotic collection** | <4 weeks | Necrosis + fluid | No |
| **Walled-off necrosis (WON)** | >4 weeks | Necrosis + fluid | Yes |

## Key Clinical Pearls

> 💎 **Pearl 1:** Enzyme elevation does NOT correlate with severity – a patient with lipase 300 can be sicker than one with lipase 3000

> 💎 **Pearl 2:** CT is NOT needed in first 48-72 hours – necrosis takes time to develop

> 💎 **Pearl 3:** Lactated Ringer's > Normal Saline for resuscitation – reduces SIRS

> 💎 **Pearl 4:** Early enteral nutrition reduces complications – "pancreatic rest" is OUTDATED

> 💎 **Pearl 5:** Wait 4 weeks before intervening on necrosis unless infected – allows maturation

> 💎 **Pearl 6:** Step-up approach (drainage before surgery) reduces mortality in infected necrosis

## ERCP for Gallstone Pancreatitis

| Indication | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| **Cholangitis** | Urgent (<24 hours) |
| **Persistent biliary obstruction** | Early (<72 hours) |
| **No cholangitis, no obstruction** | NOT indicated |

**Cholecystectomy:**
- Same admission for mild gallstone pancreatitis
- After recovery/collections resolved for severe
