# Zenker Diverticulum

> **Source**: Shackelford Ch 5 - *Pharyngoesophageal (Zenker's) Diverticulum*, Ch 6 - *Other Esophageal Diverticula*, Ch 18
> **Topic System**: [[Esophagus Index]]
> **Phase**: Shoot
> **Tier**: 3 (Pathology)

---

## Core Concepts

> [!summary] One-liner
> Zenker diverticulum is a FALSE (pulsion) diverticulum arising posteriorly through Killian's dehiscence (between thyropharyngeus and cricopharyngeus) due to UES dysfunction, presenting with dysphagia, regurgitation of undigested food, and halitosis—treated by cricopharyngeal myotomy ± diverticulectomy or pexy.

---

### Location: Killian's Dehiscence

    ═══════════════════════════════════════

    Posterior View of Pharyngoesophageal Junction:

                    Pharynx

         ┌────────────┴────────────┐
         │                         │

         │   (oblique fibers)      │   constrictor (upper)

         ├─────────────────────────┤
         │                         │
         │   CRICOPHARYNGEUS       │ ← Inferior pharyngeal
         │   (transverse fibers)   │   constrictor (lower)
         │   = UES                 │

                      │
                  Esophagus

### Pathophysiology

```

    ════════════════════════════

                │
                ▼

                │
                ▼
    Increased intrapharyngeal pressure
                │
                ▼
    Pressure transmitted to weak point (Killian's)
                │
                ▼
    Mucosal herniation POSTERIORLY
                │
                ▼
    Progressive enlargement over time
                │
                ▼
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │           ZENKER DIVERTICULUM               │
    │                                             │
    │   • FALSE diverticulum (mucosa only)        │

```

### Types of Esophageal Diverticula - Classification

| Type | Location | Mechanism | True/False | Example |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Pharyngoesophageal** | Above UES | Pulsion | FALSE | Zenker |
| **Midesophageal** | Middle 1/3 | Traction | Usually TRUE | TB, histoplasmosis |
| **Epiphrenic** | Above LES | Pulsion | FALSE | Associated with dysmotility |

## Clinical Presentation

### Symptoms

```
    ZENKER'S CLINICAL PRESENTATION
    ═══════════════════════════════

    CLASSIC TRIAD
    ─────────────
    1. DYSPHAGIA (90%+)
       • Progressive over years
       • Solids > liquids initially
       • Worse as pouch enlarges

    2. REGURGITATION OF UNDIGESTED FOOD (75%)
       • Hours after eating
       • Food NOT mixed with acid/bile
       • Often occurs when lying down
       • "Food from yesterday"

    3. HALITOSIS (50%+)
       • From retained, decomposing food
       • May be socially distressing

    ADDITIONAL SYMPTOMS
    ───────────────────
    • Neck mass/gurgling (Boyce sign)
    • Aspiration/recurrent pneumonia
    • Nocturnal coughing
    • Weight loss (in severe cases)
    • Voice changes (compression)
```

### Physical Examination

| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Boyce sign** | Gurgling in neck with palpation |
| **Neck mass** | Visible/palpable in large diverticula |
| **Pulmonary crackles** | Aspiration pneumonitis |
| **Weight loss** | Poor oral intake |

### Risk Factors

| Factor | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Age** | Typically >60 years (7th-8th decade) |
| **Sex** | Male predominance |
| **UES dysfunction** | Primary pathology |
| **GERD** | May contribute to UES dysfunction |

## Diagnosis

### Diagnostic Studies

```
    DIAGNOSTIC APPROACH
    ═══════════════════

    FIRST LINE: BARIUM SWALLOW (Video fluoroscopy)
    ─────────────────────────────────────────────
    • Diagnostic study of choice
    • Shows: Posterior outpouching above UES
    • Demonstrates size and location
    • Can see food retention
    • Safe (no perforation risk)

         ┌─────────────────────────┐
         │                         │
         │    ┌────────┐           │
         │    │ Pouch  │◄──────────│── Barium fills
         │    │(Zenker)│           │   diverticulum
         │    └───┬────┘           │
         │        │                │
         │    ════╪════ ◄──────────│── UES (cricopharyngeus)
         │        │                │
         │    Esophagus            │
         └─────────────────────────┘

    SECOND LINE: EGD (with CAUTION)
    ────────────────────────────────
    • Risk of entering diverticulum → PERFORATION
    • Should be done by experienced endoscopist
    • Enter with scope positioned anteriorly
    • Useful for: Cancer screening, associated pathology

    OPTIONAL: CT Neck/Chest
    ───────────────────────
    • If concern for malignancy
    • Aspiration pneumonia workup
    • Pre-operative planning
```

### Barium Swallow Findings

| Finding | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Posterior outpouching** | Above cricopharyngeus |
| **Barium retention** | In dependent portion |
| **"Shelf" sign** | At cricopharyngeus level |
| **Aspiration** | Into airway (if observed) |
| **Size measurement** | Guides surgical approach |

### Size Classification

| Size | Description | Surgical Implication |
|------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Small** | <2 cm | Myotomy alone may suffice |
| **Medium** | 2-4 cm | Myotomy + diverticulopexy |
| **Large** | >4-6 cm | Myotomy + diverticulectomy |

## Treatment

### Principles of Treatment

```
    TREATMENT PRINCIPLES
    ════════════════════

    KEY INSIGHT: The PRIMARY problem is UES dysfunction
                (cricopharyngeal muscle)

    Therefore: CRICOPHARYNGEAL MYOTOMY is ESSENTIAL
              in ALL surgical approaches

    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                 │
    │  Myotomy ALONE ──► Small diverticula (<2 cm)   │
    │                                                 │
    │  Myotomy + PEXY ──► Medium diverticula          │
    │  (tack pouch up)                               │
    │                                                 │
    │  Myotomy + ECTOMY ──► Large diverticula        │
    │  (resect pouch)                                │
    │                                                 │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    WITHOUT myotomy = HIGH RECURRENCE RATE
```

### Surgical Options

#### 1. Open Transcervical Approach (Traditional)

```
    OPEN CRICOPHARYNGEAL MYOTOMY + DIVERTICULECTOMY
    ═══════════════════════════════════════════════

    APPROACH:
    • Left neck incision (anterior to SCM)
    • Diverticulum found posteriorly
    • Retract thyroid, carotid laterally

    STEPS:
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                                 │
    │  1. Identify diverticulum                       │
    │                                                 │
    │  2. Dissect to neck of pouch                    │
    │                                                 │
    │  3. MYOTOMY of cricopharyngeus                  │
    │     • Extend 3-4 cm onto esophagus              │
    │     • Include ALL circular fibers               │
    │                                                 │
    │  4. Address diverticulum:                       │
    │     • Small: Myotomy alone                      │
    │     • Medium: Pexy (tack superiorly)            │
    │     • Large: Excise with stapler                │
    │                                                 │
    │  5. Drain placement (optional)                  │
    │                                                 │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    MYOTOMY DIAGRAM:
    ────────────────

         ║           ╔══════════════╗
         ║   Pouch   ║              ║
         ║     ◄─────║              ║
         ║           ╠══════════════╣
         ║        ───║──────────────║─── MYOTOMY
         ║           ║ Cricophar.   ║    (divide)
         ║           ╠══════════════╣
         ║           ║   Esophagus  ║
         ║           ╚══════════════╝
```

#### 2. Endoscopic Approach (Minimally Invasive)

```
    ENDOSCOPIC CRICOPHARYNGEAL MYOTOMY
    ══════════════════════════════════

    TECHNIQUES:
    ───────────

    1. RIGID ENDOSCOPY (Dohlman procedure)
       • Bivalve laryngoscope exposes septum
       • CO2 laser or stapler divides septum
       • Septum = common wall between pouch and esophagus
       • Dividing septum = opens pouch + performs myotomy

    2. FLEXIBLE ENDOSCOPY (Z-POEM, peroral)
       • Similar to POEM for achalasia
       • Submucosal tunnel to cricopharyngeus
       • Myotomy from inside
       • Newer technique

    SEPTUM DIVISION:
    ────────────────

         ┌───────────────────────┐
         │       Pouch           │
         │         │             │
         │    ═════╪═════        │ ← Septum (common wall)
         │         │             │   DIVIDE THIS
         │      Esophagus        │
         └───────────────────────┘

                    │
                    ▼ After division

         ┌───────────────────────┐
         │                       │
         │    ╔═══════════╗      │
         │    ║           ║      │ ← Wide opening
         │    ║  Single   ║      │   Pouch drains into
         │    ║  lumen    ║      │   esophagus
         │    ╚═══════════╝      │
         └───────────────────────┘
```

### Comparison of Approaches

| Feature | Open | Endoscopic |
|---------|------|------------|
| **Anesthesia** | General | General |
| **Hospital stay** | 2-3 days | 0-1 days |
| **Diet resumption** | POD 1-2 | Same day |
| **Success rate** | 90-95% | 85-95% |
| **Recurrence** | 3-5% | 10-15% |
| **Complications** | Wound, RLN injury, leak | Perforation, mediastinitis |
| **Best for** | Large diverticula | Small-medium, poor surgical candidates |
| **Repeat procedure** | Difficult | Can repeat |

### Surgical Complications

| Complication | Incidence | Prevention/Management |
|--------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Recurrence** | 3-15% | Adequate myotomy length |
| **Leak/fistula** | 1-5% | Meticulous closure, drain |
| **Recurrent laryngeal nerve injury** | 1-3% | Careful dissection |
| **Mediastinitis** | 1-2% | Early recognition, drainage |
| **Stricture** | 2-5% | Avoid excessive resection |
| **Hematoma** | 1-2% | Hemostasis, drain |

## Post-operative Care

### Standard Protocol

| Timeframe | Management |
|-----------|------------|
| **POD 0** | NPO, IV fluids |
| **POD 1** | Gastrografin swallow (if performed diverticulectomy) |
| **POD 1-2** | Clear liquids if swallow negative |
| **POD 2-3** | Soft diet, consider discharge |
| **Week 1-2** | Soft diet at home |
| **Week 2+** | Regular diet |

### Follow-up

- Clinical assessment at 2-4 weeks
- Repeat barium swallow only if symptoms recur
- Long-term success: >90%

## Special Considerations

### Zenker and Esophageal Cancer

- Squamous cell carcinoma can arise IN diverticulum
- Incidence: 0.5-1% of Zenker diverticula
- Long-standing, untreated pouches at higher risk
- EGD recommended to inspect pouch mucosa

## Quick Recall Questions

1. Q: Where exactly does Zenker diverticulum form?
   A: Through Killian's dehiscence - the weak point BETWEEN thyropharyngeus (above) and cricopharyngeus (below)

2. Q: Why is cricopharyngeal myotomy essential in Zenker surgery?
   A: The PRIMARY pathology is UES (cricopharyngeal) dysfunction. Without myotomy, recurrence rate is high because the underlying cause is not addressed.

3. Q: What is Boyce sign?
   A: Gurgling sound in the neck when the Zenker diverticulum is palpated (retained air/fluid in pouch)

4. Q: What distinguishes Zenker (pharyngoesophageal) from mid-esophageal diverticula?
   A: Zenker is FALSE (mucosa only) and PULSION type; mid-esophageal is usually TRUE (all layers) and TRACTION type (from external inflammation like TB)
