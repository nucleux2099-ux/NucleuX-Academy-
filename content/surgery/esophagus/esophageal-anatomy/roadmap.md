## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Esophageal Anatomy:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Superior mediastinum & esophageal relations | AN24.1 | The esophagus passes through the superior mediastinum with critical relations to the trachea, aorta, and recurrent laryngeal nerves |
| Anatomy | Diaphragmatic hiatus anatomy | AN21.1 | The esophageal hiatus and phrenoesophageal ligament form the anti-reflux barrier |
| Anatomy | GEJ anatomy | AN47.1 | The gastroesophageal junction is the surgical landmark for all esophageal procedures |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe the esophageal course, constrictions, and their clinical significance
- Identify the blood supply, lymphatic drainage, and nerve supply relevant to surgery
- Understand the anatomy of the GEJ and anti-reflux mechanisms

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **LES Physiology** — Anatomy leads to understanding the functional anti-reflux barrier
2. **Dysphagia** — Anatomical knowledge is essential for evaluating causes of difficulty swallowing
3. **GERD** — Hiatal anatomy directly relates to reflux pathophysiology

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Esophageal anatomy is the foundation for all esophageal surgery — from understanding why the left recurrent laryngeal nerve is at risk during thoracic procedures, to why the esophagus has no serosa (making anastomotic leaks more common), to how the hiatal mechanism prevents reflux.
