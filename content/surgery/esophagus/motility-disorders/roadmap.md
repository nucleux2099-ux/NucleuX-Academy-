## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Esophageal Motility Disorders:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Esophageal motility patterns | — | Normal peristalsis (primary, secondary) must be understood to recognize pathological patterns |
| Physiology | Enteric nervous system | — | The myenteric plexus controls esophageal smooth muscle — its dysfunction underlies motility disorders |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify esophageal motility disorders using the Chicago Classification v4.0
- Differentiate DES, jackhammer esophagus, absent contractility, and EGJ outflow obstruction
- Recognize the clinical significance of ineffective esophageal motility in pre-surgical workup

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Esophageal motility disorders connect physiology (peristalsis, neuromuscular control), pathology (myenteric plexus degeneration), and radiology (HRM patterns). The Chicago Classification represents a paradigm shift in how physiological measurements define disease — essential knowledge for any surgeon performing anti-reflux or foregut procedures.
