# Esophageal Motility Disorders: Current Classification & Approach to Diagnosis

## Overview

Esophageal motility disorders (EMD) encompass a spectrum of conditions characterized by abnormal esophageal peristalsis and/or lower esophageal sphincter (LES) function. The Chicago Classification v4.0 (2021) provides the current standardized framework for diagnosis based on high-resolution manometry (HRM) findings.

### Manometric Parameters (Chicago Classification v4.0)

| Parameter | Definition | Normal Value |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| IRP (Integrated Relaxation Pressure) | Mean LES pressure during 4s of relaxation | <15 mmHg |
| DCI (Distal Contractile Integral) | Amplitude Г— duration Г— length of contraction | 450-8000 mmHg.cm.s |
| DL (Distal Latency) | Time from UES relaxation to CDP | >4.5 seconds |
| CDP (Contractile Deceleration Point) | Transition from esophageal body to LES segment | - |
| Break | Gap in 20 mmHg isobaric contour | <3 cm (small) |

### Category 1: Disorders of EGJ Outflow

#### 1.1 Achalasia (IRP вүҘ15 mmHg + 100% failed peristalsis)

| Subtype | Manometric Features | Clinical Significance |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Type I | No esophageal pressurization, absent peristalsis | "Classic" - 32% of cases |
| Type II | Panesophageal pressurization вүҘ20% swallows | Best treatment response - 50% |
| Type III | Premature (spastic) contractions вүҘ20% swallows | Worst prognosis - 18% |

#### 1.2 EGJ Outflow Obstruction (EGJOO)

- IRP вүҘ15 mmHg
- NOT meeting criteria for achalasia
- Some preserved peristalsis
- **Requires additional workup** - may be:
  - Early achalasia
  - Mechanical obstruction (tumor, stricture)
  - Medication effect (opioids)
  - Artifact

### Category 2: Major Disorders of Peristalsis

| Disorder | DCI | Other Criteria | IRP |
|----------|-----|----------------|-----|
| Absent Contractility | 100% failed (DCI <100) | - | Normal |
| Distal Esophageal Spasm | вүҘ20% premature contractions | DL <4.5s | Normal |
| Hypercontractile (Jackhammer) | вүҘ20% with DCI >8000 | - | Normal |

#### 2.1 Absent Contractility
- 100% failed peristalsis
- Normal IRP (differentiates from Type I achalasia)
- Associated with:
  - Scleroderma/Systemic sclerosis
  - Severe GERD
  - Post-surgical states

#### 2.2 Distal Esophageal Spasm (DES)
- вүҘ20% premature contractions (DL <4.5s)
- Normal IRP
- "Corkscrew esophagus" on barium swallow
- Chest pain and dysphagia

#### 2.3 Hypercontractile Esophagus (Jackhammer)
- вүҘ20% swallows with DCI >8000 mmHg.cm.s
- Normal IRP
- Can affect any segment
- "Nutcracker" is outdated term (referred to amplitude only)

### Category 3: Minor Disorders of Peristalsis

| Disorder | Criteria |
|----------|----------|
| Ineffective Esophageal Motility (IEM) | >70% ineffective swallows (failed or weak: DCI <450) |
| Fragmented Peristalsis | вүҘ50% fragmented contractions (break >5 cm) |

- **L**ymph nodes/Mass on imaging
- **A**nemia (iron deficiency)

### Distinguishing Achalasia from Pseudoachalasia

| Feature | Primary Achalasia | Pseudoachalasia |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Age | Any age, peak 30-60 | Usually >55 years |
| Duration | Gradual (months-years) | Rapid (weeks-months) |
| Weight loss | Mild-moderate | Severe (>15 lbs) |
| EGD | Dilated, retained food | May show mass/infiltration |
| CT/EUS | No mass | Tumor at EGJ |
| Manometry | Classic pattern | May be atypical |

## Key Points for Examination

### Must-Know Facts

1. **Chicago Classification v4.0** is the current standard (2021)
2. **IRP вүҘ15 mmHg** defines EGJ outflow obstruction
3. **Achalasia requires**: Elevated IRP + 100% failed peristalsis
4. **Type II achalasia** has best treatment response
5. **EGJOO** needs additional workup - not all require treatment
6. **DES**: Premature contractions (DL <4.5s), NOT high amplitude
7. **Jackhammer**: DCI >8000, not just high amplitude
8. **IEM** may be clinically insignificant

### Common Exam Questions

| Question Type | Key Point |
|---------------|-----------|
| Differentiate Type I vs II achalasia | Panesophageal pressurization in Type II |
| Pseudoachalasia workup | CT/EUS to rule out malignancy |
| When to treat EGJOO | Only if symptomatic + confirmed |
| DES vs Achalasia III | IRP normal in DES, elevated in Achalasia III |
| IEM significance | Often secondary to GERD, may not need specific treatment |
