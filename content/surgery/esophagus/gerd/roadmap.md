## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into GERD:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Hiatal anatomy & anti-reflux barrier | — | The anti-reflux barrier has anatomical components — LES, crural diaphragm, angle of His, phrenoesophageal ligament |
| Physiology | Gastric acid secretion | PY4.3 | Acid is the injurious agent — understanding parietal cell physiology explains PPI mechanism |
| Physiology | LES tone & transient relaxations | — | Transient LES relaxations (TLESRs) are the main mechanism of reflux in most patients |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Explain the pathophysiology of GERD — role of TLESRs, hiatal hernia, and impaired clearance
- Interpret diagnostic investigations — pH monitoring (DeMeester score), impedance, endoscopy
- Identify indications for surgical management — failed medical therapy, large hernia, complications

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Connection |
|---------|-------|----------|------------|
| Pathology | Esophagitis & Barrett pathology | — | Chronic acid exposure causes esophagitis → metaplasia (Barrett) → dysplasia → adenocarcinoma |
| Pharmacology | PPIs & H2 blockers | — | PPIs are the mainstay of medical therapy — understanding acid secretion explains their efficacy |
| Radiology | Barium swallow, pH monitoring | — | 24-hour pH monitoring is the gold standard for diagnosis; barium swallow shows hiatal hernia |

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Barrett Esophagus** — GERD is the precursor — surveillance for Barrett is critical
2. **Hiatal Hernia** — Often coexists with GERD; hiatal repair is part of anti-reflux surgery
3. **Fundoplication** — Surgical treatment when medical therapy fails
4. **Surgical Management of GERD** — Comprehensive surgical workup and operative options

### Prerequisites

- **Esophageal Anatomy** — Hiatal anatomy forms the anti-reflux barrier
- **LES Physiology** — LES function and dysfunction is the core pathophysiology

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

GERD integrates physiology (acid secretion, LES mechanics), anatomy (hiatal integrity), pathology (esophagitis, Barrett's, adenocarcinoma), pharmacology (PPIs), and surgery (fundoplication). It's a perfect example of how understanding the basic science drives treatment escalation from lifestyle modification → PPIs → surgery.
