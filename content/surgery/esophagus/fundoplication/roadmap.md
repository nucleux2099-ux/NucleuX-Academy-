## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Fundoplication:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Hiatal anatomy for wrap | — | Crural dissection and identification of the esophageal hiatus are the critical first steps |
| Anatomy | Gastric fundus anatomy | — | The fundus must be adequately mobilized by dividing short gastric vessels for a tension-free wrap |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Compare Nissen (360°), Toupet (270° posterior), and Dor (180° anterior) fundoplication
- Select the appropriate wrap type based on esophageal motility (manometry findings)
- Recognize and manage post-fundoplication complications — dysphagia, gas bloat, wrap migration

### Prerequisites

- **GERD** — Fundoplication is the definitive surgical treatment for refractory GERD
- **Hiatal Hernia** — Hiatal repair is performed along with fundoplication

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Fundoplication integrates anatomy (hiatal dissection, vagal nerve preservation), physiology (LES augmentation, esophageal motility), and evidence-based surgery (choice of wrap based on manometry findings). The principle of tailoring the operation to the patient's motility pattern is a key concept in modern esophageal surgery.
