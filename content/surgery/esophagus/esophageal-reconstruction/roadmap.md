Study these **before** diving into Esophageal Reconstruction:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Gastric pull-up anatomy | — | The stomach is the preferred conduit — understanding the right gastroepiploic arcade preservation is critical |
| Anatomy | Colonic conduit blood supply | — | When stomach is unavailable, colon interposition requires intact marginal artery (SMA/IMA territory) |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Compare conduit options — stomach (preferred), colon, jejunum — with their advantages and limitations
- Describe the routes of reconstruction — posterior mediastinal, retrosternal, subcutaneous
- Manage post-reconstruction complications — anastomotic leak, conduit necrosis, stricture

### Prerequisites

- **Esophageal Cancer** — Reconstruction follows esophagectomy for cancer
- **Caustic Injury** — Strictures from caustic ingestion may require esophageal replacement

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Esophageal reconstruction is one of the most anatomy-intensive surgical procedures — requiring detailed knowledge of vascular supply (gastric, colonic, jejunal arcades), mediastinal anatomy (routes of reconstruction), and cervical anatomy (anastomotic technique). It integrates surgical oncology principles with reconstructive surgery.
