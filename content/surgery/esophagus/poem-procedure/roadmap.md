## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe the steps of POEM — mucosal incision, submucosal tunnel, selective myotomy, closure
- Compare POEM advantages (no fundoplication needed, flexible myotomy direction) with limitations (GERD risk)
- Identify patient selection factors — prior failed Heller, sigmoid esophagus, Type III achalasia (posterior POEM)

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

POEM represents the convergence of endoscopic innovation and surgical thinking — using the submucosal space as a natural operative field. It integrates esophageal anatomy (wall layers, muscle fiber orientation), physiology (targeted myotomy for specific motility patterns), and evidence-based practice (comparative outcomes with Heller myotomy).
