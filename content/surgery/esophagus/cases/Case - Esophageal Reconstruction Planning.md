# Case Scenario: Esophageal Reconstruction Planning

## Clinical Vignette

### Initial Presentation

**Patient:** 58-year-old male farmer

**Chief Complaint:** "Difficulty swallowing for 4 months"

**History of Present Illness:**
- Progressive dysphagia, initially solids, now soft foods
- 12 kg weight loss over 4 months
- Regurgitation of food
- No hematemesis
- Occasional retrosternal discomfort with eating
- No odynophagia

**Past Medical History:**
- Long-standing GERD (30+ years)
- Barrett's esophagus (diagnosed 5 years ago, on surveillance)
- Hypertension
- Type 2 diabetes
- No prior surgeries

**Social History:**
- Former smoker (40 pack-years, quit 5 years ago)
- Occasional alcohol
- Works on farm (physically active)

## Clinical Reasoning: Phase 1

### Stop and Think

**Question 1:** What is the staging and treatment plan?

<details>
<summary>Reveal Analysis</summary>

**Clinical Stage:** T3N1M0 - Stage IIIA

**Treatment Plan (CROSS Trial Protocol):**

1. **Neoadjuvant chemoradiation**
   - Carboplatin + Paclitaxel weekly x 5 weeks
   - 41.4 Gy radiation (23 fractions)

2. **Restaging** (6-8 weeks post-treatment)
   - CT scan
   - ± PET-CT
   - EGD to assess response

3. **Esophagectomy** (if no progression)

**Rationale:** Neoadjuvant therapy improves R0 resection rate and survival for locally advanced disease.

</details>

**Question 2:** What reconstruction options should be considered?

<details>
<summary>Reveal Analysis</summary>

**Reconstruction Planning:**

**Conduit Options:**
1. **Gastric conduit - FIRST CHOICE** (stomach available)
2. Colon - only if stomach unavailable
3. Jejunum - not typically needed here

**This patient:**
- Stomach normal on EGD
- No prior abdominal surgery
- → **Gastric conduit is appropriate**

**Approach Options:**
- Ivor Lewis (intrathoracic anastomosis)
- McKeown (cervical anastomosis)
- Transhiatal (cervical anastomosis)

Tumor at 32-37 cm (mid-distal) → **Ivor Lewis** or **McKeown** appropriate

</details>

**CT:** Significant decrease in esophageal wall thickening (partial response)

## Surgical Planning

### Pre-operative Assessment

```
SURGICAL PLANNING CONFERENCE
════════════════════════════

PATIENT FACTORS:
• Age 58, good functional status
• BMI 24 (adequate nutrition achieved)
• Cardiac clearance obtained
• Pulmonary function: FEV1 2.4L (adequate)
• No contraindications to surgery

TUMOR FACTORS:
• Mid-distal esophagus (32-37 cm)
• Good response to neoadjuvant
• No evidence of progression

SURGICAL PLAN:
• Approach: Ivor Lewis esophagectomy
• Conduit: Gastric pull-up
• Anastomosis: Intrathoracic (stapled)
• Feeding access: J-tube
• Pyloroplasty/drainage: Pyloromyotomy
```

### Informed Consent Discussion

```
CONSENT DISCUSSION
══════════════════

PROCEDURE:
Ivor Lewis esophagectomy with gastric conduit reconstruction

EXPECTED:
• OR time: 5-6 hours
• ICU stay: 1-2 days
• Hospital: 10-14 days
• Return to normal diet: 6-8 weeks

RISKS DISCUSSED:
• Anastomotic leak (5-10%)
• Pulmonary complications (15-20%)
• Stricture (10-20% long-term)
• Mortality (3-5%)
• Conduit necrosis (rare but serious)
• Recurrent laryngeal nerve injury
• Chyle leak

PATIENT: Understands, wishes to proceed
```

## Operative Procedure

### Operative Report

```
OPERATIVE REPORT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PROCEDURE: Ivor Lewis Esophagectomy with Gastric Conduit

ANESTHESIA: General + Epidural

POSITION: Supine (abdominal) → Left lateral decubitus (thoracic)

ABDOMINAL PHASE:
─────────────────
• Upper midline laparotomy
• Staging laparoscopy: No metastases
• Mobilized stomach completely
• Divided left gastric artery and vein at origin
• Preserved right gastric and right gastroepiploic
• Divided short gastric vessels
• Created gastric tube with linear stapler (5 cm width)
• Pyloromyotomy performed (Jaboulay)
• Feeding jejunostomy placed
• Kocher maneuver performed
• Hiatus widened

THORACIC PHASE:
────────────────
• Right posterolateral thoracotomy (5th intercostal space)
• Esophagus mobilized circumferentially
• Azygos vein divided
• Tumor palpable, no invasion of aorta
• Periesophageal lymph nodes excised
• Esophagus divided 5 cm above tumor
• Gastric conduit delivered to chest
• Stapled anastomosis (EEA 25mm)
• Tested with air/saline - watertight
• Nasogastric tube positioned under vision
• Two chest tubes placed

FINDINGS:
• Tumor in mid-distal esophagus, good response
• No unexpected findings
• Complete (R0) resection achieved

EBL: 350 mL
COMPLICATIONS: None

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Learning Points

### Case Summary

```
╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                      KEY LEARNING POINTS                       ║
╠═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╣
║                                                                ║
║  1. TREATMENT SEQUENCE FOR LOCALLY ADVANCED DISEASE           ║
║     • Neoadjuvant chemoradiation (CROSS protocol)            ║
║     • Restaging to assess response                            ║
║     • Esophagectomy if no progression                         ║
║                                                                ║
║  2. GASTRIC CONDUIT = FIRST CHOICE                            ║
║     • Single anastomosis                                       ║
║     • Preserve right gastric + right gastroepiploic          ║
║     • Divide left gastric + short gastrics                   ║
║                                                                ║
║  3. PYLOROPLASTY/PYLOROMYOTOMY                                ║
║     • Vagotomy effect from surgery                            ║
║     • Prevents delayed gastric emptying                       ║
║                                                                ║
║  4. ANASTOMOTIC CONSIDERATIONS                                 ║
║     • Intrathoracic: Lower leak rate, but more severe if leak║
║     • Post-op contrast study before oral intake              ║
║                                                                ║
║  5. NUTRITIONAL SUPPORT                                        ║
║     • J-tube for early enteral nutrition                     ║
║     • Slow diet progression over weeks                        ║
║     • Small, frequent meals long-term                         ║
║                                                                ║
║  6. EXCELLENT OUTCOME IN THIS CASE                             ║
║     • Near-complete pathologic response                       ║
║     • R0 resection, N0 disease                               ║
║     • Good functional recovery                                 ║
║                                                                ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

## Alternative Scenario

### What if No Stomach Available?

```
ALTERNATIVE: PREVIOUS GASTRECTOMY
═════════════════════════════════

Scenario: Same tumor, but patient had total gastrectomy
          for gastric cancer 10 years ago

Conduit Options:
1. COLON INTERPOSITION (first choice)
   - Left colon based on marginal artery
   - Preop colonoscopy to assess
   - Three anastomoses required

2. JEJUNAL INTERPOSITION
   - If colon not suitable
   - Free jejunal flap for pharyngeal

Route: Posterior mediastinal (in esophageal bed)

Challenges:
- More complex surgery
- Higher leak rate (10-15%)
- Longer hospital stay
- But achieves same oncologic goals
```
