# Case Scenario: GERD Surgical Evaluation

## Clinical Vignette

### Initial Presentation

**Patient:** 38-year-old female teacher

**Chief Complaint:** "My heartburn is ruining my life"

**History of Present Illness:**
- 8-year history of GERD symptoms
- Heartburn daily, worse after meals and at night
- Regurgitation of bitter fluid, especially when lying down
- Nocturnal awakening 3-4 times/week due to reflux
- Currently on omeprazole 40mg twice daily
- Symptoms improved but not resolved on PPI
- Recently noticed occasional difficulty swallowing solids
- No odynophagia
- No weight loss (actually gained 5 kg)
- Frustrated with lifelong medication dependence

**Review of Systems:**
- Hoarseness in morning (improved by afternoon)
- Occasional throat clearing
- No asthma or respiratory symptoms
- No chest pain

**Past Medical History:**
- GERD (diagnosed 8 years ago)
- Anxiety disorder (on sertraline)
- No prior surgeries

**Family History:**
- Mother: GERD, on PPI
- Father: Type 2 diabetes
- No GI malignancies

**Social History:**
- Non-smoker
- Occasional wine (1-2 glasses/week)
- Married, 2 children
- Active lifestyle

**Medications:**
- Omeprazole 40mg BID
- Sertraline 50mg daily

## Clinical Reasoning: Phase 1

### Stop and Think

**Question 1:** This patient wants surgery for GERD. What workup is required?

<details>
<summary>Reveal Analysis</summary>

**Pre-operative Workup for Anti-Reflux Surgery (HEMP):**

1. **H - HRM (High-Resolution Manometry)**
   - **Essential** - cannot proceed without this
   - Rules out achalasia (would be contraindication!)
   - Assesses peristalsis → guides wrap selection

2. **E - EGD (Upper Endoscopy)**
   - Grade esophagitis
   - Rule out Barrett's esophagus
   - Exclude malignancy/stricture
   - Assess hiatal hernia

3. **M - pH Monitoring (24-hour)**
   - Confirms pathological acid exposure
   - Documents symptom correlation
   - Especially if EGD normal

4. **P - Pictures (Barium Swallow)**
   - Hiatal hernia size and type
   - Esophageal length assessment
   - Anatomical detail for surgical planning

</details>

## Clinical Reasoning: Phase 2

### Stop and Think

**Question 2:** Based on the workup, is this patient a surgical candidate? What procedure would you recommend?

<details>
<summary>Reveal Analysis</summary>

**Surgical Candidacy Assessment:**

**Criteria Met:**
✓ Confirmed pathological acid reflux (DeMeester 32.4)
✓ Positive symptom correlation (SI 68%, SAP 98%)
✓ Documented esophagitis (LA Grade B)
✓ Symptoms despite twice-daily PPI
✓ Normal esophageal motility (no contraindication)
✓ No achalasia
✓ Reasonable BMI (25)
✓ Patient desire for surgery

**Procedure Recommendation:**

**Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication (360°)**

Rationale:
- Normal motility → can tolerate full wrap
- Best reflux control with 360°
- 3 cm hiatal hernia → needs crural repair
- No concerns for short esophagus

**Alternative if weak motility:** Would have recommended Toupet (270°)

</details>

**Question 3:** What are the key surgical principles to follow?

<details>
<summary>Reveal Surgical Principles</summary>

**Floppy Nissen Principles (SCUFF):**

1. **Short** wrap: 2-3 cm (not >3 cm)
2. **Calibrated**: 50-56 Fr bougie
3. **Untethered** fundus: Divide short gastric vessels
4. **Fundus-to-fundus**: True fundus wraps around
5. **Fixed** to esophagus: Include muscle in sutures

**Additional Key Steps:**

- Complete hiatal dissection
- Identify and preserve vagus nerves
- Posterior crural closure
- "Shoeshine" test to confirm proper wrap

</details>

## Surgical Planning

### Pre-operative Discussion

**Surgeon's counseling:**

```
INFORMED CONSENT DISCUSSION
═══════════════════════════

PROCEDURE: Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication + Hiatal Hernia Repair

EXPECTED BENEFITS:
• 90-95% symptom improvement
• 70-80% off PPI long-term
• Addresses hiatal hernia

RISKS:
• Dysphagia (30-50% early, usually resolves)
• Gas-bloat, difficulty belching (10-15%)
• Inability to vomit (5-10%)
• Recurrent reflux (10-15% long-term)
• Surgical risks: perforation, bleeding, infection

ALTERNATIVES DISCUSSED:
• Continued medical therapy
• LINX device
• Partial wrap (Toupet) - not needed here

PATIENT DECISION: Proceed with laparoscopic Nissen
```

## Operative Procedure

### Operative Report

```
OPERATIVE REPORT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PROCEDURE: Laparoscopic Nissen Fundoplication with
           Hiatal Hernia Repair

ANESTHESIA: General endotracheal

POSITION: Supine, split-leg, reverse Trendelenburg

FINDINGS:
- 3 cm Type I sliding hiatal hernia
- Attenuated phrenoesophageal membrane
- No evidence of Barrett's or stricture
- Adequate esophageal length

PROCEDURE:

1. PORT PLACEMENT:
   - Five ports placed in standard configuration
   - Nathanson liver retractor

2. HIATAL DISSECTION:
   - Gastrohepatic ligament divided
   - Right and left crura identified
   - Posterior window created
   - Both vagus nerves identified and preserved
   - Hernia sac reduced into abdomen

3. FUNDIC MOBILIZATION:
   - Short gastric vessels divided (x5)
   - Fundus completely mobilized
   - Floppy fundus confirmed

4. CRURAL CLOSURE:
   - Posterior cruroplasty performed
   - 3 interrupted 0 Ethibond sutures
   - Admits one finger alongside esophagus

5. FUNDOPLICATION:
   - 56 Fr bougie placed
   - Fundus passed posterior to esophagus
   - "Shoeshine" maneuver - fundus passes freely
   - 3 interrupted 2-0 silk sutures
   - Esophageal wall incorporated
   - 2.5 cm wrap created
   - Bougie removed

6. VERIFICATION:
   - Intraoperative EGD performed
   - Wrap appears symmetric on retroflexion
   - No mucosal injury
   - Scope passes through wrap easily

ESTIMATED BLOOD LOSS: 25 mL
COMPLICATIONS: None
SPECIMENS: None

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

MEDICATIONS:
• STOP PPI (omeprazole) - no longer needed

• Continue sertraline

ACTIVITY:
• No heavy lifting (>10 lbs) for 4 weeks
• Walking encouraged

FOLLOW-UP: 2 weeks post-op

CALL IF:
• Fever >38.5°C
• Severe abdominal pain
• Unable to tolerate any liquids

## Alternative Scenario

### What if Weak Motility?

```
ALTERNATIVE: WEAK MOTILITY SCENARIO
═══════════════════════════════════

If HRM showed: IEM (75% ineffective swallows)

Change in Plan:
• Recommend TOUPET (270°) instead of Nissen
• Partial wrap = less dysphagia risk
• Still effective for reflux (85-90%)

Operative difference:
• Pass fundus posterior (same)
• Suture to LEFT crus
• Suture to left esophageal wall
• Suture to RIGHT crus
• Suture to right esophageal wall
• Creates 270° wrap (90° gap anteriorly)

Expected outcomes:
• Similar reflux control
• Lower dysphagia rate
• Better for weak peristalsis
```
