## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Zenker Diverticulum:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Killian dehiscence (pharyngoesophageal junction) | — | Zenker's forms through Killian's triangle — the weak point between the thyropharyngeus and cricopharyngeus muscles |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Explain the pathogenesis — cricopharyngeal dysfunction creating a pulsion diverticulum through Killian's triangle
- Describe the classical presentation — dysphagia, regurgitation, halitosis, neck swelling (Boyce sign)
- Compare treatment options — endoscopic stapler diverticulotomy vs open cricopharyngeal myotomy + diverticulectomy

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

### Prerequisites

- **Esophageal Anatomy** — Knowledge of the pharyngoesophageal junction anatomy
- **Dysphagia** — Zenker's presents with dysphagia, regurgitation of undigested food, halitosis

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Zenker diverticulum perfectly illustrates how anatomy determines pathology — the muscular weakness at Killian's triangle creates a point of herniation when intrapharyngeal pressure is elevated. Understanding this anatomy-function relationship explains both the disease and its surgical correction (cricopharyngeal myotomy).
