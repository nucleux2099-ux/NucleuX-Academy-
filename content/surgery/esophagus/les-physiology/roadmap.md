## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into LES Physiology:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Physiology | Lower esophageal sphincter physiology | — | The LES is a 3-4 cm zone of tonic contraction — understanding its resting pressure and relaxation patterns is fundamental |
| Physiology | GI hormones affecting LES tone | — | Gastrin increases LES tone while CCK, secretin, and VIP decrease it — explains pharmacological manipulation |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe LES resting pressure, factors that increase/decrease tone, and the role of TLESRs
- Explain the components of the anti-reflux barrier — intrinsic LES, crural diaphragm, intra-abdominal esophagus
- Connect LES physiology to clinical conditions — GERD (low LES pressure), achalasia (high LES pressure with failed relaxation)

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **GERD** — LES incompetence is the primary mechanism of gastroesophageal reflux
2. **Achalasia** — LES failure to relax (opposite problem) — understanding normal function clarifies the pathology

### Prerequisites

- **Esophageal Anatomy** — Anatomical knowledge of the GEJ region provides the structural context

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

LES physiology is the critical bridge between esophageal anatomy and surgical pathology. Almost every esophageal surgical condition — GERD, achalasia, motility disorders — involves LES dysfunction. Understanding normal LES function illuminates what goes wrong and why specific surgical interventions work.
