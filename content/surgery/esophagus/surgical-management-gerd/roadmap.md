## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Apply the indications for anti-reflux surgery — failed medical therapy, volume reflux, large hiatal hernia, complications
- Describe the preoperative workup — EGD, manometry, pH monitoring — and interpret results
- Manage the post-operative patient — dysphagia, gas bloat, recurrent symptoms

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Surgical management of GERD integrates a complete preoperative workup (pH monitoring, manometry, endoscopy), anatomical surgery (crural repair, fundoplication), and evidence-based patient selection. The key principle is that surgery treats the anatomical defect (hiatal hernia, LES incompetence) that medical therapy cannot address.
