## Core Concepts (Shoot Phase)

### Definition & Overview

> [!summary] One-liner
> Achalasia is a primary esophageal motility disorder characterized by **incomplete LES relaxation** and **absent peristalsis** in the esophageal body, caused by degeneration of inhibitory ganglion cells in the myenteric plexus.

### Key Points

1. **Pathophysiology**: Loss of inhibitory neurons (NO, VIP) → unopposed excitatory input → LES fails to relax
2. **Cardinal features**: Dysphagia to BOTH solids AND liquids from onset + regurgitation
3. **Diagnosis**: High-resolution manometry (HRM) is gold standard; barium swallow shows "bird's beak"
4. **Treatment goal**: Disrupt the LES (not restore peristalsis) - PD, Heller myotomy, or POEM
5. **Cancer risk**: 0.5-1% annual risk of esophageal squamous cell carcinoma after 15-20 years

### Clinical Relevance

- **When to think about this**: Dysphagia to solids AND liquids equally, regurgitation, chest pain, weight loss
- **Red flags**: Rapid onset, age >55, significant weight loss → rule out pseudoachalasia (malignancy)
- **Decision points**: Chicago subtype guides treatment selection; Type II best outcomes, Type III most challenging

## Detailed Notes

### Anatomy Review - Lower Esophageal Sphincter

```
                    ESOPHAGEAL WALL LAYERS
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │  Mucosa (stratified squamous)               │
    │  Submucosa                                  │
    │  Muscularis propria                         │
    │    ├── Inner circular muscle ←── LES        │
    │    └── Outer longitudinal muscle            │
    │  Adventitia (no serosa above diaphragm)     │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    MYENTERIC (AUERBACH'S) PLEXUS
    - Located between circular and longitudinal muscle
    - Contains inhibitory neurons (NO, VIP) and excitatory neurons (ACh)
    - Coordinates peristalsis and LES relaxation
```

**LES Components**:
- Intrinsic: Smooth muscle thickening (3-4 cm)
- Extrinsic: Right crus of diaphragm (contributes ~50% of barrier)
- Normal resting pressure: 10-30 mmHg
- Normal relaxation: To gastric baseline with swallow

### Pathophysiology

#### The Core Defect

```
NORMAL LES RELAXATION:
Swallow → Vagal efferent → Myenteric plexus →
    Inhibitory neurons release NO + VIP →
        Smooth muscle relaxation → LES opens

ACHALASIA:
Swallow → Vagal efferent → Myenteric plexus →
    ❌ Inhibitory neurons ABSENT/DEGENERATED →
        Unopposed excitatory (ACh) input →
            LES fails to relax + no peristalsis
```

#### Etiology - What Kills the Neurons?

| Hypothesis | Evidence | Status |
|------------|----------|--------|
| **Autoimmune** | Lymphocytic infiltration of myenteric plexus; association with HLA-DQ | Leading theory |
| **Viral trigger** | HSV-1, measles, varicella antibodies elevated | Possible initiator |
| **Genetic** | Rare familial cases; Allgrove syndrome (AAA) | Minor role |
| **Chagas disease** | Trypanosoma cruzi destroys neurons | Secondary achalasia (endemic areas) |

**Histopathology**:
- Loss of ganglion cells in myenteric plexus
- Lymphocytic infiltration (CD3+ T cells)
- Fibrosis of myenteric plexus
- Relative preservation of excitatory neurons

#### Progression of Disease

```
Stage 1: Early
├── Ganglion cell loss begins
├── LES hypertensive but can still relax partially
└── Intermittent dysphagia

Stage 2: Established
├── Significant neuron loss
├── LES fails to relax
├── Absent peristalsis
└── Esophageal dilation begins

Stage 3: End-stage
├── Complete neuron loss
├── Massively dilated, tortuous esophagus ("sigmoid esophagus")
├── Megaesophagus
└── Aspiration, malnutrition
```

### Clinical Presentation

#### Symptoms

| Symptom              | Frequency | Characteristics                          |
| -------------------- | --------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Dysphagia**        | 95-100%   | Solids AND liquids equally from onset    |
| **Regurgitation**    | 75-90%    | Undigested food, often nocturnal         |
| **Chest pain**       | 40-60%    | Retrosternal, can mimic cardiac          |
| **Weight loss**      | 30-50%    | Progressive; red flag if rapid           |
| **Heartburn**        | 20-40%    | Paradoxical; from fermentation, not acid |
| **Aspiration/cough** | 10-30%    | Nocturnal, recurrent pneumonia           |

> [!tip] Clinical Pearl
> **"Solids AND liquids from the start"** = think achalasia
> **"Solids first, then liquids later"** = think mechanical obstruction (stricture, cancer)

#### Maneuvers Patients Use
- Raising arms overhead
- Standing/walking after eating
- Valsalva maneuver
- Drinking carbonated beverages
- Arching back

### Diagnosis

#### Diagnostic Algorithm

```
        Dysphagia to solids + liquids
                    │
                    ▼
        ┌───────────────────┐
        │  Upper Endoscopy  │ ← Rule out mechanical obstruction
        └─────────┬─────────┘
                  │
        ┌─────────▼─────────┐
        │ Barium Swallow    │ ← Bird's beak, dilated esophagus
        └─────────┬─────────┘
                  │
        ┌─────────▼─────────┐
        │ High-Resolution   │ ← GOLD STANDARD
        │ Manometry (HRM)   │   Chicago Classification
        └─────────┬─────────┘
                  │
            ┌─────┴─────┐
            ▼           ▼
      Type I/II      Type III
     (Classic)    (Spastic/Vigorous)
```

#### Investigations

**1. Upper Endoscopy (EGD)**
- Purpose: Rule out mechanical obstruction, pseudoachalasia (tumor)
- Findings in achalasia:
  - Dilated esophagus with retained food/saliva
  - "Puckered" or "rosette" LES
  - Resistance at GEJ but scope passes with gentle pressure
  - Normal-appearing mucosa
- **Critical**: Biopsy GEJ to exclude malignancy if suspicious

**2. Barium Swallow**

| Finding | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Bird's beak** | Smooth tapering at GEJ (pathognomonic) |
| **Dilated esophagus** | Loss of peristaltic waves |
| **Air-fluid level** | Retained barium and secretions |
| **Sigmoid esophagus** | Tortuous, massively dilated (end-stage) |
| **Epiphrenic diverticulum** | May be associated |

**3. High-Resolution Manometry (HRM) - Gold Standard**

**Chicago Classification v4.0 Criteria**:

| Parameter | Normal | Achalasia |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **IRP (Integrated Relaxation Pressure)** | <15 mmHg | **>15 mmHg** |
| **Peristalsis** | Normal | **Absent (100% failed)** |

**Chicago Classification Subtypes**:

| Type | Esophageal Body | Prevalence | Treatment Response |
|------|-----------------|------------|-------------------|
| **Type I** | Absent peristalsis, no pressurization | 20-40% | Good |
| **Type II** | Panesophageal pressurization (>20% swallows) | 50-70% | **Best** (>90% success) |
| **Type III** | Spastic contractions (≥20% premature, DCI >450) | 10-15% | **Worst** (variable) |

     No activity              Uniform pressure               Spastic waves
     (aperistalsis)           (panesophageal)               (premature)

**4. Timed Barium Esophagram (TBE)**
- Barium column height at 1, 2, 5 minutes
- Objective measure of esophageal emptying
- Useful for post-treatment follow-up

**5. EndoFLIP (Functional Lumen Imaging Probe)**
- Measures GEJ distensibility
- Emerging tool; useful intraoperatively
- Distensibility index <2.8 mm²/mmHg suggests inadequate myotomy

### Pseudoachalasia (Must Exclude!)

| Feature | True Achalasia | Pseudoachalasia |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Age** | Any (peak 30-60) | Usually >55 |
| **Duration** | Months to years | Weeks to months |
| **Weight loss** | Gradual | Rapid, significant |
| **Barium swallow** | Bird's beak, smooth | Irregular, shouldered |
| **EGD** | Passes with gentle pressure | May not pass; mass |
| **CT scan** | Normal | Thickening, mass, nodes |
| **Cause** | Primary | Cancer (GEJ adenocarcinoma, lung) |

> [!warning] Red Flags for Pseudoachalasia
> - Age >55 with new symptoms
> - Symptom duration <1 year
> - Rapid weight loss (>7 kg)
> - Difficulty passing scope at GEJ
> **→ CT chest/abdomen + EUS before treatment**

### Management

#### Treatment Goals
1. Relieve symptoms (dysphagia, regurgitation)
2. Improve esophageal emptying
3. Prevent complications (aspiration, megaesophagus)
4. **Note**: Cannot restore peristalsis - treatments disrupt LES only

#### Treatment Options Overview

| Treatment | Mechanism | Durability | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Pneumatic Dilation (PD)** | Controlled tear of LES muscle | 60-85% at 5 yrs (may need repeat) | Type I/II, older patients |
| **Heller Myotomy (LHM)** | Surgical division of LES muscle | 85-90% at 5-10 yrs | Type I/II, young patients |
| **POEM** | Endoscopic division of circular muscle | 90-95% at 2-3 yrs | Type I/II/III, failed PD |
| **Botox injection** | Inhibits ACh release | 50% at 6-12 mo | Elderly, high surgical risk |
| **Medications** | Smooth muscle relaxation | Minimal | Temporizing only |

#### 1. Pneumatic Dilation (PD)

**Technique**:
- Rigiflex balloon (30, 35, 40 mm)
- Fluoroscopic guidance
- Inflate to obliterate waist at LES
- Graded approach: Start 30mm → 35mm → 40mm if needed

**Outcomes**:
- Initial success: 85-90%
- 5-year success: 60-85% (often requires repeat dilations)
- Type II responds best

**Complications**:
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Perforation** | 2-5% | Gastrografin swallow post-procedure; surgery if large |
| **GERD** | 15-35% | PPI therapy |
| **Bleeding** | Rare | Usually self-limited |

#### 2. Laparoscopic Heller Myotomy (LHM)

**Technique**:
```
HELLER MYOTOMY STEPS:
1. Position: Supine, split legs, reverse Trendelenburg
2. Ports: 5 ports, liver retractor
3. Dissect GEJ, identify anterior vagus
4. Create window in gastrohepatic ligament
5. Mobilize distal esophagus (preserve vagus)
6. MYOTOMY:
   - Start on esophagus, 6-8 cm proximally
   - Extend onto gastric cardia 2-3 cm distally
   - Divide circular muscle fibers
   - Preserve longitudinal muscle and mucosa
7. Test for mucosal leak (air insufflation, methylene blue)
8. FUNDOPLICATION (partial):
   - Dor (anterior 180°) - preferred, covers myotomy
   - Toupet (posterior 270°) - alternative
9. Check hemostasis, remove liver retractor
```

**Key Technical Points**:
- Myotomy length: 6-8 cm esophageal + 2-3 cm gastric
- **Must extend onto stomach** or LES fibers remain
- Anterior (Dor) fundoplication covers myotomy, prevents reflux
- Avoid posterior vagus injury

**Outcomes**:
- Success rate: 85-95%
- 10-year durability: 80-85%
- GERD rate: 10-30% (less with fundoplication)

**Complications**:
| Complication | Incidence | Prevention/Management |
|--------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Mucosal perforation** | 5-10% | Intraop recognition → primary repair + Dor |
| **Incomplete myotomy** | 5-10% | Extend distally onto stomach |
| **GERD** | 10-30% | Add partial fundoplication |
| **Dysphagia recurrence** | 10-15% | May need PD or POEM |

#### 3. POEM (Peroral Endoscopic Myotomy)

**Technique**:
```
POEM PROCEDURE STEPS:
1. General anesthesia, supine, CO2 insufflation
2. Mucosal incision: 10-15 cm above GEJ (2 o'clock)
3. Submucosal tunnel: Inject saline, dissect submucosal plane
4. Extend tunnel to 2-3 cm onto gastric cardia
5. MYOTOMY:
   - Divide circular muscle fibers (±longitudinal for Type III)
   - Full thickness inner circular myotomy
   - Spare outer longitudinal muscle
6. Extend myotomy: 6-10 cm esophageal + 2-3 cm gastric
7. Close mucosal entry with clips
8. Gastrografin swallow next day
```

**Advantages over LHM**:
- No incisions, less pain
- Longer myotomy possible (useful for Type III)
- Posterior myotomy (less prior scarring)
- Shorter hospital stay

**Disadvantages**:
- Higher GERD rates (no fundoplication)
- Requires specific expertise
- Gas-related complications (pneumomediastinum, pneumoperitoneum)

**Outcomes**:
- Short-term success: 90-98%
- 2-3 year success: 90-95%
- GERD rate: 20-50% (higher than LHM)

**Complications**:
| Complication | Incidence | Management |
|--------------|-----------|------------|
| **Mucosal injury** | 3-5% | Clips, conservative |
| **Pneumomediastinum** | 10-20% | Usually benign, self-resolves |
| **Pneumoperitoneum** | 5-10% | Needle decompression if tense |
| **GERD** | 20-50% | PPI, may need fundoplication later |
| **Bleeding** | 1-2% | Endoscopic hemostasis |

#### 4. Botulinum Toxin Injection

**Mechanism**: Blocks ACh release at neuromuscular junction → temporary LES relaxation

**Technique**: EGD-guided injection, 100 units divided in 4 quadrants at LES

**Outcomes**:
- Initial response: 70-80%
- Duration: 6-12 months (repeat injections needed)
- Diminishing returns with repeat injections

**Indications**:
- Elderly patients with high surgical risk
- Bridge to definitive therapy
- Patient preference/refusal of surgery

**Downside**: Submucosal fibrosis makes future myotomy more difficult

#### 5. Medications (Limited Role)

| Drug | Class | Dose | Efficacy |
|------|-------|------|----------|
| Nifedipine | CCB | 10-20 mg SL before meals | Minimal |
| Isosorbide dinitrate | Nitrate | 5-10 mg SL before meals | Minimal |

**Use**: Temporizing only; not recommended as definitive therapy

### Treatment Selection Algorithm

```
                        ACHALASIA DIAGNOSED
                              │
                    ┌─────────┴─────────┐
                    ▼                   ▼
              Fit for intervention?   Unfit/elderly
                    │                   │
                    ▼                   ▼
              ┌─────┴─────┐         Botox or PD
              ▼           ▼
          Type I/II    Type III
              │           │
              ▼           ▼
    ┌─────────┴───────┐   │
    ▼                 ▼   │
  Young/            Older │
  prefer surgery    patient
    │                 │   │
    ▼                 ▼   ▼
  LHM + Dor      PD → POEM (preferred
  or POEM        (graded)  for Type III)
```

**Type-Specific Recommendations**:
| Type | First-line | Alternative | Rationale |
|------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Type I** | LHM or POEM | PD | Standard response |
| **Type II** | LHM, POEM, or PD | Any | Best outcomes with all modalities |
| **Type III** | POEM | Extended LHM | Longer myotomy needed for spastic segment |

### Outcomes Comparison

| Parameter | PD | LHM + Fundoplication | POEM |
|-----------|-----|---------------------|------|
| **Initial success** | 85-90% | 90-95% | 95-98% |
| **5-year success** | 60-85% | 85-90% | 90% (limited data) |
| **GERD rate** | 15-35% | 10-20% | 20-50% |
| **Perforation** | 2-5% | 5-10% (controlled) | 3-5% |
| **Hospital stay** | Outpatient | 1-2 days | 1-2 days |
| **Repeat procedures** | Often needed | Rarely | Rarely |

### Long-term Surveillance

**Cancer Risk**:
- Squamous cell carcinoma: 0.5-1% per year after 15-20 years
- Mechanism: Chronic irritation, stasis, bacterial overgrowth
- **Surveillance**: EGD every 3-5 years after 10-15 years of disease

**Follow-up**:
- TBE at 3-6 months post-treatment
- Symptom assessment
- EGD if symptoms recur or for cancer surveillance

### Special Situations

#### End-Stage Achalasia (Sigmoid Esophagus)
- Massively dilated (>6 cm), tortuous
- May not respond to myotomy/POEM
- Options:
  - Trial of POEM/LHM (some respond)
  - Esophagectomy if failed or megaesophagus

#### Failed Primary Treatment
- Failed PD → POEM or LHM
- Failed LHM → POEM (different approach - posterior)
- Failed POEM → Redo POEM (opposite side) or LHM
- Multiple failures → Esophagectomy

#### Achalasia + Hiatal Hernia
- Repair hernia at time of myotomy
- POEM may be challenging

### Quick Recall Questions

1. Q: What is the pathophysiologic basis of achalasia?
   A: Loss of inhibitory ganglion cells (NO, VIP) in myenteric plexus → unopposed excitatory input → failed LES relaxation + absent peristalsis

2. Q: What Chicago Classification type has the best treatment outcomes?
   A: Type II (panesophageal pressurization) - >90% success with any modality

3. Q: How long should a Heller myotomy extend onto the gastric cardia and why?
   A: 2-3 cm onto stomach; shorter extension leaves residual LES fibers causing persistent dysphagia
