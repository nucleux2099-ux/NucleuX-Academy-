## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Describe the steps of laparoscopic Heller myotomy including the extent of myotomy
- Explain the role of concomitant partial fundoplication (Dor/Toupet) in preventing post-myotomy reflux
- Manage intraoperative complications — mucosal perforation — and their repair

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Heller myotomy connects anatomy (GEJ, esophageal muscle layers), pathophysiology (failed LES relaxation from ganglion cell loss), and surgical technique (precise myotomy extent, fundoplication choice). The addition of Dor fundoplication addresses a key trade-off — relieving obstruction while preventing reflux.
