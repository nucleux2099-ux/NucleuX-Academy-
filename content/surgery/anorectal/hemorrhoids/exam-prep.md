# 📋 Hemorrhoids — Exam Prep Mode

---

## 🎯 One-Liners

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Hemorrhoids are | Engorged anal cushions (not varicose veins) |
| Primary hemorrhoid positions | 3, 7, 11 o'clock (lithotomy) |
| Most common symptom | Painless bright red bleeding per rectum |
| Dentate line significance | Internal above (painless), External below (painful) |
| Most effective office procedure | Rubber band ligation |
| Grade III hemorrhoids | Prolapse requiring manual reduction |
| Milligan-Morgan procedure | Open hemorrhoidectomy |
| Ferguson procedure | Closed hemorrhoidectomy |
| Stapled hemorrhoidopexy (PPH) | Reduces prolapse, less pain, earlier return |
| Thrombosed external hemorrhoid | Acutely painful, may need excision |

---

## 📝 Classification

**By Location:**
| Type | Location | Covering | Pain |
|------|----------|----------|------|
| Internal | Above dentate | Mucosa | Painless |
| External | Below dentate | Skin | Painful if thrombosed |
| Intero-external | Both | Both | Variable |

**By Grade (Internal):**
| Grade | Prolapse | Reduction | Treatment |
|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| I | No | N/A | Conservative/Banding |
| II | Yes | Spontaneous | Banding |
| III | Yes | Manual | Banding/Surgery |
| IV | Yes | Irreducible | Surgery |

---

## 🔬 Clinical Features

**Symptoms:**
- Bright red bleeding (on paper/in bowl)
- Prolapse
- Pruritus
- Mucus discharge
- Soiling

**Complications:**
- Thrombosis
- Strangulation
- Ulceration
- Anemia (chronic bleeding)

---

## 💊 Treatment Summary

| Grade | First Line | Second Line |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| I | Fiber + Banding | Sclerotherapy |
| II | Rubber band ligation | IRC |
| III | Banding (if <3) | Hemorrhoidectomy |
| IV | Hemorrhoidectomy | Stapled (PPH) |

**Contraindications to banding:**
- External hemorrhoids
- Anticoagulation
- Immunosuppression
- IBD

---

## ⚠️ Differentials for Rectal Bleeding

- Hemorrhoids (most common benign)
- Colorectal cancer (always exclude!)
- Anal fissure
- Diverticular disease
- IBD
- Polyps

**Remember:** Always examine properly and consider colonoscopy in:
- Age >40-50
- Change in bowel habit
- Weight loss
- Anemia
- Family history of CRC
