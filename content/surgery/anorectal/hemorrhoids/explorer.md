# 🔍 Hemorrhoids — Explorer Mode

> **The Big Picture:** Hemorrhoids are engorged anal cushions, not varicose veins. Internal vs external matters for treatment. Most can be managed non-operatively.

## 🎨 Grading of Internal Hemorrhoids

| Grade | Description | Treatment |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **I** | Bleed, no prolapse | Conservative + Banding |
| **II** | Prolapse, reduces spontaneously | Banding, Sclerotherapy |
| **III** | Prolapse, requires manual reduction | Banding, Hemorrhoidectomy |
| **IV** | Permanently prolapsed, irreducible | Hemorrhoidectomy |

---

## 💡 Key Atomic Concepts

### Atom 1: Anal Cushions, Not Varicose Veins
> Hemorrhoids are normal anal cushions that have become symptomatic (engorged, displaced). They contain arteriovenous connections, which is why bleeding is bright red.

### Atom 2: The 3, 7, 11 O'Clock Position
> Primary hemorrhoids occur at 3, 7, and 11 o'clock positions (lithotomy position) corresponding to terminal branches of superior rectal artery.

### Atom 3: Internal = Painless, External = Painful
> Internal hemorrhoids are above the dentate line (visceral innervation = no pain). External are below (somatic innervation = painful when thrombosed).

---

## 📊 Treatment Options

```
CONSERVATIVE
• High fiber diet, fluids
• Avoid straining
• Sitz baths
• Topical agents

OFFICE PROCEDURES (Grade I-II)
• Rubber band ligation (most effective)
• Sclerotherapy
• Infrared coagulation

SURGICAL (Grade III-IV)
• Open hemorrhoidectomy (Milligan-Morgan)
• Closed hemorrhoidectomy (Ferguson)
• Stapled hemorrhoidopexy (PPH)
• HAL-RAR (Doppler-guided)
```
