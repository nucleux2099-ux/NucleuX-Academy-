## 📐 Foundations

Study these **before** diving into Hemorrhoids:

| Subject | Topic | NMC Code | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Anatomy | Anal canal anatomy — dentate line, hemorrhoidal plexus | AN47.10 | The dentate line divides internal (painless, bleed) from external (painful) hemorrhoids |
| Anatomy | Portosystemic anastomosis | — | Portal hypertension causes secondary hemorrhoids via the superior rectal-middle rectal anastomosis |

## 🎯 Core Learning Objectives

- Classify hemorrhoids by degree (I-IV) and distinguish internal from external
- Select appropriate treatment — rubber band ligation, sclerotherapy, hemorrhoidectomy
- Recognize when hemorrhoids indicate portal hypertension or colorectal malignancy

## 🔬 Clinical Science Connections

|---------|-------|----------|------------|

## ➡️ What's Next

After mastering this topic, progress to:

1. **Anal Fissure** — Similar anorectal presentation; differentiation is a key clinical skill
2. **Rectal Prolapse** — Full-thickness rectal prolapse must be distinguished from Grade IV hemorrhoids

## 🔗 Cross-Subject Integration Points

Hemorrhoids connect anatomy (anal canal, portosystemic anastomosis), pathology (venous congestion), and medicine (portal hypertension, cirrhosis). A seemingly simple condition often demands understanding of portal hemodynamics, anorectal anatomy, and surgical technique selection.
