## Overview

Cardiac transplantation is the definitive treatment for **end-stage heart failure** refractory to medical and device therapy. It involves replacing the diseased heart with a donor heart from a brain-dead donor. Post-transplant management centers on **immunosuppression** to prevent rejection and surveillance for complications.

## Key Points

- **Most common indication:** Dilated cardiomyopathy and ischemic cardiomyopathy
- Patient must have **NYHA Class III-IV** symptoms despite optimal medical therapy
- **Median survival** post-transplant: ~12-13 years
- **Triple immunosuppression:** Calcineurin inhibitor + antimetabolite + corticosteroids
- **Acute rejection** detected by endomyocardial biopsy (gold standard)
- **Cardiac allograft vasculopathy (CAV)** is the leading cause of late graft failure
- Transplanted heart is **denervated** — no angina, relies on heart rate via catecholamines
- Increased risk of infections and malignancy due to immunosuppression

## Definition & Classification

Cardiac transplantation is an orthotopic procedure (donor heart placed in native position). Heterotopic (piggyback) transplant is rarely performed.

### Types of Rejection

| Type | Timing | Mechanism | Diagnosis |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Hyperacute** | Minutes-hours | Preformed antibodies | Rare with crossmatching |
| **Acute cellular** | Weeks-months | T-cell mediated | Endomyocardial biopsy |
| **Acute antibody-mediated** | Weeks-months | Donor-specific antibodies | C4d staining on biopsy |
| **Chronic (CAV)** | Years | Immune + non-immune factors | Coronary angiography/IVUS |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Indications
- **Dilated cardiomyopathy** (most common globally)
- **Ischemic cardiomyopathy** (most common in some series)
- Restrictive cardiomyopathy
- Congenital heart disease
- Failed prior transplant (retransplant)

### Contraindications
- **Absolute:** Active infection, active malignancy, irreversible pulmonary hypertension (PVR >5 Wood units), severe peripheral/cerebrovascular disease, active substance abuse
- **Relative:** Age >70, obesity (BMI >35), diabetes with end-organ damage, renal dysfunction

### Pathophysiology of Rejection
- **Cellular:** Donor MHC antigens recognized by recipient T-cells → lymphocytic infiltration → myocyte necrosis
- **Antibody-mediated:** Donor-specific antibodies against HLA → complement activation → endothelial injury
- **CAV:** Diffuse concentric intimal hyperplasia of coronary arteries (unlike focal atherosclerosis)

## Clinical Features

### Post-Transplant Physiology
- **Denervated heart:** Resting HR higher (~100 bpm), no vagal response
- No angina during ischemia
- Blunted response to Valsalva, carotid massage
- Heart rate increases via circulating catecholamines (slower response to exercise)

### Signs of Rejection
- **May be asymptomatic** early
- Fatigue, dyspnea, exercise intolerance
- New arrhythmias (atrial or ventricular)
- Signs of heart failure (JVP ↑, edema, S3)
- Fever (in acute rejection)

## Diagnosis

### Surveillance
- **Endomyocardial biopsy** — gold standard for acute rejection
  - Performed at scheduled intervals (weekly initially, then decreasing frequency)
  - ISHLT grading: 0R (no rejection) to 3R (severe rejection)
- **Echocardiography** — LV function, diastolic parameters
- **Gene expression profiling** (AlloMap) — non-invasive rejection monitoring
- **Donor-specific antibodies** — for antibody-mediated rejection

### CAV Detection
- Annual **coronary angiography** (may miss diffuse disease)
- **IVUS** (intravascular ultrasound) — gold standard for CAV
- **Dobutamine stress echo** — functional assessment

## Management

### Immunosuppression (Triple Therapy)
| Drug Class | Examples | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Calcineurin inhibitor** | Tacrolimus, Cyclosporine | Block T-cell activation via IL-2 |
| **Antimetabolite** | Mycophenolate, Azathioprine | Inhibit lymphocyte proliferation |
| **Corticosteroids** | Prednisolone | Broad anti-inflammatory |

### Induction Therapy
- **Anti-thymocyte globulin (ATG)** or **Basiliximab** (anti-IL2R)

### Rejection Treatment
- Mild: Increase immunosuppression
- Moderate-severe cellular: **IV methylprednisolone** pulse
- Antibody-mediated: **Plasmapheresis**, IVIG, Rituximab

### Infection Prophylaxis
- **CMV:** Valganciclovir (6-12 months)
- **PCP:** TMP-SMX
- **Fungal:** Nystatin or fluconazole

## Complications & Prognosis

### Early Complications (<1 year)
- Acute rejection (most common in first 6 months)
- Infections (CMV, PCP, fungal)
- Primary graft dysfunction
- Surgical complications

### Late Complications (>1 year)
- **Cardiac allograft vasculopathy** (leading cause of late death)
- Malignancy — PTLD (post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder), skin cancers
- Chronic kidney disease (calcineurin inhibitor nephrotoxicity)
- Metabolic: Diabetes, dyslipidemia, osteoporosis

### Prognosis
- 1-year survival: ~85-90%
- 5-year survival: ~75%
- Median survival: ~12-13 years
- Quality of life significantly improved

> **Clinical Pearl:** A transplanted heart does NOT respond to atropine (no vagal innervation). Use isoproterenol or temporary pacing for bradycardia.
