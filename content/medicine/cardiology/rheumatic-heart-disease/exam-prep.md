## 🧠 Mnemonics

### Jones Criteria — Major: **♥ JONES**
- **J** — Joint (polyarthritis)
- **♥** — Carditis
- **N** — Nodules (subcutaneous)
- **E** — Erythema marginatum
- **S** — Sydenham chorea

### Jones Criteria — Minor: **CAFE-P**
- **C** — CRP elevated
- **A** — Arthralgia
- **F** — Fever
- **E** — ESR elevated
- **P** — PR prolonged

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **Most common cause of acquired heart disease** in children worldwide = RHD
2. **Pathognomonic lesion** = Aschoff body with Anitschkow cells
3. **Most common valve affected** chronically = Mitral valve (stenosis)
4. **Most common acute valvular lesion** = Mitral regurgitation
5. **Chorea** is the ONLY major criterion that can independently diagnose ARF (no need for GAS evidence)
6. **Erythema marginatum + subcutaneous nodules** = almost always associated with carditis
7. **ASO titre** peaks at 3-5 weeks; **Anti-DNase B** better for skin infections
8. **Benzathine penicillin G** = drug of choice for both primary and secondary prophylaxis
9. **Aspirin** — dramatic response of arthritis (diagnostic clue)
10. **Steroids** — used only for severe carditis with heart failure

## 📋 Differentials of Migratory Polyarthritis

| Condition | Key Differentiator |
|---|---|
| ARF | Fleeting, large joints, dramatic aspirin response |
| Reactive arthritis | Post-enteric/urethritis, asymmetric, HLA-B27 |
| Gonococcal arthritis | Sexually active, tenosynovitis, pustular rash |
| SLE | ANA+, multi-system, young females |
| Infective endocarditis | Fever + new murmur + embolic phenomena |

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A 12-year-old presents with fever, migratory joint pain, and a new pansystolic murmur at the apex. ASO titre is elevated. What is the diagnosis?

> **ARF with carditis and polyarthritis** (2 major + evidence of GAS)

### Q2
What is the most common cause of mitral stenosis?

> **Rheumatic heart disease** (>95% of MS cases worldwide)

### Q3
A patient with known RHD and mitral stenosis presents with sudden-onset right hemiplegia. What is the most likely cause?

> **Thromboembolism from left atrial thrombus** (AF → stasis → clot → cerebral embolism)

### Q4
Duration of secondary prophylaxis in a patient with ARF + mild MR that has resolved?

> **10 years or until age 21** (whichever is longer) — carditis with no residual disease

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **Exam Trap**
> Arthritis in ARF is NEVER deforming. If there's joint deformity → think RA, not ARF.

> [!tip] **Carey Coombs Murmur**
> Short mid-diastolic murmur heard in acute rheumatic carditis due to valvulitis-induced turbulence. It is NOT mitral stenosis — there's no commissural fusion yet.

> [!warning] **Remember**
> Recurrent ARF attacks cause cumulative valve damage. Each attack worsens the prognosis. This is WHY secondary prophylaxis is lifelong in severe disease.

## Quick Revision Table

| Feature | ARF (Acute) | RHD (Chronic) |
|---|---|---|
| Valve lesion | MR | MS |
| Histology | Aschoff body | Fibrosis, calcification |
| Treatment | Penicillin + Aspirin/Steroids | Medical ± surgical (valvotomy/replacement) |
| Prevention | Primary prophylaxis | Secondary prophylaxis |
