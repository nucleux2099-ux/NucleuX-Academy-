# Ischemic Heart Disease (IHD)

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 272-274, Braunwald's Heart Disease
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Ischemic heart disease is a condition of myocardial oxygen demand-supply mismatch, primarily caused by atherosclerotic coronary artery disease. It encompasses a spectrum from stable angina to acute coronary syndromes (unstable angina, NSTEMI, STEMI). The pathophysiology involves plaque formation, rupture, and thrombosis leading to varying degrees of coronary occlusion.

## Key Points

- **Leading cause of death worldwide** - responsible for ~18 million deaths annually
- **Risk factors:** Modifiable (HTN, DM, smoking, dyslipidemia, obesity) and Non-modifiable (age, male sex, family history)
- **Angina equivalents:** Dyspnea, fatigue, diaphoresis - especially in diabetics and elderly
- **Coronary dominance:** Right dominant (85%), Left dominant (8%), Co-dominant (7%)
- **LAD = "Widow maker"** - supplies 45-55% of LV myocardium

## Clinical Features

### Stable Angina
- Retrosternal chest pain/pressure/heaviness
- Precipitated by exertion, emotional stress, cold weather
- Relieved by rest or sublingual nitrates within 5 minutes
- Duration: 2-10 minutes
- **Levine's sign:** Clenched fist over sternum

### Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS)
| Feature | Unstable Angina | NSTEMI | STEMI |
|---------|-----------------|--------|-------|
| Pain duration | >20 min | >20 min | >20 min |
| ECG | ST depression/T inversion | ST depression/T inversion | ST elevation |
| Troponin | Negative | Positive | Positive |
| Occlusion | Partial | Partial | Complete |

### Atypical Presentations
- Diabetics: Silent ischemia (autonomic neuropathy)
- Elderly: Dyspnea, confusion
- Women: Fatigue, nausea, back pain

## Diagnosis

### ECG Findings
- **STEMI:** ST elevation ≥1mm in limb leads, ≥2mm in chest leads
- **NSTEMI/UA:** ST depression, T wave inversion, or normal
- **Wellens syndrome:** Biphasic/deeply inverted T waves in V2-V3 (critical LAD stenosis)
- **De Winter T waves:** Upsloping ST depression + tall T waves (LAD occlusion)

### Cardiac Biomarkers
| Marker | Rise | Peak | Duration |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| Troponin I/T | 3-4 hrs | 24 hrs | 7-14 days |
| CK-MB | 4-6 hrs | 24 hrs | 48-72 hrs |
| Myoglobin | 1-2 hrs | 6-8 hrs | 24 hrs |

### Risk Stratification
- **TIMI score:** 7 variables (age, risk factors, prior stenosis, ST changes, symptoms, aspirin use, elevated markers)
- **GRACE score:** More accurate for mortality prediction
- **HEART score:** ED chest pain evaluation

### Investigations
1. **ECG** - First line, serial ECGs if initial normal
2. **Troponin** - High-sensitivity troponin preferred
3. **Echocardiography** - Wall motion abnormalities, EF assessment
4. **Coronary angiography** - Gold standard for anatomy
5. **CT Coronary Angiography** - Rule out CAD in low-intermediate risk
6. **Stress testing** - TMT, stress echo, nuclear imaging

## Management

### Acute Management of STEMI
**Time is muscle!**
- **Door-to-balloon time:** <90 minutes (primary PCI)
- **Door-to-needle time:** <30 minutes (thrombolysis if PCI not available)

**MONA-B Protocol:**
- **M**orphine (if pain persists)
- **O**xygen (only if SpO2 <90%)
- **N**itrates (sublingual/IV, avoid in RV infarct, hypotension)
- **A**spirin 325mg chewed
- **B**eta-blockers (avoid in cardiogenic shock, bradycardia)

**Antiplatelet/Anticoagulation:**
- Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitor (Ticagrelor preferred over Clopidogrel)
- Heparin (UFH or LMWH)
- GP IIb/IIIa inhibitors in high-risk PCI

**Reperfusion:**
- Primary PCI preferred (if available within 120 min)
- Thrombolysis: Streptokinase, Tenecteplase, Alteplase

### Long-term Management (Secondary Prevention)
**ABCDE approach:**
- **A**spirin + ACE inhibitors
- **B**eta-blockers + BP control
- **C**holesterol (Statins - high intensity), Cigarette cessation
- **D**iet + Diabetes control
- **E**xercise + Education

### Cardiac Rehabilitation
- Phase 1: Inpatient mobilization
- Phase 2: Outpatient supervised exercise (6-12 weeks)
- Phase 3: Maintenance (lifelong)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Inferior MI = RCA** (85%) - Check V4R for RV involvement, avoid nitrates
2. **Anterior MI = LAD** - Worst prognosis, highest mortality
3. **New LBBB + chest pain = STEMI equivalent** - Treat as STEMI
4. **Sgarbossa criteria:** Diagnose MI in presence of LBBB
5. **Contraindications to thrombolysis:** Active bleeding, recent stroke (<3 months), aortic dissection, BP >180/110
6. **Killip classification:** Predicts mortality in MI (Class I-IV)
7. **Dressler syndrome:** Post-MI pericarditis (2-10 weeks), treat with NSAIDs/colchicine
8. **Most common cause of death in first 24 hrs:** Ventricular fibrillation
9. **Primary PCI vs Thrombolysis:** PCI superior if available within 120 min of first medical contact
10. **Troponin:** Most sensitive and specific marker, remains elevated up to 14 days
