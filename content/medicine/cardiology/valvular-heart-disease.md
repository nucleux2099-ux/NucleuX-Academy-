# Valvular Heart Disease

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 261-262, Braunwald's Heart Disease, ACC/AHA Valvular Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Valvular heart diseases are disorders of cardiac valves causing stenosis (obstruction to flow), regurgitation (backflow), or both. They lead to pressure and/or volume overload on cardiac chambers, resulting in compensatory hypertrophy/dilatation, and eventually heart failure. Rheumatic heart disease remains the leading cause in developing countries, while degenerative disease predominates in developed nations.

## Key Points

- **Stenosis:** Pressure overload → Concentric hypertrophy
- **Regurgitation:** Volume overload → Eccentric hypertrophy (dilatation)
- **Acute vs Chronic:** Acute regurgitation is poorly tolerated (no time to compensate)
- **Most common valve affected in RHD:** Mitral valve
- **Most common valve lesion in developed world:** Aortic stenosis (degenerative)

## Mitral Stenosis (MS)

### Etiology
- **Rheumatic heart disease** (>95% in developing countries)
- Calcific (elderly)
- Congenital
- Carcinoid, SLE, RA (rare)

### Pathophysiology
Normal MVA: 4-6 cm² → Symptoms when <2.5 cm² → Severe when <1.0 cm²
- Obstruction → LA pressure ↑ → Pulmonary venous congestion
- Chronic → Pulmonary hypertension → RV failure

### Clinical Features
**Symptoms:**
- Dyspnea on exertion (earliest)
- Orthopnea, PND
- Hemoptysis
- Fatigue
- Palpitations (AF occurs in 40%)

**Signs:**
- **Malar flush** (mitral facies)
- Low volume pulse
- Irregularly irregular pulse (if AF)
- Tapping apex (palpable S1)
- **Auscultation:**
  - Loud S1 (if mobile valve)
  - Opening snap (closer to S2 = more severe)
  - Low-pitched mid-diastolic rumble (MDM) at apex
  - Presystolic accentuation (if sinus rhythm)

### Diagnosis
- **ECG:** LA enlargement (P mitrale), AF, RVH
- **CXR:** LA enlargement (double shadow, splayed carina), pulmonary congestion
- **Echo:** Gold standard - MVA, gradient, PASP, LA size, valve morphology
- **Wilkins score:** Predicts suitability for balloon valvuloplasty (≤8 favorable)

### Management
- **Medical:** Rate control for AF, anticoagulation (AF or embolic event), diuretics
- **Intervention (symptomatic + MVA <1.5 cm²):**
  - Percutaneous balloon mitral valvuloplasty (PBMV) - if favorable anatomy
  - Surgical: MV repair or replacement

## Mitral Regurgitation (MR)

### Etiology
**Primary (degenerative):**
- Mitral valve prolapse (most common in developed world)
- Rheumatic
- Infective endocarditis
- Connective tissue disorders (Marfan, Ehlers-Danlos)

**Secondary (functional):**
- Ischemic cardiomyopathy
- Dilated cardiomyopathy

### Clinical Features
**Symptoms:** Dyspnea, fatigue, palpitations

**Signs:**
- Displaced, hyperdynamic apex
- Soft S1
- **Pansystolic murmur (PSM)** at apex radiating to axilla
- S3 (volume overload)

### Severity Assessment
| Parameter | Mild | Moderate | Severe |
|-----------|------|----------|--------|
| Regurgitant volume | <30 mL | 30-59 mL | ≥60 mL |
| Regurgitant fraction | <30% | 30-49% | ≥50% |
| EROA | <0.2 cm² | 0.2-0.39 cm² | ≥0.4 cm² |

### Management
- **Medical:** Afterload reduction (ACEi/ARB), diuretics, treat AF
- **Surgery:** Repair preferred over replacement
  - Symptomatic severe MR
  - Asymptomatic with EF <60% or LVESD >40mm or new AF or PASP >50

## Aortic Stenosis (AS)

### Etiology
- **Degenerative/calcific** (most common in elderly)
- **Bicuspid aortic valve** (most common in <70 years)
- Rheumatic

### Pathophysiology
Normal AVA: 3-4 cm² → Severe when <1.0 cm² or gradient >40 mmHg
- LV pressure overload → Concentric LVH → Diastolic dysfunction → Eventually systolic failure

### Clinical Features
**Classic Triad (SAD):**
- **S**yncope (exertional)
- **A**ngina (even without CAD - supply-demand mismatch)
- **D**yspnea (heart failure)

**Prognosis after symptom onset:**
- Angina: 5 years
- Syncope: 3 years
- Heart failure: 2 years

**Signs:**
- Slow-rising, low volume pulse (pulsus parvus et tardus)
- Narrow pulse pressure
- Heaving, sustained apex
- Soft/absent A2
- **Ejection systolic murmur (ESM)** at right 2nd ICS radiating to carotids
- Reverse splitting of S2 (severe)

### Diagnosis
- **ECG:** LVH with strain pattern
- **CXR:** Boot-shaped heart, calcified aortic valve
- **Echo:** AVA, gradient, EF, LVH
- **Cardiac cath:** If discrepancy or pre-operative assessment

### Management
- **Medical:** Limited role, avoid excessive preload/afterload reduction
- **Intervention (symptomatic severe AS or asymptomatic with EF <50%):**
  - **SAVR** (Surgical AVR) - Gold standard
  - **TAVR** (Transcatheter AVR) - High/intermediate surgical risk, elderly
- **Avoid strenuous exercise** in severe AS

## Aortic Regurgitation (AR)

### Etiology
**Valvular:**
- Bicuspid valve
- Rheumatic
- Infective endocarditis
- Degenerative

**Aortic root:**
- Aortic dissection (acute)
- Marfan syndrome
- Syphilis
- Ankylosing spondylitis

### Clinical Features
**Symptoms:** Often asymptomatic until advanced; dyspnea, orthopnea, angina

**Signs - Hyperdynamic Circulation:**
| Sign | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Corrigan's pulse** | Water-hammer pulse |
| **Quincke's sign** | Capillary pulsations in nail bed |
| **De Musset's sign** | Head bobbing |
| **Muller's sign** | Uvula pulsation |
| **Hill's sign** | Popliteal > Brachial SBP by >60 mmHg (severe) |
| **Duroziez's sign** | Femoral artery bruit with mild compression |
| **Traube's sign** | Pistol shot femorals |

- Wide pulse pressure
- Displaced, hyperdynamic apex
- **Early diastolic decrescendo murmur (EDM)** at left sternal edge
- Austin-Flint murmur (functional MS from regurgitant jet hitting anterior mitral leaflet)

### Management
- **Medical:** Vasodilators (ACEi/ARB/nifedipine), treat underlying cause
- **Surgery (severe AR with):**
  - Symptoms
  - EF <50%
  - LVESD >50mm or LVEDD >70mm (LV dilatation)

## Infective Endocarditis (Brief)

**Modified Duke Criteria for diagnosis**
- **Major:** Positive blood cultures (typical organisms), Echo evidence
- **Minor:** Predisposition, fever, vascular/immunologic phenomena, suggestive echo

**Treatment:** Prolonged IV antibiotics, surgery if indicated

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Mitral stenosis:** Mid-diastolic murmur + opening snap + loud S1
2. **Severe MS:** Shorter S2-OS interval, longer MDM duration
3. **MS with AF → Anticoagulate** (high stroke risk)
4. **MR murmur:** Pansystolic, best at apex, radiates to axilla
5. **AS classical triad:** Syncope, Angina, Dyspnea (SAD)
6. **AS murmur:** ESM at right 2nd ICS radiating to carotids
7. **Severe AS = Soft/absent A2** + reverse splitting
8. **AR = Wide pulse pressure** + multiple eponymous signs
9. **Austin-Flint murmur:** Mid-diastolic murmur in AR (mimics MS)
10. **Acute severe MR/AR = Surgical emergency** (no time for compensation)
11. **Bicuspid aortic valve:** Most common cause of AS in <70 years
12. **All mechanical valves need lifelong warfarin** (target INR 2.5-3.5)
13. **Prosthetic valve endocarditis in first year:** S. epidermidis most common
