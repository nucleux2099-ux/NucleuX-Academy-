# Hypertension

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 277, JNC 8, ACC/AHA 2017 Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Hypertension is persistent elevation of systemic arterial blood pressure, defined as SBP ≥130 mmHg and/or DBP ≥80 mmHg (ACC/AHA 2017). It's the leading modifiable risk factor for cardiovascular disease and stroke. Primary (essential) hypertension accounts for 90-95% of cases, while secondary hypertension has identifiable causes. The pathophysiology involves complex interactions between cardiac output, systemic vascular resistance, and neurohormonal systems.

## Key Points

- **"Silent killer"** - Often asymptomatic until end-organ damage
- **Primary HTN:** 90-95% cases, multifactorial (genetics + environment)
- **Secondary HTN:** 5-10%, potentially curable if cause identified
- **Target organ damage:** Heart, brain, kidneys, eyes, peripheral arteries
- **BP measurement:** Average of ≥2 readings on ≥2 occasions

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Usually asymptomatic ("silent killer")
- Headache (typically occipital, morning)
- Dizziness
- Visual disturbances
- Epistaxis
- Symptoms of end-organ damage

### BP Classification (ACC/AHA 2017)

| Category | SBP (mmHg) | DBP (mmHg) |
|----------|------------|------------|
| Normal | <120 | <80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | <80 |
| Stage 1 HTN | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| Stage 2 HTN | ≥140 | ≥90 |
| Hypertensive Crisis | >180 | >120 |

### End-Organ Damage

| Organ | Manifestation |
|-------|---------------|
| **Heart** | LVH, CAD, Heart failure, AF |
| **Brain** | Stroke (ischemic/hemorrhagic), TIA, vascular dementia |
| **Kidney** | Hypertensive nephrosclerosis, CKD, proteinuria |
| **Eyes** | Hypertensive retinopathy (Keith-Wagener classification) |
| **Vessels** | Aortic dissection, PAD, aneurysms |

### Keith-Wagener Retinopathy Classification
- **Grade I:** Arteriolar narrowing (silver wiring)
- **Grade II:** AV nipping
- **Grade III:** Flame hemorrhages, cotton wool spots, exudates
- **Grade IV:** Papilledema (malignant HTN)

## Diagnosis

### Proper BP Measurement
- Rest 5 minutes, empty bladder
- Supported arm at heart level
- Appropriate cuff size (80% of arm circumference)
- Average of 2 readings, 1-2 min apart
- Measure both arms initially

### Secondary Hypertension Workup
**When to suspect:**
- Age <30 or >55 at onset
- Resistant HTN (uncontrolled on 3 drugs)
- Sudden worsening
- Hypokalemia
- Abdominal bruit
- Paroxysmal symptoms

| Cause | Clues | Screening |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Renal parenchymal** | CKD history, abnormal urinalysis | Creatinine, urinalysis, ultrasound |
| **Renovascular** | Abdominal bruit, flash pulmonary edema | Renal Doppler, MRA, CTA |
| **Primary aldosteronism** | Hypokalemia, adrenal mass | Aldosterone:Renin ratio (>30) |
| **Pheochromocytoma** | Paroxysms (5 P's: Pressure, Pain, Palpitation, Perspiration, Pallor) | 24h urine metanephrines, plasma metanephrines |
| **Cushing's** | Central obesity, striae, moon face | Overnight dexamethasone suppression |
| **Coarctation** | Upper > lower limb BP, rib notching | Echo, CT/MR angiography |
| **OSA** | Snoring, obesity, daytime somnolence | Polysomnography |
| **Thyroid** | Hyper/hypothyroid symptoms | TSH |

### Baseline Investigations
- Urinalysis
- CBC
- Basic metabolic panel (electrolytes, creatinine, glucose)
- Lipid profile
- 12-lead ECG
- Optional: Echocardiogram, urine albumin:creatinine ratio

## Management

### Lifestyle Modifications (All Patients)
**DASH diet approach:**
- **D**ietary changes (fruits, vegetables, low saturated fat)
- **A**lcohol limitation (≤2 drinks/day men, ≤1 women)
- **S**odium restriction (<2.3g/day, ideally <1.5g)
- **H**ealthy weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)

Additional:
- Regular aerobic exercise (150 min/week)
- Smoking cessation
- Stress management

### Pharmacotherapy

**When to start:**
- Stage 1 + ASCVD or 10-yr risk >10%: Start medication
- Stage 2: Start medication in all patients

**First-line agents:**
1. **ACE inhibitors/ARBs** - Preferred in DM, CKD, proteinuria, HF
2. **Calcium Channel Blockers** - Preferred in elderly, African Americans
3. **Thiazide diuretics** - Effective, low cost

**Compelling Indications:**

| Condition | Preferred Drug |
|-----------|----------------|
| Diabetes with proteinuria | ACEi/ARB |
| Heart failure (HFrEF) | ACEi/ARB + BB + diuretic |
| Post-MI | BB + ACEi |
| CKD | ACEi/ARB |
| CAD | BB, ACEi |
| Pregnancy | Methyldopa, Labetalol, Nifedipine |
| African American | CCB or Thiazide |
| Elderly/ISH | CCB or Thiazide |
| BPH | Alpha-blocker |

**Contraindications:**
- **ACEi/ARB:** Pregnancy, bilateral RAS, hyperkalemia
- **Beta-blockers:** Asthma, decompensated HF, bradycardia
- **Thiazides:** Gout, hyponatremia
- **CCBs (non-DHP):** Heart block, HFrEF

### Resistant Hypertension
**Definition:** BP uncontrolled on 3 drugs (including diuretic) at optimal doses

**Approach:**
1. Confirm true resistance (rule out pseudoresistance, white coat)
2. Screen for secondary causes
3. Add 4th agent: Spironolactone (most effective)
4. Consider renal denervation (investigational)

### Hypertensive Emergencies

**Emergency (end-organ damage) vs Urgency (no damage):**

| Emergency | Target | Drugs |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Aortic dissection | SBP <120 in 20 min | Esmolol + Nitroprusside |
| Acute pulmonary edema | 25% reduction in 1 hr | Nitroprusside, Nitroglycerin |
| Acute MI/ACS | Gradual reduction | Nitroglycerin, Beta-blockers |
| Hypertensive encephalopathy | 25% in first hour | Labetalol, Nicardipine |
| Acute stroke | Permissive HTN (see stroke guidelines) | Labetalol, Nicardipine |
| Eclampsia | DBP <105 | Labetalol, Hydralazine, MgSO4 |
| Pheochromocytoma crisis | Control gradually | Phentolamine, then Beta-blocker |

**Rule:** Lower BP by no more than 25% in first hour (except aortic dissection)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Most common cause of secondary HTN:** Renal parenchymal disease
2. **Aldosterone:Renin ratio >30** = Screen positive for primary aldosteronism
3. **Renovascular HTN:** Flash pulmonary edema, ACEi-induced AKI, abdominal bruit
4. **ACEi/ARBs contraindicated in pregnancy** (teratogenic) → Use methyldopa, labetalol
5. **Pheochromocytoma:** Alpha-blockade BEFORE beta-blockade (prevent hypertensive crisis)
6. **Thiazides:** Can cause hypokalemia, hyperuricemia, hyperglycemia, hypercalcemia
7. **Best 4th drug for resistant HTN:** Spironolactone
8. **Aortic dissection:** Most urgent - target SBP <120 in 20 minutes
9. **White coat HTN:** Office BP elevated, ambulatory BP normal - no treatment needed
10. **Masked HTN:** Office BP normal, ambulatory elevated - treat as HTN
11. **Coarctation:** Upper limb BP > Lower limb BP by >20 mmHg, rib notching on CXR
12. **JVP in HTN:** Normal (unlike in heart failure)
