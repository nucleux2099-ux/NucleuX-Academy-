## Overview

Pulmonary hypertension (PH) is defined as a **mean pulmonary artery pressure (mPAP) > 20 mmHg** at rest measured by right heart catheterization. It encompasses a diverse group of conditions that share elevated pulmonary pressures, ultimately leading to **right ventricular failure** and death if untreated.

## Key Points

- Classified into **5 WHO groups** based on etiology
- **Group 2 (left heart disease)** is the most common cause overall
- **Group 1 (PAH)** is the classic form with targeted therapies
- **Exertional dyspnea** is the earliest and most common symptom
- **Right heart catheterization** is the gold standard for diagnosis
- **Echocardiography** is the best initial screening test
- Treatment: Treat underlying cause; PAH-specific therapies for Group 1
- Prognosis: Poor without treatment; median survival 2-3 years for untreated PAH

## Definition & Classification

### WHO Classification (Updated Nice/2018)

| Group | Mechanism | Common Causes |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **1 — PAH** | Pulmonary arteriolar disease | Idiopathic, heritable, CTD, HIV, drugs |
| **2 — Left heart disease** | Elevated LA pressure | HFrEF, HFpEF, valvular (MS, MR) |
| **3 — Lung disease/hypoxia** | Hypoxic vasoconstriction | COPD, ILD, OSA |
| **4 — CTEPH** | Chronic thromboembolic | Organized PE |
| **5 — Multifactorial** | Various mechanisms | Sarcoidosis, myeloproliferative, metabolic |

### Hemodynamic Definitions

| Type | mPAP | PCWP | PVR |
|------|------|------|-----|
| **Pre-capillary** (Groups 1,3,4,5) | >20 | ≤15 | >3 WU |
| **Post-capillary** (Group 2) | >20 | >15 | ≤3 WU |
| **Combined** | >20 | >15 | >3 WU |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Group 1 — PAH
- **Idiopathic (IPAH):** Female predominance (F:M = 4:1)
- **Heritable:** BMPR2 mutation (autosomal dominant, incomplete penetrance)
- **Drug-induced:** Appetite suppressants (aminorex, fenfluramine), methamphetamine
- **Associated:** Connective tissue disease (scleroderma > SLE), HIV, portal hypertension, CHD

### Pathophysiology
1. Endothelial dysfunction → imbalance of vasoconstrictors (endothelin, thromboxane) vs vasodilators (NO, prostacyclin)
2. **Pulmonary arteriolar remodeling:** Intimal fibrosis, medial hypertrophy, plexiform lesions
3. Progressive increase in PVR → RV pressure overload
4. **RV hypertrophy** → dilation → failure
5. Reduced cardiac output → functional limitation

> **Clinical Pearl:** "Plexiform lesions" are pathognomonic for PAH (Group 1) and represent irreversible vascular remodeling.

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms (often delayed — diagnosis average 2 years after symptom onset)
- **Exertional dyspnea** (earliest and most common)
- Fatigue, weakness
- Chest pain (RV ischemia)
- Syncope (especially exertional — poor RV cardiac output)
- Peripheral edema, abdominal distension (RV failure)

### Signs
- **Loud P2** (pulmonic closure sound)
- **Right parasternal heave** (RV hypertrophy)
- **Elevated JVP** with prominent 'a' wave (RV stiffness)
- **TR murmur** (pansystolic at left lower sternal border, increases with inspiration)
- **PR murmur** (Graham Steell murmur — early diastolic)
- Hepatomegaly, ascites, peripheral edema (RV failure)
- Central cyanosis (right-to-left shunting or V/Q mismatch)

### WHO Functional Classification

| Class | Description |
|-------|------------|
| I | No limitation of physical activity |
| II | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest |
| III | Marked limitation; comfortable only at rest |
| IV | Unable to perform any physical activity; symptoms at rest |

## Diagnosis

### Echocardiography (Initial Screening Test)
- **Estimated RVSP** (from TR jet velocity) — >35 mmHg suggests PH
- RV dilation, hypertrophy
- Interventricular septal flattening ("D-shaped LV")
- RA dilation
- Pericardial effusion (poor prognostic sign)
- Assess LV function and valvular disease (Group 2)

### Right Heart Catheterization (Gold Standard)
- Measures mPAP, PCWP, CO, PVR
- **Vasoreactivity testing** — inhaled NO or IV epoprostenol
  - Positive response: mPAP drops ≥10 to ≤40 mmHg with stable CO
  - Positive responders → trial of CCB therapy

### Additional Investigations
- **CTPA:** Rule out chronic thromboembolic PH (Group 4)
- **V/Q scan:** Most sensitive for CTEPH (mismatched perfusion defects)
- **PFTs:** COPD, ILD (Group 3)
- **CT chest (HRCT):** ILD assessment
- **Polysomnography:** OSA
- **Blood tests:** BNP/NT-proBNP, ANA (CTD), HIV, LFTs (portopulmonary)
- **6-minute walk test:** Functional assessment and monitoring

## Management

### General Measures
- **Supplemental oxygen** (maintain SpO₂ >90%)
- **Diuretics** for fluid overload
- **Anticoagulation** — recommended in IPAH, CTEPH
- **Avoid pregnancy** (30-50% mortality in PAH)
- Supervised exercise rehabilitation

### PAH-Specific Therapies (Group 1)

| Pathway | Drug | Route |
|---------|------|-------|
| **Endothelin pathway** | Bosentan, Ambrisentan, Macitentan | Oral |
| **Nitric oxide pathway** | Sildenafil, Tadalafil (PDE5i) | Oral |
| **NO pathway** | Riociguat (sGC stimulator) | Oral |
| **Prostacyclin pathway** | Epoprostenol (IV), Treprostinil, Iloprost (inhaled) | Various |
| **Prostacyclin pathway** | Selexipag (IP receptor agonist) | Oral |

### Treatment Strategy
- **Vasoreactive:** CCB (nifedipine, diltiazem) — only ~10% are responders
- **Non-vasoreactive:** Combination therapy based on risk assessment
  - Low risk: Monotherapy (PDE5i or ERA)
  - Intermediate-high risk: Initial combination (ERA + PDE5i)
  - Refractory: Add prostacyclin; consider lung transplant

### Group-Specific Treatment
- **Group 2:** Treat heart failure; PH-specific drugs NOT recommended
- **Group 3:** Treat lung disease; supplemental O₂
- **Group 4 (CTEPH):** Pulmonary endarterectomy (potentially curative); Riociguat if inoperable
- **Group 5:** Treat underlying condition

## Complications & Prognosis

- **Right heart failure** (most common cause of death)
- Arrhythmias (atrial flutter/fibrillation)
- Hemoptysis (bronchial artery rupture)
- **Pericardial effusion** (poor prognostic marker)
- **Pregnancy-related mortality** (30-50%)

### Prognosis
- Untreated IPAH: Median survival ~2-3 years
- With modern therapy: 5-year survival ~60-70%
- **Poor prognostic factors:** WHO FC III-IV, elevated BNP, pericardial effusion, low 6MWD, low cardiac index

> **Clinical Pearl:** V/Q scan is the screening test of choice for CTEPH (Group 4). A normal V/Q scan essentially excludes CTEPH. CTPA may miss peripheral chronic thrombi.
