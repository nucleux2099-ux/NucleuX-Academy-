# Hypertension — Exam Prep

## High Yield Points

- ★ **Essential HTN** = 90-95% of cases; no identifiable cause
- ★ **ACC/AHA 2017:** HTN defined as ≥130/80; **ESC/ISH:** ≥140/90
- ★ **Renal artery stenosis** — most common cause of secondary HTN; FMD in young women, atherosclerotic in elderly
- ★ **Pheochromocytoma:** Episodic HTN + headache + sweating + palpitations → 24h urine metanephrines
- ★ **Conn's syndrome (primary aldosteronism):** HTN + hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis → ARR (aldosterone/renin ratio)
- ★ **First-line drugs:** ACEi/ARB, CCB, thiazide diuretics (all equal as monotherapy)
- ★ **ACEi/ARB** = preferred in DM, CKD with proteinuria, post-MI, HFrEF
- ★ **CCB (amlodipine)** = preferred in elderly, isolated systolic HTN, Black patients
- ★ **Drug of choice in pregnancy:** Labetalol (acute), Methyldopa/Nifedipine (chronic)
- ★ **Hypertensive emergency:** ≥180/120 + end-organ damage → IV labetalol/nitroprusside; reduce MAP by 25% in first hour

## MCQs

### MCQ 1
A 30-year-old woman presents with refractory hypertension. Abdominal bruit is heard. Renal Doppler shows unilateral renal artery stenosis. The most likely diagnosis is:

a) Atherosclerotic renal artery stenosis
b) Fibromuscular dysplasia
c) Takayasu arteritis
d) Polyarteritis nodosa

**Answer: b) Fibromuscular dysplasia**

*FMD causes "string of beads" appearance on angiography, typically affects young women, involves mid-distal renal artery. Atherosclerotic RAS affects ostium/proximal artery in elderly with multiple risk factors. Treatment: angioplasty (without stenting) for FMD. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 278*

### MCQ 2
A hypertensive patient has unprovoked hypokalemia (K+ 2.8), metabolic alkalosis, and suppressed renin. The most likely diagnosis is:

a) Renal artery stenosis
b) Cushing syndrome
c) Primary hyperaldosteronism
d) Liddle syndrome

**Answer: c) Primary hyperaldosteronism**

*HTN + hypokalemia + suppressed renin + elevated aldosterone = primary hyperaldosteronism (Conn's syndrome). Screen with aldosterone/renin ratio (ARR >30 with aldosterone >15). Confirm with saline suppression test. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 379*

### MCQ 3
Which antihypertensive is the drug of choice for a 65-year-old Black male with isolated systolic hypertension?

a) Enalapril
b) Amlodipine
c) Metoprolol
d) Losartan

**Answer: b) Amlodipine**

*CCBs and thiazides are preferred in elderly patients with ISH and in Black patients (who have low-renin hypertension, making ACEi/ARBs less effective as monotherapy). ALLHAT trial supports thiazides/CCBs in Black patients. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 278*

### MCQ 4
A 35-year-old pregnant woman at 28 weeks presents with BP 170/110 and proteinuria. The IMMEDIATE drug of choice is:

a) Enalapril
b) IV Labetalol
c) Oral nifedipine
d) Sodium nitroprusside

**Answer: b) IV Labetalol**

*Severe hypertension in pregnancy (preeclampsia) → IV labetalol is first-line for acute management. ACEi/ARBs are ABSOLUTELY CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy (teratogenic — renal agenesis). Nitroprusside → cyanide toxicity to fetus. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 278*

---

### MCQ 5
Target organ damage in hypertension includes all EXCEPT:

a) Left ventricular hypertrophy
b) Hypertensive retinopathy
c) Renal artery stenosis
d) Lacunar infarcts in brain

**Answer: c) Renal artery stenosis**

*RAS is a CAUSE of secondary HTN, not a consequence. Target organ damage: Heart (LVH, HF, CAD), Brain (stroke, lacunar infarcts), Kidney (nephrosclerosis, CKD), Eyes (retinopathy — Keith-Wagener-Barker classification), Vessels (aortic dissection). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 278*
