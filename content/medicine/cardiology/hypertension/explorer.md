## Overview

Hypertension (HTN) is defined as a sustained elevation of **systemic arterial blood pressure** ≥ 140/90 mmHg (or ≥ 130/80 mmHg per ACC/AHA 2017). It is the **most common modifiable cardiovascular risk factor** and affects ~30% of the adult population worldwide. **Essential (primary) hypertension** accounts for 90-95% of cases.

## Key Points

- **Essential hypertension** (90-95%) — no identifiable cause; multifactorial
- **Secondary hypertension** (5-10%) — renal, endocrine, vascular causes
- Most patients are **asymptomatic** until end-organ damage occurs
- **End-organ damage:** Heart (LVH, HF, CAD), Brain (stroke), Kidney (nephrosclerosis), Eyes (retinopathy), Vessels (aortic dissection)
- **JNC 8/ISH 2020** guidelines for treatment thresholds and targets
- First-line drugs: **ACEi/ARB, CCB, Thiazide diuretics**
- **Lifestyle modification** is cornerstone — DASH diet, salt restriction, exercise, weight loss
- BP target: <140/90 for most; <130/80 for high-risk (diabetes, CKD, established CVD)

## Definition & Classification

### JNC 7 / ESC Classification

| Category | Systolic (mmHg) | Diastolic (mmHg) |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Normal** | <120 | <80 |
| **Elevated** | 120-129 | <80 |
| **Stage 1 HTN** | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| **Stage 2 HTN** | ≥140 | ≥90 |
| **Hypertensive crisis** | >180 | >120 |

### ACC/AHA 2017 vs ESC 2018

| Guideline | HTN threshold |
|-----------|---------------|
| ACC/AHA 2017 | ≥ 130/80 |
| ESC 2018 / ISH 2020 | ≥ 140/90 |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Essential (Primary) Hypertension
- **Multifactorial:** Genetic predisposition + environmental factors
- Risk factors: High salt intake, obesity, alcohol, stress, family history, age, race (African Americans)
- Mechanisms: Increased peripheral vascular resistance, RAAS activation, sympathetic overactivity, endothelial dysfunction

### Secondary Hypertension — Causes

| Category | Cause | Clue |
|----------|-------|------|
| **Renal parenchymal** | CKD, GN | Proteinuria, raised creatinine |
| **Renovascular** | Renal artery stenosis | Young female (FMD) or elderly (atherosclerosis), bruit |
| **Endocrine** | Pheochromocytoma | Episodic HTN, headache, sweating, palpitations |
| **Endocrine** | Conn syndrome (primary aldosteronism) | Hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis |
| **Endocrine** | Cushing syndrome | Truncal obesity, striae, moon face |
| **Vascular** | Coarctation of aorta | Upper limb HTN, radio-femoral delay, rib notching |
| **Other** | OSA, drugs (OCP, NSAIDs, steroids) | Clinical context |

> **Clinical Pearl:** Suspect secondary HTN in: onset <30 or >55 years, resistant HTN (3 drugs + diuretic), sudden worsening, hypokalemia, or paroxysmal symptoms.

## Clinical Features

### Usually Asymptomatic ("Silent Killer")

### Symptoms (if present)
- Headache (occipital, morning), dizziness
- Visual disturbances
- Epistaxis
- Symptoms of end-organ damage (dyspnea, chest pain, claudication)

### End-Organ Damage Assessment

| Organ | Manifestation | Investigation |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| **Heart** | LVH, HF, CAD | ECG, Echo |
| **Brain** | Stroke, TIA, vascular dementia | CT/MRI |
| **Kidney** | Nephrosclerosis, CKD | Creatinine, eGFR, urine protein |
| **Eyes** | Hypertensive retinopathy | Fundoscopy |
| **Vessels** | Aortic aneurysm, PAD | ABI, imaging |

### Keith-Wagener-Barker Retinopathy

| Grade | Findings |
|-------|----------|
| I | Arteriolar narrowing, silver wiring |
| II | AV nipping |
| III | Flame hemorrhages, cotton-wool spots, hard exudates |
| IV | **Papilledema** (malignant hypertension) |

## Diagnosis

### Measurement
- Use **validated oscillometric device**
- Seated, rested 5 min, arm supported, appropriate cuff size
- Average of **2-3 readings** on **2-3 separate occasions**
- **Ambulatory BP monitoring (ABPM):** Gold standard for diagnosis
- **Home BP monitoring:** Average ≥ 135/85 = hypertension

### Baseline Investigations
- **Blood:** FBS/HbA1c, lipid profile, renal function, electrolytes, uric acid
- **Urine:** Protein, microalbumin
- **ECG:** LVH (Sokolow-Lyon, Cornell criteria)
- **Echocardiography:** LVH, diastolic dysfunction
- **Fundoscopy**

### Screen for Secondary Causes (if indicated)
- Renal: USG, renal Doppler, creatinine
- Aldosteronism: Aldosterone-renin ratio
- Pheochromocytoma: 24-hr urine metanephrines/catecholamines
- Cushing: Overnight dexamethasone suppression test
- Coarctation: CT angiography

## Management

### Lifestyle Modification (DASH approach)
- **Dietary salt restriction** < 6g/day (most impactful)
- **DASH diet** (fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, whole grains)
- **Weight loss** (1 mmHg drop per kg lost)
- **Exercise** (150 min/week moderate aerobic)
- **Alcohol limitation** (≤2 drinks/day men, ≤1 women)
- **Smoking cessation**

### Pharmacological — First-Line Agents

| Drug Class | Examples | Preferred In | Avoid In |
|-----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| **ACEi** | Enalapril, Ramipril | DM, CKD, HF, post-MI | Pregnancy, bilateral RAS, hyperkalemia |
| **ARB** | Losartan, Telmisartan | ACEi intolerant (cough) | Same as ACEi |
| **CCB (DHP)** | Amlodipine, Nifedipine | Elderly, isolated systolic HTN | HFrEF (for DHP — less concern) |
| **Thiazide** | Chlorthalidone, HCTZ | Elderly, osteoporosis | Gout, hyponatremia |

### Combination Therapy
- Most patients need **≥2 drugs**
- Preferred combinations: ACEi/ARB + CCB; ACEi/ARB + Thiazide
- **Never combine ACEi + ARB** (ONTARGET trial — increased adverse events)

### Resistant Hypertension
- BP above target despite **3 drugs including a diuretic** at optimal doses
- Add **spironolactone** (PATHWAY-2 trial)
- Screen for secondary causes

## Complications & Prognosis

### Hypertensive Emergencies
- **BP >180/120** with acute end-organ damage
- Hypertensive encephalopathy, acute stroke, aortic dissection, acute MI, acute pulmonary edema, eclampsia
- Treatment: **IV labetalol, nitroprusside, nicardipine** — reduce BP by 25% in first hour

### Long-term Complications
- **Heart:** LVH → HFpEF, CAD, AF
- **Brain:** Ischemic/hemorrhagic stroke, vascular dementia
- **Kidney:** Hypertensive nephrosclerosis → CKD → ESKD
- **Eyes:** Hypertensive retinopathy → blindness
- **Vessels:** Aortic aneurysm, peripheral vascular disease

### Prognosis
- Every 20/10 mmHg increase doubles CVD mortality
- Treatment reduces stroke risk by 35-40%, MI by 20-25%, HF by 50%

> **Clinical Pearl:** The most important number is the one the patient achieves consistently. Adherence to treatment is more important than the specific drug chosen.
