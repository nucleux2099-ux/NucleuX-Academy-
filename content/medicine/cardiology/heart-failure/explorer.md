# Heart Failure — Explorer

## Overview

**Heart failure (HF)** is a clinical syndrome resulting from structural or functional cardiac disorders that impair the ability of the ventricle to fill with or eject blood. It affects ~2% of the adult population and is the **final common pathway** of most cardiac diseases. HF is classified by ejection fraction into HFrEF (≤40%), HFmrEF (41-49%), and HFpEF (≥50%).

📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 257

## Key Points

- **HFrEF** (EF ≤40%) — systolic dysfunction; best-studied, most treatment options
- **HFpEF** (EF ≥50%) — diastolic dysfunction; common in elderly, hypertensive, obese women
- **BNP >100 pg/mL** or **NT-proBNP >300 pg/mL** — supports diagnosis of HF
- **NYHA classification** (I-IV) for functional assessment; **ACC/AHA stages** (A-D) for disease progression
- **Quadruple therapy for HFrEF:** ACEi/ARB/ARNI + Beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i
- ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan) superior to ACEi alone (PARADIGM-HF trial)
- SGLT2i (dapagliflozin/empagliflozin) benefit ALL EF categories
- **Do NOT give** CCBs (except amlodipine/felodipine), NSAIDs, or thiazolidinediones in HF

## Classification

### By Ejection Fraction
| Type | EF | Pathology | Typical Patient |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HFrEF** | ≤40% | Systolic dysfunction | Post-MI, DCM, myocarditis |
| **HFmrEF** | 41-49% | Mixed | Intermediate; may evolve |
| **HFpEF** | ≥50% | Diastolic dysfunction | Elderly, HTN, DM, obesity, AF |

### NYHA Functional Classification
| Class | Symptoms |
|---|---|
| I | No limitation; ordinary activity doesn't cause symptoms |
| II | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest, symptoms with ordinary activity |
| III | Marked limitation; comfortable at rest, symptoms with less than ordinary activity |
| IV | Symptoms at rest; unable to carry on any physical activity |

### ACC/AHA Stages
| Stage | Description | Key Point |
|---|---|---|
| A | At risk, no structural disease (HTN, DM, CAD) | Prevention |
| B | Structural disease, no symptoms (LVH, prior MI, low EF) | Prevent progression |
| C | Structural disease + current/prior symptoms | Standard HF therapy |
| D | Refractory HF requiring advanced therapies | Transplant, LVAD, palliative |

## Etiology

### Common Causes
- **Ischemic heart disease** — #1 cause worldwide (60-70%)
- **Hypertension** — #1 cause of HFpEF
- **Valvular heart disease** — MR, AS
- **Dilated cardiomyopathy** — idiopathic, alcoholic, peripartum, viral
- **Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy**
- **High-output failure** — thyrotoxicosis, anemia, AV fistula, Paget's, beriberi

### Precipitating Factors (FAILURE mnemonic)
- **F**orgetting medications (non-compliance)
- **A**rrhythmia (especially AF)
- **I**schemia / Infection
- **L**ifestyle (salt/fluid excess, alcohol)
- **U**pregulation (pregnancy, thyroid)
- **R**enal failure
- **E**mbolism (PE)

## Pathophysiology

### Neurohormonal Activation (Key Concept)
1. ↓ Cardiac output → ↓ renal perfusion
2. **RAAS activation** → angiotensin II + aldosterone → vasoconstriction, Na/water retention, fibrosis
3. **Sympathetic activation** → tachycardia, vasoconstriction, myocyte toxicity
4. **ADH (vasopressin)** → water retention → dilutional hyponatremia
5. **BNP/ANP** release (counter-regulatory) → natriuresis, vasodilation

> **Exam Pearl:** The goal of HF therapy is **neurohormonal blockade** — this is why ACEi/ARB + beta-blocker + MRA all improve survival.

### Ventricular Remodeling
- LV dilation, hypertrophy, fibrosis → progressive dysfunction
- β-blockers, ACEi/ARB, MRA all REVERSE remodeling

## Clinical Features

### Left Heart Failure (Backward → Pulmonary congestion)
- **Dyspnea** on exertion → orthopnea → PND (paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea)
- **Cough** (especially nocturnal, pink frothy sputum in acute pulmonary edema)
- **Bilateral basal crackles** on auscultation
- **S3 gallop** (most specific sign of LV failure)
- **Displaced apex beat** (cardiomegaly)

### Right Heart Failure (Backward → Systemic congestion)
- **JVP elevation** (most reliable sign)
- **Hepatomegaly** (tender, pulsatile in TR)
- **Pedal edema** → anasarca
- **Ascites**
- **Hepatojugular reflux** positive

### Signs of Low Output (Forward failure)
- Fatigue, exercise intolerance
- Cool extremities, narrow pulse pressure
- Altered mentation, oliguria

## Diagnosis

### Investigations
| Test | Finding |
|---|---|
| **BNP / NT-proBNP** | BNP >100, NT-proBNP >300 = HF likely; BNP <100 = HF unlikely |
| **Echocardiography** | GOLD STANDARD — EF, wall motion, valves, diastolic function |
| **Chest X-ray** | Cardiomegaly, cephalization, Kerley B lines, pleural effusion, bat-wing pulmonary edema |
| **ECG** | LVH, Q waves (prior MI), AF, LBBB (CRT candidate) |
| **Cardiac MRI** | Myocardial fibrosis, infiltrative diseases, viability |

### Framingham Criteria for Diagnosis
**2 Major** OR **1 Major + 2 Minor** criteria

| Major | Minor |
|---|---|
| PND | Bilateral ankle edema |
| Neck vein distension | Night cough |
| Rales | Dyspnea on exertion |
| Cardiomegaly (CXR) | Hepatomegaly |
| Acute pulmonary edema | Pleural effusion |
| S3 gallop | Tachycardia >120 |
| Hepatojugular reflux | ↓ Vital capacity by ⅓ |
| Weight loss >4.5 kg in 5 days with treatment | |

## Management

### HFrEF — Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT)

#### Quadruple Therapy (Start ALL early, titrate to target)

| Drug Class | Drug & Dose | Trial Evidence | Mortality Benefit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ACEi/ARB → ARNI** | Sacubitril/Valsartan 97/103mg BD | PARADIGM-HF | ✅ 20% ↓ |
| **Beta-blocker** | Carvedilol 25mg BD / Metoprolol succinate 200mg / Bisoprolol 10mg | COPERNICUS, MERIT-HF | ✅ 35% ↓ |
| **MRA** | Spironolactone 25-50mg / Eplerenone 50mg | RALES, EMPHASIS-HF | ✅ 30% ↓ |
| **SGLT2 inhibitor** | Dapagliflozin 10mg / Empagliflozin 10mg | DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced | ✅ |

#### Additional Therapies
- **Diuretics** — Furosemide (symptom relief, NOT mortality benefit); for congestion
- **Hydralazine + Isosorbide dinitrate** — Alternative if ACEi/ARB intolerant (especially Black patients — A-HeFT trial)
- **Ivabradine** — HR ≥70 despite max beta-blocker; pure sinus node If inhibitor
- **Digoxin** — Symptom relief + ↓ hospitalizations; NO mortality benefit; narrow therapeutic index

#### Device Therapy
| Device | Indication |
|---|---|
| **ICD** | EF ≤35%, NYHA II-III, on optimal GDMT ≥3 months (primary prevention of SCD) |
| **CRT (CRT-D)** | EF ≤35% + LBBB + QRS ≥150ms + NYHA II-IV on GDMT |

### HFpEF Management
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** — ONLY class with proven benefit (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER)
- Diuretics for congestion
- BP control, rate control for AF, treat comorbidities
- NO proven benefit from ACEi/ARB/beta-blockers in HFpEF

### Acute Decompensated HF
- **"LMNOP":** Lasix (furosemide IV), Morphine, Nitrates, Oxygen, Position (upright)
- **Furosemide** 40-80mg IV (double home dose)
- **Nitroglycerin** IV for preload reduction (if SBP >90)
- **Dobutamine** / **Milrinone** for cardiogenic shock
- **Norepinephrine** if hypotensive
- **NIV (BiPAP)** for acute pulmonary edema

## Complications & Prognosis

- **Arrhythmias** — AF (most common), VT/VF (sudden death accounts for 50% mortality)
- **Cardiorenal syndrome** — HF → renal dysfunction
- **Cardiac cachexia** — poor prognosis marker
- **Hyponatremia** — dilutional; serum Na <130 = poor prognosis
- **5-year mortality:** ~50% overall; Stage D = 75% 1-year mortality without advanced therapy
- **Prognostic markers:** EF, NYHA class, BNP, serum Na, peak VO2, renal function
