# Heart Failure

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 257, Braunwald's Heart Disease, ACC/AHA Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Heart failure is a clinical syndrome resulting from structural or functional cardiac abnormality leading to impaired ventricular filling or ejection of blood. It's characterized by the heart's inability to meet the metabolic demands of the body at normal filling pressures. The syndrome involves neurohormonal activation (RAAS, sympathetic nervous system) that initially compensates but eventually leads to maladaptive remodeling.

## Key Points

- **Prevalence:** 1-2% of adults, increasing with age (>10% in >70 years)
- **Two phenotypes:** HFrEF (EF ≤40%) and HFpEF (EF ≥50%), HFmrEF (41-49%)
- **Most common cause:** Ischemic heart disease (60-70% in developed countries)
- **Compensatory mechanisms:** Frank-Starling, Neurohormonal (RAAS, SNS), Ventricular remodeling
- **BNP/NT-proBNP:** Key biomarker - "wet and cold" if elevated

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms by Side of Heart

| Left Heart Failure | Right Heart Failure |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Dyspnea on exertion | Peripheral edema |
| Orthopnea | JVP elevation |
| PND (Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnea) | Hepatomegaly |
| Fatigue | Ascites |
| Nocturnal cough | Right hypochondrial pain |

### NYHA Functional Classification

| Class | Description | 1-Year Mortality |
|-------|-------------|------------------|
| I | No limitation | 5% |
| II | Slight limitation, symptoms with ordinary activity | 10% |
| III | Marked limitation, symptoms with less than ordinary activity | 20% |
| IV | Symptoms at rest | 50% |

### Physical Examination
- **Vitals:** Tachycardia, tachypnea, narrow pulse pressure
- **JVP:** Elevated (>8 cm), Kussmaul sign (RV failure)
- **Cardiac:** S3 gallop (most specific), displaced apex, murmurs
- **Lungs:** Bilateral basal crepitations, pleural effusion
- **Abdomen:** Hepatomegaly, hepatojugular reflux, ascites
- **Extremities:** Bilateral pitting edema, cool extremities

### Acute Decompensated Heart Failure (ADHF)
**Clinical profiles (Forrester/Nohria-Stevenson):**
- **Warm & Dry:** Compensated (adequate perfusion, no congestion)
- **Warm & Wet:** Most common (adequate perfusion + congestion) → Diuretics
- **Cold & Dry:** Hypovolemic (poor perfusion, no congestion) → Fluids cautiously
- **Cold & Wet:** Cardiogenic shock (poor perfusion + congestion) → Inotropes

## Diagnosis

### Framingham Criteria (2 Major OR 1 Major + 2 Minor)

**Major Criteria:**
- PND or orthopnea
- JVP elevation
- Rales
- Cardiomegaly
- Acute pulmonary edema
- S3 gallop
- Hepatojugular reflux

**Minor Criteria:**
- Bilateral ankle edema
- Nocturnal cough
- Dyspnea on exertion
- Hepatomegaly
- Pleural effusion
- Tachycardia (>120 bpm)

### Investigations

1. **BNP/NT-proBNP**
   - BNP <100 pg/mL or NT-proBNP <300 pg/mL rules out acute HF
   - BNP >400 pg/mL suggests HF
   - Elevated in: Renal failure, sepsis, PE, age, female sex

2. **Echocardiography** - Most important single test
   - EF assessment, wall motion, valvular function
   - Diastolic function (E/A ratio, E/e')
   - Structural abnormalities

3. **ECG** - Look for ischemia, arrhythmias, LVH, LBBB

4. **Chest X-ray:**
   - Cardiomegaly (CTR >0.5)
   - Cephalization of pulmonary vessels
   - Kerley B lines
   - Bat wing appearance (pulmonary edema)
   - Pleural effusion

5. **Additional:**
   - CBC, RFT, LFT, TFT, iron studies
   - Coronary angiography if ischemic etiology suspected
   - Cardiac MRI for infiltrative diseases

## Management

### HFrEF - Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT)

**The Fantastic Four (Mortality Benefit):**

1. **ACEi/ARB/ARNI**
   - ARNI (Sacubitril-Valsartan) preferred over ACEi
   - Start low, titrate to max tolerated dose
   - Monitor K+ and creatinine

2. **Beta-Blockers** (Carvedilol, Bisoprolol, Metoprolol succinate)
   - Start when euvolemic, titrate slowly
   - "Start low, go slow"
   - Reduce mortality by ~35%

3. **MRA** (Spironolactone, Eplerenone)
   - EF ≤35% + NYHA II-IV
   - Monitor K+ (avoid if K+ >5.0)
   - RALES trial: 30% mortality reduction

4. **SGLT2 Inhibitors** (Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin)
   - Benefit regardless of diabetes status
   - DAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced trials
   - Added to standard therapy

**Additional Therapies:**
- **Diuretics:** Symptom relief, no mortality benefit
- **Ivabradine:** If HR >70 on max beta-blocker
- **Hydralazine + Nitrate:** Alternative if ACEi/ARB intolerant (especially African Americans)
- **Digoxin:** Symptom control, reduces hospitalizations

### Device Therapy

- **ICD:** Primary prevention if EF ≤35% after 3 months of GDMT
- **CRT:** EF ≤35% + LBBB + QRS ≥150ms + NYHA II-IV
- **LVAD:** Bridge to transplant or destination therapy

### HFpEF Management
- Diuretics for congestion
- Treat underlying conditions (HTN, AF, CAD)
- SGLT2 inhibitors (EMPEROR-Preserved trial)
- No proven mortality benefit with ACEi/BB/MRA

### Acute Decompensated HF
- **Warm & Wet:** IV diuretics (Furosemide 40-80mg bolus → infusion)
- **Cold & Wet:** Inotropes (Dobutamine, Milrinone) + diuretics
- **Hypoxic:** NIV (CPAP/BiPAP), High-flow oxygen
- **Refractory:** Ultrafiltration, Mechanical support

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **S3 gallop = Volume overload** (systolic HF); **S4 = Stiff ventricle** (diastolic HF/HTN)
2. **BNP cut-off:** <100 rules out, >400 rules in
3. **ARNI contraindicated within 36 hours of ACEi** (angioedema risk)
4. **Beta-blockers:** DON'T start in acute decompensation
5. **EF ≤35% for 3 months despite GDMT → ICD**
6. **LBBB + EF ≤35% + QRS ≥150ms → CRT**
7. **Most common cause of RV failure:** Left heart failure
8. **Warm & Wet = Most common** ADHF profile
9. **African American patients:** Hydralazine + Isosorbide dinitrate beneficial (A-HeFT trial)
10. **Stage D HF:** Consider transplant evaluation, LVAD, palliative care
11. **Diuretic resistance:** Add thiazide (metolazone) for synergistic effect
12. **Iron deficiency:** IV iron if ferritin <100 or ferritin 100-300 with TSAT <20%
