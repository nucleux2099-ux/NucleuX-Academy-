# Cardiac Arrhythmias

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 252-256, Braunwald's Heart Disease
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Cardiac arrhythmias are disorders of heart rate, rhythm, or conduction resulting from abnormalities in impulse generation (automaticity), impulse conduction (re-entry, block), or both. They range from benign (sinus arrhythmia) to life-threatening (ventricular fibrillation). Understanding the mechanism guides management - re-entry responds to cardioversion, automaticity to rate control.

## Key Points

- **Normal conduction:** SA node → Atria → AV node (delay) → Bundle of His → Bundle branches → Purkinje fibers
- **SA node rate:** 60-100 bpm; AV node: 40-60 bpm; Ventricles: 20-40 bpm
- **Re-entry:** Most common mechanism (requires unidirectional block + slow conduction)
- **Triggered activity:** EADs (long QT), DADs (digoxin toxicity)
- **Rate vs Rhythm control:** For AF, both are equivalent for mortality

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Palpitations (awareness of heartbeat)
- Presyncope/Syncope
- Dyspnea
- Chest discomfort
- Fatigue
- Sudden cardiac death (VT/VF)

### Classification

| Type | Examples | Rate |
|------|----------|------|
| **Bradyarrhythmias** | Sinus bradycardia, Heart blocks | <60 bpm |
| **Narrow complex tachycardia** | Sinus tach, AVNRT, AVRT, AF, Flutter | >100 bpm, QRS <120ms |
| **Wide complex tachycardia** | VT, SVT with aberrancy, WPW | >100 bpm, QRS >120ms |

## Major Arrhythmias

### Atrial Fibrillation (AF)
**Most common sustained arrhythmia**

**Classification:**
- Paroxysmal: Self-terminating within 7 days
- Persistent: >7 days or requires cardioversion
- Long-standing persistent: >1 year
- Permanent: Accepted, no further rhythm control attempts

**ECG Features:**
- Irregularly irregular rhythm
- Absent P waves
- Fibrillatory baseline
- Variable RR intervals

**Complications:**
- Stroke (5x increased risk)
- Heart failure
- Tachycardia-induced cardiomyopathy

**CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score (Stroke Risk):**
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| CHF | 1 |
| Hypertension | 1 |
| Age ≥75 | 2 |
| Diabetes | 1 |
| Stroke/TIA | 2 |
| Vascular disease | 1 |
| Age 65-74 | 1 |
| Sex (female) | 1 |

**Anticoagulation:**
- Score 0 (male) or 1 (female): No anticoagulation
- Score 1 (male): Consider anticoagulation
- Score ≥2: Anticoagulation indicated
- DOACs preferred over warfarin (except mechanical valves, moderate-severe MS)

### Atrial Flutter
- **Typical:** Counter-clockwise re-entry around tricuspid annulus
- **ECG:** Sawtooth pattern, rate ~300/min, 2:1 block = ventricular rate 150
- **Treatment:** Rate control, anticoagulation (same as AF), catheter ablation highly effective

### AVNRT (AV Nodal Re-entrant Tachycardia)
- Most common SVT (60%)
- **Mechanism:** Dual AV nodal pathways
- **ECG:** Narrow complex, regular, P waves buried in QRS or just after (pseudo r' in V1)
- **Treatment:** Vagal maneuvers, Adenosine (drug of choice), catheter ablation curative

### AVRT (AV Re-entrant Tachycardia)
- Accessory pathway (Bundle of Kent in WPW)
- **Orthodromic:** Narrow complex (anterograde via AV node)
- **Antidromic:** Wide complex (anterograde via accessory pathway)
- **WPW pattern:** Short PR, delta wave, wide QRS in sinus rhythm
- **AF in WPW:** AVOID AV nodal blockers (Digoxin, Verapamil, Adenosine) → can cause VF

### Ventricular Tachycardia (VT)
**Wide complex tachycardia - always assume VT until proven otherwise**

**Classification:**
- Sustained: >30 seconds or hemodynamic compromise
- Non-sustained: <30 seconds
- Monomorphic: Single QRS morphology (structural heart disease)
- Polymorphic: Multiple morphologies (ischemia, Torsades)

**ECG Clues favoring VT over SVT with aberrancy:**
- AV dissociation (independent P waves)
- Capture/Fusion beats
- Very wide QRS (>160ms)
- Northwest axis
- Brugada criteria, Vereckei algorithm

**Torsades de Pointes:**
- Polymorphic VT with long QT
- "Twisting of points" around isoelectric line
- Causes: Drugs (antiarrhythmics, antibiotics), electrolytes (hypoK, hypoMg)
- Treatment: IV Magnesium, correct cause, overdrive pacing

### Ventricular Fibrillation
- Chaotic ventricular activity, no cardiac output
- **Treatment:** Immediate defibrillation, CPR, ACLS protocol

### Heart Blocks

**First Degree:** PR >200ms, all P waves conducted
**Second Degree:**
- Mobitz I (Wenckebach): Progressive PR prolongation until dropped beat, narrow QRS
- Mobitz II: Constant PR with sudden dropped beats, often wide QRS, risk of progression
**Third Degree (Complete):** Complete AV dissociation, P waves and QRS independent

## Diagnosis

### 12-Lead ECG - Systematic Approach
1. Rate and rhythm
2. P waves (morphology, relation to QRS)
3. PR interval (normal 120-200ms)
4. QRS duration (normal <120ms)
5. QT interval (corrected QTc)
6. ST-T changes
7. Axis

### Additional Investigations
- **Holter monitor:** 24-48 hour continuous recording
- **Event recorder:** Longer-term (weeks)
- **Implantable loop recorder:** Up to 3 years
- **Electrophysiology study:** Diagnostic and therapeutic
- **Echo:** Structural heart disease
- **Labs:** Electrolytes, TFT, troponin

## Management

### Acute Unstable Tachycardia
**Synchronized cardioversion** - AF/Flutter 120-200J, SVT/VT 100J, escalate as needed

### Acute Stable Narrow Complex Tachycardia
1. Vagal maneuvers (Valsalva, carotid massage)
2. Adenosine 6mg rapid IV push → 12mg → 12mg
3. If ineffective: Rate control (beta-blocker, diltiazem)
4. Cardioversion if persistent

### Acute Stable Wide Complex Tachycardia
- **If VT or uncertain:** Amiodarone, Procainamide, or cardioversion
- **If SVT with aberrancy (confirmed):** Treat as SVT
- **NEVER give Verapamil** empirically to wide complex tachycardia

### Bradycardia (Symptomatic)
1. Atropine 0.5-1mg IV (max 3mg)
2. Transcutaneous pacing
3. Dopamine or Epinephrine infusion
4. Transvenous pacing
5. Permanent pacemaker if indicated

### Antiarrhythmic Drugs - Vaughan-Williams Classification

| Class | Mechanism | Examples | Use |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----|
| Ia | Na+ block (moderate) | Procainamide, Quinidine | AF, VT |
| Ib | Na+ block (weak) | Lidocaine, Mexiletine | VT (ischemia) |
| Ic | Na+ block (strong) | Flecainide, Propafenone | SVT, AF (no structural heart disease) |
| II | Beta-blockers | Metoprolol, Esmolol | Rate control, SVT |
| III | K+ block | Amiodarone, Sotalol, Dofetilide | AF, VT |
| IV | Ca++ block | Verapamil, Diltiazem | SVT, AF rate control |

### Device Therapy
- **Pacemaker:** Symptomatic bradycardia, high-grade AV block, SSS
- **ICD:** Secondary prevention (survived VT/VF), Primary prevention (EF ≤35%)
- **Catheter ablation:** AVNRT, AVRT, typical flutter, AF, VT

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Irregularly irregular = AF** (most likely)
2. **Regular narrow complex at 150 bpm = Flutter with 2:1 block** (think of it!)
3. **Adenosine:** Diagnostic and therapeutic for SVT, contraindicated in asthma, WPW with AF
4. **WPW + AF = Procainamide or cardioversion** (avoid AV nodal blockers)
5. **Wide complex tachycardia = VT until proven otherwise**
6. **Torsades = Magnesium** (even if Mg level is normal)
7. **Long QT causes:** Drugs (ABCDE - Antiarrhythmics, ABx, Antiemetics, Antipsychotics, Antidepressants)
8. **Amiodarone side effects:** Pulmonary fibrosis, thyroid dysfunction, hepatotoxicity, corneal deposits, photosensitivity (check TFT, LFT, CXR regularly)
9. **Digoxin toxicity ECG:** "Scooped" ST depression, increased automaticity, decreased conduction
10. **Pacemaker code:** A = Atrium, V = Ventricle, D = Dual, I = Inhibited, R = Rate responsive
11. **CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2 = Anticoagulate** (DOACs preferred)
12. **Rate control drugs in AF:** Beta-blockers, CCBs (diltiazem/verapamil), Digoxin
