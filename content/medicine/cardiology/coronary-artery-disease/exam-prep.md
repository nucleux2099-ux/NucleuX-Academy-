## 🧠 Mnemonics

### STEMI Management: **MONA-B**
Morphine, Oxygen (if SpO₂<90%), Nitrates, Aspirin, Beta-blocker

### Secondary Prevention: **ABCDE**
Aspirin/ACE-I, Beta-blocker/BP, Cholesterol/Clopidogrel, Diet/Diabetes, Exercise

### Killip Classification (STEMI prognosis)
- **I** — No heart failure (6% mortality)
- **II** — Rales, S3, JVP raised (17%)
- **III** — Pulmonary edema (38%)
- **IV** — Cardiogenic shock (81%)

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **Most common artery involved** in MI = LAD ("widow maker")
2. **First investigation** in chest pain = 12-lead ECG within 10 min
3. **Gold standard biomarker** = Troponin I/T
4. **CK-MB** useful for detecting **re-infarction** (normalizes faster)
5. **Thrombolysis contraindicated** in: active bleeding, BP >180/110, recent stroke (<3mo), aortic dissection
6. **RV infarct** — triad: Hypotension + Clear lungs + Raised JVP. Avoid nitrates/diuretics → give IV fluids
7. **Dressler syndrome** — Post-MI pericarditis at 2-10 weeks, treat with aspirin/NSAIDs
8. **Most common complication** of MI = Arrhythmia (VF most common cause of death pre-hospital)
9. **Papillary muscle rupture** → Acute severe MR → Day 2-7 post-MI
10. **Free wall rupture** → Cardiac tamponade → Day 3-5 post-MI

## 📋 STEMI vs NSTEMI vs Unstable Angina

| Feature | STEMI | NSTEMI | UA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thrombus | Complete occlusive | Partial + micronecrosis | Partial, no necrosis |
| ECG | ST elevation | ST depression/T inv | ST depression/T inv |
| Troponin | ↑↑↑ | ↑ | Normal |
| Treatment | Emergent reperfusion | Early invasive (<24-72h) | Medical + risk stratify |

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A 55-year-old diabetic man presents with crushing chest pain for 2 hours. ECG shows ST elevation in II, III, aVF with ST depression in I, aVL. Which artery is involved?

> **Right coronary artery** (inferior STEMI). Do V4R to check for RV involvement.

### Q2
Most sensitive and specific biomarker for myocardial necrosis?

> **Troponin I/T** — Rises 3-6h, peaks 24h, remains elevated 7-14 days.

### Q3
A patient with acute inferior STEMI develops hypotension and clear lung fields. JVP is raised. What should you AVOID?

> **Nitrates and diuretics** — This is RV infarction. Give IV normal saline.

### Q4
A post-MI patient develops a new harsh systolic murmur on day 4 with hemodynamic collapse. Diagnosis?

> **Ventricular septal rupture** (harsh pansystolic murmur + thrill) or **papillary muscle rupture** (MR murmur). Urgent echo → surgery.

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **ECG Trick**
> ST elevation in aVR with diffuse ST depression = **Left main** or severe 3-vessel disease. This is NOT benign!

> [!warning] **Troponin Elevation ≠ MI**
> Troponin also rises in: PE, myocarditis, renal failure, sepsis, heart failure. Always correlate with clinical picture (Type 1 vs Type 2 MI).
