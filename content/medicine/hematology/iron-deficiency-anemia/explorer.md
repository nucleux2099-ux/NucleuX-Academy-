# Anemia — Approach & Classification — Explorer

## Overview

**Anemia** is defined as a reduction in hemoglobin concentration below the lower limit of normal (Hb <13 g/dL in males, <12 g/dL in females, <11 g/dL in pregnancy). It is not a diagnosis but a sign — always find the underlying cause. The **MCV-based approach** is the most clinically useful classification for exams.

📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 60, 97-100

## Key Points

- **Iron deficiency anemia** = #1 cause of anemia worldwide; **microcytic hypochromic**
- **MCV-based classification:** Microcytic (<80), Normocytic (80-100), Macrocytic (>100)
- **Reticulocyte count** differentiates production defects (low retic) from destruction/loss (high retic)
- **Peripheral smear** = most important investigation for morphological clues
- **Serum ferritin** = best initial test for iron stores (acute phase reactant — may be falsely normal in inflammation)
- **Iron deficiency:** ↓ ferritin, ↓ iron, ↑ TIBC, ↓ transferrin saturation
- **Anemia of chronic disease:** ↓ iron, ↓ TIBC, ↑/normal ferritin (iron trapping by hepcidin)
- **B12 deficiency:** Macrocytic + hypersegmented neutrophils + neurological features
- **Hemolytic anemia:** ↑ retic, ↑ LDH, ↑ indirect bilirubin, ↓ haptoglobin

## MCV-Based Classification

### Microcytic (MCV <80 fL) — "TAILS"
| Cause | Key Diagnostic Feature |
|---|---|
| **Thalassemia** | Microcytic with NORMAL/↑ iron stores; target cells; Hb electrophoresis |
| **Anemia of chronic disease** | ↓ iron, ↓ TIBC, ↑ ferritin; hepcidin-mediated |
| **Iron deficiency** | ↓ ferritin, ↑ TIBC, ↓ iron; pencil cells, target cells |
| **Lead poisoning** | Basophilic stippling; ↑ free erythrocyte protoporphyrin; ring sideroblasts |
| **Sideroblastic anemia** | Ring sideroblasts on bone marrow (Prussian blue stain) |

### Normocytic (MCV 80-100 fL)
| Cause | Key Feature |
|---|---|
| **Acute blood loss** | History, low retic initially → rises in 3-5 days |
| **Anemia of CKD** | ↓ EPO; treat with ESAs (erythropoietin-stimulating agents) |
| **Anemia of chronic disease** | Also can be normocytic |
| **Hemolytic anemias** | ↑ retic, ↑ LDH, ↓ haptoglobin |
| **Bone marrow failure** | Aplastic anemia, MDS, infiltration |
| **Mixed deficiency** | B12 + iron deficiency together |

### Macrocytic (MCV >100 fL)
| Cause | Key Feature |
|---|---|
| **Megaloblastic (B12/folate)** | Hypersegmented neutrophils (≥5 lobes); oval macrocytes |
| **Non-megaloblastic** | Alcohol, liver disease, hypothyroidism, MDS, drugs (methotrexate, AZT) |
| **Reticulocytosis** | High reticulocytes are large → pseudo-macrocytosis |

## Iron Deficiency Anemia — Deep Dive

### Iron Studies Comparison

| Parameter | Iron Deficiency | Chronic Disease | Thalassemia | Sideroblastic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Serum iron | ↓ | ↓ | Normal/↑ | ↑ |
| TIBC | ↑ | ↓ | Normal | Normal |
| Ferritin | ↓ (<30) | ↑/Normal | Normal | ↑ |
| Transferrin sat | ↓ (<20%) | ↓ | Normal | ↑ |

### Peripheral Smear
- **IDA:** Microcytic hypochromic, pencil cells (elliptocytes), target cells, ↑ RDW
- **Thalassemia:** Target cells, microcytic but normal RDW (uniform small cells)
- **B12 deficiency:** Oval macrocytes, hypersegmented neutrophils
- **Hemolytic:** Schistocytes (MAHA), spherocytes (hereditary spherocytosis, AIHA)

### Treatment of IDA
- **Oral iron:** Ferrous sulfate 325mg TID (65mg elemental iron TID) — take on empty stomach with vitamin C
- **IV iron:** If oral intolerant, severe anemia, CKD, malabsorption → IV ferric carboxymaltose, iron sucrose
- **Response:** Reticulocyte rise in 5-7 days; Hb rises 1g/dL per week
- **Duration:** Continue 3-6 months AFTER Hb normalizes (replenish stores)

## B12 Deficiency — Key Points

- **Causes:** Pernicious anemia (#1 in developed world), veganism, ileal disease/resection, D. latum, metformin
- **Neurological features** (NOT seen in folate deficiency): Subacute combined degeneration — demyelination of dorsal columns (loss of vibration/proprioception) + lateral corticospinal tracts (UMN signs)
- **Diagnosis:** Low serum B12; ↑ methylmalonic acid (most specific) + ↑ homocysteine
- **Treatment:** IM cyanocobalamin/hydroxocobalamin 1000μg daily × 1 week → weekly × 4 → monthly for life
- **Schilling test** — historical; identified cause of B12 malabsorption

## Hemolytic Anemia — Approach

### Intravascular vs Extravascular

| Feature | Intravascular | Extravascular |
|---|---|---|
| Site | RBCs destroyed in bloodstream | RBCs destroyed in spleen/liver |
| Hemoglobinuria | Present | Absent |
| Haptoglobin | Very low/absent | Low |
| Examples | PNH, MAHA (TTP/HUS), transfusion reaction, G6PD | Hereditary spherocytosis, AIHA, thalassemia |

### Key Hemolytic Anemias
- **G6PD deficiency:** X-linked; Heinz bodies + bite cells; triggered by fava beans, sulfonamides, primaquine, infections
- **Hereditary spherocytosis:** Osmotic fragility test ↑; spectrin defect; splenectomy curative
- **Sickle cell:** HbS; vaso-occlusive crises; target cells + sickle cells; hydroxyurea ↑ HbF
- **AIHA:** Coombs positive; warm (IgG — SLE, drugs) vs cold (IgM — Mycoplasma, EBV)
- **PNH:** CD55/CD59 deficiency; flow cytometry diagnostic; complement-mediated lysis; eculizumab
