# Diabetes Mellitus Type 2 — Explorer

## Overview

**Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM)** accounts for **90-95%** of all diabetes. It results from **insulin resistance** combined with **progressive β-cell dysfunction**. India has >100 million people with diabetes ("diabetes capital"). T2DM is strongly associated with obesity, sedentary lifestyle, and metabolic syndrome.

## Key Points

- **Most common** form of DM (90-95%)
- Pathophysiology: Insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency
- Strong association with **obesity and metabolic syndrome**
- Gradual onset; often diagnosed incidentally
- May present with **HHS** (not usually DKA)
- **Metformin** = first-line pharmacotherapy
- Cardiovascular disease = leading cause of death

## Definition & Classification

### Metabolic Syndrome (WHO/IDF/NCEP ATP III)

| Component | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Waist circumference** | ≥90 cm (M) / ≥80 cm (F) — Asian criteria |
| **Triglycerides** | ≥150 mg/dL |
| **HDL** | <40 mg/dL (M) / <50 mg/dL (F) |
| **Blood pressure** | ≥130/85 mmHg |
| **Fasting glucose** | ≥100 mg/dL |

*Diagnosis: ≥3 of 5 criteria (NCEP ATP III)*

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors
- **Obesity** (especially central/visceral) — strongest modifiable risk factor
- Physical inactivity
- Family history (strong genetic component; concordance ~90% in MZ twins)
- Age >45 (but increasingly younger)
- Ethnicity: South Asian, African, Hispanic — higher risk
- GDM history, PCOS
- Prediabetes (IFG, IGT)

### Pathophysiology — "Ominous Octet" (DeFronzo)

| Defect | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| **Muscle** | ↓ Glucose uptake (insulin resistance) |
| **Liver** | ↑ Hepatic glucose production |
| **Adipose** | ↑ Lipolysis → ↑ FFA → lipotoxicity |
| **Pancreas (β-cell)** | Progressive β-cell failure |
| **Pancreas (α-cell)** | ↑ Glucagon secretion |
| **GI tract** | ↓ Incretin effect (GLP-1, GIP) |
| **Kidney** | ↑ Glucose reabsorption (SGLT2) |
| **Brain** | Neurotransmitter dysfunction, appetite dysregulation |

### Natural History
1. Insulin resistance (years before diagnosis)
2. Compensatory hyperinsulinemia maintains normoglycemia
3. β-cell failure → prediabetes → overt diabetes
4. Progressive β-cell loss → eventual insulin requirement

## Clinical Features

### Presentation
- Often **asymptomatic** — diagnosed on screening
- Mild polyuria, polydipsia (less dramatic than T1DM)
- **Acanthosis nigricans** — velvety hyperpigmented skin (axillae, neck) — marker of insulin resistance
- Recurrent infections (skin, urinary, vaginal candidiasis)
- Poor wound healing
- Visual blurring (osmotic lens changes)
- **May already have complications at diagnosis** (30% have retinopathy at diagnosis)

### Associated Conditions
- Hypertension, dyslipidemia
- NAFLD/NASH
- Obstructive sleep apnea
- PCOS
- Gout

## Diagnosis

| Test | Prediabetes | Diabetes |
|---|---|---|
| **Fasting glucose** | 100-125 (IFG) | ≥126 mg/dL |
| **2-hr OGTT** | 140-199 (IGT) | ≥200 mg/dL |
| **HbA1c** | 5.7-6.4% | ≥6.5% |
| **Random glucose** | — | ≥200 + symptoms |

### Screening (ADA)
- All adults ≥35 years (or ≥25 for high-risk Asian Indians)
- Overweight/obese with ≥1 risk factor
- History of GDM
- Prediabetes — rescreen annually

## Management

### Stepwise Approach

| Step | Action | HbA1c Target |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Lifestyle modification (diet + exercise) + **Metformin** | <7% |
| **2** | Add second agent (based on comorbidities) | <7% |
| **3** | Triple therapy or insulin | <7% |
| **4** | Intensify insulin ± other agents | Individualize |

### Oral Hypoglycemic Agents

| Drug Class | Mechanism | Key Benefits | Key Side Effects |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Metformin** | ↓ Hepatic glucose production, ↑ insulin sensitivity | First-line; weight neutral/loss; CV benefit | GI upset, lactic acidosis (rare), B12 deficiency |
| **Sulfonylureas** (glimepiride, gliclazide) | ↑ Insulin secretion from β-cells | Rapid effect, cheap | Hypoglycemia, weight gain |
| **DPP-4 inhibitors** (sitagliptin, vildagliptin) | ↑ Incretin (GLP-1) levels | Weight neutral, low hypo risk | Pancreatitis (rare) |
| **SGLT2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) | ↓ Renal glucose reabsorption | **CV and renal protection**, weight loss | UTI, genital mycosis, euglycemic DKA, Fournier gangrene |
| **GLP-1 receptor agonists** (liraglutide, semaglutide) | ↑ Insulin, ↓ glucagon, ↓ gastric emptying, ↓ appetite | **CV benefit, weight loss** | GI (nausea), pancreatitis, medullary thyroid ca (rodents) |
| **Thiazolidinediones** (pioglitazone) | ↑ Insulin sensitivity (PPARγ) | Durable effect | Weight gain, edema, fractures, bladder cancer risk |
| **α-Glucosidase inhibitors** (acarbose, voglibose) | ↓ Carbohydrate absorption | PPG reduction | Flatulence, bloating |

### Landmark Trials

| Trial | Key Finding |
|---|---|
| **UKPDS** | Intensive glucose control ↓ microvascular complications; metformin ↓ CV events in overweight |
| **EMPA-REG** | Empagliflozin ↓ CV mortality in T2DM with CVD |
| **LEADER** | Liraglutide ↓ CV events |
| **DAPA-CKD** | Dapagliflozin ↓ CKD progression |
| **SUSTAIN-6** | Semaglutide ↓ CV events |

### Choosing Second Agent (ADA/EASD 2023)

| Comorbidity | Preferred Agent |
|---|---|
| **ASCVD** | GLP-1 RA or SGLT2 inhibitor |
| **Heart failure** | SGLT2 inhibitor |
| **CKD** | SGLT2 inhibitor (if eGFR sufficient) or GLP-1 RA |
| **Obesity** | GLP-1 RA or SGLT2 inhibitor |
| **Cost concern** | Sulfonylurea or thiazolidinedione |

## Complications & Prognosis

### Microvascular
- **Retinopathy** — NPDR, PDR, macular edema
- **Nephropathy** — microalbuminuria → ESRD
- **Neuropathy** — distal symmetric, autonomic

### Macrovascular (Leading cause of death)
- Coronary artery disease
- Cerebrovascular disease
- Peripheral arterial disease

### Prognosis
- CV disease = #1 cause of death
- SGLT2i and GLP-1RA have changed outcomes significantly
- HbA1c <7% reduces microvascular complications
- Multifactorial risk reduction (BP, lipids, glucose) is key
