# Diabetes Mellitus Type 1 — Explorer

## Overview

**Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM)** is an autoimmune disease causing **destruction of pancreatic β-cells**, leading to **absolute insulin deficiency**. It typically presents in children and young adults with polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, and may present acutely with **diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)**.

## Key Points

- **Autoimmune** β-cell destruction → absolute insulin deficiency
- Peak onset: **5-7 years** and **puberty**
- **DKA** may be first presentation
- Always requires **insulin therapy** (no role for oral hypoglycemics alone)
- **HLA-DR3, HLA-DR4** associations
- Autoantibodies: anti-GAD65, anti-IA2, anti-insulin, anti-ZnT8

## Definition & Classification

| Subtype | Features |
|---|---|
| **Type 1A (Autoimmune)** | 90%; autoantibodies positive, HLA-associated |
| **Type 1B (Idiopathic)** | 10%; no autoantibodies, more common in African/Asian descent |
| **LADA** | Latent autoimmune diabetes in adults; slow autoimmune destruction, initially resembles T2DM, GAD antibodies positive |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors
- **Genetic**: HLA-DR3/DR4 (chromosome 6p21) — strongest genetic association
- **Environmental triggers**: Viral infections (Coxsackie B, rubella, EBV), early cow's milk exposure, vitamin D deficiency
- **Concordance**: ~50% in monozygotic twins (vs ~90% in T2DM)

### Pathophysiology
1. Genetic susceptibility (HLA-DR3/DR4) + environmental trigger
2. **Autoimmune T-cell mediated** destruction of β-cells (CD8+ cytotoxic T cells)
3. **Insulitis** — lymphocytic infiltration of islets
4. Progressive β-cell loss → glucose rises when **80-90% of β-cells destroyed**
5. Absolute insulin deficiency → hyperglycemia, lipolysis, ketogenesis
6. **Honeymoon period** — transient remission after initial treatment (weeks-months)

### Autoantibodies

| Antibody | Significance |
|---|---|
| **Anti-GAD65** | Most sensitive for adult-onset T1DM and LADA |
| **Anti-IA2** | Associated with rapid progression |
| **Anti-insulin (IAA)** | Most useful in young children |
| **Anti-ZnT8** | Newest; present in ~60-80% |
| **ICA (islet cell antibodies)** | Historical marker |

## Clinical Features

### Classic Presentation — "3 P's + Weight Loss"
- **Polyuria** — osmotic diuresis
- **Polydipsia** — compensatory for fluid loss
- **Polyphagia** — with paradoxical weight loss
- **Weight loss** — catabolism of fat and protein due to insulin deficiency

### Acute Presentation — DKA
- Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain
- Kussmaul breathing (deep, rapid)
- Fruity breath odor (acetone)
- Dehydration, altered consciousness
- May be first presentation in ~30%

### Associations
- Other autoimmune diseases: **Hashimoto's thyroiditis** (most common), celiac disease, Addison's disease, pernicious anemia, vitiligo
- **Polyglandular autoimmune syndrome Type 2**: T1DM + Addison's + autoimmune thyroid disease

## Diagnosis

### Diagnostic Criteria (Same as general DM)

| Test | Diabetes |
|---|---|
| **Fasting glucose** | ≥126 mg/dL |
| **2-hr OGTT** | ≥200 mg/dL |
| **HbA1c** | ≥6.5% |
| **Random glucose** | ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms |

### Distinguishing T1DM from T2DM

| Feature | T1DM | T2DM |
|---|---|---|
| Age | Young (<30, but any age) | Older (>40, but increasingly younger) |
| Onset | Acute (days-weeks) | Gradual (months-years) |
| Body habitus | Lean | Usually obese |
| Ketosis-prone | Yes (DKA) | Usually not (HHS instead) |
| C-peptide | Low/absent | Normal/high (early) |
| Autoantibodies | Present (GAD, IA2) | Absent |
| Family history | Less strong | Strong |
| Treatment | Insulin always | Lifestyle → OHAs → insulin |

## Management

### Insulin Therapy (Lifelong)

| Regimen | Details |
|---|---|
| **Basal-bolus** (preferred) | Long-acting (glargine/detemir) + rapid-acting (lispro/aspart) before meals |
| **Insulin pump (CSII)** | Continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion; better control, fewer hypos |
| **Split-mix** | NPH + regular insulin (older regimen) |

### Monitoring
- **SMBG** (self-monitoring blood glucose) — 4-6 times/day
- **HbA1c target** — <7% (ADA); <6.5% if safe
- **Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM)** — increasingly standard

### Other Management
- **Carbohydrate counting** for dose adjustment
- **Diabetes education** — DSME
- Screen for complications annually (after 5 years/puberty)
- Screen for associated autoimmune diseases (thyroid, celiac)
- **Medical alert identification**

### Hypoglycemia Management
- Mild: Oral glucose (15 g fast-acting carbs), recheck in 15 min
- Severe (unconscious): IM/SC **glucagon** or IV dextrose
- Hypoglycemia unawareness: relax targets, CGM

## Complications & Prognosis

### Acute
- **DKA** — most feared
- **Hypoglycemia** — most common complication of insulin therapy
- Infections

### Chronic (Microvascular & Macrovascular)
- **Retinopathy** — leading cause of blindness in working-age adults
- **Nephropathy** — microalbuminuria → proteinuria → ESRD
- **Neuropathy** — distal symmetric, autonomic
- **Cardiovascular disease** — accelerated atherosclerosis
- **Diabetic foot**

### Prognosis
- Life expectancy reduced by ~11 years compared to general population
- Improved dramatically with modern insulin, CGM, pumps
- Good glycemic control (DCCT trial) reduces microvascular complications by 50-76%
