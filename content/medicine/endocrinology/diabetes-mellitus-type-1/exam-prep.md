# Diabetes Mellitus Type 1 — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Autoimmune β-cell destruction → absolute insulin deficiency. Young, lean, DKA-prone. HLA-DR3/DR4. Autoantibodies (anti-GAD65 most sensitive). Basal-bolus insulin is mainstay. DCCT trial: tight control ↓ microvascular complications.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Autoimmune** (Type 1A) = T-cell mediated β-cell destruction
- ★ **HLA-DR3/DR4** = strongest genetic association
- ★ **Anti-GAD65** = most sensitive autoantibody (especially in adults/LADA)
- ★ **C-peptide** = low/absent (vs normal/high in T2DM)
- ★ **DKA** may be first presentation (~30%)
- ★ **Honeymoon period** = transient remission after initial insulin treatment
- ★ **DCCT trial** = intensive insulin control reduces microvascular complications by 50-76%
- ★ **LADA** = "slow T1DM" in adults; GAD+, initially treated as T2DM
- ★ **Associated autoimmune diseases**: Hashimoto's (#1), celiac, Addison's, vitiligo
- ★ **Insulin** = mandatory; oral hypoglycemics alone are NEVER sufficient

## Mnemonics

- **T1DM features: "LDAY"** — Lean, DKA-prone, Autoimmune, Young
- **Autoantibodies: "GIZA"** — GAD65, IA2, ZnT8, Anti-insulin
- **Polyendocrine type 2: "AAT"** — Addison's, Autoimmune thyroid, Type 1 DM

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **T2DM** | Older, obese, gradual, C-peptide normal/high, no autoantibodies |
| **LADA** | Adult, slow onset, GAD+, initially responds to OHAs |
| **MODY** | AD inheritance, mild, young, specific gene mutations, C-peptide present |
| **Neonatal DM** | <6 months, KCNJ11/ABCC8 mutations, may respond to sulfonylureas |
| **Secondary DM** | Pancreatitis, Cushing's, steroids, hemochromatosis |
| **DKA from T2DM** | Rare "ketosis-prone T2DM" in African Americans |
