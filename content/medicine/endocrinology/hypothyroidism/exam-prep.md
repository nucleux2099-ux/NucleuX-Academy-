# Thyroid Disorders — Exam Prep

## High Yield Points

- ★ **Hashimoto's** = #1 cause of hypothyroidism; anti-TPO antibodies; can progress to thyroid lymphoma
- ★ **Graves'** = #1 cause of hyperthyroidism; TRAb/TSI positive; diffuse goiter + ophthalmopathy + pretibial myxedema
- ★ **Delayed ankle jerk relaxation** = classic sign of hypothyroidism
- ★ **De Quervain's (subacute) thyroiditis:** Painful thyroid + fever post-viral; elevated ESR; ↓ RAIU; self-limiting (hyper → hypo → euthyroid)
- ★ **Amiodarone thyroid toxicity:** Type 1 (↑ synthesis — in pre-existing thyroid disease) vs Type 2 (destructive thyroiditis)
- ★ **Agranulocytosis** = most dangerous side effect of anti-thyroid drugs; warn patient about sore throat/fever
- ★ **PTU preferred** in 1st trimester pregnancy and thyroid storm (blocks peripheral T4→T3)
- ★ **Thyroid storm treatment order:** PTU → Iodine (1h later) → Propranolol → Hydrocortisone
- ★ **Myxedema coma:** Give IV hydrocortisone BEFORE levothyroxine (prevent adrenal crisis)
- ★ **Pendred syndrome:** Goiter + sensorineural deafness (organification defect — positive perchlorate discharge test)

## MCQs

### MCQ 1
A 45-year-old woman has fatigue, weight gain, constipation, and periorbital puffiness. TSH is 45 mU/L, free T4 is 0.3 ng/dL. Anti-TPO antibodies are strongly positive. The diagnosis is:

a) Graves' disease
b) Hashimoto's thyroiditis
c) Subacute thyroiditis
d) Iodine deficiency

**Answer: b) Hashimoto's thyroiditis**

*Hypothyroidism (high TSH, low T4) + anti-TPO antibodies = Hashimoto's thyroiditis. Pathology: lymphocytic infiltration with germinal centers and Hürthle cell (oncocytic) change. Risk of thyroid B-cell lymphoma (MALT type). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 380*

### MCQ 2
A patient with Graves' disease becomes pregnant. The preferred anti-thyroid drug in the FIRST trimester is:

a) Carbimazole
b) Methimazole
c) Propylthiouracil
d) Radioactive iodine

**Answer: c) Propylthiouracil**

*PTU is preferred in 1st trimester because methimazole/carbimazole causes aplasia cutis and choanal atresia. Switch to methimazole in 2nd/3rd trimester (PTU → hepatotoxicity risk). RAI is absolutely contraindicated in pregnancy. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 381*

### MCQ 3
A patient on carbimazole develops sudden high fever and sore throat. CBC shows WBC 1200 with neutrophils 2%. The most likely complication is:

a) Drug-induced lupus
b) Agranulocytosis
c) Aplastic anemia
d) Thyroid storm

**Answer: b) Agranulocytosis**

*Agranulocytosis (ANC <500) is the most dangerous idiosyncratic reaction to thionamides (0.2-0.5%). Present with fever + sore throat. STOP the drug immediately. Give G-CSF + broad-spectrum antibiotics. Patient should never receive thionamides again. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 381*

### MCQ 4
A 30-year-old woman presents with painful swollen thyroid gland 2 weeks after an URTI. ESR is 85. RAIU is <5%. The most likely diagnosis is:

a) Hashimoto's thyroiditis
b) Graves' disease
c) De Quervain's (subacute granulomatous) thyroiditis
d) Acute suppurative thyroiditis

**Answer: c) De Quervain's (subacute granulomatous) thyroiditis**

*Post-viral + painful thyroid + very high ESR + low RAIU = De Quervain's. Natural course: thyrotoxicosis (4-6 weeks) → hypothyroidism → euthyroidism. Treatment: NSAIDs (mild) or steroids (severe pain). Self-limiting. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 380*

### MCQ 5
In thyroid storm, iodine (Lugol's solution) should be given:

a) Before anti-thyroid drugs
b) At least 1 hour AFTER anti-thyroid drugs
c) Simultaneously with anti-thyroid drugs
d) Only after beta-blockers

**Answer: b) At least 1 hour AFTER anti-thyroid drugs**

*PTU/methimazole must be given first to block new hormone synthesis. Iodine given 1h later blocks thyroid hormone RELEASE (Wolff-Chaikoff effect). If given before ATDs, iodine provides substrate for MORE T4/T3 synthesis (Jod-Basedow). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 381*
