# Diabetes Mellitus — Exam Prep

## High Yield Points

- ★ **HbA1c ≥ 6.5%** is diagnostic; reflects average glucose over 2-3 months (RBC lifespan)
- ★ **Anti-GAD65** is the most sensitive autoantibody for Type 1 DM
- ★ **HLA-DR3/DR4** — strongest genetic association with Type 1 DM
- ★ **C-peptide** differentiates Type 1 (low/absent) from Type 2 (normal/high)
- ★ **Metformin** — first-line for Type 2; contraindicated if eGFR <30; causes B12 deficiency
- ★ **SGLT2 inhibitors** — reduce CV mortality + slow CKD progression; cause euglycemic DKA
- ★ **GLP-1 RA** — best for weight loss + CV benefit; contraindicated in MEN2/medullary thyroid ca
- ★ **Diabetic nephropathy** — #1 cause of ESRD worldwide; first sign = microalbuminuria
- ★ **DCCT** (Type 1) and **UKPDS** (Type 2) proved tight glycemic control reduces microvascular complications
- ★ **Somogyi effect** = rebound hyperglycemia after nocturnal hypoglycemia; **Dawn phenomenon** = early morning hyperglycemia from GH/cortisol surge (no preceding hypoglycemia)

## Mnemonics

**OMINOUS OCTET** (8 pathogenic mechanisms of T2DM — DeFronzo):
- β-cell, α-cell, Liver, Muscle, Adipose, Gut (incretins), Kidney (SGLT2), Brain

## MCQs

### MCQ 1
A 25-year-old lean male presents with polyuria, polydipsia, and 8 kg weight loss over 2 months. His random blood glucose is 380 mg/dL. Which investigation will BEST confirm the type of diabetes?

a) HbA1c
b) Fasting C-peptide level
c) Anti-GAD antibodies
d) Oral glucose tolerance test

**Answer: c) Anti-GAD antibodies**

*Anti-GAD65 is the most sensitive autoantibody for Type 1 DM. C-peptide would be low but doesn't differentiate autoimmune from other causes of β-cell failure. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 403*

### MCQ 2
A 55-year-old obese diabetic on metformin 2g/day has HbA1c 8.2%. He has a history of heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (EF 35%). Which add-on drug is MOST appropriate?

a) Glimepiride
b) Pioglitazone
c) Empagliflozin
d) Sitagliptin

**Answer: c) Empagliflozin**

*SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) have proven CV and HF benefits (EMPA-REG, DAPA-HF trials). Pioglitazone is contraindicated in HF (fluid retention). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 404*

---

### MCQ 3
A diabetic patient on insulin develops hypoglycemia at 3 AM followed by hyperglycemia at 7 AM. This is best explained by:

a) Dawn phenomenon
b) Somogyi effect
c) Insulin resistance
d) Waning insulin effect

**Answer: b) Somogyi effect**

*Somogyi = nocturnal hypoglycemia → counter-regulatory hormone surge → rebound morning hyperglycemia. Dawn phenomenon = morning hyperglycemia from physiological GH/cortisol without preceding hypoglycemia. Management: reduce evening insulin dose (Somogyi) vs. increase (Dawn). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 404*

---

### MCQ 4
Which of the following is TRUE about SGLT2 inhibitors?

a) They increase insulin secretion
b) They can cause euglycemic DKA
c) They are contraindicated in heart failure
d) They cause weight gain

**Answer: b) They can cause euglycemic DKA**

*SGLT2i block glucose reabsorption in PCT → glycosuria → calorie loss (weight reduction). They promote ketogenesis, leading to euglycemic DKA (normal blood glucose + metabolic acidosis + ketonemia). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 404*

---

### MCQ 5
A 40-year-old woman with Type 2 DM and persistent microalbuminuria (UACR 180 mg/g) with BP 138/85. Which drug is MOST appropriate to slow nephropathy progression?

a) Amlodipine
b) Enalapril
c) Metoprolol
d) Hydrochlorothiazide

**Answer: b) Enalapril**

*ACE inhibitors (or ARBs) are the cornerstone of diabetic nephropathy management — they reduce intraglomerular pressure by dilating the efferent arteriole and reduce proteinuria. Indicated even in normotensive diabetics with microalbuminuria. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 404*

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Key Distinguishing Feature |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Type 1 DM | Young, lean, autoantibodies+, C-peptide low, DKA |
| Type 2 DM | Older, obese, metabolic syndrome, C-peptide normal/high |
| MODY | Autosomal dominant FH, young onset, no autoantibodies |
| LADA | Adult-onset, initially non-insulin requiring, anti-GAD+ |
| Secondary DM | History of pancreatitis, steroids, Cushing's etc. |
| GDM | Onset during pregnancy, resolves postpartum (usually) |
