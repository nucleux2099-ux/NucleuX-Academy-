## 🧠 Mnemonics

### DKA Management: **FIG-PICK**
Fluids, Insulin, Glucose monitoring, Potassium, Identify precipitant, Correct acidosis, Keep monitoring

### DKA Precipitants: **5 I's**
- **I** — Infection (#1 cause)
- **I** — Insulin missed/insufficient
- **I** — Infarction (MI)
- **I** — Intoxication (alcohol)
- **I** — Iatrogenic (steroids)

### Microvascular Complications: **RNN**
Retinopathy, Nephropathy, Neuropathy — controlled by **glycemic control** (HbA1c)

### Macrovascular Complications: **CSP**
CAD, Stroke, PVD — controlled by **cardiovascular risk management** (BP, lipids, smoking)

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **HbA1c** reflects average glucose over **2-3 months** (RBC lifespan). Target: <7% (individualized)
2. **Metformin** = first-line in ALL T2DM. Contraindicated if eGFR <30. Does NOT cause hypoglycemia.
3. **SGLT2 inhibitors** — proven CV and renal benefit. Side effects: UTI, genital mycosis, **euglycemic DKA**
4. **Sulfonylureas** are the most common cause of **drug-induced hypoglycemia**
5. **Whipple's triad** for hypoglycemia: Low glucose + symptoms + resolution with glucose administration
6. **Somogyi effect**: Nocturnal hypoglycemia → rebound morning hyperglycemia (treat: ↓ evening insulin)
7. **Dawn phenomenon**: Early morning hyperglycemia from GH/cortisol surge (treat: ↑ evening insulin or adjust timing)
8. **Diabetic ketoacidosis**: Anion gap metabolic acidosis + Kussmaul breathing + fruity breath
9. **Cerebral edema** — dreaded complication of DKA treatment in children (too-rapid correction)
10. **MODY** — autosomal dominant, young onset, mild hyperglycemia, no autoantibodies. MODY 2 (glucokinase) most common.

## 📋 HbA1c Targets

| Patient | Target |
|---|---|
| Most adults | <7% |
| Young, newly diagnosed, no complications | <6.5% |
| Elderly, multiple comorbidities, hypoglycemia risk | <8% |
| Pregnancy | <6% (use insulin, not OHA) |

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A Type 1 diabetic presents with vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing. pH 7.1, glucose 450, K⁺ 5.8. What is the first step?

> **IV normal saline** (aggressive fluid resuscitation). K⁺ is high but will drop with insulin. Start insulin once K >3.3 and fluids initiated.

### Q2
A 55-year-old T2DM patient on metformin has HbA1c 8.5% and established CVD. What add-on drug has proven CV benefit?

> **SGLT2 inhibitor** (Empagliflozin — EMPA-REG) or **GLP-1 RA** (Liraglutide — LEADER trial).

### Q3
Fasting glucose 115 mg/dL. What is this classified as?

> **Impaired fasting glucose (IFG)** — prediabetes. Lifestyle modification + screen annually.

### Q4
Earliest sign of diabetic nephropathy?

> **Microalbuminuria** (albumin 30-300 mg/day). Start ACE-I/ARB even if normotensive.

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **Euglycemic DKA**
> SGLT2 inhibitors can cause DKA with **near-normal glucose** (<250). Suspect in any SGLT2i patient with nausea, vomiting, and acidosis. Check ketones even if glucose is not sky-high!

> [!warning] **Insulin in T2DM**
> Start insulin if: HbA1c >10%, glucose >300, or features of catabolism. Basal insulin (Glargine) at bedtime is the typical starting regimen.
