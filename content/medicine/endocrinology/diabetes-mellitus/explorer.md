# Diabetes Mellitus — Explorer

## Overview

**Diabetes mellitus (DM)** is a group of metabolic disorders characterized by chronic **hyperglycemia** due to defective insulin secretion, action, or both. India is the "diabetes capital of the world" with >100 million diabetics. DM is a cornerstone topic in NEET-PG, frequently tested across endocrinology, nephrology, ophthalmology, and pharmacology.

📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 403-404

## Key Points

- **Type 1:** Autoimmune β-cell destruction → absolute insulin deficiency → DKA-prone
- **Type 2:** Insulin resistance + progressive β-cell failure → 90% of all DM
- **HbA1c ≥ 6.5%** or **FPG ≥ 126 mg/dL** or **2h OGTT ≥ 200 mg/dL** = diagnostic
- **Metformin** is first-line for Type 2 DM (unless contraindicated)
- **SGLT2 inhibitors** (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) — cardio- and renoprotective; preferred in HF/CKD
- **GLP-1 RA** (liraglutide, semaglutide) — weight loss + CV benefit
- **Microvascular:** Retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy
- **Macrovascular:** CAD, stroke, PVD
- India-specific: **RSSDI guidelines** recommend HbA1c target <7% for most adults

## Classification

| Type | Mechanism | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Type 1** | Autoimmune β-cell destruction (anti-GAD, anti-IA2, anti-ZnT8) | Young, lean, absolute insulin deficiency, DKA-prone |
| **Type 2** | Insulin resistance + relative β-cell failure | Older, obese, metabolic syndrome, gradual onset |
| **GDM** | Glucose intolerance first recognized in pregnancy | Screen at 24-28 weeks (75g OGTT — DIPSI criteria in India) |
| **MODY** | Monogenic — autosomal dominant | Young onset, family history, no autoantibodies |
| **LADA** | Slow autoimmune destruction | Adult onset Type 1 — anti-GAD positive, initially non-insulin requiring |
| **Secondary** | Pancreatitis, steroids, Cushing's, acromegaly, hemochromatosis | Treat underlying cause |

## Diagnostic Criteria (ADA 2024)

| Test | Normal | Prediabetes | Diabetes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fasting plasma glucose | <100 mg/dL | 100-125 (IFG) | **≥126 mg/dL** |
| 2h OGTT (75g) | <140 mg/dL | 140-199 (IGT) | **≥200 mg/dL** |
| HbA1c | <5.7% | 5.7-6.4% | **≥6.5%** |
| Random glucose | — | — | **≥200 + symptoms** |

> **Clinical Pearl:** Two abnormal tests on the SAME sample confirm diagnosis. A single random glucose ≥200 with classic symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss) is diagnostic without confirmation.

## Pathophysiology

### Ominous Octet (DeFronzo) — Type 2 DM
1. **↓ Insulin secretion** (β-cell failure)
2. **↑ Glucagon secretion** (α-cell dysfunction)
3. **↑ Hepatic glucose production**
4. **↓ Peripheral glucose uptake** (muscle insulin resistance)
5. **↑ Lipolysis** (adipocyte insulin resistance)
6. **↓ Incretin effect** (GLP-1 deficiency)
7. **↑ Renal glucose reabsorption** (SGLT2 upregulation)
8. **Neurotransmitter dysfunction** (hypothalamic appetite dysregulation)

### Type 1 Pathogenesis
- Genetic susceptibility: **HLA-DR3/DR4** (strongest association)
- Environmental trigger (viral — Coxsackie B, rubella)
- Autoimmune destruction of β-cells
- Autoantibodies: **Anti-GAD65** (most sensitive), anti-IA2, anti-insulin, anti-ZnT8
- Clinical DM when >80-90% β-cells destroyed

## Clinical Features

### Classic Triad
- **Polyuria** (osmotic diuresis from glycosuria)
- **Polydipsia** (compensatory)
- **Polyphagia with weight loss** (catabolic state in Type 1)

### Chronic Complications

#### Microvascular
| Complication | Screening | Key Points |
|---|---|---|
| **Diabetic Retinopathy** | Annual fundoscopy from diagnosis (T2) / 5 yrs (T1) | #1 cause of blindness age 20-74; nonproliferative → proliferative |
| **Diabetic Nephropathy** | Annual urine albumin/creatinine ratio (UACR) | Microalbuminuria (30-300 mg/g) → macroalbuminuria → ESRD |
| **Diabetic Neuropathy** | Annual monofilament exam | Distal symmetric polyneuropathy (glove-stocking), autonomic neuropathy |

#### Macrovascular
- **CAD** — #1 cause of death in DM; DM = CHD risk equivalent
- **Stroke** — 2-4x increased risk
- **Peripheral vascular disease** — claudication, gangrene

### Diabetic Foot
- Neuropathy + PVD + infection → ulcers → gangrene → amputation
- **Wagner classification** for grading ulcers (0-5)
- Annual foot exam mandatory

## Management

### Type 2 DM — Stepwise Approach

```
Step 1: Lifestyle + Metformin (500mg → titrate to 2g/day)
         ↓ If HbA1c not at target in 3 months
Step 2: Add second agent based on comorbidities:
         • ASCVD/high risk → GLP-1 RA (liraglutide, semaglutide)
         • Heart failure → SGLT2i (empagliflozin 10-25mg, dapagliflozin 10mg)
         • CKD → SGLT2i (if eGFR >20) or finerenone
         • Obesity → GLP-1 RA or tirzepatide
         • Cost concern → Sulfonylurea (glimepiride 1-4mg) or pioglitazone
         ↓ If still not at target
Step 3: Triple therapy or Basal insulin (Glargine/Degludec)
         ↓
Step 4: Basal-bolus or premixed insulin
```

### Type 1 DM
- **Basal-bolus insulin** (MDI or insulin pump/CSII)
- Basal: Glargine/Detemir/Degludec
- Bolus: Lispro/Aspart/Glulisine (pre-meal)
- Carb counting + insulin-to-carb ratios
- **CGM** (continuous glucose monitoring) — standard of care

### HbA1c Targets
| Population | Target |
|---|---|
| Most adults | <7% |
| Young, newly diagnosed, no complications | <6.5% |
| Elderly, comorbidities, hypoglycemia-prone | <8% |
| Pregnant (pregestational DM) | <6% |

### Key Drug Classes

| Class | Mechanism | Key Side Effects | Weight Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Metformin** | ↓ hepatic glucose output, ↑ insulin sensitivity | Lactic acidosis (rare), B12 deficiency, GI upset | Neutral/↓ |
| **Sulfonylureas** | ↑ insulin secretion (close K-ATP channels) | Hypoglycemia, weight gain | ↑ |
| **SGLT2 inhibitors** | Block renal glucose reabsorption | UTI, euglycemic DKA, Fournier's gangrene | ↓ |
| **GLP-1 RA** | ↑ insulin, ↓ glucagon, delay gastric emptying | Nausea, pancreatitis risk, thyroid C-cell tumors (rodents) | ↓↓ |
| **DPP-4 inhibitors** | ↑ endogenous incretins | Well tolerated; nasopharyngitis | Neutral |
| **Thiazolidinediones** | PPARγ agonist → ↑ insulin sensitivity | Edema, weight gain, fractures, bladder ca? | ↑ |
| **Insulin** | Replaces deficient insulin | Hypoglycemia, weight gain | ↑ |

> **Exam Pearl:** Metformin is **contraindicated** when eGFR <30. Hold before iodinated contrast. Lactic acidosis is the most feared side effect.

> **Exam Pearl:** SGLT2 inhibitors cause **euglycemic DKA** — normal glucose + ketoacidosis. Think of this in post-surgical diabetic patients on SGLT2i.

## Screening Recommendations

- **Type 2 DM screening:** All adults ≥35 years, or earlier if BMI ≥25 + risk factors (Indian ethnicity is a risk factor)
- **GDM screening:** All pregnant women at 24-28 weeks (India: universal screening with DIPSI 75g non-fasting OGTT)
- **Type 1 DM:** No routine screening in general population; screen first-degree relatives if research/trial setting

## Complications & Prognosis

- **DKA** — More common in Type 1; medical emergency (see DKA topic)
- **HHS** — Hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state; Type 2; mortality up to 20%
- **Hypoglycemia** — Most common acute complication of treatment
- **Cardiovascular disease** — #1 cause of death
- Good glycemic control reduces microvascular complications by **25-40%** (DCCT/UKPDS)
