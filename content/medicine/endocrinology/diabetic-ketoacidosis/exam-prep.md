# Diabetic Ketoacidosis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Triad: hyperglycemia (>250) + ketonemia + HAGMA (pH <7.3). Mainly T1DM. Precipitants: infection, insulin omission. Management: IV fluids FIRST → check K⁺ → insulin infusion → add dextrose at glucose 200. Cerebral edema in children is most feared complication.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **High anion gap metabolic acidosis** — AG = Na - (Cl + HCO3)
- ★ **β-hydroxybutyrate** is the predominant ketone (urine dipstick misses it — detects acetoacetate)
- ★ **Total body K⁺ is ALWAYS depleted** even if serum K⁺ is high
- ★ **Do NOT start insulin if K⁺ <3.3** — fatal hypokalemia risk
- ★ **Add dextrose** when glucose reaches 200 mg/dL (continue insulin to close AG)
- ★ **Infection** = most common precipitant
- ★ **Kussmaul breathing** = deep rapid respiration to compensate metabolic acidosis
- ★ **Cerebral edema** = leading cause of DKA death in children (rapid osmolar changes)
- ★ **Euglycemic DKA** = SGLT2 inhibitor-related (normal glucose + ketoacidosis)
- ★ **Mucormycosis** (rhinocerebral) associated with DKA
- ★ **Corrected sodium** = measured Na⁺ + 1.6 for each 100 mg/dL glucose above 100

## Mnemonics

- **Precipitants "5 I's"** — Infection, Insulin omission, Infarction, Intoxication, Initial presentation
- **Management "FIK"** — Fluids, Insulin, K⁺
- **DKA vs HHS: "DKA = Acidosis, HHS = Hyperosmolar"**

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **HHS** | Higher glucose (>600), no significant ketosis/acidosis, older T2DM |
| **Alcoholic ketoacidosis** | History of alcohol binge, low/normal glucose |
| **Starvation ketosis** | Mild ketosis, normal glucose, no acidosis |
| **Lactic acidosis** | Elevated lactate, no ketones |
| **Uremic acidosis** | Elevated BUN/Cr, no ketones |
| **Toxic ingestion** | Methanol, ethylene glycol — osmolar gap elevated |
