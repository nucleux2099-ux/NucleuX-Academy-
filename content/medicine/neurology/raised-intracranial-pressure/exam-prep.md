# Raised Intracranial Pressure — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

ICP >20 mmHg. Monro-Kellie doctrine: brain + CSF + blood = constant volume. Cushing's triad (hypertension, bradycardia, irregular respiration) = late/ominous sign. Papilledema = hallmark. CN VI palsy = false localizing. Mannitol/hypertonic saline for acute management. Herniation = emergency.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Normal ICP** = 5-15 mmHg; **raised** >20 mmHg
- ★ **Cushing's triad** = hypertension + bradycardia + irregular breathing (LATE sign)
- ★ **Papilledema** = bilateral disc swelling; hallmark of raised ICP
- ★ **CN VI palsy** = false localizing sign (long intracranial course)
- ★ **LP contraindicated** if mass lesion on CT (risk of herniation)
- ★ **Uncal herniation** = ipsilateral CN III palsy (blown pupil) + contralateral hemiparesis
- ★ **Tonsillar herniation** = respiratory arrest → most lethal
- ★ **Mannitol** = osmotic diuretic; draws water out of brain
- ★ **Dexamethasone** works for vasogenic edema (tumors) NOT cytotoxic edema (stroke)
- ★ **IIH** = young obese woman, papilledema, normal MRI, raised opening pressure → acetazolamide + weight loss
- ★ **CPP = MAP - ICP** (target CPP >60)

## Mnemonics

- **Cushing's triad: "HBI"** — Hypertension, Bradycardia, Irregular respiration
- **Herniation types: "USCT"** — Uncal, Subfalcine, Central, Tonsillar
- **Uncal herniation "3-3-3"** — CN 3 palsy, 3rd structure affected (PCA), shifts to 3rd ventricle

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Brain tumor** | Progressive symptoms, enhancing mass on MRI |
| **Hydrocephalus** | Dilated ventricles on CT |
| **IIH** | Normal MRI, obese young woman, raised opening pressure |
| **Hypertensive encephalopathy** | Severe HTN, PRES on MRI, reversible |
| **Meningitis** | Fever, meningism, CSF abnormalities |
| **CVT** | Non-arterial territory infarct, MRV shows thrombosis |
