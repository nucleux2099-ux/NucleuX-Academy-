# Peripheral Neuropathy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Damage to peripheral nerves causing sensory/motor/autonomic dysfunction. DM is most common cause. Glove-stocking pattern. NCS/EMG differentiates axonal (↓amplitude) from demyelinating (↓velocity). Treat cause + neuropathic pain (pregabalin/duloxetine).

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Diabetes** = most common cause worldwide
- ★ **Glove and stocking** = classic distribution
- ★ **Axonal** = reduced amplitude; **Demyelinating** = reduced conduction velocity
- ★ **GBS** = acute demyelinating polyradiculoneuropathy; ascending weakness, albuminocytologic dissociation
- ★ **B12 deficiency** = subacute combined degeneration (posterior columns + lateral corticospinal)
- ★ **Mononeuritis multiplex** = vasculitis until proven otherwise
- ★ **Diabetic CN III palsy** = pupil-SPARING (vs PComA aneurysm = pupil-INVOLVING)
- ★ **Pregabalin/duloxetine** = first-line for neuropathic pain
- ★ **CMT** = most common hereditary neuropathy (pes cavus + distal wasting)
- ★ **Lead neuropathy** = motor predominant, wrist drop
- ★ **Leprosy** = most common infectious cause of neuropathy worldwide

## Mnemonics

- **Causes: "DANG THERAPIST"** — Diabetes, Alcohol, Nutritional, GBS, Toxins, Hereditary, Endocrine, Renal, Amyloid, Porphyria, Infections, Systemic, Tumor
- **Axonal causes: "ABCDE"** — Alcohol, B12, Chemicals (drugs/toxins), Diabetes, Everything metabolic

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **MND** | No sensory involvement, UMN+LMN signs |
| **Myopathy** | Proximal weakness, normal sensation, elevated CK |
| **Myasthenia gravis** | Fatigability, no wasting early, antibodies |
| **Cervical myelopathy** | UMN signs in legs, sensory level |
| **Spinal cord lesion** | UMN below, sensory level, sphincter involvement |
| **Functional weakness** | Inconsistent exam, Hoover sign positive |
