# Cerebral Venous Thrombosis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Thrombosis of dural venous sinuses. Young women (OCP/pregnancy). Headache + seizures + hemorrhagic infarct in non-arterial territory. MR venography diagnostic. Empty delta sign on CT. Treat with heparin EVEN IF hemorrhagic.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Superior sagittal sinus** = most commonly thrombosed
- ★ **OCP** = most common acquired risk factor
- ★ **Anticoagulate even with hemorrhagic infarction** — this is counterintuitive but correct
- ★ **Empty delta sign** = filling defect in sagittal sinus on contrast CT
- ★ **Cord sign** = hyperdense thrombosed vein on plain CT
- ★ Hemorrhagic infarct in **non-arterial territory** = clue to CVT
- ★ **Bilateral thalamic lesions** = deep cerebral vein thrombosis
- ★ **Cavernous sinus thrombosis** = painful ophthalmoplegia, proptosis, chemosis
- ★ **Seizures more common** in CVT than arterial stroke
- ★ MRI + MRV = **gold standard**
- ★ Good prognosis overall — 80% recover well

## Mnemonics

- **Risk factors "POST CLOT"** — Pregnancy, OCP, Surgery, Thrombophilia, Cancer, Lumbar puncture, Otitis media, Trauma
- **Cavernous sinus contents: "O TOM CAT"** — Oculomotor (III), Trochlear (IV), Ophthalmic (V1), Maxillary (V2), Carotid artery, Abducens (VI), Things sympathetic

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Arterial ischemic stroke** | Arterial territory, older, no hemorrhage usually |
| **Idiopathic intracranial hypertension** | Normal MRV, obese young woman |
| **Meningitis** | Fever, meningism, CSF cells |
| **SAH** | Thunderclap headache, blood in subarachnoid space |
| **Eclampsia** | Pregnancy, hypertension, proteinuria, PRES on MRI |
| **Brain tumor** | Chronic progressive, enhancing mass |
