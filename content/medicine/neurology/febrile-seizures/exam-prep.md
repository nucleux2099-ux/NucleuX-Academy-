# Febrile Seizures — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Seizures with fever in 6 months–5 years without CNS infection. Simple (80%): generalized, <15 min, single. Complex: focal, >15 min, or recurrent in 24 hrs. Simple FS need no investigations or AEDs. LP if <12 months or meningitis suspected. Prognosis excellent.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Age**: 6 months to 5 years; peak 12-18 months
- ★ **Simple FS** = generalized, <15 min, single, no post-ictal deficit → NO investigations needed in well child ≥12 months
- ★ **Complex FS** = focal, >15 min, or recurrent within 24 hrs
- ★ **LP** indicated if <12 months, signs of meningitis, or partially treated meningitis
- ★ **No daily AEDs** for simple febrile seizures
- ★ **Antipyretics do NOT prevent recurrence** — important counseling point
- ★ **Recurrence risk** ~30% after first episode
- ★ **Epilepsy risk** = 2-4% (only slightly increased)
- ★ **HHV-6** (roseola) = most common viral trigger
- ★ **Rectal diazepam** or buccal midazolam for prolonged seizure

## Mnemonics

- **Simple FS "rule of S"** — Short (<15 min), Single (no recurrence), Symmetric (generalized)
- **Complex FS "FLP"** — Focal, Long (>15 min), Plural (recurrent in 24 hrs)

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Meningitis** | Meningeal signs, bulging fontanelle, toxic-appearing, abnormal CSF |
| **Encephalitis** | Altered consciousness, focal deficits, abnormal MRI |
| **Epilepsy** | Afebrile seizures, abnormal EEG |
| **Rigor/shivering** | Not true seizure, responsive, no post-ictal |
| **Febrile delirium** | Confusion but no tonic-clonic movements |
| **Breath-holding spells** | Triggered by crying/upset, cyanotic/pallid |
