# Parkinson Disease — Explorer

## Overview

**Parkinson disease (PD)** is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder caused by loss of **dopaminergic neurons** in the **substantia nigra pars compacta**. It is the second most common neurodegenerative disease after Alzheimer's. Characterized by the clinical tetrad of **resting tremor, rigidity, bradykinesia, and postural instability (TRAP)**.

## Key Points

- Second most common neurodegenerative disease
- Mean age of onset: **60 years**
- Cardinal features: **TRAP** — Tremor (resting), Rigidity, Akinesia/Bradykinesia, Postural instability
- Pathological hallmark: **Lewy bodies** (α-synuclein inclusions)
- Gold standard treatment: **Levodopa + Carbidopa**
- Diagnosis is clinical — DaTSCAN supports diagnosis

## Definition & Classification

| Type | Features |
|---|---|
| **Idiopathic PD** | >90%; sporadic, classic features |
| **Young-onset PD** | <40 years; consider genetic causes (PARK genes) |
| **Secondary parkinsonism** | Drug-induced (antipsychotics), vascular, post-encephalitic |
| **Parkinson-plus syndromes** | MSA, PSP, CBD, DLB — poor levodopa response |

### Parkinson-Plus Syndromes

| Syndrome | Key Features |
|---|---|
| **Multiple System Atrophy (MSA)** | Autonomic failure + cerebellar ataxia or parkinsonism; "hot cross bun" sign on MRI |
| **Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP)** | Vertical gaze palsy (downgaze first), early falls (backwards), axial rigidity |
| **Corticobasal Degeneration (CBD)** | Asymmetric rigidity, alien limb phenomenon, cortical sensory loss |
| **Dementia with Lewy Bodies (DLB)** | Fluctuating cognition, visual hallucinations, parkinsonism |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors

| Risk Factor | Effect |
|---|---|
| Age >60 | Strongest risk factor |
| Male sex | 1.5x more common |
| Family history | 5-10% monogenic (LRRK2, PARK2/Parkin, PINK1, SNCA) |
| Pesticide exposure | Increased risk |
| Head trauma | Mild increase |
| **Coffee, smoking** | Paradoxically **protective** |

### Pathophysiology

1. Loss of **dopaminergic neurons** in substantia nigra pars compacta
2. **Lewy body** formation — intracytoplasmic inclusions of misfolded **α-synuclein**
3. Dopamine depletion in **striatum** (putamen > caudate)
4. Imbalance: ↓ dopamine (inhibitory) → ↑ relative acetylcholine activity
5. **Direct pathway** (movement facilitation) underactive → bradykinesia
6. **Indirect pathway** (movement inhibition) overactive → rigidity
7. Symptoms appear when **60-80%** of nigral neurons are lost
8. **Braak staging** — disease progresses from medulla/olfactory → nigra → cortex

## Clinical Features

### Motor Features (Cardinal)

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Resting tremor** | 4-6 Hz, "pill-rolling," asymmetric onset, decreases with action |
| **Rigidity** | Lead-pipe rigidity; cogwheel rigidity (rigidity + superimposed tremor) |
| **Bradykinesia** | Slowness of movement; micrographia, hypomimia (mask-like face), reduced arm swing |
| **Postural instability** | Late feature; pull test positive; causes falls |

### Other Motor Features
- **Gait**: Shuffling, festinating (involuntary acceleration), difficulty initiating (start hesitation), freezing
- **Speech**: Hypophonia (soft), monotonous
- **Micrographia**: Progressively smaller handwriting

### Non-Motor Features (Often precede motor symptoms)

| System | Features |
|---|---|
| **Neuropsychiatric** | Depression (40%), anxiety, apathy, dementia (late), psychosis |
| **Autonomic** | Constipation (earliest), orthostatic hypotension, urinary urgency, erectile dysfunction |
| **Sleep** | REM sleep behavior disorder (RBD — acts out dreams), excessive daytime sleepiness |
| **Sensory** | Anosmia/hyposmia (earliest non-motor), pain |
| **Others** | Seborrheic dermatitis, sialorrhea (drooling) |

## Diagnosis

- **Clinical diagnosis** — based on UK Brain Bank criteria
- **Bradykinesia** is essential + at least one of tremor or rigidity
- **Asymmetric onset** and **good levodopa response** support diagnosis

### Investigations (Supportive)

| Test | Role |
|---|---|
| **DaTSCAN (I-123 ioflupane SPECT)** | Reduced dopamine transporter uptake in striatum; differentiates PD from essential tremor |
| **MRI brain** | Normal in PD; excludes structural causes; abnormal in Parkinson-plus |
| **PET scan** | Research; reduced fluorodopa uptake |
| **Olfactory testing** | Reduced in PD (early marker) |

### PD vs Essential Tremor

| Feature | PD | Essential Tremor |
|---|---|---|
| Tremor type | Resting | Postural/action |
| Frequency | 4-6 Hz | 8-12 Hz |
| Asymmetry | Yes | Usually symmetric |
| Alcohol effect | No benefit | Improves tremor |
| Family history | Less common | Often positive |
| Other features | Rigidity, bradykinesia | Isolated tremor |

## Management

### Pharmacological

| Drug | Mechanism | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Levodopa + Carbidopa/Benserazide** | Dopamine precursor + peripheral decarboxylase inhibitor | Most effective; start when functional impairment |
| **Dopamine agonists** (pramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotine) | D2/D3 receptor agonists | Young patients; impulse control disorders risk |
| **MAO-B inhibitors** (selegiline, rasagiline, safinamide) | Prevent dopamine breakdown | Early/mild PD; adjunctive |
| **COMT inhibitors** (entacapone, opicapone, tolcapone) | Block peripheral levodopa metabolism | Adjunct to levodopa; extend "on" time |
| **Amantadine** | NMDA antagonist, ↑ DA release | Dyskinesia management |
| **Anticholinergics** (trihexyphenidyl) | Reduce ACh excess | Tremor-predominant young PD; avoid in elderly |

### Motor Complications of Levodopa

| Complication | Description |
|---|---|
| **Wearing off** | Symptoms return before next dose |
| **On-off fluctuations** | Unpredictable switching between mobile and immobile states |
| **Dyskinesias** | Involuntary choreiform movements during "on" state (peak-dose) |
| **Freezing** | Sudden inability to move, especially with gait |

### Surgical Treatment

- **Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS)** of subthalamic nucleus (STN) or globus pallidus interna (GPi)
- Indicated for motor fluctuations/dyskinesias refractory to medical therapy
- Does NOT slow progression; improves quality of life

### Non-Pharmacological
- Physiotherapy (gait training, balance exercises)
- Speech therapy (Lee Silverman Voice Treatment — LSVT LOUD)
- Occupational therapy
- Nutritional counseling (protein timing with levodopa)

## Complications & Prognosis

### Complications
- **Dementia** — 80% at 20 years
- **Depression** — most common psychiatric comorbidity
- **Falls and fractures** — postural instability
- **Aspiration pneumonia** — dysphagia (common cause of death)
- **Levodopa motor complications** — develop in 50% by 5 years

### Prognosis
- Progressive disease — no cure
- Life expectancy reduced by ~2-5 years
- Tremor-predominant PD has better prognosis than akinetic-rigid type
- Levodopa response correlates with prognosis
