# Parkinson Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Progressive neurodegenerative disorder from dopaminergic neuron loss in substantia nigra. TRAP: Tremor (resting), Rigidity, Akinesia, Postural instability. Lewy bodies (α-synuclein). Treatment: Levodopa + Carbidopa. DBS for refractory motor complications.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Cardinal features**: TRAP — resting tremor (pill-rolling), cogwheel rigidity, bradykinesia, postural instability
- ★ **Pathology**: Lewy bodies = α-synuclein; loss of dopaminergic neurons in substantia nigra pars compacta
- ★ **Symptoms appear when 60-80%** of nigral neurons lost
- ★ **Levodopa** = most effective drug; causes dyskinesias with long-term use
- ★ **Carbidopa/Benserazide** = peripheral decarboxylase inhibitor (prevents peripheral conversion)
- ★ **Resting tremor** (PD) vs **action/postural tremor** (essential tremor)
- ★ **Essential tremor** improves with alcohol; PD does not
- ★ **REM sleep behavior disorder** = early non-motor feature, predates motor symptoms
- ★ **DaTSCAN** differentiates PD from essential tremor
- ★ **Drug-induced parkinsonism** = antipsychotics, metoclopramide (symmetric, no tremor usually)
- ★ **PSP** = vertical gaze palsy (downgaze) + early falls backwards
- ★ **MSA** = autonomic failure + "hot cross bun sign" on MRI

## Mnemonics

- **TRAP** — Tremor, Rigidity, Akinesia/bradykinesia, Postural instability
- **Lewy bodies = Love of α-Synuclein** (α-synuclein aggregates)
- **Non-motor early features: "CARDS"** — Constipation, Anosmia, RBD, Depression, Seborrhea

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Essential tremor** | Postural/action tremor, symmetric, FH+, improves with alcohol |
| **Drug-induced parkinsonism** | Symmetric, temporal relation to drug, reversible |
| **PSP** | Vertical gaze palsy, early falls, poor levodopa response |
| **MSA** | Severe autonomic failure, cerebellar signs, poor levodopa response |
| **DLB** | Visual hallucinations early, fluctuating cognition |
| **Normal pressure hydrocephalus** | Triad: gait apraxia, dementia, incontinence; ventriculomegaly |
| **Wilson disease** | Young, KF rings, low ceruloplasmin, liver disease |
