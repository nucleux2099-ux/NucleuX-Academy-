# Motor Neuron Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Progressive degeneration of UMN+LMN (ALS most common). No sensory/sphincter/eye movement involvement. Fasciculations + wasting + hyperreflexia = classic combo. Riluzole extends survival ~3 months. NIV for respiratory failure. Median survival 3-5 years.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **ALS** = combined UMN + LMN signs; most common MND
- ★ **Spared**: Sensory, Sphincters, Saccades (eye movements)
- ★ **Fasciculations + wasting + hyperreflexia** in same limb = pathognomonic
- ★ **Tongue fasciculations** = classic finding (LMN bulbar)
- ★ **EMG**: Active denervation + reinnervation in multiple myotomes, normal sensory NCS
- ★ **Riluzole** = only drug extending survival (glutamate antagonist, ~3 months)
- ★ **NIV** improves survival more than riluzole
- ★ **Respiratory failure** = most common cause of death
- ★ **Bulbar onset** = worst prognosis (~2 years)
- ★ **C9orf72** = most common genetic cause; links ALS with FTD
- ★ **Split hand sign** = preferential thenar wasting (APB) over hypothenar — characteristic of ALS

## Mnemonics

- **"3 S's Spared"** — Sensory, Sphincter, Saccades
- **MND types by prognosis**: "PLS > PMA > ALS > PBP" (best to worst survival)
- **El Escorial**: UMN + LMN in multiple regions

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Cervical myelopathy** | Sensory level, MRI shows cord compression |
| **Multifocal motor neuropathy** | Pure LMN, anti-GM1 antibodies, conduction block on NCS, treatable with IVIg |
| **Myasthenia gravis** | Fatigability, no wasting, anti-AChR antibodies |
| **Spinal muscular atrophy** | Pure LMN, childhood onset, SMN1 gene |
| **Kennedy disease** | X-linked, gynecomastia, bulbar signs, CAG repeat in androgen receptor |
| **Peripheral neuropathy** | Sensory involvement, distal > proximal |
