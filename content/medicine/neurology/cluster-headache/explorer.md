# Cluster Headache — Explorer

## Overview

**Cluster headache** is a trigeminal autonomic cephalalgia (TAC) characterized by excruciating **strictly unilateral periorbital/temporal pain** with ipsilateral autonomic features. Attacks occur in **clusters** lasting weeks to months, separated by remission periods. It is the most severe primary headache, sometimes called "suicide headache."

## Key Points

- **Most painful** primary headache
- Strictly unilateral, periorbital/temporal
- Duration: **15-180 minutes** per attack
- Frequency: 1-8 attacks/day during cluster period
- **Male predominance** (3-5:1)
- Ipsilateral autonomic features (lacrimation, conjunctival injection, rhinorrhea)
- **Restlessness/agitation** during attack (vs. lying still in migraine)

## Definition & Classification

| Type | Features |
|---|---|
| **Episodic cluster** (80-90%) | Cluster periods lasting 7 days–1 year with remissions ≥3 months |
| **Chronic cluster** (10-20%) | Attacks for >1 year without remission or remission <3 months |

### Diagnostic Criteria (ICHD-3)

At least **5 attacks** with:
- Severe unilateral orbital/supraorbital/temporal pain lasting **15-180 min**
- ≥1 ipsilateral autonomic feature: conjunctival injection, lacrimation, nasal congestion, rhinorrhea, eyelid edema, forehead sweating, miosis, ptosis
- Restlessness or agitation
- Frequency: 1 every other day to 8/day

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors
- Male sex, smoking, alcohol (triggers during cluster period only)
- Age of onset: **20-40 years**
- Family history (5-20x risk in first-degree relatives)

### Pathophysiology
- **Hypothalamic activation** — the "pacemaker" (explains circadian/circannual periodicity)
- Trigeminal-autonomic reflex activation
- Posterior hypothalamic grey matter activation on PET/fMRI
- **Parasympathetic** activation → lacrimation, rhinorrhea, conjunctival injection
- **Sympathetic** dysfunction → miosis, ptosis (partial Horner's)

## Clinical Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Pain quality** | Excruciating, boring, stabbing, "red-hot poker" |
| **Location** | Strictly unilateral — periorbital, retro-orbital, temporal |
| **Duration** | 15-180 minutes (typically 45-90 min) |
| **Timing** | Clockwork regularity; often nocturnal (1-2 hours after sleep onset) |
| **Behavior** | **Restless, pacing, rocking** (vs migraine patients lie still) |
| **Autonomic features** | Ipsilateral: lacrimation, conjunctival injection, rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, ptosis, miosis, eyelid edema, forehead sweating |

### Cluster Period
- Lasts 4-12 weeks (often same time each year — circannual)
- 1-3 attacks/day during cluster
- Alcohol triggers attack within 30-60 min during cluster (not during remission)
- Remission: months to years

## Diagnosis

- **Clinical diagnosis** based on ICHD-3 criteria
- **MRI brain** (with pituitary views) — to exclude secondary causes (pituitary tumor, cavernous sinus lesion)
- No specific biomarker or test
- **Red flags**: First episode, atypical features, lack of autonomic features → image

## Management

### Acute Treatment

| Agent | Details |
|---|---|
| **High-flow oxygen** (100%, 12-15 L/min, 15-20 min via non-rebreather) | First-line; effective in ~80% |
| **Sumatriptan SC** (6 mg) | First-line pharmacological; works in 10-15 min |
| **Sumatriptan nasal** (20 mg) or **Zolmitriptan nasal** (5 mg) | Alternative if SC not available |
| **Lidocaine intranasal** (4-10%) | Adjunctive |

⚠️ **Oral triptans too slow** for cluster headache (short attack duration)

### Transitional Therapy (Bridge while starting preventive)

- **Prednisolone** taper (60-80 mg/day, taper over 2-3 weeks)
- **Greater occipital nerve block** (steroid + local anesthetic)

### Preventive Treatment

| Agent | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Verapamil** | 240-960 mg/day in divided doses | First-line preventive; monitor ECG (heart block) |
| **Lithium** | 600-1200 mg/day | For chronic cluster; monitor levels, renal, thyroid |
| **Topiramate** | 50-200 mg/day | Alternative |
| **Melatonin** | 10 mg at night | Adjunctive |
| **Galcanezumab** | 300 mg SC monthly | FDA-approved anti-CGRP for episodic cluster |

### Refractory Chronic Cluster

- **Deep brain stimulation** of posterior hypothalamus
- **Occipital nerve stimulation**
- **Sphenopalatine ganglion stimulation**

## Complications & Prognosis

### Complications
- Severe impact on quality of life and work
- Depression, suicidal ideation ("suicide headache")
- Medication overuse (if analgesics used instead of specific treatment)
- Chronic cluster (10-20%) — no sustained remission

### Prognosis
- Episodic cluster may remit permanently in some patients
- ~10-20% evolve from episodic to chronic
- Adequate prophylaxis significantly reduces attack frequency
- Cluster periods may shorten with age
