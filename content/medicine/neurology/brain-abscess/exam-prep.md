# Brain Abscess — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Focal pus collection in brain parenchyma. Classic triad (<50%): headache + fever + focal deficit. Ring-enhancing lesion on contrast imaging. DWI restriction differentiates from tumor. LP CONTRAINDICATED. Treatment: Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole ± surgery.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **LP contraindicated** — risk of herniation
- ★ **DWI restriction** (bright on DWI) = abscess; tumors do NOT restrict
- ★ **Most common organisms** = Streptococci + anaerobes
- ★ **Otitis media** → temporal lobe/cerebellum abscess
- ★ **Frontal sinusitis** → frontal lobe abscess
- ★ **Cyanotic heart disease** → brain abscess (R-to-L shunt bypasses lung filter)
- ★ **HIV + ring-enhancing lesion** → Toxoplasma (treat empirically; biopsy if no response)
- ★ **Capsule thinner on ventricular side** → risk of intraventricular rupture
- ★ **Intraventricular rupture** = mortality >80%
- ★ **Aspiration** for abscess >2.5 cm

## Mnemonics

- **Ring-enhancing DDx: "MAGIC DR"** — Metastasis, Abscess, Glioblastoma, Infarct, Contusion, Demyelination, Radiation necrosis
- **Abscess antibiotic: "CM"** — Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole

## Common MCQ Topics

1. LP contraindication in brain abscess
2. DWI finding (restricted diffusion in abscess)
3. Source-location correlation (otitis→temporal, sinusitis→frontal)
4. Cyanotic heart disease association
5. Toxoplasma in HIV
6. Ring-enhancing lesion differentials
7. Empirical antibiotic regimen
8. Surgical indications (>2.5 cm)

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Brain tumor (GBM)** | No DWI restriction; choline peak on MRS; irregular ring |
| **Metastasis** | Multiple, at grey-white junction, known primary |
| **Toxoplasmosis** | HIV, multiple ring-enhancing, responds to empirical Rx |
| **Tuberculoma** | Granulomatous, may calcify, CSF if safe |
| **Neurocysticercosis** | Calcified lesion, scolex, endemic area |
| **Demyelination (tumefactive MS)** | Open ring enhancement, young, white matter |
