# Brain Abscess — Explorer

## Overview

**Brain abscess** is a focal collection of pus within the brain parenchyma, surrounded by a vascularized capsule. It can arise from **contiguous spread** (otitis media, sinusitis, dental infections), **hematogenous spread**, or **direct inoculation** (trauma, surgery). Mortality has decreased with modern imaging and antibiotics but remains significant.

## Key Points

- **Triad** (classic but seen in <50%): Headache, fever, focal neurological deficit
- Most common location: **frontal and temporal lobes**
- **Ring-enhancing lesion** on contrast CT/MRI
- Most common organisms: **Streptococci, anaerobes, Staphylococci**
- Treatment: Antibiotics ± surgical aspiration/excision
- **LP is contraindicated** (risk of herniation)

## Definition & Classification

### By Source

| Source | Location | Common Organisms |
|---|---|---|
| **Otitis media/mastoiditis** | Temporal lobe, cerebellum | Streptococci, anaerobes, Pseudomonas |
| **Sinusitis (frontal)** | Frontal lobe | Streptococci, Haemophilus, anaerobes |
| **Dental infections** | Frontal lobe | Mixed anaerobes |
| **Hematogenous (lungs, endocarditis)** | Multiple, MCA territory | S. aureus, Streptococci |
| **Trauma/surgery** | Adjacent to site | S. aureus, Enterobacteriaceae |
| **Immunocompromised** | Variable | Toxoplasma, Nocardia, Aspergillus, TB |
| **Cyanotic heart disease** | Multiple | Streptococci (right-to-left shunt bypasses lung filter) |

### Stages of Formation

| Stage | Time | Pathology |
|---|---|---|
| Early cerebritis | Days 1-3 | Ill-defined inflammation |
| Late cerebritis | Days 4-9 | Necrotic center forming |
| Early capsule | Days 10-14 | Capsule formation begins |
| Late capsule | >14 days | Well-formed capsule (ring enhancement) |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Microbiology
- **Streptococci** (aerobic and anaerobic) — most common overall
- **Bacteroides, Prevotella** — anaerobes, especially from ear/dental
- **S. aureus** — trauma, endocarditis
- **Enterobacteriaceae** — neonates, post-surgical
- **Polymicrobial** — common (30-60%)
- **Immunocompromised**: Toxoplasma (HIV, ring-enhancing, multiple), Nocardia, Aspergillus, TB

### Pathophysiology
1. Inoculation of organism → focal cerebritis
2. Necrosis and pus formation
3. Capsule formation (fibroblast and neovascular response)
4. Surrounding vasogenic edema → mass effect
5. **Capsule thinner on ventricular side** → risk of intraventricular rupture (catastrophic)

## Clinical Features

| Feature | Frequency |
|---|---|
| **Headache** | 70-90% (most common symptom) |
| **Fever** | 50% (may be absent!) |
| **Focal neurological deficit** | 50-65% |
| **Seizures** | 25-45% |
| **Nausea/vomiting** | 40-50% |
| **Altered consciousness** | 30-50% |
| **Papilledema** | 25% |
| **Neck stiffness** | If near meninges or rupture |

### Classic Triad (present in <50%)
**Headache + Fever + Focal deficit**

### By Location

| Location | Features |
|---|---|
| **Frontal** | Personality change, hemiparesis, expressive aphasia |
| **Temporal** | Receptive aphasia (dominant), visual field defect |
| **Cerebellar** | Ataxia, nystagmus, headache, vomiting |
| **Brainstem** | Cranial nerve palsies, long tract signs |

## Diagnosis

| Investigation | Finding |
|---|---|
| **Contrast CT** | Ring-enhancing lesion with central hypodensity and surrounding edema |
| **MRI with DWI** | **Gold standard**; DWI shows restricted diffusion (bright) — differentiates from tumor (tumor does NOT restrict) |
| **Blood cultures** | Positive in 10-30% |
| **Aspiration/biopsy** | Definitive; Gram stain, culture, sensitivity |
| **Blood tests** | Leukocytosis (60%), raised ESR/CRP |
| ⚠️ **LP contraindicated** | Risk of uncal herniation |

### Differentiating from Brain Tumor
- **DWI restriction** = abscess (pus restricts diffusion)
- **MR spectroscopy**: Abscess shows amino acids, lactate, succinate; tumor shows choline peak

## Management

### Medical (Antibiotics — 6-8 weeks)

| Empirical | Regimen |
|---|---|
| **Standard** | Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole ± Vancomycin |
| **Post-trauma/surgery** | Vancomycin + Ceftazidime/Meropenem |
| **Immunocompromised (Toxoplasma)** | Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin |

### Surgical

| Indication | Procedure |
|---|---|
| **Abscess >2.5 cm** | Stereotactic aspiration (preferred) |
| **Posterior fossa abscess** | Urgent surgery (risk of hydrocephalus/herniation) |
| **Multiloculated/failed aspiration** | Craniotomy and excision |
| **Intraventricular rupture** | Emergency surgery |
| **Small abscess (<2.5 cm), early cerebritis** | Medical management alone may suffice |

### Adjunctive
- **Dexamethasone** — only if significant mass effect/edema (controversial; may reduce antibiotic penetration)
- **Antiepileptics** — if seizures occur; prophylactic use debated
- Treat source (mastoidectomy, sinus surgery, dental extraction)

## Complications & Prognosis

### Complications
- **Intraventricular rupture** — mortality >80%
- Hydrocephalus
- Recurrence (10-20%)
- Seizures (long-term in 30-50%)
- Persistent neurological deficits
- Herniation

### Prognosis
- Mortality: **5-15%** with modern treatment (was 40-60% pre-CT era)
- Poor prognostic factors: Intraventricular rupture, deep/brainstem location, multiple abscesses, immunocompromised
- Most patients need prolonged follow-up with serial imaging

---

*"Ring-enhancing lesion with restricted diffusion = brain abscess until proven otherwise."*
