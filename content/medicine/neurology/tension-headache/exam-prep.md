# Tension Headache — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Most common primary headache. Bilateral, pressing/tightening, mild-moderate, 30 min–7 days. No nausea, no aura. Acute: NSAIDs. Prophylaxis: amitriptyline. Avoid analgesic overuse (>15 days/month).

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common headache** worldwide
- ★ Bilateral, pressing, NOT pulsating, NOT aggravated by activity
- ★ **No nausea/vomiting** (key differentiator from migraine)
- ★ Duration **30 min to 7 days**
- ★ Chronic TTH = ≥15 days/month for >3 months
- ★ **Amitriptyline** = first-line prophylaxis
- ★ **Medication overuse headache** = major pitfall
- ★ Stress = most common trigger

## Mnemonics

- **TTH vs Migraine**: "TTH is BORING" — Bilateral, Ordinary intensity, Ring-like, Infrequent nausea, Not worsened by activity, Generally no aura

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Migraine** | Unilateral, pulsating, nausea, photo+phonophobia, aura |
| **Medication overuse headache** | >15 days/month analgesic use, daily headache |
| **Sinusitis** | Facial pain, nasal discharge, fever |
| **Cervicogenic headache** | Unilateral, triggered by neck movement |
| **Intracranial hypertension** | Papilledema, worse on bending/morning |
| **Depression** | Pervasive low mood, daily headache, sleep disturbance |
