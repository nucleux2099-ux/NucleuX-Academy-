# Brain Tumors (Medical) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Metastases most common in adults (lung #1 source). GBM = most common primary malignant. Meningioma = most common benign. Children = posterior fossa. MRI with gadolinium is gold standard. GBM: Stupp protocol (surgery+RT+temozolomide), median survival 15 months.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **GBM** = "butterfly" lesion crossing corpus callosum, necrosis, pseudopalisading
- ★ **Meningioma** = extra-axial, dural tail sign, calcified (psammoma bodies)
- ★ **Oligodendroglioma** = "fried egg" cells, calcification, **1p/19q co-deletion** (good prognosis marker)
- ★ **Vestibular schwannoma** = CP angle mass, bilateral in **NF2**
- ★ **Medulloblastoma** = most common malignant posterior fossa tumor in children; drop metastasis
- ★ **Craniopharyngioma** = suprasellar, calcified, bitemporal hemianopia, from Rathke's pouch
- ★ **Hemorrhagic metastases**: "MT RCC" — Melanoma, Thyroid, Renal cell carcinoma, Choriocarcinoma
- ★ **Primary CNS lymphoma** = treat with high-dose methotrexate, NOT surgery
- ★ **MGMT promoter methylation** in GBM → better response to temozolomide
- ★ **Dexamethasone** = reduces peritumoral vasogenic edema

## Mnemonics

- **Hemorrhagic mets: "Mr CT"** — Melanoma, Renal, Choriocarcinoma, Thyroid
- **NF2 = chromosome 22** = "22 = 2 schwannomas" (bilateral)
- **VHL = "3 H's"** — Hemangioblastoma, Hypernephroma (RCC), High EPO (polycythemia)

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Brain abscess** | DWI restriction, fever, ring enhancement |
| **Demyelinating disease** | Young, relapsing, open ring enhancement, MS history |
| **Stroke** | Acute onset, vascular territory, DWI+/ADC- |
| **Neurocysticercosis** | Calcified lesion, scolex, endemic area |
| **Tuberculoma** | Granuloma, meningeal enhancement, endemic area |
| **Radiation necrosis** | History of RT, PET cold (vs tumor PET hot) |
