# Ischemic Stroke — Explorer

## Overview

**Ischemic stroke** accounts for **80-85%** of all strokes and results from occlusion of a cerebral artery leading to focal brain infarction. It is a leading cause of death and disability worldwide. **"Time is brain"** — every minute of untreated large-vessel occlusion destroys ~1.9 million neurons.

## Key Points

- Most common type of stroke
- Thrombolysis window: **≤4.5 hours** (IV alteplase)
- Mechanical thrombectomy: **≤24 hours** in selected patients
- **NIHSS** score guides severity and treatment decisions
- Secondary prevention is critical to prevent recurrence

## Definition & Classification

### TOAST Classification (Etiologic)

| Subtype | Frequency | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Large artery atherosclerosis** | 20-25% | Carotid/vertebral stenosis >50%, cortical infarcts |
| **Cardioembolism** | 20-25% | AF, valvular disease, wedge-shaped infarcts |
| **Small vessel occlusion (lacunar)** | 20-25% | Lipohyalinosis, <1.5 cm subcortical infarcts |
| **Other determined** | 5% | Dissection, vasculitis, hypercoagulable states |
| **Cryptogenic** | 25-30% | No cause identified despite workup |

### Bamford (Oxford) Classification (Clinical)

| Syndrome | Territory | Features |
|---|---|---|
| **TACI** | Total anterior circulation | All 3: hemiparesis, hemianopia, higher cortical dysfunction |
| **PACI** | Partial anterior circulation | 2 of 3 above features |
| **LACI** | Lacunar | Pure motor/sensory, ataxic hemiparesis, dysarthria-clumsy hand |
| **POCI** | Posterior circulation | Cranial nerve palsy, cerebellar signs, bilateral motor/sensory |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors

| Modifiable | Non-modifiable |
|---|---|
| **Hypertension** (most important) | Age >55 |
| Diabetes mellitus | Male sex |
| Atrial fibrillation | Family history |
| Dyslipidemia | Prior stroke/TIA |
| Smoking | Ethnicity |
| Obesity, physical inactivity | |

### Pathophysiology

1. **Arterial occlusion** → cessation of blood flow
2. **Ischemic core** — irreversible damage within minutes (CBF <10 mL/100g/min)
3. **Ischemic penumbra** — at-risk but salvageable tissue (CBF 10-22 mL/100g/min)
4. **Excitotoxicity** — glutamate release → calcium influx → cell death
5. **Reperfusion injury** — free radicals, inflammation after recanalization

## Clinical Features

### By Vascular Territory

| Artery | Clinical Features |
|---|---|
| **MCA** (most common) | Contralateral hemiparesis (face/arm > leg), hemisensory loss, homonymous hemianopia; Dominant: aphasia; Non-dominant: neglect |
| **ACA** | Contralateral leg weakness > arm, abulia, urinary incontinence |
| **PCA** | Contralateral homonymous hemianopia, visual agnosia, memory impairment |
| **Basilar** | Locked-in syndrome, quadriplegia, cranial nerve palsies, coma |
| **PICA (lateral medullary/Wallenberg)** | Ipsilateral: Horner's, facial pain/temp loss, ataxia; Contralateral: body pain/temp loss; Dysphagia, vertigo |

### NIHSS (National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale)

- Scores 0-42; higher = more severe
- **<5**: minor stroke; **5-15**: moderate; **>15**: severe
- Used to guide thrombolysis decisions

## Diagnosis

### Imaging

| Modality | Findings | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| **NCCT head** | First-line; excludes hemorrhage | Immediate — **must do before thrombolysis** |
| **CT angiography** | Large vessel occlusion | With initial CT |
| **CT perfusion** | Core vs penumbra mismatch | For extended window (>4.5 hrs) |
| **MRI DWI** | Most sensitive for early ischemia | Gold standard but takes longer |
| **MRA** | Vascular anatomy | Alternative to CTA |

### Early CT Signs of Ischemia

- **Hyperdense MCA sign** (thrombus)
- Loss of insular ribbon
- Sulcal effacement
- Loss of grey-white differentiation

### Workup

- **ECG** — atrial fibrillation
- **Echocardiography** — cardiac source of emboli
- **Carotid Doppler** — stenosis assessment
- **Blood glucose**, CBC, coagulation, lipid profile
- **HbA1c** for diabetes screening

## Management

### Acute Management — "Time Is Brain"

#### IV Thrombolysis (Alteplase)

- **Dose**: 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg); 10% bolus, 90% over 1 hour
- **Window**: ≤4.5 hours from onset
- **Key contraindications**: Active bleeding, BP >185/110, platelets <100,000, INR >1.7, recent surgery

#### Mechanical Thrombectomy

- **Indication**: Large vessel occlusion (ICA, M1 MCA)
- **Window**: ≤6 hours (standard); up to **24 hours** with favorable perfusion imaging (DAWN/DEFUSE-3 trials)
- **NIHSS ≥6** and **ASPECTS ≥6**

#### Supportive Care

- **BP management**: Permissive hypertension (do NOT lower unless >220/120 or if thrombolysis planned — target <185/110)
- **Blood glucose**: Maintain 140-180 mg/dL
- **Head of bed**: 0-30°
- **NPO until swallow screen**
- **DVT prophylaxis**: Intermittent pneumatic compression

### Secondary Prevention

| Cause | Prevention |
|---|---|
| **Atherosclerotic** | Antiplatelet (aspirin ± clopidogrel), statin, BP control |
| **Cardioembolic (AF)** | Anticoagulation (DOACs preferred over warfarin) |
| **Carotid stenosis >70%** | Carotid endarterectomy (CEA) or stenting |
| **All** | Lifestyle modification, treat risk factors |

### Key Trials

| Trial | Finding |
|---|---|
| **NINDS** | IV tPA within 3 hours improves outcomes |
| **ECASS-III** | Extended tPA window to 4.5 hours |
| **MR CLEAN, ESCAPE, EXTEND-IA** | Thrombectomy effective for LVO |
| **DAWN, DEFUSE-3** | Extended thrombectomy window to 24 hours |

## Complications & Prognosis

### Complications

- **Hemorrhagic transformation** (6-10% post-thrombolysis)
- **Cerebral edema** — malignant MCA syndrome (peaks day 3-5)
- **Seizures** (5-10%)
- **Aspiration pneumonia**
- **DVT/PE**
- **Depression** (post-stroke, up to 30%)

### Prognosis

- 30-day mortality: ~15-20%
- 1-year recurrence: ~10-15%
- Functional outcome depends on stroke severity, time to treatment, and rehabilitation
- **Favorable prognostic factors**: Young age, low NIHSS, early recanalization, small infarct volume
