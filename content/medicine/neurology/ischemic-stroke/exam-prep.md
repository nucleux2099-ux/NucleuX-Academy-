# Ischemic Stroke — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Ischemic stroke (80-85% of strokes) = acute focal neurological deficit from cerebral artery occlusion. First step: **NCCT to exclude hemorrhage**. IV alteplase within 4.5 hrs; thrombectomy for LVO up to 24 hrs. Secondary prevention based on etiology (antiplatelet vs anticoagulation).

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common stroke type** = ischemic (80-85%)
- ★ **Most common artery** involved = MCA
- ★ **First investigation** = Non-contrast CT head (rule out bleed)
- ★ **Thrombolysis window** = 4.5 hours (ECASS-III); dose = 0.9 mg/kg alteplase
- ★ **Thrombectomy** extended to 24 hrs (DAWN/DEFUSE-3) with perfusion mismatch
- ★ **Hyperdense MCA sign** = early CT sign of thrombus
- ★ **Wallenberg syndrome** (PICA) = ipsilateral Horner's + contralateral body pain/temp loss + dysphagia
- ★ **Lacunar infarcts** = pure motor/sensory, <1.5 cm, in basal ganglia/pons/internal capsule
- ★ **AF** = most common cause of cardioembolic stroke → DOACs for prevention
- ★ **Do NOT lower BP** aggressively unless >220/120 (or >185/110 if thrombolysis planned)
- ★ **Hemorrhagic transformation** = major complication of thrombolysis

## Mnemonics

- **FAST** — Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency
- **BE FAST** — Balance, Eyes (vision), Face, Arm, Speech, Time
- **TOAST** classification: Large artery, Cardioembolism, Small vessel, Other, Cryptogenic

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Hemorrhagic stroke** | Sudden severe headache, CT shows hyperdensity |
| **TIA** | Symptoms resolve within 24 hrs, no infarct on imaging |
| **Hypoglycemia** | Low blood glucose, reversible with dextrose |
| **Todd's paralysis** | Post-seizure weakness, self-resolving |
| **Migraine with aura** | Gradual onset, positive symptoms (scintillation), headache follows |
| **Brain tumor** | Subacute/chronic, progressive, may have seizures |
| **Subdural hematoma** | Trauma history, fluctuating consciousness |
