# Hemorrhagic Stroke — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Hemorrhagic stroke (15-20% of strokes, 40% mortality) = ICH or SAH. Hypertension → basal ganglia ICH (most common). SAH → thunderclap headache from berry aneurysm rupture. NCCT is diagnostic. BP target <140 in ICH. Nimodipine for SAH vasospasm prevention.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common site of hypertensive ICH** = putamen/basal ganglia
- ★ **Most common cause of non-traumatic SAH** = berry aneurysm (AComA most common site)
- ★ **Thunderclap headache** = pathognomonic for SAH
- ★ **CT negative SAH** → LP for xanthochromia
- ★ **Vasospasm** peaks days 4-14 after SAH → **nimodipine** prevents poor outcomes
- ★ **Pontine hemorrhage** = pinpoint pupils + quadriplegia + hyperthermia
- ★ **Cerebellar hemorrhage >3 cm** = surgical emergency
- ★ **ICH BP target** = SBP <140 mmHg (INTERACT2)
- ★ **SAH associations** = ADPKD, Ehlers-Danlos, coarctation of aorta
- ★ **Cerebral amyloid angiopathy** = recurrent lobar hemorrhages in elderly
- ★ **Spot sign** on CTA = active bleeding (poor prognosis)

## Mnemonics

- **SAH complications**: "Re-Ve-Hy-Se-Na" — Rebleeding, Vasospasm, Hydrocephalus, Seizures, hypoNatremia
- **Hypertensive ICH sites**: "Put That Pons Cerebellum Lobe" — Putamen, Thalamus, Pons, Cerebellum, Lobar

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Ischemic stroke** | No blood on CT, may be eligible for thrombolysis |
| **Meningitis** | Fever, neck stiffness, CSF shows cells/organisms |
| **Migraine** | Recurrent, gradual, positive aura symptoms |
| **Hypertensive encephalopathy** | Diffuse symptoms, papilledema, reversible |
| **Brain tumor with hemorrhage** | Subacute history, ring enhancement on contrast |
| **Cerebral venous thrombosis** | Headache + seizures, hemorrhagic infarct in non-arterial territory |
