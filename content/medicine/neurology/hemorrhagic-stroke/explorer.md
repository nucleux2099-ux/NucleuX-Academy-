# Hemorrhagic Stroke — Explorer

## Overview

**Hemorrhagic stroke** accounts for **15-20%** of all strokes but causes **~40% of stroke mortality**. It includes **intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH)** and **subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH)**. Hypertension is the most common cause of spontaneous ICH.

## Key Points

- Higher mortality than ischemic stroke
- **Hypertension** = #1 cause of spontaneous ICH
- **Berry aneurysm rupture** = #1 cause of non-traumatic SAH
- NCCT head is diagnostic — shows hyperdense lesion
- No role for thrombolysis — must always exclude hemorrhage first

## Definition & Classification

| Type | Location | Common Cause |
|---|---|---|
| **Intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH)** | Within brain parenchyma | Hypertension (basal ganglia most common) |
| **Subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH)** | Between arachnoid and pia | Berry aneurysm (AComA most common site) |
| **Intraventricular hemorrhage** | Within ventricles | Extension of ICH, germinal matrix bleeding |

### ICH by Location (Hypertensive)

| Location | Frequency | Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Basal ganglia (putamen)** | 60-65% | Contralateral hemiplegia, eyes deviate toward lesion |
| **Thalamus** | 15-20% | Contralateral sensory loss, eyes deviate downward/inward |
| **Pons** | 5-10% | Quadriplegia, pinpoint pupils, hyperthermia, rapid death |
| **Cerebellum** | 5-10% | Ataxia, vomiting, occipital headache — surgical emergency |
| **Lobar** | 10-20% | Often non-hypertensive (CAA, AVM, tumor) |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Causes of ICH

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Hypertension** | Charcot-Bouchard microaneurysms in penetrating arteries |
| **Cerebral amyloid angiopathy** | Elderly, lobar hemorrhages, recurrent |
| **Vascular malformations** | AVM, cavernoma, dural AV fistula |
| **Coagulopathy** | Anticoagulants (warfarin), thrombocytopenia |
| **Tumors** | Metastatic (melanoma, RCC, choriocarcinoma) |
| **Drugs** | Cocaine, amphetamines |
| **Vasculitis** | CNS vasculitis |

### Causes of SAH

- **Berry (saccular) aneurysm** — 85% of non-traumatic SAH
- Common sites: AComA (most common), PComA, MCA bifurcation
- Associated with: ADPKD, Ehlers-Danlos, coarctation of aorta
- **AVM** — 10%
- **Perimesencephalic** — 5%, benign prognosis

### Pathophysiology

1. Vessel rupture → hematoma formation
2. Mass effect → surrounding tissue compression
3. Raised ICP → reduced cerebral perfusion
4. Perilesional edema → secondary injury
5. Hematoma expansion (30% within first hours)

## Clinical Features

### ICH Presentation

- **Sudden onset** severe headache
- Focal neurological deficit (depends on location)
- **Progressive** worsening (unlike ischemic stroke)
- Vomiting, altered consciousness
- Seizures (10-15%)
- Signs of raised ICP: Cushing triad (hypertension, bradycardia, irregular breathing)

### SAH Presentation

- **"Thunderclap headache"** — worst headache of life, sudden onset
- Neck stiffness (meningism) — develops over hours
- Photophobia, vomiting
- Loss of consciousness (50%)
- **Sentinel headache** — warning leak days-weeks before
- Subhyaloid (preretinal) hemorrhage on fundoscopy

## Diagnosis

### ICH

| Investigation | Finding |
|---|---|
| **NCCT head** | Hyperdense lesion (blood appears white), immediate diagnosis |
| **CT angiography** | Spot sign (active contrast extravasation = ongoing bleeding) |
| **MRI (GRE/SWI)** | Blooming artifact; detects old microbleeds (CAA) |
| **DSA** | Gold standard for AVM/aneurysm if suspected |

### SAH

| Step | Investigation |
|---|---|
| **1st** | NCCT head — sensitivity 95% day 1, decreases with time |
| **2nd (if CT negative)** | Lumbar puncture — xanthochromia (yellow CSF after centrifugation) |
| **3rd** | CT angiography / DSA — locate aneurysm |

### Grading

- **ICH Score** (0-6): GCS, volume, IVH, infratentorial, age → predicts 30-day mortality
- **Hunt & Hess** (SAH): Grade I-V based on clinical severity
- **Fisher grade** (SAH): Based on blood volume on CT → predicts vasospasm risk

## Management

### ICH Management

- **ABCs and airway protection** — GCS ≤8 = intubate
- **BP control**: Target SBP **<140 mmHg** (INTERACT2 trial) — IV labetalol/nicardipine
- **Reverse anticoagulation**: Warfarin → vitamin K + PCC/FFP; DOACs → specific reversal agents
- **ICP management**: Head elevation 30°, osmotherapy (mannitol/hypertonic saline)
- **Surgical evacuation**: Cerebellar hematoma >3 cm with deterioration; lobar ICH >30 mL with deterioration
- **Seizure prophylaxis**: Not routine; treat if seizures occur
- **DVT prevention**: Intermittent pneumatic compression (avoid heparin initially)

### SAH Management

- **Secure the aneurysm** (within 24-72 hrs): Endovascular coiling (preferred) or surgical clipping
- **Nimodipine** 60 mg q4h × 21 days — reduces vasospasm-related poor outcomes (prevents cerebral infarction)
- **Triple-H therapy** for vasospasm: Hypertension, Hypervolemia, Hemodilution
- **EVD** for hydrocephalus
- **Monitor for complications**: Rebleeding, vasospasm (days 4-14), hydrocephalus, hyponatremia (SIADH/CSW)

## Complications & Prognosis

### Complications

| Complication | Timing |
|---|---|
| **Hematoma expansion** | First 24 hours |
| **Rebleeding (SAH)** | First 24 hours (highest risk) |
| **Vasospasm (SAH)** | Days 4-14 |
| **Hydrocephalus** | Acute (days) or chronic (weeks) |
| **Seizures** | Acute or delayed |
| **Hyponatremia** | SIADH or cerebral salt wasting |

### Prognosis

- **ICH**: 30-day mortality 30-50%; depends on ICH score
- **SAH**: 30-day mortality ~40%; 30% of survivors have significant disability
- **Cerebellar hemorrhage**: Surgical emergency — good outcome if evacuated early
- **Pontine hemorrhage**: Very poor prognosis

---

*"Hemorrhagic stroke kills more — always exclude bleed before treating stroke."*
