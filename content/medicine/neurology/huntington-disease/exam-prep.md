# Huntington Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Autosomal dominant, CAG repeat expansion in HTT gene (chr 4). Triad: chorea + psychiatric disturbance + dementia. Caudate atrophy on MRI. Tetrabenazine for chorea. Fatal in 15-20 years. Anticipation through paternal transmission.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Autosomal dominant** with **complete penetrance** at ≥40 CAG repeats
- ★ Gene: **HTT, chromosome 4p16.3** — CAG (glutamine) repeat
- ★ **Anticipation** — earlier onset in successive generations (paternal > maternal)
- ★ Pathology: **caudate nucleus atrophy** → box-car ventricles
- ★ **Medium spiny neurons** (GABAergic) lost in striatum
- ★ Chorea = **hyperkinetic** movement disorder (vs PD = hypokinetic)
- ★ **Tetrabenazine** = VMAT2 inhibitor, depletes presynaptic dopamine → treats chorea
- ★ **Juvenile (Westphal) variant** = rigid-akinetic, seizures, >60 repeats, paternal inheritance
- ★ **Depression** = most common psychiatric feature; suicide risk 4x
- ★ **No disease-modifying treatment** available
- ★ **Motor impersistence** = "jack-in-the-box" tongue (cannot keep tongue protruded)

## Mnemonics

- **"HunTington = chromosome 4"** (4 letters in "Hunt")
- **CAG = "CAG causes A Glutamine repeat"**
- **Triad = "CDQ"** — Chorea, Dementia, Quirky behavior (psychiatric)
- **Trinucleotide repeat diseases**: "My Fairy Huntsman Fried Spinach" — Myotonic dystrophy, Fragile X, Huntington, Friedreich ataxia, Spinocerebellar ataxia

## Common MCQ Topics

1. Inheritance pattern and anticipation
2. CAG repeat threshold (≥40 = full penetrance)
3. Caudate atrophy on imaging

5. Westphal variant features

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Sydenham chorea** | Post-streptococcal, children, self-limiting, anti-basal ganglia antibodies |
| **Wilson disease** | Young, KF rings, low ceruloplasmin, liver disease, treatable |
| **Tardive dyskinesia** | Drug-induced (antipsychotics), orofacial predominance |
| **Chorea gravidarum** | Pregnancy-related, resolves postpartum |
| **SLE chorea** | Antiphospholipid antibodies, young women |
| **Benign hereditary chorea** | Non-progressive, NKX2-1 mutations |
