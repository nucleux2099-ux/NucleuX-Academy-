# Migraine — Explorer

## Overview

**Migraine** is a primary headache disorder characterized by recurrent episodes of **unilateral, pulsating headache** lasting 4-72 hours, associated with nausea, photophobia, and phonophobia. It affects ~15% of the population with a **3:1 female predominance**. It is the second leading cause of disability worldwide (WHO).

## Key Points

- Unilateral, pulsating, moderate-to-severe headache
- Duration: **4-72 hours**
- Aura present in ~25% (migraine with aura)
- Triggers: stress, menstruation, sleep disturbance, certain foods
- Treatment: Triptans for acute attacks; beta-blockers/valproate for prophylaxis

## Definition & Classification

### Types (ICHD-3)

| Type | Features |
|---|---|
| **Migraine without aura** | 75% of migraines; recurrent headache with nausea/photo-phonophobia |
| **Migraine with aura** | 25%; visual/sensory/speech aura preceding headache by 5-60 min |
| **Chronic migraine** | ≥15 headache days/month for >3 months, ≥8 with migraine features |
| **Hemiplegic migraine** | Motor weakness as part of aura; familial (CACNA1A, ATP1A2, SCN1A) or sporadic |
| **Basilar migraine** | Aura symptoms from brainstem (vertigo, dysarthria, tinnitus, diplopia) |
| **Retinal migraine** | Monocular visual disturbance |
| **Status migrainosus** | Debilitating attack lasting >72 hours |

### Diagnostic Criteria (Migraine without aura) — ICHD-3

At least **5 attacks** with:
- Duration **4-72 hours**
- ≥2 of: Unilateral, Pulsating, Moderate-severe intensity, Aggravated by routine activity (**UPMA**)
- ≥1 of: Nausea/vomiting, Photophobia AND phonophobia

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Triggers (5 S's + more)

- **S**tress, **S**leep changes, **S**ensory stimuli (bright light, loud noise)
- **S**kipping meals, **S**pecific foods (chocolate, cheese, red wine — tyramine)
- Hormonal changes (menstruation, OCP)
- Weather changes, altitude

### Pathophysiology — Trigeminovascular Theory

1. **Cortical spreading depression (CSD)** → wave of neuronal depolarization → aura
2. CSD activates **trigeminal nucleus caudalis**
3. Release of **CGRP, substance P, neurokinin A** from trigeminal nerve endings
4. **Neurogenic inflammation** of meningeal blood vessels
5. **Central sensitization** → allodynia, photophobia
6. **Serotonin (5-HT)** plays key role — low 5-HT between attacks, high during attacks

## Clinical Features

### Phases of Migraine

| Phase | Duration | Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Prodrome** | Hours to 1 day before | Yawning, food cravings, mood changes, neck stiffness |
| **Aura** | 5-60 min | Visual (scintillating scotoma, fortification spectra), sensory (tingling), speech |
| **Headache** | 4-72 hours | Unilateral pulsating, nausea/vomiting, photo/phonophobia, worsened by activity |
| **Postdrome** | Up to 48 hours | Fatigue, difficulty concentrating, mood changes |

### Aura Characteristics

- **Visual** (most common, 90%): Scintillating scotoma, fortification spectra, zigzag lines
- **Sensory**: Unilateral tingling/numbness spreading over minutes
- **Speech**: Dysphasia
- Develops over **5-20 minutes**, lasts **<60 minutes**
- **Positive symptoms** (scintillation) followed by **negative symptoms** (scotoma)

## Diagnosis

- **Clinical diagnosis** — based on ICHD-3 criteria
- **No investigations needed** for typical migraine
- **Red flags (SNOOP)**: Systemic symptoms, Neurological signs, Onset sudden, Older age (>50), Pattern change
- **When to image**: Red flags, first/worst headache, focal neurological signs, atypical features
- **MRI brain** preferred over CT if imaging needed

## Management

### Acute Treatment

| Step | Agent | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Mild** | NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen), paracetamol | With antiemetic (metoclopramide/domperidone) |
| **Moderate-Severe** | **Triptans** (sumatriptan, rizatriptan) | 5-HT1B/1D agonists; take early; contraindicated in IHD/uncontrolled HTN |
| **Refractory** | IV metoclopramide, IV dexamethasone, dihydroergotamine | Status migrainosus |
| **NEW agents** | Gepants (ubrogepant), Ditans (lasmiditan) | CGRP receptor antagonists, 5-HT1F agonists |

### Prophylaxis (Indications: ≥4 attacks/month or significant disability)

| First-line | Dose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Propranolol** | 40-160 mg/day | Avoid in asthma |
| **Topiramate** | 25-100 mg/day | Weight loss, contraceptive failure, avoid in pregnancy |
| **Amitriptyline** | 10-50 mg/night | Good for coexisting tension headache/insomnia |
| **Valproate** | 500-1500 mg/day | Avoid in women of childbearing age |
| **Flunarizine** | 5-10 mg/night | Calcium channel blocker, weight gain |

### CGRP Monoclonal Antibodies (Newer)

- **Erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab**
- Monthly SC injections
- For chronic migraine refractory to ≥2 preventives

### Non-Pharmacological

- Trigger avoidance, regular sleep, stress management
- Biofeedback, cognitive behavioral therapy
- Regular aerobic exercise

## Complications & Prognosis

### Complications

- **Chronic migraine** — transformation from episodic (often medication overuse)
- **Medication overuse headache** — analgesics >15 days/month or triptans >10 days/month
- **Migrainous infarction** — rare; aura symptoms persist >60 min with ischemia on imaging
- **Migraine with aura** increases stroke risk, especially with OCP + smoking

### Prognosis

- Episodic migraine often improves with age
- ~3% per year transform to chronic migraine
- Proper prophylaxis reduces frequency by ≥50%
- Women often improve after menopause
