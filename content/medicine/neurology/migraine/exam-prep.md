# Migraine — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Recurrent unilateral pulsating headache (4-72 hrs) with nausea, photo/phonophobia. Aura in 25% (visual most common). Acute: triptans. Prophylaxis: propranolol/topiramate/amitriptyline. CGRP pathway is key target.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Diagnostic criteria**: ≥5 attacks, 4-72 hrs, UPMA (2 of 4), nausea or photo+phonophobia
- ★ **Aura** = cortical spreading depression; visual most common (fortification spectra)
- ★ **Triptans** = 5-HT1B/1D agonists; contraindicated in IHD
- ★ **CGRP** = key mediator; anti-CGRP antibodies for refractory chronic migraine
- ★ **Medication overuse headache** = analgesics >15 days/month → withdrawal is treatment
- ★ **Migraine with aura + OCP + smoking** = significantly increases stroke risk
- ★ **Propranolol** = first-line prophylaxis
- ★ **Topiramate** = causes weight loss (unlike other preventives); teratogenic
- ★ **Hemiplegic migraine** = motor aura; triptans relatively contraindicated
- ★ **Status migrainosus** = attack >72 hours, treat with IV steroids

## Mnemonics

- **UPMA** — Unilateral, Pulsating, Moderate-severe, Aggravated by activity
- **SNOOP** red flags — Systemic, Neurological, Onset sudden, Older, Pattern change
- **Prophylaxis "PAVE-F"** — Propranolol, Amitriptyline, Valproate, Ergot derivatives, Flunarizine

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Differentiating Feature |
|---|---|
| **Tension headache** | Bilateral, pressing/tightening, no nausea/aura, mild-moderate |
| **Cluster headache** | Strictly unilateral, periorbital, autonomic features, 15-180 min, male predominance |
| **SAH** | Thunderclap onset, neck stiffness, CT/LP positive |
| **Temporal arteritis** | Age >50, jaw claudication, raised ESR, temporal artery tenderness |
| **Intracranial tumor** | Progressive, worse in morning, focal signs, papilledema |
| **Sinusitis** | Facial pain/pressure, nasal discharge, fever |
