## 🧠 Mnemonics

### Severe Malaria Criteria: **ARCHSS**
- **A** — Anemia (Hb <5), Acidosis, AKI
- **R** — Respiratory distress (ARDS)
- **C** — Cerebral malaria (coma)
- **H** — Hypoglycemia, Hemoglobinuria (blackwater fever)
- **S** — Shock
- **S** — Seizures, hyperparasitemia (>5%)

### Dengue Warning Signs: **ABCHLM**
Abdominal pain, Bleeding mucosal, Clinical fluid accumulation, Hepatomegaly, Lethargy, Massive hemoconcentration (↑Hct + ↓platelets)

### Weil-Felix Reactions
- OXK → Scrub typhus
- OX19 → Epidemic typhus, Rocky Mountain spotted fever
- OX2 → Indian tick typhus

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **P. falciparum** — banana gametocytes, multiple ring forms, Maurer's clefts, parasitized RBCs don't enlarge
2. **P. vivax** — enlarged RBCs, Schüffner's dots, amoeboid trophozoites
3. **Blackwater fever** — massive hemolysis in falciparum → hemoglobinuria → dark urine → AKI
4. **Chloroquine-resistant P. falciparum** — treat with ACT. Chloroquine still works for P. vivax in India
5. **Dengue**: Platelet transfusion only if <10,000 (without bleeding) or <20,000 (with bleeding)
6. **Typhoid**: Blood culture positive Week 1, Stool culture positive Week 2-3, Urine culture Week 3
7. **Relative bradycardia** (Faget sign) — fever with inappropriately slow pulse: Typhoid, Legionella, Brucella
8. **Scrub typhus eschar** — painless, necrotic, found in skin folds (axilla, groin). May be missed!
9. **Leptospirosis** — conjunctival suffusion WITHOUT chemosis is characteristic (unlike other infections)
10. **Doxycycline** treats: Scrub typhus, leptospirosis, rickettsial infections, brucellosis — "tropical wonder drug"

## 📋 Fever with Thrombocytopenia — Differential

| Infection | Key Clue |
|---|---|
| Dengue | Hemoconcentration, transaminitis, day 3-7 critical |
| Malaria | Periodic fever, smear/RDT positive |
| Scrub typhus | Eschar, responds to doxycycline |
| Leptospirosis | Conjunctival suffusion, AKI, jaundice |
| Typhoid | Stepladder fever, relative bradycardia |
| Viral hemorrhagic fever | Travel history, bleeding manifestations |

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A patient from a flood-affected area presents with fever, jaundice, oliguria, and conjunctival suffusion. Diagnosis?

> **Leptospirosis (Weil disease)**. Treat with IV Penicillin G. Check for AKI, coagulopathy.

### Q2
A child with fever for 5 days, now afebrile, develops abdominal pain and cold extremities. Hematocrit rising, platelets falling. Diagnosis?

> **Dengue shock syndrome** — critical phase during defervescence. Immediate IV fluid bolus (crystalloids).

### Q3
Drug of choice for severe P. falciparum malaria?

> **IV Artesunate** — superior to IV quinine (SEAQUAMAT and AQUAMAT trials).

### Q4
A patient with fever has an eschar in the axilla and lymphadenopathy. Weil-Felix OXK positive. Treatment?

> **Scrub typhus** — Doxycycline 100mg BD for 7 days (or until 48h afebrile).

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **Empiric Doxycycline**
> In undifferentiated acute febrile illness in India with eschar, rash, or AKI — start empiric doxycycline while awaiting investigations. It covers scrub typhus, leptospirosis, and other rickettsial infections.

> [!warning] **Primaquine + G6PD**
> Always screen for G6PD deficiency before prescribing primaquine for P. vivax radical cure. Primaquine causes oxidative hemolysis in G6PD-deficient individuals.
