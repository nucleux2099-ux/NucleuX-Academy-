# HIV/AIDS — Exam Prep

## High Yield Points

- ★ **CD4 <200** = AIDS + start PCP prophylaxis (TMP-SMX)
- ★ **PCP:** Bilateral GGO, elevated LDH, CD4 <200; treat TMP-SMX; add steroids if PaO2 <70
- ★ **Toxoplasma:** Multiple ring-enhancing lesions; **CNS lymphoma:** Single ring-enhancing lesion + EBV
- ★ **Cryptococcal meningitis:** CD4 <100; CSF CrAg; India ink; treat with Amphotericin B + Flucytosine
- ★ **CMV retinitis:** CD4 <50; "pizza pie" fundus; IV ganciclovir
- ★ **NACO first-line:** TDF + 3TC + DTG (single pill, once daily)
- ★ **Viral load** = best marker of treatment response; CD4 = best marker of immune status
- ★ **U=U:** Undetectable = Untransmittable; viral load <50 = no sexual transmission
- ★ **IRIS:** Paradoxical worsening after ART start; common with TB, Crypto; continue ART + treat OI
- ★ **PEP within 72 hours** × 28 days; **PrEP** = TDF + FTC daily for high-risk individuals

## MCQs

### MCQ 1
An HIV+ patient with CD4 count 80 presents with headache, fever, and neck stiffness. CSF shows lymphocytic pleocytosis, high opening pressure, and positive India ink. The treatment of choice is:

a) IV Acyclovir
b) Amphotericin B + Flucytosine
c) IV Ceftriaxone
d) TMP-SMX

**Answer: b) Amphotericin B + Flucytosine**

*Cryptococcal meningitis (CD4 <100): India ink+ (50% sensitivity), CSF CrAg (>95% sensitive). Induction: IV Amphotericin B + oral flucytosine × 2 weeks → Fluconazole 400mg × 8 weeks → Fluconazole 200mg maintenance. Serial LPs for raised ICP. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 213*

### MCQ 2
An HIV+ patient with CD4 150 presents with ring-enhancing lesions in the basal ganglia on CT. The most likely diagnosis and empiric treatment is:

a) CNS lymphoma; radiation
b) Toxoplasma encephalitis; pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine
c) Brain abscess; antibiotics + surgery
d) Tuberculoma; ATT

**Answer: b) Toxoplasma encephalitis; pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine**

*Multiple ring-enhancing lesions + CD4 <200 = toxoplasma until proven otherwise. Empiric treatment for 2 weeks → if no response, consider biopsy (may be lymphoma). Toxo serology (IgG) is usually positive. Single lesion + EBV = think lymphoma. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 203*

### MCQ 3
Which of the following is the MOST common AIDS-defining malignancy?

a) Cervical carcinoma
b) Kaposi sarcoma
c) Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
d) Anal carcinoma

**Answer: b) Kaposi sarcoma**

*Kaposi sarcoma (HHV-8/KSHV): most common AIDS-defining malignancy. Presents as violaceous skin lesions/plaques. Can involve viscera (GI, lungs). Treatment: ART (often sufficient) + chemotherapy if advanced. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 203*

### MCQ 4
A healthcare worker sustains a needlestick injury from an HIV+ patient. PEP should be started within:

a) 24 hours
b) 48 hours
c) 72 hours
d) 1 week

**Answer: c) 72 hours**

*PEP: Start within 72 hours (ideally within 2 hours). Regimen: TDF + 3TC + DTG × 28 days. Test source and exposed. Follow-up HIV testing at 6 weeks, 3 months, 6 months. PEP not indicated if source is confirmed HIV-negative. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 202*

### MCQ 5
Which antiretroviral drug class has the fewest drug interactions and is preferred as the third agent in first-line ART?

a) NNRTIs
b) Protease inhibitors
c) Integrase strand transfer inhibitors (INSTIs)
d) NRTIs

**Answer: c) Integrase strand transfer inhibitors (INSTIs)**

*INSTIs (dolutegravir, raltegravir, bictegravir) have the fewest drug interactions, best tolerability, and highest barrier to resistance (DTG). DTG is now the preferred third agent globally and in India (NACO TLD regimen). 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 202*
