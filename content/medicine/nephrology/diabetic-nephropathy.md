# Diabetic Nephropathy

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 418, KDIGO Diabetes & CKD Guidelines 2020
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Diabetic nephropathy (DN) is a microvascular complication of diabetes mellitus, representing the leading cause of CKD and ESKD worldwide. It develops in 20-40% of diabetics, typically after 10-20 years of disease. The hallmark is progressive albuminuria followed by declining GFR. Pathologically, it shows nodular glomerulosclerosis (Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules). Early detection via microalbuminuria screening and aggressive management with ACEi/ARBs and SGLT2 inhibitors can slow or prevent progression.

## Key Points

- **Leading cause of ESKD** in developed countries
- **Natural history:** Hyperfiltration → Microalbuminuria → Overt proteinuria → ↓GFR → ESKD
- **Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules:** Pathognomonic nodular glomerulosclerosis
- **ACEi/ARBs + SGLT2i:** Cornerstone of renoprotection
- **Other microvascular disease** often present (retinopathy, neuropathy)

## Epidemiology

- Develops in 20-40% of type 1 and type 2 diabetics
- Type 1: Peak incidence at 15-20 years of DM
- Type 2: May be present at diagnosis (undetected DM duration)
- Leading cause of ESKD: ~45% of new dialysis patients

## Pathophysiology

### Stages of Development

| Stage | Timeline | Features |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **1. Hyperfiltration** | At diagnosis | ↑GFR (>120), enlarged kidneys |
| **2. Silent phase** | 2-5 years | Normal urinalysis, GBM thickening begins |
| **3. Incipient DN** | 5-15 years | Microalbuminuria (30-300 mg/day) |
| **4. Overt DN** | 15-25 years | Macroalbuminuria (>300 mg/day), ↓GFR |
| **5. ESKD** | 20-30 years | GFR <15, need for RRT |

### Mechanisms
- **Hyperglycemia:** AGE products, PKC activation, polyol pathway
- **Hemodynamic:** Glomerular hypertension, hyperfiltration
- **Genetic:** Family history of DN increases risk
- **Other factors:** Hypertension, dyslipidemia, smoking

## Clinical Features

### Presentation
- Often asymptomatic until advanced
- Microalbuminuria detected on screening
- Progressive proteinuria
- Hypertension (often precedes nephropathy in Type 2)
- Edema in nephrotic-range proteinuria

### Associated Findings
| Finding | Significance |
|---------|--------------|
| **Diabetic retinopathy** | Present in >90% of Type 1 with DN |
| **Diabetic neuropathy** | Common association |
| **Cardiovascular disease** | Major cause of mortality |
| **Hypertension** | Both cause and consequence |

> **Pearl:** If albuminuria present WITHOUT retinopathy in Type 1 → consider alternative diagnosis

## Diagnosis

### Screening Recommendations
- **Type 1:** Annual screening starting 5 years after diagnosis
- **Type 2:** At diagnosis and annually thereafter
- **Test:** Spot urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR)

### Albuminuria Categories

| Category | UACR (mg/g) | Description |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **A1** | <30 | Normal |
| **A2** | 30-300 | Moderately increased (microalbuminuria) |
| **A3** | >300 | Severely increased (macroalbuminuria) |

### When to Suspect Alternative Diagnosis
- Rapid decline in GFR
- Active urinary sediment (RBC casts)
- Absence of diabetic retinopathy (Type 1)
- Duration of DM <5 years (Type 1)
- Rapid onset of heavy proteinuria
- Consider renal biopsy if atypical features

### Pathology (Biopsy)
- **Diffuse glomerulosclerosis:** Early, common
- **Nodular glomerulosclerosis:** Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules (pathognomonic)
- **Arteriolar hyalinosis:** Afferent AND efferent (characteristic)
- **GBM thickening:** Universal finding

## Treatment

### Glycemic Control
| Target | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **HbA1c** | <7% for most; individualize for elderly/comorbid |
| **Avoid hypoglycemia** | Risk increases as GFR declines |
| **SGLT2 inhibitors** | Preferred agent for renal protection |

### Blood Pressure Control
- **Target:** <130/80 mmHg (or <120/80 if tolerated)
- **First-line:** ACEi or ARB
- Can add dihydropyridine CCB, diuretic as needed

### ACEi/ARBs
- Reduce intraglomerular pressure
- Reduce proteinuria independent of BP
- Slow GFR decline
- Monitor potassium and creatinine (up to 30% ↑Cr acceptable)
- **Do NOT combine ACEi + ARB** (ONTARGET trial: more harm)

### SGLT2 Inhibitors (Game-Changers!)
| Drug | Evidence |
|------|----------|
| **Dapagliflozin** | DAPA-CKD: 39% ↓ renal composite |
| **Empagliflozin** | EMPA-KIDNEY: Benefit across GFR ranges |
| **Canagliflozin** | CREDENCE: 30% ↓ renal events |

- Benefit independent of glucose lowering
- Can use down to GFR 20-25 mL/min
- SE: UTI, DKA risk, volume depletion

### GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
- Secondary renoprotective effects
- Semaglutide, liraglutide
- Also reduce CV events

### Other Measures
| Intervention | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| **Statin therapy** | For CV risk reduction |
| **Finerenone** | Non-steroidal MRA; additional renoprotection |
| **Smoking cessation** | Slows progression |
| **Dietary protein** | 0.8 g/kg/day in advanced CKD |
| **Avoid NSAIDs** | Nephrotoxic |

## Treatment Algorithm

```
Diabetic with Albuminuria/CKD
           |
           v
    ACEi or ARB (first-line)
           |
           v
    Add SGLT2 inhibitor (if eGFR ≥20)
           |
           v
    Consider Finerenone (if K+ normal)
           |
           v
    BP control (<130/80)
           |
           v
    HbA1c <7% (individualize)
           |
           v
    Statin, lifestyle, avoid nephrotoxins
```

## High-Yield Points

1. **Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules** = pathognomonic for DN
2. **Afferent + Efferent arteriolar hyalinosis** = DN (only efferent in HTN)
3. **No retinopathy + proteinuria in Type 1** = likely NOT DN, biopsy!
4. **SGLT2i** = standard of care, benefit beyond glucose control
5. **ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i** = dual blockade is GOOD (unlike ACEi + ARB)
6. **Cr rise up to 30%** with ACEi/ARB is acceptable - don't stop!
