# Acute Kidney Injury

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 310, KDIGO Guidelines 2012
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) is an abrupt decline in kidney function occurring over hours to days, characterized by the accumulation of nitrogenous wastes (azotemia) and decreased urine output. AKI is defined by KDIGO criteria as an increase in serum creatinine by ≥0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours, OR increase in creatinine to ≥1.5 times baseline within 7 days, OR urine volume <0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6 hours. The causes are classified as prerenal (decreased perfusion), intrinsic (parenchymal damage), or postrenal (obstruction).

## Key Points

- **Definition:** ↑Creatinine ≥0.3 mg/dL in 48h OR ≥1.5× baseline in 7 days OR oliguria <0.5 mL/kg/hr × 6h
- **Pre-renal (60-70%):** Most common, reversible if treated early
- **Intrinsic (25-40%):** ATN most common cause, direct parenchymal injury
- **Post-renal (5-10%):** Obstruction, bilateral or solitary kidney
- **FENa** <1% = prerenal, >2% = intrinsic (ATN)

## Classification by Cause

### Pre-renal AKI (60-70%)
| Cause | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| **Volume depletion** | Hemorrhage, GI losses, burns, diuretics |
| **Decreased cardiac output** | Heart failure, cardiogenic shock, PE |
| **Systemic vasodilation** | Sepsis, anaphylaxis, cirrhosis (HRS) |
| **Renal vasoconstriction** | NSAIDs, ACEi/ARBs, cyclosporine |
| **Renal artery** | Stenosis, thrombosis, embolism |

### Intrinsic AKI (25-40%)
| Location | Cause |
|----------|-------|
| **Tubular (ATN)** | Ischemic, nephrotoxic (aminoglycosides, contrast, cisplatin) |
| **Glomerular** | RPGN, anti-GBM, lupus nephritis, IgA |
| **Interstitial (AIN)** | Drug-induced (NSAIDs, PPIs, antibiotics), infections |
| **Vascular** | TTP-HUS, malignant HTN, vasculitis, cholesterol emboli |

### Post-renal AKI (5-10%)
| Level | Cause |
|-------|-------|
| **Ureteral** | Stones, malignancy, retroperitoneal fibrosis |
| **Bladder** | BPH, neurogenic bladder, tumor |
| **Urethral** | Stricture, phimosis |

## Clinical Features

### Phases of ATN
1. **Initiation:** Hours to days, injury occurring
2. **Maintenance:** Days to weeks, oliguria, azotemia
3. **Recovery:** Polyuric phase, tubular recovery

### Symptoms
- Oliguria (most common) or anuria
- Volume overload: edema, dyspnea, JVP elevation
- Uremic symptoms: nausea, vomiting, encephalopathy
- Pericarditis (late), asterixis

## Diagnosis

### KDIGO Staging

| Stage | Serum Creatinine | Urine Output |
|-------|------------------|--------------|
| **1** | 1.5-1.9× baseline OR ↑≥0.3 mg/dL | <0.5 mL/kg/hr × 6-12h |
| **2** | 2.0-2.9× baseline | <0.5 mL/kg/hr × ≥12h |
| **3** | ≥3× baseline OR ≥4 mg/dL OR RRT | <0.3 mL/kg/hr × ≥24h OR anuria × 12h |

### Differentiating Pre-renal vs ATN

| Parameter | Pre-renal | ATN |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **FENa** | <1% | >2% |
| **FEUrea** | <35% | >50% |
| **Urine Na** | <20 mEq/L | >40 mEq/L |
| **Urine osmolality** | >500 mOsm/kg | <350 mOsm/kg |
| **BUN:Creatinine** | >20:1 | <15:1 |
| **Urine sediment** | Bland, hyaline casts | Muddy brown granular casts |
| **Response to fluids** | Improves | No improvement |

> ⚠️ **FENa unreliable in:** diuretic use, CKD, glycosuria, contrast

### Workup
- **Urinalysis + sediment:** RBC casts (GN), WBC casts (AIN), muddy casts (ATN)
- **Renal ultrasound:** Rule out obstruction, assess kidney size
- **Urine electrolytes:** FENa, FEUrea calculation
- **Serologies:** If GN suspected (ANA, ANCA, anti-GBM, C3/C4)
- **Renal biopsy:** If etiology unclear, rapidly progressive

## Treatment

### General Principles
1. **Identify and treat cause**
2. **Optimize hemodynamics** (fluids vs diuretics)
3. **Avoid nephrotoxins** (NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, contrast)
4. **Adjust drug doses** for GFR
5. **Manage complications** (hyperkalemia, acidosis, volume overload)

### Pre-renal
- Volume resuscitation (crystalloids)
- Treat underlying cause
- Avoid/stop offending agents (NSAIDs, ACEi)

### Intrinsic
- ATN: Supportive care, avoid further injury
- AIN: Remove offending drug, consider steroids
- GN: Immunosuppression per etiology

### Post-renal
- Urgent decompression (Foley, nephrostomy)
- Urology consultation

### Indications for Dialysis (AEIOU)
- **A**cidosis (pH <7.1, refractory)
- **E**lectrolytes (K >6.5, refractory)
- **I**ntoxication (methanol, ethylene glycol, salicylates)
- **O**verload (pulmonary edema, refractory)
- **U**remia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding)

## High-Yield Points

1. **FENa <1%** suggests prerenal; **>2%** suggests ATN (use FEUrea if on diuretics)
2. **Muddy brown granular casts** = ATN pathognomonic finding
3. **BUN:Cr >20:1** suggests prerenal or GI bleed
4. **Post-renal** requires bilateral obstruction (or solitary kidney)
5. **Contrast-induced AKI:** ↑Cr 24-48h post, peaks 3-5 days, recovers 7-10 days
6. **Hepatorenal syndrome:** Prerenal physiology in cirrhosis, poor prognosis
