## 1. Definition **[UG]**

AKI = Rise in serum creatinine ≥0.3 mg/dL in 48h OR ≥1.5× baseline in 7 days OR UOP <0.5 mL/kg/h for 6h (KDIGO).

**Key fact:** AKI is *reversible*; CKD is *irreversible* (but AKI → CKD transition occurs).

---

## 2. RIFLE / AKIN / KDIGO Staging **[UG]**

| Stage | Creatinine | Urine Output |
|-------|-----------|--------------|
| **1** (Risk) | 1.5–1.9× baseline OR ↑≥0.3 mg/dL | <0.5 mL/kg/h × 6–12h |
| **2** (Injury) | 2.0–2.9× baseline | <0.5 mL/kg/h × ≥12h |
| **3** (Failure) | ≥3× baseline OR ≥4.0 mg/dL OR RRT initiated | <0.3 mL/kg/h × ≥24h OR anuria ≥12h |

> **MCQ pearl:** KDIGO is the current standard. RIFLE used GFR criteria; AKIN added the 0.3 mg/dL absolute rise. KDIGO unified both.

---

## 3. Pre-renal vs Renal (ATN) vs Post-renal **[UG]**

| Parameter | Pre-renal | Renal (ATN) | Post-renal |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **FeNa** | <1% | >2% | Variable |
| **FeUrea** | <35% | >50% | Variable |
| **Urine Na** | <20 mEq/L | >40 mEq/L | Variable |
| **Urine osmolality** | >500 | <350 | Variable |
| **BUN:Cr ratio** | >20:1 | <15:1 | Variable |
| **Urine sediment** | Bland/hyaline casts | Muddy brown granular casts | Bland |
| **Response to fluids** | Yes ✓ | No ✗ | No (needs decompression) |

> **MCQ trap:** Diuretics make FeNa unreliable → use **FeUrea** instead.

**Mnemonic for Pre-renal causes — "HYPO":** Hypovolemia, Your heart failing, Peripheral vasodilation (sepsis), Obstruction of renal artery.

## 4. Intrinsic Renal AKI — Localize the Lesion **[PG]**

| Site | Example | Clue |
|------|---------|------|
| Tubule | ATN (ischemic/nephrotoxic) | Muddy brown casts |
| Glomerulus | RPGN, lupus nephritis | RBC casts, dysmorphic RBCs |
| Interstitium | AIN (drugs) | WBC casts, eosinophiluria, fever-rash-eosinophilia triad |
| Vessels | TTP-HUS, renal vein thrombosis | Schistocytes (TTP), flank pain (RVT) |

> **AIN triad:** Fever + Rash + Eosinophilia (classic but present in <30%).

## 5. Dialysis Indications — **AEIOU** **[UG]**

| Letter | Indication |
|--------|-----------|
| **A** | Acidosis (refractory metabolic, pH <7.1) |
| **E** | Electrolytes (refractory hyperkalemia >6.5) |
| **I** | Intoxication (methanol, ethylene glycol, lithium, salicylates) |
| **O** | Overload (fluid, refractory pulmonary edema) |
| **U** | Uremia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding) |

> **MCQ pearl:** Uremic pericarditis is an **absolute** indication for emergent dialysis.

---

## 6. Hyperkalemia Management **[UG]**

**Stepwise — "C BIG K Drop":**

1. **C**alcium gluconate → Cardiac membrane stabilization (immediate, no K+ change)
2. **B**icarbonate (NaHCO₃) → Shifts K+ intracellularly
3. **I**nsulin + Glucose (10U regular + 25g dextrose) → Shifts K+ in
4. **G**ive salbutamol (nebulized) → β2-mediated shift
5. **K** binders — Kayexalate / Patiromer / SZC → Remove K+
6. **D**ialysis → Definitive removal

> **ECG changes (progressive):** Tall peaked T → Prolonged PR → Wide QRS → Sine wave → Asystole.

## 7. CKD Staging by GFR **[UG]**

| Stage | GFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| G1 | ≥90 | Normal/high (with kidney damage markers) |
| G2 | 60–89 | Mildly decreased |
| G3a | 45–59 | Mild-moderate decrease |
| G3b | 30–44 | Moderate-severe decrease |
| G4 | 15–29 | Severely decreased |
| G5 | <15 | Kidney failure (dialysis territory) |

> **MCQ pearl:** CKD diagnosis requires abnormality present for **>3 months**. Most common cause worldwide = **Diabetes mellitus**.

---

## 8. Rapid-Fire MCQ Pearls **[UG]**

- **Most common cause of AKI in hospitalized patients:** ATN (ischemic)
- **Most common cause of community-acquired AKI:** Pre-renal
- **Contrast nephropathy** peaks at 48–72h; prevent with **IV NS hydration**
- **Rhabdomyolysis AKI:** CK >5× ULN, tea-colored urine, ↑K⁺, ↑PO₄, ↓Ca²⁺ → aggressive NS
- **Tumor lysis syndrome:** ↑K⁺, ↑PO₄, ↑uric acid, ↓Ca²⁺ → rasburicase
- **HRS (Hepatorenal syndrome):** FeNa <1% but doesn't respond to fluids → albumin + terlipressin
