# Rheumatoid Arthritis & SLE — Exam Prep

## High Yield Points

- ★ **Anti-CCP** = most specific for RA (95%); **RF** = sensitive but not specific (also + in Sjögren's, SLE, infective endocarditis)
- ★ RA spares **DIP joints** (DIP involvement = OA or psoriatic arthritis)
- ★ **Felty syndrome:** RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia
- ★ **Caplan syndrome:** RA + coal worker's pneumoconiosis → rheumatoid pneumoconiosis
- ★ **Methotrexate** = anchor DMARD in RA; always give with **folic acid**; screen for TB before biologics
- ★ **ANA** = most sensitive for SLE (>95%); **Anti-Sm** = most specific; **Anti-dsDNA** = specific + correlates with nephritis
- ★ **Lupus nephritis Class IV** (diffuse proliferative) = worst prognosis; wire-loop lesion; full-house IF
- ★ **Drug-induced lupus:** Anti-histone antibodies; procainamide (#1 cause), hydralazine (#1 drug to cause it per prevalence); spares kidneys/CNS
- ★ **Hydroxychloroquine** = given to ALL SLE patients; reduces flares and improves long-term survival
- ★ **Jaccoud's arthropathy** in SLE = non-erosive, reducible deformities (unlike RA which is erosive)

## MCQs

### MCQ 1
A 35-year-old woman with symmetric polyarthritis of MCPs and PIPs for 3 months. Morning stiffness lasts 2 hours. RF positive, Anti-CCP positive. X-ray shows periarticular osteopenia. The FIRST-LINE DMARD is:

a) Hydroxychloroquine
b) Methotrexate
c) Infliximab
d) Prednisolone

**Answer: b) Methotrexate**

*Methotrexate is the anchor DMARD in RA — started as early as possible ("treat to target" strategy). Dose: 7.5-25mg/week with folic acid supplementation. Biologics are added only if inadequate response to MTX after 3-6 months. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 358*

### MCQ 2
A 22-year-old woman presents with malar rash, oral ulcers, arthritis, and proteinuria. ANA is 1:640. Which antibody best correlates with renal disease activity?

a) Anti-Sm
b) Anti-dsDNA
c) Anti-histone
d) Anti-Ro (SSA)

**Answer: b) Anti-dsDNA**

*Anti-dsDNA antibodies are specific for SLE and their titers correlate with disease activity, particularly lupus nephritis. Rising titers with falling complement (C3/C4) suggest active nephritis. Anti-Sm is most specific but doesn't track activity. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 357*

---

### MCQ 3
A patient develops SLE-like symptoms while on procainamide. Which antibody is most characteristic?

a) Anti-dsDNA
b) Anti-Sm
c) Anti-histone
d) Anti-Ro

**Answer: c) Anti-histone**

*Drug-induced lupus: anti-histone antibodies in >95%. Key differences from idiopathic SLE: spares kidneys and CNS, resolves after stopping the drug, anti-dsDNA usually negative. Common culprits: procainamide, hydralazine, INH, methyldopa. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 357*

### MCQ 4
Renal biopsy in a lupus nephritis patient shows "wire-loop" thickening of capillary walls and subendothelial deposits. The ISN/RPS class is:

a) Class II
b) Class III
c) Class IV
d) Class V

**Answer: c) Class IV**

*Class IV (diffuse proliferative GN): most common and most severe form. Wire-loop lesion = thickened capillary walls from subendothelial immune deposits. Full-house immunofluorescence (IgG, IgM, IgA, C3, C1q). Treatment: cyclophosphamide/MMF induction → MMF/AZA maintenance. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 357*

### MCQ 5
A patient with RA has atlantoaxial subluxation. The most important initial investigation is:

a) MRI cervical spine
b) Lateral flexion X-ray of cervical spine
c) CT cervical spine
d) Nerve conduction study

**Answer: b) Lateral flexion X-ray of cervical spine**

*Atlantoaxial subluxation (C1-C2): pannus erodes the transverse ligament of atlas → ADI (atlantodental interval) >3mm on lateral flexion X-ray. Risk of cord compression → myelopathy. Must screen before intubation/surgery in RA patients. 📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 358*
