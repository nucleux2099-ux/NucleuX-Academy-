# Rheumatoid Arthritis & SLE — Explorer

## Overview

**Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)** is a chronic, systemic autoimmune disease characterized by **symmetrical inflammatory polyarthritis** primarily affecting small joints of hands and feet. **Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)** is a multisystem autoimmune disorder with protean manifestations. Both are among the most frequently tested rheumatology topics in NEET-PG.

📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 358 (RA), Ch. 357 (SLE)

---

## RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS

## Key Points — RA

- **Symmetric** inflammatory polyarthritis; **MCP, PIP, wrist** joints (spares DIP — DIP = OA/psoriatic)
- **Morning stiffness >1 hour** (improves with activity; OA worsens)
- **RF** (70-80% sensitive) and **Anti-CCP** (most specific — 95%)
- **Pannus** — proliferative synovium that erodes cartilage and bone
- **2010 ACR/EULAR criteria** for classification (score ≥6/10)
- **Early aggressive DMARD therapy** — "treat to target" (remission or low disease activity)
- **Methotrexate** = anchor DMARD (first-line); add biologics if inadequate response

## Clinical Features — RA

### Joint Involvement
- **Early:** MCP, PIP, wrist (bilateral, symmetric)
- **Late deformities:**
  - **Swan-neck:** PIP hyperextension + DIP flexion
  - **Boutonnière:** PIP flexion + DIP hyperextension
  - **Ulnar deviation** at MCPs
  - **Z-deformity** of thumb
  - **Atlantoaxial subluxation** (C1-C2) — risk of cord compression

### Extra-articular Manifestations
| System | Manifestation |
|---|---|
| Skin | Rheumatoid nodules (extensor surfaces; pathognomonic) |
| Lung | ILD, pleural effusion (low glucose, high LDH), Caplan syndrome (RA + pneumoconiosis) |
| Eye | Scleritis, episcleritis, keratoconjunctivitis sicca (Sjögren's) |
| Vascular | Vasculitis |
| Heme | Anemia of chronic disease; Felty syndrome (RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia) |

## Diagnosis — RA

### 2010 ACR/EULAR Classification Criteria (Score ≥ 6)
| Domain | Criteria | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Joint involvement | 1 large joint (0); 2-10 large (1); 1-3 small (2); 4-10 small (3); >10 with ≥1 small (5) | 0-5 |
| Serology | Negative RF and anti-CCP (0); Low + (2); High + (3) | 0-3 |
| Acute phase | Normal CRP and ESR (0); Abnormal (1) | 0-1 |
| Duration | <6 weeks (0); ≥6 weeks (1) | 0-1 |

### Imaging
- **X-ray hands:** Soft tissue swelling → periarticular osteopenia → joint space narrowing → erosions
- **USG/MRI:** Early synovitis and erosions

## Management — RA

### DMARDs (Start early — within 3 months!)
| Drug | Dose | Key Toxicity |
|---|---|---|
| **Methotrexate** | 7.5-25mg/week + folic acid | Hepatotoxicity, bone marrow suppression, pneumonitis |
| **Hydroxychloroquine** | 200-400mg/day | Retinal toxicity (bull's eye maculopathy) |
| **Sulfasalazine** | 2-3g/day | GI, bone marrow suppression |
| **Leflunomide** | 20mg/day | Hepatotoxicity, teratogenic |

### Biologics (if DMARD failure)
| Agent | Target |
|---|---|
| **TNF inhibitors** (infliximab, adalimumab, etanercept) | TNF-α |
| **Rituximab** | CD20 (B cells) |
| **Tocilizumab** | IL-6 receptor |
| **Abatacept** | CTLA-4 (T-cell costimulation) |
| **Tofacitinib** | JAK inhibitor (oral) |

> **Exam Pearl:** Screen for **latent TB** before starting TNF inhibitors (risk of TB reactivation).

## SYSTEMIC LUPUS ERYTHEMATOSUS

## Key Points — SLE

- **Young women** (F:M = 9:1, peak 15-45 years)
- **Multisystem** autoimmune disease; "the great imitator"
- **ANA** = most sensitive test (>95%); **Anti-dsDNA** = most specific + correlates with disease activity/nephritis
- **Anti-Smith (Sm)** = most specific antibody for SLE
- **Lupus nephritis** = major cause of morbidity/mortality; Class IV (diffuse proliferative) is worst
- **Drug-induced lupus:** Hydralazine, procainamide, INH → anti-histone antibodies; spares kidneys/CNS
- **Hydroxychloroquine** = background therapy for ALL SLE patients (reduces flares, improves survival)

## 2019 EULAR/ACR Classification Criteria

**Entry criterion:** ANA ≥1:80

**Additive criteria (score ≥10 with ≥1 clinical domain):**

| Domain | Criteria | Points |
|---|---|---|
| Constitutional | Fever | 2 |
| Hematologic | Leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, autoimmune hemolysis | 2-4 |
| Neuropsychiatric | Seizures, psychosis, delirium | 2-5 |
| Mucocutaneous | Oral ulcers, alopecia, malar rash, discoid rash | 2-6 |
| Serosal | Pleural/pericardial effusion | 5-6 |
| Musculoskeletal | Joint involvement | 6 |
| Renal | Proteinuria, Class III-V nephritis | 4-10 |
| Immunology | Anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm, antiphospholipid, complement (low C3/C4) | 2-6 |

## Clinical Features — SLE

### Classic Presentations
- **Malar (butterfly) rash** — spares nasolabial folds (unlike rosacea which involves them)
- **Discoid rash** — scarring alopecia
- **Photosensitivity**
- **Oral ulcers** (painless)
- **Non-erosive arthritis** (Jaccoud's arthropathy — reducible deformities)
- **Serositis** — pleuritis, pericarditis
- **Libman-Sacks endocarditis** — verrucous vegetations on BOTH surfaces of mitral valve

### Lupus Nephritis (WHO/ISN Classification)
| Class | Type | Prognosis |
|---|---|---|
| I | Minimal mesangial | Excellent |
| II | Mesangial proliferative | Good |
| III | Focal proliferative | Moderate |
| **IV** | **Diffuse proliferative** | **Worst; most common; wire-loop lesion** |
| V | Membranous | Nephrotic syndrome |
| VI | Sclerosing | End-stage |

### Lab Findings
- **ANA** — most sensitive (>95%); not specific
- **Anti-dsDNA** — specific; correlates with nephritis activity; titers track disease
- **Anti-Sm** — most specific for SLE
- **Low complement (C3, C4)** — active disease (immune complex consumption)
- **Anti-phospholipid antibodies** — thrombosis, recurrent miscarriage (APLA syndrome)
- **Pancytopenia** — autoimmune; Coombs-positive hemolytic anemia

## Management — SLE

| Severity | Treatment |
|---|---|
| **All patients** | Hydroxychloroquine 200-400mg/day + sunscreen |
| **Mild** (skin, joints) | NSAIDs + low-dose steroids |
| **Moderate** (serositis, cytopenias) | Moderate-dose steroids + azathioprine/MMF |
| **Severe** (nephritis Class III-V, CNS) | High-dose IV methylprednisolone pulse → cyclophosphamide (induction) → MMF/azathioprine (maintenance) |
| **Refractory** | Rituximab, belimumab (anti-BLyS), voclosporin |

> **Exam Pearl:** **Lupus nephritis Class IV** — "wire-loop" lesion on LM, subendothelial deposits on EM, "full-house" immunofluorescence (IgG, IgM, IgA, C3, C1q). Treat with cyclophosphamide or MMF induction.
