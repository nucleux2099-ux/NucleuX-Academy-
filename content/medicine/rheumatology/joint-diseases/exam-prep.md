## 🧠 Mnemonics

### SLE Criteria: **SOAP BRAIN MD**
- **S** — Serositis
- **O** — Oral ulcers
- **A** — Arthritis (non-erosive)
- **P** — Photosensitivity
- **B** — Blood (cytopenias)
- **R** — Renal (nephritis)
- **A** — ANA
- **I** — Immunologic (dsDNA, Sm, APL)
- **N** — Neurologic (seizures, psychosis)
- **M** — Malar rash
- **D** — Discoid rash

### Crystal Arthropathy: **"Gout is Negative, Pseudo is Positive"**
- Gout: **Negatively** birefringent, **needle**-shaped, yellow parallel
- Pseudogout: **Positively** birefringent, **rhomboid**, blue parallel

### RA Hand Deformities: **"SBU-Z"**
- **S** — Swan neck (PIP hyperextension + DIP flexion)
- **B** — Boutonnière (PIP flexion + DIP hyperextension)
- **U** — Ulnar deviation at MCPs
- **Z** — Z-thumb

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **RA spares DIP joints**. If DIP involved → think OA (Heberden's) or psoriatic arthritis
2. **Methotrexate** = first-line DMARD in RA. Side effects: hepatotoxicity, bone marrow suppression, pulmonary fibrosis. Always give with **folic acid**.
3. **Anti-CCP** = most specific antibody for RA
4. **Lupus nephritis** = most common cause of death in SLE. Class IV (diffuse proliferative) = worst prognosis
5. **Drug-induced lupus** = Hydralazine, Procainamide, Isoniazid. **Anti-histone antibody** positive. Spares kidneys and CNS.
6. **Gout risk factors**: Obesity, alcohol (beer), diuretics (thiazide), renal disease, tumor lysis syndrome
7. **Allopurinol hypersensitivity**: HLA-B*5801 screening recommended in Southeast Asians
8. **Pseudogout associated with**: Hyperparathyroidism, hemochromatosis, hypothyroidism (**"H's"**)
9. **Felty syndrome**: RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia → recurrent infections
10. **Jaccoud arthropathy**: Non-erosive deforming arthritis in SLE (reducible deformities)

## 📋 Monoarthritis — Differential Approach

| Acute | Chronic |
|---|---|
| Septic arthritis (EMERGENCY) | OA |
| Gout | RA (if monoarticular onset) |
| Pseudogout | TB arthritis |
| Trauma/hemarthrosis | Tumor |

> [!warning] **Septic arthritis is a joint emergency!**
> Hot, swollen, painful joint + fever = septic until proven otherwise. Aspirate FIRST (before antibiotics). Turbid fluid, WBC >50,000, >75% neutrophils. Most common: **S. aureus** (adults), **N. gonorrhoeae** (young sexually active).

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A 35-year-old woman presents with symmetric swelling of MCPs and PIPs, morning stiffness >1 hour, and RF positive. CRP elevated. What is the first-line DMARD?

> **Methotrexate** — start within 3 months of diagnosis with folic acid supplementation.

### Q2
A 50-year-old man with sudden onset excruciating pain in the big toe. Joint aspiration shows needle-shaped negatively birefringent crystals. Management?

> **Acute gout** — Colchicine or Indomethacin or Prednisolone. Do NOT start allopurinol during acute attack.

### Q3
ANA positive, anti-dsDNA positive, low C3/C4, proteinuria 3.5g/day in a 25-year-old female. Most likely renal pathology?

> **Lupus nephritis** (likely Class IV — diffuse proliferative GN). Needs renal biopsy. Treatment: IV cyclophosphamide or mycophenolate + steroids.

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **Psoriatic Arthritis Patterns**
> 5 patterns: DIP predominant, asymmetric oligoarthritis, symmetric polyarthritis (RA-like), spondylitis, arthritis mutilans. "Pencil-in-cup" deformity on XR. **Dactylitis** (sausage digit) is characteristic.
