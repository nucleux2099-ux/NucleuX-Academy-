## 🧠 Mnemonics

### Obstructive vs Restrictive PFT
- **Obstructive**: FEV1/FVC **↓** (<0.70), FEV1 ↓↓, FVC normal/↓
- **Restrictive**: FEV1/FVC **normal/↑**, both FEV1 and FVC ↓↓, TLC ↓

### Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency: **PANEL**
- **P** — Panacinar emphysema
- **A** — Autosomal codominant (PiZZ)
- **N** — Non-smoker with COPD (young!)
- **E** — Elevated liver enzymes (PAS+ globules)
- **L** — Lower lobe predominance

### Asthma Life-Threatening Signs: **SCAM-BS**
Silent chest, Cyanosis, Altered consciousness, Measured PaCO₂ normal/↑, Bradycardia, SpO₂ <92%

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **FEV1/FVC <0.70 post-bronchodilator** = diagnostic of COPD (doesn't normalize)
2. **Reversibility** = ≥12% AND ≥200mL improvement in FEV1 = asthma
3. **Reid index >0.5** = chronic bronchitis (mucus gland/wall thickness ratio)
4. **Pink puffer** = emphysema; **Blue bloater** = chronic bronchitis
5. **Only ICS contraindicated as monotherapy in COPD** (unlike asthma). Always combine with LABA.
6. **Tiotropium (LAMA)** = most effective single bronchodilator in COPD
7. **Theophylline** — narrow therapeutic index, toxicity: arrhythmias, seizures
8. **Aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease (AERD)** = Samter's triad: Asthma + Nasal polyps + Aspirin sensitivity
9. **Exercise-induced asthma** — Prophylaxis: inhaled SABA or LABA before exercise
10. **ABPA (Allergic Bronchopulmonary Aspergillosis)** — Asthma + proximal bronchiectasis + ↑IgE + eosinophilia

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A 30-year-old non-smoker presents with progressive dyspnea. CXR shows hyperinflation with lower lobe predominance. PFT shows irreversible obstruction. Diagnosis?

> **Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency** (panacinar emphysema, lower lobes, young non-smoker). Check serum A1AT level.

### Q2
What is the target SpO₂ in acute COPD exacerbation?

> **88-92%** — Controlled oxygen via Venturi mask (24-28%). Avoid high-flow O₂.

### Q3
A normal PaCO₂ in acute severe asthma is a danger sign. Why?

> In acute asthma, hyperventilation should cause **respiratory alkalosis** (low PaCO₂). A "normal" PaCO₂ indicates **respiratory muscle fatigue** and impending respiratory failure.

### Q4
An asthmatic patient on Step 3 therapy still has poor control. What is the next step?

> **Step up to Step 4**: Medium-dose ICS-formoterol maintenance + as-needed ICS-formoterol.

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **Inhaler Technique**
> Poor inhaler technique is the #1 cause of uncontrolled asthma. Always check technique before escalating therapy.

> [!warning] **COPD + ICS Risk**
> Long-term ICS in COPD increases risk of **pneumonia** (especially fluticasone). Use only when indicated (eosinophils ≥300, frequent exacerbations).
