## Overview

Lung cancer is the **leading cause of cancer-related death** worldwide. It is broadly classified into **small cell lung cancer (SCLC)** and **non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)**, with smoking being the predominant risk factor. Early-stage disease is potentially curable with surgery, but most patients present at advanced stages.

## Key Points

- **Smoking** causes ~85% of lung cancers
- **NSCLC** accounts for ~85% (adenocarcinoma > squamous > large cell)
- **SCLC** accounts for ~15% (central, aggressive, paraneoplastic syndromes)
- **Adenocarcinoma** is the most common type overall and in non-smokers
- **Squamous cell** is most associated with hypercalcemia (PTHrP) and cavitation
- **SCLC** — SIADH, Cushing syndrome (ectopic ACTH), Lambert-Eaton
- Staging: NSCLC by **TNM**; SCLC by **limited vs extensive**
- Treatment: Surgery (early NSCLC), chemoradiation, targeted therapy, immunotherapy

## Definition & Classification

### Histological Types

| Type | % | Location | Associations |
|------|---|----------|-------------|
| **Adenocarcinoma** | 40% | Peripheral | Non-smokers, women, scars, EGFR/ALK mutations |
| **Squamous cell** | 25-30% | Central | Smokers, cavitation, hypercalcemia (PTHrP) |
| **Large cell** | 10% | Peripheral | Poorly differentiated |
| **Small cell (SCLC)** | 15% | Central (hilar mass) | Smokers, paraneoplastic, early metastasis |

### Paraneoplastic Syndromes

| Syndrome | Type | Mechanism |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **SIADH** | SCLC | Ectopic ADH |
| **Cushing syndrome** | SCLC | Ectopic ACTH |
| **Hypercalcemia** | Squamous | PTHrP |
| **Lambert-Eaton** | SCLC | Antibodies against presynaptic Ca²⁺ channels |
| **Hypertrophic pulmonary osteoarthropathy** | All (esp. adenocarcinoma) | Clubbing + periostitis |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors
- **Cigarette smoking** (~85%; risk proportional to pack-years)
- Passive smoking (1.2-1.5x risk)
- **Asbestos** (synergistic with smoking — multiplicative risk)
- Radon gas
- Occupational: Arsenic, chromium, nickel
- Radiation (post-radiation therapy)
- **Pre-existing lung disease:** IPF, COPD
- Genetic: Family history, EGFR, ALK, ROS1 mutations

### Molecular Drivers (NSCLC)

| Mutation | Frequency | Targeted Therapy |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **EGFR** | 15-30% (higher in Asians) | Osimertinib, Gefitinib, Erlotinib |
| **ALK rearrangement** | 5% | Crizotinib, Alectinib |
| **ROS1** | 1-2% | Crizotinib |
| **BRAF V600E** | 2% | Dabrafenib + Trametinib |
| **KRAS G12C** | 25% | Sotorasib |
| **PD-L1 expression** | Variable | Pembrolizumab, Nivolumab |

## Clinical Features

### Local Symptoms
- **Cough** (most common, often persistent change in chronic cough)
- **Hemoptysis**
- Dyspnea, wheeze (endobronchial obstruction)
- Chest pain (pleural invasion)
- Recurrent pneumonia (post-obstructive)

### Local Invasion
- **Hoarseness** — recurrent laryngeal nerve (left, from aortopulmonary window tumor)
- **SVC syndrome** — facial swelling, dilated neck veins, plethora (esp. SCLC)
- **Pancoast tumor** (superior sulcus) — shoulder pain, Horner syndrome (ptosis, miosis, anhidrosis), T1 root pain
- **Phrenic nerve palsy** — elevated hemidiaphragm
- **Pericardial effusion** — from direct invasion

### Metastatic
- **Brain** (seizures, focal deficits), **Bone** (pain, fractures), **Liver** (hepatomegaly), **Adrenal** (usually asymptomatic)

### Constitutional
- Weight loss, anorexia, fatigue, cachexia

## Diagnosis

### Imaging
- **Chest X-ray:** Solitary pulmonary nodule, hilar mass, pleural effusion, mediastinal widening
- **CT chest with contrast:** Staging, lymph node assessment
- **PET-CT:** Staging, detect distant metastases (NSCLC)
- **MRI brain:** If neurological symptoms or SCLC staging

### Tissue Diagnosis (Essential)
- **CT-guided biopsy** (peripheral lesions)
- **Bronchoscopy + biopsy** (central lesions)
- **EBUS-TBNA** (mediastinal lymph nodes)
- **Thoracentesis** (malignant effusion)
- **Mediastinoscopy** (staging)

### Molecular Testing (NSCLC)
- EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF, KRAS, PD-L1 — guides targeted therapy

### Staging
- **NSCLC:** TNM (Stage I-IV)
- **SCLC:** Limited (confined to one hemithorax + regional nodes) vs Extensive (beyond)

## Management

### NSCLC
| Stage | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **I-II** | Surgical resection (lobectomy) ± adjuvant chemotherapy |
| **III (unresectable)** | Concurrent chemoradiation + Durvalumab (immunotherapy) |
| **IV (metastatic)** | Systemic therapy: Targeted (if driver mutation) / Immunotherapy (PD-L1 >50%) / Chemotherapy |

### SCLC
| Stage | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **Limited** | Chemoradiation (cisplatin + etoposide) + prophylactic cranial irradiation (PCI) |
| **Extensive** | Chemotherapy (platinum + etoposide) + Atezolizumab (immunotherapy) |

### Supportive/Palliative
- Pain management
- Airway stenting (endobronchial obstruction)
- Pleural drainage (malignant effusion)
- SVC stenting (SVC syndrome)
- Bone-directed therapy (bisphosphonates/denosumab)

## Complications & Prognosis

- **SVC syndrome, spinal cord compression, brain metastases** — oncological emergencies
- **Malignant pleural effusion**
- **Paraneoplastic syndromes**
- **Treatment toxicity** (chemotherapy, radiation pneumonitis)

### 5-Year Survival

| Stage | NSCLC | SCLC |
|-------|-------|------|
| I | 70-90% | — |
| II | 50-60% | — |
| III | 15-35% | — |
| IV | <5% | — |
| Limited | — | 20-25% |
| Extensive | — | <5% |

> **Clinical Pearl:** Any smoker >40 years with persistent cough change, hemoptysis, or non-resolving pneumonia warrants urgent chest X-ray and consideration for CT/bronchoscopy to rule out lung cancer.
