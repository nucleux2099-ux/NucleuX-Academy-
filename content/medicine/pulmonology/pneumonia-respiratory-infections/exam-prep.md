## 🧠 Mnemonics

### CURB-65: Already a mnemonic!
Confusion, Urea, Respiratory rate, Blood pressure, 65 years

### Causes of Cavitary Pneumonia: **CAVITY**
- **C** — Cancer (squamous cell)
- **A** — Autoimmune (Wegener's/GPA)
- **V** — Vascular (septic emboli)
- **I** — Infection (TB, Klebsiella, Staph, anaerobes, fungal)
- **T** — Trauma
- **Y** — Youth (congenital — sequestration)

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **Most common cause of CAP** worldwide = **S. pneumoniae**
2. **Most common cause of atypical pneumonia** = **Mycoplasma pneumoniae**
3. **Rusty sputum** = Pneumococcal; **Currant jelly** = Klebsiella; **Foul-smelling** = Anaerobes
4. **Legionella** — hyponatremia + diarrhea + pneumonia in AC-exposed patient. Urine antigen test.
5. **PCP pneumonia** — HIV + CD4 <200 + bilateral ground-glass + ↑LDH. Treat: TMP-SMX. Prophylaxis when CD4 <200.
6. **Cold agglutinins** = Mycoplasma (IgM anti-I antibody)
7. **Empyema** = pH <7.2, glucose <40, LDH >1000, pus on aspiration → chest tube
8. **Lung abscess** — foul sputum, air-fluid level on CXR. Most common cause = aspiration (anaerobes). Treat: Clindamycin/Amoxicillin-clavulanate
9. **Post-influenza pneumonia** → Think **S. aureus** (pneumatoceles on CXR)
10. **Pneumococcal vaccine** — PCV13 + PPSV23 for elderly and immunocompromised

## 📋 Typical vs Atypical Pneumonia

| Feature | Typical | Atypical |
|---|---|---|
| Onset | Acute, high fever, rigors | Gradual, low-grade fever |
| Cough | Productive, purulent | Dry, non-productive |
| CXR | Lobar consolidation | Interstitial/patchy |
| WBC | Leukocytosis, neutrophilia | Normal or mild elevation |
| Organisms | Pneumococcus, H. influenzae | Mycoplasma, Chlamydia, Legionella |
| Treatment | β-lactams | Macrolides/Doxycycline |

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A 25-year-old college student has dry cough, low-grade fever, headache, and bilateral patchy infiltrates on CXR. Cold agglutinin test is positive. Diagnosis?

> **Mycoplasma pneumonia**. Treat with Azithromycin or Doxycycline.

### Q2
An alcoholic presents with high fever, productive cough with thick bloody sputum, and CXR showing right upper lobe consolidation with cavitation. Organism?

> **Klebsiella pneumoniae** — "currant jelly" sputum, upper lobe predilection, cavitation.

### Q3
CURB-65 score of 3 in a 70-year-old with CAP. Management?

> **ICU admission**. Start IV antibiotics: Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin. Blood cultures, sputum culture before antibiotics.

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **Aspiration Pneumonia**
> Right lower lobe (if upright) or posterior segment of right upper lobe (if supine) — due to the straighter, wider right main bronchus.

> [!warning] **Non-resolving Pneumonia**
> If no improvement in 72h → consider resistant organism, empyema, lung abscess, or alternative diagnosis (TB, malignancy, PE).
