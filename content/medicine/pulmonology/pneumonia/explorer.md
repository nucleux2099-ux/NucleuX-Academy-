## Overview

Pneumonia is an **acute infection of the lung parenchyma** causing inflammation and consolidation of the alveoli. It is classified by setting of acquisition — **community-acquired (CAP)**, **hospital-acquired (HAP)**, and **ventilator-associated (VAP)**. Pneumonia is a leading cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide, especially in children and the elderly.

## Key Points

- **S. pneumoniae** is the most common cause of CAP worldwide
- **CURB-65** score guides severity assessment and site of care
- **Chest X-ray consolidation** is the radiological hallmark
- HAP (>48 hrs after admission) — gram-negatives and MRSA are common
- **Atypical pneumonia:** Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydophila — dry cough, extrapulmonary features
- Empirical antibiotics should be started within 1 hour of diagnosis
- CAP: Amoxicillin ± macrolide; severe: Co-amoxiclav + macrolide or respiratory fluoroquinolone
- **Vaccination** (pneumococcal, influenza) is key prevention

## Definition & Classification

| Type | Definition | Common Organisms |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **CAP** | Acquired outside hospital | S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, Mycoplasma, Legionella |
| **HAP** | ≥48 hrs after hospital admission | Pseudomonas, Klebsiella, S. aureus (MRSA), Acinetobacter |
| **VAP** | ≥48 hrs after intubation | Pseudomonas, Acinetobacter, MRSA |
| **Aspiration** | Altered consciousness, poor swallowing | Anaerobes + mixed oral flora |

### Typical vs Atypical Pneumonia

| Feature | Typical | Atypical |
|---------|---------|----------|
| Onset | Acute, high fever, rigors | Gradual, low-grade fever |
| Cough | Productive, purulent/rusty | Dry, non-productive |
| Organism | S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae | Mycoplasma, Legionella, Chlamydophila |
| X-ray | Lobar consolidation | Diffuse/patchy infiltrates |
| WBC | Neutrophilic leukocytosis | Normal or mildly elevated |
| Extrapulmonary | Uncommon | Common (rash, diarrhea, hepatitis) |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Common Organisms by Setting

| Setting | Organisms |
|---------|----------|
| **Previously healthy** | S. pneumoniae, Mycoplasma |
| **Elderly/comorbid** | S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, Gram-negatives |
| **Alcoholism** | Klebsiella, S. pneumoniae, anaerobes |
| **COPD** | H. influenzae, Moraxella, S. pneumoniae |
| **Post-influenza** | S. aureus, S. pneumoniae |
| **Immunocompromised** | PCP, CMV, Aspergillus, TB |
| **Travel/AC exposure** | Legionella |

### Organism-Specific Clues

| Organism | Clue |
|----------|------|
| **S. pneumoniae** | Rusty sputum, lobar consolidation |
| **Klebsiella** | Currant jelly sputum, upper lobe, bulging fissure, alcoholics |
| **Mycoplasma** | Young adults, dry cough, cold agglutinins, erythema multiforme |
| **Legionella** | Hyponatremia, diarrhea, confusion, AC/water exposure |
| **S. aureus** | Post-influenza, cavitation, pneumatoceles |
| **PCP** | HIV (CD4 <200), bilateral ground-glass infiltrates |

### Pathophysiology
1. Organisms reach alveoli (inhalation, aspiration, hematogenous)
2. Alveolar macrophage response → inflammatory mediator release
3. **Congestion** → **Red hepatization** (RBC-filled alveoli) → **Grey hepatization** (fibrin + neutrophils) → **Resolution**
4. Consolidation: Alveoli filled with exudate → bronchial breath sounds, dullness, increased vocal resonance

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Fever** with chills/rigors (acute onset in typical)
- **Cough** — productive (purulent/rusty) or dry (atypical)
- **Dyspnea**
- **Pleuritic chest pain** (sharp, worse on inspiration)
- Malaise, myalgia

### Signs
- Fever, tachypnea, tachycardia
- **Signs of consolidation:**
  - Decreased expansion on affected side
  - Dull percussion
  - **Bronchial breath sounds**
  - **Increased vocal resonance/fremitus**
  - **Whispering pectoriloquy**
  - **Crackles** (coarse, inspiratory)
- Cyanosis (severe)

## Diagnosis

### CURB-65 Severity Score

| Criterion | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| **C**onfusion (AMT ≤8) | 1 |
| **U**rea > 7 mmol/L | 1 |
| **R**espiratory rate ≥ 30 | 1 |
| **B**P: SBP <90 or DBP ≤60 | 1 |
| Age **≥65** | 1 |

| Score | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 0-1 | Home treatment |
| 2 | Consider hospital admission |
| 3-5 | Hospital; ≥4 consider ICU |

### Investigations
- **Chest X-ray:** Lobar consolidation, air bronchograms (typical); patchy/diffuse infiltrates (atypical)
- **Blood:** CBC, CRP, procalcitonin, urea, blood cultures (before antibiotics)
- **Sputum:** Gram stain + culture (before antibiotics)
- **Urine antigen:** Legionella, Pneumococcal (rapid)
- **ABG:** If severe (assess respiratory failure)
- **CT chest:** If complications suspected or non-resolving
- **Atypical serology:** Mycoplasma IgM, Legionella urine antigen

## Management

### Empirical Antibiotic Therapy (CAP)

| Severity | Setting | Regimen |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Mild (CURB 0-1)** | Outpatient | Amoxicillin 500mg TDS × 5 days |
| **Moderate (CURB 2)** | Hospital | Amoxicillin-clavulanate + Macrolide (azithromycin) |
| **Severe (CURB ≥3)** | ICU | IV Co-amoxiclav + IV Macrolide; OR IV Ceftriaxone + Macrolide |
| **Atypical suspected** | Any | Macrolide (azithromycin) or Doxycycline |

### HAP/VAP
- **Anti-pseudomonal coverage:** Piperacillin-tazobactam or Meropenem
- **+ MRSA coverage** if risk factors: Vancomycin or Linezolid

### Supportive
- Oxygen (target SpO₂ ≥94%; 88-92% in COPD)
- IV fluids if dehydrated
- Antipyretics
- VTE prophylaxis (hospitalized patients)

### Follow-up
- Chest X-ray at **6 weeks** to confirm resolution (especially >50 yrs, smokers — exclude malignancy)

## Complications & Prognosis

- **Parapneumonic effusion / Empyema** (most common complication)
- **Lung abscess** (necrotizing pneumonia)
- **Sepsis / Septic shock**
- **ARDS**
- **Respiratory failure**

### Prognosis
- Overall CAP mortality: ~5-15% (hospitalized)
- ICU CAP mortality: 25-50%
- **Risk factors for poor outcome:** Advanced age, comorbidities, multilobar involvement, bacteremia, high CURB-65

> **Clinical Pearl:** Always get a follow-up chest X-ray at 6 weeks in patients >50 years or smokers. A non-resolving pneumonia may be hiding a lung cancer.
