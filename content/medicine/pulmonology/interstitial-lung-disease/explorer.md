## Overview

Interstitial lung diseases (ILDs) are a heterogeneous group of disorders characterized by **inflammation and/or fibrosis of the lung interstitium** (alveolar walls, perivascular, and perilymphatic tissue). They cause **restrictive lung disease** with impaired gas exchange, presenting as progressive dyspnea and dry cough.

## Key Points

- **Restrictive pattern** on spirometry: Reduced FVC and TLC with normal/elevated FEV1/FVC ratio
- **Reduced DLCO** (diffusion capacity) is a hallmark — earliest PFT abnormality
- **HRCT** is the key imaging investigation (characteristic patterns)
- **IPF (Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis)** is the most common idiopathic ILD — worst prognosis
- IPF HRCT pattern: **UIP (Usual Interstitial Pneumonia)** — basal, subpleural, honeycombing
- Treatment: Cause-specific; **Pirfenidone/Nintedanib** for IPF (slow progression)
- **Sarcoidosis:** Non-caseating granulomas, bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy, elevated ACE
- ILD can be secondary to drugs, CTDs, occupational exposures, or radiation

## Definition & Classification

### Major Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Idiopathic Interstitial Pneumonias** | IPF (UIP), NSIP, COP, AIP, DIP, RB-ILD, LIP |
| **Granulomatous** | Sarcoidosis, Hypersensitivity pneumonitis |
| **CTD-associated** | RA, Scleroderma, SLE, Dermatomyositis |
| **Occupational** | Asbestosis, Silicosis, Coal workers' pneumoconiosis |
| **Drug-induced** | Methotrexate, Amiodarone, Bleomycin, Nitrofurantoin |
| **Other** | Radiation, Eosinophilic pneumonia, LAM, Pulmonary alveolar proteinosis |

### Idiopathic Interstitial Pneumonias

| Type | Pattern | Prognosis | Key Feature |
|------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **IPF** | UIP | Worst (3-5 yr) | Honeycombing, basal predominant |
| **NSIP** | NSIP | Better | Ground glass, responds to steroids |
| **COP** | OP | Good | Migratory consolidations |
| **AIP** | DAD | Very poor | Acute, like ARDS |
| **DIP** | DIP | Good | Smokers, ground glass |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Pathogenesis (IPF as prototype)
1. Repetitive epithelial injury (genetic susceptibility + environmental triggers)
2. Aberrant wound healing response
3. Fibroblast proliferation → myofibroblast transformation
4. Excessive collagen deposition → architectural distortion
5. **Honeycombing** — end-stage fibrotic lung

### Occupational ILDs

| Disease | Exposure | Pathology |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **Asbestosis** | Asbestos | Basal fibrosis, ferruginous bodies, pleural plaques |
| **Silicosis** | Silica | Upper lobe nodules, eggshell calcification of hilar LN |
| **Coal workers'** | Coal dust | Simple (nodules) → complicated (PMF) |
| **Berylliosis** | Beryllium | Non-caseating granulomas (mimics sarcoidosis) |

### Sarcoidosis
- **Non-caseating granulomas** in multiple organs (lung, skin, eyes, liver)
- **Bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy** (90%) ± pulmonary infiltrates
- **Elevated serum ACE**, hypercalcemia (1,25-dihydroxy vitamin D production by granulomas)
- **Löfgren syndrome:** Erythema nodosum + bilateral hilar LAD + polyarthralgia + fever — excellent prognosis

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Progressive exertional dyspnea** (most common)
- **Dry cough** (non-productive)
- Fatigue, weight loss
- Symptoms of underlying cause (joint pain, skin rash in CTD)

### Signs
- **Bilateral fine end-inspiratory ("Velcro") crackles** — hallmark
- **Clubbing** (especially IPF — 50%)
- Cyanosis (advanced)
- Cor pulmonale (late) — elevated JVP, pedal edema
- **Extrapulmonary signs:** Skin (sarcoidosis, CTD), joints (RA), eyes (sarcoidosis)

## Diagnosis

### Pulmonary Function Tests
- **Restrictive pattern:** ↓ FVC, ↓ TLC, normal or ↑ FEV1/FVC ratio
- **Reduced DLCO** — earliest abnormality, most sensitive
- **Exercise desaturation** (6MWT with oximetry)

### HRCT (Key Investigation)

| Pattern | Disease | Distribution |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **UIP** (honeycombing, traction bronchiectasis, minimal GGO) | IPF | Basal, subpleural |
| **NSIP** (ground glass ± fine reticular) | NSIP | Lower zones, subpleural sparing |
| **OP** (patchy consolidations) | COP | Peripheral, migratory |
| **Bilateral hilar LAD ± nodules** | Sarcoidosis | Perilymphatic |
| **Centrilobular nodules, GGO, mosaic** | HP | Upper/mid zones |

### Other Investigations
- **Blood:** ANA, RF, anti-CCP (CTD screen), ACE (sarcoidosis), anti-Jo1 (dermatomyositis)
- **BAL:** Lymphocytosis (sarcoidosis, HP), neutrophilia (IPF), eosinophilia (eosinophilic)
- **Biopsy:** Surgical lung biopsy for uncertain cases; transbronchial biopsy for sarcoidosis
- **Serum KL-6, SP-D:** Biomarkers (research)

### Multidisciplinary Discussion (MDD)
- **Clinical + Radiological + Pathological** correlation is the gold standard for ILD diagnosis
- Essential for IPF diagnosis

## Management

### IPF
- **Pirfenidone** — antifibrotic (reduces FVC decline)
- **Nintedanib** — tyrosine kinase inhibitor (reduces FVC decline)
- **NOT steroids** (no benefit; may be harmful in IPF)
- **Lung transplant** — only curative option
- Supportive: O₂, pulmonary rehab, palliation

### Sarcoidosis
- **Stage I (hilar LAD only):** Observation (60-80% remit spontaneously)
- **Stage II-IV with symptoms/decline:** **Corticosteroids** (prednisolone 0.5mg/kg × 6-12 months)
- Steroid-sparing: Methotrexate, Azathioprine
- **Extrapulmonary:** Steroids for cardiac, neuro, hypercalcemia

### Other ILDs
- **COP:** Steroids (excellent response)
- **NSIP:** Steroids ± immunosuppression
- **CTD-ILD:** Treat CTD + Nintedanib/Mycophenolate
- **HP:** Antigen avoidance + steroids if chronic
- **Drug-induced:** Stop offending drug ± steroids

## Complications & Prognosis

- **Pulmonary hypertension** (Group 3) — worsens prognosis
- **Respiratory failure**
- **Lung cancer** (especially in IPF and asbestosis)
- **Pneumothorax**
- **Cor pulmonale**

### Prognosis by Type
- **IPF:** Median survival 3-5 years (worst among IIPs)
- **NSIP:** 5-year survival >80%
- **Sarcoidosis:** Most remit; <5% develop progressive fibrosis
- **COP:** Excellent with steroids (>80% resolution)

> **Clinical Pearl:** In IPF, the HRCT pattern of UIP (honeycombing + basal predominance) is so specific that surgical lung biopsy can be avoided if the clinical-radiological pattern is definite.
