## Overview

Bronchiectasis is a chronic airway disease characterized by **permanent, abnormal dilatation of bronchi** due to destruction of the muscular and elastic components of the bronchial wall. It results from a **vicious cycle** of infection, inflammation, and airway damage, presenting with chronic productive cough and recurrent respiratory infections.

## Key Points

- **Irreversible bronchial dilatation** — defined radiologically on HRCT
- **Chronic productive cough with copious purulent sputum** is the hallmark
- **Post-infectious** (TB, childhood pneumonia) is the most common cause in developing countries
- **Cystic fibrosis** is the most common cause in developed countries
- **HRCT** is the gold standard for diagnosis (signet ring sign, tram-track sign)
- Management: **Airway clearance + antibiotics + treat underlying cause**
- **H. influenzae** and **Pseudomonas aeruginosa** are the most common colonizing organisms
- Massive hemoptysis may require **bronchial artery embolization**

## Definition & Classification

### Morphological Types (Reid Classification)

| Type | Description | HRCT Appearance |
|------|------------|-----------------|
| **Cylindrical** | Uniform dilatation | Tram-track sign, signet ring sign |
| **Varicose** | Irregular beaded dilatation | Beaded bronchi |
| **Cystic (Saccular)** | Cluster of cysts | Cluster of cysts, often with air-fluid levels |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Causes

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Post-infectious** | TB, childhood pneumonia, measles, pertussis, allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis (ABPA) |
| **Genetic** | Cystic fibrosis, primary ciliary dyskinesia (Kartagener syndrome), α1-antitrypsin deficiency |
| **Immune deficiency** | Hypogammaglobulinemia, HIV, CVID |
| **Aspiration** | Foreign body, GERD |
| **Obstruction** | Tumor, lymph node, foreign body |
| **Autoimmune** | RA, Sjögren, IBD |
| **Allergic** | ABPA (proximal bronchiectasis) |
| **Idiopathic** | ~50% remain unexplained |

### Vicious Cycle Hypothesis (Cole)
1. Initial insult (infection, obstruction) → mucosal damage
2. Impaired mucociliary clearance → mucus stasis
3. Bacterial colonization → chronic infection
4. Neutrophilic inflammation → protease release → further wall destruction
5. **Progressive bronchial dilatation** → more stasis → cycle repeats

> **Clinical Pearl:** ABPA causes **proximal (central) bronchiectasis** — unique pattern. All others cause distal bronchiectasis. If you see central bronchiectasis on HRCT, think ABPA.

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- **Chronic cough with copious mucopurulent sputum** (often postural — worse in morning)
- **Recurrent respiratory infections**
- Hemoptysis (may be massive — bronchial artery hypertrophy)
- Dyspnea (progressive, with advanced disease)
- Pleuritic chest pain

### Signs
- **Coarse crackles** (persistent, often bilateral, lower zones)
- Wheeze
- Clubbing (in severe/chronic cases)
- Signs of underlying cause (dextrocardia in Kartagener)
- In advanced disease: Cor pulmonale signs

## Diagnosis

### HRCT (Gold Standard)
- **Signet ring sign:** Dilated bronchus with adjacent pulmonary artery (bronchus > artery)
- **Tram-track sign:** Thickened, dilated bronchi seen longitudinally
- **Lack of tapering:** Bronchi visible in peripheral lung
- Air-fluid levels in cystic bronchiectasis
- Tree-in-bud pattern (mucoid impaction)

### Sputum
- Culture and sensitivity (guide antibiotic therapy)
- **Common organisms:** H. influenzae (most common), Pseudomonas aeruginosa (associated with worse prognosis), S. pneumoniae, M. catarrhalis
- AFB (rule out TB)
- Fungal culture (ABPA)

### Additional Investigations
- **Chest X-ray:** Tram tracks, ring shadows, cystic spaces (less sensitive than HRCT)
- **PFTs:** Obstructive pattern (may be mixed)
- **Immunoglobulins:** IgG, IgA, IgM (immune deficiency)
- **Aspergillus IgE, skin prick test, total IgE:** ABPA screen
- **Sweat chloride test:** Cystic fibrosis
- **Nasal NO, electron microscopy of cilia:** Primary ciliary dyskinesia
- **α1-antitrypsin level**

## Management

### Airway Clearance (Cornerstone)
- **Chest physiotherapy** — postural drainage, percussion
- **Active cycle of breathing techniques**
- Oscillatory PEP devices (Flutter, Acapella)
- Nebulized hypertonic saline (7%) — improves mucociliary clearance

### Antibiotic Therapy
- **Acute exacerbations:** 14 days oral antibiotics (amoxicillin-clavulanate, doxycycline; ciprofloxacin if Pseudomonas)
- **Chronic Pseudomonas colonization:** Nebulized tobramycin/colistin (long-term suppressive)
- **Eradication therapy:** For new Pseudomonas isolation

### Other Medical
- **Bronchodilators** — if reversible component on PFTs
- **Mucolytics** (nebulized DNase — ONLY in CF; avoid in non-CF bronchiectasis)
- **Macrolides** (azithromycin 3×/week) — anti-inflammatory, reduces exacerbations
- **Treat underlying cause:** ABPA (steroids + itraconazole), immune deficiency (IVIG)

### Surgical
- **Lobectomy** — localized disease with recurrent infections/hemoptysis
- **Bronchial artery embolization** — for massive hemoptysis
- **Lung transplant** — end-stage bilateral disease (especially CF)

## Complications & Prognosis

- **Massive hemoptysis** (bronchial artery erosion — medical emergency)
- **Recurrent pneumonia**
- **Lung abscess**
- **Cor pulmonale** (chronic hypoxia → pulmonary hypertension)
- **Amyloidosis** (chronic inflammation — AA type)
- **Respiratory failure**

### Prognosis
- Variable — depends on underlying cause and extent
- CF bronchiectasis: Median survival ~50 years with modern care
- Non-CF: Generally better prognosis; Pseudomonas colonization worsens outcomes
- **Pseudomonas colonization** is the strongest independent predictor of mortality

> **Clinical Pearl:** In a patient with recurrent sinusitis + bronchiectasis + situs inversus (dextrocardia), think **Kartagener syndrome** (primary ciliary dyskinesia).
