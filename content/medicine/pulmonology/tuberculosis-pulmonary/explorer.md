# Tuberculosis (Pulmonary) — Explorer

## Overview

**Tuberculosis (TB)** is a chronic granulomatous infection caused by **Mycobacterium tuberculosis**, an acid-fast bacillus. India bears the highest TB burden globally (~27% of world's cases). TB is the #1 infectious cause of death worldwide and a perennial high-yield topic for NEET-PG.

📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 179

## Key Points

- **M. tuberculosis** — obligate aerobe, slow-growing (doubling time 15-20 hrs), acid-fast (Ziehl-Neelsen stain)
- **Primary TB:** Ghon focus (subpleural mid/lower zone) + hilar lymphadenopathy = **Ghon complex** → usually self-limited
- **Post-primary (reactivation) TB:** Upper lobes (apical/posterior segments) — high oxygen tension favors growth
- **Diagnosis:** Sputum AFB smear + culture (gold standard) + CBNAAT/GeneXpert (rapid, detects rifampicin resistance)
- **NTEP (India) regimen:** 2HRZE/4HR (DOTS) — Isoniazid, Rifampicin, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol
- **MDR-TB:** Resistant to at least INH + Rifampicin → bedaquiline-based shorter regimen
- **Latent TB:** Tuberculin skin test (Mantoux) ≥10mm in most; treat with INH 6-9 months
- **Miliary TB:** Hematogenous dissemination → diffuse miliary nodules on CXR

## Pathophysiology

### Primary Infection
1. Inhalation of droplet nuclei (1-5 μm) → reach alveoli
2. Engulfed by alveolar macrophages → survive intracellularly
3. **Ghon focus** forms (granuloma with caseous necrosis)
4. Spread to hilar lymph nodes → **Ghon complex**
5. Cell-mediated immunity (CMI) develops in 2-8 weeks → tuberculin conversion
6. **95% contained** → latent TB; **5% progress** → primary progressive TB

### Reactivation
- Immunosuppression (HIV, steroids, malnutrition, diabetes) → breakdown of containment
- Preferentially affects lung **apices** (high PaO2, low lymphatic drainage)
- Cavitation → high bacillary load → highly infectious

### Immune Response
- **Type IV hypersensitivity** (delayed-type) — hallmark; basis of Mantoux test
- **Epithelioid granuloma with Langhans giant cells and caseous necrosis** — pathological hallmark
- **TNF-α** — critical for granuloma maintenance (anti-TNF therapy → TB reactivation)

## Clinical Features

### Pulmonary TB
- **Chronic cough** (>2 weeks) — productive, may be hemoptysis
- **Evening rise of temperature** (low-grade)
- **Night sweats, weight loss, anorexia** (constitutional symptoms)
- **Hemoptysis** — erosion of vessel in cavity (Rasmussen's aneurysm → massive hemoptysis)

### CXR Patterns
| Type | Pattern |
|---|---|
| Primary TB | Hilar lymphadenopathy ± parenchymal lesion (mid/lower zone) |
| Post-primary | Upper lobe infiltrates, cavitation, fibrosis |
| Miliary TB | Diffuse bilateral 1-3mm nodules ("millet seed") |

### Extrapulmonary TB (NEET favorite)
| Site | Key Features |
|---|---|
| **Lymph node** | Most common EPTB; cervical (scrofula) — matted, cold abscess |
| **Pleural** | Exudative effusion; ADA >40; lymphocyte-predominant |
| **Spinal (Pott's)** | Lower thoracic; gibbus deformity; cold abscess; subligamentous spread |
| **Meningeal** | Basal meningitis; CN VI palsy; CSF: ↑protein, ↓glucose, lymphocytes |
| **GU** | "Sterile pyuria"; beaded ureter; thimble bladder |
| **Intestinal** | Ileocecal junction (transverse ulcers vs Crohn's longitudinal) |
| **Pericardial** | Constrictive pericarditis |

## Diagnosis

| Test | Details | Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|
| **Sputum AFB smear** (ZN stain) | 2 samples; need >5000 AFB/mL | 50-60% |
| **Culture** (LJ medium / BACTEC MGIT) | Gold standard; takes 2-8 weeks | 80-85% |
| **CBNAAT / GeneXpert MTB/RIF** | PCR-based; result in 2 hrs; detects rifampicin resistance | 98% (smear+), 67% (smear−) |
| **Mantoux test (TST)** | 0.1mL PPD intradermally; read at 48-72 hrs | Latent TB screening; ≥10mm positive |
| **IGRA (QuantiFERON)** | IFN-γ release; not affected by BCG | Better specificity than TST |
| **Histopathology** | Caseating granulomas | Definitive in EPTB |

> **Exam Pearl:** CBNAAT/GeneXpert is the **initial diagnostic test** recommended by WHO and NTEP for all presumptive TB cases.

## Management

### NTEP Regimen (India, Weight-based)

**Drug-sensitive TB:**
| Phase | Duration | Drugs | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Intensive** | 2 months | HRZE (INH + Rifampicin + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol) | Daily, fixed-dose combinations |
| **Continuation** | 4 months | HR (INH + Rifampicin) | Daily |

**Total: 6 months (2HRZE + 4HR)**

### Drug Doses & Key Side Effects

| Drug | Dose | Key Side Effect | Monitoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Isoniazid (H)** | 5 mg/kg (max 300mg) | Peripheral neuropathy (prevent with pyridoxine B6), hepatitis, SLE-like | LFT |
| **Rifampicin (R)** | 10 mg/kg (max 600mg) | Hepatitis, orange body fluids, P450 inducer (drug interactions!), flu-like syndrome | LFT |
| **Pyrazinamide (Z)** | 25 mg/kg | Hyperuricemia (gout), hepatotoxicity (MOST hepatotoxic ATT) | LFT, uric acid |
| **Ethambutol (E)** | 15 mg/kg | **Optic neuritis** (retrobulbar; red-green color blindness) | Visual acuity |
| **Streptomycin (S)** | 15 mg/kg | Ototoxicity, nephrotoxicity (VIII nerve) | Audiometry |

> **Exam Pearl:** **Pyrazinamide** is the most hepatotoxic ATT drug. **Rifampicin** is the most important drug (its loss = MDR-TB). **INH** is the most common cause of drug-induced peripheral neuropathy.

### MDR-TB (Resistant to INH + Rifampicin)
- **Shorter regimen (9-11 months):** Bedaquiline + Linezolid + Levofloxacin + Clofazimine + Cycloserine/Ethionamide
- **Longer regimen (18-20 months)** for complex cases
- **Bedaquiline** — ATP synthase inhibitor; QTc prolongation risk

### Latent TB Treatment
- **INH** × 9 months (or 6 months minimum)
- **3HP regimen:** INH + Rifapentine weekly × 12 weeks

### Special Situations
- **TB + HIV:** Start ATT first → ART within 2-8 weeks; watch for IRIS (immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome)
- **TB in pregnancy:** HRZE safe; avoid streptomycin (ototoxic to fetus)
- **Drug-induced hepatitis:** Stop HRZ; rechallenge sequentially (R → H → Z)

## Indian National Guidelines (NTEP)

- **Nikshay** — national TB notification portal (mandatory)
- **DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer)** — ₹500/month nutritional support
- **Universal DST** — CBNAAT for all presumptive TB
- **Contact tracing** mandatory
- **Treatment outcome categories:** Cured, treatment completed, failure, lost to follow-up, died, not evaluated

## Complications & Prognosis

- **Massive hemoptysis** (Rasmussen's aneurysm)
- **Aspergilloma** (fungus ball in TB cavity)
- **Bronchiectasis** (post-TB sequel)
- **Fibrothorax, calcification**
- **Amyloidosis** (chronic TB)
- Drug-sensitive TB: cure rate >85% with proper treatment
- MDR-TB: cure rate ~55-75% with newer regimens
