# Liver Cirrhosis

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 342-345, Sleisenger & Fordtran's GI and Liver Disease
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Cirrhosis is the end-stage of chronic liver disease characterized by diffuse hepatic fibrosis and replacement of normal liver architecture with regenerative nodules. It results from sustained hepatocyte injury triggering inflammation, hepatic stellate cell activation, and collagen deposition. Cirrhosis is classified as compensated (asymptomatic) or decompensated (ascites, variceal bleeding, encephalopathy, jaundice), with dramatically different prognoses.

## Key Points

- **Irreversible** (generally) but removing causative factor can prevent progression
- **Leading causes:** Alcohol, Hepatitis B/C, NAFLD/NASH
- **Decompensation events:** Ascites (most common), variceal bleed, HE, jaundice
- **Hepatocellular carcinoma:** Cirrhosis is the strongest risk factor
- **Liver transplantation:** Only definitive treatment for end-stage disease

## Clinical Features

### Compensated Cirrhosis
- Often asymptomatic
- Fatigue, weakness
- Anorexia, weight loss
- Hepatomegaly (early), then small shrunken liver

### Decompensated Cirrhosis

**Portal Hypertension Manifestations:**
| Feature | Mechanism |
|---------|-----------|
| Ascites | Portal HTN + low albumin + Na retention |
| Esophageal varices | Porto-systemic collaterals |
| Splenomegaly | Splenic vein congestion |
| Caput medusae | Paraumbilical vein dilatation |
| Hemorrhoids | Inferior rectal vein dilatation |

**Hepatocellular Failure:**
- Jaundice
- Coagulopathy (easy bruising, bleeding)
- Hypoalbuminemia (edema)
- Hepatic encephalopathy
- Fetor hepaticus (sweet, musty breath)

**Other Signs:**
- Spider angiomata (arterial)
- Palmar erythema
- Gynecomastia, testicular atrophy
- Dupuytren's contracture (alcoholic)
- Terry's nails (white nails)
- Asterixis (hepatic flap)

### Child-Pugh Classification

| Parameter | 1 Point | 2 Points | 3 Points |
|-----------|---------|----------|----------|
| Bilirubin (mg/dL) | <2 | 2-3 | >3 |
| Albumin (g/dL) | >3.5 | 2.8-3.5 | <2.8 |
| INR | <1.7 | 1.7-2.3 | >2.3 |
| Ascites | None | Mild | Moderate-severe |
| Encephalopathy | None | Grade 1-2 | Grade 3-4 |

- **Class A (5-6):** Compensated, 1-year survival ~100%
- **Class B (7-9):** Significant impairment, 1-year survival ~80%
- **Class C (10-15):** Decompensated, 1-year survival ~45%

### MELD Score
- **Formula:** 3.78×ln(bilirubin) + 11.2×ln(INR) + 9.57×ln(creatinine) + 6.43
- Used for transplant prioritization
- MELD-Na includes sodium (better predictor)

## Diagnosis

### Laboratory
- **LFTs:** AST/ALT often modestly elevated, AST:ALT >2 suggests alcohol
- **Synthetic function:** Low albumin, prolonged PT/INR
- **Bilirubin:** Elevated in decompensation
- **CBC:** Thrombocytopenia (hypersplenism), anemia
- **Electrolytes:** Hyponatremia (poor prognosis)

### Etiology Workup
| Cause | Tests |
|-------|-------|
| Viral | HBsAg, Anti-HCV, HBV DNA, HCV RNA |
| Alcohol | History, AST:ALT >2, GGT elevated |
| NAFLD/NASH | Metabolic syndrome, exclude other causes |
| Autoimmune hepatitis | ANA, ASMA, anti-LKM1, elevated IgG |
| PBC | AMA, elevated ALP/GGT |
| PSC | p-ANCA, MRCP (beaded ducts) |
| Hemochromatosis | Iron studies, HFE gene |
| Wilson's disease | Ceruloplasmin, 24h urine copper, K-F rings |
| α1-antitrypsin | α1-AT level, phenotype |

### Imaging
- **Ultrasound:** Nodular liver, splenomegaly, ascites, portal vein patency
- **Fibroscan (TE):** Liver stiffness measurement (>12.5 kPa suggests cirrhosis)
- **CT/MRI:** HCC surveillance, portal vein assessment
- **Upper GI endoscopy:** Variceal screening

### Liver Biopsy
- Gold standard but often not needed
- Indicated when diagnosis uncertain or for grading/staging
- Transjugular approach if coagulopathy/ascites

## Management

### General Measures
- **Remove causative agent:** Alcohol abstinence, antiviral therapy
- **Nutrition:** High protein (1.2-1.5 g/kg), adequate calories, avoid malnutrition
- **Vaccinations:** Hepatitis A/B, pneumococcal, influenza
- **Avoid hepatotoxins:** NSAIDs, unnecessary medications
- **HCC surveillance:** Ultrasound ± AFP every 6 months

### Ascites Management

**First-line:**
- Sodium restriction (<2g/day)
- Diuretics: Spironolactone 100mg + Furosemide 40mg (100:40 ratio)
- Titrate to max 400mg:160mg
- Goal: Weight loss 0.5 kg/day (without edema), 1 kg/day (with edema)

**Refractory Ascites:**
- Large volume paracentesis (LVP) + albumin (6-8g per liter removed if >5L)
- TIPS (transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt)
- Liver transplant evaluation

**Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP):**
- Ascitic fluid PMN >250/mm³
- Treatment: Ceftriaxone or Cefotaxime
- Albumin 1.5 g/kg day 1, 1 g/kg day 3
- Prophylaxis: Norfloxacin/Ciprofloxacin

### Variceal Management

**Primary Prophylaxis (medium-large varices):**
- Non-selective beta-blockers (Propranolol, Carvedilol)
- Target HR 55-60 or 25% reduction
- OR Endoscopic variceal ligation (EVL)

**Acute Variceal Bleeding:**
1. Resuscitation, airway protection
2. IV Octreotide (50μg bolus → 50μg/hr) or Terlipressin
3. IV antibiotics (Ceftriaxone) - reduces mortality
4. Urgent endoscopy (<12 hours): EVL preferred
5. Balloon tamponade (Sengstaken-Blakemore) if uncontrolled
6. TIPS for refractory bleeding

**Secondary Prophylaxis:**
- Beta-blocker + EVL (combination preferred)

### Hepatic Encephalopathy

**Grading:**
- Grade 1: Sleep disturbance, mild confusion
- Grade 2: Lethargy, moderate confusion, asterixis
- Grade 3: Somnolence, gross disorientation
- Grade 4: Coma

**Management:**
1. Identify and treat precipitants (infection, GI bleed, constipation, medications, electrolyte imbalance)
2. **Lactulose:** 30-45 mL q1-2h until 2-3 soft stools/day, then titrate
3. **Rifaximin:** 550mg BID (add if recurrent despite lactulose)
4. Protein restriction NOT recommended (worsens malnutrition)

### Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)

**Criteria:**
- Cirrhosis with ascites
- Creatinine >1.5 mg/dL
- No improvement after volume expansion with albumin
- No shock, nephrotoxins, or parenchymal kidney disease

**Types:**
- HRS-AKI (Type 1): Rapidly progressive, poor prognosis
- HRS-CKD (Type 2): Gradual, associated with refractory ascites

**Treatment:**
- Albumin + Vasoconstrictors (Terlipressin preferred, or Norepinephrine/Midodrine+Octreotide)
- Liver transplant (definitive)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **AST:ALT >2** = Alcoholic liver disease (mitochondrial AST release)
2. **Thrombocytopenia** often first sign of portal hypertension
3. **Ascitic fluid SAAG ≥1.1** = Portal hypertension
4. **SBP:** PMN >250/mm³, single organism, treat empirically
5. **Child-Pugh C** = Decompensated, consider transplant
6. **MELD score** = Transplant prioritization (higher = sicker)
7. **Beta-blockers contraindicated** in refractory ascites, SBP, severe alcoholic hepatitis
8. **Lactulose mechanism:** Converts ammonia (NH3) to ammonium (NH4+), not absorbed
9. **HCC surveillance:** Every 6 months with US ± AFP
10. **Hepatorenal syndrome:** Functional, kidneys are normal, responds to liver transplant
11. **Avoid NSAIDs** in cirrhosis (precipitate renal failure, GI bleed)
12. **Hyponatremia in cirrhosis:** Poor prognosis, dilutional (water excess)
13. **Coffee:** 2+ cups/day associated with slower fibrosis progression
