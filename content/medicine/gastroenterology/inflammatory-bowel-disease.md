# Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)

> 📚 **Sources:** Harrison's 22nd Ed Ch. 326, Sleisenger & Fordtran's GI and Liver Disease
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Inflammatory bowel disease encompasses Crohn's disease and Ulcerative colitis—chronic, relapsing-remitting inflammatory disorders of the GI tract. The pathogenesis involves dysregulated immune response to gut microbiota in genetically susceptible individuals. While UC is limited to colonic mucosa (continuous from rectum), Crohn's can affect any GI segment with transmural, skip lesions. Both have significant extraintestinal manifestations.

## Key Points

- **UC:** Continuous, mucosal, rectum always involved, bloody diarrhea
- **Crohn's:** Skip lesions, transmural, any GI location (ileum most common)
- **Bimodal age:** 15-30 and 50-70 years
- **Smoking:** Protective in UC, worsens Crohn's
- **Appendectomy:** Protective in UC

## Clinical Features

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Ulcerative Colitis | Crohn's Disease |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Location** | Colon only (starts at rectum) | Mouth to anus (terminal ileum most common) |
| **Pattern** | Continuous | Skip lesions |
| **Depth** | Mucosa/submucosa | Transmural |
| **Rectal involvement** | Always (95%) | Often spared (50%) |
| **Gross appearance** | Pseudopolyps, loss of haustra | Cobblestone, strictures, fistulae |
| **Histology** | Crypt abscesses, goblet cell depletion | Non-caseating granulomas (30%) |
| **Bloody diarrhea** | Very common | Less common |
| **Fistulae/Abscesses** | Rare | Common (perianal) |
| **Strictures** | Rare (consider malignancy) | Common |
| **Smoking effect** | Protective | Worsens disease |
| **Surgery curative** | Yes (proctocolectomy) | No (recurrence common) |

### Ulcerative Colitis Symptoms
- **Bloody diarrhea** (hallmark)
- Mucus in stool
- Tenesmus
- Urgency
- Lower abdominal cramping
- Nocturnal symptoms (severe disease)

**Severity (Truelove & Witts):**
| Parameter | Mild | Moderate | Severe |
|-----------|------|----------|--------|
| Stools/day | <4 | 4-6 | >6 |
| Blood in stool | Intermittent | Moderate | Frequent |
| Temperature | Normal | ≤37.8°C | >37.8°C |
| Pulse | Normal | ≤90 | >90 |
| Hemoglobin | >11 g/dL | 10.5-11 | <10.5 |
| ESR | <20 | 20-30 | >30 |

### Crohn's Disease Symptoms
- Chronic diarrhea (often non-bloody)
- Abdominal pain (RLQ, postprandial)
- Weight loss, malnutrition
- Fatigue
- Perianal disease (fistulae, abscesses, fissures)
- Mouth ulcers (aphthous)

**Phenotypes (Montreal Classification):**
- B1: Non-stricturing, non-penetrating
- B2: Stricturing
- B3: Penetrating (fistulae, abscesses)

### Extraintestinal Manifestations

| System | Manifestation | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **MSK** | Peripheral arthritis | Correlates with bowel activity |
| | Sacroiliitis, Ankylosing spondylitis | Independent of bowel activity |
| **Skin** | Erythema nodosum | Correlates with disease activity |
| | Pyoderma gangrenosum | More common in UC |
| **Eyes** | Uveitis, Episcleritis | May precede GI symptoms |
| **Hepatobiliary** | Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) | Strongly associated with UC |
| | Gallstones (Crohn's) | Bile salt malabsorption |
| **Renal** | Nephrolithiasis (oxalate stones) | Crohn's with ileal disease |
| **Hematologic** | Anemia, Thromboembolism | Hypercoagulable state |

## Diagnosis

### Laboratory
- CBC: Anemia (Fe deficiency, chronic disease, B12 in ileal Crohn's)
- Inflammatory markers: Elevated CRP, ESR
- Albumin: Low (malnutrition, protein-losing)
- LFTs: Elevated ALP suggests PSC
- **Stool studies:** Rule out C. diff, cultures, ova/parasites
- **Fecal calprotectin:** Elevated in active inflammation (>250 μg/g suggests IBD)

### Serology (Supportive, not diagnostic)
- **p-ANCA:** 65% in UC, 10% in Crohn's
- **ASCA:** 60% in Crohn's, 10% in UC

### Endoscopy

**Colonoscopy with biopsies (gold standard):**

*UC findings:*
- Continuous inflammation starting at rectum
- Loss of vascular pattern
- Erythema, friability, ulceration
- Pseudopolyps
- Lead-pipe colon (chronic)

*Crohn's findings:*
- Skip lesions
- Aphthous ulcers (early)
- Deep linear/serpiginous ulcers
- Cobblestone appearance
- Strictures

**Upper GI endoscopy:** Consider in Crohn's (gastroduodenal involvement)

### Imaging
- **CT/MR Enterography:** Assess small bowel in Crohn's, detect strictures, fistulae, abscesses
- **MRI Pelvis:** Perianal Crohn's evaluation
- **Plain X-ray:** Toxic megacolon (colonic diameter >6 cm)

### Histology
- **UC:** Crypt distortion, crypt abscesses, goblet cell depletion, no granulomas
- **Crohn's:** Non-caseating granulomas (30%), transmural inflammation, lymphoid aggregates

## Management

### Goals
- Induce and maintain remission
- Improve quality of life
- Prevent complications
- Minimize steroid exposure

### Ulcerative Colitis

**Mild-Moderate (Distal):**
- Topical 5-ASA (mesalamine suppositories/enemas)
- Oral 5-ASA if extensive

**Moderate:**
- Oral 5-ASA (mesalamine, sulfasalazine)
- Oral steroids (Prednisone) for flares
- Topical + oral 5-ASA combination

**Severe/Refractory:**
- IV corticosteroids (Methylprednisolone)
- If no response in 3-5 days: IV Cyclosporine or Infliximab
- Surgery if toxic megacolon or failure

**Maintenance:**
- 5-ASA (first-line)
- Thiopurines (Azathioprine, 6-MP)
- Biologics (Anti-TNF, Vedolizumab, Ustekinumab)

### Crohn's Disease

**Induction:**
- Mild: Budesonide (ileal/right colonic disease)
- Moderate-Severe: Systemic steroids + early biologic/immunomodulator
- Perianal: Antibiotics (Ciprofloxacin, Metronidazole) + biologics + surgery

**Maintenance:**
- Thiopurines (Azathioprine, 6-MP)
- Methotrexate
- Biologics: Anti-TNF (Infliximab, Adalimumab), Vedolizumab, Ustekinumab

**5-ASA is NOT effective for Crohn's disease** (except mild colonic disease)

### Biologic Agents

| Class | Drugs | Mechanism |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Anti-TNF | Infliximab, Adalimumab, Certolizumab, Golimumab | Block TNF-α |
| Anti-integrin | Vedolizumab | Block α4β7 integrin (gut-selective) |
| Anti-IL-12/23 | Ustekinumab | Block IL-12 and IL-23 |
| JAK inhibitors | Tofacitinib, Upadacitinib | Block Janus kinases |

### Surgical Management

**UC:**
- Proctocolectomy with ileal pouch-anal anastomosis (IPAA) = curative
- Indications: Refractory disease, dysplasia/cancer, toxic megacolon

**Crohn's:**
- Surgery for complications (strictures, fistulae, abscesses)
- Bowel-sparing approach (strictureplasty)
- High recurrence rate (50% need repeat surgery)

### Surveillance
- **Colonoscopy for dysplasia:** Start 8-10 years after diagnosis
- Annual if PSC
- Every 1-3 years based on risk

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Bloody diarrhea + rectal involvement = UC** (until proven otherwise)
2. **Skip lesions + granulomas + transmural = Crohn's**
3. **Smoking:** Protective in UC, worsens Crohn's
4. **PSC strongly associated with UC** (70% of PSC patients have UC)
5. **Toxic megacolon:** >6 cm colonic diameter + systemic toxicity = surgical emergency
6. **5-ASA works in UC, NOT in Crohn's** (except mild colonic)
7. **Perianal disease = Think Crohn's**
8. **Fecal calprotectin:** Non-invasive marker for intestinal inflammation
9. **Anti-TNF before starting:** Check for TB (latent), Hepatitis B
10. **Crohn's surgery doesn't cure** - 50% recurrence at anastomotic site
11. **Lead-pipe colon on barium:** Chronic UC (loss of haustra)
12. **String sign on barium:** Terminal ileum stricture in Crohn's
13. **Colorectal cancer risk:** Increases with disease duration and extent
