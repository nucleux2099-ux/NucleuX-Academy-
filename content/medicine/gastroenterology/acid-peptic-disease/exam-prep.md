# Acid Peptic Disease — Exam Prep

## NMC Competencies
**[UG]** IM5.1-IM5.5, IM5.7-IM5.12 (K/KH) | **[PG]** IM5.6 (SH)

---

## 🧠 Mnemonics

### PPI Mechanism: **PPIs are Proton Pump Irreversible inhibitors**
Block H⁺/K⁺ ATPase on parietal cell apical membrane — **irreversible** (unlike H2 blockers)

### H. pylori Triple Therapy: **PCA-14**
PPI + Clarithromycin + Amoxicillin × 14 days

### Forrest Classification: **"Squirt, Ooze, Vessel, Clot, Spot, Clean"**
Ia → Ib → IIa → IIb → IIc → III (decreasing rebleed risk)

## ⚡ High-Yield Facts

1. **Most common cause of PUD** = H. pylori; **Most common cause of drug-induced PUD** = NSAIDs
2. **DU is 4× more common** than GU
3. **All gastric ulcers must be biopsied** to rule out malignancy; DU does not need biopsy
4. **Anterior DU → perforation** (free air under diaphragm). **Posterior DU → bleeding** (GDA erosion)
5. **PPI side effects**: ↓ Mg²⁺, ↓ Ca²⁺ absorption (osteoporosis), C. difficile risk, B12 deficiency
6. **Misoprostol** (PGE1 analog) — NSAID-ulcer prophylaxis; **contraindicated in pregnancy** (abortifacient)
7. **Sucralfate** — mucosal protectant, binds to ulcer base; needs acidic pH → give before meals, don't combine with PPI
8. **Glasgow-Blatchford score** — predicts need for intervention in UGIB (score 0 = safe for outpatient)
9. **Rockall score** — predicts mortality post-endoscopy in UGIB
10. **ZE syndrome** — suspect when ulcers are multiple, recurrent, distal, refractory, or associated with diarrhea

## 📋 Indications for Endoscopy in Dyspepsia (Alarm Features)

**"VBAD"**
- **V** — Vomiting (persistent)
- **B** — Bleeding (hematemesis/melena)
- **A** — Anemia, Anorexia, weight loss
- **D** — Dysphagia

Also: age >55 with new-onset dyspepsia, family history of GI cancer

## 🔥 Exam-Style Questions

### Q1
A 60-year-old on NSAIDs for osteoarthritis presents with melena. Endoscopy shows a gastric ulcer with visible vessel (Forrest IIa). Management?

> **Endoscopic hemostasis** (injection + clips/thermal) + **IV PPI infusion** (80mg bolus → 8mg/h × 72h). Stop NSAIDs. Test for H. pylori.

### Q2
A patient has multiple duodenal ulcers including one in the jejunum, with secretory diarrhea. Fasting gastrin is 1200 pg/mL. Diagnosis?

> **Zollinger-Ellison syndrome** (gastrinoma). Check for MEN-1 (calcium, PTH, prolactin).

### Q3
Best test to confirm H. pylori eradication 4 weeks after treatment?

> **Urea breath test** (ensure PPI stopped ≥2 weeks prior)

### Q4
A patient with acute UGIB has tachycardia but BP is maintained. Hb is 7 g/dL. What is the transfusion threshold?

> Transfuse at **Hb <7 g/dL** (restrictive strategy). Aim for Hb 7-9. Liberal transfusion worsens outcomes in UGIB.

## 🎯 Clinical Pearls

> [!tip] **NSAID + H. pylori**
> Both are independent risk factors. Test and treat H. pylori in all patients starting long-term NSAIDs. Use PPI co-prescription or switch to COX-2 selective.

> [!warning] **Perforation**
> Free air under diaphragm on erect CXR = perforated viscus until proven otherwise. **Do NOT do endoscopy** in suspected perforation.
