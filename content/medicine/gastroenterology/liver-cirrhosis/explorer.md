# Liver Cirrhosis & Portal Hypertension — Explorer

## Overview

**Liver cirrhosis** is the end-stage of progressive hepatic fibrosis characterized by distortion of hepatic architecture and formation of **regenerative nodules**. It is irreversible and leads to **portal hypertension** and **hepatocellular dysfunction**. Alcohol and hepatitis B/C are the leading causes globally; in India, alcohol and hepatitis B predominate.

📚 Harrison's, 22nd Ed, Ch. 337-338

## Key Points

- **Cirrhosis** = diffuse fibrosis + regenerative nodules → architectural distortion
- **Portal hypertension:** HVPG (hepatic venous pressure gradient) **>5 mmHg** = portal HTN; **>10 mmHg** = clinically significant (varices form); **>12 mmHg** = variceal bleeding risk
- **Child-Pugh score** (A/B/C) and **MELD score** — assess prognosis and transplant priority
- **Complications:** Variceal bleeding, ascites, SBP, hepatic encephalopathy, HRS, HCC
- **Variceal bleeding prophylaxis:** Non-selective β-blocker (propranolol/carvedilol) or EVL
- **Ascites:** Sodium restriction + spironolactone ± furosemide; large-volume paracentesis + albumin
- **SBP:** PMN >250/μL in ascitic fluid → ceftriaxone; prophylaxis with norfloxacin
- **Hepatic encephalopathy:** Lactulose + rifaximin
- **Only definitive treatment:** Liver transplantation

## Etiology

| Cause | Key Features |
|---|---|
| **Alcohol** | #1 in West; >60-80g/day for >10 yrs; Mallory-Denk bodies, steatohepatitis |
| **Hepatitis B** | #1 in India/Asia; ground-glass hepatocytes; HCC risk even without cirrhosis |
| **Hepatitis C** | #1 in Japan/Egypt; slow progression; DAAs now cure >95% |
| **NAFLD/NASH** | Rising cause; associated with metabolic syndrome |
| **Autoimmune hepatitis** | Young women; ANA/SMA positive; respond to steroids |
| **Wilson's disease** | Copper overload; KF ring; low ceruloplasmin; young patient |
| **Hemochromatosis** | Iron overload; bronze diabetes; HFE gene C282Y |
| **PBC** | Anti-mitochondrial antibody (AMA); middle-aged women; cholestatic |
| **PSC** | p-ANCA; associated with UC; beaded bile ducts on MRCP |

## Pathophysiology

### Portal Hypertension Mechanism
1. Cirrhosis → ↑ intrahepatic resistance (fibrosis + vasoconstriction from ↓ NO)
2. → ↑ portal pressure (HVPG >5)
3. → **Portosystemic collaterals** develop (varices)
4. → **Splanchnic vasodilation** (↑ NO in splanchnic bed — paradox!)
5. → ↓ Effective arterial blood volume → RAAS/ADH activation → Na/water retention → **ascites**
6. → Renal vasoconstriction → **hepatorenal syndrome**

### Sites of Portosystemic Anastomoses (NEET favorite)
| Site | Normal → Abnormal | Clinical |
|---|---|---|
| **Esophageal** | Left gastric → azygos | Esophageal varices |
| **Rectal** | Superior rectal → middle/inferior rectal | Hemorrhoids |
| **Umbilical** | Paraumbilical → superficial epigastric | Caput medusae |
| **Retroperitoneal** | Colic/splenic → renal/phrenic | Retroperitoneal varices |
| **Falciform ligament** | Portal → umbilical | Cruveilhier-Baumgarten murmur |

## Clinical Features

### Signs of Hepatocellular Failure
- Jaundice, spider angiomata (SVC distribution), palmar erythema
- Gynecomastia, testicular atrophy (hyperestrogenemia)
- Fetor hepaticus (breath smells of mercaptans)
- Asterixis (flapping tremor — hepatic encephalopathy)
- Dupuytren's contracture (alcoholic cirrhosis)

### Signs of Portal Hypertension
- **Splenomegaly** (most reliable sign)
- **Ascites** (most common complication)
- **Caput medusae** (dilated periumbilical veins)
- **Esophageal/gastric varices**

## Prognostic Scoring

### Child-Pugh Score

| Parameter | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bilirubin (mg/dL) | <2 | 2-3 | >3 |
| Albumin (g/dL) | >3.5 | 2.8-3.5 | <2.8 |
| INR | <1.7 | 1.7-2.3 | >2.3 |
| Ascites | Absent | Mild | Moderate-severe |
| Encephalopathy | None | Grade 1-2 | Grade 3-4 |

- **Class A** (5-6): Well-compensated; 1-yr survival 100%
- **Class B** (7-9): Significant compromise; 1-yr survival 80%
- **Class C** (10-15): Decompensated; 1-yr survival 45%

### MELD Score
- Formula: 3.78 × ln(bilirubin) + 11.2 × ln(INR) + 9.57 × ln(creatinine) + 6.43
- Used for **transplant prioritization** (higher = sicker = higher priority)

## Management of Complications

### Variceal Bleeding
- **Acute:** IV octreotide/terlipressin + urgent EGD with **band ligation (EVL)** within 12h; IV PPI; antibiotics (ceftriaxone)
- **Secondary prophylaxis:** EVL + NSBB (propranolol/carvedilol)
- **Primary prophylaxis:** NSBB (propranolol target HR 55-60) OR EVL for large varices
- **Refractory:** TIPS (transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt)
- **Sengstaken-Blakemore tube** — rescue bridge to definitive therapy

### Ascites
- **Grade 1:** Sodium restriction (<2g/day) + spironolactone 100mg (↑ to 400mg) ± furosemide 40mg (↑ to 160mg)
- **Ratio:** Spironolactone:Furosemide = 100:40 (maintains K+ balance)
- **Refractory ascites:** Large-volume paracentesis (LVP) + albumin (8g/L removed) → consider TIPS
- **SAAG ≥1.1 g/dL** = portal hypertension-related ascites

### Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)
- **PMN count ≥250/μL** in ascitic fluid = diagnostic (culture may be negative)
- **Most common organism:** E. coli, Klebsiella
- **Treatment:** IV ceftriaxone (or cefotaxime) × 5 days + IV albumin (day 1 and 3)
- **Prophylaxis:** Norfloxacin 400mg/day in: prior SBP, GI bleed, ascitic fluid protein <1.5 g/dL

### Hepatic Encephalopathy
- **Grading:** 1 (asterixis, sleep disturbance) → 4 (coma)
- **Precipitants:** GI bleed, infection (SBP), constipation, hypokalemia, excess protein, sedatives
- **Treatment:** Lactulose (target 2-3 soft stools/day) + Rifaximin 550mg BD
- **Mechanism of lactulose:** ↓ colonic pH → converts NH3 to NH4+ (non-absorbable) + osmotic laxative effect

### Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)
- **Type 1 (AKI-HRS):** Rapidly progressive; creatinine doubles in <2 weeks; very poor prognosis
- **Type 2:** Slowly progressive; associated with refractory ascites
- **Treatment:** IV albumin + terlipressin (vasoconstrictor); bridge to transplant
- **Definitive:** Liver transplantation

### HCC Screening
- **Every 6 months:** Ultrasound + serum AFP in all cirrhotics
- **Diagnosis:** Arterial enhancement + portal venous washout on CT/MRI (no biopsy needed if classic)
