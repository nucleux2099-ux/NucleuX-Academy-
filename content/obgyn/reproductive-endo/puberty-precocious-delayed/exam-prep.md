# Puberty — Precocious & Delayed — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Normal puberty 8-13 years in girls; first sign = thelarche. Precocious = before 8; idiopathic central PP most common in girls. GnRH stimulation test is gold standard; treat with GnRH agonists. Delayed = no breast by 13 or no menarche by 15. Constitutional delay is most common; Turner syndrome is most common pathological cause.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **First sign of puberty in girls** → thelarche (breast bud)
- ★ **Most common type of precocious puberty in girls** → central/idiopathic
- ★ **Gold standard for central PP diagnosis** → GnRH stimulation test (LH rise >5)
- ★ **Treatment of central PP** → GnRH agonists (leuprolide)
- ★ **MRI brain** → mandatory in all central precocious puberty
- ★ **McCune-Albright triad** → café-au-lait spots + fibrous dysplasia + precocious puberty (peripheral)
- ★ **Most common CNS cause of central PP** → hypothalamic hamartoma
- ★ **Constitutional delay** → most common cause of delayed puberty; delayed bone age, family history+
- ★ **Turner syndrome** → most common pathological cause of delayed puberty in girls
- ★ **Kallmann syndrome** → anosmia + hypogonadotropic hypogonadism
- ★ **Van Wyk-Grumbach syndrome** → hypothyroidism causing precocious puberty (peripheral)
- ★ **Bone age advanced** → precocious puberty; **bone age delayed** → constitutional delay

## Mnemonics

**Puberty sequence: "The Puber Grows Menarche"**
- **T**helarche → **P**ubarche → **G**rowth spurt → **M**enarche

**McCune-Albright**: **"CPF"** — Café-au-lait, Precocious puberty, Fibrous dysplasia

**Delayed puberty causes — "TACK"**:
- **T**urner syndrome (hypergonadotropic)
- **A**norexia/constitutional (hypogonadotropic)
- **C**raniopharyngioma
- **K**allmann syndrome
