# Menopause Management — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Menopause = 12 months amenorrhea, average age 51 (India ~47). FSH >40 confirms. HRT is gold standard for vasomotor symptoms. Combined E+P for women with uterus, estrogen alone post-hysterectomy. Transdermal preferred (lower VTE). Start within 10-year window. WHI trial: combined HRT increases breast cancer risk slightly; estrogen alone does not.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Diagnosis of menopause** → clinical (12 months amenorrhea); FSH >40 if <45 years
- ★ **Most common symptom** → hot flushes (75%)
- ★ **Premature menopause/POI** → before age 40
- ★ **HRT in women with uterus** → must add progesterone (prevents endometrial cancer)
- ★ **Estrogen alone** → only for hysterectomized women
- ★ **Transdermal estrogen** → preferred route (no VTE risk increase)
- ★ **Window of opportunity** → start HRT within 10 years of menopause or before age 60
- ★ **WHI finding** → combined HRT increases breast cancer; estrogen alone does NOT
- ★ **Non-hormonal for hot flushes** → paroxetine (only FDA-approved SSRI)
- ★ **GSM** → progressive, needs local estrogen; does not improve with systemic HRT alone
- ★ **Raloxifene** → SERM, prevents vertebral fractures, does NOT help hot flushes (may worsen)
- ★ **Tibolone** → synthetic with estrogenic + progestogenic + androgenic activity

## Mnemonics

**"HRT Contraindications — BAD CLOT"**
- **B**reast cancer
- **A**ctive liver disease
- **D**VT/VTE history
- **C**oronary heart disease
- **L**iver disease
- **O**ther thrombophilia
- **T**hrombotic stroke

**"STRAW stages"**: -5 to +2 (reproductive → menopausal transition → postmenopause)

**POI causes — "MAGIC"**: Metabolic/autoimmune, Autoimmune, Genetic, Iatrogenic, Cryptogenic
