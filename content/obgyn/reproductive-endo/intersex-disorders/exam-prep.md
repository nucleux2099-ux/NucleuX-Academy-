# Intersex Disorders — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

DSD = discordance between chromosomal, gonadal, and phenotypic sex. CAH (21-hydroxylase deficiency) is the most common cause of ambiguous genitalia. CAIS = 46,XY female phenotype with absent uterus. 5α-reductase deficiency virilizes at puberty. Dysgenetic gonads with Y material need gonadectomy (gonadoblastoma risk).

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common cause of ambiguous genitalia** → CAH (21-hydroxylase deficiency)
- ★ **First investigation** in ambiguous genitalia → Karyotype
- ★ **17-OHP elevated** → 21-hydroxylase deficiency CAH
- ★ **Inguinal hernia in phenotypic girl** → suspect CAIS
- ★ **46,XY + female phenotype + absent uterus + no pubic hair** → CAIS
- ★ **46,XY + virilization at puberty** → 5α-reductase deficiency
- ★ **T/DHT ratio >30** → 5α-reductase deficiency
- ★ **46,XY + streak gonads + uterus present** → Swyer syndrome
- ★ **Gonadoblastoma risk** → any dysgenetic gonad with Y chromosome
- ★ **Salt-wasting crisis** → presents at 1-2 weeks of life in severe CAH
- ★ **SRY gene** → master switch for male sex determination (on Y chromosome)
- ★ **Most common enzyme deficiency in CAH** → 21-hydroxylase (90-95%)

## Mnemonics

**"CAIS has NO HAIR, NO UTERUS, NO response"**
- No pubic/axillary hair, No uterus, No androgen response

**Prader Staging** (I → V): Increasing virilization
- I = Clitoromegaly only → V = Complete male phenotype

**CAH triad**: Ambiguous genitalia + Salt wasting + Elevated 17-OHP

**"5-alpha at 12"** — 5α-reductase deficiency virilizes at puberty (~12 years)
