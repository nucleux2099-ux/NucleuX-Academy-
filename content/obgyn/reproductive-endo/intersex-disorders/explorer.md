# Intersex Disorders (Disorders of Sex Development)

## Overview

Disorders of Sex Development (DSD), previously termed intersex disorders, encompass conditions where chromosomal, gonadal, or anatomical sex is atypical. These are classified based on karyotype into 46,XX DSD, 46,XY DSD, and sex chromosome DSD. Early diagnosis, multidisciplinary management, and sensitive counseling are essential.

## Key Points

- DSD replaces older terms like hermaphroditism and pseudohermaphroditism
- Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (CAH) is the **most common cause** of ambiguous genitalia
- 21-hydroxylase deficiency accounts for **90-95%** of CAH cases
- Sex assignment depends on karyotype, gonadal function, genital anatomy, and surgical options
- SRY gene on Y chromosome is the master switch for male sex determination
- Müllerian Inhibiting Substance (MIS/AMH) from Sertoli cells causes Müllerian duct regression

## Definition

DSD is defined as congenital conditions in which development of chromosomal, gonadal, or anatomical sex is atypical, leading to discordance between genetic sex, gonadal sex, and phenotypic sex.

### Classification (Chicago Consensus 2006)

| Old Term | New Term |
|----------|----------|
| Female pseudohermaphrodite | 46,XX DSD |
| Male pseudohermaphrodite | 46,XY DSD |
| True hermaphrodite | Ovotesticular DSD |
| Mixed gonadal dysgenesis | 45,X/46,XY DSD |

## Etiology

### 46,XX DSD (Virilized Female)
- **CAH** (most common): 21-hydroxylase, 11β-hydroxylase, 3β-HSD deficiency
- Maternal androgen exposure (luteoma, exogenous androgens)
- Aromatase deficiency
- 46,XX ovotesticular DSD

### 46,XY DSD (Undervirilized Male)
- **Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (AIS)** — Complete (CAIS) and Partial (PAIS)
- 5α-reductase deficiency
- Testosterone biosynthesis defects (17β-HSD deficiency)
- Gonadal dysgenesis (Swyer syndrome — 46,XY pure gonadal dysgenesis)
- Persistent Müllerian Duct Syndrome (AMH deficiency)

### Sex Chromosome DSD
- Turner syndrome (45,X)
- Klinefelter syndrome (47,XXY)
- Mixed gonadal dysgenesis (45,X/46,XY)
- 46,XX/46,XY chimeric ovotesticular DSD

## Clinical Features

### Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia (21-Hydroxylase Deficiency)
- **Classic salt-wasting** (75%): Ambiguous genitalia in females, salt-wasting crisis at 1-2 weeks
- **Classic simple virilizing**: Ambiguous genitalia, no salt wasting
- **Non-classic (late-onset)**: Hirsutism, acne, oligomenorrhea in adolescent/adult females
- Prader staging (I-V) for degree of virilization

### Complete Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome (CAIS)
- **46,XY karyotype with female phenotype**
- Primary amenorrhea with normal breast development (peripheral aromatization)
- Absent or sparse pubic and axillary hair
- Blind vaginal pouch, absent uterus
- Testes in abdomen, inguinal canal, or labia majora
- **Inguinal hernia in a phenotypic girl** → think CAIS

### 5α-Reductase Deficiency
- 46,XY with ambiguous genitalia at birth
- **Virilization at puberty** ("guevedoces" — penis at 12)
- Male gender identity often emerges at puberty
- Small phallus, hypospadias, bifid scrotum at birth

### Swyer Syndrome (46,XY Gonadal Dysgenesis)
- Female phenotype with streak gonads
- Primary amenorrhea, absent secondary sexual characters
- **Normal Müllerian structures present** (uterus, tubes)
- High risk of gonadoblastoma → prophylactic gonadectomy

## Diagnosis

### Initial Evaluation of Ambiguous Genitalia
1. **Karyotype** — first and most important investigation
2. **17-hydroxyprogesterone** — elevated in 21-hydroxylase deficiency CAH
3. **Serum electrolytes** — hyponatremia/hyperkalemia in salt-wasting CAH
4. **Pelvic ultrasound** — presence of uterus and gonads
5. **Genitogram** — delineate urogenital sinus anatomy

### Hormonal Workup
- Testosterone, DHT, T/DHT ratio (elevated in 5α-reductase deficiency)
- ACTH stimulation test for CAH
- hCG stimulation test — testosterone response indicates functioning testicular tissue
- AMH/MIS levels — marker of Sertoli cell function
- LH, FSH levels

### Advanced Investigations
- FISH for SRY gene
- Molecular genetics for specific mutations
- Gonadal biopsy if needed
- Laparoscopy for gonadal visualization

## Management

### General Principles
- **Multidisciplinary team**: Pediatric endocrinologist, urologist, gynecologist, geneticist, psychologist
- **Do not rush sex assignment** — thorough evaluation first
- **Avoid unnecessary cosmetic surgery** in infancy (current trend)
- Psychological support and genetic counseling are essential

### CAH Management
- **Glucocorticoid replacement** (hydrocortisone in children)
- **Mineralocorticoid** (fludrocortisone) for salt-wasting type
- Surgical correction: Feminizing genitoplasty (clitoroplasty, vaginoplasty)
- Prenatal treatment: Dexamethasone to mother (controversial)
- Newborn screening with 17-OHP

### AIS Management
- **CAIS**: Raise as female; gonadectomy after puberty (allow feminization), then HRT
- **PAIS**: Sex assignment based on degree of virilization and response to androgens
- Vaginal dilation or vaginoplasty if needed

### Gonadal Management
- Remove dysgenetic gonads with Y chromosome material → gonadoblastoma risk (30%)
- CAIS: Gonadectomy can be delayed until after puberty
- Streak gonads in Swyer syndrome: Early gonadectomy recommended

## Complications

- Salt-wasting crisis in CAH (life-threatening)
- Gonadoblastoma/dysgerminoma in dysgenetic gonads with Y material
- Infertility
- Psychological distress, gender dysphoria
- Adrenal crisis if glucocorticoids not maintained in CAH
- Osteoporosis if gonadectomy performed without adequate HRT
