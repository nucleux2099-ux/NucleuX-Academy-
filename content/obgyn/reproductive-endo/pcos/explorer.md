# Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

## Overview
Polycystic ovary syndrome is the most common endocrine disorder in women of reproductive age, affecting 5-15% of women. It is characterized by a combination of hyperandrogenism, ovulatory dysfunction, and polycystic ovarian morphology.

PCOS is a leading cause of anovulatory infertility and is associated with significant metabolic complications including insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes, dyslipidemia, and cardiovascular disease. It has a substantial impact on quality of life due to cosmetic concerns and psychological effects.

## Key Points
- Diagnosed by Rotterdam criteria (2 of 3: oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on USS)
- Most common cause of anovulatory infertility
- Insulin resistance is central to pathophysiology
- First-line treatment for menstrual irregularity: Combined oral contraceptives
- First-line ovulation induction: Letrozole (aromatase inhibitor)
- Long-term metabolic surveillance essential

## Definition & Classification
### Rotterdam Criteria (2003) — Need 2 of 3
1. **Oligo-ovulation or anovulation** (cycles >35 days or <8 cycles/year)
2. **Clinical and/or biochemical hyperandrogenism** (hirsutism, acne, elevated testosterone)
3. **Polycystic ovarian morphology** (≥12 follicles 2-9 mm per ovary OR ovarian volume >10 mL)

### Phenotypes
- **A**: All three criteria (classic, most severe)
- **B**: Hyperandrogenism + ovulatory dysfunction (without PCO morphology)
- **C**: Hyperandrogenism + PCO morphology (ovulatory PCOS)
- **D**: Ovulatory dysfunction + PCO morphology (non-hyperandrogenic)

## Etiology & Pathophysiology
### Core Defects
- **Insulin resistance and compensatory hyperinsulinemia** (60-70% of patients)
- **Hypothalamic-pituitary dysfunction**: Increased GnRH pulse frequency → elevated LH:FSH ratio (>2:1)
- **Ovarian hyperandrogenism**: Theca cell overproduction of androgens driven by LH and insulin
- **Arrested follicular development**: Multiple small follicles fail to reach dominance → anovulation

### Metabolic Consequences
- Insulin resistance → hyperinsulinemia → hepatic SHBG reduction → more free testosterone
- Insulin acts as co-gonadotropin with LH on theca cells
- Adipose tissue aromatizes androgens to estrogens → chronic estrogen exposure → endometrial hyperplasia risk

## Clinical Features
### Reproductive
- Oligomenorrhea or amenorrhea
- Infertility (anovulatory)
- Recurrent pregnancy loss

### Hyperandrogenic
- Hirsutism (modified Ferriman-Gallwey score ≥8)
- Acne (especially jawline and chin)
- Androgenic alopecia (male-pattern)

### Metabolic
- Obesity (central, 50-70%)
- Acanthosis nigricans (insulin resistance marker)
- Impaired glucose tolerance / type 2 diabetes
- Dyslipidemia (high TG, low HDL)

### Psychological
- Anxiety and depression
- Poor body image
- Reduced quality of life

## Diagnosis
### Investigations
- **Hormonal**: LH, FSH (LH:FSH >2:1), total/free testosterone, DHEAS, 17-OH progesterone (to rule out CAH)
- **Metabolic**: Fasting glucose, insulin, OGTT, lipid profile, HbA1c
- **Thyroid**: TSH (rule out hypothyroidism)
- **Prolactin**: Rule out hyperprolactinemia
- **Ultrasound**: Transvaginal — ovarian volume, antral follicle count, endometrial thickness

### Differential Diagnosis
- Congenital adrenal hyperplasia (21-hydroxylase deficiency)
- Cushing syndrome
- Thyroid dysfunction
- Hyperprolactinemia
- Androgen-secreting tumors
- Idiopathic hirsutism

## Management
### Lifestyle Modification (First-line for ALL)
- Weight loss of 5-10% improves ovulation and metabolic parameters
- Exercise: 150 minutes/week moderate intensity
- Dietary modification (low glycemic index)

### Menstrual Regulation
- **COC pills**: First-line for cycle regulation + anti-androgen effect
- **Cyclical progesterone**: For endometrial protection if COCs contraindicated

### Hirsutism/Acne
- COCs with anti-androgenic progestins (cyproterone acetate, drospirenone)
- Spironolactone (anti-androgen)
- Topical eflornithine
- Laser/electrolysis for cosmetic management

### Infertility
- **Letrozole**: First-line for ovulation induction (superior to clomiphene)
- **Clomiphene citrate**: Second-line
- **Gonadotropins**: Low-dose step-up protocol if resistant
- **Laparoscopic ovarian drilling**: For clomiphene-resistant cases
- **IVF**: If above fails

### Metabolic
- **Metformin**: For insulin resistance, glucose intolerance, adjunct for ovulation
- Statin therapy if dyslipidemia persists despite lifestyle changes

### Long-term Surveillance
- Annual OGTT or HbA1c
- Lipid profile
- Endometrial thickness monitoring (if prolonged amenorrhea)
- Screening for obstructive sleep apnea
- Cardiovascular risk assessment

## Complications
- Type 2 diabetes mellitus (30-40% by age 40)
- Endometrial hyperplasia and carcinoma
- Cardiovascular disease
- Obstructive sleep apnea
- Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
- Gestational diabetes and pre-eclampsia (if pregnant)
- Depression and anxiety
