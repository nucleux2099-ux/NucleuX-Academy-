# PCOS — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
PCOS is the most common endocrine disorder in reproductive-age women. Diagnosed by Rotterdam criteria (2 of 3: oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, PCO on USS). Core pathophysiology is insulin resistance + LH-driven ovarian hyperandrogenism. Letrozole is first-line for ovulation induction.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Rotterdam criteria: 2 of 3 — oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries
- ★ LH:FSH ratio >2:1 (but not required for diagnosis)
- ★ Most common cause of anovulatory infertility
- ★ Insulin resistance present in 60-70% (even lean PCOS)
- ★ First-line ovulation induction: Letrozole (not clomiphene — NEJM 2014)
- ★ First-line for menstrual regulation: Combined oral contraceptives
- ★ Endometrial cancer risk increased due to chronic unopposed estrogen
- ★ Acanthosis nigricans = clinical marker of insulin resistance
- ★ Modified Ferriman-Gallwey score ≥8 = clinical hirsutism
- ★ 17-OH progesterone: Rule out congenital adrenal hyperplasia (non-classical)
- ★ Metformin: Adjunct for metabolic features, not first-line for fertility

## Mnemonics

- **H**irsutism
- **A**cne
- **I**nfertility
- **R**esistance (insulin)
- **A**ndrogenic alopecia

**Rotterdam** — "HAP" (need 2 of 3):
- **H**yperandrogenism
- **A**novulation
- **P**olycystic ovaries on USS
