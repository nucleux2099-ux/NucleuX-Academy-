# Ovulation Induction — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Pharmacological stimulation to achieve ovulation in anovulatory women. Letrozole is first-line for PCOS (NEJM 2014). CC is second-line. Gonadotropins for CC-resistant or WHO Group I. Trigger with hCG when follicle ≥18 mm. OHSS is most serious complication (VEGF-mediated capillary leak). Monitor with serial TVS folliculometry.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Letrozole > clomiphene for PCOS: higher live birth, less multiples, better endometrium
- ★ CC mechanism: blocks estrogen receptors at hypothalamus → ↑GnRH → ↑FSH
- ★ CC anti-estrogenic effects: thin endometrium + hostile cervical mucus (limits conception)
- ★ CC-resistant: fails to ovulate at 150 mg; CC-failure: ovulates but no conception after 6 cycles
- ★ Low-dose step-up protocol for gonadotropins minimizes OHSS
- ★ LOD: 4-6 punctures/ovary; alternative to gonadotropins in CC-resistant PCOS
- ★ OHSS pathophysiology: VEGF → capillary permeability → third-space fluid shift
- ★ OHSS prevention: GnRH agonist trigger (instead of hCG), freeze-all, antagonist protocol
- ★ Cancel OI cycle if >3 follicles ≥14 mm (risk of multiples)
- ★ WHO Group I: pulsatile GnRH is most physiological treatment
- ★ WHO Group III (POI): OI is NOT effective → donor oocyte IVF

## Mnemonics

**PCOS OI ladder — "LLCG-DI":** Lifestyle, Letrozole, Clomiphene, Gonadotropins, Drilling (LOD), IVF

**OHSS severity — "BNATR":** Bloating (mild), Nausea+ascites (moderate), Anasarca (severe), Thrombosis, Renal failure (critical)

**CC timing — "Rule of 5":** 50 mg, Day 5, for 5 days (traditional; now Day 2-6 common)
