# Ovulation Induction

## Overview

Ovulation induction (OI) is the pharmacological stimulation of ovulation in women with anovulatory infertility. The goal is to achieve unifollicular ovulation (single dominant follicle) to restore fertility while minimizing multiple pregnancy risk. Controlled ovarian hyperstimulation (COH/COS) for IVF aims for multifollicular development. The choice of agent depends on WHO ovulatory dysfunction group.

## Key Points

- Goal of OI: single dominant follicle and ovulation
- Goal of COS (for IVF): multiple follicles
- Letrozole is now first-line for PCOS (WHO Group II)
- Clomiphene citrate: anti-estrogen; has been standard for decades
- Gonadotropins: directly stimulate follicles; higher OHSS/multiple pregnancy risk
- Monitoring essential: ultrasound folliculometry ± serum estradiol
- OHSS is the most serious iatrogenic complication

## Definition & Classification

### Types of Ovarian Stimulation
| Type | Goal | Setting |
|------|------|---------|
| Ovulation induction (OI) | Monofollicular ovulation | Anovulatory women (natural conception/IUI) |
| Controlled ovarian stimulation (COS) | Multifollicular development | IVF/ICSI |
| Superovulation | Multiple follicles + IUI | Unexplained infertility, mild male factor |

### Agents Used
| Agent | Mechanism | Primary Use |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Letrozole | Aromatase inhibitor → decreased estrogen → increased FSH | PCOS (first-line) |
| Clomiphene citrate | Selective estrogen receptor modulator (anti-estrogen at hypothalamus) | PCOS (second-line) |
| Gonadotropins (FSH ± LH) | Direct follicular stimulation | WHO Group I, clomiphene-resistant PCOS, IVF |
| Pulsatile GnRH | Mimics physiological GnRH release | WHO Group I (hypothalamic amenorrhea) |
| Metformin | Insulin sensitizer | Adjunct in PCOS (insulin resistance) |
| hCG | LH surge mimic → triggers ovulation | Ovulation trigger (all protocols) |
| GnRH agonist trigger | Endogenous LH surge | IVF (reduces OHSS risk) |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### By WHO Group
| Group | Cause | Treatment |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| I | Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism | Pulsatile GnRH or gonadotropins (FSH+LH) |
| II | Normogonadotropic anovulation (PCOS) | Letrozole → clomiphene → gonadotropins → LOD |
| III | Hypergonadotropic (POI) | Donor oocyte (OI not effective) |

### Mechanism of Key Agents

#### Letrozole
- Blocks aromatase → decreased estrogen → releases hypothalamus from negative feedback → increased FSH
- Short half-life → less anti-estrogenic effect on endometrium and cervical mucus than CC
- Monofollicular development in 70%

#### Clomiphene Citrate (CC)
- SERM: blocks estrogen receptors at hypothalamus → perceives low estrogen → increased GnRH → FSH/LH surge
- Anti-estrogenic effects on endometrium (thin) and cervical mucus (hostile) — disadvantages
- 80% ovulation rate, 40-50% conception rate (discrepancy due to anti-estrogenic effects)
- Maximum 6 cycles recommended

#### Gonadotropins
- Recombinant FSH (follitropin) ± recombinant LH
- Direct follicular stimulation bypassing hypothalamic-pituitary axis
- Requires close monitoring (ultrasound + estradiol)
- Higher risk of OHSS and multiple pregnancy

## Clinical Features

### Indications
- Anovulatory infertility (PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhea, hyperprolactinemia)
- Controlled ovarian stimulation for IUI
- COS for IVF/ICSI
- Unexplained infertility (empirical with IUI)

### Monitoring Protocol
1. Baseline TVS (Day 2-3): exclude cysts, measure AFC
2. Start medication
3. Folliculometry: serial TVS every 2-3 days from Day 8-10
4. Goal: dominant follicle 18-20 mm (OI) or multiple follicles 16-18 mm (IVF)
5. Trigger ovulation with hCG (5000-10000 IU) when lead follicle ≥18 mm
6. Timed intercourse or IUI 36 hours after trigger
7. Luteal phase support: progesterone supplementation

### Criteria to Cancel/Withhold Trigger
- >3 follicles ≥14 mm (OI/IUI) → risk of multiples
- Estradiol excessively high
- Signs of early OHSS

## Diagnosis

### Pre-Treatment Evaluation
- Confirm anovulation (irregular cycles, low Day 21 progesterone)
- WHO group classification
- Partner semen analysis (normal or mild factor)
- Tubal patency assessment (at least one patent tube)
- Baseline hormones: FSH, LH, estradiol, TSH, prolactin, AMH
- BMI optimization

## Management

### PCOS Ovulation Induction Protocol (Stepwise)

#### Step 1: Lifestyle Modification
- Weight loss 5-10% if overweight (improves ovulation in 50%)

#### Step 2: Letrozole (First-Line)
- 2.5-7.5 mg/day, Day 2-6 (or 3-7)
- Superior to CC: higher live birth rate, less multiple pregnancy, better endometrial thickness

#### Step 3: Clomiphene Citrate (Second-Line)
- 50-150 mg/day, Day 2-6 (start at 50 mg, increase by 50 mg if no response)
- Maximum dose: 150 mg; maximum 6 cycles
- Add metformin if insulin resistant

#### Step 4: Gonadotropins (Low-Dose Step-Up)
- Start FSH 37.5-75 IU/day; increase by 37.5 IU every 7-14 days
- "Low-dose step-up protocol" minimizes OHSS risk
- Close monitoring mandatory

#### Step 5: Laparoscopic Ovarian Drilling (LOD)
- 4-6 punctures per ovary with diathermy/laser
- Reduces androgens and LH; restores ovulation in 50-70%
- Alternative to gonadotropins in CC-resistant PCOS

#### Step 6: IVF
- If above fail; or if coexisting tubal/male factor

### Clomiphene Resistance vs Failure
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| CC-resistant | Fails to ovulate on maximum dose (150 mg) |
| CC-failure | Ovulates but doesn't conceive after 6 cycles |

## Complications

### Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS)
- Most serious complication of ovulation induction
- Pathophysiology: VEGF-mediated increased capillary permeability → third-space fluid shift
- **Mild:** Bloating, mild pain, ovaries <8 cm
- **Moderate:** Nausea, vomiting, ascites, ovaries 8-12 cm
- **Severe:** Tense ascites, pleural effusion, oliguria, hemoconcentration (Hct >45%), hyponatremia, VTE, ARDS, renal failure
- **Critical:** Anasarca, thromboembolism, organ failure

#### OHSS Prevention
- Low-dose protocols
- GnRH antagonist protocol (IVF)
- GnRH agonist trigger instead of hCG
- "Freeze-all" strategy (no fresh embryo transfer)
- Metformin in PCOS
- Cancel cycle if excessive response

#### OHSS Treatment
- Mild: outpatient, oral hydration, monitor
- Severe: hospitalization, IV crystalloids, albumin, paracentesis, thromboprophylaxis (LMWH)
- Self-limiting (resolves in 7-10 days, longer if pregnancy occurs)

### Multiple Pregnancy
- CC: 5-10% twins
- Gonadotropins: 20-30% multiples
- Cancel cycle or selective reduction
