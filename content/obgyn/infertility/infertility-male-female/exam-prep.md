# Infertility — Male & Female — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Infertility = failure to conceive after 12 months. Evaluate both partners. Female: ovulatory disorders most common (PCOS). Male: varicocele most common correctable cause. Day 21 progesterone confirms ovulation. AMH = best ovarian reserve marker. Semen analysis = first male test. Letrozole is first-line for PCOS ovulation induction. IVF for tubal factor; ICSI for severe male factor.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common female cause** → ovulatory disorder (PCOS — WHO Group II)
- ★ **Most common correctable male cause** → varicocele
- ★ **First male investigation** → semen analysis
- ★ **Ovulation confirmation** → Day 21 serum progesterone >3 ng/mL
- ★ **Best ovarian reserve marker** → AMH (any day of cycle)
- ★ **First-line tubal test** → HSG; **Gold standard** → laparoscopy + chromopertubation
- ★ **First-line ovulation induction for PCOS** → letrozole (aromatase inhibitor)
- ★ **Treatment for bilateral tubal block** → IVF
- ★ **Hydrosalpinx before IVF** → salpingectomy (improves outcomes)
- ★ **ICSI indication** → severe male factor (severe oligospermia/OA)
- ★ **Most common cause of tubal infertility** → Chlamydia PID
- ★ **WHO semen: count ≥16M/mL, motility ≥30% progressive, morphology ≥4%**

## Mnemonics

**Female infertility causes — "TOUPEE"**:
- **T**ubal, **O**vulatory, **U**terine, **P**eritoneal, **E**ndometriosis, **E**xplained/unexplained

**WHO ovulatory groups — "1-2-3 = Low-Normal-High"**:
- Group I: Low FSH (hypogonadotropic)
- Group II: Normal FSH (PCOS)
- Group III: High FSH (POI)

**Semen analysis — "CoMM" ≥16, ≥30, ≥4**:
- **Co**unt ≥16M/mL, **M**otility ≥30%, **M**orphology ≥4%

**ART ladder**: IUI → IVF → ICSI → Donor gametes → Surrogacy
