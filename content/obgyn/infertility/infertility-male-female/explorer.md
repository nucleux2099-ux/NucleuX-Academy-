# Infertility — Male and Female

## Overview

Infertility is defined as failure to conceive after 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse. It affects approximately 10-15% of couples. Female factors account for ~40%, male factors ~40%, combined ~10%, and unexplained ~10%. Systematic evaluation of both partners simultaneously is essential. ART (Assisted Reproductive Technology) has revolutionized management.

## Key Points

- **Primary infertility**: Never conceived before
- **Secondary infertility**: Previous conception but unable to conceive again
- Evaluate **both partners simultaneously** — male evaluation is simpler, start with semen analysis
- **Ovulatory disorders** are the most common female cause
- **Varicocele** is the most common correctable male cause
- Day 21 progesterone confirms ovulation; HSG assesses tubal patency
- IVF is the final common pathway for most refractory infertility

## Definition

- **Infertility**: Failure to achieve clinical pregnancy after 12 months of regular unprotected sexual intercourse
- **Subfertility**: Reduced fertility with possibility of natural conception
- **Fecundability**: Probability of achieving pregnancy in one menstrual cycle (~20-25% in normal couples)

## Etiology

### Female Factors (~40%)

#### Ovulatory Disorders (WHO Classification)
| Group | Cause | FSH/LH | Example |
|-------|-------|--------|---------|
| I | Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism | Low | Kallmann, anorexia |
| II | Normogonadotropic | Normal | **PCOS** (most common) |
| III | Hypergonadotropic hypogonadism | High | POI, Turner |

#### Tubal/Peritoneal Factors (25-35%)
- PID/salpingitis (Chlamydia — most common cause)
- Endometriosis
- Previous ectopic pregnancy
- Tubal ligation/surgery
- Pelvic adhesions (TB, surgery)

#### Uterine Factors
- Fibroids (submucosal)
- Asherman syndrome (intrauterine adhesions)
- Müllerian anomalies
- Endometrial polyps

#### Cervical Factors
- Cervical stenosis, hostile cervical mucus
- Post-cone biopsy/LEEP

### Male Factors (~40%)

#### Pre-testicular
- Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (Kallmann, pituitary tumors)
- Hyperprolactinemia
- Drugs, anabolic steroids

#### Testicular
- **Varicocele** (most common correctable cause — 40% of infertile men)
- Cryptorchidism
- Klinefelter syndrome (47,XXY)
- Y chromosome microdeletion
- Mumps orchitis
- Chemotherapy/radiation

#### Post-testicular
- Obstructive azoospermia (CBAVD — associated with CF mutations)
- Ejaculatory dysfunction
- Antisperm antibodies

## Clinical Features

### History
- Duration of infertility, primary vs secondary
- Menstrual history (regularity, dysmenorrhea)
- Sexual history (frequency, timing, dysfunction)
- Previous pregnancies, contraception
- Medical/surgical history, medications
- Lifestyle: Smoking, alcohol, BMI, occupation

### Examination — Female
- BMI, hirsutism, acne (PCOS features)
- Thyroid examination
- Breast examination (galactorrhea)
- Abdominal/pelvic examination
- Speculum: Cervical pathology

### Examination — Male
- Testicular size and consistency
- Varicocele (examine standing, Valsalva)
- Secondary sexual characters
- Gynecomastia

## Diagnosis

### Female Investigations

#### Ovulation Assessment
- **Day 21 (mid-luteal) serum progesterone** >3 ng/mL confirms ovulation (>10 = good ovulation)
- Day 2/3 FSH, LH, estradiol (ovarian reserve)
- **AMH** — best marker of ovarian reserve (any day of cycle)
- **Antral follicle count (AFC)** on ultrasound — ovarian reserve
- Thyroid function, prolactin

#### Tubal Assessment
- **HSG (Hysterosalpingography)** — first-line tubal test; radiopaque dye through cervix, X-ray
- **Sonosalpingography** — saline/contrast through tubes, ultrasound visualization
- **Laparoscopy + chromopertubation** — gold standard for tubal assessment; dye through tubes at surgery

#### Uterine Assessment
- Transvaginal ultrasound
- Saline infusion sonography (SIS)
- Hysteroscopy — gold standard for intrauterine pathology
- MRI if Müllerian anomaly suspected

### Male Investigations
- **Semen analysis** — first and most important test

| Parameter | WHO 2021 Reference |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Volume | ≥1.4 mL |
| Count | ≥16 million/mL |
| Total count | ≥39 million |
| Motility (progressive) | ≥30% |
| Total motility | ≥42% |
| Morphology (strict) | ≥4% normal forms |

- If abnormal → repeat in 3 months
- Hormonal: FSH, LH, testosterone
- Karyotype, Y microdeletion (if severe oligospermia/azoospermia)
- Scrotal ultrasound (varicocele, obstruction)
- Testicular biopsy if azoospermia (obstructive vs non-obstructive)

## Management

### Female — Ovulatory Disorders
- **WHO Group I**: GnRH pulsatile therapy or gonadotropins
- **WHO Group II (PCOS)**: Letrozole (first-line) > clomiphene citrate > gonadotropins > LOD
- **WHO Group III (POI)**: Donor oocytes/IVF
- Hyperprolactinemia: Cabergoline/bromocriptine

### Female — Tubal Factor
- Mild tubal disease: Laparoscopic adhesiolysis, fimbrioplasty
- Severe tubal disease / bilateral block: **IVF** (treatment of choice)
- Hydrosalpinx: Salpingectomy before IVF (improves success)

### Female — Uterine Factor
- Submucosal fibroid: Hysteroscopic myomectomy
- Asherman syndrome: Hysteroscopic adhesiolysis
- Polyps: Hysteroscopic polypectomy

### Male Factor
- Varicocele: Varicocelectomy (if clinical varicocele + abnormal semen)
- Mild male factor: IUI with partner's sperm
- Severe oligospermia: IVF with ICSI
- Obstructive azoospermia: TESA/PESA + ICSI or vasoepididymostomy
- Non-obstructive azoospermia: Micro-TESE + ICSI or donor sperm

### Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART)
- **IUI**: Washed sperm placed in uterus; for mild male factor, unexplained, cervical factor
- **IVF**: Ovarian stimulation → egg retrieval → fertilization in lab → embryo transfer
- **ICSI**: Single sperm injected into oocyte; for severe male factor
- **Donor gametes**: Oocyte donation (POI), sperm donation (azoospermia)
- **Surrogacy**: Uterine factor infertility (MRKH, absent uterus)

### Unexplained Infertility
- Lifestyle optimization → IUI (3-6 cycles) → IVF

## Complications

- OHSS (Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome) — with gonadotropin use
- Multiple pregnancy (with ovulation induction/IUI)
- Ectopic pregnancy risk with tubal disease
- Psychological stress, relationship strain
- Financial burden of ART
