# Surrogacy Legal Aspects — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Commercial surrogacy BANNED in India (Surrogacy Regulation Act 2021). Only altruistic gestational surrogacy permitted for Indian married couples. Surrogate must be close relative, 25-35 years, married with child, only once. Intending couple: no surviving child, medical indication needed. 2023 amendment allows single women (widow/divorcee, 35-45). ART Act 2021 regulates clinics and gamete banks.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Commercial surrogacy BANNED in India (2021 Act)
- ★ Only ALTRUISTIC GESTATIONAL surrogacy permitted
- ★ Traditional surrogacy (surrogate's egg) is BANNED
- ★ Surrogate: close relative, age 25-35, married with child, ONCE in lifetime
- ★ Intending couple: Indian, married, female 25-50, male 26-55, no surviving child
- ★ 2023 amendment: single woman (widow/divorcee) aged 35-45 can commission surrogacy
- ★ Foreigners/NRIs/PIOs/OCIs CANNOT commission surrogacy in India
- ★ Insurance for surrogate: 36 months from embryo transfer
- ★ Child is legitimate child of intending parents (not surrogate)
- ★ Penalty for commercial surrogacy: up to 10 years imprisonment + ₹10 lakhs fine
- ★ ART Act 2021: regulates ART clinics, gamete banks, donor anonymity

## Mnemonics

**Surrogate eligibility — "CARMO":** Close relative, Age 25-35, Related (married with child), Medical fitness, Once only

**Intending couple — "MINAC":** Married, Indian, No surviving child, Age (F:25-50, M:26-55), Certificate of essentiality

**BANNED — "CAST":** Commercial, Advertising, Sex selection, Traditional surrogacy
