# Surrogacy — Legal Aspects

## Overview

Surrogacy is an arrangement where a woman (surrogate mother) agrees to carry and deliver a child for another person or couple (intending parents). India was once a global hub for commercial surrogacy until legislation was enacted to regulate it. The Surrogacy (Regulation) Act, 2021 and the Assisted Reproductive Technology (Regulation) Act, 2021 are landmark Indian laws governing surrogacy and ART respectively.

## Key Points

- **Commercial surrogacy is BANNED in India** (Surrogacy Regulation Act, 2021)
- Only **altruistic surrogacy** permitted
- Surrogate must be a close relative of the intending couple
- Intending couple must be Indian married heterosexual couple
- Single parents, live-in couples, same-sex couples CANNOT commission surrogacy
- National/State Surrogacy Boards established for regulation
- ART (Regulation) Act, 2021 regulates ART clinics and banks

## Definition & Classification

### Types of Surrogacy
| Type | Definition | Legal Status in India |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| Traditional surrogacy | Surrogate's own egg + intending father's sperm (genetic mother = surrogate) | Banned |
| Gestational surrogacy | Intending parents' embryo transferred to surrogate (no genetic link to surrogate) | Permitted (altruistic only) |
| Commercial surrogacy | Surrogate compensated beyond medical expenses | Banned |
| Altruistic surrogacy | No monetary compensation beyond medical expenses and insurance | Permitted |

### Key Terminology
- **Intending couple:** Married Indian couple who commission the surrogacy
- **Intending woman:** Single woman (widow/divorcee, 35-45 yrs) — allowed under 2023 amendment
- **Surrogate mother:** Woman who carries the child
- **Surrogacy clinic:** Registered clinic conducting surrogacy procedures

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Medical Indications for Surrogacy
- Absent uterus (Mayer-Rokitansky-Küster-Hauser syndrome)
- Hysterectomy (post-cancer, obstetric emergency)
- Repeated IVF failure
- Medical conditions making pregnancy dangerous
- Recurrent pregnancy loss
- Asherman syndrome (severe, refractory)

## Management

### The Surrogacy (Regulation) Act, 2021 — Key Provisions

#### Eligibility of Intending Couple
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Nationality | Indian citizens |
| Marital status | Legally married |
| Age (female) | 25-50 years |
| Age (male) | 26-55 years |
| Children | No surviving child (biological, adopted, or surrogate) — exception: child with mental/physical disability or life-threatening disorder |
| Medical indication | Certificate of essentiality from District Medical Board |

#### 2023 Amendment
- **Intending woman** (single): widow or divorcee, age 35-45 years, can also commission surrogacy
- Can use donor gametes (earlier donor gametes were restricted)

#### Eligibility of Surrogate Mother
| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Relationship | Close relative of intending couple |
| Age | 25-35 years |
| Marital status | Married with at least one child of her own |
| Number of times | Only ONCE in lifetime |
| Medical/psychological fitness | Certified fit |
| Written consent | Informed consent mandatory |
| Insurance | Medical insurance for 36 months (from embryo transfer) |

#### Prohibited Activities
- Commercial surrogacy
- Sale/purchase of human embryo or gametes
- Exploitation of surrogate mother
- Sex selection
- Abandonment of child born through surrogacy
- Surrogacy for foreigners/NRIs/OCIs/PIOs
- Advertising for surrogacy

#### Regulatory Bodies
| Body | Role |
|------|------|
| National Surrogacy Board | Policy, guidelines, supervision |
| State Surrogacy Board | State-level implementation |
| Appropriate Authority | District-level registration, enforcement |

#### Registration
- All surrogacy clinics must register with Appropriate Authority
- Registration valid for 5 years
- Maintenance of records for 25 years

#### Parentage and Legal Status
- Intending couple are legal parents from birth
- Surrogate has no parental rights
- Child has same legal status as biological child
- Birth certificate in name of intending parents

#### Penalties
- Commercial surrogacy: imprisonment up to 10 years + fine up to ₹10 lakhs
- Abandoning surrogate child: similar penalties
- Exploitation of surrogate: imprisonment + fine

### ART (Regulation) Act, 2021 — Key Points
- Regulates ART clinics and ART banks (sperm/oocyte/embryo banks)
- Mandatory registration of all ART clinics
- Written informed consent from all parties
- Donor anonymity maintained
- Gamete donor: woman 23-35 years (max 7 oocyte retrievals lifetime); man 21-55 years
- Rights of children born through ART: legitimate children of commissioning couple
- National and State ART Boards for regulation

### International Comparison
| Country | Status |
|---------|--------|
| India | Altruistic gestational only (2021) |
| UK | Altruistic only; surrogate is legal mother until parental order |
| USA | Varies by state; commercial allowed in some |
| Australia | Altruistic only (commercial banned) |
| France/Germany | All surrogacy banned |
| Ukraine/Russia | Commercial surrogacy was permitted |

## Complications

### Ethical Issues
- Commodification of women's bodies (commercial surrogacy)
- Exploitation of economically disadvantaged women
- Psychological impact on surrogate
- Child welfare and identity issues
- Restrictive criteria may deny access to deserving individuals
- Exclusion of LGBTQ+ individuals and single men

### Legal Complications
- Cross-border surrogacy disputes (pre-ban cases)
- Citizenship issues for children born to foreign parents
- Abandonment of children with disabilities
- Breach of surrogacy agreement
