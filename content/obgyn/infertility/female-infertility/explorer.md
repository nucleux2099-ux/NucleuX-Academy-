# Female Infertility

## Overview

Infertility is defined as the inability to conceive after 12 months of regular, unprotected intercourse (6 months if woman >35 years). It affects approximately 10-15% of couples. Female factors contribute to about 40-50% of cases. A systematic evaluation of ovulation, tubal patency, uterine cavity, and peritoneal factors is essential. Treatment depends on the underlying cause and may range from ovulation induction to assisted reproductive technology (ART).

## Key Points

- Definition: failure to conceive after 12 months (6 months if >35 years)
- Female factors account for 40-50% of infertility
- Ovulatory dysfunction is the most common female cause
- Tubal factor: most commonly due to PID/Chlamydia
- Both partners must be evaluated simultaneously
- Age is the single most important prognostic factor (ovarian reserve declines with age)

## Definition & Classification

### Types
| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Primary infertility | Never conceived |
| Secondary infertility | Previous conception (live birth, abortion, ectopic) but unable to conceive again |
| Subfertility | Reduced fertility (takes longer but may conceive) |
| Fecundability | Probability of conception per cycle (~20-25% in healthy couples) |

### Causes by Category
| Factor | Percentage | Examples |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Ovulatory | 25-40% | PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhea, hyperprolactinemia, POI |
| Tubal/peritoneal | 25-35% | PID, endometriosis, previous surgery, TB |
| Uterine | 5-10% | Fibroids (submucosal), polyps, Asherman syndrome, Müllerian anomalies |
| Cervical | 3-5% | Cervical stenosis, hostile mucus |
| Unexplained | 10-15% | Normal investigation results |
| Male factor | 25-40% | Often coexists |

### WHO Classification of Ovulatory Disorders
| Group | Cause | FSH | Estrogen |
|-------|-------|-----|----------|
| I | Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism (hypothalamic) | Low | Low |
| II | Normogonadotropic (PCOS most common) | Normal | Normal |
| III | Hypergonadotropic hypogonadism (ovarian failure/POI) | High | Low |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Ovulatory Dysfunction
- **PCOS** (most common cause of anovulatory infertility): hyperandrogenism, oligo/anovulation, polycystic ovaries
- **Hypothalamic amenorrhea:** stress, weight loss, exercise, eating disorders → low GnRH
- **Hyperprolactinemia:** prolactinoma, drugs → suppresses GnRH pulsatility
- **Thyroid disorders:** both hypo- and hyperthyroidism
- **POI (Premature Ovarian Insufficiency):** <40 years, FSH >40

### Tubal Factor
- Post-PID adhesions and tubal occlusion (Chlamydia #1 cause)
- Endometriosis
- Genital tuberculosis (important in India — causes rigid, beaded "tobacco pouch" tubes)
- Previous ectopic/tubal surgery
- Tubal ligation (sterilization)

### Uterine Factor
- Submucosal fibroids, endometrial polyps
- Asherman syndrome (intrauterine adhesions)
- Müllerian anomalies (septate uterus — most common, most treatable)
- Adenomyosis

### Peritoneal Factor
- Endometriosis (distorts anatomy, toxic peritoneal environment)
- Pelvic adhesions

## Clinical Features

### History
- Duration of infertility, primary vs secondary
- Menstrual history (cycle regularity → ovulatory status)
- Sexual history (frequency, timing, dyspareunia)
- Obstetric history (previous pregnancies, abortions, ectopics)
- Medical: thyroid, diabetes, autoimmune, TB exposure
- Surgical: pelvic/abdominal surgery
- Drug history, lifestyle (smoking, alcohol, BMI)
- Partner: semen analysis results

### Examination
- BMI, hirsutism, acne, acanthosis nigricans (PCOS)
- Thyroid examination
- Breast examination (galactorrhea)
- Abdominal examination (scars, masses)
- Pelvic: cervical, uterine size/position, adnexal masses, tenderness, nodularity in POD (endometriosis)

## Diagnosis

### Basic Workup

#### 1. Ovulation Assessment
- **Mid-luteal progesterone** (Day 21 of 28-day cycle): >3 ng/mL confirms ovulation
- Basal body temperature charting (biphasic pattern)
- LH surge detection (urinary kits — Day 12-14)
- Ultrasound follicular tracking (most reliable)

#### 2. Ovarian Reserve Testing
| Test | Interpretation |
|------|---------------|
| Day 2-3 FSH | >10 IU/L: diminished reserve |
| AMH (Anti-Müllerian Hormone) | Best single marker; <1 ng/mL: poor reserve |
| Antral Follicle Count (AFC) | <5-7: poor reserve |
| Day 3 Estradiol | >80 pg/mL with normal FSH: early ovarian aging |

#### 3. Tubal Assessment
| Test | Description |
|------|-------------|
| HSG (Hysterosalpingography) | First-line; X-ray with contrast; assesses tubal patency and uterine cavity |
| Laparoscopy + chromopertubation | Gold standard for tubal assessment |
| Sonosalpingography (SSG) | Saline/air contrast USG — no radiation |
| Selective salpingography | For proximal tubal block |

#### 4. Uterine Assessment
- Transvaginal ultrasound
- Saline infusion sonography (SIS)
- Hysteroscopy (gold standard for intracavitary pathology)
- MRI (Müllerian anomalies, adenomyosis)

#### 5. Other Investigations
- Prolactin, TSH
- Testosterone, DHEAS (if hyperandrogenism)
- Karyotype (if POI)
- TB workup (in endemic areas): endometrial biopsy, PCR, culture

### Semen Analysis (Partner — MUST be done)
- Volume ≥1.5 mL, count ≥15 million/mL, motility ≥40%, morphology ≥4% (WHO 2010)

## Management

### General
- Lifestyle modification: weight loss if BMI >25 (even 5-10% improves ovulation in PCOS)
- Folic acid supplementation
- Smoking cessation
- Optimize timing of intercourse (every 2-3 days)

### Ovulatory Dysfunction
| WHO Group | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| I (Hypogonadotropic) | Pulsatile GnRH or gonadotropins (FSH + LH) |
| II (PCOS) | Letrozole (first-line) > clomiphene citrate > gonadotropins > LOD |
| III (POI) | Donor oocyte IVF |
| Hyperprolactinemia | Cabergoline/bromocriptine |

### Tubal Factor
- Proximal block: hysteroscopic tubal cannulation or IVF
- Distal block: laparoscopic fimbrioplasty/neosalpingostomy (limited success)
- Severe tubal damage: IVF (preferred)
- Hydrosalpinx: salpingectomy before IVF (improves success)

### Uterine Factor
- Submucosal fibroid: hysteroscopic myomectomy
- Polyps: hysteroscopic polypectomy
- Asherman syndrome: hysteroscopic adhesiolysis
- Septate uterus: hysteroscopic septoplasty

### Unexplained Infertility
- Stepwise: expectant management → IUI with ovarian stimulation → IVF

### Assisted Reproductive Technology (ART)
- **IUI:** Washed sperm placed in uterus; combined with ovarian stimulation
- **IVF:** Ovarian stimulation → oocyte retrieval → fertilization in lab → embryo transfer
- **ICSI:** Single sperm injected into oocyte (for severe male factor)

## Complications

- Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS) — with ovulation induction/IVF
- Multiple pregnancy
- Ectopic pregnancy
- Psychological distress
- Financial burden
