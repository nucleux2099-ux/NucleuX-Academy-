# Female Infertility — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Failure to conceive after 12 months (6 if >35). Female factors 40-50%. Most common cause: ovulatory dysfunction (PCOS #1). Tubal factor: PID/Chlamydia. Age is most important prognostic factor. Basic workup: Day 21 progesterone, AMH/AFC, HSG, semen analysis. PCOS treatment: letrozole (first-line). Severe tubal damage → IVF. Unexplained → IUI → IVF.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Most common cause of anovulatory infertility: PCOS (WHO Group II)
- ★ WHO Group I: low FSH, low estrogen (hypothalamic) → pulsatile GnRH
- ★ WHO Group III: high FSH (POI) → donor oocyte IVF
- ★ AMH: best single marker for ovarian reserve
- ★ Mid-luteal progesterone >3 ng/mL confirms ovulation
- ★ HSG: first-line tubal assessment; laparoscopy + chromopertubation = gold standard
- ★ Genital TB (India): beaded "tobacco pouch" tubes; diagnose by endometrial biopsy/PCR
- ★ Hydrosalpinx: salpingectomy BEFORE IVF improves outcomes
- ★ Letrozole > clomiphene for ovulation induction in PCOS (NEJM 2014)
- ★ Most common Müllerian anomaly causing infertility: septate uterus (hysteroscopic septoplasty)
- ★ Semen analysis: ≥15 million/mL, ≥40% motility, ≥4% morphology (WHO 2010)

## Mnemonics

**Infertility causes — "OUTCU":** Ovulatory, Uterine, Tubal, Cervical, Unexplained

**WHO ovulatory groups — "1-Low, 2-Normal, 3-High"** (FSH levels)

**Ovarian reserve tests — "FAA":** FSH (Day 2-3), AMH, AFC (Antral Follicle Count)

**PCOS treatment ladder — "LCGI":** Letrozole, Clomiphene, Gonadotropins, IVF
