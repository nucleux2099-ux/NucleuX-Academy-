# Contraception Methods — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Contraception ranges from natural methods (rhythm, LAM) to barrier, hormonal, IUCD, surgical, and emergency options. Cu-T 380A is the most effective reversible LARC. COCs are most popular worldwide. WHO MEC categories guide safe prescribing.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Most effective reversible contraception: Subdermal implant (Pearl Index 0.05%)
- ★ Cu-T 380A: Works 10 years, spermicidal copper + sterile inflammatory reaction
- ★ LNG-IUS (Mirena): Reduces menstrual bleeding, used in menorrhagia treatment
- ★ COC mechanism: Inhibits ovulation (primary), thickens cervical mucus, thins endometrium
- ★ DMPA: Causes amenorrhea, bone density loss (reversible), delayed return to fertility (up to 18 months)
- ★ LAM criteria: ALL three needed — exclusive breastfeeding + amenorrhea + <6 months postpartum
- ★ Most effective emergency contraception: Copper IUCD (up to 5 days)
- ★ Levonorgestrel EC: Within 72 hours, delays ovulation
- ★ COC contraindicated: Migraine with aura, smoking >35 years, active liver disease
- ★ Vasectomy: Effective only after 20 ejaculations or 3 months (azoospermia confirmed)

## Mnemonics
**ACHES** — Warning signs on COC:
- **A**bdominal pain (hepatic vein thrombosis)
- **C**hest pain (PE)
- **H**eadache severe (stroke)
- **E**ye problems (retinal vein thrombosis)
- **S**evere leg pain (DVT)

**LAM needs ALL 3**: Lactation + Amenorrhea + <6 Months
