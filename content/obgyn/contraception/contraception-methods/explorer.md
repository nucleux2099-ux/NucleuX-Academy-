# Contraception Methods — Overview

## Overview
Contraception refers to the deliberate prevention of pregnancy using various methods, devices, agents, or surgical procedures. The choice of contraceptive depends on efficacy, safety, reversibility, convenience, cost, and individual patient factors.

India's National Family Planning Programme promotes both spacing and limiting methods. Understanding the full spectrum of contraceptive options is essential for counseling and clinical practice.

## Key Points
- Ideal contraceptive: Safe, effective, reversible, acceptable, inexpensive, easy to use
- Pearl Index measures contraceptive failure rate per 100 woman-years
- Combined oral contraceptives are the most commonly used reversible method worldwide
- IUCD (Cu-T 380A) is the most effective long-acting reversible contraceptive
- Lactational amenorrhea method (LAM) is effective for 6 months postpartum if fully breastfeeding
- Emergency contraception should be offered within 72-120 hours of unprotected intercourse

## Classification

### Natural / Behavioral Methods
- **Calendar/rhythm method**: Avoid intercourse during fertile window
- **Basal body temperature**: Temperature rise of 0.5°F after ovulation
- **Cervical mucus method** (Billings): Spinnbarkeit mucus indicates fertility
- **Lactational amenorrhea (LAM)**: Effective if exclusive breastfeeding, amenorrheic, <6 months postpartum
- **Coitus interruptus**: Withdrawal before ejaculation

### Barrier Methods
- **Male condom**: 85-98% effective, also prevents STIs
- **Female condom**: Less effective than male condom
- **Diaphragm**: Used with spermicide, fitted by clinician
- **Cervical cap**: Smaller than diaphragm, covers cervix

### Hormonal Methods
- **Combined oral contraceptives (COC)**: Estrogen + progesterone, inhibit ovulation
- **Progesterone-only pills (POP)**: "Mini-pill," thickens cervical mucus
- **Injectable**: DMPA (Depo-Provera) 3-monthly, NET-EN 2-monthly
- **Implants**: Implanon/Nexplanon — subdermal, 3-5 year efficacy
- **Transdermal patch**: Weekly application
- **Vaginal ring**: NuvaRing, monthly use

### Intrauterine Devices
- **Copper IUCD** (Cu-T 380A): 10-year efficacy, works by spermicidal copper ions + inflammatory reaction
- **Hormonal IUS** (Mirena/LNG-IUS): 5-year efficacy, local progesterone, reduces menstrual bleeding

### Surgical (Permanent)
- **Female sterilization**: Tubectomy (laparoscopic or mini-lap)
- **Male sterilization**: Vasectomy (no-scalpel technique preferred)

### Emergency Contraception
- **Levonorgestrel** 1.5 mg single dose (within 72 hours)
- **Ulipristal acetate** 30 mg (effective up to 120 hours)
- **Copper IUCD**: Most effective EC, can be inserted up to 5 days

## WHO Medical Eligibility Criteria (MEC)
- **Category 1**: No restriction
- **Category 2**: Advantages outweigh risks
- **Category 3**: Risks outweigh advantages
- **Category 4**: Unacceptable health risk (absolute contraindication)

### COC Absolute Contraindications (Category 4)
- Age >35 with smoking >15 cigarettes/day
- Hypertension (>160/100)
- History of DVT/PE
- Migraine with aura
- Breast cancer (current)
- Liver disease (active)

## Management of Side Effects
- **Breakthrough bleeding on COC**: Reassure (common in first 3 months), consider dose adjustment
- **IUCD expulsion**: Reinsert or switch method
- **DMPA weight gain**: Counsel and monitor
- **Post-insertion IUCD pain**: NSAIDs, rule out perforation

## India-Specific Programs
- Antara (injectable MPA) — free in government facilities
- Chhaya (emergency contraceptive pills)
- IUCD 375 and Cu-T 380A provided free
- Interval and postpartum IUCD insertion promoted
- Compensation scheme for sterilization
