# Natural Family Planning

## Overview

Natural family planning (NFP), also called fertility awareness-based methods (FABMs), involves identifying the fertile window in a woman's menstrual cycle and avoiding intercourse during that period to prevent pregnancy. These methods rely on physiological signs of ovulation and do not involve any hormones, devices, or surgical procedures.

NFP methods are acceptable to all religions and cultures, have no side effects, and are free of cost. However, they require motivation, education, regular cycles, and cooperation from both partners. The typical-use failure rates are higher than most modern contraceptive methods.

These methods can also be used in reverse — to identify the fertile window for couples trying to conceive. The calendar method (rhythm method), basal body temperature (BBT), cervical mucus method (Billings), and symptothermal method are the main NFP techniques.

## Key Points

- No side effects, no cost, acceptable to all religions
- Requires regular menstrual cycles and motivated couples
- Calendar (rhythm) method — oldest and least reliable
- BBT rises 0.5°C after ovulation (progesterone effect)
- Cervical mucus becomes thin, clear, and stretchable (spinnbarkeit) at ovulation
- Symptothermal method combines BBT + mucus — most effective NFP method

## Definition & Classification

**Calendar/Rhythm Method (Ogino-Knaus):**
- Fertile period calculated from past 6-12 cycles
- First fertile day = shortest cycle – 18
- Last fertile day = longest cycle – 11
- Abstinence during this window

**Basal Body Temperature (BBT):**
- Temperature taken daily at same time before rising
- Rises 0.3-0.5°C after ovulation due to progesterone
- Infertile period begins after 3 consecutive days of elevated temperature
- Only identifies post-ovulatory infertile period

**Cervical Mucus Method (Billings):**
- Monitors changes in cervical mucus throughout cycle
- Post-menstrual dry days → sticky/cloudy mucus → clear, thin, stretchable (peak mucus = ovulation) → thick, opaque
- Peak day: last day of clear, slippery mucus
- Infertile period begins 4 days after peak day

**Symptothermal Method:**
- Combines BBT + cervical mucus + other symptoms (mittelschmerz, breast tenderness)
- Most effective NFP method
- Typical use failure rate: 2-5% (best among NFP)

**Standard Days Method (SDM):**
- For cycles of 26-32 days
- Avoid intercourse on days 8-19
- CycleBeads used for tracking

**Lactational Amenorrhea Method (LAM):**
- Effective for 6 months postpartum if:
  1. Exclusive breastfeeding
  2. Amenorrheic
  3. <6 months postpartum
- All three criteria must be met
- Failure rate: 2% when used correctly

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

**Physiological basis:**
- Ovulation occurs 14 days before the next period (luteal phase is constant)
- Ovum survives 24 hours after ovulation
- Sperm survive up to 5 days in female reproductive tract
- Fertile window: 5 days before ovulation + day of ovulation + 1 day after = approximately 6-7 days

**Hormonal basis:**
- Estrogen → thin, watery, stretchable cervical mucus (spinnbarkeit)
- Progesterone → thick, sticky mucus; raised BBT; closed cervix

## Clinical Features

Not applicable — these are behavioral methods.

**Advantages:**
- No side effects
- No cost
- Acceptable to Catholic Church and most religions
- Empowers women to understand their cycles
- Can be used to achieve or avoid pregnancy

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires regular cycles
- Requires abstinence during fertile period
- High typical-use failure rates (except symptothermal)
- Not suitable for irregular cycles (PCOS, perimenopause)
- Does not protect against STIs

## Diagnosis

Assessment of suitability:
- Regular menstrual cycles (26-32 days)
- Motivated and educated couple
- Ability to track and record signs
- No conditions causing irregular cycles
- Willingness to abstain during fertile window

## Management

**Counseling:**
- Teach the chosen method thoroughly
- Provide charts/tools (BBT thermometer, CycleBeads)
- First 3-6 cycles: use backup method while learning
- Combine methods (symptothermal) for better efficacy

**Failure rates (typical use per 100 women-years):**
- Calendar method: 25
- BBT alone: 20
- Cervical mucus alone: 20
- Symptothermal: 2-5
- Standard Days Method: 12
- LAM: 2

**When to switch methods:**
- Irregular cycles developing
- Difficulty tracking signs
- Unacceptable failure for the couple
- Postpartum/breastfeeding (LAM may be appropriate)

## Complications & Prognosis

- No physical complications (no hormones/devices)
- Main risk: unintended pregnancy due to incorrect use
- No effect on future fertility
- Psychological stress from abstinence periods
- Relationship strain if partners disagree
