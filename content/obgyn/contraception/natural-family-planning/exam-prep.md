# Natural Family Planning — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Fertility awareness methods to identify and avoid the fertile window. Calendar, BBT, cervical mucus, and symptothermal are main methods. Symptothermal is most effective NFP method. No side effects but high typical-use failure rates.

## High Yield Points

- ★ Calendar method: first fertile day = shortest cycle – 18; last = longest cycle – 11
- ★ BBT rises 0.3-0.5°C AFTER ovulation (progesterone effect)
- ★ Spinnbarkeit = stretchable, clear cervical mucus at ovulation (estrogen effect)
- ★ Symptothermal method = most effective NFP (combines BBT + mucus)
- ★ LAM criteria: exclusive breastfeeding + amenorrhea + <6 months postpartum
- ★ Ovum survives 24h; sperm survive up to 5 days
- ★ Luteal phase is constant (14 days); follicular phase varies
- ★ Standard Days Method: avoid days 8-19 (for 26-32 day cycles)
- ★ Calendar method also called Ogino-Knaus method / rhythm method
- ★ NFP acceptable to Catholic Church

## Mnemonics

**LAM — 3 conditions (ALL must be met):**
- L — Lactation (exclusive breastfeeding)
- A — Amenorrhea (no periods yet)
- M — Months <6 postpartum

**Mucus changes through cycle: "DSTC"**
- D — Dry days (post-menses)
- S — Sticky, cloudy mucus
- T — Thin, clear, stretchable (FERTILE — ovulation)
- C — Cloudy, thick again (post-ovulation)
