# Female Sterilization — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Permanent contraception by tubal occlusion. Modified Pomeroy is the most common puerperal method. Laparoscopic sterilization (Falope ring/Filshie clip) is preferred for interval procedures. Pearl index 0.5.

## High Yield Points

- ★ Most popular family planning method in India — tubectomy
- ★ Modified Pomeroy — most common puerperal method; uses plain catgut
- ★ Irving method — lowest failure rate (proximal stump buried in myometrium)
- ★ Filshie clip — most commonly used clip worldwide
- ★ Puerperal sterilization — within 48 hours, infraumbilical minilaparotomy
- ★ Interval sterilization — proliferative phase, laparoscopic approach
- ★ If sterilization fails → 30% chance of ectopic pregnancy
- ★ Minimum age for sterilization in India: 22 years
- ★ Best reversal results: after clip methods (least tissue damage)
- ★ Tubal sterilization may reduce ovarian cancer risk by 20-30%

## Mnemonics

**Techniques with increasing tissue destruction:**
Clip < Ring < Cautery < Excision
*(Less destruction = better reversibility)*

**"PUMP" — Puerperal sterilization:**
- P — Pomeroy (modified)
- U — Under spinal/local
- M — Minilaparotomy
- P — Postpartum within 48h

## Common MCQ Topics

- Modified Pomeroy — why plain catgut? (absorbs → stumps separate → less failure)
- Irving vs Pomeroy vs Uchida — where is stump buried?
- Timing: puerperal (48h) vs interval (proliferative phase)
- Post-sterilization ectopic pregnancy risk
- Most common method in India
- Filshie clip vs Falope ring
