# Male Sterilization (Vasectomy) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Vasectomy is the simplest and most effective permanent contraception (Pearl index 0.1). No-scalpel vasectomy is the preferred technique. Not immediately effective — azoospermia confirmed at 3 months.

## High Yield Points

- ★ Pearl index 0.1 — more effective than female sterilization (0.5)
- ★ No-scalpel vasectomy developed in China by Dr. Li Shunqiang (1974)
- ★ NOT immediately effective — azoospermia at 3 months/20 ejaculations
- ★ Hematoma is the most common complication
- ★ RISUG — Indian innovation by Prof. S.K. Guha (reversible male contraception)
- ★ Ejaculate volume barely changes (95% from seminal vesicles/prostate)
- ★ No proven link with prostate cancer or cardiovascular disease
- ★ Minimum age in India: 25 years for vasectomy
- ★ Reversal (vasovasostomy): 80-90% patency but only 40-70% pregnancy rate
- ★ Fascial interposition reduces failure rate

## Mnemonics

**"NSV" advantages:**
- N — No scalpel, No suture
- S — Safer (less bleeding, infection)
- V — Very effective (Pearl index 0.1)
