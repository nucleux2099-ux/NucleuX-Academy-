# Cervical Cancer

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Gynecology 4th Ed; Shaw's Textbook of Gynaecology 17th Ed; FIGO Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Cervical cancer is the fourth most common cancer in women worldwide and remains the leading cause of cancer death among women in developing countries. It is predominantly caused by persistent infection with high-risk Human Papillomavirus (HPV), particularly types 16 and 18. The disease progresses through well-defined precancerous stages (CIN), offering opportunities for screening and prevention. The transformation zone is the primary site of origin.

## Key Points

- **Epidemiology**: 4th most common cancer in women globally; 2nd in developing countries
- **Etiology**: HPV causes >99% of cervical cancers
- **High-risk HPV types**: 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 (16 & 18 cause 70%)
- **Histology**: Squamous cell carcinoma (80%), Adenocarcinoma (15-20%)
- **Site of origin**: Transformation zone (squamocolumnar junction)
- **Prevention**: HPV vaccination + screening programs
- **Screening**: Pap smear, HPV testing, VIA

## Risk Factors

| Factor | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| **HPV infection** | Necessary cause; E6/E7 oncoproteins |
| Early sexual debut | ↑ HPV exposure during metaplasia |
| Multiple sexual partners | ↑ HPV exposure |
| High-risk male partner | ↑ HPV transmission |
| Multiparity | Hormonal effects, cervical trauma |
| Smoking | Direct carcinogen, ↓ immune response |
| Immunosuppression (HIV) | Impaired HPV clearance |
| OCP use (>5 years) | Hormonal effects |
| Low socioeconomic status | Poor access to screening |
| DES exposure in utero | Rare; clear cell adenocarcinoma |

## HPV Biology

### Oncogenic Mechanism
- **E6 protein**: Inactivates p53 (tumor suppressor) → Prevents apoptosis
- **E7 protein**: Inactivates pRb → Uncontrolled cell cycle progression

### Natural History
```
HPV infection → 90% clear spontaneously (1-2 years)
      ↓
Persistent infection (10%) → CIN 1 → CIN 2 → CIN 3 → Invasive cancer
                                            (10-20 years)
```

## Precancerous Lesions (CIN)

| Classification | Description | Regression | Progression to Cancer |
|----------------|-------------|------------|----------------------|
| **CIN 1** (LSIL) | Mild dysplasia, lower 1/3 | 60-80% | <1% |
| **CIN 2** | Moderate dysplasia, lower 2/3 | 40-50% | 5% |
| **CIN 3** (HSIL) | Severe dysplasia/CIS, full thickness | 30% | 12-30% |

## Clinical Features

### Preclinical Stage
- Usually asymptomatic
- Detected by screening

### Early Symptoms
- **Postcoital bleeding** (most common presenting symptom)
- Intermenstrual bleeding
- Postmenopausal bleeding
- Foul-smelling vaginal discharge (blood-tinged, watery)

### Advanced Disease
- Pelvic pain
- Low back pain (nerve involvement)
- Leg edema (lymphatic obstruction)
- Urinary symptoms (frequency, hematuria, fistula)
- Rectal symptoms (tenesmus, bleeding, fistula)
- Weight loss, anemia

### Signs (Speculum Examination)
| Early | Advanced |
|-------|----------|
| Erosion, friable cervix | Ulcerated, cauliflower-like growth |
| Bleeds on touch | Barrel-shaped cervix (endocervical) |
| Indurated area | Fixed pelvis, parametrial thickening |

## Diagnosis

### Screening Methods
| Method | Description | Sensitivity |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Pap smear** | Cytology (conventional or liquid-based) | 60-80% |
| **HPV DNA test** | Detects high-risk HPV | >90% |
| **Co-testing** | Pap + HPV | Highest sensitivity |
| **VIA** (Visual Inspection with Acetic acid) | Low-resource settings | 60-70% |
| **VILI** (Visual Inspection with Lugol's Iodine) | Low-resource settings | 60-70% |

### Bethesda System (Cytology Reporting)
| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| NILM | Negative for intraepithelial lesion or malignancy |
| ASC-US | Atypical squamous cells of undetermined significance |
| ASC-H | Atypical squamous cells, cannot exclude HSIL |
| LSIL | Low-grade squamous intraepithelial lesion |
| HSIL | High-grade squamous intraepithelial lesion |
| SCC | Squamous cell carcinoma |
| AGC | Atypical glandular cells |
| AIS | Adenocarcinoma in situ |

### Colposcopy
**Indications**: Abnormal Pap, positive HPV, suspicious cervix

| Feature | LSIL | HSIL |
|---------|------|------|
| **Acetowhite** | Faint, transient | Dense, opaque |
| **Margins** | Indistinct | Sharp, rolled |
| **Vessels** | Fine punctation | Coarse punctation, mosaic |
| **Iodine (Schiller)** | Partial uptake | Non-staining (Schiller positive) |

### Biopsy
- **Punch biopsy**: For visible lesions
- **Endocervical curettage (ECC)**: Sample endocervical canal
- **Cone biopsy/LEEP**: If colposcopy inadequate, discrepancy, or suspected invasion

### Staging Workup (Invasive Cancer)
| Investigation | Purpose |
|---------------|---------|
| Complete clinical exam | Stage determination (FIGO is clinical) |
| Chest X-ray | Lung metastases |
| IVP / USG | Hydronephrosis (Stage IIIB) |
| Cystoscopy | Bladder involvement (Stage IVA) |
| Proctoscopy | Rectal involvement (Stage IVA) |
| MRI pelvis | Parametrial, nodal involvement |
| PET-CT | Metastases, nodal staging |

## FIGO Staging (2018)

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **IA1** | Stromal invasion ≤3 mm depth |
| **IA2** | Stromal invasion >3 mm and ≤5 mm depth |
| **IB1** | Tumor ≤2 cm in greatest dimension |
| **IB2** | Tumor >2 cm and ≤4 cm |
| **IB3** | Tumor >4 cm |
| **IIA1** | Upper 2/3 vagina, ≤4 cm |
| **IIA2** | Upper 2/3 vagina, >4 cm |
| **IIB** | Parametrial involvement (not to pelvic wall) |
| **IIIA** | Lower 1/3 vagina |
| **IIIB** | Pelvic wall and/or hydronephrosis |
| **IIIC1** | Pelvic lymph node metastasis |
| **IIIC2** | Para-aortic lymph node metastasis |
| **IVA** | Bladder/rectal mucosa involvement |
| **IVB** | Distant metastasis |

## Management

### CIN Management
| Grade | Management |
|-------|------------|
| **CIN 1** | Observation (repeat Pap/colposcopy at 6-12 months) |
| **CIN 2/3** | Treatment (excision preferred over ablation) |

**Treatment Options for CIN:**
- **LEEP/LLETZ**: Loop electrosurgical excision - most common
- **Cold knife conization**: If adenocarcinoma suspected
- **Laser ablation**: If colposcopy satisfactory
- **Cryotherapy**: Low-resource settings

### Invasive Cancer Treatment
| Stage | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| **IA1** (no LVSI) | Cone biopsy / Simple hysterectomy |
| **IA1** (with LVSI) | Modified radical hysterectomy + pelvic LND |
| **IA2** | Radical hysterectomy + pelvic LND |
| **IB1-IIA1** | Radical hysterectomy + pelvic LND OR Chemoradiation |
| **IB2-IIA2** | Chemoradiation (preferred) OR Neo-adjuvant chemo + surgery |
| **IIB-IVA** | Concurrent chemoradiation |
| **IVB** | Palliative chemotherapy ± radiation |

### Radical Hysterectomy (Wertheim's)
- Uterus, cervix, upper vagina, parametria, uterosacral ligaments
- Bilateral pelvic lymphadenectomy
- Ovaries can be preserved in young women (SCC)

### Concurrent Chemoradiation
- **External beam radiation** + **Brachytherapy**
- **Cisplatin** 40 mg/m² weekly (radiosensitizer)
- Standard for stage IB2 and above

### Fertility-Sparing Options
- **Cone biopsy**: Stage IA1 without LVSI
- **Radical trachelectomy**: Stage IA2-IB1, tumor ≤2 cm

## Prevention

### HPV Vaccination
| Vaccine | Types Covered | Schedule |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| **Cervarix** (bivalent) | 16, 18 | 2-3 doses |
| **Gardasil** (quadrivalent) | 6, 11, 16, 18 | 2-3 doses |
| **Gardasil 9** (nonavalent) | 6, 11, 16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58 | 2-3 doses |

**Target age**: 9-14 years (before sexual debut)
**Catch-up**: Up to 26 years (45 in some countries)

### Screening Recommendations (ACOG)
| Age | Recommendation |
|-----|----------------|
| 21-29 years | Cytology every 3 years |
| 30-65 years | Cytology every 3 years OR HPV testing every 5 years OR Co-testing every 5 years |
| >65 years | Stop if adequate prior screening and low risk |

## Prognosis

| Stage | 5-Year Survival |
|-------|-----------------|
| IA | 95-100% |
| IB | 80-90% |
| IIA | 75-80% |
| IIB | 65-70% |
| IIIA-B | 35-50% |
| IV | 15-20% |

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **HPV 16 & 18 cause 70%** of cervical cancers; 16 is most common (SCC), 18 (adenocarcinoma)
2. **E6 inactivates p53, E7 inactivates pRb** - key oncogenic mechanism
3. **Transformation zone** is the site of origin
4. **Postcoital bleeding** is the most common presenting symptom
5. **FIGO staging is CLINICAL** (2018 update allows imaging and pathology to modify stage)
6. **Hydronephrosis = Stage IIIB** (pelvic wall involvement)
7. **Concurrent chemoradiation with cisplatin** is standard for stage IB2 and above
8. **Wertheim's hysterectomy** = Radical hysterectomy
9. **Schiller test positive** = Abnormal (non-staining with iodine)
10. **CIN 1 usually regresses**; CIN 2/3 requires treatment
11. **HPV vaccine is prophylactic**, not therapeutic
12. **Clear cell adenocarcinoma**: Associated with DES exposure in utero
13. **Barrel-shaped cervix**: Endocervical growth pattern
14. **Stage IVA**: Bladder or rectal mucosal involvement (not just proximity)
