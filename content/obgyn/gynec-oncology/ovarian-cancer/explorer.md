# Ovarian Cancer

## Overview

Ovarian cancer is the leading cause of death among gynecological malignancies and ranks third in incidence after cervical and uterine cancers. It is often called the "silent killer" due to late presentation. Epithelial ovarian cancer (EOC) accounts for 90% of all ovarian malignancies. Peak incidence is between 55-65 years.

## Key Points

- **Most lethal** gynecological malignancy
- 90% are epithelial; serous carcinoma is the most common subtype
- Risk factors: nulliparity, early menarche, late menopause, family history (BRCA1/2)
- Protective: OCP use, multiparity, breastfeeding, tubal ligation
- CA-125 is the most useful tumor marker for epithelial ovarian cancer
- Most present at advanced stage (III-IV)

## Definition & Classification

### Histological Classification (WHO)

#### Epithelial Tumors (90%)
| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| High-grade serous (HGSC) | Most common (70%), p53 mutation, BRCA-associated |
| Low-grade serous | Younger age, KRAS/BRAF mutations |
| Mucinous | Large size, unilateral, appendiceal primary must be excluded |
| Endometrioid | Associated with endometriosis, PTEN mutation |
| Clear cell | Associated with endometriosis, poor prognosis, chemo-resistant |
| Brenner (transitional) | Rare, usually benign |

#### Non-Epithelial Tumors (10%)
- **Germ cell tumors:** Dysgerminoma, yolk sac tumor, immature teratoma, choriocarcinoma
- **Sex cord-stromal tumors:** Granulosa cell tumor, Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor, fibroma-thecoma

### Dualistic Model (Kurman & Shih)
- **Type I:** Low-grade serous, mucinous, endometrioid, clear cell — slow growing, confined at diagnosis
- **Type II:** High-grade serous, carcinosarcoma — aggressive, p53 mutations, advanced at diagnosis

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors
- **Incessant ovulation hypothesis:** Nulliparity, early menarche, late menopause
- **Genetic:** BRCA1 (40% lifetime risk), BRCA2 (20%), Lynch syndrome
- **Hormonal:** HRT (slight increase), infertility drugs (controversial)
- **Environmental:** Talc use (debated), obesity

### Protective Factors
- Combined OCP use (40-50% risk reduction with 5+ years)
- Multiparity, breastfeeding
- Tubal ligation, hysterectomy
- Risk-reducing salpingo-oophorectomy (BRCA carriers)

### Pathogenesis
- HGSC now believed to originate from **fallopian tube fimbrial epithelium** (STIC lesions — serous tubal intraepithelial carcinoma)
- Type I tumors arise from borderline tumors or endometriosis

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms (often vague and late)
- Abdominal distension and bloating
- Pelvic/abdominal pain
- Early satiety, change in bowel habits
- Urinary frequency
- Weight loss, fatigue

### Signs
- Pelvic mass (solid, irregular, fixed, bilateral)
- Ascites
- Pleural effusion (Meigs syndrome with fibroma; malignant with carcinoma)
- Omental cake (palpable epigastric mass)
- Sister Mary Joseph nodule
- Virchow's node (left supraclavicular)

### Paraneoplastic Syndromes
- Hypercalcemia (small cell carcinoma)
- Cushing syndrome
- Cerebellar degeneration (anti-Yo antibodies)

## Diagnosis

### Tumor Markers
| Marker | Tumor Type |
|--------|------------|
| CA-125 | Epithelial (serous) |
| HE4 | Epithelial (complementary to CA-125) |
| AFP | Yolk sac tumor, immature teratoma |
| β-hCG | Choriocarcinoma, dysgerminoma (10%) |
| LDH | Dysgerminoma |
| Inhibin | Granulosa cell tumor |
| Estrogen | Granulosa cell, thecoma |
| Testosterone | Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor |

### ROMA Score
- Risk of Ovarian Malignancy Algorithm
- Uses CA-125 + HE4 + menopausal status

### RMI (Risk of Malignancy Index)
- RMI = Ultrasound score × Menopausal score × CA-125
- RMI >200 = high risk → refer to gynecologic oncologist

### Imaging
- **TVS:** First-line; assess morphology (solid areas, papillary projections, septations)
- **CT abdomen/pelvis:** For staging and surgical planning
- **MRI:** For indeterminate masses
- **PET-CT:** For recurrence detection

### Diagnosis is confirmed by **histopathology** (surgical specimen, NOT FNAC/biopsy to avoid tumor spillage)

## Management

### Surgical
- **Early stage (I-II):** Complete surgical staging (TAH + BSO + omentectomy + lymph node dissection + peritoneal biopsies + washings)
- **Advanced (III-IV):** Primary debulking surgery (maximal cytoreduction) + adjuvant chemotherapy
- **Fertility-sparing:** Unilateral salpingo-oophorectomy in Stage IA, Grade 1, young patients

### Chemotherapy
- Standard: **Paclitaxel + Carboplatin** (TC regimen), 6 cycles
- Neoadjuvant chemotherapy (NACT) + interval debulking: for unresectable disease
- Maintenance: Bevacizumab or PARP inhibitors (Olaparib) in BRCA-mutated

### PARP Inhibitors
- Olaparib, Niraparib, Rucaparib
- Maintenance therapy in platinum-sensitive recurrence and BRCA-mutated tumors
- Exploit **synthetic lethality** in homologous recombination deficient tumors

### Recurrent Disease
- Platinum-sensitive (relapse >6 months): Re-challenge with platinum-based chemo
- Platinum-resistant (relapse <6 months): Non-platinum agents (liposomal doxorubicin, topotecan, gemcitabine)

## Complications

- Bowel obstruction (most common cause of death)
- Malignant ascites
- Pleural effusion
- DVT/PE
- Chemotherapy toxicity
- Psychosocial impact

## Screening

- **General population:** No effective screening recommended (UKCTOCS trial — no mortality benefit)
- **High-risk (BRCA carriers):** 6-monthly CA-125 + TVS from age 30; risk-reducing BSO by 35-40 years
