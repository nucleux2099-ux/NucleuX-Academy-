# Ovarian Cancer — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Most lethal gynecological malignancy. 90% epithelial, most commonly high-grade serous (from fallopian tube fimbriae). BRCA1/2 are key genetic risk factors. Presents late with ascites, abdominal mass. CA-125 is the primary marker. Treatment: surgery (debulking) + paclitaxel-carboplatin chemotherapy. PARP inhibitors (olaparib) for BRCA-mutated maintenance.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Most common ovarian malignancy = High-grade serous carcinoma
- ★ Origin of HGSC = Fallopian tube fimbrial epithelium (STIC lesion)
- ★ BRCA1 = 40% lifetime risk; BRCA2 = 20%
- ★ OCP use for 5+ years = 40-50% risk reduction
- ★ CA-125 elevated in epithelial; AFP in yolk sac; LDH in dysgerminoma; Inhibin in granulosa cell
- ★ RMI >200 = refer to oncologist
- ★ NO role of FNAC/biopsy preoperatively (risk of spillage/upstaging)
- ★ Platinum-sensitive recurrence = relapse >6 months after completing chemo
- ★ PARP inhibitors work via synthetic lethality in BRCA-mutated/HRD tumors
- ★ Meigs syndrome = fibroma + ascites + pleural effusion (BENIGN)
- ★ Type I (low grade, slow) vs Type II (high grade, aggressive) — Kurman model

## Mnemonics

**"BRCA Saves" — Protective factors:** Breastfeeding, Reduced ovulation (OCP), Children (multiparity), Ablation of tubes (tubal ligation)

**Tumor markers — "CLAIM":**
- **C**A-125 → Epithelial
- **L**DH → Dysgerminoma
- **A**FP → Yolk sac
- **I**nhibin → Granulosa cell
- **M** (β-hCG) → Choriocarcinoma

**Type I vs Type II:** "Type ONE = slONE (slow), Type TWO = aggressTWO (aggressive)"
