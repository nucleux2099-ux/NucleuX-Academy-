# Ovarian Tumors Classification — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Three main categories: epithelial (90% of malignant), germ cell (most common in young), sex cord-stromal (hormone-producing). Each has benign, borderline, and malignant variants. Histological hallmarks and tumor markers are high-yield. Dermoid cyst = most common benign tumor in reproductive age. Dysgerminoma = most common malignant germ cell tumor. Granulosa cell = most common malignant sex cord-stromal tumor.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Psammoma bodies = Serous tumors (also papillary thyroid, meningioma)
- ★ Schiller-Duval bodies = Yolk sac tumor
- ★ Call-Exner bodies = Granulosa cell tumor
- ★ Signet ring cells = Krukenberg tumor (stomach primary)
- ★ Coffee bean nuclei = Brenner tumor
- ★ Hobnail cells = Clear cell carcinoma
- ★ Reinke crystals = Sertoli-Leydig (also Leydig cell tumor of testis)
- ★ Meigs syndrome = Fibroma + ascites + R pleural effusion (BENIGN)
- ★ Pseudomyxoma peritonei = Mucinous tumors (rule out appendiceal primary)
- ★ Struma ovarii = Thyroid tissue in teratoma → hyperthyroidism
- ★ Gonadoblastoma → dysgenetic gonads (46,XY) → prophylactic gonadectomy
- ★ BEP regimen for malignant germ cell tumors (fertility-sparing surgery)
- ★ Granulosa cell tumor: late recurrence up to 20 years; follow with inhibin B

## Mnemonics

**Histological bodies — "PS-CC-HR":**
- **P**sammoma → **S**erous
- **S**chiller-Duval → Yolk **S**ac
- **C**all-Exner → Granulosa **C**ell
- **C**offee bean → Brenner
- **H**obnail → Clear cell
- **R**einke → Sertoli-Leydig

**Germ cell markers — "DALCE":**
- **D**ysgerminoma → LDH
- **A**FP → Yolk sac
- **L**DH → Dysgerminoma
- **C**horiocarcinoma → β-hCG
- **E**mbryonal → AFP + β-hCG

**Estrogen-producing tumors — "GET":** Granulosa, Endometrioid (association), Thecoma

## Common MCQ Topics

1. Histological body matched to tumor type (MOST tested)
2. Tumor marker matched to tumor type
3. Most common ovarian tumor in children (germ cell)
4. Krukenberg tumor origin and histology
5. Meigs syndrome components
6. Borderline tumor — no stromal invasion, excellent prognosis
7. Dermoid cyst malignant transformation → SCC
8. Endometriosis-associated tumors (endometrioid, clear cell)
9. BEP regimen for germ cell tumors
10. Granulosa cell tumor → endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
