# Cervical Cancer — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Most common gynecological cancer in India. Caused by HPV 16/18. SCC is most common type (80-85%). Clinically staged (FIGO). Early stages: surgery; IIB onwards: concurrent chemoradiation with cisplatin.

## High Yield Points

- ★ HPV 16 → SCC; HPV 18 → Adenocarcinoma
- ★ E6 inactivates p53; E7 inactivates Rb
- ★ Post-coital bleeding — most common early symptom
- ★ Cervical cancer is CLINICALLY staged (EUA mandatory)
- ★ Stage IIB onwards → concurrent chemoradiation (cisplatin weekly)
- ★ Wertheim's radical hysterectomy — for Stage IB1-IIA1
- ★ Most common cause of death — uremia (bilateral ureteric obstruction)
- ★ Barrel-shaped cervix — endocervical/adenocarcinoma variant
- ★ SCC antigen — tumor marker for squamous cell carcinoma
- ★ Radical trachelectomy — fertility-sparing surgery for early cervical cancer
- ★ Large cell non-keratinizing SCC — most common histological subtype

## Mnemonics

**E6 and E7 targets: "6 before 7, p before R"**
- E6 → p53
- E7 → Rb

**Cervical cancer spread: "PLEASE"**
- P — Parametrium (most common route)
- L — Lymphatics (pelvic → para-aortic)
- E — Extension to vagina
- A — Anterior to bladder
- S — Sacral/posterior to rectum
- E — Endometrium (upward spread rare)
