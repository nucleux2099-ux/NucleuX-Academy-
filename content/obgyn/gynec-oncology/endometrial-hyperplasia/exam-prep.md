# Endometrial Hyperplasia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Unopposed estrogen causes abnormal endometrial proliferation. Without atypia (<5% cancer risk) → progestins. With atypia (25-30% cancer risk) → hysterectomy. PTEN mutation is the most common molecular change.

## High Yield Points

- ★ Atypia is the KEY determinant of cancer risk (not simple vs complex)
- ★ Without atypia: <5% cancer risk; with atypia: 25-30% cancer risk
- ★ LNG-IUS (Mirena) — most effective treatment for hyperplasia without atypia
- ★ 25-30% of atypical hyperplasia specimens have concurrent cancer at hysterectomy
- ★ PTEN loss — most common genetic change (same as Type I endometrial cancer)
- ★ Causes: PCOS, obesity, tamoxifen, estrogen-only HRT, granulosa cell tumor
- ★ WHO 2014 simplified to just 2 categories (replaced 1994 four-tier system)
- ★ Hyperplasia without atypia: 80-90% regression rate with progestins
- ★ Tamoxifen — anti-estrogen on breast but estrogenic on endometrium
- ★ Complex atypical hyperplasia (old WHO 1994): 29% cancer progression

## Mnemonics

**Risk of cancer progression (old 1994 WHO): "1-3-8-29"**
- Simple without atypia: 1%
- Complex without atypia: 3%
- Simple with atypia: 8%
- Complex with atypia: 29%

**Unopposed estrogen causes: "POTENT"**
- P — PCOS (anovulation)
- O — Obesity
- T — Tamoxifen
- E — Estrogen-only HRT
- N — Nulliparity
- T — Tumors (granulosa cell)

## Common MCQ Topics

- Which feature determines cancer risk? (Atypia, not simple/complex)
- Old WHO classification — cancer risk percentages (1-3-8-29)
- LNG-IUS vs oral progestins — which is better? (LNG-IUS)
- Concurrent cancer rate in atypical hyperplasia at hysterectomy (25-30%)
- Granulosa cell tumor → estrogen → endometrial hyperplasia
- PTEN mutation — which type of hyperplasia/cancer?
- WHO 2014 vs 1994 classification
- Treatment: without atypia → progestins; with atypia → hysterectomy
