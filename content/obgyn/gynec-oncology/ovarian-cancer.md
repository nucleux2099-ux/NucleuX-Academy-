# Ovarian Cancer

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Gynecology 4th Ed; Shaw's Textbook of Gynaecology 17th Ed; NCCN Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Ovarian cancer is the most lethal gynecologic malignancy, often called the "silent killer" because it typically presents at advanced stages due to the lack of early symptoms and effective screening. Epithelial ovarian cancer (EOC) accounts for 90% of cases, with high-grade serous carcinoma being the most common subtype. BRCA1/2 mutations significantly increase lifetime risk, making genetic counseling crucial for at-risk women.

## Key Points

- **Incidence**: 3rd most common gynecologic cancer; highest mortality
- **Presentation**: 70% present at stage III/IV (poor prognosis)
- **Origin**: New paradigm - Many "ovarian" cancers originate from fallopian tube
- **CA-125**: Most useful tumor marker (elevated in 80% of EOC)
- **BRCA1/2 mutations**: 15-20% of EOC are hereditary
- **Treatment cornerstone**: Cytoreductive surgery + platinum-based chemotherapy

## Classification

### By Cell of Origin
| Type | Frequency | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Epithelial** | 85-90% | Serous, Mucinous, Endometrioid, Clear cell |
| **Germ Cell** | 5-10% | Dysgerminoma, Yolk sac tumor, Teratoma |
| **Sex Cord-Stromal** | 3-5% | Granulosa cell, Sertoli-Leydig cell |
| **Metastatic** | 5-10% | Krukenberg tumor (GI origin) |

### Epithelial Ovarian Cancer (EOC) Subtypes
| Subtype | Frequency | Features | Prognosis |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **High-grade serous** | 70% | TP53 mutations, BRCA associated | Poor |
| **Low-grade serous** | 5% | KRAS/BRAF mutations, indolent | Better |
| **Endometrioid** | 10% | Endometriosis associated | Good |
| **Clear cell** | 10% | Endometriosis associated, chemo-resistant | Poor |
| **Mucinous** | 3% | Unilateral, large, GI-like | Variable |

## Risk Factors

### Increased Risk
| Factor | Relative Risk |
|--------|---------------|
| **BRCA1 mutation** | 40-60% lifetime risk |
| **BRCA2 mutation** | 15-30% lifetime risk |
| **Lynch syndrome** (HNPCC) | 10-15% lifetime risk |
| Family history (1st degree) | 3.1× |
| Nulliparity | 2× |
| Early menarche, late menopause | 1.5× |
| Endometriosis | 2-3× |
| Infertility | 1.5-2× |
| Obesity | 1.3× |
| HRT (estrogen only) | 1.2-1.5× |

### Decreased Risk (Protective)
| Factor | Risk Reduction |
|--------|---------------|
| Combined OCP use (≥5 years) | 50% |
| Breastfeeding | 30% |
| Multiparity | 30-40% |
| Tubal ligation | 30% |
| Salpingectomy | 40-60% |
| Hysterectomy | 30% |

**"Incessant ovulation hypothesis"**: More ovulatory cycles = higher risk

## Clinical Features

### Early Stage (Often Asymptomatic)
- Vague abdominal discomfort
- Bloating
- Fatigue

### Advanced Stage
| Symptom | Cause |
|---------|-------|
| Abdominal distension | Ascites, mass |
| Bloating, early satiety | Omental cake, intestinal involvement |
| Pelvic/abdominal pain | Mass effect, peritoneal disease |
| Urinary frequency | Bladder compression |
| Constipation | Rectal compression |
| Weight loss (paradoxically may gain) | Cachexia (but ascites adds weight) |
| Dyspnea | Pleural effusion |
| Irregular bleeding | Hormonal tumors |

### Signs
- Abdominal mass (may be palpable)
- Ascites (fluid thrill, shifting dullness)
- Pelvic mass (fixed, irregular, bilateral)
- Sister Mary Joseph nodule (umbilical metastasis)
- Pleural effusion (Meigs syndrome or metastasis)
- Cachexia

## Diagnosis

### Tumor Markers
| Marker | Tumor Type | Notes |
|--------|------------|-------|
| **CA-125** | Epithelial (serous) | Elevated in 80%; not specific |
| **HE4** | Epithelial | More specific than CA-125 |
| **ROMA score** | Epithelial | CA-125 + HE4 + menopausal status |
| **AFP** | Yolk sac tumor, embryonal | |
| **β-hCG** | Choriocarcinoma, embryonal | |
| **LDH** | Dysgerminoma | |
| **Inhibin** | Granulosa cell tumor | |
| **CEA** | Mucinous, metastatic GI | |

### CA-125 Limitations
**Elevated in benign conditions:**
- Endometriosis
- PID
- Fibroids
- Liver disease
- Pregnancy
- Menstruation

### Risk of Malignancy Index (RMI)
**RMI = U × M × CA-125**

| Component | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| **U (Ultrasound features)** | 0, 1, or 3 |
| - Bilateral | Score 1 each for present |
| - Solid areas | |
| - Ascites | |
| - Metastases | |
| - Multiloculated | |
| **M (Menopausal status)** | Premenopausal = 1, Postmenopausal = 3 |
| **CA-125** | Absolute value (U/mL) |

**Interpretation**: RMI ≥200 → High malignancy risk → Refer to gynecologic oncologist

### Imaging
| Modality | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Transvaginal USG** | First-line; characterize mass |
| **CT abdomen/pelvis** | Staging, surgical planning |
| **MRI** | Mass characterization if USG equivocal |
| **PET-CT** | Recurrence detection |
| **CXR** | Pleural effusion, metastases |

### Diagnosis is Surgical
- **No preoperative biopsy for resectable disease** (risk of tumor spillage)
- Exception: Apparent advanced stage with planned neoadjuvant chemotherapy

## FIGO Staging (Surgical)

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **IA** | Tumor limited to one ovary, capsule intact |
| **IB** | Both ovaries, capsules intact |
| **IC** | IA/IB with: surgical spill (IC1), capsule rupture (IC2), or positive washings (IC3) |
| **IIA** | Extension to uterus/tubes |
| **IIB** | Extension to other pelvic tissues |
| **IIIA** | Retroperitoneal LN positive or microscopic peritoneal metastasis |
| **IIIB** | Peritoneal metastasis ≤2 cm |
| **IIIC** | Peritoneal metastasis >2 cm and/or retroperitoneal LN |
| **IVA** | Pleural effusion with positive cytology |
| **IVB** | Parenchymal metastases, extra-abdominal LN |

## Management

### Surgical Treatment

#### Complete Surgical Staging (Early Stage)
1. Peritoneal washings
2. Total abdominal hysterectomy
3. Bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy
4. Omentectomy (infracolic)
5. Pelvic and para-aortic lymphadenectomy
6. Appendectomy (if mucinous)
7. Peritoneal biopsies (random and directed)

#### Cytoreductive Surgery (Advanced Stage)
**Goal**: Maximum debulking (optimal = no residual disease)

| Residual Disease | Median Survival |
|------------------|-----------------|
| No residual (R0) | 106 months |
| <1 cm | 66 months |
| >1 cm | 26 months |

**May include:**
- Bowel resection
- Splenectomy
- Diaphragm stripping
- Peritoneal stripping
- Liver surface resection

#### Fertility-Sparing Surgery
**Criteria:**
- Stage IA, grade 1-2
- Desire for fertility
- Reliable follow-up

**Procedure**: Unilateral salpingo-oophorectomy + complete staging

### Chemotherapy

#### First-Line Regimen
| Regimen | Drugs | Cycles |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Standard** | Carboplatin (AUC 5-6) + Paclitaxel 175 mg/m² | 6 cycles, q3 weeks |
| **+ Bevacizumab** | Add to above in advanced stage | Continue maintenance |
| **IP chemotherapy** | For optimally debulked stage III | Survival benefit but toxic |

#### Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy (NACT)
**Indications:**
- Unresectable disease
- Poor performance status
- High surgical risk

**Approach**: 3-4 cycles NACT → Interval debulking surgery → 3-4 cycles adjuvant

### Targeted Therapy & Maintenance

| Agent | Target | Indication |
|-------|--------|------------|
| **PARP inhibitors** (Olaparib, Niraparib) | DNA repair | BRCA+ or HRD+ maintenance |
| **Bevacizumab** | VEGF | Advanced stage maintenance |

### Recurrence Management
| Type | Definition | Treatment |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Platinum-sensitive** | Recurrence ≥6 months after chemo | Platinum-based regimen |
| **Platinum-resistant** | Recurrence <6 months | Non-platinum (liposomal doxorubicin, topotecan) |
| **Platinum-refractory** | Progression during chemo | Clinical trial, palliative care |

## Germ Cell Tumors

| Tumor | Age | Markers | Treatment |
|-------|-----|---------|-----------|
| **Dysgerminoma** | 10-30 | LDH | Chemo-sensitive; fertility-sparing |
| **Yolk sac tumor** | Children | AFP | BEP chemotherapy |
| **Immature teratoma** | 10-20 | AFP (if grade 3) | Surgery ± chemo (grade 2-3) |
| **Choriocarcinoma** | Reproductive | β-hCG | Highly chemo-sensitive |

**Standard chemotherapy**: BEP (Bleomycin, Etoposide, Cisplatin)

## Sex Cord-Stromal Tumors

| Tumor | Features | Markers |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Granulosa cell** | Estrogenic; endometrial hyperplasia | Inhibin, AMH |
| **Sertoli-Leydig** | Androgenic; virilization | Testosterone |
| **Fibroma** | Meigs syndrome (ascites, pleural effusion) | None |

## Hereditary Syndromes

| Syndrome | Gene | Cancers | Risk Reduction |
|----------|------|---------|----------------|
| **HBOC** | BRCA1/2 | Ovary, breast, pancreas, prostate | RRSO by age 35-40 (BRCA1), 40-45 (BRCA2) |
| **Lynch** | MLH1, MSH2, MSH6 | Ovary, endometrial, colorectal | Screening, RRSO after childbearing |

**RRSO** = Risk-Reducing Salpingo-Oophorectomy

## Prognosis

| Stage | 5-Year Survival |
|-------|-----------------|
| I | 80-90% |
| II | 60-70% |
| III | 30-40% |
| IV | 15-20% |

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **70% present at stage III/IV** due to silent early disease
2. **CA-125** is elevated in 80% but not specific; use with RMI or ROMA
3. **BRCA1**: 40-60% lifetime risk; BRCA2: 15-30%
4. **Optimal cytoreduction (R0)** is the most important prognostic factor
5. **Staging is surgical** - peritoneal washings, omentectomy, LN sampling essential
6. **First-line chemotherapy**: Carboplatin + Paclitaxel × 6 cycles
7. **PARP inhibitors** (Olaparib) for BRCA-mutated or HRD+ tumors
8. **Platinum-sensitive recurrence**: ≥6 months after chemo completion
9. **Dysgerminoma**: Most common malignant germ cell tumor; chemo-sensitive
10. **Granulosa cell tumor**: Produces estrogen; associated with endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
11. **Krukenberg tumor**: Metastatic (usually gastric signet ring cells); bilateral
12. **Meigs syndrome**: Ovarian fibroma + ascites + right pleural effusion
13. **Pseudomyxoma peritonei**: "Jelly belly" from ruptured mucinous tumor/appendix
14. **Many serous carcinomas originate from fimbria** - consider salpingectomy for risk reduction
15. **Sister Mary Joseph nodule**: Umbilical metastasis (suggests advanced disease)
