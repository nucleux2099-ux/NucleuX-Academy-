# Gestational Trophoblastic Disease (GTD)

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Obstetrics 26th Ed, Ch 20; Shaw's Textbook of Gynaecology 17th Ed; FIGO Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Gestational Trophoblastic Disease (GTD) encompasses a spectrum of disorders arising from abnormal placental trophoblastic tissue, ranging from benign hydatidiform mole to malignant gestational trophoblastic neoplasia (GTN). These tumors are unique in producing β-hCG, which serves as both a diagnostic marker and a reliable monitor of disease activity. GTN is highly curable even in advanced stages, with chemotherapy achieving cure rates exceeding 90%.

## Key Points

- **Spectrum**: Hydatidiform mole → Invasive mole → Choriocarcinoma → PSTT/ETT
- **Tumor marker**: β-hCG (essential for diagnosis, staging, and monitoring)
- **Complete mole**: 46,XX (diploid, paternal); "snowstorm" USG; no fetus
- **Partial mole**: 69,XXX or 69,XXY (triploid); fetal parts may be present
- **Highly chemosensitive**: Even metastatic disease often curable
- **Risk of GTN after mole**: Complete 15-20%, Partial 1-5%

## Classification

### WHO Classification of GTD
| Category | Types |
|----------|-------|
| **Molar Pregnancy** | Complete mole, Partial mole |
| **Gestational Trophoblastic Neoplasia (GTN)** | Invasive mole, Choriocarcinoma, PSTT, ETT |

### Comparison: Complete vs Partial Mole

| Feature | Complete Mole | Partial Mole |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| **Karyotype** | 46,XX (90%) or 46,XY | 69,XXX or 69,XXY |
| **Origin** | Paternal only (androgenetic) | Paternal + maternal |
| **Mechanism** | Empty ovum + 1-2 sperm | Normal ovum + 2 sperm |
| **Fetal tissue** | Absent | Present (abnormal) |
| **Amnion/RBCs** | Absent | Present |
| **Villi** | Diffuse swelling, "grapes" | Focal swelling |
| **Trophoblast hyperplasia** | Diffuse, marked | Focal, mild |
| **β-hCG level** | Very high (>100,000) | Moderately elevated |
| **Uterine size** | Large for dates (50%) | Small/normal for dates |
| **Theca lutein cysts** | 25-30% | Rare |
| **USG appearance** | "Snowstorm" / "cluster of grapes" | "Swiss cheese" with fetal parts |
| **GTN risk** | 15-20% | 1-5% |
| **Clinical presentation** | More symptomatic | Often missed abortion |

## Risk Factors

| Factor | Risk |
|--------|------|
| Previous molar pregnancy | 10× (1-2% recurrence) |
| Age >40 years | 5× |
| Age <20 years | Slightly increased |
| Previous GTN | Higher recurrence |
| Blood group A (woman) × O (partner) | Increased |
| Low dietary carotene/vitamin A | Possible |

## Clinical Features

### Hydatidiform Mole (Classic Presentation)
| Symptom/Sign | Complete Mole | Partial Mole |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| Vaginal bleeding | 90% | Common |
| Uterine size > dates | 50% | Rare |
| Hyperemesis | 20-30% | Less common |
| Preeclampsia (<20 weeks) | 25% | Rare |
| Hyperthyroidism | 5-10% | Rare |
| Theca lutein cysts | 25-30% | Rare |
| Passage of vesicles | Pathognomonic | - |
| Absent fetal heart | Always | May have abnormal FH |

### GTN Clinical Features
| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Invasive mole** | Persistent bleeding after evacuation, rising hCG |
| **Choriocarcinoma** | Metastatic symptoms (lung, brain, liver); may present after any pregnancy |
| **PSTT** | Slow-growing, low hCG, amenorrhea, virilization |
| **ETT** | Similar to PSTT |

### Metastatic Sites (Choriocarcinoma)
- **Lung** (80%) - cannon ball metastases
- **Vagina** (30%)
- **Brain** (10%)
- **Liver** (10%)
- **Kidney, GI** (rare)

## Diagnosis

### Laboratory
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **β-hCG** | Markedly elevated (>100,000 in complete mole) |
| **TSH, T4** | Hyperthyroidism (hCG cross-reacts with TSH receptor) |
| **CBC** | Anemia (bleeding) |
| **LFT, RFT** | If metastatic disease |

### Imaging
| Modality | Finding |
|----------|---------|
| **Transvaginal USG** | "Snowstorm" / "cluster of grapes" (complete); Swiss cheese (partial) |
| **Doppler** | High vascularity, low resistance |
| **Chest X-ray** | Lung metastases (staging) |
| **CT/MRI brain** | If neurological symptoms |
| **CT chest/abdomen** | Metastatic workup |

### Histopathology
| Complete Mole | Partial Mole |
|---------------|--------------|
| Diffuse villous swelling | Focal villous swelling |
| Circumferential trophoblast hyperplasia | Focal, minimal hyperplasia |
| Cisterns (central cavitation) | Scalloped outline, trophoblast inclusions |
| No fetal tissue | Fetal tissue present |
| p57 negative (paternal imprinting) | p57 positive |

## Management

### Initial Management of Molar Pregnancy

#### Preoperative Workup
- CBC, blood group, crossmatch
- LFT, RFT, TFT
- Chest X-ray
- β-hCG level

#### Evacuation
- **Suction evacuation (preferred)**: Under USG guidance
- **Oxytocin**: After cervix dilated (avoid before - risk of embolism)
- **Medical evacuation**: NOT recommended (incomplete, increased GTN risk)
- **Hysterectomy**: If family complete and suspicious for invasive disease

#### Anti-D: Give if Rh-negative

### Post-Evacuation Surveillance

**Serial β-hCG monitoring** (essential):
| Timeline | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Weekly | Until 3 consecutive normal values |
| Monthly | For 6 months (partial mole) or 6-12 months (complete mole) |

**Contraception**: Mandatory during surveillance (OCP preferred)

### Criteria for GTN Diagnosis (Post-Mole)

| FIGO Criteria | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| hCG plateau | <10% change over 4 values in 3 weeks |
| hCG rise | >10% rise over 3 values in 2 weeks |
| Persistent hCG | Detectable at 6 months |
| Histological choriocarcinoma | |
| Metastases | Any metastatic disease |

## FIGO Staging & Scoring (GTN)

### Anatomic Staging
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **I** | Confined to uterus |
| **II** | Extends outside uterus, limited to genital structures |
| **III** | Lung metastases ± genital involvement |
| **IV** | Other metastatic sites (brain, liver, kidney, GI) |

### Modified WHO Prognostic Scoring

| Factor | 0 | 1 | 2 | 4 |
|--------|---|---|---|---|
| Age | <40 | ≥40 | - | - |
| Antecedent pregnancy | Mole | Abortion | Term | - |
| Interval (months) | <4 | 4-6 | 7-12 | >12 |
| Pre-treatment hCG | <10³ | 10³-10⁴ | 10⁴-10⁵ | >10⁵ |
| Largest tumor (cm) | <3 | 3-4 | ≥5 | - |
| Metastatic sites | Lung | Spleen, kidney | GI | Brain, liver |
| Number of metastases | - | 1-4 | 5-8 | >8 |
| Previous chemotherapy | - | - | Single agent | ≥2 agents |

**Score interpretation:**
- **0-6**: Low risk
- **≥7**: High risk

### Complete FIGO Stage
Example: Stage III:9 = Lung metastases with score 9 (high risk)

## Treatment of GTN

### Low-Risk GTN (Score 0-6)
| Regimen | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Methotrexate** | 1 mg/kg IM days 1,3,5,7 with folinic acid rescue |
| **Actinomycin D** | Alternative; used if MTX-resistant |

Continue until:
- hCG normal
- Then 2-3 consolidation cycles

**Cure rate**: >90%

### High-Risk GTN (Score ≥7)
| Regimen | Components |
|---------|------------|
| **EMA-CO** | Etoposide, Methotrexate, Actinomycin D / Cyclophosphamide, Vincristine (Oncovin) |
| **EMA-EP** | For ultra-high risk or brain metastases |

**Cure rate**: 80-90%

### Brain Metastases
- Whole brain radiation
- High-dose methotrexate (crosses BBB)
- EMA-EP regimen

### PSTT/ETT
- **Hysterectomy** (first-line; less chemo-sensitive)
- EP/EMA chemotherapy if metastatic

### Drug-Resistant Disease
- Combination regimens
- Consider hysterectomy for uterine disease
- May require multiple regimens

## Follow-up After GTN Treatment

| Timeline | Monitoring |
|----------|------------|
| Weekly | Until hCG undetectable |
| Monthly | For 12 months (low-risk) or 24 months (high-risk) |
| Contraception | Mandatory during surveillance |

## Future Pregnancy

- **Safe to conceive** after surveillance complete
- Risk of repeat mole: 1-2%
- Early USG in next pregnancy to confirm viability
- hCG at 6 weeks postpartum (any pregnancy)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Complete mole**: 46,XX (paternal only); no fetal parts; "snowstorm" on USG
2. **Partial mole**: 69,XXY (triploid); fetal parts present; 1-5% GTN risk
3. **β-hCG is THE marker** - diagnosis, staging, monitoring, and cure assessment
4. **Preeclampsia before 20 weeks** suggests molar pregnancy
5. **Theca lutein cysts**: Due to high hCG (regress after evacuation)
6. **Hyperthyroidism**: hCG mimics TSH
7. **Suction evacuation** is treatment of choice (NOT medical)
8. **Weekly hCG** until 3 consecutive normal values, then monthly
9. **GTN criteria**: hCG plateau ×4 in 3 weeks, rise ×3 in 2 weeks, or at 6 months
10. **Low-risk GTN**: Methotrexate single agent; >90% cure
11. **High-risk GTN**: EMA-CO regimen
12. **PSTT**: Less chemo-sensitive; hysterectomy is first-line
13. **Choriocarcinoma**: Can occur after ANY pregnancy (mole, abortion, normal)
14. **Cannon ball metastases** on CXR: Choriocarcinoma until proven otherwise
15. **p57 staining**: Negative in complete mole (paternal imprinting loss)
16. **Contraception mandatory** during surveillance (pregnancy confuses hCG monitoring)
