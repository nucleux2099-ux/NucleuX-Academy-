# Vaginal Cancer — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Rarest primary gynecological malignancy (1-2%). SCC is most common type (80-90%), HPV-related, affects postmenopausal women, posterior wall upper third. DES exposure → clear cell adenocarcinoma in young women. Sarcoma botryoides in children (<5 yrs). Staged clinically (FIGO). Radiation is mainstay of treatment. If tumor involves cervix → it's cervical cancer.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ If tumor involves cervix = cervical cancer; involves vulva = vulvar cancer
- ★ Most common site: posterior wall, upper one-third
- ★ Most common type: Squamous cell carcinoma (80-90%)
- ★ DES exposure in utero → clear cell adenocarcinoma + vaginal adenosis + T-shaped uterus
- ★ DES peak age: 19-20 years
- ★ Sarcoma botryoides = embryonal rhabdomyosarcoma; grape-like mass; **cambium layer**
- ★ Sarcoma botryoides treatment: VAC chemotherapy + conservative surgery
- ★ Staged CLINICALLY (like cervical cancer), not surgically
- ★ Radiation therapy is mainstay for most stages
- ★ VAIN is precursor (HPV-related); analogous to CIN
- ★ Vaginal melanoma: distal vagina, very poor prognosis

## Mnemonics

**"DES Daughters" — DES triad:** Clear cell adenoCarcinoma, Adenosis (vaginal), T-shaped uterus

**Sarcoma Botryoides — "GRAB":** Grape-like, Rhabdomyosarcoma, Age <5, Botryoides (cambium layer)

**Vaginal cancer classification rule — "CVV":** if involves Cervix → Cervical; if involves Vulva → Vulvar; if Vagina only → Vaginal
