# Cervical Cancer Screening — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Screen to detect CIN before invasive cancer. HPV 16/18 cause 70% cervical cancers. Pap smear = traditional; HPV DNA = more sensitive, now preferred. VIA = low-resource screen-and-treat. Start screening at 21 (ACOG) or 25 (WHO). LEEP/LLETZ is treatment of choice for CIN 2/3. Colposcopy for abnormal screening.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most sensitive screening test** → HPV DNA testing (>90%)
- ★ **Traditional screening** → Pap smear (Bethesda reporting)
- ★ **HPV 16 and 18** → cause 70% cervical cancers
- ★ **VIA** → 3-5% acetic acid; acetowhite = positive; screen-and-treat
- ★ **Schiller's test** → Lugol's iodine; non-staining = positive (VILI)
- ★ **ASC-US management** → HPV triage; if positive → colposcopy
- ★ **CIN 1** → observe (most regress); treat only if persistent >2 years
- ★ **CIN 2/3** → LEEP/LLETZ (treatment of choice)
- ★ **Cold knife conization** → preferred for adenocarcinoma in situ
- ★ **Stop screening at 65** → if adequate prior negative screening
- ★ **No screening before 21** → regardless of sexual activity
- ★ **LEEP complication** → cervical shortening → risk of preterm birth

## Mnemonics

**Bethesda — "ALL HAVE SIL"**:
- **A**SC-US, **A**SC-H, **L**SIL, **H**SIL, **A**GC, **S**CC, **I**n-situ, **L**esions

**"VIA = Vinegar Inspection Always"** — acetic acid = vinegar

**Screening intervals — "3-5 rule"**:
- Cytology alone = every **3** years
- HPV co-test or HPV primary = every **5** years

**HPV oncoproteins**: E6 → degrades p53; E7 → degrades Rb
