# Cervical Screening — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Screen for precancerous CIN lesions using Pap smear, HPV DNA testing, or VIA. Bethesda system for reporting. CIN 1 usually regresses; CIN 2-3 needs treatment (LEEP). HPV DNA testing is the most sensitive method.

## High Yield Points

- ★ HPV DNA test — most SENSITIVE screening method (>95%)
- ★ Pap smear — most widely used; sensitivity only 50-80%
- ★ VIA — best for low-resource settings (screen-and-treat, single visit)
- ★ Schiller's test: non-staining areas are ABNORMAL (Schiller-positive = abnormal)
- ★ LSIL = CIN 1; HSIL = CIN 2/3 (Bethesda System)
- ★ ASC-US → reflex HPV testing (most common abnormal Pap result)
- ★ CIN 1 regresses in 60% — observation is appropriate
- ★ LEEP/LLETZ — most common treatment for CIN 2-3
- ★ Colposcopy is DIAGNOSTIC, not a screening test
- ★ Screening age: 21-65 years; interval: every 3-5 years depending on method

## Mnemonics

**Bethesda categories: "ALAN-S"**
- A — ASC-US
- L — LSIL
- A — ASC-H
- N — (there's no N — just remember the escalation)
- H — HSIL
- S — SCC

**CIN 1 regression: "60-30-10-1"**
- 60% regress
- 30% persist
- 10% → CIS
- 1% → invasion

**VIA vs VILI:**
- VIA = Acetic acid = Acetowhite (abnormal = white)
- VILI = Lugol's Iodine = normal stains brown (abnormal = non-staining)
