# Endometrial Cancer — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Most common gynecological cancer in developed countries. Postmenopausal bleeding is key symptom. Unopposed estrogen → Type I (endometrioid, 80%, good prognosis). Surgically staged. TAH-BSO is standard surgery.

## High Yield Points

- ★ Type I (endometrioid, 80%): estrogen-dependent, PTEN mutation, good prognosis
- ★ Type II (serous/clear cell, 20%): p53 mutation, aggressive, arises from atrophic endometrium
- ★ Postmenopausal bleeding — investigate until cancer excluded
- ★ ET >4mm on TVS in postmenopausal woman → endometrial biopsy
- ★ Surgically staged (FIGO 2009) — NOT clinically staged
- ★ Tamoxifen — estrogenic on endometrium → increases endometrial cancer risk
- ★ Lynch syndrome — most common extracolonic cancer is endometrial cancer
- ★ OCPs are protective (50% risk reduction)
- ★ Smoking is protective (anti-estrogenic effect)
- ★ Vaginal cuff — most common site of local recurrence
- ★ MRI — best for assessing depth of myometrial invasion

## Mnemonics

**Type I vs Type II: "OPEN vs CAPS"**
Type I (OPEN):
- O — Obese
- P — PTEN mutation
- E — Estrogen-dependent
- N — Nice prognosis

Type II (CAPS):
- C — Clear cell / serous
- A — Atrophic endometrium
- P — p53 mutation
- S — Savage prognosis

**Risk factors: "ONTE"**
- O — Obesity
- N — Nulliparity
- T — Tamoxifen
- E — Estrogen (unopposed)

## Common MCQ Topics
