# Endometrial Cancer

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Gynecology 4th Ed; Shaw's Textbook of Gynaecology 17th Ed; NCCN Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Endometrial cancer is the most common gynecologic malignancy in developed countries, typically affecting postmenopausal women. It is classified into two distinct types: Type I (estrogen-dependent, endometrioid, favorable prognosis) and Type II (estrogen-independent, high-grade, poor prognosis). Early presentation with abnormal uterine bleeding leads to diagnosis at early stages in most cases, resulting in overall good survival rates. Obesity and unopposed estrogen are the primary modifiable risk factors.

## Key Points

- **Epidemiology**: Most common gynecologic cancer in developed countries; median age 63
- **Hallmark symptom**: Postmenopausal bleeding (90% present with this)
- **Risk factors**: Obesity, diabetes, hypertension ("triad")
- **Two types**: Type I (estrogen-driven, 80%) vs Type II (non-estrogen, 20%)
- **Diagnosis**: Endometrial sampling (pipelle biopsy, D&C)
- **Treatment mainstay**: Surgery (TAH-BSO)
- **Staging is SURGICAL** (FIGO 2009)

## Classification: Type I vs Type II

| Feature | Type I | Type II |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Frequency** | 80% | 20% |
| **Histology** | Endometrioid | Serous, clear cell, carcinosarcoma |
| **Grade** | Low grade (1-2) | High grade (3) |
| **Estrogen-related** | Yes | No |
| **Precursor lesion** | Atypical hyperplasia | Atrophic endometrium, EIC |
| **Genetics** | PTEN, PIK3CA, MSI | TP53, HER2 |
| **Age** | Younger postmenopausal | Older, advanced age |
| **Obesity association** | Strong | Weak |
| **Prognosis** | Good (85% 5-year survival) | Poor (50% 5-year survival) |
| **Myometrial invasion** | Superficial | Deep |
| **Behavior** | Indolent | Aggressive |

## Risk Factors

### Type I (Estrogen Excess)
| Factor | Mechanism | Relative Risk |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Obesity** | ↑ Peripheral estrogen conversion | BMI >40: 6× |
| **Unopposed estrogen** | No progesterone to oppose | 4-8× |
| **Tamoxifen** | Estrogen agonist on endometrium | 2-7× |
| **Nulliparity** | Lack of progesterone from pregnancy | 2× |
| **Polycystic ovarian syndrome** | Anovulation, estrogen excess | 3× |
| **Early menarche/late menopause** | Prolonged estrogen exposure | 1.5-2× |
| **Diabetes mellitus** | Hyperinsulinemia, growth factors | 2-3× |
| **Estrogen-producing tumors** | Granulosa cell tumor | Variable |

### Type II (Non-Estrogen Related)
- Advanced age
- Prior pelvic radiation
- TP53 mutations
- Lynch syndrome (hereditary)

### Protective Factors
| Factor | Risk Reduction |
|--------|---------------|
| Combined OCP use | 50% (persists for years) |
| Multiparity | 30-40% |
| Smoking | 20-30% (anti-estrogenic) |
| Physical activity | 20-30% |
| Progestin IUD | 50% |

## Hereditary Risk: Lynch Syndrome

- **Genes**: MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM
- **Lifetime endometrial cancer risk**: 40-60%
- **Also increased**: Colorectal, ovarian cancer
- **Screening**: Annual endometrial sampling from age 30-35
- **Diagnosis**: Immunohistochemistry (MMR proteins), MSI testing
- **Management**: Prophylactic hysterectomy after childbearing

## Endometrial Hyperplasia

| Type | Malignant Potential | Management |
|------|--------------------| ------------|
| **Hyperplasia without atypia** | 1-3% | Progestin therapy |
| **Atypical hyperplasia (AH/EIN)** | 25-50% | Hysterectomy (preferred) or high-dose progestin |

**Progestin options**: Medroxyprogesterone acetate, Levonorgestrel IUD, Megestrol

## Clinical Features

### Presenting Symptoms
| Symptom | Frequency |
|---------|-----------|
| **Postmenopausal bleeding** | 90% |
| **Abnormal premenopausal bleeding** | 10% |
| Vaginal discharge | 10% |
| Pelvic pain | Late stage |
| Weight loss | Late stage |

### Key Clinical Points
- **Any postmenopausal bleeding requires evaluation** until cancer excluded
- 10% of postmenopausal bleeding is endometrial cancer
- Premenopausal women: Heavy, prolonged, or irregular bleeding

### Signs
- Often normal examination
- Bulky uterus (if advanced)
- Cervical involvement (if stage II)
- Adnexal mass (ovarian involvement)

## Diagnosis

### Initial Evaluation (Postmenopausal Bleeding)
1. **Transvaginal ultrasound**: Endometrial thickness
   - **Threshold**: >4 mm requires biopsy
   - <4 mm: 99% NPV for cancer
2. **Endometrial sampling**: If ET >4 mm or high clinical suspicion

### Tissue Diagnosis Methods
| Method | Description | Sensitivity |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Pipelle biopsy** | Office-based, no anesthesia | 90-95% |
| **D&C** | OR procedure, if pipelle inadequate | >95% |
| **Hysteroscopy + biopsy** | Direct visualization | Highest |

### Staging Workup
| Investigation | Purpose |
|---------------|---------|
| **Pelvic MRI** | Myometrial invasion, cervical involvement |
| **CT chest/abdomen/pelvis** | Distant metastases (advanced cases) |
| **CA-125** | Elevated in advanced/serous types |
| **Intraoperative frozen section** | Guide lymphadenectomy |

## FIGO Staging (2009, Surgical)

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **IA** | Tumor confined to endometrium or <50% myometrial invasion |
| **IB** | ≥50% myometrial invasion |
| **II** | Cervical stromal invasion |
| **IIIA** | Serosal and/or adnexal involvement |
| **IIIB** | Vaginal and/or parametrial involvement |
| **IIIC1** | Pelvic lymph node metastasis |
| **IIIC2** | Para-aortic lymph node metastasis |
| **IVA** | Bladder and/or bowel mucosa invasion |
| **IVB** | Distant metastases |

## Histological Grading (Endometrioid)

| Grade | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **Grade 1** | ≤5% solid growth pattern |
| **Grade 2** | 6-50% solid growth pattern |
| **Grade 3** | >50% solid growth pattern |

**Nuclear grade**: Adds 1 grade if marked nuclear atypia

## Molecular Classification (TCGA)

| Subtype | Frequency | Features | Prognosis |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **POLE ultramutated** | 7% | Excellent, even if high-grade | Excellent |
| **MSI hypermutated** | 28% | Lynch-like, immunotherapy responsive | Intermediate |
| **Copy-number low** | 39% | Most Type I tumors | Good |
| **Copy-number high (p53 mutant)** | 26% | Serous-like | Poor |

## Management

### Surgical Treatment (Standard)

**Minimum surgery**: 
- Total hysterectomy + bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy
- Peritoneal washings
- ± Lymph node assessment

**Approach**: 
- Minimally invasive (laparoscopic/robotic) preferred
- Open if large uterus or suspicious for advanced disease

### Lymph Node Assessment
| Risk | Definition | Management |
|------|------------|------------|
| **Low risk** | Grade 1-2, <50% invasion, ≤2 cm | May omit LN assessment |
| **Intermediate** | Grade 1-2 with >50% invasion OR Grade 3 <50% | Sentinel LN ± full LND |
| **High risk** | Grade 3 with >50% invasion, serous, clear cell | Full pelvic + para-aortic LND |

### Sentinel Lymph Node Mapping
- Emerging standard for early-stage disease
- ICG or blue dye injection into cervix
- Reduces lymphedema compared to full LND

### Adjuvant Therapy

| Stage/Risk | Adjuvant Treatment |
|------------|-------------------|
| **IA, Grade 1-2** | Observation |
| **IA Grade 3 or IB Grade 1-2** | Vaginal brachytherapy |
| **IB Grade 3** | Pelvic radiation ± chemotherapy |
| **Stage II** | Pelvic radiation ± vaginal brachytherapy |
| **Stage III-IV** | Chemotherapy + radiation |
| **Type II (serous, clear cell)** | Chemotherapy (even stage I) |

### Chemotherapy Regimens
| Setting | Regimen |
|---------|---------|
| **Advanced/high-risk** | Carboplatin + Paclitaxel × 6 cycles |
| **Serous/clear cell** | As above ± radiation |

### Hormonal Therapy
| Indication | Agent |
|------------|-------|
| Atypical hyperplasia (fertility desired) | Progestins (Mirena IUD, MPA) |
| Recurrent, ER+ | Progestins, aromatase inhibitors |
| Maintenance | Megestrol, tamoxifen |

### Immunotherapy
- **Pembrolizumab**: MSI-high/dMMR tumors
- **Dostarlimab**: dMMR advanced endometrial cancer
- **Lenvatinib + Pembrolizumab**: MSS (microsatellite stable) advanced

### Fertility-Sparing Management
**Criteria:**
- Desire for fertility
- Grade 1 endometrioid
- No myometrial invasion (MRI)
- No contraindication to medical therapy
- Willing to accept close surveillance

**Protocol:**
- High-dose progestin (Mirena IUD or oral MPA 400-600 mg/day)
- Re-biopsy every 3-6 months
- If complete response: Attempt conception
- Hysterectomy after childbearing or if fails

## Recurrence

### Sites of Recurrence
- **Vaginal vault**: Most common (local)
- **Pelvis**: Regional
- **Distant**: Lung, liver, peritoneum, bone

### Management of Recurrence
| Type | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| **Isolated vaginal** | Radiation (if not previously irradiated) |
| **Pelvic** | Radiation + chemotherapy |
| **Distant** | Systemic chemotherapy, hormonal therapy |
| **Oligometastatic** | Consider metastasectomy |

## Prognosis

| Stage | 5-Year Survival |
|-------|-----------------|
| I | 85-90% |
| II | 75-80% |
| III | 50-60% |
| IV | 15-25% |

| Histology | 5-Year Survival |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Endometrioid Grade 1 | 90% |
| Endometrioid Grade 3 | 65% |
| Serous | 40-50% |
| Clear cell | 40-50% |
| Carcinosarcoma | 30-40% |

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Postmenopausal bleeding = cancer until proven otherwise** (10% are cancer)
2. **Endometrial thickness >4 mm** on TVS requires biopsy
3. **Type I**: Estrogen-driven, endometrioid, good prognosis (80%)
4. **Type II**: p53 mutant, serous/clear cell, poor prognosis (20%)
5. **Obesity triad**: Obesity + DM + HTN (classic risk factor profile)
6. **Tamoxifen increases risk** (2-7×); annual screening recommended
7. **Atypical hyperplasia**: 25-50% progress to cancer; hysterectomy preferred
8. **Lynch syndrome**: MLH1/MSH2 mutations; 40-60% lifetime risk
9. **FIGO staging is surgical** - includes LN assessment
10. **Stage IA Grade 1-2**: Surgery alone (observation, no adjuvant)
11. **Serous/clear cell needs chemotherapy** even at early stage
12. **Mirena IUD** is effective for hyperplasia and fertility preservation
13. **Carboplatin + Paclitaxel**: Standard chemotherapy regimen
14. **Pembrolizumab**: For MSI-high/dMMR tumors (immunotherapy)
15. **POLE-mutated tumors**: Best prognosis despite high-grade appearance
