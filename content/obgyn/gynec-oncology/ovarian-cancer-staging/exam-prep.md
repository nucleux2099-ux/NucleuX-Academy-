# Ovarian Cancer Staging — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Ovarian cancer is staged surgically using FIGO 2014 system. Four stages: I (confined to ovaries), II (pelvic extension), III (peritoneal/nodal spread beyond pelvis), IV (distant metastases). 70% present at Stage III-IV. Surgical staging includes TAH+BSO, omentectomy, peritoneal biopsies, lymph node sampling, and washings. Optimal debulking (R0) is key prognostic factor.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ FIGO 2014: IC subdivided — IC1 (surgical spill), IC2 (preop rupture/surface tumor), IC3 (positive washings)
- ★ Stage IIIA1 = retroperitoneal nodes ONLY (no peritoneal disease)
- ★ Stage IVA = pleural effusion with positive cytology
- ★ Stage IVB = parenchymal liver/spleen mets or extra-abdominal spread
- ★ Liver CAPSULE involvement = Stage IIIC (not IV)
- ★ 30% of apparent Stage I are upstaged after complete surgical staging
- ★ Standard chemo: Paclitaxel + Carboplatin
- ★ Appendectomy important in mucinous tumors
- ★ Omental cake = Stage IIIB/IIIC
- ★ Optimal debulking = residual ≤1 cm (ideal = R0)

## Mnemonics

**"STOP COWL" — Surgical Staging Steps:**
- **S**ampling of peritoneal washings
- **T**AH + BSO
- **O**mentectomy
- **P**eritoneal biopsies
- **C**areful exploration
- **O**ther suspicious lesion biopsy
- **W**hole lymph node dissection (pelvic + para-aortic)
- **L**ook at diaphragm

**Stage IV distinction:** "A for Ascites-in-chest (pleural), B for Beyond abdomen"
