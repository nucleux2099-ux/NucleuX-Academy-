# Ovarian Cancer Staging

## Overview

Ovarian cancer staging is crucial for determining prognosis and guiding treatment. The FIGO (International Federation of Gynecology and Obstetrics) staging system, revised in 2014, is the standard classification used worldwide. Staging is primarily surgical, requiring comprehensive surgical exploration.

## Key Points

- Ovarian cancer is staged **surgically** (not clinically)
- FIGO 2014 is the current staging system
- Majority of patients present at **Stage III or IV** (70%)
- Complete surgical staging includes total abdominal hysterectomy, bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy, omentectomy, peritoneal biopsies, and lymph node sampling
- Stage is the **single most important prognostic factor**

## Definition & Classification

### FIGO Staging (2014 Revision)

#### Stage I — Tumor Confined to Ovaries
| Substage | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| IA | One ovary, capsule intact, no surface tumor, negative washings |
| IB | Both ovaries, capsule intact, no surface tumor, negative washings |
| IC1 | Surgical spill |
| IC2 | Capsule ruptured before surgery or tumor on surface |
| IC3 | Positive washings or ascites with malignant cells |

#### Stage II — Pelvic Extension
| Substage | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| IIA | Extension to uterus, tubes |
| IIB | Extension to other pelvic tissues |

#### Stage III — Peritoneal Spread Beyond Pelvis and/or Retroperitoneal Lymph Nodes
| Substage | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| IIIA1 | Retroperitoneal lymph nodes only (IIIA1i ≤10mm, IIIA1ii >10mm) |
| IIIA2 | Microscopic extrapelvic peritoneal involvement ± nodes |
| IIIB | Macroscopic peritoneal metastasis ≤2 cm ± nodes |
| IIIC | Macroscopic peritoneal metastasis >2 cm ± nodes (includes liver/spleen capsule) |

#### Stage IV — Distant Metastases
| Substage | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| IVA | Pleural effusion with positive cytology |
| IVB | Parenchymal liver/spleen metastases, extra-abdominal spread (including inguinal/supraclavicular nodes) |

### Key Changes in FIGO 2014
- Stage IC subdivided into IC1, IC2, IC3
- Stage IIIA redefined — retroperitoneal-only nodes now IIIA1
- Stage IV subdivided into IVA and IVB

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

Staging reflects the natural spread pattern of ovarian cancer:
1. **Direct extension** — to adjacent pelvic structures
2. **Transperitoneal dissemination** — via peritoneal fluid circulation (most common)
3. **Lymphatic spread** — para-aortic > pelvic nodes
4. **Hematogenous spread** — rare, late (liver parenchyma, lungs)

The omentum is a common site of metastasis ("omental cake").

## Clinical Features

- **Early stage (I-II):** Often asymptomatic; vague abdominal discomfort, bloating
- **Advanced stage (III-IV):** Abdominal distension (ascites), omental mass, pleural effusion, bowel symptoms, weight loss
- **Sister Mary Joseph nodule** — umbilical metastasis
- **Krukenberg tumor** — metastasis TO ovary (not from)

## Diagnosis

### Preoperative Workup
- Pelvic ultrasound (transvaginal)
- CT abdomen and pelvis (to assess extent)
- CA-125 (elevated in epithelial ovarian cancer)
- Chest X-ray / CT chest
- Other markers: HE4, ROMA score

### Surgical Staging Procedure
1. Midline vertical incision
2. Collection of peritoneal washings/ascitic fluid
3. Thorough exploration of all peritoneal surfaces
4. Total abdominal hysterectomy + bilateral salpingo-oophorectomy
5. Infracolic omentectomy
6. Pelvic and para-aortic lymph node dissection
7. Multiple peritoneal biopsies (paracolic gutters, diaphragm, pelvic peritoneum)
8. Appendectomy (especially in mucinous tumors)
9. Biopsy of any suspicious lesions

### Upstaging
- 30% of apparently Stage I tumors are upstaged after comprehensive surgical staging

## Management

### Stage-Based Treatment
| Stage | Primary Treatment |
|-------|-------------------|
| IA/IB (Grade 1-2) | Surgery alone; observation |
| IA/IB (Grade 3), IC | Surgery + adjuvant chemotherapy (3-6 cycles) |
| II | Surgery + adjuvant chemotherapy (6 cycles) |
| III-IV | Primary debulking surgery + chemotherapy OR neoadjuvant chemotherapy + interval debulking |

### Chemotherapy Regimen
- Standard: **Paclitaxel + Carboplatin** (6 cycles, q3 weekly)
- Bevacizumab may be added in advanced stages
- Intraperitoneal chemotherapy in optimally debulked Stage III

### Optimal Debulking
- Goal: **No residual disease** (R0) or residual ≤1 cm
- Residual disease is the strongest predictor of survival in advanced stages

## Complications

- **Surgical:** Bowel injury, ureteric injury, hemorrhage, DVT
- **Chemotherapy:** Myelosuppression, neuropathy, nephrotoxicity
- **Disease-related:** Bowel obstruction, cachexia, malignant ascites
- **Recurrence:** 70-80% in advanced stages

## Prognosis by Stage

| Stage | 5-Year Survival |
|-------|----------------|
| I | 80-90% |
| II | 60-70% |
| III | 30-40% |
| IV | <20% |
