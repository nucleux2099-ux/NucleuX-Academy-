# HPV Vaccination — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Prophylactic VLP-based vaccines against HPV. Three types: bivalent (16,18), quadrivalent (6,11,16,18), nonavalent (adds 31,33,45,52,58). Target: girls 9-14 years before sexual debut. Does NOT replace screening.

## High Yield Points

- ★ VLPs (L1 capsid protein) — non-infectious, no viral DNA
- ★ HPV 16+18 → 70% cervical cancers; nonavalent covers ~90%
- ★ HPV 6+11 → 90% genital warts (only quadrivalent/nonavalent cover these)
- ★ Prophylactic ONLY — does NOT treat existing HPV infection
- ★ Best efficacy when given BEFORE sexual debut
- ★ Cervavac — Indian indigenous quadrivalent vaccine (SII)
- ★ 2-dose schedule for age 9-14; 3 doses for age ≥15
- ★ WHO 2022: single-dose may be sufficient (emerging data)
- ★ Safe — no proven link to autoimmune disease or infertility
- ★ Vaccination does NOT replace cervical screening

## Mnemonics

**Vaccine types: "2-4-9"**
- Bivalent: 2 types (16, 18) — "Cer-va-rix" (Cervical cancer)
- Quadrivalent: 4 types (6, 11, 16, 18) — "Gar-da-sil" (Guards against warts + cancer)
- Nonavalent: 9 types — "Gardasil 9" (9 types, ~90% cancer prevention)

**HPV numbers to remember:**
- 6, 11 — warts (LOW risk)
- 16, 18 — cancer (HIGH risk)
- 16 → SCC; 18 → Adenocarcinoma

## Common MCQ Topics

- Which vaccine covers genital warts? (quadrivalent/nonavalent, NOT bivalent)

- Prophylactic vs therapeutic distinction
