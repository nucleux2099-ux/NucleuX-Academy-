# Cervical Cancer Staging — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

FIGO clinical staging. 2018 update allows imaging and lymph node pathology. Stage IIB (parametrial involvement) is the surgical vs chemoradiation cutoff. Stage IIIC (new) = lymph node involvement.

## High Yield Points

- ★ Cervical cancer = clinically staged (unlike ovarian/endometrial = surgical staging)
- ★ EUA is mandatory; per rectal examination essential for parametrial assessment
- ★ Stage IIB = parametrial involvement → chemoradiation, not surgery
- ★ Stage IIIB = pelvic wall fixation OR hydronephrosis/non-functioning kidney
- ★ Stage IIIC (NEW 2018): IIIC1 = pelvic LN; IIIC2 = para-aortic LN
- ★ Bullous edema of bladder is NOT Stage IVA (needs mucosal biopsy proof)
- ★ IA1: ≤3mm depth; IA2: >3-5mm depth (2018: no horizontal limit)
- ★ IB subdivided into IB1 (≤2cm), IB2 (2-4cm), IB3 (>4cm) in 2018
- ★ MRI — best for parametrial assessment; PET-CT — best for lymph nodes
- ★ 2018 FIGO removed horizontal spread limit for stage IA

## Mnemonics

**Stages in order: "Cervix → Vagina → Pelvis → Beyond"**
- I = Cervix only
- II = Beyond cervix (upper vagina/parametrium)
- III = Pelvic wall / lower vagina / hydronephrosis / LN
- IV = Bladder/rectum mucosa or distant

**Stage IIIB causes: "PHF"**
- P — Pelvic wall fixation
- H — Hydronephrosis
- F — Non-functioning kidney

## Common MCQ Topics

- Why cervical cancer is clinically staged (resource-limited settings)
- What changed in 2018 FIGO revision (imaging, LN status, Stage IIIC, IB subdivisions)
- Bullous edema — which stage? (NOT IVA — still considered Stage III or less)
- Parametrial involvement assessment (per rectal > per vaginal)
- IA1 vs IA2 depth cutoff (3mm)
- Treatment cutoff at Stage IIB
- Which investigation best for parametrium (MRI) vs lymph nodes (PET-CT)
