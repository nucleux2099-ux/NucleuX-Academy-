# Multiple Pregnancy

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Obstetrics 26th Ed, Ch 45; DC Dutta's Textbook of Obstetrics 9th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Multiple pregnancy refers to the presence of more than one fetus in the uterus simultaneously. Twin pregnancies occur in 1-2% of all pregnancies (increasing with ART), and are classified by zygosity (genetic origin) and chorionicity (placental arrangement). Chorionicity is the primary determinant of risk, with monochorionic twins facing significantly higher complications due to shared placental circulation.

## Key Points

- **Incidence**: Twins 1:80 (natural), increasing to 1:40 with ART
- **Zygosity**: Dizygotic (fraternal, 70%) vs Monozygotic (identical, 30%)
- **Chorionicity determines risk** - not zygosity
- **Monochorionic twins**: Share placenta → unique complications (TTTS, TRAP, sIUGR)
- **Hellin's law**: Twins 1:80, Triplets 1:80², Quadruplets 1:80³
- **Best time to determine chorionicity**: First trimester USG (11-14 weeks)

## Classification

### By Zygosity
| Type | Origin | Genetics | Always Same Sex |
|------|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **Dizygotic** | Two eggs, two sperm | Different | No (70% M/F) |
| **Monozygotic** | One egg splits | Identical | Yes |

### By Chorionicity and Amnionicity
| Type | Timing of Division | Placentas | Amniotic Sacs |
|------|-------------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Dichorionic-Diamniotic (DCDA)** | 0-3 days | 2 (may fuse) | 2 |
| **Monochorionic-Diamniotic (MCDA)** | 4-8 days | 1 | 2 |
| **Monochorionic-Monoamniotic (MCMA)** | 8-13 days | 1 | 1 |
| **Conjoined twins** | >13 days | 1 | 1 |

### USG Signs for Chorionicity (First Trimester)

| Sign | DCDA | MCDA |
|------|------|------|
| **Lambda (λ) sign** | Present (twin peak) | Absent |
| **T sign** | Absent | Present |
| Membrane thickness | Thick (>2mm) | Thin |
| Separate placentae | Often visible | Single |
| Intervening membrane layers | 4 | 2 |

## Risk Factors for Multiple Pregnancy

- Advanced maternal age
- Assisted reproductive technology (ART)
- Family history (maternal)
- High parity
- Previous twins
- Race (highest in Africans)
- Discontinuing oral contraceptives

## Clinical Features

### Symptoms
- Exaggerated pregnancy symptoms (hyperemesis)
- Excessive weight gain
- Increased fetal movements

### Signs
- Uterus large for dates
- Multiple fetal poles palpable
- Multiple fetal heart sounds
- Polyhydramnios

## Diagnosis

### Clinical Suspicion
- Uterus larger than dates
- Excessive maternal weight gain
- Palpation of multiple fetal parts

### Ultrasound (Definitive)
- First trimester: Crown-rump length, chorionicity assessment
- Second trimester: Anomaly scan, growth assessment
- Serial scans for growth and complications

## Maternal Complications

| Complication | Mechanism |
|--------------|-----------|
| Hyperemesis gravidarum | ↑ hCG levels |
| Anemia | ↑ Iron/folate demand |
| Preeclampsia | 3× increased risk |
| Gestational diabetes | Metabolic demands |
| Polyhydramnios | Especially with TTTS |
| Preterm labor | 50% deliver before 37 weeks |
| APH (abruption/previa) | Larger placental site |
| Malpresentation | Limited intrauterine space |
| Cesarean section | Higher rate (60-70%) |
| Postpartum hemorrhage | Uterine overdistension |

## Fetal Complications

### All Twins
| Complication | Risk |
|--------------|------|
| Preterm birth | 50-60% |
| Low birth weight | 50% |
| IUGR | Discordant growth |
| Congenital anomalies | 2× increased |
| Perinatal mortality | 3-5× increased |
| Cord accidents | Especially MCMA |

### Monochorionic-Specific Complications

#### Twin-to-Twin Transfusion Syndrome (TTTS)
- **Incidence**: 10-15% of MCDA twins
- **Cause**: Unbalanced arteriovenous anastomoses
- **Donor**: Anemia, oligohydramnios, IUGR, "stuck twin"
- **Recipient**: Polycythemia, polyhydramnios, cardiomegaly, hydrops

**Quintero Staging:**
| Stage | Features |
|-------|----------|
| I | Oligo-poly sequence (MVP <2cm donor, >8cm recipient) |
| II | Absent bladder in donor |
| III | Abnormal Doppler (AEDF/REDF umbilical, DV abnormal) |
| IV | Hydrops in either twin |
| V | Fetal demise of one or both |

**Treatment:**
- Mild: Serial amnioreduction
- Severe (Stage II+): **Fetoscopic laser ablation** (treatment of choice)
- Selective reduction if severe discordance

#### Twin Anemia Polycythemia Sequence (TAPS)
- Chronic form of TTTS without amniotic fluid discordance
- Diagnosed by MCA Doppler discordance

#### Twin Reversed Arterial Perfusion (TRAP) Sequence
- Acardiac/acephalic twin perfused by normal "pump" twin
- Pump twin at risk of cardiac failure
- Treatment: Cord occlusion of acardiac twin

#### Selective IUGR (sIUGR)
- EFW discordance >25%
- Type I, II, III based on Doppler patterns

#### Single Fetal Demise
- Risk to surviving co-twin (especially monochorionic)
- MC: 15-20% risk of neurological damage, death
- DC: Lower risk but monitoring needed

## Management

### Antenatal Care
- **Early chorionicity determination** (11-14 weeks) - CRUCIAL
- More frequent visits (every 2-4 weeks)
- Increased folic acid (5mg/day)
- Iron supplementation
- Nutritional counseling (extra 300 kcal/day)
- Low threshold for hospitalization

### Monitoring Schedule

| Chorionicity | Scan Frequency | Special Monitoring |
|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| DCDA | Every 4 weeks from 20 weeks | Routine |
| MCDA | Every 2 weeks from 16 weeks | TTTS screening |
| MCMA | Weekly from 16 weeks | Cord entanglement |

### Delivery Timing

| Type | Recommended Delivery |
|------|---------------------|
| DCDA (uncomplicated) | 37-38 weeks |
| MCDA (uncomplicated) | 36-37 weeks |
| MCMA | 32-34 weeks (after steroids) |
| Complicated twins | Individualized |

### Mode of Delivery

**Vaginal Delivery Criteria:**
- Twin 1 cephalic
- No obstetric contraindications
- Experienced team available
- Capability for emergency CS

**Cesarean Section Indications:**
- Twin 1 non-cephalic
- Monoamniotic twins
- Conjoined twins
- Previous cesarean (relative)
- TTTS, discordant twins
- Non-reassuring fetal status

### Intrapartum Management
- Continuous CTG of both twins
- IV access, crossmatch blood
- Anesthesia standby
- NICU standby
- After delivery of Twin 1:
  - Stabilize Twin 2 presentation
  - Clamp cord, await second delivery
  - Second stage should not exceed 30 minutes

## Complications of Twin Delivery

| Complication | Management |
|--------------|------------|
| Cord prolapse (Twin 2) | Emergency CS |
| Locked twins | Rare; emergency intervention |
| PPH | Active management, uterotonics |
| Retained placenta | Manual removal if needed |

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Lambda sign (twin peak)**: DCDA | **T sign**: MCDA
2. **Chorionicity determines risk** - always establish at first-trimester USG
3. **TTTS occurs only in monochorionic twins** (shared placenta with AV anastomoses)
4. **Quintero Stage II**: Absent donor bladder on USG
5. **Fetoscopic laser ablation**: Treatment of choice for severe TTTS
6. **MCMA twins**: Highest risk (cord entanglement); deliver by 32-34 weeks
7. **Hellin's law**: Twin 1:80, Triplet 1:80², etc.
8. **All dizygotic twins are DCDA**; monozygotic can be any type
9. **Twin 1 must be cephalic** for vaginal delivery attempt
10. **Single fetal demise in MC twins**: Risk of co-twin neurological damage (acute exsanguination)
11. **Delivery timing**: DCDA 37-38w, MCDA 36-37w, MCMA 32-34w
12. **Acardiac twin (TRAP)**: "Pump twin" does all the work; needs intervention if >50% of pump twin size
