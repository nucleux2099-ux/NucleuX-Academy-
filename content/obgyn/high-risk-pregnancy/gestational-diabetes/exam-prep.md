# Gestational Diabetes Mellitus — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
GDM = glucose intolerance first recognized in pregnancy. Screen at 24–28 weeks with OGTT. Pedersen hypothesis: maternal hyperglycemia → fetal hyperinsulinemia → macrosomia. Insulin is first-line treatment. Postpartum OGTT at 6–12 weeks.

## High Yield Points
- ★ DIPSI criteria (India): 75g non-fasting, 2hr ≥140 mg/dL
- ★ IADPSG criteria: FPG ≥92, 1hr ≥180, 2hr ≥153 (any ONE positive)
- ★ Pedersen hypothesis: maternal glucose crosses placenta; insulin does NOT
- ★ Insulin is first-line pharmacotherapy for GDM
- ★ Fasting glucose target: <95 mg/dL; 2hr PP: <120 mg/dL
- ★ Most common fetal complication: macrosomia
- ★ Most dangerous neonatal complication: hypoglycemia
- ★ 50% lifetime risk of type 2 DM in mother
- ★ Postpartum: OGTT at 6–12 weeks to reclassify
- ★ hPL is the main diabetogenic hormone of pregnancy

## Mnemonics
**Neonatal complications of GDM — "HyPo HyPo HyPo + RMP":**
- **Hypo**glycemia
- **Hypo**calcemia
- **Hypo**magnesemia (rare)
- **R**DS
- **M**acrosomia
- **P**olycythemia

**GDM risk factors — "FAT PCOS":**
- **F**amily history DM
- **A**ge >25
- **T**hick (obesity BMI >30)
- **P**revious GDM / big baby
- **C**OS (PCOS)
- **S**tillbirth history
