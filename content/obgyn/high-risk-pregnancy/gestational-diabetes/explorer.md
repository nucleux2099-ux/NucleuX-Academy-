# Gestational Diabetes Mellitus

## Overview
Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM) is defined as glucose intolerance first recognized during pregnancy. It complicates 3–14% of pregnancies depending on the population and diagnostic criteria used. GDM is increasingly prevalent due to rising obesity and sedentary lifestyles.

The condition results from insulin resistance of pregnancy (driven by placental hormones) superimposed on inadequate pancreatic beta-cell compensation. GDM increases the risk of macrosomia, shoulder dystocia, neonatal hypoglycemia, and future type 2 diabetes in both mother and child.

Screening is recommended for all pregnant women at 24–28 weeks, though high-risk women should be screened earlier. Management involves medical nutrition therapy, glucose monitoring, and insulin if targets are not met.

## Key Points
- Defined as glucose intolerance first recognized in pregnancy
- Screen all pregnant women at 24–28 weeks with OGTT
- IADPSG/WHO criteria: 75g OGTT — FPG ≥92, 1hr ≥180, 2hr ≥153 mg/dL (any one positive)
- DIPSI criteria (India): 75g glucose non-fasting, 2hr ≥140 mg/dL
- Insulin is first-line pharmacotherapy; metformin is alternative
- GDM resolves postpartum but women have 50% lifetime risk of type 2 DM

## Definition & Classification
### White's Classification (Historical)
- **Class A1:** GDM, diet-controlled
- **Class A2:** GDM, medication-required
- **Classes B–T:** Pre-gestational diabetes based on duration and complications

### Diagnostic Criteria
| Test | Criteria |
|------|---------|
| IADPSG (75g OGTT) | FPG ≥92, 1hr ≥180, 2hr ≥153 mg/dL (any one) |
| DIPSI (India) | 75g non-fasting, 2hr ≥140 mg/dL |
| Carpenter-Coustan (100g) | FPG ≥95, 1hr ≥180, 2hr ≥155, 3hr ≥140 (two values) |
| GCT screening | 50g non-fasting, 1hr ≥140 → proceed to OGTT |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology
### Normal Pregnancy
- Progressive insulin resistance from 2nd trimester
- Hormones: hPL, progesterone, cortisol, prolactin, TNF-α
- Compensatory hyperinsulinemia from beta cells

### GDM
- Insufficient beta-cell compensation → hyperglycemia
- Fetal hyperglycemia → fetal hyperinsulinemia (Pedersen hypothesis)
- Fetal hyperinsulinemia → macrosomia, organomegaly, increased fat deposition

### Risk Factors
- Obesity (BMI >30), age >25
- Family history of DM, previous GDM
- Previous macrosomic baby (>4 kg)
- PCOS, ethnicity (South Asian, Hispanic, African)
- Previous unexplained stillbirth

## Clinical Features
- Usually asymptomatic — detected by screening
- Polyuria, polydipsia (if severe hyperglycemia)
- Large for gestational age (LGA) fetus
- Polyhydramnios
- Recurrent infections (vulvovaginal candidiasis, UTI)

## Diagnosis
### Screening Strategy
1. **Universal screening** at 24–28 weeks (recommended in India)
2. **High-risk women:** Screen in first trimester; if negative, repeat at 24–28 weeks
3. **One-step approach (IADPSG):** 75g OGTT directly
4. **Two-step approach:** 50g GCT → if positive → 100g OGTT

### Target Glucose Levels (During Treatment)
| Timing | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Fasting | <95 mg/dL |
| 1-hour postprandial | <140 mg/dL |
| 2-hour postprandial | <120 mg/dL |

## Management
### Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)
- Caloric intake: 30 kcal/kg/day for normal BMI
- Carbohydrate restriction: 40% of total calories
- 3 main meals + 2–3 snacks
- Avoid simple sugars
- Regular moderate exercise (30 min/day)

### Pharmacotherapy
- **Insulin:** First-line if MNT fails (most commonly used regimen: basal-bolus)
  - Short-acting: regular insulin or insulin lispro
  - Intermediate: NPH insulin
- **Metformin:** Alternative; crosses placenta but no proven teratogenicity
- **Glyburide:** Less preferred; associated with higher neonatal hypoglycemia

### Monitoring
- Self-monitoring of blood glucose (SMBG): 4 times/day (fasting + post-meals)
- Serial ultrasound for fetal growth
- Fetal surveillance from 32–34 weeks (NST, BPP)

### Delivery
- Well-controlled GDM, no complications: await spontaneous labor up to 40 weeks
- Insulin-requiring or poorly controlled: deliver by 38–39 weeks
- EFW >4.5 kg: consider elective CS
- Intrapartum: monitor blood glucose hourly; sliding scale insulin

### Postpartum
- Discontinue insulin/metformin immediately after delivery
- 75g OGTT at 6–12 weeks postpartum to reclassify
- Annual screening for type 2 DM lifelong
- Encourage breastfeeding (reduces future DM risk)
- Lifestyle modification counseling

## Complications & Prognosis
### Maternal
- Pre-eclampsia, polyhydramnios, operative delivery
- Recurrence in future pregnancies (30–50%)
- 50% lifetime risk of type 2 DM

### Fetal/Neonatal
- Macrosomia, shoulder dystocia, birth injury
- Neonatal hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia, polycythemia
- Respiratory distress syndrome
- Hyperbilirubinemia

### Long-term (Offspring)
- Childhood obesity, metabolic syndrome
- Increased risk of type 2 DM
