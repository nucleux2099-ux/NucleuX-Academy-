# Preterm Labor — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Preterm labor = regular contractions + cervical change between 20-36+6 weeks. Leading cause of neonatal death. Strongest risk factor: previous preterm birth. Diagnosis: clinical + TVS cervical length (<25 mm) + fFN (high NPV). Management: antenatal corticosteroids (single most important intervention) + tocolysis (48h only) + MgSO4 neuroprotection (<32 wk) + GBS prophylaxis. Nifedipine = 1st line tocolytic.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Strongest risk factor**: previous preterm birth (recurrence 15-50%)
- ★ **fFN negative** = 99% won't deliver in 7-14 days (best NPV of any test)
- ★ **Cervical length <25 mm** on TVS = significant risk
- ★ **Betamethasone** 12 mg IM × 2 doses 24h apart (preferred steroid)
- ★ **Steroids reduce**: RDS 50%, IVH 70%, NEC 50%, neonatal death 40%
- ★ **Nifedipine** = 1st line tocolytic (CCB)
- ★ **Indomethacin** contraindicated >32 weeks (premature DA closure)
- ★ **MgSO4 neuroprotection** <32 weeks → reduces CP by 30-40%
- ★ **Tocolysis only for 48 hours** — to complete steroid course
- ★ **Progesterone prophylaxis**: vaginal progesterone for short cervix; 17-OHP for prior preterm birth
- ★ **Four pathways**: infection, decidual hemorrhage, overdistension, HPA activation

## Mnemonics

### **"STOP LABOR"** — Tocolytics
- **S**ulfate of Magnesium
- **T**erbutaline (β2-agonist)
- **O**xytocin antagonist (Atosiban)
- **P**rostaglandin inhibitor (Indomethacin)
- **L**ong-acting CCB (Nifedipine)

### **"BEST"** — Steroid Benefits
- **B**reathing improved (↓RDS)
- **E**nteritis reduced (↓NEC)
- **S**urvival increased (↓death)
- **T**iny vessel bleeds reduced (↓IVH)

### **"PRETERM"** — Risk Factors
- **P**revious preterm birth
- **R**upture of membranes (PPROM)
- **E**xtreme age / Ethnicity
- **T**wins/multiple pregnancy
- **E**ndocervical infection
- **R**educed cervical length
- **M**alformation of uterus

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Single most effective intervention** → Antenatal corticosteroids
2. **Best tocolytic with least side effects** → Atosiban (but expensive); Nifedipine (practical 1st line)
3. **fFN significance** → Negative value rules out preterm delivery (99% NPV)
4. **Why not tocolysis >48h?** → No proven benefit, increases maternal side effects
5. **Indomethacin complication** → Premature closure of ductus arteriosus, oligohydramnios
6. **Terbutaline side effects** → Tachycardia, pulmonary edema, hyperglycemia
7. **Contraindications to tocolysis** → Chorioamnionitis, fetal distress, severe preeclampsia
8. **Cervical length cutoff** → <25 mm (screen), <15 mm (very high risk)
9. **MgSO4 neuroprotection gestational age cutoff** → <32 weeks
10. **Prevention in high-risk women** → 17-OHP IM weekly from 16-36 weeks
