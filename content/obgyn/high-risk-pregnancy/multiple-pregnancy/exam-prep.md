# Multiple Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Twins: DZ (70%, DCDA) and MZ (30%, variable chorionicity). Chorionicity is KEY determinant of outcome. Lambda sign = DCDA; T-sign = MCDA. TTTS complicates 10–15% MCDA twins. Delivery: DCDA 37–38 wk, MCDA 36 wk, MCMA 32–34 wk (CS).

## High Yield Points
- ★ Lambda sign (twin peak) = dichorionic; T-sign = monochorionic
- ★ Chorionicity must be determined in 1st trimester (<14 weeks)
- ★ TTTS: donor (oligo, small) vs recipient (poly, large, hydrops)
- ★ Treatment of TTTS Stage II+: fetoscopic laser ablation
- ★ MCMA twins: highest risk — cord entanglement; deliver 32–34 wk by CS
- ★ Most common complication of twins: preterm birth
- ★ MZ splitting Day 4–8 → MCDA (most common type of MZ twin)
- ★ Split after Day 13 → conjoined twins
- ★ Twin A non-cephalic → cesarean section
- ★ Quintero staging: I (oligo-poly) → V (fetal death)

## Mnemonics
**MZ twin splitting timing — "3-8-13":**
- <3 days → DCDA
- 4–8 days → MCDA
- 8–13 days → MCMA
- >13 days → Conjoined

**TTTS Quintero stages — "OB DAD Has Died":**
- Stage I: **O**ligo-poly
- Stage II: **B**ladder absent (donor)
- Stage III: **D**oppler **A**bnormal
- Stage IV: **H**ydrops
- Stage V: **D**emise

## Common MCQ Topics
- Lambda vs T-sign (chorionicity)
- Timing of MZ splitting and resulting membrane configuration
- TTTS — pathophysiology, staging, treatment (laser)
- Delivery timing by chorionicity
- Mode of delivery based on presentation of Twin A
- Complications unique to MC twins (TTTS, TAPS, TRAP)
- Hellin's law (frequency of multiple pregnancy)
