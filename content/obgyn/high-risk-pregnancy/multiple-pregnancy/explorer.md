# Multiple Pregnancy

## Overview
Multiple pregnancy refers to the presence of more than one fetus in the uterus simultaneously. Twins account for the vast majority, occurring in approximately 1 in 80 pregnancies naturally (higher with ART). The incidence has risen significantly due to assisted reproduction techniques and increasing maternal age.

Multiple pregnancies are classified based on zygosity (identical vs fraternal) and chorionicity (number of placentas and amniotic sacs). Chorionicity is the most important determinant of outcome, with monochorionic twins carrying significantly higher risks due to shared placental circulation.

All multiple pregnancies are considered high-risk due to increased maternal and fetal complications including preterm birth, pre-eclampsia, IUGR, and twin-specific conditions like TTTS.

## Key Points
- Twin pregnancies: dizygotic (70%) and monozygotic (30%)
- Chorionicity determines risk — monochorionic > dichorionic
- Determine chorionicity by first-trimester ultrasound (lambda vs T-sign)
- TTTS complicates 10–15% of monochorionic diamniotic twins
- Preterm birth is the most common complication
- Delivery timing: DCDA at 37–38 wk, MCDA at 36 wk, MCMA at 32–34 wk

## Definition & Classification
### By Zygosity
- **Dizygotic (DZ):** Two ova + two sperm; always DCDA; 70% of twins
- **Monozygotic (MZ):** One ovum + one sperm; splits → chorionicity depends on timing

### By Chorionicity and Amnionicity
| Type | Split Timing | Frequency (MZ) | Membrane |
|------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| DCDA | Day 0–3 | 30% | Lambda sign (twin peak) |
| MCDA | Day 4–8 | 68% | T-sign, thin membrane |
| MCMA | Day 8–13 | 1–2% | No membrane |
| Conjoined | >Day 13 | Rare | No membrane, fused |

### Ultrasound Signs
- **Lambda sign (twin peak):** Dichorionic — triangular projection of placental tissue between membranes
- **T-sign:** Monochorionic — membranes meet placenta at right angle

## Etiology & Pathophysiology
### Risk Factors for DZ Twins
- ART (especially IVF with multiple embryo transfer)
- Family history (maternal side), race (highest in Yoruba, Nigeria)
- Advanced maternal age, high parity
- Clomiphene citrate, gonadotropins

### MZ Twin-Specific Pathology
- **TTTS:** Unbalanced arteriovenous anastomoses in shared placenta
  - Donor: oligohydramnios, IUGR, anemia
  - Recipient: polyhydramnios, cardiac overload, hydrops
- **TAPS:** Twin anemia-polycythemia sequence (chronic form)
- **TRAP:** Twin reversed arterial perfusion — acardiac twin
- **Cord entanglement:** MCMA twins — leading cause of mortality

## Clinical Features
- Uterus large for dates
- Excessive weight gain, hyperemesis
- Multiple fetal poles/heart sounds
- Polyhydramnios
- Maternal complications: anemia, pre-eclampsia, GDM, preterm labor

## Diagnosis
- **Ultrasound:** Confirm number of fetuses, chorionicity (1st trimester), amnionicity
- **Chorionicity determination:** Best assessed <14 weeks
- **Serial growth scans:** Every 2 weeks for MCDA, every 4 weeks for DCDA
- **TTTS staging (Quintero):**
  - Stage I: Oligo-polyhydramnios sequence
  - Stage II: Absent donor bladder
  - Stage III: Abnormal Doppler
  - Stage IV: Hydrops
  - Stage V: Fetal death

## Management
### Monitoring
- DCDA: Scans every 4 weeks from 20 weeks
- MCDA: Scans every 2 weeks from 16 weeks (TTTS surveillance)
- MCMA: Intensive monitoring; often inpatient from 26 weeks

### Complications Management
- **TTTS:** Fetoscopic laser ablation of anastomoses (treatment of choice for Stage II+)
- **Selective IUGR:** Depends on type and Doppler patterns
- **Discordant anomaly:** Selective feticide with intracardiac KCl (DCDA) or cord occlusion (MCDA)
- **Preterm labor prophylaxis:** Progesterone; cervical cerclage controversial

### Delivery
| Type | Recommended Timing | Mode |
|------|-------------------|------|
| DCDA | 37–38 weeks | Vaginal if Twin A cephalic |
| MCDA | 36 weeks | Vaginal if Twin A cephalic |
| MCMA | 32–34 weeks | Cesarean section |
| Higher order | 34–35 weeks | Cesarean section |

### Intrapartum Management
- Twin A cephalic + Twin B cephalic: vaginal delivery
- Twin A cephalic + Twin B non-cephalic: vaginal delivery with internal podalic version for Twin B (if experienced)
- Twin A non-cephalic: cesarean section
- Continuous CTG monitoring of both twins
- Oxytocin augmentation for second twin if needed

## Complications & Prognosis
### Maternal
- Pre-eclampsia (3x risk), GDM, anemia, PPH, operative delivery
- Preterm labor (50% deliver before 37 weeks)

### Fetal/Neonatal
- Prematurity (leading cause of morbidity/mortality)
- IUGR, discordant growth (>20% weight difference)
- TTTS (MCDA), cord entanglement (MCMA)
- Congenital anomalies (2x risk in MZ)
- Malpresentation

### Perinatal Mortality
- Singletons: ~5/1000; Twins: ~30/1000; MCMA: highest risk
