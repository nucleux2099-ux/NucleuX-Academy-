# Rh Isoimmunization

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Obstetrics 26th Ed, Ch 15; DC Dutta's Textbook of Obstetrics 9th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Rh isoimmunization (alloimmunization) occurs when an Rh-negative mother develops antibodies against Rh-positive fetal red blood cells after fetomaternal hemorrhage. These IgG antibodies cross the placenta in subsequent pregnancies and cause hemolysis of Rh-positive fetal RBCs, leading to Hemolytic Disease of the Fetus and Newborn (HDFN). Prevention with anti-D immunoglobulin has dramatically reduced the incidence, but management of sensitized pregnancies remains challenging.

## Key Points

- **Rh system**: D antigen is most immunogenic; others (C, c, E, e) less common
- **Inheritance**: Rh is autosomal dominant; Rh-negative is dd genotype
- **15% of population is Rh-negative** (varies by ethnicity)
- **First pregnancy**: Usually unaffected (sensitization occurs)
- **Subsequent Rh+ pregnancies**: Progressively more severe
- **Prevention**: Anti-D immunoglobulin within 72 hours of sensitizing event

## Pathophysiology

### Sensitization Process
1. Fetomaternal hemorrhage (FMH) occurs
2. Rh+ fetal RBCs enter Rh- maternal circulation
3. Primary immune response: IgM (does not cross placenta)
4. Memory B cells formed
5. Re-exposure in subsequent pregnancy → Secondary response: IgG
6. IgG crosses placenta → Attacks fetal Rh+ RBCs
7. Fetal hemolysis → Anemia → Complications

### Sensitizing Events
| Event | Estimated FMH Volume |
|-------|---------------------|
| Spontaneous abortion | 0.1-1 mL |
| Induced abortion | 0.1-1 mL |
| Ectopic pregnancy | Variable |
| Amniocentesis | 1-10 mL |
| CVS | 1-2 mL |
| External cephalic version | 1-5 mL |
| Delivery | 0.5-15 mL |
| Abdominal trauma | Variable |
| Placental abruption | Large |
| Manual removal of placenta | Large |

### Fetal Effects of Hemolysis
```
Fetal RBC destruction
↓
Anemia → Extramedullary hematopoiesis (liver, spleen)
↓
↓ Albumin production → Hypoproteinemia
↓
Hydrops fetalis (ascites, pleural effusion, edema)
↓
Cardiac failure → Fetal death
↓
Unconjugated bilirubin (postnatal) → Kernicterus
```

## Spectrum of Disease (HDFN)

| Severity | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Mild** | Anemia, mild jaundice, no treatment needed |
| **Moderate** | Significant anemia, jaundice, hepatosplenomegaly |
| **Severe** | Hydrops fetalis, cardiac failure, IUFD |
| **Kernicterus** | Bilirubin encephalopathy (postnatal) |

## Clinical Features

### Maternal
- Usually asymptomatic
- History of previous affected baby, transfusions, injections

### Fetal (Ultrasound Findings)
| Stage | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Early | Hepatosplenomegaly |
| Moderate | Polyhydramnios, placentomegaly |
| Severe | Ascites, pericardial effusion |
| Hydrops | Generalized edema, scalp edema, pleural effusion |
| End-stage | Cardiomegaly, decreased movements, IUFD |

## Diagnosis

### Initial Assessment (All Pregnancies)
- Blood group and Rh typing at first visit
- Indirect Coombs test (ICT) / Antibody screen

### If Rh-Negative and ICT Negative
- Repeat ICT at 28 weeks
- Give prophylactic anti-D at 28 weeks

### If ICT Positive (Sensitized)
Workup includes:

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Antibody identification** | Confirm anti-D, rule out others |
| **Antibody titer** | Quantify severity |
| **Paternal Rh typing** | Determine fetal risk |
| **Cell-free fetal DNA** | Non-invasive fetal Rh typing |
| **MCA Doppler (PSV)** | Detect fetal anemia |
| **Amniocentesis** | ΔOD450 (Liley curve) - less used now |

### Critical Titer
- **Titer ≥1:16 to 1:32**: Significant; requires close monitoring
- Higher titer = Higher risk (but not linearly correlated with severity)

### Middle Cerebral Artery Doppler
**Non-invasive gold standard for detecting fetal anemia**

| MCA-PSV | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| <1.5 MoM | No significant anemia |
| ≥1.5 MoM | Moderate-severe anemia (sensitivity 100%) |

**Advantages over amniocentesis:**
- Non-invasive
- No risk of boosting antibodies
- Repeatable
- More accurate

### Amniocentesis (Historical)
- ΔOD450: Measures bilirubin in amniotic fluid
- **Liley zones** (>24 weeks) or **Queenan curve** (any GA)
- Largely replaced by MCA Doppler

## Management

### Prevention (Non-Sensitized Rh-Negative Woman)

#### Anti-D Immunoglobulin Dosing
| Indication | Dose | Timing |
|------------|------|--------|
| **Routine antenatal prophylaxis** | 300 μg IM | 28 weeks |
| **Postpartum** (if baby Rh+) | 300 μg IM | Within 72 hours |
| **First trimester events** | 50-120 μg IM | Within 72 hours |
| **Second/third trimester events** | 300 μg IM | Within 72 hours |
| **Large FMH confirmed** | Additional doses | Kleihauer-Betke guided |

#### Indications for Anti-D
- Delivery of Rh+ baby
- Abortion (spontaneous, induced, threatened)
- Ectopic pregnancy
- Molar pregnancy
- Amniocentesis, CVS, cordocentesis
- External cephalic version
- Abdominal trauma
- Antepartum hemorrhage
- Manual removal of placenta

#### Kleihauer-Betke Test
- Detects fetal cells in maternal blood
- Estimates FMH volume
- Guide additional anti-D dosing
- Formula: % fetal cells × 50 = mL FMH
- Extra 300 μg anti-D needed per 30 mL FMH

### Management of Sensitized Pregnancy

#### Monitoring Protocol
| Titer | Management |
|-------|------------|
| <1:16 | Repeat titer monthly |
| ≥1:16 | Begin MCA Doppler (every 1-2 weeks) |
| MCA-PSV ≥1.5 MoM | Cordocentesis and possible IUT |

#### Intrauterine Transfusion (IUT)
**Indicated when MCA-PSV ≥1.5 MoM (moderate-severe anemia)**

| Type | Technique |
|------|-----------|
| **Intravascular** (preferred) | Transfusion into umbilical vein |
| **Intraperitoneal** | Into fetal peritoneal cavity |

**Blood used**: O-negative, CMV-negative, irradiated, cross-matched with mother, Hct 75-85%

**Can be repeated** every 2-4 weeks until delivery feasible

**Survival**: >90% in experienced centers

#### Delivery Timing
| Scenario | Timing |
|----------|--------|
| Mild disease | 37-38 weeks |
| Moderate, stable | 34-37 weeks |
| Severe, requiring multiple IUT | 32-34 weeks (after steroids) |
| Hydrops | May need earlier delivery |

### Neonatal Management
- Blood group and Coombs test (cord blood)
- Hemoglobin, bilirubin, reticulocyte count
- Phototherapy (mild-moderate jaundice)
- Exchange transfusion (severe anemia/hyperbilirubinemia)
- IVIG (to reduce need for exchange transfusion)

## ABO Incompatibility

| Feature | Rh HDFN | ABO HDFN |
|---------|---------|----------|
| First pregnancy | Rare | Can occur |
| Severity | Severe | Usually mild |
| Hydrops | Common if severe | Rare |
| DCT (Coombs) | Strongly positive | Weakly positive/negative |
| Phototherapy | Often needed | Usually sufficient |
| Exchange transfusion | Often needed | Rarely needed |

**ABO incompatibility**: Mother O, Baby A or B
- Naturally occurring anti-A/anti-B (IgM mostly, some IgG)
- Usually mild because A/B antigens expressed on many tissues (absorb antibodies)

## Prevention Failures

Anti-D may fail if:
- Dose insufficient for FMH volume
- Given >72 hours after sensitizing event
- Pre-existing sensitization not detected
- Antibody to other Rh antigens (C, E)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Anti-D should be given within 72 hours** of sensitizing event (ideally <24 hours)
2. **MCA Doppler PSV ≥1.5 MoM** indicates moderate-severe fetal anemia
3. **Indirect Coombs test (ICT)** screens for antibodies in maternal serum
4. **Direct Coombs test (DCT)** detects antibodies on fetal/neonatal RBCs
5. **Critical titer ≥1:16-1:32** warrants close surveillance
6. **IUT is the treatment** for severe fetal anemia; can be done from 18-34 weeks
7. **First pregnancy is usually unaffected** (sensitization occurs at delivery)
8. **O-negative blood** used for IUT (crossmatched with mother)
9. **Kleihauer-Betke test**: % fetal cells × 50 = FMH in mL
10. **Standard anti-D dose covers 30 mL FMH** (300 μg IM)
11. **ABO incompatibility** is usually mild and does not require antenatal intervention
12. **Hydrops fetalis** = End-stage disease; high fetal mortality without intervention
13. **Cell-free fetal DNA** can determine fetal Rh status non-invasively
