# Antepartum Hemorrhage — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
APH = bleeding after 28 weeks. Major causes: placenta previa (painless) and abruption (painful). Never do vaginal exam before excluding previa by ultrasound. Abruption can cause DIC; previa is managed by CS if complete.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Placenta previa: painless, bright red, recurrent bleeding; soft uterus
- ★ Abruption: painful, dark blood, tense/tender "woody hard" uterus
- ★ NEVER do vaginal exam until placenta previa excluded by USG
- ★ Shock out of proportion to visible blood = concealed abruption
- ★ DIC is common in abruption, rare in previa
- ★ Most important RF for previa: previous cesarean section
- ★ Most important RF for abruption: hypertension
- ★ Couvelaire uterus = blood infiltrating myometrium in severe abruption
- ★ Vasa previa: Apt test positive (fetal blood); emergency CS
- ★ Fibrinogen <200 mg/dL in abruption = likely DIC
- ★ Placenta accreta spectrum: suspect when previa + previous CS

## Mnemonics
**Previa vs Abruption — "PREVIA is Painless":**
- Previa = Painless, Placenta seen on USG
- Abruption = Agonizing, Associated with hypertension

**Abruption risk factors — "HATCHED":**
- **H**ypertension
- **A**bruption previous
- **T**rauma
- **C**ocaine
- **H**igh parity
- **E**arly ROM (PROM)
- **D**rug (cocaine/smoking)
