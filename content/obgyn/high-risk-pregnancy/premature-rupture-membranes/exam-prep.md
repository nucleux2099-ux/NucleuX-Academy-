# Premature Rupture of Membranes — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

PROM = membrane rupture before labor onset. PPROM = before 37 weeks. Diagnosis: history + sterile speculum (pooling, nitrazine, ferning). NO digital exam. Management: term → deliver within 24h; 34-37 wk → deliver; 24-34 wk → expectant (steroids + latency antibiotics + monitoring); <24 wk → counsel (pulmonary hypoplasia risk). Chorioamnionitis = deliver immediately regardless of GA.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common cause of preterm birth** — PPROM accounts for ~1/3 of preterm deliveries
- ★ **Strongest risk factor** — previous PPROM (recurrence 16-32%)
- ★ **NO digital vaginal exam** — increases infection, shortens latency
- ★ **Nitrazine test**: amniotic fluid alkaline (pH 7.0-7.5) → turns blue; false +ve with blood, semen, BV
- ★ **Fern test**: arborization pattern on air-dried slide — most specific bedside test
- ★ **AmniSure (PAMG-1)**: most accurate test (98-99% sensitivity)
- ★ **Latency antibiotics**: Ampicillin + Azithromycin — prolongs latency 5-7 days
- ★ **Avoid tocolysis beyond 48 hours** — only to complete steroid course
- ★ **MgSO4 neuroprotection** if <32 weeks and delivery imminent
- ★ **Pulmonary hypoplasia** — lethal complication if PPROM <20 weeks
- ★ **Chorioamnionitis signs**: fever, tachycardia (maternal + fetal), uterine tenderness, foul discharge

## Mnemonics

### **"PROM DRAINS"** — Risk Factors
- **P**revious PROM
- **R**upture (cervical insufficiency)
- **O**verdistension (polyhydramnios, twins)
- **M**ultiple pregnancy
- **D**eficiency (Vitamin C, zinc)
- **R**ecurrent infection (BV, GBS)
- **A**mniocentesis
- **I**nflammation / Infection
- **N**utrition poor
- **S**moking

### **"FACT"** — Diagnosis of PROM
- **F**erning test
- **A**mniSure (PAMG-1)
- **C**linical history (gush of fluid)
- **T**urn blue (Nitrazine test)

### **"CHORIO"** — Signs of Chorioamnionitis
- **C**RP elevated
- **H**eart rate up (fetal tachycardia)
- **O**dor (foul-smelling discharge)
- **R**ise in temperature (≥38°C)
- **I**rritable uterus (tenderness)
- **O**verall WBC elevated

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Most accurate test for PROM** → AmniSure (PAMG-1)
2. **Why avoid digital exam?** → Increases infection risk, shortens latency
3. **Management at 30 weeks PPROM** → Steroids + latency antibiotics + expectant
4. **False positive nitrazine** → Blood, semen, BV, alkaline antiseptics
5. **Antibiotic regimen** → Ampicillin IV 48h → Amoxicillin PO 5 days + Azithromycin
6. **When to deliver immediately** → Chorioamnionitis, fetal distress, cord prolapse, abruption
7. **Complication of previable PROM** → Pulmonary hypoplasia, Potter sequence
8. **Latency period** → Inversely proportional to gestational age
9. **% entering labor within 24h at term** → 80%
10. **Recurrence risk** → 16-32%
