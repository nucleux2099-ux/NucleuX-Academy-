# Postpartum Hemorrhage — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
PPH = blood loss ≥500 mL after vaginal delivery. Most common cause: uterine atony (70%). AMTSL with oxytocin is best prevention. Management: resuscitate → identify cause (4 T's) → stepwise uterotonics → surgical if refractory.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Most common cause of maternal mortality WORLDWIDE: PPH
- ★ Most common cause of PPH: uterine atony (70–80%)
- ★ 4 T's: Tone, Trauma, Tissue, Thrombin
- ★ AMTSL: Oxytocin 10 IU IM + controlled cord traction + uterine massage
- ★ Ergometrine: contraindicated in HYPERTENSION
- ★ Carboprost (PGF2α): contraindicated in ASTHMA
- ★ Tranexamic acid 1 g IV — give within 3 hours of delivery
- ★ B-Lynch suture: compression suture for atony refractory to uterotonics
- ★ Sheehan syndrome: pituitary necrosis from PPH — failure of lactation
- ★ Massive transfusion: RBC:FFP:Platelets = 1:1:1

## Mnemonics
**4 T's of PPH:**
- **T**one (atony — 70%)
- **T**rauma (lacerations — 20%)
- **T**issue (retained — 10%)
- **T**hrombin (coagulopathy — 1%)

**Uterotonics order — "OEM-CT-BUH":**
- **O**xytocin
- **E**rgometrine
- **M**isoprostol
- **C**arboprost
- **T**ranexamic acid
- **B**alloon tamponade
- **U**terine compression sutures
