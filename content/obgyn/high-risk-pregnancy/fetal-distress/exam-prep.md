# Fetal Distress — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Non-reassuring fetal status detected by CTG abnormalities during labor. Normal FHR 110–160 bpm. Late decelerations = uteroplacental insufficiency. Fetal scalp pH <7.20 = acidosis. Manage with intrauterine resuscitation or expedited delivery.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Normal FHR baseline: 110–160 bpm; normal variability: 5–25 bpm
- ★ Late decelerations = uteroplacental insufficiency (OMINOUS)
- ★ Early decelerations = head compression (BENIGN)
- ★ Variable decelerations = cord compression
- ★ Sinusoidal pattern = severe fetal anemia (most ominous)
- ★ Fetal scalp pH <7.20 = acidosis → expedite delivery
- ★ Intrauterine resuscitation: left lateral + IV fluids + stop oxytocin + tocolysis
- ★ Meconium-stained liquor: warning sign but NOT diagnostic of distress alone
- ★ Cord prolapse: elevate presenting part + emergency CS
- ★ Therapeutic hypothermia for HIE: within 6 hours of birth

## Mnemonics
**CTG interpretation — "DR C BRAVADO":**
- **D**efine **R**isk
- **C**ontractions
- **B**aseline **R**ate
- **A**ccelerations
- **V**ariability
- **A**nd **D**ecelerations
- **O**verall impression

**Decelerations — "EVL":**
- **E**arly = head compression (benign)
- **V**ariable = cord compression
- **L**ate = placental insufficiency (bad)
