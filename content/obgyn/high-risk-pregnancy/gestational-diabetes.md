# Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM)

> 📚 **Sources:** Williams Obstetrics 26th Ed, Ch 57; ACOG Practice Bulletin; DIPSI Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG | SS

## Core Concept

Gestational Diabetes Mellitus is defined as carbohydrate intolerance of variable severity with onset or first recognition during pregnancy. The diabetogenic state of pregnancy results from placental hormones (hPL, cortisol, progesterone) causing insulin resistance. GDM affects 3-14% of pregnancies and is associated with significant maternal and fetal morbidity if uncontrolled.

## Key Points

- **Definition**: Glucose intolerance first recognized during pregnancy
- **Pathophysiology**: Placental hormones → insulin resistance → β-cell compensation failure
- **Peak insulin resistance**: 24-28 weeks (optimal screening time)
- **hPL (Human Placental Lactogen)**: Primary diabetogenic hormone
- **Risk factors**: Obesity, age >25, family history of DM, previous GDM, macrosomia, PCOS, ethnicity (South Asian, Hispanic)
- **Recurrence rate**: 30-70% in subsequent pregnancies
- **Future DM risk**: 50% develop Type 2 DM within 20 years

## Screening & Diagnosis

### Who to Screen?
- **Universal screening** recommended (especially in high-prevalence populations like India)
- Timing: 24-28 weeks gestation
- Earlier if high-risk factors present

### Screening Methods

#### 1. DIPSI (Diabetes in Pregnancy Study Group India) - Single Step
- **75g OGTT** (non-fasting, any time of day)
- **Diagnostic**: 2-hour plasma glucose ≥140 mg/dL
- Advantages: Single step, practical, doesn't require fasting

#### 2. Two-Step Approach (ACOG)
**Step 1: Glucose Challenge Test (GCT)**
- 50g glucose, non-fasting
- 1-hour plasma glucose measured
- Positive if ≥130-140 mg/dL → proceed to Step 2

**Step 2: 100g OGTT (Carpenter-Coustan Criteria)**
| Time | Threshold |
|------|-----------|
| Fasting | ≥95 mg/dL |
| 1 hour | ≥180 mg/dL |
| 2 hours | ≥155 mg/dL |
| 3 hours | ≥140 mg/dL |
- **Diagnosis**: ≥2 abnormal values

#### 3. IADPSG/WHO Criteria (One-Step 75g OGTT)
| Time | Threshold |
|------|-----------|
| Fasting | ≥92 mg/dL |
| 1 hour | ≥180 mg/dL |
| 2 hours | ≥153 mg/dL |
- **Diagnosis**: ≥1 abnormal value

### Differentiating GDM from Overt Diabetes
| Feature | GDM | Overt DM in Pregnancy |
|---------|-----|----------------------|
| FPG | <126 mg/dL | ≥126 mg/dL |
| Random glucose | - | ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms |
| HbA1c | <6.5% | ≥6.5% |
| First trimester diagnosis | Consider overt DM | Likely overt DM |

## Clinical Features

### Maternal Symptoms
- Often asymptomatic (detected on screening)
- Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia (if severe)
- Recurrent infections (candidiasis, UTI)
- Excessive weight gain

### Risk Indicators
- Previous macrosomic baby (>4 kg)
- Previous unexplained stillbirth
- Previous congenital malformation
- Recurrent pregnancy loss
- Polyhydramnios in current pregnancy
- Large for gestational age fetus

## Complications

### Maternal Complications
| Immediate | Long-term |
|-----------|-----------|
| Preeclampsia (2-4× increased) | Type 2 DM (50% in 20 years) |
| Polyhydramnios | Metabolic syndrome |
| Preterm labor | Cardiovascular disease |
| Cesarean delivery | Recurrent GDM |
| Operative vaginal delivery | |
| Postpartum hemorrhage | |
| Infections | |

### Fetal/Neonatal Complications
| Fetal | Neonatal |
|-------|----------|
| Macrosomia (>4 kg) | Hypoglycemia |
| IUGR (if vasculopathy) | Hypocalcemia |
| Polyhydramnios | Hyperbilirubinemia |
| Organomegaly | Polycythemia |
| Congenital anomalies (overt DM) | Respiratory distress syndrome |
| IUFD | Birth injuries |
| | Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy |

### Congenital Anomalies (Pre-gestational DM)
- **Caudal regression syndrome** (sacral agenesis) - MOST SPECIFIC
- Neural tube defects
- Cardiac defects (VSD, TGA)
- Renal anomalies
- GI atresia

## Management

### Goals of Therapy
| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Fasting glucose | <95 mg/dL |
| 1-hour postprandial | <140 mg/dL |
| 2-hour postprandial | <120 mg/dL |
| HbA1c | <6% (ideally <5.5%) |

### Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)
- **First-line treatment** for all GDM
- Total calories: 30-35 kcal/kg (25 kcal/kg if obese)
- Carbohydrates: 40-50% (complex, low glycemic index)
- Protein: 20-25%
- Fat: 25-30%
- 3 meals + 2-3 snacks
- **Success rate**: 70-85% controlled with diet alone

### Exercise
- 30 minutes moderate activity daily
- Walking, swimming (low-impact preferred)
- Avoid supine position after first trimester

### Pharmacotherapy

#### Insulin (Gold Standard)
| Type | Onset | Peak | Duration | Use |
|------|-------|------|----------|-----|
| **Rapid-acting** (Lispro, Aspart) | 5-15 min | 1-2 hr | 3-5 hr | Prandial |
| **Short-acting** (Regular) | 30-60 min | 2-4 hr | 6-8 hr | Prandial |
| **Intermediate** (NPH) | 1-3 hr | 6-12 hr | 16-24 hr | Basal |
| **Long-acting** (Glargine, Detemir) | 1-2 hr | Flat | 24 hr | Basal |

**Starting dose**: 0.7-1.0 units/kg/day (divided)
- Basal:Bolus = 50:50
- Higher insulin requirement as pregnancy advances

#### Oral Hypoglycemics
| Drug | Crosses Placenta | Use |
|------|------------------|-----|
| **Metformin** | Yes (minimal) | May use if insulin refused; ↑preterm risk |
| **Glyburide** | Minimal | Alternative but less preferred now |
| Acarbose | No | Limited data |
| Others | - | CONTRAINDICATED |

### Monitoring

#### Self-Monitoring Blood Glucose (SMBG)
- Fasting + post-meal (1 or 2 hours after meals)
- Minimum 4 times/day
- Target: Fasting <95, 1-hr <140, 2-hr <120 mg/dL

#### Fetal Surveillance
- **USG**: Every 4 weeks for growth, anomaly scan at 18-20 weeks
- **NST**: Weekly from 32-34 weeks (earlier if complications)
- **BPP**: If NST non-reactive
- **Doppler**: If IUGR suspected
- **Fetal echocardiography**: If pregestational DM or poor control

### Delivery

#### Timing
| Scenario | Timing |
|----------|--------|
| GDM, diet-controlled | 39-40 weeks (await spontaneous) |
| GDM, well-controlled on meds | 39 weeks |
| GDM, poor control | 37-39 weeks |
| Macrosomia or complications | Individual decision |

#### Mode
- **Vaginal delivery** preferred if EFW <4-4.5 kg
- **Cesarean**: Consider if EFW >4.5 kg (high shoulder dystocia risk)
- Be prepared for shoulder dystocia (McRoberts, suprapubic pressure)

### Intrapartum Glucose Management
- Target: 70-110 mg/dL
- IV dextrose + sliding scale insulin
- Stop long-acting insulin; use regular/rapid-acting

### Postpartum Care
- **Insulin requirements drop dramatically** (may not need any)
- Breastfeeding encouraged (improves glucose tolerance)
- **6-12 week postpartum OGTT** (75g) to reclassify:
  - Normal
  - Impaired fasting glucose
  - Impaired glucose tolerance
  - Type 2 DM
- Annual screening thereafter
- Lifestyle modification counseling
- Contraception counseling

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Optimal screening time**: 24-28 weeks (peak insulin resistance)
2. **DIPSI criteria (India)**: 75g OGTT, 2-hr ≥140 mg/dL (non-fasting)
3. **hPL** is the main diabetogenic hormone of pregnancy
4. **Caudal regression syndrome** is most specific for diabetic embryopathy
5. **MNT controls 70-85%** of GDM; insulin is added if targets not met
6. **Insulin is the gold standard**; metformin may be used if insulin refused
7. **Macrosomia** is the most common fetal complication
8. **Neonatal hypoglycemia** occurs due to fetal hyperinsulinism
9. **Postpartum OGTT at 6-12 weeks** is mandatory to reclassify
10. **50% develop Type 2 DM** within 20 years - lifelong surveillance needed
11. **Shoulder dystocia risk** increases with macrosomia - anticipate and prepare
12. **HbA1c is NOT used for GDM diagnosis** (only for pregestational DM assessment)
