# Pre-eclampsia & Eclampsia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Pre-eclampsia is hypertension + proteinuria/organ dysfunction after 20 weeks. Eclampsia = seizures in pre-eclampsia. MgSO4 is drug of choice. Definitive treatment is delivery. HELLP syndrome is a severe variant with hemolysis, elevated liver enzymes, low platelets.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Drug of choice for eclampsia: Magnesium sulfate (NOT diazepam)
- ★ MgSO4 antidote: Calcium gluconate 1g IV
- ★ MgSO4 toxicity monitoring: Patellar reflex (first to disappear), respiratory rate, urine output
- ★ HELLP triad: Hemolysis + Elevated Liver enzymes + Low Platelets
- ★ Pathophysiology: Defective spiral artery remodeling → placental ischemia → endothelial dysfunction
- ★ Anti-angiogenic factors: sFlt-1 raised, PlGF reduced (sFlt-1:PlGF ratio diagnostic)
- ★ Aspirin prophylaxis: 75-150 mg from 12 weeks in high-risk women
- ★ Most common cause of death in eclampsia: Cerebral hemorrhage
- ★ Postpartum eclampsia: Can occur up to 6 weeks after delivery (most within 48 hours)
- ★ Glomerular endotheliosis: Pathognomonic renal lesion of pre-eclampsia
- ★ Pritchard regimen: 4g IV + 10g IM (loading), 5g IM q4h (maintenance)
- ★ Uric acid: Earliest lab change in pre-eclampsia

## Mnemonics
**HELLP** — the name itself:
- **H**emolysis
- **EL**evated Liver enzymes
- **LP** — Low Platelets

**MgSO4 Toxicity** — "RRU" (monitor these):
- **R**eflexes (patellar — absent = stop)
- **R**espiratory rate (<16 = stop)
- **U**rine output (<25 mL/hr = stop)
