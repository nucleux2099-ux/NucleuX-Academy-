# Ectopic Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Ectopic = implantation outside uterine cavity. Ampulla is most common site (70%). Triad: amenorrhea + pain + bleeding. β-hCG >1500 + empty uterus on TVS = ectopic. Medical (MTX) for unruptured; surgery for ruptured.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Most common site: ampulla of fallopian tube (70%)
- ★ Most common cause of 1st trimester maternal mortality
- ★ Classic triad: amenorrhea + vaginal bleeding + lower abdominal pain
- ★ Discriminatory zone: β-hCG >1500 mIU/mL with empty uterus = ectopic
- ★ Normal β-hCG doubling time: ≥66% rise in 48 hours
- ★ MTX criteria: stable, unruptured, β-hCG <5000, size <4 cm, no cardiac activity
- ★ MTX dose: 50 mg/m² IM; check β-hCG days 4 and 7
- ★ Ruptured ectopic: emergency salpingectomy
- ★ Interstitial (cornual) ectopic: ruptures late (8–16 wk), most dangerous
- ★ Shoulder tip pain = diaphragmatic irritation from hemoperitoneum
- ★ Recurrence risk: 10–15%

## Mnemonics
**Ectopic risk factors — "PIPES":**
- **P**ID / previous ectopic
- **I**UD in situ
- **P**elvic surgery / tubal surgery
- **E**ndometriosis
- **S**moking

**Sites of tubal ectopic (frequency order):**
- **A**mpulla > **I**sthmus > **F**imbrial > **I**nterstitial
