# Vaccination in Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
TT is universally recommended in pregnancy. Inactivated vaccines (influenza, Tdap, Hep B) are safe. Live vaccines (MMR, varicella, BCG, OPV) are contraindicated. Tdap at 27–36 weeks protects neonates from pertussis.

## High Yield Points
- ★ TT is the MOST IMPORTANT vaccine in pregnancy — prevents neonatal tetanus
- ★ Live vaccines are CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy
- ★ Tdap given at 27–36 weeks in EVERY pregnancy
- ★ Influenza (inactivated) is safe in ALL trimesters
- ★ Rubella vaccine: give POSTPARTUM if non-immune; avoid conception for 1 month
- ★ Inadvertent rubella vaccination is NOT an indication for MTP
- ★ Hepatitis B vaccine can be given if indicated (inactivated, safe)
- ★ Anti-D is NOT a vaccine — it's passive immunoprophylaxis
- ★ IgG crosses placenta maximally in 3rd trimester via FcRn receptors
- ★ HPV vaccine: defer until postpartum (not studied in pregnancy)
