# Medical Disorders in Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Medical disorders are a leading cause of maternal morbidity. Key conditions include hypertensive disorders, GDM, anemia, thyroid disease, and heart disease. Management requires knowledge of safe drugs in pregnancy and multidisciplinary care.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Most common medical disorder in Indian pregnancy: anemia
- ★ Anti-hypertensives safe in pregnancy: labetalol, methyldopa, nifedipine
- ★ ACE inhibitors & ARBs are CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy
- ★ MgSO4 is drug of choice for eclampsia prevention and treatment
- ★ PTU preferred in 1st trimester; methimazole in 2nd/3rd trimester
- ★ Valproate is most teratogenic anti-epileptic (NTDs)
- ★ Warfarin crosses placenta — causes nasal hypoplasia, chondrodysplasia punctata
- ★ LMWH is anticoagulant of choice in pregnancy
- ★ Insulin is first-line for GDM not controlled by diet
- ★ Levothyroxine dose needs 30–50% increase in pregnancy
- ★ Heart disease with highest mortality: Eisenmenger syndrome, pulmonary HTN

## Mnemonics
**Drugs contraindicated in pregnancy — "WAVED":**
- **W**arfarin
- **A**CE inhibitors
- **V**alproate
- **E**nalapril (ARBs)

**Safe antihypertensives — "LMN":**

- **M**ethyldopa
