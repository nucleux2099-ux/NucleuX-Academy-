# Antenatal Screening Tests

## Overview
Antenatal screening tests are a critical component of prenatal care designed to identify pregnancies at increased risk for chromosomal abnormalities, structural defects, and other complications. These tests are offered to all pregnant women regardless of age or risk factors, and include both biochemical markers and ultrasonographic assessments.

The screening approach has evolved from second-trimester-only protocols to integrated first- and second-trimester strategies. Modern screening combines maternal serum biomarkers (such as PAPP-A, free β-hCG, AFP, uE3, and inhibin A) with nuchal translucency measurement by ultrasound, and more recently, cell-free fetal DNA (cfDNA/NIPT) testing.

Early and accurate screening allows timely counseling, further diagnostic testing (CVS or amniocentesis), and informed decision-making by the parents. It is essential to distinguish screening tests (which estimate risk) from diagnostic tests (which confirm or exclude a condition).

## Key Points
- Screening tests estimate probability; they do not diagnose
- First-trimester combined test: NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG (11–13+6 weeks)
- Quadruple test (second trimester): AFP + hCG + uE3 + inhibin A (15–20 weeks)
- Integrated screening combines first- and second-trimester markers for highest detection rate
- NIPT (cfDNA) has >99% sensitivity for trisomy 21 but is still a screening test
- Abnormal screening requires confirmation by CVS or amniocentesis

## Definition & Classification
**Screening test:** A test applied to an asymptomatic population to identify individuals at higher risk for a specific condition.

### Classification by Trimester
| Test | Timing | Markers |
|------|--------|---------|
| First-trimester combined | 11–13+6 wk | NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG |
| Triple test | 15–20 wk | AFP + hCG + uE3 |
| Quadruple test | 15–20 wk | AFP + hCG + uE3 + inhibin A |
| Integrated test | 1st + 2nd trimester | Combined + Quad markers |
| Sequential/contingent | 1st + 2nd trimester | Stepwise with early results |
| NIPT (cfDNA) | ≥10 wk | Cell-free fetal DNA in maternal blood |

### Classification by Modality
- **Biochemical:** Serum markers (PAPP-A, β-hCG, AFP, uE3, inhibin A)
- **Ultrasonographic:** Nuchal translucency, nasal bone, ductus venosus
- **Genetic:** cfDNA / NIPT

## Etiology & Pathophysiology
Chromosomal aneuploidies (trisomy 21, 18, 13) arise from meiotic non-disjunction. The incidence increases with maternal age. Screening biomarkers reflect altered placental or fetal physiology:

- **↓ PAPP-A** in trisomy 21 (first trimester) — reduced placental function
- **↑ free β-hCG** in trisomy 21 — altered trophoblast differentiation
- **↑ NT** — subcutaneous fluid accumulation due to cardiac defects, lymphatic dysplasia, or altered connective tissue
- **↓ AFP** in trisomy 21 — reduced fetal hepatic production
- **↑ AFP** in neural tube defects — leakage from open defect
- **↓ uE3** in trisomy 21, trisomy 18 — impaired feto-placental steroidogenesis

## Clinical Features
Screening tests are performed on asymptomatic women. Indications for offering screening include:
- All pregnancies (universal screening recommended)
- Advanced maternal age (≥35 years)
- Previous child with chromosomal abnormality
- Family history of genetic disorders
- Abnormal ultrasound findings

## Diagnosis
### Interpretation of Screening Results
Results are expressed as a risk ratio (e.g., 1:250). Screen-positive cutoffs vary by institution.

**Down syndrome (Trisomy 21):**
- ↑ NT, ↑ β-hCG, ↓ PAPP-A (1st trimester)
- ↓ AFP, ↑ hCG, ↓ uE3, ↑ inhibin A (2nd trimester)

**Edwards syndrome (Trisomy 18):**
- ↓ all markers (AFP, hCG, uE3) in second trimester
- ↓ PAPP-A, ↓ β-hCG in first trimester

**Neural Tube Defects:**
- ↑ AFP (second trimester), ↑ amniotic fluid AChE

### Detection Rates
| Test | Detection Rate (T21) | False Positive Rate |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|
| First-trimester combined | 82–87% | 5% |
| Quadruple test | 81% | 5% |
| Integrated test | 94–96% | 5% |
| NIPT | >99% | <0.1% |

### Diagnostic Confirmation
- **CVS** (10–13 weeks): chorionic villus sampling for karyotype
- **Amniocentesis** (15–20 weeks): amniotic fluid for karyotype, AFP, AChE

## Management
1. **Pre-test counseling:** Explain difference between screening and diagnosis, detection rates, false-positive rates
2. **Offer appropriate test based on gestational age:**
   - <14 weeks → first-trimester combined or NIPT
   - 14–20 weeks → quadruple test
   - Any gestation ≥10 weeks → NIPT
3. **Screen-positive:** Counsel and offer diagnostic testing (CVS/amniocentesis)
4. **Confirmed aneuploidy:** Non-directive counseling, multidisciplinary input, discuss options
5. **Screen-negative:** Routine antenatal care; explain residual risk
6. **Anomaly scan at 18–20 weeks** for all pregnancies regardless of screening result

## Complications & Prognosis
- Screening tests themselves carry no procedural risk
- **CVS:** 0.5–1% miscarriage risk
- **Amniocentesis:** 0.1–0.3% miscarriage risk
- False positives cause significant parental anxiety
- False negatives may lead to missed diagnoses
- With modern integrated or NIPT screening, detection rates for trisomy 21 exceed 95%
