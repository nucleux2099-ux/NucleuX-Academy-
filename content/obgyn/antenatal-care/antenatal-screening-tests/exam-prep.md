# Antenatal Screening Tests — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Antenatal screening uses biochemical markers and ultrasound to estimate risk of chromosomal abnormalities and NTDs. First-trimester combined test (NT + PAPP-A + β-hCG) and quadruple test (AFP + hCG + uE3 + inhibin A) are standard. NIPT has highest sensitivity but is still a screening test.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Down syndrome: ↑ NT, ↑ β-hCG, ↓ PAPP-A (1st tri); ↓ AFP, ↑ hCG, ↓ uE3, ↑ inhibin A (2nd tri)
- ★ Edwards syndrome (T18): ALL markers LOW in 2nd trimester
- ★ NTD: ↑ AFP + ↑ AChE in amniotic fluid
- ★ NT measured at 11–13+6 weeks; CRL 45–84 mm
- ★ NIPT can be done from 10 weeks; >99% sensitivity for T21
- ★ CVS done at 10–13 wk; amniocentesis at 15–20 wk
- ★ Integrated test has highest detection rate (~95%) among conventional screens
- ★ Screening ≠ Diagnosis — always confirm with CVS/amniocentesis
- ★ PAPP-A is LOW in all trisomies in first trimester
- ★ Inhibin A is the 4th marker that upgrades triple → quadruple test

## Mnemonics
**Down syndrome 2nd trimester markers — "DAUI":**
- **D**own = ↓ AFP, ↑ hCG (↑ Up), ↓ uE3 (↓), ↑ Inhibin A

**Quad test markers — "AHEI":**
- **A**FP, **H**CG, u**E**3, **I**nhibin A

## Common MCQ Topics
- Marker patterns for T21 vs T18 vs NTD
