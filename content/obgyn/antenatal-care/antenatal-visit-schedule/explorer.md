# Antenatal Visit Schedule

## Overview
Antenatal care (ANC) is the systematic supervision of pregnancy from conception to the onset of labor. A structured visit schedule ensures timely detection of complications, health education, and appropriate interventions. The WHO recommends a minimum of 8 contacts during pregnancy for a positive pregnancy experience.

In India, the Government of India under the National Health Mission recommends at least 4 ANC visits, though recent guidelines align with WHO's 8-contact model. The schedule is designed so that visits are more frequent in the third trimester when complications are more likely.

Each visit has specific objectives including clinical assessment, laboratory investigations, screening for complications, nutritional supplementation, immunization, birth preparedness, and counseling.

## Key Points
- WHO recommends minimum 8 ANC contacts (2016 model)
- Indian national program: minimum 4 visits (1st, 2nd, 3rd trimester, and 36+ weeks)
- First visit ideally before 12 weeks (booking visit)
- Monthly visits until 28 weeks, fortnightly until 36 weeks, weekly until delivery
- Each visit has specific screening and intervention goals
- High-risk pregnancies require more frequent monitoring

## Definition & Classification
**Antenatal care:** Comprehensive care provided to a pregnant woman from confirmation of pregnancy until the onset of labor, aimed at achieving a healthy mother and healthy baby.

### WHO 2016 ANC Model (8 Contacts)
| Contact | Gestation | Key Activities |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Up to 12 wk | Booking, history, baseline investigations, dating scan |
| 2 | 20 wk | Anomaly scan, BP, urine, Hb |
| 3 | 26 wk | BP, urine, Hb, GDM screening |
| 4 | 30 wk | BP, growth assessment, anemia check |
| 5 | 34 wk | BP, growth, presentation, pre-eclampsia screening |
| 6 | 36 wk | BP, presentation, birth plan |
| 7 | 38 wk | BP, growth, plan for delivery |
| 8 | 40 wk | BP, post-dates plan |

### Indian National Guidelines (4 ANC Visits — NRHM)
| Visit | Timing | Key Activities |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| 1st | Within 12 wk | Registration, history, baseline investigations, TT, IFA |
| 2nd | 14–26 wk | Weight, BP, Hb, fundal height, fetal heart |
| 3rd | 28–34 wk | Same + presentation, edema check |
| 4th | 36 wk–term | Presentation, birth plan, referral if needed |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology
The rationale for structured visits is based on the natural history of pregnancy complications:
- **First trimester:** Ectopic pregnancy, miscarriage, dating
- **Second trimester:** Anomalies, cervical incompetence, gestational diabetes
- **Early third trimester:** Pre-eclampsia, IUGR, anemia
- **Late third trimester:** Malpresentation, placenta previa, preterm labor

## Clinical Features
### Booking Visit (First Visit) — Most Important
- Complete obstetric and medical history
- General and systemic examination
- Per-abdomen and per-vaginal examination
- Height, weight, BMI, BP
- Investigations: blood group + Rh, Hb, urine routine, blood sugar, VDRL, HIV, HBsAg
- Dating ultrasound
- Risk stratification

### Subsequent Visits
Each visit should include:
- Blood pressure measurement
- Weight gain assessment
- Fundal height (symphysis-fundal height after 24 weeks)
- Fetal heart rate auscultation
- Urine for protein and sugar
- Assessment for danger signs

## Diagnosis
ANC visits are not diagnostic per se but aim to identify:
- **Hypertensive disorders:** BP ≥140/90 mmHg, proteinuria
- **Gestational diabetes:** OGTT at 24–28 weeks
- **Anemia:** Hb <11 g/dL
- **IUGR:** SFH less than expected, confirmed by ultrasound
- **Malpresentation:** After 36 weeks
- **Rh isoimmunization:** Rh-negative mother with antibody titers

## Management
### Nutritional Supplementation
- **Iron + Folic Acid (IFA):** 100 mg elemental iron + 500 μg folic acid daily from 2nd trimester
- **Calcium:** 1 g/day (especially in low-intake populations)
- **Folic acid alone:** 400 μg/day in periconceptional period (ideally 3 months preconception)

### Immunization
- **Tetanus Toxoid (TT):** 2 doses — TT1 early in pregnancy, TT2 at least 4 weeks after TT1
- Previously immunized: booster dose

### Birth Preparedness
- Identification of skilled birth attendant
- Place of delivery
- Transport arrangement
- Funds for emergency
- Blood donor identification

### Danger Signs to Counsel
- Vaginal bleeding
- Severe headache / blurred vision
- Convulsions
- Severe abdominal pain
- Reduced fetal movements
- Leaking per vaginum
- Fever

## Complications & Prognosis
- Irregular ANC is associated with higher maternal and perinatal mortality
- Early registration and regular visits reduce risk of undetected pre-eclampsia, IUGR, and anemia
- Countries with universal ANC coverage have significantly lower MMR
- WHO evidence shows 8-contact model reduces perinatal mortality by up to 8 per 1000 births compared to 4-visit model
