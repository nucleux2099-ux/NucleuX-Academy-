# Abnormal Uterine Bleeding (AUB)

## Overview

Abnormal uterine bleeding is any deviation from the normal menstrual cycle in frequency, regularity, duration, or volume. It is one of the most common gynecological complaints, affecting up to 30% of women during reproductive years. The FIGO PALM-COEIN classification system (2011, revised 2018) provides a standardized approach to etiology and management.

## Key Points

- Normal menstrual cycle: frequency 24-38 days, duration 4-8 days, volume 5-80 mL
- AUB is classified by the **PALM-COEIN** system
- PALM = structural causes (visible on imaging/histology)
- COEIN = non-structural causes
- Heavy menstrual bleeding (HMB) = >80 mL per cycle or patient perception of excessive loss
- Evaluation must exclude pregnancy first in reproductive age

## Definition & Classification

### Normal Menstrual Parameters (FIGO 2018)
| Parameter | Normal Range |
|-----------|-------------|
| Frequency | 24-38 days |
| Regularity | Variation ≤7-9 days |
| Duration | ≤8 days |
| Volume | 5-80 mL (normal); >80 mL (heavy) |

### PALM-COEIN Classification

#### PALM (Structural)
| Category | Cause |
|----------|-------|
| **P** — Polyp | Endometrial or cervical polyps |
| **A** — Adenomyosis | Ectopic endometrial tissue within myometrium |
| **L** — Leiomyoma | Submucosal (Lsm) > intramural (Lo) — FIGO subclassification 0-8 |
| **M** — Malignancy & hyperplasia | Endometrial hyperplasia, endometrial cancer, cervical cancer |

#### COEIN (Non-structural)
| Category | Cause |
|----------|-------|
| **C** — Coagulopathy | von Willebrand disease, platelet disorders, anticoagulant use |
| **O** — Ovulatory dysfunction | PCOS, hypothyroidism, hyperprolactinemia, hypothalamic |
| **E** — Endometrial | Primary disorder of endometrial hemostasis (diagnosis of exclusion) |
| **I** — Iatrogenic | IUDs, hormonal contraceptives, anticoagulants, tamoxifen |
| **N** — Not yet classified | AV malformations, myometrial hypertrophy, other |

### Leiomyoma FIGO Subclassification (0-8)
- 0: Pedunculated intracavitary
- 1: Submucosal, <50% intramural
- 2: Submucosal, ≥50% intramural
- 3: 100% intramural, contacts endometrium
- 4: Intramural
- 5: Subserosal, ≥50% intramural
- 6: Subserosal, <50% intramural
- 7: Pedunculated subserosal
- 8: Other (cervical, parasitic)

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Age-Based Differential
| Age Group | Common Causes |
|-----------|---------------|
| Adolescent | Anovulation (immature HPO axis), coagulopathy (vWD), pregnancy |
| Reproductive | Pregnancy complications, PCOS, fibroids, polyps, endometriosis |
| Perimenopausal | Anovulation, fibroids, adenomyosis, hyperplasia |
| Postmenopausal | Endometrial atrophy (most common), endometrial cancer, polyps, HRT |

### Pathophysiology of Ovulatory Dysfunction AUB
- Anovulation → unopposed estrogen → proliferative endometrium → irregular shedding
- No progesterone → no secretory transformation → no organized withdrawal bleed

### Pathophysiology of Heavy Menstrual Bleeding
- Increased prostaglandins (PGE2/PGI2) and decreased PGF2α
- Increased fibrinolysis (elevated tPA, reduced PAI-1)
- Abnormal endometrial angiogenesis (VEGF)

## Clinical Features

### History
- Pattern: Heavy, prolonged, irregular, intermenstrual, postcoital
- Associated symptoms: dysmenorrhea, pelvic pain, pressure symptoms
- Impact: fatigue, quality of life, iron deficiency anemia
- Reproductive: pregnancy status, desire for fertility
- Medications: anticoagulants, hormonal therapy

### Examination
- General: pallor, thyroid enlargement, signs of PCOS, bleeding tendency
- Abdominal: uterine size (fibroids)
- Speculum: cervical pathology, source of bleeding
- Bimanual: uterine size, tenderness (adenomyosis), adnexal masses

## Diagnosis

### First-Line Investigations
- **Pregnancy test** (all reproductive age women)
- **CBC** (hemoglobin, platelet count)
- **Coagulation screen** (if HMB since menarche → vWD screening)
- **Thyroid function tests**
- **Transvaginal ultrasound** (structural assessment)

### Second-Line
- **Endometrial biopsy:** Age >45, or <45 with risk factors (obesity, PCOS, tamoxifen, failed treatment)
- **Saline infusion sonohysterography (SIS):** Superior to TVS for intracavitary lesions
- **Hysteroscopy:** Gold standard for intracavitary pathology
- **MRI:** Adenomyosis, fibroid mapping pre-surgery

### When to Biopsy
- All postmenopausal bleeding
- Age >45 with AUB
- Age <45 with risk factors for endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
- Failed medical management
- Endometrial thickness >4 mm in postmenopausal women

## Management

### Medical Management

#### First-Line for HMB (No Structural Cause)
1. **LNG-IUS (Mirena):** Most effective medical treatment; 90% reduction in blood loss
2. **Tranexamic acid:** Antifibrinolytic; 1g TDS during menses; 50% reduction
3. **NSAIDs (mefenamic acid):** Reduce prostaglandins; 20-50% reduction
4. **Combined OCP:** Cycle regulation + reduced flow
5. **Cyclical progestogens:** Days 5-26 or 15-26; norethisterone, medroxyprogesterone

#### For Ovulatory Dysfunction
- Cyclical progestogens
- Combined OCP
- LNG-IUS
- If fertility desired: ovulation induction

#### For Acute Heavy Bleeding
- IV conjugated estrogen
- High-dose oral progestogens (norethisterone 5mg TDS)
- Tranexamic acid
- If hemodynamically unstable: resuscitation, uterine tamponade (Foley balloon), surgical intervention

### Surgical Management
| Procedure | Indication |
|-----------|------------|
| Polypectomy | Endometrial/cervical polyps |
| Myomectomy | Fibroids (fertility desired) |
| Endometrial ablation | HMB, completed family, normal uterus |
| Uterine artery embolization | Fibroids (alternative to surgery) |
| Hysterectomy | Definitive treatment; failed medical/conservative management |

## Complications

- Iron deficiency anemia (most common)
- Impaired quality of life
- Endometrial hyperplasia/cancer (prolonged unopposed estrogen)
- Complications of treatment (surgical, hormonal)
- Psychological impact
