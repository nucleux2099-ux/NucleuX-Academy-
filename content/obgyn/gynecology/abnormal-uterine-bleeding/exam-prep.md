# Abnormal Uterine Bleeding — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

AUB classified by PALM-COEIN (FIGO 2011). PALM = structural (Polyp, Adenomyosis, Leiomyoma, Malignancy). COEIN = non-structural (Coagulopathy, Ovulatory dysfunction, Endometrial, Iatrogenic, Not classified). Normal cycle: 24-38 days, ≤8 days duration, ≤80 mL. LNG-IUS is most effective medical treatment. Always exclude pregnancy first.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ PALM-COEIN: P=Polyp, A=Adenomyosis, L=Leiomyoma, M=Malignancy; C=Coagulopathy, O=Ovulatory, E=Endometrial, I=Iatrogenic, N=Not classified
- ★ Normal cycle: frequency 24-38 days, duration ≤8 days, volume ≤80 mL
- ★ LNG-IUS (Mirena) = most effective medical treatment for HMB (90% reduction)
- ★ Tranexamic acid = antifibrinolytic, 50% reduction (during menses only)
- ★ von Willebrand disease: screen if HMB since menarche
- ★ Endometrial biopsy: all postmenopausal bleeding; >45 with AUB; <45 with risk factors
- ★ Postmenopausal bleeding: most common cause = atrophy; most important to exclude = cancer
- ★ Endometrial thickness >4 mm in postmenopausal = needs biopsy
- ★ Anovulatory AUB: unopposed estrogen → irregular shedding
- ★ FIGO fibroid 0-2 = submucosal (cause HMB); 3-8 = other locations

## Mnemonics

**PALM-COEIN:** "Palm has 4 fingers (structural), COEIN has 5 letters (non-structural)"

**Medical treatment order — "LT-NOC":** LNG-IUS, Tranexamic acid, NSAIDs, OCP, Cyclical progestogens

**Postmenopausal bleeding causes — "CAPE":** Cancer, Atrophy (most common), Polyps, Estrogen (HRT)
