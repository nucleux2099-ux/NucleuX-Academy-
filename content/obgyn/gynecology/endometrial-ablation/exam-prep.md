# Endometrial Ablation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Destroys endometrial lining for HMB when medical therapy fails and family is complete. First-gen (TCRE — hysteroscopic, gold standard) and second-gen (NovaSure, thermal balloon — global, simpler). NOT a contraceptive — concurrent sterilization recommended. Endometrial biopsy mandatory pre-procedure. 80-90% satisfaction; 20-40% amenorrhea rate.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Absolute prerequisite: completed family (pregnancy post-ablation is DANGEROUS)
- ★ NOT a contraceptive — reliable contraception must be arranged
- ★ Endometrial biopsy mandatory before ablation (exclude malignancy)
- ★ TCRE = gold standard first-gen; uses resectoscope + glycine/saline distension
- ★ Glycine toxicity: hyponatremia → cerebral edema; stop if deficit >1000 mL
- ★ NovaSure: bipolar RF, 90 seconds, cavity integrity test required
- ★ Cavity integrity test: CO2 at 50 mmHg — if pressure drops, perforation suspected
- ★ Post-ablation tubal sterilization syndrome (PATSS): cornual hematometra
- ★ Pregnancy after ablation: risk of accreta, ectopic, IUGR
- ★ 15-25% need hysterectomy within 5 years

## Mnemonics

**Pre-ablation checklist — "BENCH":** Biopsy (endometrial), Exclude pregnancy, No future fertility desire, Contraception plan, Hysteroscopy/imaging

**Second-gen techniques — "NTMHC":** NovaSure, Thermal balloon, Microwave, HydroTherm, Cryo

## Common MCQ Topics

1. Absolute contraindication: desire for future pregnancy
2. Mandatory pre-procedure investigation (endometrial biopsy)

4. First-gen vs second-gen differences
