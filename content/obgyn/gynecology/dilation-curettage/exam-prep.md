# Dilation and Curettage — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

D&C = cervical dilation + endometrial curettage. Diagnostic (endometrial sampling) or therapeutic (evacuation). Fractional curettage: endocervical FIRST, then endometrial (for cancer). Suction preferred for pregnancy-related. Key complications: uterine perforation (immediate), Asherman syndrome (delayed). Cervical priming with misoprostol reduces complications.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Fractional curettage: endocervical sample taken FIRST (before dilation), then endometrial
- ★ Most common serious complication = uterine perforation (usually at fundus)
- ★ Most important delayed complication = Asherman syndrome (intrauterine adhesions)
- ★ Asherman syndrome: hypomenorrhea/amenorrhea + infertility; diagnose by hysteroscopy
- ★ Suction evacuation preferred over sharp curettage for molar pregnancy and abortion
- ★ Gritty sensation = complete curettage of endometrium
- ★ Cervical priming: misoprostol 400 μg PV, 3-4 hours before procedure
- ★ Hegar's dilators: graduated, blunt-tipped; dilate sequentially
- ★ Uterine sound used to determine cavity depth and direction BEFORE dilation
- ★ Post-molar pregnancy: suction evacuation + serial β-hCG monitoring

## Mnemonics

**D&C Complications — "PACHI":** Perforation, Asherman syndrome, Cervical trauma, Hemorrhage, Infection

**Fractional curettage order — "EC before EN":** EndoCervical first, then ENdometrial

**Asherman triad — "AHI":** Amenorrhea/Hypomenorrhea, Hysteroscopy diagnosis, Infertility
