# Pelvic Inflammatory Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Ascending infection: cervix → endometrium → tubes → peritoneum. Most common cause: Chlamydia (often subclinical). Polymicrobial. Chandelier sign (cervical motion tenderness) is most characteristic. Low threshold for empirical treatment. Standard: ceftriaxone IM + doxycycline 14 days ± metronidazole. Major sequelae: tubal infertility (10-15% after 1 episode), ectopic pregnancy, chronic pelvic pain.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Most common causative organism: Chlamydia trachomatis
- ★ Chandelier sign = cervical motion tenderness (most characteristic sign)
- ★ Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome: perihepatitis with "violin string" adhesions
- ★ Tubal infertility: 10-15% after 1 episode, 50-75% after 3+
- ★ CDC minimum criteria: cervical motion OR uterine OR adnexal tenderness (low threshold)
- ★ MUST exclude ectopic pregnancy (crucial differential)
- ★ Outpatient Rx: Ceftriaxone IM + Doxycycline 14 days ± Metronidazole
- ★ IUD-associated PID: Actinomyces israelii
- ★ OCP is protective (thickens cervical mucus)
- ★ Ruptured TOA: surgical emergency → laparotomy
- ★ Treat partners from past 60 days

## Mnemonics

**PID long-term complications — "ICE-R":** Infertility (tubal), Chronic pelvic pain, Ectopic pregnancy, Recurrence

**Fitz-Hugh-Curtis — "FHC = Fiddle strings on Hepatic Capsule"**

**PID treatment — "CDM":** Ceftriaxone, Doxycycline (14 days), Metronidazole
