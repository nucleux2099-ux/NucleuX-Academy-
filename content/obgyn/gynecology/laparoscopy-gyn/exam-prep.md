# Laparoscopy in Gynecology — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Minimally invasive surgery using CO2 pneumoperitoneum (12-15 mmHg). Entry: Veress needle (closed) or Hasson's (open, safest). Palmer's point for adhesions. Diagnostic (chromopertubation, staging) and operative. Most feared complication: CO2 embolism (Durant's maneuver). Shoulder tip pain from diaphragmatic irritation (C3-C5). Close fascia for ports >10 mm.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ CO2 is the standard insufflation gas (absorbed rapidly, non-combustible)
- ★ Intra-abdominal pressure: 12-15 mmHg
- ★ Palmer's point: left MCL, 3 cm below costal margin — for previous surgery/adhesions
- ★ Hasson's (open) technique: safest entry method
- ★ Veress safety tests: aspiration, saline drop, opening pressure <8 mmHg
- ★ CO2 embolism treatment: left lateral decubitus (Durant's maneuver), aspirate gas
- ★ Shoulder tip pain: CO2 irritates diaphragm → referred pain C3-C5 (phrenic nerve)
- ★ Port site hernia: close fascia for ports ≥10 mm
- ★ Chromopertubation: methylene blue through cervix → dye spill = patent tubes
- ★ Trendelenburg position for pelvic visualization

## Mnemonics

**Veress tests — "ASO":** Aspiration, Saline drop, Opening pressure (<8)

**Entry complications — "VBB":** Vascular, Bowel, Bladder

**CO2 embolism — "DALI":** Durant's maneuver (left lateral), Aspirate gas, Left decubitus, ICU

**Palmer's point — "P for Previous surgery, P for Palmer's"**
