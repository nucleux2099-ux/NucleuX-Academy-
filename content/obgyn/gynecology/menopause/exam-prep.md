# Menopause — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Permanent cessation of menses; retrospective diagnosis after 12 months amenorrhea. Average age 51. FSH >40 IU/L confirms. Estrone is predominant postmenopausal estrogen (from adipose aromatization). HRT is most effective for vasomotor symptoms. Combined HRT for women with uterus; estrogen-only if no uterus. POI = menopause <40 years; needs HRT until age 51.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ 12 months amenorrhea = menopause (retrospective diagnosis)
- ★ FSH >40 IU/L; NOT needed if age >45 with typical symptoms
- ★ Predominant postmenopausal estrogen = ESTRONE (from peripheral aromatization)
- ★ Inhibin B = earliest hormonal change to decline
- ★ Estrogen-only HRT: post-hysterectomy ONLY (unopposed → endometrial cancer)
- ★ Combined HRT: increases breast cancer (after 5+ years); estrogen-only does NOT
- ★ Transdermal HRT: lower VTE risk than oral
- ★ "Window of opportunity": start HRT within 10 years of menopause or age <60
- ★ WHI: combined HRT increased breast cancer, VTE, stroke; estrogen-only did NOT increase breast cancer
- ★ POI: FSH >40 on two occasions, 4 weeks apart, age <40
- ★ POI causes: idiopathic, Turner, Fragile X premutation, autoimmune, galactosemia

## Mnemonics

**Postmenopausal hormones — "FSH up, Estradiol down, Estrone is the ONE"**

**HRT risks — "BEST-V":** Breast cancer (combined), Endometrial cancer (unopposed), Stroke, Thrombosis (VTE), gallbladder (Vesica)

**POI causes — "FATAL-G":** Fragile X, Autoimmune, Turner, Acquired (chemo/radiation), Idiopathic (Last), Galactosemia
