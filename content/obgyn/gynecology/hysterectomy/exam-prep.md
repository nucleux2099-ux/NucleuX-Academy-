# Hysterectomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Most common major gynecological surgery. Most common indication: fibroids/AUB. Routes: vaginal (preferred for benign), abdominal, laparoscopic, robotic. Total = uterus + cervix. Radical (Wertheim's) = uterus + cervix + parametrium + upper vagina + nodes. Key complication: ureteric injury ("water under the bridge"). Conserve ovaries in premenopausal women with benign disease.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Vaginal route preferred for benign disease (ACOG recommendation)
- ★ "Water under the bridge" = ureter passes UNDER uterine artery (at risk during ligation)
- ★ Most common indication = leiomyoma / AUB
- ★ Wertheim's radical hysterectomy: for cervical cancer Stage IA2-IIA
- ★ Piver Class III = classical radical (Wertheim's)
- ★ Conserve ovaries in premenopausal women with benign disease (<65 years)
- ★ Vault prolapse = important late complication (especially without colpopexy)
- ★ LAVH = upper pedicles laparoscopic, completed vaginally; TLH = entirely laparoscopic
- ★ Bladder reflected DOWN before colpotomy (anterior to cervix)
- ★ Peripartum hysterectomy = last resort for uncontrollable PPH

## Mnemonics

**Structures clamped in order — "RIUCU-V":** Round ligament, Infundibulopelvic/utero-ovarian, Uterine artery, Cardinal ligament, Uterosacral ligament, Vaginal vault

**Ureteric injury sites — "IVCU":** Infundibulopelvic ligament, Vault angle, Cardinal ligament, Uterine artery crossing

**Piver classes — "1-Simple, 2-Modified, 3-Radical, 4-Extended, 5-Exenteration"**
