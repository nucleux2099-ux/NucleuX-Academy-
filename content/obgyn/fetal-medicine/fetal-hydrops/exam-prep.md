# Fetal Hydrops — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Hydrops fetalis = fluid in ≥2 fetal compartments. Immune (Rh disease) vs non-immune (90% cases now; cardiovascular anomalies most common cause). MCA-PSV Doppler detects fetal anemia non-invasively. IUT is treatment for immune hydrops.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Definition: fluid in ≥2 compartments (ascites, pleural, pericardial effusion, skin edema)
- ★ Non-immune hydrops is now 90% of cases
- ★ Most common cause of NIHF: cardiovascular anomalies
- ★ Most common cause of immune hydrops: Anti-D (Rh) alloimmunization
- ★ MCA-PSV >1.5 MoM = moderate-severe fetal anemia
- ★ Anti-D prophylaxis: 300 μg at 28 weeks + within 72 hours postpartum
- ★ IUT uses O Rh-negative, irradiated, CMV-negative packed RBCs
- ★ Parvovirus B19 causes aplastic crisis → hydrops (treat with IUT)
- ★ Turner syndrome (45,X): common chromosomal cause of NIHF — cystic hygroma
- ★ Mirror syndrome: maternal edema mimicking pre-eclampsia alongside fetal hydrops

## Mnemonics
**Causes of NIHF — "CATCH IT":**
- **C**ardiovascular
- **A**neuploidies (chromosomal)
- **T**wins (TTTS)
- **C**hest (thoracic masses)
- **H**ematological (thalassemia)
- **I**nfections (Parvo B19, CMV)
- **T**umors (sacrococcygeal teratoma)
