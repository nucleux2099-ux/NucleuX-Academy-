# Obstetric Ultrasound

## Overview
Obstetric ultrasound is the cornerstone of modern prenatal care, providing non-invasive, real-time imaging of the fetus, placenta, amniotic fluid, and maternal pelvic structures. It uses high-frequency sound waves (2–10 MHz) and is considered safe with no proven adverse effects.

Ultrasound is used for dating, viability assessment, screening for anomalies, growth monitoring, and guiding invasive procedures. Transvaginal (TVS) and transabdominal (TAS) approaches are used depending on gestational age and indication.

The three routine scans recommended are: dating scan (11–13+6 weeks), anomaly scan (18–22 weeks), and growth scan (third trimester). Each scan has specific objectives and standardized measurements.

## Key Points
- No ionizing radiation; considered safe at diagnostic intensities
- CRL is most accurate for dating (6–13 weeks, ±5 days)
- Nuchal translucency measured at 11–13+6 weeks (CRL 45–84 mm)
- Anomaly scan at 18–22 weeks evaluates all major organ systems
- BPD, HC, AC, FL used for fetal biometry and growth assessment
- Doppler studies (umbilical artery, MCA, uterine artery) assess fetal well-being

## Definition & Classification
### Types of Obstetric Ultrasound
| Type | Description |
|------|------------|
| Transvaginal (TVS) | Early pregnancy; higher resolution; 5–10 MHz probe |
| Transabdominal (TAS) | Standard approach; 2–5 MHz probe |
| Doppler | Pulsed-wave, color flow; assess blood flow |
| 3D/4D | Volume rendering; facial and surface anomalies |

### Routine Scans
1. **First trimester / Dating scan (11–13+6 wk)**
2. **Second trimester / Anomaly scan (18–22 wk)**
3. **Third trimester / Growth scan (28–32 wk)**

## Etiology & Pathophysiology
Ultrasound physics:
- **Piezoelectric effect:** Crystal vibration produces and receives sound waves
- **Frequency:** Higher frequency = better resolution but less penetration
- **B-mode:** Standard 2D grayscale imaging
- **M-mode:** Time-motion display; used for fetal heart rate assessment
- **Doppler:** Measures blood flow velocity; uses frequency shift principle

## Clinical Features
### First Trimester Scan
- Confirm intrauterine pregnancy (gestational sac visible at 4–5 weeks TVS)
- Yolk sac: first structure seen inside GS (5 weeks)
- Fetal pole and cardiac activity: 6 weeks TVS
- CRL measurement for dating
- Nuchal translucency at 11–13+6 weeks
- Number of fetuses and chorionicity

### Anomaly Scan (18–22 weeks)
Systematic survey of:
- **Head:** BPD, HC, ventricles, cerebellum, cisterna magna
- **Face:** Lips, nose, orbits
- **Spine:** Integrity in sagittal and transverse
- **Heart:** Four-chamber view, outflow tracts
- **Abdomen:** AC, stomach bubble, kidneys, bladder, cord insertion
- **Limbs:** Long bones, hands, feet
- **Placenta:** Location, cord insertion, number of vessels (3-vessel cord)
- **Amniotic fluid:** AFI or single deepest pocket

### Third Trimester Scan
- Fetal biometry (BPD, HC, AC, FL) → estimated fetal weight
- Growth assessment: plot on growth charts, assess IUGR or macrosomia
- Amniotic fluid volume
- Placental location (rule out previa)
- Fetal presentation
- Doppler studies if indicated

## Diagnosis
### Key Measurements
| Parameter | Measurement | Use |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| CRL | Crown–rump length | Dating (most accurate 7–13 wk) |
| NT | Nuchal translucency | Aneuploidy screening (11–13+6 wk) |
| BPD | Biparietal diameter | Dating, growth |
| HC | Head circumference | Growth assessment |
| AC | Abdominal circumference | Best predictor of fetal weight; first to change in IUGR |
| FL | Femur length | Growth, skeletal dysplasia screening |
| AFI | Amniotic fluid index | Normal 8–24 cm |
| EFW | Estimated fetal weight | Hadlock formula (BPD + HC + AC + FL) |

### Doppler Assessment
| Vessel | Normal | Abnormal |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Umbilical artery | Low resistance, forward diastolic flow | Absent/reversed end-diastolic flow (AREDF) |
| MCA | High resistance | Low PI = brain sparing (redistribution) |
| Uterine artery | Low resistance, no notch | Bilateral notching = pre-eclampsia risk |
| Ductus venosus | Forward a-wave | Reversed a-wave = impending fetal demise |

## Management
- **First trimester:** CRL for dating; NT + nasal bone for screening
- **18–22 weeks:** Complete anomaly survey; identify major malformations
- **Third trimester:** Growth monitoring, amniotic fluid, Doppler if IUGR suspected
- **Serial scans:** For high-risk pregnancies (GDM, hypertension, IUGR, multiple pregnancy)
- **Guided procedures:** Amniocentesis, CVS, cordocentesis, IUT

### ALARA Principle
- As Low As Reasonably Achievable — minimize exposure time and acoustic output
- Avoid unnecessary Doppler in first trimester

## Complications & Prognosis
- No proven biological effects at diagnostic intensities
- Theoretical risk of thermal and cavitation effects (not demonstrated clinically)
- False positives on anomaly scan → parental anxiety
- Operator-dependent: quality varies with skill and equipment
- Detection rate for major anomalies: 60–85% (anomaly scan)
- Early dating reduces need for post-dates induction
