# Obstetric Ultrasound — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Three routine scans: dating (11–13+6 wk), anomaly (18–22 wk), growth (3rd trimester). CRL is most accurate for dating. NT screens for aneuploidy. AC is first parameter altered in IUGR. Umbilical artery Doppler with AREDF = ominous.

## High Yield Points
- ★ CRL is MOST accurate dating parameter (7–13 wk, ±5 days)
- ★ NT measured at 11–13+6 weeks; CRL 45–84 mm
- ★ Anomaly scan at 18–22 weeks — systematic survey of all organs
- ★ AC is the BEST single predictor of fetal weight and FIRST to change in IUGR
- ★ Hadlock formula uses BPD + HC + AC + FL for EFW
- ★ Normal AFI: 8–24 cm; polyhydramnios >24; oligohydramnios <5
- ★ Absent/reversed end-diastolic flow (AREDF) in umbilical artery = ominous sign
- ★ MCA low PI = brain-sparing effect (fetal redistribution in IUGR)
- ★ Reversed a-wave in ductus venosus = impending fetal demise
- ★ GS seen at 4–5 wk (TVS); cardiac activity at 6 wk

## Mnemonics
