# Pelvic Organ Prolapse — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

POP = descent of pelvic organs due to pelvic floor weakness. Vaginal delivery is top risk factor. Cystocele is most common type. POP-Q is standard staging. Conservative: Kegels + pessary. Surgical: Vaginal hysterectomy + pelvic floor repair (definitive). Manchester repair preserves uterus. Sacrocolpopexy is gold standard for vault prolapse. Le Fort's for elderly not sexually active.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common type of prolapse** → cystocele (anterior compartment)
- ★ **Most important risk factor** → vaginal delivery
- ★ **Primary uterine support** → cardinal + uterosacral ligaments (Level I)
- ★ **DeLancey Level II** → paravaginal support (mid-vagina)
- ★ **Gold standard staging** → POP-Q system
- ★ **Definitive surgery** → vaginal hysterectomy + pelvic floor repair
- ★ **Manchester repair** → cervical amputation + cardinal ligament shortening (preserves uterus)
- ★ **Le Fort's colpocleisis** → obliterates vagina; elderly, not sexually active
- ★ **Gold standard for vault prolapse** → sacrocolpopexy
- ★ **Decubitus ulcer** → treat ulcer FIRST, then surgery
- ★ **Ring pessary** → most commonly used pessary
- ★ **Shelf pessary** → for severe prolapse when ring fails

## Mnemonics

**POP Compartments — "ABC"**:
- **A**nterior = cystocele/urethrocele
- **B** (middle) = uterine prolapse
- **C** (posterior) = rectocele/enterocele

**DeLancey Levels — "1-2-3 = Suspend-Attach-Fuse"**:
- Level I: Suspension (cardinal/uterosacral)
- Level II: Attachment (paravaginal)
- Level III: Fusion (perineal body)

**"MR Le Fort"** — Manchester Repairs, Le Fort's colpocleisis (both vaginal uterus operations for prolapse)
