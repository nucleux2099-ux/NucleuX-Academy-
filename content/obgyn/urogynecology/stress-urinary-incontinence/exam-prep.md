# Stress Urinary Incontinence — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

SUI = involuntary leak with effort/cough/sneeze. Two mechanisms: urethral hypermobility and ISD. PFMT is first-line. Mid-urethral sling (TVT/TOT) is gold standard surgery (80-90% success). Duloxetine is only drug with evidence. ALPP <60 = ISD. Burch colposuspension is historical gold standard. Urodynamics confirms diagnosis.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common incontinence in young women** → SUI
- ★ **First-line treatment** → pelvic floor muscle training (Kegels) for 3 months
- ★ **Gold standard surgery** → mid-urethral sling (TVT/TOT)
- ★ **TVT** → retropubic; risk = bladder perforation; better for ISD
- ★ **TOT** → transobturator; risk = groin pain; better for hypermobility
- ★ **ALPP <60 cmH₂O** → intrinsic sphincter deficiency
- ★ **Duloxetine** → only drug with evidence for SUI (SNRI)
- ★ **Burch colposuspension** → historical gold standard, sutures to Cooper's ligament
- ★ **Bonney's test** → elevate bladder neck, leak stops = urethral hypermobility
- ★ **Q-tip test >30°** → urethral hypermobility confirmed
- ★ **Urodynamics** → gold standard diagnostic test
- ★ **Weight loss of 5-10%** → reduces SUI episodes by 50%

## Mnemonics

**"SUI = Sneeze Urine Involuntarily"**

**TVT vs TOT — "T goes UP, O goes OUT"**:
- TVT = retropubic (up) → bladder perforation risk
- TOT = transobturator (out) → groin pain risk

**ISD diagnosis — "60 is the number"**: ALPP <60 = ISD

**Conservative — "KEEP FIT"**:
- **K**egels, **E**lectrical stimulation, **E**strogen (vaginal), **P**essary
- **F**luid management, **I**nformation/education, **T**rim weight
