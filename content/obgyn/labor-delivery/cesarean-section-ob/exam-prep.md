# Cesarean Section — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

CS = delivery through abdominal + uterine incisions. Most common indication: previous CS. Most common emergency indication: fetal distress. Standard: Pfannenstiel skin + lower segment transverse uterine incision. Regional anesthesia preferred. Category 1 emergency: decision-to-delivery <30 min. VBAC success 60-80%, rupture risk 0.5-1% with prior LSCS. Rising CS rates increase placenta accreta spectrum risk.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common indication overall**: repeat/previous CS
- ★ **Most common emergency indication**: non-reassuring fetal status
- ★ **Category 1 CS**: decision-to-delivery interval <30 minutes
- ★ **Pfannenstiel incision**: most common skin incision (transverse suprapubic)
- ★ **Most common organ injured**: urinary bladder
- ★ **Prophylactic antibiotic**: Cefazolin 2g IV BEFORE skin incision
- ★ **Classical CS rupture risk**: 4-9% vs LSCS 0.5-1%
- ★ **Neonatal complication of elective CS**: TTN (transient tachypnea of newborn)
- ★ **Placenta accreta risk** increases with each prior CS
- ★ **VBAC contraindicated**: prior classical incision, prior uterine rupture
- ★ **Elective CS timing**: 39 completed weeks (avoid iatrogenic prematurity)

## Mnemonics

### **"CESAREAN"** — Indications
- **C**PD / Contracted pelvis
- **E**clampsia / severe preeclampsia
- **S**car (previous CS)
- **A**bnormal presentation (breech, transverse)
- **R**epeated CS
- **E**lderly primi with complications
- **A**bruption / APH
- **N**on-reassuring fetal status

### **"BLADE"** — Complications
- **B**ladder injury
- **L**aceration (uterine extension, fetal)
- **A**dhesions / Accreta
- **D**VT / Death (rare)
- **E**ndometritis / wound infection

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Most common indication for CS** → Previous cesarean section
2. **Absolute indication** → Complete placenta previa, transverse lie, cord prolapse
3. **Timing of prophylactic antibiotic** → Before skin incision (not after cord clamp — outdated)
4. **Classical CS indication** → Transverse lie with back down, extreme prematurity, anterior placenta previa
5. **VBAC rupture risk** → 0.5-1% with low transverse scar
6. **Category 1 time limit** → <30 minutes decision-to-delivery
7. **Risk of accreta with 3 prior CS** → ~2.4%
8. **Most common neonatal complication** → TTN
9. **Preferred anesthesia** → Spinal (subarachnoid block)
10. **Joel-Cohen technique advantage** → Faster, less blood loss, blunt dissection
