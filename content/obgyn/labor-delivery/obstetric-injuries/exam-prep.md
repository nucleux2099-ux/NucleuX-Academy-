# Obstetric Injuries — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Birth canal injuries = 2nd most common cause of PPH (after atony). Systematic exam after every delivery: vulva → vagina → cervix → rectum. Cervical tears at 3 & 9 o'clock. Perineal tears: 1st (skin), 2nd (muscle), 3rd (sphincter), 4th (rectal mucosa). 3rd/4th degree repair in OT. VVF develops 7-14 days after obstructed labor from pressure necrosis. Broad ligament hematoma = concealed hemorrhage + shock.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **PPH with contracted uterus** = think birth canal injury (cervical/vaginal tear)
- ★ **Cervical tears**: most common at 3 and 9 o'clock (lateral angles)
- ★ **3rd degree tear subdivisions**: 3a (<50% EAS), 3b (>50% EAS), 3c (IAS involved)
- ★ **Rectal exam mandatory** after every vaginal delivery
- ★ **VVF**: continuous dribbling of urine per vaginum, 7-14 days post obstructed labor
- ★ **Broad ligament hematoma**: shock out of proportion to visible blood loss → laparotomy
- ★ **Paravaginal hematoma**: perineal pain, pressure sensation, urinary retention
- ★ **3rd/4th degree repair**: OT + regional/GA + antibiotics + laxatives
- ★ **Forceps > vacuum** for maternal soft tissue injury
- ★ **Precipitate labor**: rapid uncontrolled delivery → cervical tears, perineal tears

## Mnemonics

### **"TEARS"** — Risk Factors for Perineal Injury
- **T**oo big baby (macrosomia)
- **E**pisiotomy inadequate
- **A**ssisted delivery (forceps)
- **R**igid perineum
- **S**houlder dystocia

### **"3-9 CLOCK"** — Cervical Tear Sites
- Cervical tears at **3** and **9** o'clock = lateral angles where cervix retracts over presenting part

### **"1234"** — Perineal Tears (What's Involved)
- **1** = **S**kin
- **2** = **M**uscle
- **3** = **S**phincter
- **4** = **R**ectum (mucosa)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **PPH with well-contracted uterus** → Genital tract injury (examine cervix!)
2. **Most common site of cervical tear** → 3 and 9 o'clock positions
3. **3a tear** → <50% external anal sphincter
4. **Continuous urinary leakage post-delivery** → Vesicovaginal fistula
5. **VVF surgery timing** → 3-6 months after delivery (let inflammation subside)
6. **Concealed hemorrhage post-delivery with shock** → Broad ligament hematoma
7. **Post 3rd degree tear management** → Antibiotics + laxatives × 2 weeks
8. **Pudendal nerve injury** → Perineal numbness, stress incontinence
9. **Most common cause of obstetric fistula** → Obstructed labor (pressure necrosis)
10. **Examination technique for cervical tears** → Ring/sponge forceps, systematic clockwise inspection
