# Forceps Delivery — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Forceps = metal instruments applied to fetal head for assisted vaginal delivery. Prerequisites: full dilatation, ruptured membranes, engaged head, known position, empty bladder, adequate analgesia, no CPD. Classification: outlet/low/mid (high abandoned). Left blade first. Pajot's maneuver for traction. Kielland's for rotation. More maternal trauma than vacuum but less scalp injury.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Prerequisites** (ALL must be met): full dilatation, engaged head, ruptured membranes, known position, empty bladder, adequate analgesia
- ★ **Left blade applied first** (held in left hand, left side of pelvis)
- ★ **Kielland's forceps**: minimal pelvic curve, sliding lock — used for ROTATION (OP→OA)
- ★ **Piper's forceps**: for after-coming head in BREECH
- ★ **Wrigley's forceps**: short, light — outlet/lift-out delivery, also used at CS
- ★ **High forceps**: ABANDONED — never done
- ★ **Pajot's maneuver**: traction along pelvic axis (downward + outward)
- ★ **Correct application check**: posterior fontanelle 1 cm above shanks, centered; sagittal suture ⊥ to shanks
- ★ **Forceps > vacuum**: for maternal trauma; vacuum > forceps for scalp injury
- ★ **Trial of forceps**: done in OT with readiness for immediate CS
- ★ **Facial nerve palsy**: characteristic neonatal complication (usually transient)

### **"Wrigley-Kielland-Piper"** — Remember by Use
- **W**rigley = **W**ee (small, outlet)
- **K**ielland = **K**ey rotation (OP→OA)
- **P**iper = **P**odalic (breech, after-coming head)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Forceps for rotation** → Kielland's (minimal pelvic curve, sliding lock)
2. **Forceps for breech after-coming head** → Piper's
3. **Which blade first?** → Left blade first
4. **Forceps with sliding lock** → Kielland's
5. **Prerequisite NOT needed** → Unruptured membranes (membranes MUST be ruptured)
6. **Most common neonatal complication** → Facial bruising/marks (transient)
7. **Facial nerve palsy branch affected** → Peripheral (lower motor neuron), temporal/buccal
8. **Prolonged 2nd stage in nullipara** → >3h with epidural, >2h without
9. **Contraindication** → Unengaged head, face presentation (mento-posterior), preterm <34 wk (relative)
10. **Sequential instruments (vacuum then forceps)** → Increases neonatal injury — AVOID
