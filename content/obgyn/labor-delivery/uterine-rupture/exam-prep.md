# Uterine Rupture — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Full-thickness uterine wall disruption. Most common cause (developed): previous CS scar rupture; (developing): obstructed labor. Classical scar rupture 4-9% vs LSCS 0.5-1%. Classic presentation: sudden pain → cessation of contractions → fetal distress → shock. Bandl's ring = impending rupture sign. Emergency laparotomy mandatory. Repair if feasible; hysterectomy if extensive. Dehiscence ≠ rupture (dehiscence = serosa intact, usually asymptomatic).

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Rupture vs dehiscence**: rupture = all layers, symptomatic; dehiscence = serosa intact, asymptomatic
- ★ **Classical CS scar rupture risk**: 4-9%; LSCS: 0.5-1%
- ★ **Bandl's ring**: pathological retraction ring → sign of IMPENDING rupture
- ★ **Cessation of contractions** after sudden pain = hallmark of completed rupture
- ★ **Fetal bradycardia**: most reliable CTG sign of scar rupture
- ★ **Fetal parts palpable superficially**: fetus extruded into peritoneal cavity
- ★ **Recession of presenting part**: head goes up on vaginal exam
- ★ **Misoprostol in scarred uterus**: CONTRAINDICATED for induction (high rupture risk)
- ★ **Grand multiparity**: risk factor for spontaneous rupture of unscarred uterus
- ★ **Fetal mortality 50-75%** if extruded into peritoneal cavity
- ★ **Inter-delivery interval <18 months**: increases scar rupture risk

## Mnemonics

### **"RUPTURE"** — Signs of Uterine Rupture
- **R**ecession of presenting part
- **U**terus changes shape / tender
- **P**ain sudden then contractions STOP
- **T**achycardia maternal / fetal bradycardia
- **U**nstable vitals (shock)
- **R**ed bleeding (vaginal or concealed)
- **E**asy palpation of fetal parts

### **"BANDL"** — Impending Rupture Signs
- **B**andl's ring visible/rising
- **A**nxiety and distress (maternal)
- **N**on-stop pain (continuous)
- **D**istress fetal
- **L**ower segment tender

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Most reliable sign of scar rupture on CTG** → Prolonged fetal bradycardia
2. **Bandl's ring** → Pathological retraction ring, impending rupture in obstructed labor
3. **Classical CS rupture risk** → 4-9% (vs 0.5-1% for LSCS)
4. **Rupture vs dehiscence** → Rupture = full-thickness + symptoms; dehiscence = serosa intact
5. **Cessation of contractions** → Pathognomonic after sudden pain
6. **Contraindicated drug in scarred uterus** → Misoprostol for induction
7. **Surgery of choice** → Repair if feasible; hysterectomy if uncontrollable/extensive
8. **Future pregnancy after repair** → Mandatory elective CS at 36-37 weeks
9. **Cause in developing countries** → Obstructed labor (neglected transverse lie)
10. **Fetus outside uterus** → Superficially palpable fetal parts abdominally
