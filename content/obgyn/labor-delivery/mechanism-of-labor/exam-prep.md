# Mechanism of Labor — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Seven cardinal movements: Engagement → Descent → Flexion → Internal Rotation → Extension → Restitution → Expulsion. Head enters pelvis in transverse/oblique (inlet widest transverse), rotates to AP at mid-pelvis (outlet widest AP). Ideal presenting diameter: suboccipitobregmatic (9.5 cm) in fully flexed vertex. Internal rotation is the most critical movement. Levator ani is the key muscle for internal rotation.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Seven movements**: Engagement, Descent, Flexion, Internal Rotation, Extension, Restitution, Expulsion
- ★ **Suboccipitobregmatic (9.5 cm)**: presenting diameter in well-flexed vertex — IDEAL
- ★ **Mentovertical (13.5 cm)**: brow presentation — LARGEST, cannot deliver vaginally
- ★ **Internal rotation**: most critical movement; brought about by levator ani (gutter shape)
- ★ **Engagement**: biparietal diameter through inlet; ≤2/5 palpable abdominally
- ★ **Inlet widest diameter**: transverse (13 cm); Outlet widest: AP
- ★ **Extension**: symphysis pubis acts as fulcrum, head sweeps over perineum
- ★ **Restitution**: head un-twists to realign with shoulders (45° external rotation)
- ★ **Molding +++**: irreducible overlapping of skull bones → significant CPD
- ★ **Caput crosses sutures; cephalohematoma does NOT**

## Mnemonics

### **"Every Dead Fetus In ED Really Explodes"** — Cardinal Movements
- **E**ngagement
- **D**escent
- **F**lexion
- **I**nternal rotation
- **E**xtension
- **R**estitution
- **E**xpulsion

### **"SOB-SOF-OF-MV-SMB"** — Fetal Head Diameters (flexed → extended)
- **SOB** (suboccipitobregmatic) = 9.5 cm (vertex flexed)
- **SOF** (suboccipitofrontal) = 10 cm (partially flexed)
- **OF** (occipitofrontal) = 11.5 cm (military)
- **MV** (mentovertical) = 13.5 cm (brow — LARGEST)
- **SMB** (submentobregmatic) = 9.5 cm (face)

### Inlet vs Outlet
- **IN**let = **T**ransverse widest (both start with consonant)
- **OUT**let = **A**P widest (both have vowel emphasis)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Presenting diameter in vertex** → Suboccipitobregmatic (9.5 cm)
2. **Largest fetal head diameter** → Mentovertical (13.5 cm) — brow
3. **Movement caused by levator ani** → Internal rotation
4. **Engagement criterion (abdominal)** → ≤2/5 head palpable above brim
5. **Narrowest pelvic diameter** → Interspinous (10 cm) at mid-pelvis
6. **Fulcrum during extension** → Subocciput under symphysis pubis
7. **Caput vs cephalohematoma** → Caput crosses sutures, cephalo does NOT
8. **Deep transverse arrest** → Failure of internal rotation at mid-pelvis
9. **Persistent OP delivery** → Face-to-pubes, larger diameter (OF 11.5 cm)
10. **When does engagement occur in nullipara?** → 2-3 weeks before labor
