# Uterine Inversion — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Rare life-threatening emergency. Fundus inverts through cervix. Cause: mismanaged 3rd stage (excessive cord traction). Classic triad: hemorrhage + shock (disproportionate to blood loss) + pain. DO NOT remove placenta before replacement. Johnson's maneuver = manual replacement. O'Sullivan's = hydrostatic method. Avoid oxytocin/ergometrine until AFTER replacement. Neurogenic shock (parasympathetic) = bradycardia.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common cause**: excessive cord traction during 3rd stage
- ★ **Shock out of proportion to blood loss** = neurogenic (parasympathetic) + hypovolemic
- ★ **DO NOT remove placenta before replacement** — worsens bleeding
- ★ **DO NOT give oxytocin/ergometrine before replacement** — cervix constricts, traps fundus
- ★ **Johnson's maneuver**: push fundus through cervix toward umbilicus with palm
- ★ **O'Sullivan's method**: hydrostatic pressure (warm saline 2-5L into vagina, seal vulva)
- ★ **Uterine relaxants if manual fails**: halothane GA, nitroglycerin IV, terbutaline, MgSO4
- ★ **Huntington's operation**: abdominal — pull up round ligaments with Allis forceps
- ★ **Haultain's operation**: abdominal — incise posterior cervical constriction ring
- ★ **Fundus NOT palpable abdominally**: pathognomonic finding
- ★ **Atropine for bradycardia** (neurogenic shock)

## Mnemonics

### **"INVERT"** — Management Sequence
- **I**V access + resuscitate
- **N**o oxytocin yet (avoid before replacement)
- **V**aginal — manual replacement (Johnson's)
- **E**ase uterus — relaxants if needed (halothane, NTG)
- **R**efill with saline (O'Sullivan's hydrostatic)
- **T**heater — surgical (Huntington's / Haultain's)

### **"HHH"** — Surgical Options
- **H**untington's — pull up round ligaments (abdominal)
- **H**aultain's — incise constriction ring posteriorly (abdominal)
- **H**ydrostatic (O'Sullivan's) — warm saline (non-surgical alternative)

### Degrees: **"IN-COM-PRO-TOTAL"**
- 1st: **IN**complete (fundus at internal os)
- 2nd: **COM**plete (through cervix into vagina)
- 3rd: **PRO**lapsed (outside vulva)
- 4th: **TOTAL** (vagina inverted too)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Shock disproportionate to blood loss in PPH** → Uterine inversion (neurogenic shock)
2. **First step in management** → Manual replacement (Johnson's maneuver), NOT removing placenta
3. **Drug to AVOID before replacement** → Oxytocin/Ergometrine (constricts cervix)
4. **Drug for neurogenic bradycardia** → Atropine 0.6 mg IV
5. **Hydrostatic method** → O'Sullivan's (warm saline, seal vulva)
6. **Surgical method via abdomen** → Huntington's (traction on round ligaments)
7. **Uterine relaxant for replacement** → Nitroglycerin IV (fastest onset)
8. **Most common cause** → Mismanaged 3rd stage / excessive cord traction
9. **Fundal placenta** → Risk factor for inversion
10. **After replacement** → Oxytocin infusion to maintain tone + antibiotics
