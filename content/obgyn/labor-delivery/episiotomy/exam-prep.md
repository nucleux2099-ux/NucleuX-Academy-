# Episiotomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Episiotomy = surgical perineal incision during 2nd stage of labor. Selective/restrictive use recommended (NOT routine). Mediolateral (45-60°) preferred — protects anal sphincter. Midline easier to repair but higher extension risk. Repair: continuous Vicryl Rapide, 3 layers. Always do rectal exam post-repair. Indications: fetal distress, operative delivery, shoulder dystocia, breech.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Restrictive > routine episiotomy** (Cochrane evidence)
- ★ **Mediolateral preferred**: 45-60° from midline, less 3rd/4th degree tear risk
- ★ **Midline/median**: easier repair, better healing, BUT higher anal sphincter injury
- ★ **Timing**: at crowning, during contraction, perineum thinned
- ★ **3rd degree tear**: 3a (<50% EAS), 3b (>50% EAS), 3c (IAS involved)
- ★ **4th degree**: involves anal/rectal mucosa
- ★ **Suture**: continuous absorbable (Vicryl Rapide 2-0) — less pain than interrupted
- ★ **Rectal exam mandatory** after repair to check for missed injury/sutures
- ★ **Dyspareunia**: most common late complication
- ★ **Layers cut in mediolateral**: skin, subcutaneous tissue, bulbospongiosus, transverse perineal muscle

## Mnemonics

### **"EPISIO"** — Indications
- **E**xpedited delivery (fetal distress)
- **P**rocedural delivery (forceps/vacuum)
- **I**nflexible perineum (rigid)
- **S**houlder dystocia
- **I**mmature baby (preterm — debated)
- **O**bstructed after-coming head (breech)

### **"1-2-3-4"** — Perineal Tear Classification
- **1**st: skin/mucosa only
- **2**nd: perineal muscles (= episiotomy depth)
- **3**rd: anal sphincter
- **4**th: rectal mucosa

### **"ABC"** — Repair Layers (inside→out)
- **A**pex of vaginal mucosa (start here, continuous)
- **B**ulk of perineal muscles (continuous/interrupted)
- **C**utaneous skin (subcuticular continuous)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Episiotomy that protects anal sphincter best** → Mediolateral
2. **Most common complication of midline episiotomy** → Extension to 3rd/4th degree tear
3. **Preferred suture material** → Vicryl Rapide (polyglactin 910) — continuous
4. **Most common late complication** → Dyspareunia
5. **3b tear involves** → >50% of external anal sphincter
6. **When is routine episiotomy indicated?** → It is NOT — restrictive use only
7. **Muscles cut in mediolateral** → Bulbospongiosus + superficial transverse perineal
8. **3rd/4th degree repair setting** → Operating theater, regional/GA, experienced surgeon
9. **Post-3rd degree tear management** → Antibiotics + laxatives + perineal clinic follow-up
10. **Angle of mediolateral episiotomy** → 45-60° from midline
