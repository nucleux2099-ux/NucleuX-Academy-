# Normal Labor & Partograph — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Normal labor has 4 stages. The partograph tracks active phase progress with alert and action lines. Crossing the action line (4 hours right of alert) mandates intervention. Active management of third stage (AMTSL) reduces PPH by 60%.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Active phase starts at 4 cm cervical dilatation
- ★ Normal dilation rate: ≥1 cm/hour (active phase)
- ★ Action line: 4 hours to the right of alert line
- ★ Most favorable pelvis: Gynecoid (50% of women)
- ★ AMTSL: Oxytocin 10 IU IM + controlled cord traction + uterine massage
- ★ Second stage duration: Up to 2 hours (primi), 1 hour (multi)
- ★ FHR monitoring: Every 15 min (1st stage) → every 5 min (2nd stage)
- ★ Normal contractions in active labor: 3-5 in 10 minutes, >40 seconds each
- ★ Partograph recording starts only in ACTIVE phase (not latent)
- ★ Latent phase: Up to 20 hours (primi) is normal

## Mnemonics
**Stages of Labor Duration** — "20-2-30-2":
- 1st stage: Up to 20 hours (primi latent + active)
- 2nd stage: Up to 2 hours
- 3rd stage: Up to 30 minutes
- 4th stage: 2 hours observation

**AMTSL**:
- **A**ctive **M**anagement of **T**hird **S**tage of **L**abor
- Oxytocin + Traction + Massage
