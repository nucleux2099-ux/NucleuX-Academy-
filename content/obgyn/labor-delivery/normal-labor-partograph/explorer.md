# Normal Labor & Partograph

## Overview
Normal labor is the process by which the fetus, placenta, and membranes are expelled through the birth canal after 37 completed weeks of gestation. The partograph is a graphical record of the progress of labor and key maternal-fetal observations, serving as an early warning system to detect deviations from normal.

The WHO modified partograph (2000) begins recording in the active phase (cervical dilatation ≥4 cm) and uses an alert line and action line to guide clinical decisions.

## Key Points
- Labor has 4 stages: dilation, expulsion, placental delivery, and the fourth stage (1 hour post-delivery)
- Active phase begins at 4 cm cervical dilatation
- Normal cervical dilation rate: ≥1 cm/hour in active phase
- Partograph alert line: Expected progress at 1 cm/hour
- Partograph action line: 4 hours to the right of alert line
- Crossing the action line mandates intervention

## Definition & Classification
### Stages of Labor
- **First stage**: Onset of true labor to full cervical dilatation (10 cm)
  - Latent phase: 0-4 cm (up to 20 hours in primi, 14 hours in multi)
  - Active phase: 4-10 cm (rate ≥1 cm/hour)
- **Second stage**: Full dilatation to delivery of baby
  - Primi: up to 2 hours (3 hours with epidural)
  - Multi: up to 1 hour (2 hours with epidural)
- **Third stage**: Delivery of baby to delivery of placenta (up to 30 minutes)
- **Fourth stage**: First 1-2 hours after placental delivery (observation period)

### Types of Pelvis (Caldwell-Moloy)
- Gynecoid (most favorable, 50%)
- Android
- Anthropoid
- Platypelloid (least favorable)

## The Partograph
### Components
1. **Fetal condition**: Heart rate, membranes/liquor, moulding
2. **Progress of labor**: Cervical dilatation, descent of head, contractions
3. **Maternal condition**: Pulse, BP, temperature, urine (volume, protein, acetone)

### Plotting
- Cervical dilatation plotted with 'X' on the graph
- Descent plotted with 'O'
- First plot at the alert line when active phase begins (4 cm)
- Alert line: Slope of 1 cm/hour starting from 4 cm
- Action line: Parallel, 4 hours to the right

### Interpretation
- **Progress on or left of alert line**: Normal progress, continue observation
- **Between alert and action lines**: Increased vigilance, identify cause of delay
- **On or beyond action line**: Intervention needed (ARM, oxytocin augmentation, or cesarean section)

### Contractions
- Plotted every 30 minutes
- Count in 10-minute periods
- Shade: Dots (<20 sec), diagonal lines (20-40 sec), solid (>40 sec)
- Normal active labor: 3-5 contractions in 10 minutes, each >40 seconds

## Clinical Features of Normal Labor
### Onset Signs
- Show (blood-stained mucus plug)
- Regular painful contractions (increasing frequency and intensity)
- Progressive cervical effacement and dilatation
- Spontaneous rupture of membranes (may occur)

### Monitoring During Labor
- Fetal heart rate: Every 15-30 minutes in first stage, every 5 minutes in second stage
- Contractions: Every 30 minutes
- Cervical dilatation: Every 4 hours
- Maternal vitals: BP and pulse hourly, temperature 4-hourly

## Diagnosis of Labor
- True labor vs false labor differentiation
- True: Regular contractions, progressive dilation, pain radiates to back
- False (Braxton Hicks): Irregular, no cervical change, relieved by walking

## Management
### First Stage
- Encourage mobilization and upright positions
- Oral fluids and light diet in latent phase
- Partograph monitoring from active phase
- ARM if indicated (augmentation)
- Pain relief: Non-pharmacological, Entonox, epidural

### Second Stage
- Guide pushing with contractions
- Monitor fetal heart after every contraction
- Controlled delivery of head (Ritgen maneuver)
- Check for nuchal cord
- Deliver shoulders with gentle traction

### Third Stage
- Active management (AMTSL): Oxytocin 10 IU IM, controlled cord traction, uterine massage
- Reduces PPH risk by 60%
- Inspect placenta and membranes for completeness

### Fourth Stage
- Monitor vitals every 15 minutes for 1-2 hours
- Monitor uterine tone, bleeding, and bladder
- Initiate breastfeeding

## Complications
- Prolonged labor (crossing action line)
- Obstructed labor
- Fetal distress
- Cord prolapse
- Postpartum hemorrhage
- Perineal tears
- Retained placenta
