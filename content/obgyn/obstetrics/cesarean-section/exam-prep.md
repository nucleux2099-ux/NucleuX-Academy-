# Cesarean Section — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Surgical delivery via abdominal + uterine incision. Most common indication: previous CS. Emergency: fetal distress. Standard: Pfannenstiel + LSCS. Category 1 DDI <30 min. Antibiotic before skin incision. Regional anesthesia preferred. VBAC 60-80% success, 0.5-1% rupture risk. Elective CS at 39 weeks. Each CS increases accreta risk. Most common organ injured: bladder.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Most common indication**: previous cesarean section
- ★ **Category 1**: immediate threat → DDI <30 minutes
- ★ **Antibiotic timing**: BEFORE skin incision (Cefazolin 2g IV)
- ★ **Elective CS timing**: 39 completed weeks
- ★ **Most common organ injured**: urinary bladder
- ★ **VBAC rupture risk**: 0.5-1% (LSCS scar); 4-9% (classical scar)
- ★ **Neonatal complication**: TTN (no labor = no catecholamine surge)
- ★ **Accreta risk**: ↑ with each CS (1st: 0.3%, 3rd: 2.4%, ≥5: 6.7%)
- ★ **Joel-Cohen**: faster, less blood loss, blunt dissection
