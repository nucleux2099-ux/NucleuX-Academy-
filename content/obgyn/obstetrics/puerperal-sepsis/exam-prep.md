# Puerperal Sepsis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Puerperal sepsis = genital tract infection within 42 days postpartum. Most common: endometritis (polymicrobial). #1 risk factor: cesarean section. Diagnosis: fever + uterine tenderness + foul lochia. Gold standard treatment: IV clindamycin + gentamicin. Add ampicillin if no response (triple regimen). Septic pelvic thrombophlebitis: persistent fever despite antibiotics → add heparin.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Cesarean section** is the single greatest risk factor for puerperal infection
- ★ Most puerperal infections are **polymicrobial** (ascending vaginal flora)
- ★ Endometritis diagnosis: **fever + uterine tenderness + foul lochia**
- ★ Gold standard antibiotics: **IV clindamycin + gentamicin**
- ★ If no response in 48h → add **ampicillin** (covers enterococcus) = triple regimen
- ★ Group A Streptococcus: historically most virulent; causes **fulminant sepsis**
- ★ Septic pelvic thrombophlebitis: **picket-fence fever** despite antibiotics → **heparin** → dramatic response
- ★ Classical definition: ≥ **38°C** on any **2 of first 10 days** (excluding day 1)
- ★ PROM > **18 hours** = significant risk factor
- ★ Source control essential: drain abscess, evacuate retained products
- ★ Antibiotics within **1 hour** in sepsis (Hour-1 Bundle)

## Mnemonics

### **"4 Fs"** — Features of Endometritis
- **F**ever
- **F**oul lochia
- **F**undal tenderness
- **F**ailure of involution (subinvolution)

### **"CGAm"** — Triple Antibiotic Regimen
- **C**lindamycin
- **G**entamicin
- **Am**picillin (add if no response)

### **"SEPT"** — Risk Factors for Puerperal Sepsis
- **S**urgery (cesarean section)
- **E**xtended ROM (>18 hours)
- **P**rolonged labor / multiple vaginal exams
- **T**issue retained (products of conception)

### **"Picket Fence = Pelvic Vein"**
- Spiking fever despite antibiotics → think **septic pelvic thrombophlebitis** → add **heparin**
