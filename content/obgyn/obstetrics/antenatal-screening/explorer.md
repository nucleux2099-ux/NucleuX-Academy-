# Antenatal Screening

## Overview

Antenatal screening refers to systematic testing offered to all pregnant women to identify those at increased risk of specific conditions, primarily chromosomal abnormalities (Down syndrome, Edwards syndrome, Patau syndrome), structural anomalies, and certain maternal conditions. Screening tests provide a risk estimate and are not diagnostic. A positive screen requires follow-up with diagnostic tests (CVS or amniocentesis). Modern screening achieves detection rates >90% for Down syndrome with first-trimester combined screening or NIPT.

## Key Points

- Screening ≠ diagnosis: screening gives risk, diagnosis confirms/excludes
- First-trimester combined test: NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG (11-13+6 weeks)
- Detection rate for Down syndrome: combined test ~90%, NIPT ~99%
- Screen positive cutoff: risk >1:250 (or >1:150 in some centers)
- Quadruple test (2nd trimester): AFP, hCG, uE3, inhibin A
- NIPT (cell-free DNA): most sensitive non-invasive screening test
- Diagnostic tests: CVS (11-13 weeks), Amniocentesis (15-18 weeks)
- Anomaly scan at 18-22 weeks screens structural defects

## Definition & Classification

### Types of Screening

#### First-Trimester Screening (11-13+6 weeks)
| Test | Components | Detection Rate |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **NT alone** | Nuchal translucency measurement | 70-75% |
| **Combined test** | NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG | 85-90% |
| **NIPT (cfDNA)** | Cell-free fetal DNA in maternal blood | 99% |

#### Second-Trimester Screening (15-20 weeks)
| Test | Components | Detection Rate |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Triple test** | AFP + hCG + uE3 | 65-70% |
| **Quadruple test** | AFP + hCG + uE3 + inhibin A | 75-80% |

#### Integrated Screening
| Test | Components | Detection Rate |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Integrated test** | 1st trimester (NT + PAPP-A) + 2nd trimester (quad) | 90-95% |
| **Sequential screening** | Combined test → quad test if intermediate risk | 90-95% |

### Ultrasound Screening
- **NT scan** (11-13+6 weeks): CRL 45-84 mm
- **Anomaly scan** (18-22 weeks): detailed structural survey
- **Soft markers**: choroid plexus cyst, echogenic bowel, short femur, pyelectasis, echogenic intracardiac focus

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Nuchal Translucency (NT)
- Subcutaneous fluid collection at back of fetal neck
- Normal: <3 mm (or <95th percentile for CRL)
- Increased NT associated with: trisomy 21, 18, 13, Turner syndrome, cardiac defects
- Mechanism: cardiac dysfunction, lymphatic malformation, altered extracellular matrix

### Biochemical Markers
| Marker | Down Syndrome | Edwards (T18) | NTD |
|--------|--------------|---------------|-----|
| **PAPP-A** | ↓ | ↓↓ | Normal |
| **Free β-hCG** | ↑↑ | ↓↓ | Normal |
| **AFP** | ↓ | ↓ | ↑↑ |
| **uE3** | ↓ | ↓↓ | Normal |
| **Inhibin A** | ↑ | ↓ | Normal |

### NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing)
- Analyzes cell-free fetal DNA (cffDNA) from maternal plasma
- fetal fraction: ~10-15% of total cfDNA at 10 weeks
- Screens for trisomy 21, 18, 13, sex chromosome aneuploidies
- Can be done from 10 weeks onwards
- **Still a screening test** — positive NIPT requires confirmatory amniocentesis/CVS
- PPV varies with prevalence: higher in older mothers

## Clinical Features

### NT Measurement — Technical Requirements
- CRL 45-84 mm (11-13+6 weeks)
- Mid-sagittal plane of fetus
- Fetal neck in neutral position
- Calipers placed on inner borders of NT space
- Largest measurement recorded
- Nasal bone assessed simultaneously

### Anomaly Scan (18-22 weeks) — Systematic Survey
1. **Head**: BPD, HC, cerebellum, posterior fossa, choroid plexus, midline structures
2. **Face**: lips, orbits, profile
3. **Spine**: continuity, sacrum
4. **Heart**: four-chamber view, outflow tracts, three-vessel view
5. **Abdomen**: stomach, kidneys, bladder, cord insertion, AC
6. **Limbs**: long bones, hands, feet
7. **Placenta**: location, cord, amniotic fluid

### Soft Markers for Down Syndrome
| Marker | Likelihood Ratio |
|--------|-----------------|
| Increased NT (2nd trimester nuchal fold >6 mm) | 11-18 |
| Absent/hypoplastic nasal bone | 6.6 |
| Short humerus | 5.1 |
| Short femur | 1.5 |
| Echogenic intracardiac focus | 1.8 |
| Echogenic bowel | 6.7 |
| Mild pyelectasis (>4 mm) | 1.5 |

## Diagnosis

### Diagnostic Tests (Invasive — Confirmatory)
| Test | Timing | Sample | Risk |
|------|--------|--------|------|
| **CVS** | 11-13 weeks | Trophoblast | Miscarriage 1-2% |
| **Amniocentesis** | 15-18 weeks | Amniotic fluid | Miscarriage 0.5-1% |
| **Cordocentesis** | >18 weeks | Fetal blood | Miscarriage 1-2% |

### Indications for Invasive Testing
- Screen positive (combined test/NIPT positive)
- Advanced maternal age (≥35 years at delivery)
- Previous child with chromosomal abnormality
- Known parental chromosomal translocation
- Abnormal ultrasound findings
- Family history of genetic disorder

### Newer Diagnostic Technologies
- **QF-PCR**: rapid results for trisomy 21, 18, 13, sex chromosomes (24-48 hours)
- **Microarray (aCGH)**: detects microdeletions/duplications
- **Karyotyping**: gold standard but takes 2-3 weeks

## Management

### Screening Pathway
1. **Offer screening** to all women with counseling
2. **Low risk** (<1:1000) → reassure, continue routine care
3. **Intermediate risk** (1:150 to 1:1000) → offer NIPT
4. **High risk** (>1:150) → offer CVS or amniocentesis directly
5. **Positive diagnostic test** → genetic counseling, discuss options

### If Abnormality Detected
- Multidisciplinary counseling (obstetrician, geneticist, neonatologist)
- Options: continue pregnancy with planning, termination (if legal and desired)
- Psychological support
- Written information
- Non-directive counseling

### Neural Tube Defect Screening
- AFP elevated (>2.5 MoM) in maternal serum → suggestive
- Confirmed on anomaly scan (anencephaly, spina bifida)
- Prevention: folic acid 400 μg daily preconception and through 1st trimester
- High-risk women: folic acid 5 mg/day (previous NTD, epilepsy drugs)

## Complications

### False Positive Results
- Unnecessary anxiety
- Invasive testing with miscarriage risk
- Psychological distress
- Cost burden

### False Negative Results
- False reassurance
- Missed diagnosis → unprepared delivery
- Legal implications

### Invasive Procedure Risks
- Miscarriage (main risk)
- Infection
- Fetomaternal hemorrhage (Rh sensitization — give anti-D)
- Limb defects (CVS before 10 weeks — now avoided)
- Preterm labor
- Amniotic fluid leak (amniocentesis)
