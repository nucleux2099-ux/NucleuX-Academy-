# Antenatal Screening — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Screening estimates risk; diagnostic tests confirm. Combined test (NT + PAPP-A + β-hCG) at 11-13+6 weeks: 90% detection. NIPT (cfDNA) from 10 weeks: 99% detection but still screening. Quadruple test (15-20 weeks): AFP, hCG, uE3, inhibin A. Screen positive >1:150-250. Diagnosis: CVS (11-13w) or amniocentesis (15-18w). Anomaly scan 18-22 weeks. Down syndrome: ↓PAPP-A, ↑β-hCG, ↑NT. NTD: ↑AFP.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Down syndrome biochemistry**: ↓PAPP-A, ↑↑free β-hCG (1st tri); ↓AFP, ↑hCG, ↓uE3, ↑inhibin A (2nd tri)
- ★ **Edwards (T18)**: ALL markers ↓↓ (including hCG — unlike Down)
- ★ **NTD**: ↑↑AFP (>2.5 MoM); normal other markers
- ★ **NT cutoff**: ≥3 mm or >95th percentile for CRL = increased
- ★ **NIPT**: most sensitive screening (99% for T21), NOT diagnostic
- ★ **NIPT positive** → must confirm with CVS/amniocentesis
- ★ **Combined test timing**: 11-13+6 weeks (CRL 45-84 mm)
- ★ **Anomaly scan**: 18-22 weeks — systematic structural survey
- ★ **Folic acid 400 μg** prevents NTD; 5 mg if high risk
- ★ **CVS before 10 weeks** → limb defects (contraindicated)

## Mnemonics

### **"Down = β-hCG UP"** — Down Syndrome Markers
- β-hCG ↑↑, Inhibin A ↑, NT ↑
- PAPP-A ↓, AFP ↓, uE3 ↓
- **"Inhibited PAPE, Elevated Baby hCG"**

### **"Edwards = Everything DOWN"**
- All markers decreased including hCG (distinguishes from Down)

### **"NT-PAPP-β"** — Combined Test Components
- **N**uchal **T**ranslucency
- **P**APP-A (↓ in Down)
- **β**-hCG free (↑ in Down)

### **"QUAD"** — Quadruple Test
- **Q**: (AFP)
- **U**: Unconjugated estriol (uE3)
- **A**: (hCG)
- **D**: Dimeric inhibin A

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **Most sensitive non-invasive screening for Down** → NIPT (cfDNA), 99%
2. **Combined test components** → NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG
3. **Timing for NT scan** → 11-13+6 weeks (CRL 45-84 mm)
4. **Elevated AFP in maternal serum** → NTD, omphalocele, twins, underestimated GA
5. **↓AFP + ↑hCG** → Down syndrome
6. **All markers decreased** → Edwards syndrome (T18)
7. **Best time for anomaly scan** → 18-22 weeks
8. **NIPT positive — next step** → Confirmatory CVS or amniocentesis (not reassurance)
9. **Folic acid dose for NTD prevention** → 400 μg (routine); 5 mg (high risk)
10. **CVS advantage over amniocentesis** → Earlier (11-13 weeks vs 15-18 weeks)
