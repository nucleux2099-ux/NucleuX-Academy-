# Postpartum Hemorrhage — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

PPH = blood loss ≥500 mL (vaginal) or ≥1000 mL (CS). Primary <24 hr, secondary 24 hr–12 wk. "4 Ts": Tone (70–80%), Trauma, Tissue, Thrombin. Prevention: AMTSL (oxytocin 10 IU IM). Stepwise: uterotonics → tamponade (Bakri balloon) → surgical (B-Lynch, artery ligation) → hysterectomy. Tranexamic acid 1 g IV within 3 hours (WOMAN trial). Sheehan syndrome = pituitary necrosis.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Most common cause of PPH: **uterine atony** (70–80%)
- ★ **4 Ts**: Tone, Trauma, Tissue, Thrombin
- ★ AMTSL: **oxytocin 10 IU IM** immediately after baby delivery
- ★ **Carboprost (PGF₂α)**: 250 µg IM q15min (max 8 doses); **contraindicated in asthma**
- ★ **Ergometrine**: contraindicated in **hypertension**
- ★ **Tranexamic acid 1 g IV** within **3 hours** of delivery (WOMAN trial)
- ★ B-Lynch suture: **compression suture** for atonic PPH
- ★ Internal iliac artery ligation reduces pulse pressure by **85%**
- ★ Massive transfusion: **1:1:1** PRBC:FFP:Platelets
- ★ Fibrinogen < **200 mg/dL** → poor prognosis; give cryoprecipitate
- ★ **Sheehan syndrome**: failure of **lactation** is earliest sign → panhypopituitarism

## Mnemonics

### **"4 Ts"** — Causes of PPH
- **T**one (atony — #1)
- **T**rauma (tears, rupture)
- **T**issue (retained placenta)
- **T**hrombin (coagulopathy)

### **"OECMT"** — Uterotonics (order)
- **O**xytocin (first line)
- **E**rgometrine (avoid in HTN)
- **C**arboprost / PGF₂α (avoid in asthma)
- **M**isoprostol (sublingual/rectal)
- **T**ranexamic acid (antifibrinolytic)

### **"BUTSH"** — Stepwise PPH Management
- **B**imanual compression
- **U**terotonics
- **T**amponade (Bakri balloon)
- **S**urgery (B-Lynch, artery ligation)
- **H**ysterectomy (last resort)

### **"Sheehan = She Can't Lactate"**
- Earliest sign of Sheehan syndrome = **failure of lactation** (prolactin deficiency)
