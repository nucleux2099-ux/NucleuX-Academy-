# Contraception — Postpartum

## Overview

Postpartum contraception is critical to prevent unintended pregnancies and ensure adequate interpregnancy intervals. WHO recommends a minimum of 24 months between delivery and next conception for optimal maternal and neonatal outcomes. Fertility can return as early as 21 days postpartum in non-breastfeeding women. Choice of contraceptive must consider breastfeeding status, timing postpartum, medical eligibility, and patient preference. Lactational amenorrhea method (LAM) provides natural protection under strict conditions.

## Key Points

- Fertility returns as early as 21 days postpartum (non-lactating)
- WHO recommends ≥24 months interpregnancy interval
- LAM effective (98%) only with exclusive breastfeeding + amenorrhea + <6 months
- Combined hormonal contraceptives (CHCs) avoided in first 6 weeks if breastfeeding
- Progestogen-only methods safe during breastfeeding
- IUCD can be inserted within 48 hours (PPIUCD) or after 4-6 weeks
- Postpartum sterilization: within 7 days or after 6 weeks

## Definition & Classification

### Timing Categories
| Period | Duration | Options |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Immediate postpartum | Within 48 hours | PPIUCD, sterilization, implant |
| Early postpartum | 48 hours - 6 weeks | Progestogen-only methods |
| Late postpartum | >6 weeks | All methods (with considerations) |

### Methods Available Postpartum
1. **Lactational amenorrhea method (LAM)**
2. **Barrier methods** (condoms, diaphragm)
3. **Progestogen-only pills (POP)**
4. **Injectable (DMPA)**
5. **Implants (etonogestrel)**
6. **Intrauterine devices (Cu-IUD, LNG-IUS)**
7. **Combined hormonal contraceptives** (COC, patch, ring)
8. **Sterilization** (tubal ligation, vasectomy)
9. **Natural methods** (unreliable in postpartum)

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Return of Fertility
- **Non-breastfeeding**: ovulation can occur by day 21; first menses by 6-8 weeks
- **Breastfeeding**: ovulation delayed (varies: 6 weeks to >12 months)
- Prolactin suppresses GnRH pulsatility → anovulation
- As breastfeeding frequency decreases → fertility returns

### LAM Mechanism
- Suckling stimulus → prolactin release → GnRH suppression → anovulation
- Effective only if ALL three criteria met:
  1. Exclusive/nearly exclusive breastfeeding (no supplementation)
  2. Amenorrhea (no menstrual bleeding after 56 days postpartum)
  3. Baby <6 months old
- Failure rate: 1-2% if criteria strictly followed

### Hormonal Considerations in Breastfeeding
- **Estrogen** (in CHCs): may reduce milk production, passes into breast milk
- **Progestogen-only**: minimal effect on lactation, safe
- Combined methods avoided until 6 months if exclusively breastfeeding (WHO MEC category 3)
- If not breastfeeding: CHCs can start from 21 days postpartum (with VTE risk consideration)

## Clinical Features

### WHO Medical Eligibility Criteria (MEC) — Postpartum

#### Breastfeeding Women
| Method | <6 weeks | 6 weeks-6 months | >6 months |
|--------|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| CHCs (COC/patch/ring) | 4 (contraindicated) | 3 (generally not) | 2 (generally use) |
| POP | 2 | 1 | 1 |
| DMPA | 3 | 1 | 1 |
| Implant | 2 | 1 | 1 |
| Cu-IUD | See timing | 1 | 1 |
| LNG-IUS | See timing | 1 | 1 |

*MEC: 1 = no restriction, 2 = generally use, 3 = generally don't, 4 = contraindicated*

#### Non-Breastfeeding Women
| Method | <21 days | 21-42 days | >42 days |
|--------|----------|-----------|----------|
| CHCs (no VTE risk) | 3 | 2 | 1 |
| CHCs (with VTE risk) | 4 | 3 | 2 |
| POP | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| DMPA | 1 | 1 | 1 |

### PPIUCD (Postplacental/Postpartum IUD)
- **Postplacental**: within 10 minutes of placental delivery
- **Immediate postpartum**: within 48 hours
- **After 48 hours**: wait until 4-6 weeks (higher expulsion if inserted 48h-4 weeks)
- **At cesarean**: inserted through uterine incision
- Expulsion rate: higher with immediate insertion (5-15%) vs interval (2-5%)
- Advantages: convenient timing, high motivation, long-acting

## Diagnosis

Not applicable — this is a preventive care topic. Key assessment involves:
- Breastfeeding status
- Time since delivery
- Medical history / contraindications
- VTE risk factors
- Future fertility desires
- Previous contraceptive experience

## Management

### Counseling Framework (WHO/GATHER)
- **G**reet
- **A**sk about reproductive goals
- **T**ell about available methods
- **H**elp choose appropriate method
- **E**xplain how to use
- **R**eturn visit scheduled

### Method-Specific Guidance

#### LAM
- Counsel on strict criteria; transition to another method at 6 months or when any criterion fails

#### Progestogen-Only Methods
- **POP**: start from day 21 (or any time if additional contraception × 48h)
- **DMPA**: from 6 weeks if breastfeeding (WHO MEC 3 before 6 weeks)
- **Implant (Nexplanon)**: can insert before discharge or at 4-6 week visit

#### Combined Hormonal Methods
- **Breastfeeding**: avoid until 6 months (WHO MEC)
- **Non-breastfeeding**: from 21 days (if no VTE risk factors)
- **VTE risk**: avoid until 42 days postpartum

#### IUDs
- Cu-IUD: effective immediately; LNG-IUS: effective in 7 days (if not postplacental)
- Counsel on higher expulsion with immediate insertion
- Check for expulsion at 6-week visit

#### Sterilization
- **Minilap tubal ligation**: within 7 days of delivery (subumbilical incision, easy access) OR after 6 weeks
- **Laparoscopic sterilization**: after 6 weeks
- **Modified Pomeroy technique**: most common (excise segment, ligate with absorbable suture)
- Counsel: permanent, regret risk, informed consent mandatory
- Vasectomy: partner option, highly effective after 3 months/20 ejaculations

#### Emergency Contraception Postpartum
- Not needed before day 21 (fertility not returned)
- After day 21: Cu-IUD (most effective) or levonorgestrel 1.5 mg (safe in breastfeeding)

## Complications

### Method-Specific Complications
| Method | Key Complications |
|--------|------------------|
| CHCs | ↓Milk supply (if early), VTE risk (elevated postpartum) |
| DMPA | Delayed fertility return, weight gain, bone density ↓ |
| IUCD | Expulsion (higher with PPIUCD), perforation, infection |
| Implant | Irregular bleeding, difficult removal |
| Sterilization | Surgical risks, regret, failure (1/200 lifetime) |
| LAM failure | Unintended pregnancy if criteria not met |

### VTE Risk Postpartum
- Postpartum period = hypercoagulable state
- Risk highest in first 6 weeks (especially first 3 weeks)
- Estrogen-containing methods add to VTE risk
- Hence CHCs avoided in first 21-42 days
