# Contraception Postpartum — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Fertility returns day 21 (non-lactating). LAM effective 98% if: exclusive breastfeeding + amenorrhea + <6 months. CHCs contraindicated <6 weeks if breastfeeding (MEC 4). Progestogen-only safe during lactation. PPIUCD within 48h (higher expulsion). Sterilization: within 7 days or after 6 weeks. CHCs in non-breastfeeding: from day 21 (no VTE risk). Interpregnancy interval: ≥24 months recommended.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Fertility returns by day 21** in non-breastfeeding women
- ★ **LAM criteria** (ALL 3): exclusive breastfeeding + amenorrhea + <6 months
- ★ **CHCs + breastfeeding <6 weeks** = MEC Category 4 (contraindicated)
- ★ **Progestogen-only methods**: safe during breastfeeding from day 21
- ★ **PPIUCD timing**: within 48h or wait until 4-6 weeks (48h-4wk = high expulsion)
- ★ **Postpartum sterilization**: within 7 days (subumbilical minilap) or after 6 weeks
- ★ **Modified Pomeroy**: most common sterilization technique
- ★ **VTE risk highest**: first 3-6 weeks postpartum → avoid estrogen
- ★ **DMPA in breastfeeding**: MEC 3 before 6 weeks, MEC 1 after 6 weeks
- ★ **Emergency contraception**: not needed before day 21

## Mnemonics

### **"LAM-BEA"** — LAM Criteria
- **B**reastfeeding exclusively
- **E**ndometrium not shedding (amenorrhea)
- **A**ge of baby <6 months

### **"No Estrogen Early"** — CHC Timing
- Breastfeeding: NO estrogen for 6 months
- Non-breastfeeding: NO estrogen for 21 days (42 if VTE risk)

### **"7 or 6"** — Sterilization Timing
- Within **7** days postpartum
- OR after **6** weeks postpartum
- (In between = difficult due to uterine involution)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. **When does fertility return postpartum?** → Day 21 (non-lactating)
2. **LAM failure rate** → 1-2% if all criteria met
3. **CHCs in breastfeeding** → MEC 4 (<6 weeks), MEC 3 (6 weeks-6 months)
4. **PPIUCD expulsion rate** → 5-15% (higher than interval insertion)
5. **Safest hormonal method during breastfeeding** → POP or implant
6. **Most common sterilization technique** → Modified Pomeroy
7. **Why avoid CHCs early postpartum?** → VTE risk (hypercoagulable state)
8. **PPIUCD insertion window** → Within 48 hours, or wait 4-6 weeks
9. **EC in breastfeeding** → Levonorgestrel 1.5 mg (safe) or Cu-IUD
10. **WHO recommended interpregnancy interval** → ≥24 months
