# Antenatal Investigations

## Overview

Antenatal investigations are a series of laboratory tests, imaging studies, and screening procedures performed during pregnancy to assess maternal health, identify risk factors, detect fetal abnormalities, and monitor pregnancy progression. These investigations are organized by trimester and follow national guidelines to ensure timely detection and management of complications. A structured approach to antenatal care reduces maternal and perinatal morbidity and mortality.

## Key Points

- Investigations organized by trimester: booking, first, second, third trimester
- Booking visit investigations: blood group, Rh, CBC, HIV, HBsAg, VDRL, urine R/M
- First trimester: dating scan (11-14 weeks), combined screening (NT + biochemistry)
- Second trimester: anomaly scan (18-22 weeks), quadruple test
- Third trimester: growth scans, GBS screening (35-37 weeks), CTG
- Blood group and Rh typing essential at first visit (anti-D prophylaxis for Rh-negative)
- OGTT for GDM screening at 24-28 weeks
- Indirect Coombs test (ICT) for Rh-negative mothers

## Definition & Classification

### Schedule of Investigations by Timing

#### Booking Visit (First Contact)
| Investigation | Purpose |
|--------------|---------|
| Blood group + Rh typing | ABO/Rh incompatibility risk |
| Complete blood count | Anemia, thrombocytopenia |
| Hemoglobin electrophoresis | Thalassemia/sickle cell screening |
| HIV screening | PPTCT (Prevention of Parent to Child Transmission) |
| HBsAg | Hepatitis B carrier status |
| VDRL/RPR | Syphilis screening |
| Urine routine/microscopy | UTI, proteinuria, glycosuria |
| Blood sugar (fasting/random) | Pre-existing diabetes |
| TSH | Thyroid disorders |
| Rubella IgG | Immunity status |

#### First Trimester (11-14 weeks)
| Investigation | Purpose |
|--------------|---------|
| Dating ultrasound | Confirm viability, GA, CRL, number of fetuses |
| NT scan + nasal bone | Down syndrome screening |
| Combined test (NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG) | Trisomy 21/18/13 screening |
| Chorionic villus sampling (if indicated) | Karyotyping (11-13 weeks) |

#### Second Trimester (15-22 weeks)
| Investigation | Purpose |
|--------------|---------|
| Anomaly scan (18-22 weeks) | Structural fetal abnormalities |
| Quadruple test (15-20 weeks) | Down syndrome screening (AFP, hCG, uE3, inhibin A) |
| Amniocentesis (15-18 weeks, if indicated) | Karyotyping, genetic testing |
| OGTT (24-28 weeks) | GDM screening |
| Indirect Coombs test | Rh antibodies (Rh-negative mothers) |

#### Third Trimester (28-40 weeks)
| Investigation | Purpose |
|--------------|---------|
| CBC (28, 36 weeks) | Anemia |
| Growth scan (28, 32, 36 weeks) | Fetal growth, AFI, placental grading |
| GBS culture (35-37 weeks) | Group B Streptococcus screening |
| Non-stress test / CTG | Fetal well-being (from 32 weeks) |
| Biophysical profile | Fetal well-being (high-risk) |
| Doppler studies | IUGR, high-risk pregnancies |
| Repeat Rh antibody screen (28 weeks) | Rh-negative mothers |
| Anti-D prophylaxis (28 weeks) | Rh-negative, unsensitized |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Rationale for Screening
- **Universal screening**: conditions with high prevalence or serious consequences (HIV, HBsAg, anemia)
- **Risk-based screening**: based on risk factors (amniocentesis for advanced maternal age)
- **Trimester-specific**: timing matters — some tests only valid at specific gestational ages

### Principles of Screening (Wilson & Jungner Criteria)
- Important health problem
- Accepted treatment available
- Suitable test available
- Natural history of condition understood
- Cost-effective

## Clinical Features

### Key Investigations in Detail

#### Blood Group & Rh Typing
- Determines ABO and Rh(D) status
- Rh-negative mothers: risk of hemolytic disease of newborn
- Indirect Coombs test (ICT): detects anti-D antibodies
- Anti-D immunoglobulin 300 μg IM at 28 weeks + within 72 hours of delivery

#### Complete Blood Count
- Hemoglobin: normal in pregnancy 11 g/dL (physiological hemodilution)
- Mild anemia: 10-10.9, Moderate: 7-9.9, Severe: <7 g/dL
- Platelet count: mild gestational thrombocytopenia is common
- WBC: physiologically elevated in pregnancy (up to 16,000)

#### OGTT (Oral Glucose Tolerance Test) — GDM Screening
- **Timing**: 24-28 weeks (earlier if high risk)
- **WHO/IADPSG criteria** (75g OGTT):
  - Fasting ≥92 mg/dL
  - 1 hour ≥180 mg/dL
  - 2 hour ≥153 mg/dL
  - ANY one value abnormal = GDM
- **Carpenter-Coustan** (100g OGTT): 2 or more abnormal values

#### Urine Analysis
- Proteinuria: preeclampsia screening (dipstick → confirm with spot PCR or 24-hour protein)
- Glycosuria: GDM screening (low threshold in pregnancy)
- UTI: asymptomatic bacteriuria → treat (risk of pyelonephritis and preterm labor)

## Diagnosis

### Interpreting Key Results

#### Anemia in Pregnancy
| Severity | Hemoglobin |
|----------|-----------|
| Normal | ≥11 g/dL |
| Mild | 10-10.9 g/dL |
| Moderate | 7-9.9 g/dL |
| Severe | <7 g/dL |

#### Thyroid in Pregnancy
- TSH reference: trimester-specific (1st: <2.5, 2nd/3rd: <3.0 mU/L — older guidelines)
- Current: use population-based reference ranges
- Subclinical hypothyroidism: treat if TSH >4.0 mU/L or >2.5 with TPO antibodies

#### Rh-Negative Management
1. Blood group + Rh at booking
2. ICT at booking, 28 weeks, 36 weeks
3. Anti-D 300 μg at 28 weeks (routine antenatal prophylaxis)
4. Anti-D within 72 hours of delivery (if baby Rh-positive)
5. Anti-D after sensitizing events (APH, amniocentesis, ECV, trauma)

## Management

### Recommended Visit Schedule (WHO)
- Minimum 8 contacts during pregnancy
- First contact: before 12 weeks
- Subsequent: 20, 26, 30, 34, 36, 38, 40 weeks

### When to Refer for Further Investigation
- Abnormal screening results → diagnostic testing
- Combined test high risk (>1:150) → CVS or amniocentesis
- Anomaly scan abnormality → tertiary center referral
- IUGR on growth scan → Doppler surveillance
- Abnormal OGTT → diabetology referral
- Positive VDRL → confirmatory FTA-ABS + treatment

### Additional Investigations in High-Risk Pregnancies
- Echocardiography (maternal cardiac disease)
- Renal function tests (pre-existing renal disease, preeclampsia)
- Liver function tests (cholestasis, HELLP)
- Coagulation profile (thrombophilia, DIC risk)
- Bile acids (obstetric cholestasis — >10 μmol/L diagnostic)
- Anti-phospholipid antibodies (recurrent miscarriage)
- TORCH screening (if clinically indicated, NOT routine)

## Complications

### Risks of Invasive Procedures
| Procedure | Timing | Risk of Miscarriage |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| CVS | 11-13 weeks | 1-2% |
| Amniocentesis | 15-18 weeks | 0.5-1% |
| Cordocentesis | >18 weeks | 1-2% |

### Consequences of Missed Screening
- Undetected Rh sensitization → hydrops fetalis
- Undetected GDM → macrosomia, birth injury, neonatal hypoglycemia
- Undetected HIV → vertical transmission
- Missed congenital anomaly → unprepared delivery
- Untreated syphilis → congenital syphilis
- Untreated asymptomatic bacteriuria → pyelonephritis, preterm labor
