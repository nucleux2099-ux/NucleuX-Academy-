# Placenta Physiology

## Overview

The placenta is the vital organ of pregnancy, serving as the interface between maternal and fetal circulations. It performs respiratory, nutritional, excretory, endocrine, and immunological functions for the fetus. Understanding placental development, structure, and function is essential for comprehending pregnancy complications including preeclampsia, FGR, and placental abruption.

## Key Points

- Definitive placenta formed by end of first trimester (chorion frondosum + decidua basalis)
- At term: discoid, 15–20 cm diameter, 2–3 cm thick, ~500 g
- Maternal blood bathes fetal villi in intervillous space (hemochorial placentation)
- Functions: gas exchange, nutrient transfer, waste removal, hormone production, immune barrier
- Placental hormones: hCG, hPL, estrogen, progesterone
- Placental barrier: syncytiotrophoblast + cytotrophoblast + basement membrane + fetal capillary endothelium
- Transfer mechanisms: simple diffusion (O₂, CO₂), facilitated diffusion (glucose), active transport (amino acids, iron, calcium)

## Definition & Classification

### Components of the Placenta

| Component | Origin |
|-----------|--------|
| **Chorionic plate** (fetal surface) | Fetal — amnion + chorion |
| **Villous tree** | Fetal — trophoblast + fetal vessels |
| **Intervillous space** | Maternal blood |
| **Basal plate** (maternal surface) | Maternal — decidua basalis |
| **Cotyledons** | 15–20 lobes on maternal surface |

### Decidua Classification

| Type | Location |
|------|----------|
| **Decidua basalis** | Under the implantation site → forms maternal part of placenta |
| **Decidua capsularis** | Over the gestational sac |
| **Decidua parietalis (vera)** | Lines the rest of the uterus |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Placental Development

1. **Implantation** (Day 6–7): Trophoblast invades endometrium
2. **Primary villi** (Day 11–13): Cytotrophoblast core + syncytiotrophoblast cover
3. **Secondary villi** (Day 16): Mesenchymal core added
4. **Tertiary villi** (Day 21): Fetal blood vessels appear → functional placenta
5. **Trophoblast invasion**: Extravillous trophoblast invades spiral arteries → converts them from high-resistance to low-resistance vessels
6. Failure of spiral artery remodeling → preeclampsia, FGR

### Placental Barrier (Layers)

At term, the barrier thins to facilitate transfer:
1. Syncytiotrophoblast (continuous; no gap junctions)
2. Cytotrophoblast (discontinuous at term — Langhans cells disappear)
3. Trophoblast basement membrane
4. Fetal capillary endothelium

### Transfer Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Substances |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Simple diffusion** | O₂, CO₂, water, electrolytes, urea, drugs, anesthetic gases |
| **Facilitated diffusion** | Glucose (GLUT transporters) |
| **Active transport** | Amino acids, calcium, iron, phosphate, vitamins |
| **Pinocytosis** | IgG (bulk transport; only immunoglobulin to cross) |
| **Receptor-mediated** | Transferrin-bound iron, LDL cholesterol |
| **Breaks in barrier** | Fetal RBCs (small amount; basis of Rh sensitization) |

### Factors Affecting Transfer

- Maternal-fetal concentration gradient
- Molecular weight (< 500 Da crosses easily; > 1000 Da does not)
- Lipid solubility (lipophilic crosses better)
- Ionization (non-ionized crosses better)
- Protein binding (bound drugs cross poorly)
- Placental blood flow
- Surface area and thickness of barrier

## Clinical Features

### Placental Endocrine Function

| Hormone | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **hCG** | Maintains corpus luteum (first 7–9 weeks); basis of pregnancy tests; TSH-like activity |
| **hPL (human placental lactogen)** | Anti-insulin; lipolytic; mobilizes maternal glucose for fetus; mammogenic |
| **Progesterone** | Maintains pregnancy; immunosuppression; smooth muscle relaxation |
| **Estrogen** (estriol = E₃) | Uterine growth; blood flow; breast development; requires fetal DHEA-S (fetoplacental unit) |
| **CRH** | Increases exponentially; "placental clock" for labor timing |
| **Inhibin, activin** | Modulate hCG and GnRH |

### Fetoplacental Unit (Estrogen Synthesis)

1. Fetal adrenal → DHEA-S
2. Fetal liver → 16α-hydroxylation → 16-OH-DHEA-S
3. Placenta → aromatization → **Estriol (E₃)**
4. Placenta lacks 17α-hydroxylase → cannot make estrogen from cholesterol alone
5. Low estriol → may indicate fetal compromise or adrenal hypoplasia

### Immunological Function

- Syncytiotrophoblast lacks HLA class I and II → immune privilege
- Placenta produces IDO (indoleamine 2,3-dioxygenase) → depletes tryptophan → suppresses T cells
- Fas ligand expression → induces apoptosis of activated maternal T cells
- Allows semi-allogeneic fetus to survive in maternal environment

## Diagnosis

### Placental Assessment

| Method | What it Assesses |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Ultrasound** | Location, thickness, calcification, grading (Grannum) |
| **Doppler** | Uterine artery (notching → poor spiral artery remodeling), UA flow |
| **Serum markers** | PAPP-A, hCG, PlGF, sFlt-1 (preeclampsia biomarkers) |
| **Histopathology** | Post-delivery; infarcts, vasculopathy, chorioamnionitis |

### Placental Grading (Grannum)

| Grade | Chorionic Plate | Placental Body | Basal Plate | Gestational Age |
|-------|----------------|----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| 0 | Smooth | Homogeneous | No densities | < 28 weeks |
| I | Subtle undulations | Scattered echogenic areas | No densities | 28–32 weeks |
| II | Indentations | Comma-like echogenic areas | Linear densities | 32–36 weeks |
| III | Complete indentations reaching basal plate | Ring-shaped densities | Large, confluent | > 36 weeks |

- Grade III before 34 weeks → associated with FGR, preeclampsia

### Placental Weight Ratio

- Normal placental:fetal weight ratio at term = 1:6 (placenta ~500 g, baby ~3000 g)
- High ratio → Rh disease, maternal diabetes, syphilis
- Low ratio → FGR, postmaturity

## Management

### Placental Insufficiency

- Screen high-risk women with uterine artery Doppler at 20–24 weeks
- Bilateral notching + high PI → ↑ risk of preeclampsia/FGR
- Aspirin 150 mg from 12 weeks if high risk
- Serial growth scans and Doppler surveillance
- Delivery timing based on Doppler staging

### Retained Placenta

- If not delivered within 30 minutes of baby → retained
- Manual removal under anesthesia
- Risk of PPH and infection
- If morbidly adherent → suspect placenta accreta spectrum

### Placenta Accreta Spectrum

| Type | Depth of Invasion |
|------|------------------|
| **Accreta** | Attaches to myometrium (no decidua basalis) |
| **Increta** | Invades into myometrium |
| **Percreta** | Penetrates through myometrium (may invade bladder) |

- Risk factors: previous CS, placenta previa
- Management: planned cesarean hysterectomy

## Complications

- **Placental abruption**: Premature separation → hemorrhage, DIC, fetal death
- **Placenta previa**: Covers internal os → painless bleeding
- **Placental insufficiency**: FGR, oligohydramnios, stillbirth
- **Preeclampsia**: Failure of spiral artery remodeling
- **Placenta accreta spectrum**: Massive hemorrhage at delivery
- **Chorioamnionitis**: Infection of membranes and placenta
- **Gestational trophoblastic disease**: Abnormal trophoblast proliferation
