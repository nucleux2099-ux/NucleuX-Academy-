# Fetal Growth Assessment — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

SFH (cm) ≈ GA (weeks) from 20–36 weeks. Ultrasound biometry (BPD, HC, AC, FL) is gold standard. AC is the most sensitive parameter for FGR. SGA = EFW < 10th centile. FGR = SGA + pathological evidence. Asymmetric FGR (head-sparing) = placental insufficiency. Doppler staging guides delivery timing. Macrosomia → shoulder dystocia risk.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **AC** is the single most sensitive USG parameter for FGR detection
- ★ SGA: EFW < **10th centile**; severe SGA: < **3rd centile**
- ★ Asymmetric FGR: ↑ HC:AC ratio (**head-sparing**) — placental insufficiency
- ★ Symmetric FGR: early onset, chromosomal/TORCH — **worse prognosis**
- ★ UA AEDF → deliver by **34 weeks**; REDF → deliver by **32 weeks**
- ★ DV reversed a-wave → **immediate delivery** if viable
- ★ CPR < 1 → brain sparing / **centralization**
- ★ Aspirin **150 mg from 12 weeks** for FGR/preeclampsia prophylaxis
- ★ Macrosomia: shoulder dystocia risk; cesarean if EFW > **4500 g** (diabetic)
- ★ Barker hypothesis: FGR → adult **metabolic syndrome** and CVD
- ★ Hadlock formula uses **BPD + HC + AC + FL** for EFW (±10–15%)

## Mnemonics

### **"BHAF"** — USG Biometry Parameters
- **B**PD (head width)
- **H**C (head circumference)
- **A**C (abdominal circumference) — **most important for FGR**
- **F**L (femur length)

### **"AARD"** — Doppler Staging in FGR (worsening)
- **A**bnormal UA PI
- **A**bsent end-diastolic flow
- **R**eversed end-diastolic flow
- **D**V reversed a-wave (worst)

### **"3-2"** — Delivery Cutoffs
- AEDF → **3**4 weeks
- REDF → **3**2 weeks
