# Fetal Growth Assessment

## Overview

Fetal growth assessment is a cornerstone of antenatal care, enabling detection of growth abnormalities — both fetal growth restriction (FGR/IUGR) and macrosomia. Accurate assessment relies on clinical methods (symphysis-fundal height) and ultrasonographic biometry. Identifying abnormal growth patterns allows timely intervention to reduce perinatal morbidity and mortality.

## Key Points

- Symphysis-fundal height (SFH) is the primary clinical screening tool
- SFH in cm ≈ gestational age in weeks (20–36 weeks)
- Ultrasound biometry (BPD, HC, AC, FL) is the gold standard for growth assessment
- Abdominal circumference (AC) is the single most sensitive parameter for FGR
- Estimated fetal weight (EFW) < 10th centile → small for gestational age (SGA)
- SGA ≠ FGR; FGR implies pathological growth restriction
- Customized growth charts (GROW/INTERGROWTH-21) improve detection

## Definition & Classification

### Small for Gestational Age (SGA)

- EFW or AC < 10th centile for gestational age
- **Severe SGA**: < 3rd centile

### Fetal Growth Restriction (FGR)

- SGA + evidence of pathological restriction (abnormal Dopplers, oligohydramnios, growth deceleration)

| Type | Onset | Features | Etiology |
|------|-------|----------|----------|
| **Early-onset FGR** | < 32 weeks | Severe; asymmetric → symmetric; abnormal UA Doppler | Placental insufficiency, preeclampsia |
| **Late-onset FGR** | ≥ 32 weeks | Milder; often normal UA Doppler; abnormal CPR/MCA | Placental aging, mild insufficiency |

### Symmetric vs Asymmetric FGR

| Parameter | Symmetric | Asymmetric |
|-----------|-----------|------------|
| HC:AC ratio | Normal | Increased (head-sparing) |
| Onset | Early (< 20 weeks) | Late (> 24 weeks) |
| Cause | Chromosomal, TORCH, drugs | Placental insufficiency |
| Prognosis | Poorer | Better (if managed) |

### Large for Gestational Age (LGA)

- EFW > 90th centile
- **Macrosomia**: EFW > 4000 g (some define > 4500 g)

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Causes of FGR

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Maternal** | Hypertension, preeclampsia, chronic renal disease, malnutrition, smoking, substance abuse, antiphospholipid syndrome |
| **Placental** | Placental insufficiency, abruption, infarction, confined placental mosaicism |
| **Fetal** | Chromosomal anomalies (T13, T18, triploidy), TORCH infections, multiple pregnancy |

### Causes of Macrosomia

- Gestational/pregestational diabetes mellitus
- Maternal obesity
- Post-term pregnancy
- Multiparity
- Previous macrosomic infant
- Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome

## Clinical Features

### Clinical Assessment

- **Symphysis-fundal height (SFH)**: Measured from pubic symphysis to uterine fundus
  - 20–36 weeks: SFH (cm) ≈ gestational age (weeks) ± 2 cm
  - Sensitivity for SGA: ~50% (limited)
  - Serial measurements more useful than single
- **Maternal weight gain**: Poor gain may indicate FGR
- **Abdominal palpation**: Subjective; experienced clinicians may detect large/small fetuses

### Signs Suggesting FGR

- SFH < expected by ≥ 3 cm
- Reduced liquor volume (clinical)
- Non-engagement of head at term (may indicate oligohydramnios)

### Signs Suggesting Macrosomia

- SFH > expected by ≥ 3 cm
- Polyhydramnios
- Difficult palpation of fetal parts

## Diagnosis

### Ultrasound Biometry

| Parameter | Measures | Best Use |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| **BPD** (Biparietal diameter) | Head width | Dating (< 20 weeks) |
| **HC** (Head circumference) | Cranial size | Growth assessment |
| **AC** (Abdominal circumference) | Liver/soft tissue | **Most sensitive for FGR** |
| **FL** (Femur length) | Long bone | Growth + dating |

### Estimated Fetal Weight (EFW)

- Hadlock formula: Uses BPD, HC, AC, FL
- Accuracy: ±10–15% of actual birth weight
- Plot on growth chart (customized or population-based)

### Growth Velocity

- Serial scans ≥ 2 weeks apart
- Crossing centiles (downward) is concerning even if still > 10th centile
- RCOG recommends growth scans every 2–4 weeks in high-risk pregnancies

### Doppler Assessment in FGR

| Stage | Finding | Implication |
|-------|---------|------------|
| 1 | UA PI > 95th centile | Increased placental resistance |
| 2 | UA absent end-diastolic flow (AEDF) | Severe placental dysfunction |
| 3 | UA reversed end-diastolic flow (REDF) | Critical; high perinatal mortality |
| 4 | DV abnormal (reversed a-wave) | Cardiac decompensation; imminent stillbirth |
| — | MCA PI low (< 5th centile) | Brain-sparing / redistribution |
| — | CPR < 1 | Centralization of circulation |

### Amniotic Fluid Assessment

- **AFI < 5 cm** or **single deepest pocket < 2 cm** → oligohydramnios (associated with FGR)
- Polyhydramnios may accompany macrosomia (especially diabetic)

## Management

### FGR Management

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| SGA with normal Dopplers | Serial growth scans q2–4 weeks; deliver by 40 weeks |
| Abnormal UA Doppler (high PI) | Twice weekly CTG; deliver by 37 weeks |
| UA AEDF | Daily/alternate day CTG; deliver by 34 weeks (steroids if < 34 weeks) |
| UA REDF | Hospitalize; continuous monitoring; deliver by 32 weeks (steroids) |
| DV reversed a-wave | Immediate delivery if viable (≥ 26–28 weeks) |

### General Measures

- **Corticosteroids**: Betamethasone 12 mg × 2 doses (24 hours apart) if delivery < 34 weeks
- **MgSO₄ for neuroprotection**: If delivery < 32 weeks
- **Aspirin 150 mg**: Prophylactic from 12 weeks in high-risk for preeclampsia/FGR
- **Mode of delivery**: Vaginal preferred if tolerating labor; cesarean for non-reassuring fetal status

### Macrosomia Management

- Screen for GDM; optimize glycemic control
- Discuss risks: shoulder dystocia, birth trauma
- Consider elective cesarean if EFW > 4500 g (diabetic) or > 5000 g (non-diabetic)
- Active management of labor; prepare for shoulder dystocia

## Complications

### FGR Complications

- Stillbirth, neonatal death
- Preterm delivery (iatrogenic)
- Neonatal hypoglycemia, hypothermia, polycythemia
- Necrotizing enterocolitis
- Long-term: metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disease (Barker hypothesis)

### Macrosomia Complications

- Shoulder dystocia → Erb's palsy, clavicle fracture
- Birth asphyxia
- Postpartum hemorrhage
- Perineal tears / cesarean section
- Neonatal hypoglycemia
