# Lactation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Prolactin = milk PRODUCTION; oxytocin = milk EJECTION. Placental progesterone withdrawal triggers lactogenesis II (day 2–3). Colostrum (days 1–5): rich in IgA, low fat. Mature milk: high fat/lactose, whey:casein 60:40. Suckling is the key stimulus. LAM = 98% effective if exclusive BF + amenorrhea + <6 months. Early initiation within 1 hour. Domperidone is preferred galactogogue.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ **Prolactin** = milk production; **Oxytocin** = milk ejection (let-down)
- ★ Lactogenesis II triggered by **fall in progesterone** after placental delivery
- ★ Colostrum: high **IgA**, protein, lactoferrin; **low fat** and lactose
- ★ Mature milk: whey:casein = **60:40** (cow milk = 20:80)
- ★ Breast milk protein: **1 g/dL**; fat: 3.5–4.5 g/dL; lactose: 7 g/dL
- ★ LAM criteria: exclusive BF + amenorrhea + **< 6 months** → 98% effective
- ★ Suckling → ↓ dopamine → ↑ prolactin; suckling → ↑ oxytocin
- ★ Mastitis: **continue breastfeeding** + antibiotics (flucloxacillin)
- ★ Lactation suppression: **cabergoline** (dopamine agonist → ↓ prolactin)
- ★ **Domperidone** preferred galactogogue (D₂ antagonist → ↑ prolactin)
- ★ Early initiation: **within 1 hour** of birth (golden hour)

## Mnemonics

### **"PO"** — Hormones of Lactation
- **P**rolactin = **P**roduction
- **O**xytocin = **O**utput (ejection)

### **"CLAM"** — Colostrum Properties
- **C**ells (WBCs) rich
- **L**ow fat
- **A**ntibodies (IgA)
- **M**ore protein (than mature milk)

### **"LAM"** — Lactational Amenorrhea Method
- **L**actating exclusively
- **A**menorrheic
- **M**onths < 6

### **"DOME"** — Galactogogues
- **DO**mperidone (preferred)
- **ME**toclopramide (more side effects)
