# Lactation

## Overview

Lactation is the process of milk production and secretion by the mammary glands following delivery. It is under complex hormonal control involving prolactin, oxytocin, and the withdrawal of placental steroids. Breast milk is the ideal nutrition for the newborn, providing complete nutrition, immunological protection, and promoting maternal-infant bonding. Understanding lactation physiology is essential for managing breastfeeding problems and supporting successful lactation.

## Key Points

- Mammogenesis (breast development) occurs during pregnancy under estrogen, progesterone, prolactin, and hPL
- Lactogenesis is triggered by the fall in progesterone after placental delivery
- Prolactin stimulates milk PRODUCTION; oxytocin stimulates milk EJECTION (let-down reflex)
- Colostrum (first 3–5 days) is rich in IgA, proteins, and low in fat
- Mature milk has higher fat and lactose content
- Exclusive breastfeeding recommended for first 6 months (WHO)
- Suckling stimulus is the key driver: the more the baby suckles, the more milk is produced

## Definition & Classification

### Stages of Lactation

| Stage | Timing | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Mammogenesis** | Pregnancy | Breast development and alveolar proliferation |
| **Lactogenesis I** | Mid-pregnancy (16 weeks) | Colostrum production begins; held in check by progesterone |
| **Lactogenesis II** | 30–40 hours postpartum | Onset of copious milk secretion ("milk coming in") |
| **Galactopoiesis** | Day 9 onwards | Maintenance of established milk production |
| **Involution** | After weaning | Regression of mammary gland |

### Types of Breast Milk

| Type | Timing | Composition |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Colostrum** | Days 1–5 | Yellow; high protein, IgA, lactoferrin, WBCs; low fat and lactose |
| **Transitional milk** | Days 5–14 | Increasing fat and lactose |
| **Mature milk** | > 2 weeks | High fat, lactose; foremilk (watery) and hindmilk (fat-rich) |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Hormonal Control

| Hormone | Role |
|---------|------|
| **Prolactin** | Milk synthesis in alveolar cells; rises with suckling |
| **Oxytocin** | Milk ejection (let-down) via myoepithelial cell contraction |
| **Estrogen** | Ductal growth during pregnancy; inhibits lactation at high levels |
| **Progesterone** | Alveolar development during pregnancy; inhibits lactogenesis II |
| **hPL** | Mammogenic; synergizes with prolactin |
| **Cortisol, insulin, thyroid** | Permissive roles in milk production |

### The Suckling Reflex

1. Baby suckles → sensory impulses via intercostal nerves (T4–T6) → hypothalamus
2. **Anterior pituitary** → ↑ prolactin → milk production (takes hours)
3. **Posterior pituitary** → ↑ oxytocin → immediate milk ejection
4. Prolactin inhibited by dopamine; suckling suppresses dopamine → ↑ prolactin
5. Stress, anxiety → inhibit oxytocin → poor let-down

### Lactational Amenorrhea (LAM)

- Prolactin suppresses GnRH pulsatility → suppresses LH/FSH → anovulation
- Effective contraception (98%) if: exclusively breastfeeding + amenorrheic + < 6 months postpartum
- All three criteria must be met

## Clinical Features

### Composition of Breast Milk

| Component | Amount | Function |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Water | 87% | Hydration |
| Lactose | 7 g/dL | Primary carbohydrate; promotes Lactobacillus |
| Fat | 3.5–4.5 g/dL | Major energy source (50% of calories); DHA for brain |
| Protein | 1 g/dL | Whey:casein = 60:40 (vs 20:80 in cow milk) |
| IgA (secretory) | Present | Mucosal immunity; protects GI tract |
| Lactoferrin | Present | Iron-binding; antimicrobial |
| Lysozyme | Present | Bacteriolytic |
| Bifidus factor | Present | Promotes Lactobacillus bifidus |

### Benefits of Breastfeeding

| For Baby | For Mother |
|----------|------------|
| Optimal nutrition | Uterine involution (oxytocin) |
| Passive immunity (IgA) | Lactational amenorrhea (contraception) |
| Reduced infections (GI, respiratory) | Reduced breast and ovarian cancer risk |
| Reduced NEC in preterm | Faster postpartum weight loss |
| Better cognitive development | Bonding |
| Reduced SIDS risk | Reduced type 2 diabetes risk |

## Diagnosis

### Assessing Adequate Lactation

- ≥ 6–8 feeds per 24 hours in first weeks
- ≥ 6 wet diapers per day after day 4
- Adequate weight gain: regains birth weight by day 10–14
- Weight loss < 7–10% in first week is acceptable
- Audible swallowing during feeds
- Soft breasts after feeding

### Problems in Lactation

| Problem | Features | Management |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **Engorgement** | Painful, swollen, warm breasts (day 3–5) | Frequent feeding, warm compresses, express milk |
| **Cracked/sore nipples** | Pain during feeds; improper latch | Correct latch technique; lanolin cream |
| **Mastitis** | Fever, tender red area, flu-like symptoms | Antibiotics (flucloxacillin/cloxacillin); continue breastfeeding |
| **Breast abscess** | Fluctuant swelling; non-resolving mastitis | I&D; can continue feeding from other breast |
| **Insufficient milk** | Poor weight gain; few wet diapers | Frequent feeding; galactogogues; assess latch |
| **Inverted nipples** | Baby cannot latch | Nipple shields; Hoffman exercises |

## Management

### Supporting Successful Breastfeeding

- **Early initiation**: Within 1 hour of birth (golden hour)
- **Rooming-in**: Mother and baby together 24/7
- **Demand feeding**: Feed on cue, not on schedule
- **Proper latch**: Wide mouth, lower lip flanged out, areola in mouth
- **Baby-Friendly Hospital Initiative** (WHO/UNICEF): 10 steps to successful breastfeeding
- **Avoid**: Supplementary feeds, pacifiers, and bottles in early weeks

### Drugs and Lactation

| Safe | Avoid/Caution |
|------|---------------|
| Paracetamol, ibuprofen | Methotrexate, cyclophosphamide |
| Amoxicillin, cephalosporins | Lithium, carbamazepine (monitor) |
| Metformin | Radioactive iodine |
| Levothyroxine | Ergot alkaloids (suppress lactation) |
| Heparin/LMWH | Tetracycline (short course OK) |

### Galactogogues

- **Domperidone**: D₂ antagonist → ↑ prolactin; preferred over metoclopramide
- **Metoclopramide**: Similar mechanism; more CNS side effects
- **Herbal**: Fenugreek, shatavari (limited evidence)

### Suppression of Lactation

- Indications: Stillbirth, IUFD, maternal wish, HIV (in some settings), certain drugs
- **Cabergoline** (0.25 mg BD × 2 days): Dopamine agonist → suppresses prolactin
- Supportive: Tight bra, ice packs, avoid stimulation
- Bromocriptine: Older agent; more side effects; rarely used now

## Complications

- Breast engorgement → pain, difficulty latching
- Mastitis → breast abscess if untreated
- Inadequate lactation → neonatal dehydration, hypernatremia, jaundice
- Galactocele (milk-filled cyst)
- Lactation failure → maternal guilt, postpartum depression
- Drug passage to infant via breast milk
