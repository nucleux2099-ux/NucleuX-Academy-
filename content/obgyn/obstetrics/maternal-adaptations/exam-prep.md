# Maternal Adaptations in Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Blood volume ↑40–50%, CO ↑30–50%, SVR ↓, BP nadir at 24–28 weeks. Dilutional anemia (plasma > RBC expansion). GFR ↑50% → creatinine FALLS. Tidal volume ↑40% → respiratory alkalosis (PaCO₂ ~30). Hypercoagulable: ↑fibrinogen, ↓protein S. Progesterone → smooth muscle relaxation → GERD, constipation, hydroureter. hPL + cortisol → insulin resistance → diabetogenic.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Blood volume peaks at **32–34 weeks**; increases **40–50%**
- ★ Plasma volume ↑ > RBC mass ↑ → **dilutional/physiological anemia**
- ★ Cardiac output ↑ **30–50%** (peaks in 2nd trimester and labor)
- ★ BP nadir at **24–28 weeks**; SVR ↓ due to progesterone + NO
- ★ GFR ↑ **50%** → serum creatinine **falls** (0.4–0.8 mg/dL is normal)
- ★ PaCO₂ ≈ **30 mmHg** (compensated respiratory alkalosis)
- ★ Fibrinogen ↑ to **300–600 mg/dL**; D-dimer elevated (useless for DVT diagnosis)
- ★ **Protein S decreases**; pregnancy is a prothrombotic state
- ★ ESR markedly elevated — **not reliable** in pregnancy
- ★ WBC up to **15,000** normal; in labor up to **25,000**
- ★ hPL = **anti-insulin** / diabetogenic effect; ↑ in 2nd–3rd trimester

## Mnemonics

### **"CUPS DOWN"** — Things that DECREASE in pregnancy
- **C**reatinine
- **U**rea / BUN
- **P**aCO₂
- **S**VR (systemic vascular resistance)
- **D**iastolic BP (2nd trimester)
- **O**smolality
- **W**BC? No! (increases) — **W**ait, Protein S ↓
- **N**othing else — Albumin ↓

### **"HELP UP"** — Things that INCREASE
- **H**eart rate, **H**b needed (↑ iron demand)
- **E**SR, Estrogen
- **L**eukocytes
- **P**lasma volume, Progesterone
- **U**terine blood flow
- **P**rolactin, Platelets? No (slight ↓)

### **"Pro-G"** — Progesterone Effects
- **P**eristalsis ↓ (constipation)
- **R**espiratory stimulant (↑ TV)
- **O**esophageal sphincter relaxation (GERD)
- **G**allbladder stasis (stones)
