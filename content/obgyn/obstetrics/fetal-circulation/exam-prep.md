# Fetal Circulation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Oxygenated blood from placenta travels via umbilical vein → ductus venosus (bypasses liver) → IVC → RA → foramen ovale → LA → LV → ascending aorta → brain/heart. Deoxygenated blood: SVC → RA → RV → PA → ductus arteriosus → descending aorta → umbilical arteries → placenta. Three shunts close at birth due to ↑PaO₂, ↓PVR, cord clamping.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Umbilical vein (single) carries **oxygenated** blood; umbilical arteries (×2) carry **deoxygenated**
- ★ Most oxygenated blood goes to **brain and heart** via foramen ovale → LA → ascending aorta
- ★ Ductus arteriosus: maintained open by **PGE₂ and low O₂**; closed by **indomethacin**
- ★ PGE₁ (alprostadil) keeps DA open in **duct-dependent** CHD (TGA, critical PS, HLHS)
- ★ Foramen ovale: functional closure in **hours**, anatomical by **3–12 months**
- ★ DA: functional closure **10–15 hours**, anatomical **2–3 weeks**
- ★ HbF: **2α2γ**, higher O₂ affinity, left-shifted curve, doesn't bind 2,3-DPG well
- ★ UV → ligamentum teres; DV → ligamentum venosum; DA → ligamentum arteriosum; FO → fossa ovalis
- ★ Only **10%** of RV output goes to lungs in fetal life
- ★ CPR (MCA PI / UA PI) < 1 → **brain sparing** / fetal redistribution

## Mnemonics

### **"VV-AA"** — Umbilical Cord
- **V**ein = **1** = oxygenated (VV = Vein-Vida/life)
- **A**rteries = **2** = deoxygenated (AA = Arteries-Away from fetus)

### **"DFL"** — Three Shunts (Proximal to Distal)
- **D**uctus Venosus → bypasses liver
- **F**oramen Ovale → bypasses lungs (atrial level)
- **L** (Ductus Arteriosus = Ligamentum) → bypasses lungs (great vessel level)

### **"CLOSE"** — What closes DA
- **C**ord clamping (↑SVR)
- **L**ung expansion (↓PVR)
- **O**xygen increase (constricts DA)
- **S**top prostaglandins (indomethacin)
- **E**nd of placental circuit
