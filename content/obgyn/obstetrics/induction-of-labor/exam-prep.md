# Induction of Labor — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

IOL = artificial initiation of labor before spontaneous onset. Bishop score assesses cervical favorability: ≥6 = favorable (oxytocin), <6 = ripen first (prostaglandins/Foley). Methods: PGE₂ (dinoprostone), PGE₁ (misoprostol), Foley catheter, oxytocin, amniotomy. Prostaglandins contraindicated in scarred uterus. Tachysystole = >5 contractions/10 min.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Bishop score components: **Dilation, Effacement, Station, Consistency, Position** (DESCP)
- ★ Bishop ≥ **6** = favorable cervix; < 6 = needs ripening
- ★ **Misoprostol (PGE₁)**: Most potent; 25 µg vaginal q4–6h; **contraindicated** in previous CS
- ★ **Dinoprostone (PGE₂)**: Safer; intracervical 0.5 mg q6h or vaginal insert 10 mg
- ★ **Foley catheter**: Safe in **scarred uterus**; purely mechanical
- ★ Oxytocin: low-dose start **1–2 mU/min**; titrate to 3–5 contractions/10 min
- ★ Tachysystole: > **5 contractions/10 min** — stop oxytocin ± tocolysis
- ★ Most common indication: **post-term** pregnancy (≥ 41 weeks)
- ★ ARRIVE trial: elective IOL at **39 weeks** in nullipara → ↓ cesarean rate
- ★ Previous CS + IOL: use **Foley + low-dose oxytocin**; avoid prostaglandins
- ★ Water intoxication: complication of high-dose oxytocin (ADH-like effect)

## Mnemonics

### **"DESCP"** — Bishop Score Parameters
- **D**ilation
- **E**ffacement
- **S**tation
- **C**onsistency
- **P**osition

### **"FOM-PA"** — IOL Methods
- **F**oley catheter (mechanical)
- **O**xytocin
- **M**isoprostol (PGE₁)
- **P**rostaglandin E₂ (dinoprostone)
- **A**mniotomy

### **"6 = GO"** — Bishop Score
- Score ≥ **6** = **GO** ahead with oxytocin
- Score < 6 = **SLOW** down, ripen first
