# Induction of Labor

## Overview

Induction of labor (IOL) is the artificial initiation of uterine contractions before spontaneous onset to achieve vaginal delivery. It is one of the most common obstetric interventions, performed when continuing pregnancy poses greater risk to mother or fetus than delivery. The success of induction depends heavily on cervical favorability, assessed by the Bishop score.

## Key Points

- IOL is indicated when benefits of delivery outweigh risks of continuing pregnancy
- Bishop score ≥ 6 → favorable cervix → direct oxytocin induction
- Bishop score < 6 → cervical ripening needed first (prostaglandins/mechanical)
- Methods: prostaglandins (PGE₂, misoprostol), mechanical (Foley catheter), oxytocin, amniotomy
- Failed induction: failure to achieve active labor despite adequate ripening and oxytocin
- Major risk: uterine tachysystole/rupture (especially with prostaglandins in scarred uterus)

## Definition & Classification

### Bishop Score

| Parameter | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|-----------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Dilation** (cm) | 0 | 1–2 | 3–4 | ≥ 5 |
| **Effacement** (%) | 0–30 | 40–50 | 60–70 | ≥ 80 |
| **Station** | –3 | –2 | –1, 0 | +1, +2 |
| **Consistency** | Firm | Medium | Soft | — |
| **Position** | Posterior | Mid | Anterior | — |

- **Score ≥ 6**: Favorable → high success rate with oxytocin
- **Score < 6**: Unfavorable → cervical ripening required

### Types of Induction

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Elective** | Without medical indication (controversial; ≥ 39 weeks — ARRIVE trial) |
| **Indicated** | For maternal or fetal indication |
| **Cervical ripening** | Pre-induction preparation of unfavorable cervix |
| **Augmentation** | Stimulation of already-established but inadequate labor (not induction) |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Indications for IOL

| Maternal | Fetal | Other |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Preeclampsia/eclampsia | FGR with abnormal Dopplers | Post-term (≥ 41–42 weeks) |
| Gestational hypertension | Oligohydramnios | PPROM at term |
| Diabetes (GDM/pregestational) | Non-reassuring fetal status | Chorioamnionitis |
| Cholestasis of pregnancy | Fetal demise (stillbirth) | Logistic (remote area) |
| Chronic hypertension | Rh isoimmunization | ARRIVE trial (≥ 39 weeks nullipara) |

### Contraindications

| Absolute | Relative |
|----------|----------|
| Placenta previa / vasa previa | Previous uterine surgery (not lower segment) |
| Transverse lie | Grand multiparity |
| Cord prolapse | Breech presentation |
| Active genital herpes | Overdistended uterus |
| Previous classical CS | Multiple pregnancy |

## Clinical Features

### Cervical Ripening Methods

#### Pharmacological

| Agent | Route | Dose | Notes |
|-------|-------|------|-------|
| **Dinoprostone (PGE₂)** | Intracervical gel | 0.5 mg; repeat q6h (max 1.5 mg) | Standard; removable (Cervidil vaginal insert 10 mg) |
| **Misoprostol (PGE₁)** | Vaginal | 25 µg q4–6h | Potent; lower dose preferred |
| **Misoprostol** | Oral | 25–50 µg q4h | Increasing evidence; WHO-recommended |
| **Mifepristone** | Oral | 200 mg | Used for IUFD; not routine live fetus |

#### Mechanical

| Method | Mechanism | Advantage |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Foley catheter** (transcervical) | Mechanical dilation + local prostaglandin release | Safe in scarred uterus; cheap; removable |
| **Double-balloon catheter** (Cook) | Cervical and extra-amniotic balloon | Theoretically more effective |
| **Hygroscopic dilators** (laminaria) | Gradual cervical dilation | Rarely used for IOL now |
| **Membrane sweeping/stripping** | Separates membranes from lower segment | Outpatient; reduces need for formal IOL |

### Oxytocin Infusion

- Started after cervical ripening or if Bishop ≥ 6
- Low-dose protocol: Start 1–2 mU/min; increase by 1–2 mU/min every 30 min
- High-dose protocol: Start 4–6 mU/min; increase by 4–6 mU/min every 15–30 min
- Titrate to achieve 3–5 contractions per 10 minutes
- Maximum dose: typically 20–40 mU/min (varies by protocol)

### Amniotomy (ARM)

- Artificial rupture of membranes
- Often combined with oxytocin (complementary)
- Requirements: vertex presentation, engaged head, no vasa previa
- Risk: cord prolapse (especially if head not engaged)

## Diagnosis

### Monitoring During Induction

- Continuous CTG monitoring (especially with oxytocin/prostaglandins)
- Uterine activity: aim for 3–5 contractions in 10 minutes
- **Tachysystole**: > 5 contractions in 10 minutes (averaged over 30 min)
  - Without FHR changes: reduce/stop oxytocin
  - With FHR changes: stop oxytocin + tocolysis (terbutaline 250 µg SC)

### Failed Induction — Definition

- No standard universal definition
- Generally: failure to achieve active labor (cervical dilation ≥ 4 cm with regular contractions) after adequate oxytocin and amniotomy for ≥ 12–18 hours
- Not the same as failed trial of labor or arrest of labor

## Management

### IOL Protocol (Typical)

1. Assess Bishop score
2. If < 6 → cervical ripening (prostaglandin or Foley catheter; 12–24 hours)
3. Reassess → amniotomy when possible
4. Start oxytocin infusion
5. Continuous CTG
6. Aim for progressive cervical dilation
7. If failed → cesarean section

### Special Situations

| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| **Previous 1 LSCS** | Mechanical ripening (Foley catheter); low-dose oxytocin; AVOID prostaglandins (↑ rupture risk) |
| **PPROM at term** | Immediate induction (oxytocin) OR expectant up to 24 hours; antibiotics |
| **IUFD** | Mifepristone + misoprostol (high-dose); can use mechanical methods |
| **Post-term** | IOL at 41+0 to 42+0 weeks reduces stillbirth risk |
| **GDM** | IOL at 38–40 weeks depending on control and complications |

## Complications

| Complication | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Uterine tachysystole** | > 5 contractions/10 min; may cause fetal distress |
| **Uterine rupture** | Risk with prostaglandins in scarred uterus; catastrophic |
| **Cord prolapse** | After amniotomy with unengaged head |
| **Failed induction** | → Cesarean section |
| **Chorioamnionitis** | Prolonged induction, prolonged ROM |
| **Postpartum hemorrhage** | Uterine atony from prolonged oxytocin |
| **Water intoxication** | High-dose oxytocin (antidiuretic effect) |
| **Amniotic fluid embolism** | Rare; associated with augmentation |
| **Neonatal** | Hyperbilirubinemia, NICU admission |
