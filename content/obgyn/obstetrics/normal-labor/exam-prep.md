# Normal Labor — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Normal labor = spontaneous onset, 37–42 weeks, cephalic, vaginal delivery. 3 Ps: Powers, Passage, Passenger. 1st stage: latent (0–4 cm) + active (4–10 cm, ≥1 cm/hr). 2nd stage: expulsion (<2 hr primi). 3rd stage: placenta (AMTSL — oxytocin 10 IU IM). 7 cardinal movements: engagement → descent → flexion → internal rotation → extension → restitution → expulsion. Partograph: alert line + action line (4 hours apart).

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Active phase starts at **4 cm** dilation (not 3 cm — updated)
- ★ Cervical dilation rate: **≥ 1 cm/hr** (primi); **≥ 1.5 cm/hr** (multi)
- ★ Partograph action line: **4 hours** right of alert line
- ★ Well-flexed vertex presents **suboccipitobregmatic** diameter = **9.5 cm**
- ★ Brow presentation: **mentovertical 13.5 cm** → CANNOT deliver vaginally
- ★ Internal rotation: occiput rotates to **anterior** (OT → OA)
- ★ AMTSL: **oxytocin 10 IU IM** immediately after delivery of baby
- ★ Delayed cord clamping: **≥ 1 minute** (WHO recommendation)
- ★ **Gynecoid** pelvis = best for vaginal delivery; **platypelloid** = worst
- ★ Fundal dominance: contractions start and are strongest at **fundus**
- ★ Effacement precedes dilation in **primigravida**

## Mnemonics

### **"ED-FIRE"** — 7 Cardinal Movements (simplified)
- **E**ngagement
- **D**escent
- **F**lexion
- **I**nternal rotation
- **R**estitution (+ extension)
- **E**xpulsion

### **"3 Ps"** — Factors of Labor
- **P**owers (contractions)
- **P**assage (pelvis)
- **P**assenger (fetus)

### **"AMTSL"** — Active Management of Third Stage
- **A**dminister uterotonic (oxytocin)
- **M**assage uterus
- **T**raction (controlled cord)
- **S**eparation signs
- **L**ate clamping

### **"GAAP"** — Pelvic Types (Best → Worst)
- **G**ynecoid (round — best)
- **A**nthropoid (oval AP)
- **A**ndroid (heart-shaped)
- **P**latypelloid (flat — worst)
