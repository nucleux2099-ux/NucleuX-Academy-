# Normal Labor

## Overview

Normal labor (eutocia) is the spontaneous onset and progression of labor at term (37–42 weeks) resulting in vaginal delivery of a singleton fetus in cephalic presentation without undue complications. Understanding the stages of labor, the mechanisms of labor, and the partograph is fundamental to obstetric practice. Normal labor involves the interplay of the "3 Ps" — Powers, Passage, and Passenger.

## Key Points

- Normal labor: spontaneous onset at 37–42 weeks, cephalic presentation, vaginal delivery
- Three stages: 1st (cervical dilation), 2nd (expulsion), 3rd (placental delivery)
- 1st stage: latent phase (0–4 cm) + active phase (4–10 cm)
- Active phase cervical dilation: ≥ 1 cm/hour (primigravida); ≥ 1.5 cm/hour (multigravida)
- Partograph (WHO) is the key tool for monitoring labor progress
- 7 cardinal movements of labor: engagement, descent, flexion, internal rotation, extension, external rotation (restitution), expulsion

## Definition & Classification

### Stages of Labor

| Stage | Phase | Duration (Primigravida) | Duration (Multigravida) |
|-------|-------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **1st** — Cervical dilation | Latent (0→4 cm) | < 20 hours | < 14 hours |
| | Active (4→10 cm) | ~6 hours (≥1 cm/hr) | ~3 hours (≥1.5 cm/hr) |
| **2nd** — Expulsion | Delivery of baby | < 2 hours (< 3 with epidural) | < 1 hour (< 2 with epidural) |
| **3rd** — Placental | Delivery of placenta | 5–15 min (up to 30 min) | 5–15 min |
| **4th** — Observation | First 1–2 hours postpartum | Monitoring for hemorrhage | — |

### The "3 Ps" of Labor

| Factor | Components |
|--------|-----------|
| **Powers** | Uterine contractions + maternal expulsive effort (bearing down) |
| **Passage** | Bony pelvis (inlet, cavity, outlet) + soft tissues (cervix, vagina, pelvic floor) |
| **Passenger** | Fetus (size, lie, presentation, position, attitude) + placenta |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Onset of Labor — Theories

- **Progesterone withdrawal** (functional, not absolute in humans)
- **Oxytocin receptor upregulation** in myometrium near term
- **Fetal HPA axis activation** → ↑ fetal cortisol → ↑ placental CRH → prostaglandins
- **Prostaglandins** (PGE₂, PGF₂α): cervical ripening + myometrial contractions
- **Mechanical stretch**: ↑ gap junctions, oxytocin receptors
- **Inflammatory pathway**: IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α in membranes and decidua

### Uterine Contractions in Labor

- Normal active labor: 3–5 contractions per 10 minutes
- Each contraction: 40–60 seconds duration, 40–60 mmHg pressure
- Fundal dominance: contractions begin at fundus, strongest at fundus
- Retraction: muscle fibers do not return to original length → progressive cervical dilation and fetal descent

### Cervical Changes

- **Effacement**: Thinning and shortening of cervix (measured in %)
- **Dilation**: Opening of cervical os (0–10 cm = full dilation)
- Effacement precedes dilation in primigravida; occurs simultaneously in multigravida

## Clinical Features

### 7 Cardinal Movements of Labor

| Movement | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 1. **Engagement** | Biparietal diameter passes pelvic inlet; head at 0 station |
| 2. **Descent** | Progressive downward movement; continues throughout |
| 3. **Flexion** | Chin touches chest; smallest diameter (suboccipitobregmatic 9.5 cm) presented |
| 4. **Internal rotation** | Occiput rotates from transverse to anterior (OT → OA); 45° in LOT |
| 5. **Extension** | Head extends under symphysis pubis; face sweeps over perineum |
| 6. **External rotation (Restitution)** | Head realigns with shoulders; then shoulders rotate to AP |
| 7. **Expulsion** | Anterior shoulder under symphysis, then posterior shoulder; body follows |

### Fetal Skull Diameters

| Diameter | Presenting in | Measurement |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Suboccipitobregmatic** | Well-flexed vertex (normal) | 9.5 cm |
| **Suboccipitofrontal** | Incompletely flexed | 10 cm |
| **Occipitofrontal** | Deflexed / military | 11.5 cm |
| **Mentovertical** | Brow | 13.5 cm (cannot deliver vaginally) |
| **Submentobregmatic** | Face (mentoanterior) | 9.5 cm |

### Assessment During Labor

- **Abdominal examination**: Contractions (frequency, duration, strength); fifths palpable above brim
- **Vaginal examination**: Cervical dilation, effacement, station, position, membranes, presenting part
- **Station**: Relationship of presenting part to ischial spines (–3 to +3)

## Diagnosis

### Diagnosis of Labor

- Regular painful uterine contractions + progressive cervical dilation + show (bloodstained mucus)
- False labor: irregular contractions, no cervical change, no show

### Partograph (WHO Modified)

- Graphical record of labor progress
- Cervical dilation plotted against time
- **Alert line**: Expected progress at 1 cm/hour
- **Action line**: 4 hours to the right of alert line
- Crossing action line → intervention needed (augmentation, CS)
- Also records: FHR, contractions, descent, maternal vitals, urine output

### Assessment of Pelvic Adequacy

| Pelvic Type | Shape | Prognosis |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| **Gynecoid** | Round inlet | Best for vaginal delivery (50%) |
| **Android** | Heart-shaped/triangular | ↑ Risk of arrest |
| **Anthropoid** | Oval (AP > transverse) | Usually delivers in OP |
| **Platypelloid** | Flat (transverse > AP) | Worst; arrest at inlet |

## Management

### First Stage

- Supportive care: mobility, hydration, emotional support
- Continuous fetal monitoring (high risk) or intermittent auscultation (low risk)
- Vaginal examination every 4 hours
- Analgesia: non-pharmacological (positions, water, massage); pharmacological (epidural, pethidine)
- Amniotomy if slow progress (after 4 cm)

### Second Stage

- Active pushing with contractions once fully dilated and urge to push
- Controlled delivery of head (Ritgen maneuver)
- Check for nuchal cord
- Deliver shoulders (anterior → posterior)
- Delayed cord clamping (≥ 1 minute; WHO)

### Third Stage — Active Management (AMTSL)

- **A**dministration of uterotonic: Oxytocin 10 IU IM immediately after delivery
- **M**assage of uterus
- **T**raction: Controlled cord traction (Brandt-Andrews method)
- **S**eparation signs: Gush of blood, cord lengthening, uterus becomes globular
- **L**ate clamping (≥ 1 min)

### Fourth Stage

- Monitor every 15 minutes for 1–2 hours
- Assess: vitals, uterine tone, bleeding, bladder
- Initiate breastfeeding (golden hour)

## Complications

- **Prolonged labor**: Latent phase > 20 hours (primi) or > 14 hours (multi)
- **Arrest disorders**: No progress in active phase for ≥ 4 hours (adequate contractions)
- **Perineal tears**: 1st degree (mucosa), 2nd (perineal muscles), 3rd (anal sphincter), 4th (rectal mucosa)
- **Cord prolapse**: Emergency CS
- **Shoulder dystocia**: Turtle sign → McRoberts + suprapubic pressure
- **PPH**: Atonic uterus most common after prolonged/augmented labor
- **Birth asphyxia**: From prolonged 2nd stage or cord complications
