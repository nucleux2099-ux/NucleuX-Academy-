# Embryology Overview

## Overview

Embryology is the study of development from fertilization to the end of the 8th week (embryonic period), after which the developing organism is termed a fetus. Understanding embryology is critical for comprehending congenital anomalies, teratogenesis, and the basis of prenatal screening. Key events include fertilization, cleavage, implantation, gastrulation, neurulation, and organogenesis.

## Key Points

- Fertilization occurs in the ampulla of the fallopian tube
- Implantation occurs on day 6–7 post-fertilization (blastocyst stage)
- Gastrulation (week 3) establishes three germ layers: ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm
- Organogenesis (weeks 3–8) is the most vulnerable period for teratogenesis
- Neural tube closure: day 21–28; failure → NTDs (anencephaly, spina bifida)
- The critical period for most major organ malformations is weeks 3–8
- Folic acid supplementation before conception reduces NTD risk by 70%

## Definition & Classification

### Developmental Timeline

| Period | Duration | Key Events |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Pre-embryonic** | Weeks 1–2 | Fertilization, cleavage, implantation, bilaminar disc |
| **Embryonic** | Weeks 3–8 | Gastrulation, neurulation, organogenesis |
| **Fetal** | Weeks 9–40 | Growth, maturation, functional development |

### Germ Layer Derivatives

| Germ Layer | Derivatives |
|------------|-------------|
| **Ectoderm** | Skin, CNS, PNS, lens, enamel, anterior pituitary, adrenal medulla |
| **Mesoderm** | Muscle, bone, cartilage, blood, heart, kidneys, gonads, adrenal cortex |
| **Endoderm** | GI epithelium, liver, pancreas, thyroid, parathyroid, lungs, bladder |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Week 1: Fertilization to Implantation

1. **Fertilization** (Day 0): Sperm + oocyte in ampulla → zygote (46 chromosomes)
2. **Cleavage** (Days 1–3): Mitotic divisions → morula (16-cell stage)
3. **Blastocyst formation** (Days 4–5): Inner cell mass (embryoblast) + outer trophoblast
4. **Implantation** (Days 6–7): Blastocyst embeds in posterior-superior endometrium
   - Trophoblast differentiates into syncytiotrophoblast and cytotrophoblast

### Week 2: Bilaminar Disc ("Week of Twos")

- Two layers: epiblast + hypoblast
- Two cavities: amniotic cavity + yolk sac
- Two trophoblast layers: cyto- + syncytiotrophoblast
- Extraembryonic mesoderm forms

### Week 3: Gastrulation ("Week of Threes")

- Primitive streak appears → three germ layers established
- Notochord forms → induces neural plate
- Neurulation begins: neural plate → neural folds → neural tube
- Mesoderm differentiates: paraxial (somites), intermediate, lateral plate

### Weeks 4–8: Organogenesis

| Week | Major Events |
|------|-------------|
| 4 | Heart begins beating; limb buds appear; neural tube closes (day 28) |
| 5 | Upper limb paddle-shaped; lens placodes |
| 6 | Digital rays; external ear forming |
| 7 | Fingers distinct; eyelids forming |
| 8 | All major organs formed; human-like appearance; CRL ~30 mm |

### Placental Development

- Chorionic villi develop from trophoblast
- Primary → secondary → tertiary villi (with fetal blood vessels by week 4)
- Chorion frondosum + decidua basalis → definitive placenta
- Chorion laeve (smooth chorion) forms the chorionic membrane

## Clinical Features

### Teratogenesis — Critical Periods

| Organ System | Critical Period | Common Teratogens |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| CNS / Neural tube | Weeks 3–4 | Valproate, folate deficiency, isotretinoin |
| Heart | Weeks 3–6 | Lithium, alcohol, rubella |
| Limbs | Weeks 4–8 | Thalidomide |
| Eyes | Weeks 4–8 | Rubella, isotretinoin |
| External genitalia | Weeks 8–12 | Androgens, DES |

### All-or-None Period

- Weeks 1–2: Insult either kills the embryo or causes no malformation
- No organ-specific teratogenesis before gastrulation

## Diagnosis

### Prenatal Assessment of Embryological Abnormalities

- **First trimester ultrasound**: NT measurement (11–14 weeks) screens for chromosomal anomalies
- **NIPT/cfDNA**: From 10 weeks; screens for trisomies 13, 18, 21
- **CVS**: 10–13 weeks; samples chorionic villi for karyotype
- **Amniocentesis**: 15–20 weeks; amniotic fluid analysis
- **Anomaly scan**: 18–22 weeks; detailed structural survey

### Key Markers

- Increased NT → Trisomy 21, Turner syndrome, cardiac defects
- Absent nasal bone → Trisomy 21
- Neural tube defects → elevated AFP (maternal serum + amniotic fluid)

## Management

### Preconception

- Folic acid 400 µg daily (4 mg if previous NTD)
- Avoid known teratogens (Category X drugs)
- Rubella vaccination if non-immune (before conception)
- Optimize diabetes control (HbA1c < 6.5%)

### Prenatal Screening Strategy

1. First trimester combined screen (NT + PAPP-A + free β-hCG): 11–14 weeks
2. NIPT if high risk or maternal age ≥ 35
3. Second trimester quadruple test if first trimester screen not done
4. Anomaly scan at 18–22 weeks for all pregnancies

### When Anomaly Detected

- Multidisciplinary counseling (MFM, genetics, neonatology)
- Offer confirmatory invasive testing (CVS/amniocentesis)
- Discuss prognosis, management options including termination if legally permissible
- Plan delivery at tertiary center if continuing pregnancy

## Complications

- **Neural tube defects**: Anencephaly, spina bifida, encephalocele
- **Cardiac defects**: Most common congenital anomalies (8/1000 live births)
- **Chromosomal anomalies**: Trisomy 21, 18, 13; Turner syndrome; Klinefelter syndrome
- **TORCH infections**: Cause embryopathy — rubella, CMV, toxoplasma
- **Diabetic embryopathy**: Caudal regression, cardiac defects, NTDs
- **Twinning abnormalities**: Conjoined twins (incomplete division after day 13)
