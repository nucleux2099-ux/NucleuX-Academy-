# Embryology Overview — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Fertilization occurs in the ampulla. Implantation at day 6–7 (blastocyst). Week 2 = bilaminar disc ("week of twos"). Week 3 = gastrulation → 3 germ layers ("week of threes"). Organogenesis weeks 3–8 is the critical teratogenic period. Neural tube closes by day 28. Folic acid 400 µg preconception prevents NTDs.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Fertilization site: **ampulla** of fallopian tube
- ★ Implantation: **Day 6–7**, posterior-superior uterine wall
- ★ Week 1–2: **All-or-none** period (no organ-specific teratogenesis)
- ★ Gastrulation: **Week 3** — establishes ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm
- ★ Neural tube closure: **Day 21–28**; failure → NTDs
- ★ Organogenesis: **Weeks 3–8** — most vulnerable to teratogens
- ★ Heart starts beating: **Week 4**
- ★ Ectoderm → CNS, skin; Mesoderm → muscle, bone, kidneys; Endoderm → GI, liver, lungs
- ★ Trophoblast → syncytio (produces hCG) + cytotrophoblast
- ★ Folic acid: **400 µg** (4 mg if prior NTD)
- ★ Conjoined twins: division after **day 13**

## Mnemonics

### **"Week of 2s, Week of 3s"**
- Week **2**: **2** layers (epiblast/hypoblast), **2** cavities, **2** trophoblast layers
- Week **3**: **3** germ layers via gastrulation

### **"ECTO-MESO-ENDO" — Surface to Deep**
- **Ecto**: Everything you can see/feel — Skin, Nerves, Eyes, Teeth enamel
- **Meso**: In the Middle — Muscle, Bone, Blood, Kidneys, Heart
- **Endo**: Deep Inside — GI lining, Liver, Pancreas, Lungs, Thyroid

### **"A-S-L"** — Teratogen critical periods
- **A**lcohol → Heart (weeks 3–6) + CNS (throughout)
- **S**odium valproate → Neural tube (weeks 3–4)
- **L**ithium → Heart (Ebstein's anomaly)

## Common MCQ Topics

1. Site of fertilization (ampulla vs isthmus vs fimbriae)
2. Germ layer derivatives — which organ from which layer
3. All-or-none period — what it means, when it occurs
4. Neural tube closure timing and NTD types
5. Teratogen–defect associations (thalidomide, valproate, lithium, isotretinoin)
6. Week 2 vs Week 3 — bilaminar vs trilaminar disc
7. Implantation timing and site
8. Trophoblast differentiation — which layer produces hCG
9. First trimester screening components (NT + PAPP-A + β-hCG)
10. Folic acid dose: normal vs high-risk for NTD
