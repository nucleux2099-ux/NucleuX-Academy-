# Operative Vaginal Delivery — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

OVD = forceps or vacuum to assist vaginal delivery in 2nd stage. Prerequisites: full dilation, engaged head, known position, empty bladder, adequate analgesia. Classification: outlet/low/mid (high abandoned). Forceps: more perineal tears, facial nerve palsy. Vacuum: more cephalohematoma, subgaleal hemorrhage. Kielland's for rotation. Failed OVD → cesarean.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Prerequisites: cervix **fully dilated**, head **engaged**, position **known**, bladder **empty**
- ★ **Kielland's forceps**: rotation forceps; minimal pelvic curve; sliding lock
- ★ **Wrigley's forceps**: outlet/lift-out; short; also used at CS
- ★ **Piper's forceps**: aftercoming head of **breech**
- ★ Vacuum cup placed over **flexion point** (3 cm anterior to posterior fontanelle)
- ★ Cephalohematoma: **subperiosteal**; does NOT cross suture lines; vacuum > forceps
- ★ Subgaleal hemorrhage: **subaponeurotic**; crosses sutures; **DANGEROUS** (can be fatal)
- ★ Facial nerve palsy → **forceps** (blade over mastoid)
- ★ Vacuum contraindicated at < **34 weeks** and in fetal bleeding disorders
- ★ Sequential instruments (vacuum → forceps) → **↑ neonatal morbidity** → discouraged
- ★ Prolonged 2nd stage: nullipara > **2 hr** (3 with epidural); multipara > **1 hr** (2 with epidural)

## Mnemonics

### **"ABCDEFGH"** — Prerequisites for OVD
- **A**nalgesia
- **B**ladder empty
- **C**ervix fully dilated
- **D**escent (engaged)
- **E**xact position known
- **F**orceps/vacuum ready
- **G**entle traction; ready for failure
- **H**ead: membranes ruptured, ≤ 1/5 palpable

### **"3-3-3"** — Vacuum Abandonment Rule
- **3** pull-offs
- **3** pulls without progress
- **30** minutes maximum

### **"CeSub"** — Vacuum Scalp Injuries
- **Ce**phalohematoma = sub**periosteal** (limited by sutures)
- **Sub**galeal = sub**aponeurotic** (crosses sutures — dangerous!)
