# Operative Vaginal Delivery

## Overview

Operative vaginal delivery (OVD) refers to the use of instruments — forceps or vacuum extractor — to assist vaginal delivery. It is performed when the second stage is prolonged or when there is an indication for expediting delivery while the prerequisites for vaginal delivery are met. Proper classification, technique, and knowledge of prerequisites are essential to minimize maternal and neonatal morbidity.

## Key Points

- Two instruments: forceps and vacuum extractor (ventouse)
- Prerequisites must be fulfilled before any OVD attempt
- Classification by station: outlet, low, mid-cavity (high-cavity abandoned)
- Most common indication: prolonged second stage
- Forceps can rotate; vacuum should not be used for rotation
- Failed OVD → cesarean section; sequential instruments are discouraged
- Neonatal risks: cephalohematoma (vacuum), facial nerve palsy (forceps)

## Definition & Classification

### Classification by Station (ACOG)

| Type | Station | Rotation |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Outlet** | Scalp visible at introitus; head on pelvic floor | ≤ 45° |
| **Low** | Station ≥ +2 (not outlet) | ≤ 45° or > 45° |
| **Mid-cavity** | Station 0 to +2; head engaged | Any rotation |
| **High** | Above 0 station | **ABANDONED** — not performed |

### Types of Forceps

| Forceps | Use |
|---------|-----|
| **Wrigley's** | Outlet/lift-out; low-cavity | Short; used at CS for head delivery too |
| **Neville-Barnes / Simpson** | Low and mid-cavity | Standard traction forceps |
| **Kielland's** | Rotation + traction | For OT/OP positions; sliding lock; minimal pelvic curve |
| **Piper's** | Aftercoming head of breech | Specialized |

### Vacuum Extractor Types

| Type | Cup |
|------|-----|
| **Malmström** | Metal cup (original) |
| **Silastic/soft cup** | Flexible; less scalp trauma; lower success for malposition |
| **Kiwi OmniCup** | Handheld; can be used for OP positions |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Indications for OVD

| Maternal | Fetal |
|----------|-------|
| Prolonged second stage | Non-reassuring fetal status (not requiring immediate CS) |
| Maternal exhaustion | — |
| Medical conditions contraindicating Valsalva (cardiac, cerebrovascular) | — |
| Epidural — reduced bearing down | — |

### Prolonged Second Stage Definition

| Parity | Without Epidural | With Epidural |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Nullipara | > 2 hours | > 3 hours |
| Multipara | > 1 hour | > 2 hours |

### Contraindications

- Cervix not fully dilated
- Fetal head not engaged
- Unknown position
- Suspected CPD
- Prematurity < 34 weeks (vacuum); forceps may be used with caution
- Fetal bleeding disorders (vacuum contraindicated)

## Clinical Features

### Prerequisites for OVD — "ABCDEFGH"

| Letter | Prerequisite |
|--------|-------------|
| **A** | Analgesia adequate |
| **B** | Bladder empty (catheterized) |
| **C** | Cervix fully dilated |
| **D** | Descent adequate (head engaged, station ≥ 0) |
| **E** | Exact position of head known (confirmed by palpating sutures/fontanelles) |
| **F** | Forceps/vacuum ready; operator experienced |
| **G** | Gentle traction; prepared for failure |
| **H** | Head — membranes ruptured; no more than 1/5 palpable abdominally |

### Technique — Forceps

1. Identify position (confirm OA or OL/OR)
2. Assemble and check forceps
3. Apply left blade first (into right side of pelvis) then right blade
4. Lock blades; check application (sagittal suture equidistant, posterior fontanelle 1 cm above plane)
5. Apply traction with contractions — downward initially (Pajot's maneuver), then upward
6. Episiotomy if needed
7. Remove blades in reverse order after head delivery

### Technique — Vacuum

1. Apply cup over flexion point (3 cm anterior to posterior fontanelle along sagittal suture)
2. Ensure no maternal tissue trapped
3. Build negative pressure to 0.6–0.8 kg/cm² (stepwise or rapid)
4. Apply traction with contractions perpendicular to cup
5. **3-3-3 Rule**: Abandon if — 3 pull-offs, or no progress in 3 pulls, or > 30 minutes total (varies by guideline)

## Diagnosis

### Assessment Before OVD

- Abdominal palpation: ≤ 1/5 head palpable above brim
- Vaginal examination: full dilation, station, position, caput/moulding
- Confirm adequate pelvis for vaginal delivery
- Consent obtained; plan for failure (CS backup)

### Grading of Difficulty

| Grade | Description | Risk |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Easy | Outlet, OA, minimal traction | Low |
| Moderate | Low cavity, minor rotation | Moderate |
| Difficult | Mid-cavity, rotation needed | High; senior operator only |

## Management

### Forceps vs Vacuum — Comparison

| Feature | Forceps | Vacuum |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Success rate | Higher | Slightly lower |
| Can rotate | Yes (Kielland's) | No (except Kiwi) |
| Maternal perineal injury | Higher (3rd/4th degree tears) | Lower |
| Cephalohematoma | Lower | Higher |
| Facial nerve palsy | Yes | No |
| Retinal hemorrhage | Less | More (usually benign) |
| Subgaleal hemorrhage | Rare | Rare but dangerous |
| Minimum GA | Can use preterm (Wrigley's) | > 34 weeks preferred |

### Failed OVD

- If delivery not imminent after 3 contractions or 3 vacuum pop-offs → abandon
- Sequential use of instruments (vacuum → forceps) is associated with ↑ neonatal morbidity → generally discouraged
- Failed OVD → cesarean section (may be difficult: head impacted in pelvis → reverse breech extraction or Patwardhan technique)

### Post-Delivery

- Examine for perineal and vaginal tears systematically
- Examine cervix for lacerations
- Neonatal examination: facial nerve, scalp (cephalohematoma, subgaleal), retinal hemorrhage if vacuum
- Document indication, position, instrument, traction, outcome

## Complications

### Maternal

- 3rd and 4th degree perineal tears (especially forceps)
- Cervical/vaginal lacerations
- Postpartum hemorrhage
- Urinary retention / bladder injury
- Anal sphincter injury → fecal incontinence

### Neonatal

| Complication | Instrument |
|-------------|-----------|
| Cephalohematoma | Vacuum (subperiosteal; does not cross suture lines) |
| Subgaleal hemorrhage | Vacuum (subaponeurotic; crosses sutures; **dangerous**) |
| Facial nerve palsy | Forceps (blade over mastoid) |
| Skull fracture | Forceps (rare) |
| Retinal hemorrhage | Vacuum (usually self-limiting) |
| Intracranial hemorrhage | Both (rare with proper technique) |
| Facial marks/bruising | Forceps |
| Chignon | Vacuum (localized edema at cup site; resolves) |
