# Fetal Wellbeing Monitoring — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Fetal surveillance: DFMC (simplest), NST (most common), BPP (comprehensive, /10), Doppler (hemodynamic). Reactive NST = ≥2 accelerations of ≥15 bpm × 15 sec in 20 min. BPP includes NST + breathing + movements + tone + AFI. Intrapartum CTG: variability is the best marker of oxygenation. Late decelerations = uteroplacental insufficiency.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ Reactive NST: ≥ 2 accelerations (**15 bpm × 15 sec**) in 20 min; before 32 wk: 10×10
- ★ **Variability** (5–25 bpm) is the single best indicator of fetal oxygenation
- ★ BPP score: 8–10 = normal; **0–2 = deliver** regardless of GA
- ★ BPP components: **NST + breathing + movements + tone + AFI** (2 points each)
- ★ Late decelerations = **uteroplacental insufficiency** — always concerning
- ★ Early decelerations = **head compression** — benign
- ★ Variable decelerations = **cord compression** — common, usually benign
- ★ CST positive = late decels with ≥ **50%** of contractions
- ★ Modified BPP = **NST + AFI** (quick screening)
- ★ Cardiff count-to-ten: ≥ **10 movements in 12 hours** = normal
- ★ Sinusoidal pattern → **severe fetal anemia** (Rh disease) or asphyxia

## Mnemonics

### **"DR C BRAVADO"** — CTG Interpretation
- **D**efine Risk
- **R**ate (baseline)
- **C**ontractions
- **B**aseline **R**ate
- **A**ccelerations
- **V**ariability
- **A**ccelerations (again — confirm)
- **D**ecelerations
- **O**verall impression

### **"BBMTA"** — BPP Components
- **B**reathing movements
- **B**ody movements
- **M**uscle tone
- **T**est (NST)
- **A**mniotic fluid

### **"ELV"** — Deceleration Types
- **E**arly = head compression (benign)
- **L**ate = uteroplacental insufficiency (bad)
- **V**ariable = cord compression (common)
