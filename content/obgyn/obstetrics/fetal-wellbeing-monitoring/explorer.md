# Fetal Wellbeing Monitoring

## Overview

Fetal wellbeing monitoring (antepartum and intrapartum surveillance) aims to identify fetuses at risk of hypoxia and acidemia to enable timely intervention. Methods range from simple maternal fetal movement counting to sophisticated Doppler velocimetry and computerized CTG. The goal is to reduce preventable stillbirths and birth asphyxia while minimizing unnecessary interventions.

## Key Points

- Daily fetal movement counting (DFMC) is the simplest screening method
- Non-stress test (NST/CTG) is the most commonly used antepartum test
- Reactive NST: ≥ 2 accelerations in 20 minutes (each ≥ 15 bpm × 15 sec after 32 weeks)
- Biophysical profile (BPP) combines NST + 4 ultrasound parameters (score /10)
- Doppler velocimetry (UA, MCA, DV) assesses placental and fetal hemodynamics
- Intrapartum: continuous electronic fetal monitoring (EFM) with CTG interpretation using DR C BRAVADO

## Definition & Classification

### Methods of Fetal Surveillance

| Method | Type | Timing |
|--------|------|--------|
| Fetal movement counting | Clinical | Antepartum (daily) |
| Non-stress test (NST) | Electronic | Antepartum |
| Contraction stress test (CST) | Electronic | Antepartum |
| Biophysical profile (BPP) | USG + CTG | Antepartum |
| Modified BPP | NST + AFI | Antepartum |
| Doppler velocimetry | USG | Antepartum |
| Intrapartum CTG | Electronic | Intrapartum |
| Fetal scalp blood sampling | Invasive | Intrapartum |
| Fetal pulse oximetry | Invasive | Intrapartum (limited use) |

## Etiology / Pathophysiology

### Fetal Response to Hypoxia

1. **Acute hypoxia**: Fetal heart rate decelerations, redistribution of blood flow
2. **Chronic hypoxia**: Brain-sparing → reduced body growth (asymmetric FGR)
3. **Decompensation**: Loss of heart rate variability, absent accelerations, abnormal DV Doppler
4. **Terminal**: Absent fetal movements, sinusoidal pattern, metabolic acidosis

### Physiological Basis of CTG

- **Baseline rate**: Controlled by balance of sympathetic and parasympathetic tone
- **Variability**: Reflects intact CNS–cardiac axis; best marker of fetal oxygenation
- **Accelerations**: Sympathetic response to movement; indicate intact neurological function
- **Decelerations**: Vagal response to various stimuli (head compression, cord compression, uteroplacental insufficiency)

## Clinical Features

### Fetal Movement Counting

- Normal: ≥ 10 movements in 12 hours (Cardiff count-to-ten method)
- Decreased fetal movements (DFM): May precede stillbirth by 24–48 hours
- Action: If < 10 movements in 12 hours → NST

### Non-Stress Test (NST)

| Result | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Reactive** | ≥ 2 accelerations (≥ 15 bpm × 15 sec) in 20 minutes |
| **Non-reactive** | Fewer than 2 accelerations in 40 minutes |
| **Before 32 weeks** | Acceleration = ≥ 10 bpm × 10 sec |

### Contraction Stress Test (CST)

- Observes FHR response to contractions (3 contractions in 10 minutes)
- **Negative**: No late decelerations → reassuring
- **Positive**: Late decelerations with ≥ 50% contractions → non-reassuring
- **Contraindicated** in: preterm labor risk, placenta previa, prior classical CS

### Biophysical Profile (BPP)

| Parameter | Normal (2 points) | Abnormal (0 points) |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| NST | Reactive | Non-reactive |
| Fetal breathing movements | ≥ 1 episode of ≥ 30 sec in 30 min | Absent |
| Fetal movements | ≥ 3 body/limb movements in 30 min | < 3 movements |
| Fetal tone | ≥ 1 episode of flexion-extension | Absent |
| Amniotic fluid | SDP ≥ 2 cm | SDP < 2 cm |

| BPP Score | Interpretation | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| 8–10 | Normal | Reassuring; repeat weekly |
| 6 | Equivocal | Repeat in 6–24 hours; deliver if ≥ 36 weeks |
| 4 | Abnormal | Deliver if ≥ 32 weeks |
| 0–2 | Strongly abnormal | Deliver regardless of GA (if viable) |

## Diagnosis

### Intrapartum CTG Interpretation — DR C BRAVADO

| Component | Normal | Suspicious | Pathological |
|-----------|--------|------------|-------------|
| **D**efine Risk | Low risk | — | High risk |
| **R**ate (Baseline) | 110–160 bpm | 100–109 or 161–180 | < 100 or > 180 |
| **C**ontractions | Documented | — | — |
| **B**aseline Rate | 110–160 | As above | As above |
| **R**hythm | Regular | — | — |
| **A**ccelerations | Present | — | Absent (not pathological alone) |
| **V**ariability | 5–25 bpm | < 5 for 30–50 min | < 5 for > 50 min |
| **A**ccelerations | Present | — | — |
| **D**ecelerations | None | Variable | Late, prolonged, or atypical variable |
| **O**verall | Normal | Suspicious | Pathological |

### Types of Decelerations

| Type | Timing | Cause | Significance |
|------|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Early** | Mirror contractions | Head compression | Benign |
| **Late** | Onset after contraction peak | Uteroplacental insufficiency | Concerning |
| **Variable** | Variable timing & shape | Cord compression | Common; atypical = concerning |
| **Prolonged** | > 3 minutes | Various (cord prolapse, abruption) | Emergency if > 5 min |

## Management

### Antepartum Surveillance Protocol

| Indication | Test | Frequency |
|------------|------|-----------|
| Decreased fetal movements | NST ± BPP | Immediate |
| GDM on insulin | NST | Twice weekly from 32 weeks |
| Preeclampsia | NST + Doppler + BPP | 1–2× weekly |
| FGR | NST + Doppler | Per Doppler staging |
| Post-term (> 40 weeks) | NST + AFI | Twice weekly |
| Previous stillbirth | NST | Weekly from 32 weeks |

### Intrapartum Management Based on CTG

| Classification | Action |
|----------------|--------|
| Normal | Continue monitoring |
| Suspicious | Conservative measures (left lateral, IV fluids, stop oxytocin) |
| Pathological | Urgent assessment; consider fetal blood sampling or expedite delivery |

### Intrauterine Resuscitation

- Left lateral position
- IV fluid bolus
- Stop oxytocin
- Tocolysis (terbutaline 250 µg SC) if uterine tachysystole
- Oxygen (debated but still used)
- If not improving → expedite delivery

## Complications

- False-positive NST → unnecessary intervention, preterm delivery
- False-negative → missed fetal compromise → stillbirth
- Over-reliance on CTG → increased cesarean rate without improved outcomes
- Fetal scalp sampling: rare infection, bleeding
- Failure to act on pathological CTG → birth asphyxia, cerebral palsy, neonatal death
