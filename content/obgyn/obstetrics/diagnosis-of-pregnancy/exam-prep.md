# Diagnosis of Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Pregnancy diagnosis relies on presumptive (symptoms), probable (signs + UPT), and positive (US visualization, FHS) evidence. β-hCG doubles every 48–72 hours; discriminatory level for TVS is 1500–2000 mIU/mL. CRL at 7–13 weeks is the gold standard for dating. Always rule out ectopic when β-hCG is positive without IUP on scan.

## High Yield Points ★

- ★ β-hCG detectable in serum **8–10 days** post-conception
- ★ Doubling time of β-hCG: **48–72 hours** (slower = ectopic/non-viable)
- ★ Gestational sac on TVS: **4.5–5 weeks**; cardiac activity: **6–6.5 weeks**
- ★ Hegar's sign = softening of **isthmus** (6–8 weeks)
- ★ Chadwick's sign = **bluish discoloration** of vagina/cervix
- ★ Non-viable criteria: CRL ≥ 7 mm without cardiac activity; MSD ≥ 25 mm without embryo
- ★ Naegele's rule: EDD = LMP + **9 months + 7 days**
- ★ Most accurate dating: **first trimester CRL** (±5 days)
- ★ Quickening: primigravida **~20 weeks**, multigravida **~16 weeks**
- ★ β-hCG peaks at **8–10 weeks**, then declines
- ★ Heterotopic pregnancy risk increases with **ART**

## Mnemonics

### **"HCG Peak 8-10"** — hCG timeline
- **H**CG detectable day 8–10 post-conception
- **C**ardiac activity at 6 weeks
- **G**estational sac at 4.5–5 weeks
- **Peak** at 8–10 weeks

### **"HCGO"** — Classical Signs
- **H**egar's — isthmus softening
- **C**hadwick's — cervicovaginal cyanosis
- **G**oodell's — cervix softening
- **O**siander's — lateral fornix pulsation

### **"725"** — Non-viability cutoffs
- CRL ≥ **7** mm (no heartbeat)
- MSD ≥ **25** mm (no embryo)
