# Ectopic Pregnancy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Ectopic pregnancy is implantation outside the uterine cavity, most commonly in the fallopian tube ampulla. Diagnosed by serial beta-hCG + TVS. Ruptured ectopic is a surgical emergency; unruptured may be managed medically with methotrexate.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Most common site: Ampulla of fallopian tube (70%)
- ★ Most common cause: PID / chronic salpingitis
- ★ Discriminatory zone: beta-hCG >1500-2000 with empty uterus on TVS
- ★ Suboptimal hCG rise: <66% increase in 48 hours
- ★ Methotrexate criteria: Stable, unruptured, <3.5 cm, no cardiac activity, hCG <5000
- ★ Most dangerous site: Interstitial (cornual) — ruptures late with massive hemorrhage
- ★ Cervical ectopic: "Barrel-shaped" cervix, bleeding on touch
- ★ Heterotopic pregnancy incidence increases with ART (1:100 vs 1:30,000 natural)
- ★ Always give anti-D immunoglobulin to Rh-negative patients
- ★ Culdocentesis: Non-clotting blood = hemoperitoneum

## Mnemonics
**ECTOPIC** — Risk factors:
- **E**ndometriosis
- **C**ontraception (IUCD)
- **T**ubal surgery/damage
- **O**lder age (>35)
- **P**ID history
- **I**VF/ART
- **C**igarette smoking
