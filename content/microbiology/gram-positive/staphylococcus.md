# Staphylococcus

> 📚 **Sources:** Jawetz, Melnick & Adelberg's Medical Microbiology 28th Ed, Murray's Medical Microbiology 9th Ed, Ananthanarayan & Paniker's Textbook of Microbiology 11th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Staphylococci are **Gram-positive cocci** arranged in **grape-like clusters**. They are **catalase-positive** (distinguishes from Streptococci). *Staphylococcus aureus* is **coagulase-positive** (distinguishes from CoNS). S. aureus is a major human pathogen causing skin infections, bacteremia, endocarditis, osteomyelitis, and toxin-mediated diseases (TSS, food poisoning, SSSS).

## Key Points

- Gram-positive cocci in **clusters** (Greek: staphyle = bunch of grapes)
- **Catalase-positive** (Staph) vs Catalase-negative (Strep)
- **Coagulase-positive** = S. aureus; **Coagulase-negative** = S. epidermidis, S. saprophyticus
- Major pathogen: **S. aureus** (toxins, enzymes, biofilm)
- **MRSA**: Methicillin-resistant S. aureus (mecA gene → PBP2a)
- **MSSA** treatment: Nafcillin/Oxacillin; **MRSA** treatment: Vancomycin

## Detailed Content

### Classification of Staphylococci

| Species | Coagulase | Mannitol Fermentation | Novobiocin | Clinical Significance |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|------------|----------------------|
| **S. aureus** | + | + (yellow on MSA) | Sensitive | Major pathogen |
| **S. epidermidis** | - | - | Sensitive | Device-related infections |
| **S. saprophyticus** | - | - | **Resistant** | UTI in young women |
| **S. lugdunensis** | - (may show bound) | Variable | Sensitive | Aggressive like S. aureus |

### Staphylococcus aureus

#### Morphology & Culture

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Gram stain** | Gram-positive cocci in clusters |
| **Arrangement** | Grape-like clusters (divides in multiple planes) |
| **Colony** | Golden yellow (carotenoid pigment), β-hemolytic on blood agar |
| **Selective media** | Mannitol Salt Agar (MSA) - yellow colonies (ferments mannitol) |
| **Biochemical** | Catalase +, Coagulase +, DNase +, Phosphatase + |

#### Virulence Factors

**Mnemonic: "STAPH CLUE"**

##### Surface Factors (Adhesins & Capsule)

| Factor | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Capsule** | Antiphagocytic (Type 5, 8 most common) |
| **Protein A** | Binds Fc portion of IgG → prevents opsonization |
| **Clumping factor (Bound coagulase)** | Binds fibrinogen → clumping |
| **MSCRAMMs** | Microbial Surface Components Recognizing Adhesive Matrix Molecules |
| **Biofilm** | Slime layer on devices → antibiotic resistance |

##### Enzymes

| Enzyme | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Coagulase (free)** | Converts fibrinogen → fibrin (clot formation) |
| **Catalase** | Converts H₂O₂ → H₂O + O₂ (protects from oxidative killing) |
| **Hyaluronidase** | "Spreading factor" - degrades hyaluronic acid |
| **Lipase** | Degrades lipids in sebaceous glands |
| **β-lactamase** | Penicillin resistance |
| **DNase (Thermonuclease)** | Degrades DNA (diagnostic test) |
| **Staphylokinase** | Activates plasminogen → dissolves clots (spread) |

##### Toxins

| Toxin | Mechanism | Disease |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **α-toxin (Alpha-hemolysin)** | Pore-forming toxin → cell lysis | Dermonecrosis, hemolysis |
| **β-toxin** | Sphingomyelinase → hot-cold lysis | Less important clinically |
| **γ-toxin & δ-toxin** | Membrane damage | Hemolysis, leukocyte lysis |
| **PVL (Panton-Valentine Leukocidin)** | Destroys neutrophils, causes necrosis | CA-MRSA skin infections, necrotizing pneumonia |
| **Exfoliative toxin A, B (ETA, ETB)** | Serine protease → cleaves desmoglein-1 | **SSSS** (Scalded Skin Syndrome) |
| **TSST-1** | Superantigen (MHC II + Vβ TCR) | **Toxic Shock Syndrome** |
| **Enterotoxins (A-E, G-I)** | Superantigen, heat-stable | **Food poisoning** |

**Superantigens:** Bind MHC II + TCR Vβ region → massive T cell activation → cytokine storm

#### Diseases Caused by S. aureus

##### Toxin-Mediated Diseases

| Disease | Toxin | Features | Treatment |
|---------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS)** | TSST-1 (menstrual), Enterotoxins (non-menstrual) | Fever, rash, desquamation, hypotension, multi-organ failure | Fluids, remove source, clindamycin + vancomycin, IVIG |
| **Scalded Skin Syndrome (SSSS)** | Exfoliative toxin (ETA/ETB) | Nikolsky sign, superficial blistering, no mucosal involvement | Anti-staph antibiotics (NOT steroids) |
| **Food Poisoning** | Enterotoxins (preformed, heat-stable) | Rapid onset (1-6 hrs), vomiting > diarrhea, no fever, short duration | Supportive (self-limiting) |

**SSSS vs TEN:**
| Feature | SSSS | TEN |
|---------|------|-----|
| Age | Neonates/children | Adults |
| Cleavage | Intra-epidermal (granular layer) | Dermo-epidermal junction |
| Mucosal involvement | NO | YES |
| Cause | Staph toxin | Drug reaction |
| Mortality | Low | High (30%) |

##### Pyogenic (Suppurative) Infections

| Infection | Features |
|-----------|----------|
| **Skin/Soft tissue** | Folliculitis, furuncle (boil), carbuncle, impetigo (bullous), cellulitis, surgical site infections |
| **Bacteremia** | Source: skin, IV catheters, injection drug use |
| **Endocarditis** | Acute, destructive (valve perforation), emboli; High mortality; Affects normal valves (unlike Strep) |
| **Osteomyelitis** | Most common cause; Hematogenous (children), direct (trauma/surgery) |
| **Septic arthritis** | Most common cause in adults |
| **Pneumonia** | Post-influenza, IVDU, necrotizing (PVL+ strains) |
| **Empyema** | Complication of pneumonia |

#### MRSA (Methicillin-Resistant S. aureus)

##### Mechanism of Resistance

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **mecA gene** | Located on SCCmec (Staphylococcal Cassette Chromosome) |
| **PBP2a** | Altered penicillin-binding protein with low affinity for β-lactams |
| **Resistance pattern** | Resistant to ALL β-lactams (penicillins, cephalosporins, carbapenems) |

##### HA-MRSA vs CA-MRSA

| Feature | HA-MRSA | CA-MRSA |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Setting** | Hospital (>48h admission) | Community (no healthcare contact) |
| **Risk factors** | Catheters, surgery, dialysis, nursing home | Athletes, prisoners, children, close contact |
| **SCCmec type** | I, II, III (larger) | IV, V (smaller) |
| **PVL toxin** | Usually absent | Usually present (necrotizing infections) |
| **Antibiotic resistance** | Multi-drug resistant | Often susceptible to non-β-lactams |
| **Common infections** | Pneumonia, bacteremia, surgical site | Skin/soft tissue (boils, abscesses) |

##### MRSA Treatment

| Infection | Treatment Options |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Skin abscess (uncomplicated)** | Incision & drainage ± TMP-SMX or doxycycline |
| **Skin/soft tissue (complicated)** | Vancomycin, daptomycin, linezolid, ceftaroline |
| **Bacteremia/Endocarditis** | Vancomycin (trough 15-20) or daptomycin |
| **Pneumonia** | Vancomycin or linezolid (linezolid has better lung penetration) |
| **Osteomyelitis** | Vancomycin (prolonged course), consider linezolid |

### Coagulase-Negative Staphylococci (CoNS)

#### S. epidermidis

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Habitat** | Normal skin flora |
| **Virulence** | Biofilm formation on devices |
| **Infections** | Prosthetic valve endocarditis, catheter infections, CSF shunts, prosthetic joints |
| **Treatment** | Vancomycin + Rifampin ± Gentamicin (device removal often needed) |

#### S. saprophyticus

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Unique feature** | Novobiocin RESISTANT (all other CoNS are sensitive) |
| **Epidemiology** | UTI in sexually active young women (2nd most common after E. coli) |
| **Presentation** | Dysuria, frequency (honeymoon cystitis) |
| **Treatment** | TMP-SMX, fluoroquinolones, nitrofurantoin |

#### S. lugdunensis

- Coagulase-negative but clinically behaves like S. aureus
- Causes aggressive endocarditis, skin infections
- May produce bound coagulase (clumping factor)

### Laboratory Diagnosis

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Gram stain** | Gram-positive cocci in clusters |
| **Catalase test** | Positive (distinguishes from Streptococci) |
| **Coagulase test (slide & tube)** | Positive = S. aureus |
| **Mannitol Salt Agar** | S. aureus = yellow colonies |
| **DNase test** | Positive = S. aureus (thermostable DNase) |
| **Latex agglutination** | Detects Protein A and clumping factor |
| **Novobiocin sensitivity** | S. saprophyticus = Resistant |
| **MALDI-TOF** | Rapid species identification |
| **mecA PCR / PBP2a latex** | MRSA confirmation |
| **Nasal swab PCR** | MRSA screening (decolonization programs) |

**Coagulase Test:**
- **Slide test (bound coagulase):** Rapid, clumping with fibrinogen
- **Tube test (free coagulase):** Gold standard, clot formation in rabbit plasma

### Treatment Summary

| Organism | First-Line | Alternatives |
|----------|------------|--------------|
| **MSSA** | Nafcillin/Oxacillin (IV), Cephalexin (PO) | Cefazolin, Clindamycin |
| **MRSA (mild skin)** | I&D ± TMP-SMX, Doxycycline | Clindamycin |
| **MRSA (serious)** | Vancomycin | Daptomycin, Linezolid, Ceftaroline |
| **VRSA** | Linezolid, Daptomycin, Tigecycline | Ceftaroline |
| **S. epidermidis (device)** | Vancomycin + Rifampin + device removal | Daptomycin |
| **S. saprophyticus** | TMP-SMX, Nitrofurantoin | Fluoroquinolones |

### Prevention

| Measure | Target |
|---------|--------|
| **Hand hygiene** | All healthcare settings |
| **MRSA decolonization** | Mupirocin nasal + chlorhexidine baths |
| **Contact precautions** | MRSA-positive patients |
| **Surgical prophylaxis** | Cefazolin (MSSA), Vancomycin (MRSA carriers) |

## Clinical Applications

1. **Boil/Carbuncle**: I&D ± antibiotics (TMP-SMX for MRSA prevalence areas)
2. **MRSA bacteremia**: Vancomycin (trough 15-20 µg/mL), source control
3. **Prosthetic valve endocarditis**: Vancomycin + Rifampin + Gentamicin + surgery
4. **Post-influenza pneumonia**: Consider S. aureus coverage (vancomycin)
5. **Rapid-onset food poisoning**: Think S. aureus (preformed enterotoxin)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Gram-positive cocci in clusters, catalase-positive** → Staphylococcus
2. **Coagulase-positive Staph** → S. aureus
3. **Golden yellow colonies on MSA** → S. aureus (mannitol fermenter)
4. **Novobiocin-resistant CoNS** → S. saprophyticus
5. **MRSA mechanism** → mecA gene → PBP2a
6. **Superantigen toxins** → TSST-1, Enterotoxins
7. **Exfoliative toxin** → SSSS (cleaves desmoglein-1)
8. **Food poisoning characteristics** → 1-6 hr onset, vomiting, preformed toxin
9. **PVL toxin** → CA-MRSA, necrotizing pneumonia
10. **Most common cause of osteomyelitis** → S. aureus
11. **Prosthetic device infection** → S. epidermidis (biofilm)
12. **UTI in young sexually active women** → S. saprophyticus
13. **MSSA treatment DOC** → Nafcillin/Oxacillin
14. **MRSA treatment DOC** → Vancomycin
15. **Protein A function** → Binds Fc of IgG → prevents opsonization
