# Streptococcus

> 📚 **Sources:** Jawetz, Melnick & Adelberg's Medical Microbiology 28th Ed, Murray's Medical Microbiology 9th Ed, Ananthanarayan & Paniker's Textbook of Microbiology 11th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Streptococci are **Gram-positive cocci** arranged in **chains or pairs**. They are **catalase-negative** (distinguishes from Staphylococci). Classification is based on **hemolysis pattern** (α, β, γ) and **Lancefield grouping** (cell wall carbohydrate antigens A-H, K-V). Key pathogens include *S. pyogenes* (Group A - pharyngitis, rheumatic fever), *S. agalactiae* (Group B - neonatal sepsis), and *S. pneumoniae* (pneumonia, meningitis).

## Key Points

- Gram-positive cocci in **chains** (Greek: streptos = twisted chain)
- **Catalase-negative** (Strep) vs Catalase-positive (Staph)
- Classification: **Hemolysis** (α = partial/green, β = complete, γ = none) + **Lancefield groups**
- **S. pyogenes (GAS)**: Pharyngitis, cellulitis, necrotizing fasciitis, **rheumatic fever**, **PSGN**
- **S. agalactiae (GBS)**: Neonatal meningitis/sepsis, pregnant women
- **S. pneumoniae**: #1 cause of bacterial pneumonia, meningitis, otitis media

## Detailed Content

### Classification of Streptococci

#### By Hemolysis on Blood Agar

| Type | Appearance | Key Organisms |
|------|------------|---------------|
| **β-hemolysis** | Complete clearing (transparent) | S. pyogenes, S. agalactiae |
| **α-hemolysis** | Partial (greenish) | S. pneumoniae, Viridans group |
| **γ-hemolysis (non-hemolytic)** | No change | Enterococcus, some Group D |

#### By Lancefield Grouping (Cell Wall C-carbohydrate)

| Group | Species | Key Features |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| **A** | S. pyogenes | Pharyngitis, rheumatic fever |
| **B** | S. agalactiae | Neonatal infections |
| **C, G** | S. dysgalactiae | Pharyngitis (like GAS) |
| **D** | Enterococcus (formerly), S. bovis | GI source, endocarditis |

### Streptococcus pyogenes (Group A Streptococcus - GAS)

#### Morphology & Culture

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Gram stain** | Gram-positive cocci in chains |
| **Hemolysis** | β-hemolytic |
| **Colony** | Small, gray, with zone of β-hemolysis |
| **Bacitracin** | Sensitive (distinguishes from other β-hemolytic strep) |
| **PYR test** | Positive (L-pyrrolidonyl-β-naphthylamide) |
| **Lancefield** | Group A (detected by latex agglutination) |

#### Virulence Factors

##### Cell Wall Components

| Factor | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **M protein** | Major virulence factor; anti-phagocytic; basis for serotyping (>200 types) |
| **Hyaluronic acid capsule** | Anti-phagocytic (identical to human tissue - non-immunogenic) |
| **Lipoteichoic acid** | Adherence to epithelial cells |
| **F protein (Fibronectin-binding)** | Adherence |
| **C5a peptidase** | Degrades complement C5a → ↓ chemotaxis |

**M protein importance:**
- Anti-phagocytic (binds factor H → ↓ C3b opsonization)
- Antibodies to M protein = type-specific immunity
- Certain M types (1, 3, 5, 6, 18, 24) → Rheumatic fever (molecular mimicry)

##### Enzymes

| Enzyme | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Streptokinase (fibrinolysin)** | Activates plasminogen → dissolves clots → spread |
| **Hyaluronidase** | "Spreading factor" → tissue invasion |
| **DNases (Streptodornase)** | Degrades DNA in pus → spread |
| **C5a peptidase** | Inhibits neutrophil chemotaxis |

##### Toxins

| Toxin | Mechanism | Disease |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Streptolysins O (SLO)** | Oxygen-labile hemolysin; immunogenic (ASO titer) | Hemolysis, leukocyte lysis |
| **Streptolysins S (SLS)** | Oxygen-stable hemolysin; non-immunogenic | β-hemolysis on agar |
| **Pyrogenic exotoxins (SpeA, SpeB, SpeC)** | Superantigens (like staph TSST-1) | Scarlet fever, Streptococcal TSS |
| **Exotoxin A (SpeA)** | Most potent superantigen | Severe invasive disease, TSS |

---

#### Diseases Caused by GAS

##### Suppurative (Pyogenic) Infections

| Disease | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **Pharyngitis** | Most common bacterial cause; fever, exudative tonsillitis, cervical LAD, NO cough |
| **Scarlet fever** | Pharyngitis + diffuse erythematous rash (sandpaper), strawberry tongue, Pastia's lines, circumoral pallor |
| **Impetigo** | Honey-crusted lesions; can cause PSGN (not rheumatic fever) |
| **Erysipelas** | Superficial dermis; sharply demarcated, raised, "butterfly" on face |
| **Cellulitis** | Deeper dermis; spreading, indistinct borders |
| **Necrotizing fasciitis** | "Flesh-eating disease"; surgical emergency; pain out of proportion |
| **Myositis** | Muscle involvement; severe |
| **Puerperal sepsis** | Postpartum endometritis; historically common (Semmelweis) |

##### Toxin-Mediated Diseases

| Disease | Toxin | Features |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Scarlet fever** | Pyrogenic exotoxins (SPE) | Rash, fever, strawberry tongue |
| **Streptococcal TSS (STSS)** | Superantigen (SpeA) | Shock, organ failure; often with necrotizing fasciitis; HIGH mortality |

##### Post-Streptococcal Immunologic Diseases

| Disease | Latent Period | Features | Prevention |
|---------|---------------|----------|------------|
| **Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF)** | 2-4 weeks after pharyngitis | Jones criteria; carditis, chorea, arthritis | Treat pharyngitis with penicillin |
| **Post-Streptococcal GN (PSGN)** | 1-2 weeks (pharyngitis) or 3-6 weeks (skin) | Nephritic syndrome; follows pharyngitis OR skin infection | NOT prevented by treatment |

**Rheumatic Fever - Jones Criteria (Mnemonic: JONES)**

| Major Criteria | Minor Criteria |
|---------------|----------------|
| **J**oints (migratory polyarthritis) | Fever |
| **O** (Carditis) | Arthralgia |
| **N**odules (subcutaneous) | ↑ ESR/CRP |
| **E**rythema marginatum | Prolonged PR interval |
| **S**ydenham's chorea | Previous ARF |

**Diagnosis:** 2 Major OR 1 Major + 2 Minor + evidence of recent GAS infection

**Pathogenesis:** Molecular mimicry - antibodies against M protein cross-react with cardiac myosin, valve tissue, joints, brain (basal ganglia)

---

#### Laboratory Diagnosis

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Rapid Antigen Detection Test (RADT)** | Point-of-care; high specificity, moderate sensitivity |
| **Throat culture** | Gold standard; confirm negative RADT in children |
| **ASO titer** | Evidence of recent infection (positive 1-5 weeks post-infection) |
| **Anti-DNase B** | Better for skin infections (ASO may be negative) |
| **Streptozyme test** | Multiple antibodies (ASO, anti-DNase B, etc.) |
| **Bacitracin sensitivity** | GAS = sensitive; other β-strep = resistant |
| **PYR test** | GAS and Enterococcus = positive |

---

#### Treatment & Prevention

| Condition | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Pharyngitis** | Penicillin V (10 days) or Amoxicillin or IM Benzathine Pen G (single dose) |
| **Severe invasive (necrotizing fasciitis, STSS)** | Penicillin + Clindamycin (↓ toxin production) + surgical debridement |
| **Rheumatic fever prophylaxis** | Benzathine Pen G monthly or Pen V daily (for years/lifelong) |
| **Penicillin allergy** | Azithromycin, cephalexin (if non-anaphylactic) |

**Why treat pharyngitis?**
- Prevents rheumatic fever (treatment within 9 days)
- Reduces suppurative complications
- ↓ Transmission
- DOES NOT prevent PSGN

### Streptococcus agalactiae (Group B Streptococcus - GBS)

#### Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Gram stain** | Gram-positive cocci in chains |
| **Hemolysis** | β-hemolytic (narrow zone) or non-hemolytic |
| **CAMP test** | Positive (arrowhead hemolysis with S. aureus) |
| **Hippurate hydrolysis** | Positive |
| **Bacitracin** | Resistant (distinguishes from GAS) |
| **Lancefield** | Group B |

#### Clinical Significance

**#1 cause of neonatal meningitis and sepsis**

| Population | Disease |
|------------|---------|
| **Neonates - Early onset (<7 days)** | Pneumonia, sepsis, meningitis (acquired during delivery) |
| **Neonates - Late onset (7 days-3 months)** | Meningitis (nosocomial or community) |
| **Pregnant women** | Asymptomatic colonization (vaginal/rectal), chorioamnionitis |
| **Adults (non-pregnant)** | Skin infections, UTI, bacteremia, endocarditis (diabetics, elderly) |

#### Prevention

**Universal GBS screening at 35-37 weeks gestation**

| Indication for Intrapartum Prophylaxis |
|---------------------------------------|
| Positive GBS culture at 35-37 weeks |
| GBS bacteriuria during pregnancy |
| Previous infant with GBS disease |
| Unknown GBS status + risk factors (preterm, ROM >18h, fever) |

**Prophylaxis:** IV Penicillin G (or Ampicillin) during labor

### Streptococcus pneumoniae (Pneumococcus)

#### Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Morphology** | Lancet-shaped diplococci (Gram-positive) |
| **Hemolysis** | α-hemolytic (green zone) |
| **Capsule** | Polysaccharide capsule (>100 serotypes) - MAJOR virulence factor |
| **Optochin** | Sensitive (distinguishes from Viridans strep) |
| **Bile solubility** | Positive (lyses in 10% bile/deoxycholate) |
| **Quellung reaction** | Capsule swelling with type-specific antibody |
| **Culture** | Requires blood/chocolate agar; grows best with 5% CO₂ |

#### Virulence Factors

| Factor | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Polysaccharide capsule** | Anti-phagocytic; #1 virulence factor; basis for vaccines |
| **IgA protease** | Destroys IgA → colonization of respiratory tract |
| **Pneumolysin** | Cytotoxin; damages respiratory epithelium |
| **Teichoic acid (C-polysaccharide)** | Binds CRP → complement activation |
| **Pili** | Adherence |
| **Autolysin (LytA)** | Cell wall degradation → releases inflammatory components |

#### Clinical Significance

**#1 cause of:**
- **Community-acquired pneumonia**
- **Bacterial meningitis** (adults)
- **Otitis media** (children)
- **Sinusitis**

| Disease | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **Pneumonia** | Lobar pneumonia, "rusty" sputum, rigors; complication of viral URI/influenza |
| **Meningitis** | Most common in adults; high mortality |
| **Otitis media** | Most common bacterial cause in children |
| **Sinusitis** | With H. influenzae, M. catarrhalis |
| **Bacteremia** | Often secondary to pneumonia |

**Risk factors:** Extremes of age, asplenia (MOST important), sickle cell disease, HIV, hypogammaglobulinemia, chronic illness

**Post-splenectomy sepsis:** Encapsulated organisms (S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, N. meningitidis) - "SHiN"

#### Antibiotic Resistance

| Mechanism | Clinical Impact |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Altered PBPs** | Penicillin resistance (NOT β-lactamase) |
| **Intermediate resistance** | MIC 2-4 µg/mL - treat with high-dose penicillin |
| **High-level resistance** | MIC ≥8 µg/mL - use ceftriaxone, vancomycin |

**Treatment by site:**
- **Pneumonia (non-meningitis):** High-dose amoxicillin, respiratory fluoroquinolone
- **Meningitis:** Ceftriaxone + Vancomycin (empiric), dexamethasone before antibiotics

#### Prevention

| Vaccine | Type | Coverage | Indicated For |
|---------|------|----------|---------------|
| **PCV13 (Prevnar)** | Conjugate | 13 serotypes | All children (<2 years), adults ≥65 |
| **PCV15, PCV20** | Conjugate | 15, 20 serotypes | Newer, replacing PCV13 |
| **PPSV23 (Pneumovax)** | Polysaccharide | 23 serotypes | Adults ≥65, high-risk adults |

**Conjugate vaccines:** Better immunogenicity in children (T-cell dependent response)

### Viridans Group Streptococci

#### Characteristics

- **α-hemolytic** (green zone)
- **Optochin RESISTANT** (distinguishes from pneumococcus)
- **Bile solubility:** Negative
- **Normal oral flora**

#### Species & Clinical Significance

| Species | Association |
|---------|-------------|
| **S. mutans** | Dental caries (#1 cause) |
| **S. sanguinis** | Subacute bacterial endocarditis (SBE) - on damaged valves |
| **S. mitis** | SBE, bacteremia in neutropenic patients |
| **S. anginosus group** | Abscesses (brain, liver) |

**Subacute Bacterial Endocarditis:**
- Affects previously damaged/abnormal valves
- Insidious onset
- Treat with prolonged penicillin ± gentamicin

---

### Enterococcus (Formerly Group D Strep)

#### Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Gram stain** | Gram-positive cocci in chains/pairs |
| **Hemolysis** | α, β, or γ (variable) |
| **Growth** | Hardy - grows in 6.5% NaCl, bile, 10-45°C, pH 9.6 |
| **PYR test** | Positive |
| **Lancefield** | Group D |
| **Species** | E. faecalis (most common), E. faecium (more resistant) |

#### Clinical Significance

- **UTI** (hospitalized, catheterized patients)
- **Endocarditis** (subacute, GI source)
- **Biliary tract infections**
- **Intra-abdominal infections** (mixed flora)
- **Bacteremia**

#### Antibiotic Resistance

| Resistance | Mechanism | Treatment |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Intrinsic to cephalosporins** | Low PBP affinity | Use ampicillin/penicillin |
| **High-level aminoglycoside** | Modifying enzymes | Synergy lost; use different agent |
| **VRE (Vancomycin-resistant)** | VanA (E. faecium), VanB | Linezolid, daptomycin, tigecycline |

**Enterococcal Endocarditis:** Ampicillin + Gentamicin (synergy) for 4-6 weeks

---

### Streptococcus bovis/gallolyticus

- **Group D Streptococcus** (non-enterococcus)
- α-hemolytic, bile esculin positive
- **STRONG ASSOCIATION WITH COLON CANCER**
- S. bovis bacteremia/endocarditis → **Colonoscopy required**

## Clinical Applications

1. **Pharyngitis in child**: RADT → if negative, throat culture → Penicillin if positive
2. **Rheumatic fever prophylaxis**: Monthly benzathine penicillin G
3. **Pregnant woman GBS+**: IV Penicillin during labor
4. **Lobar pneumonia**: Think S. pneumoniae → ceftriaxone + azithromycin
5. **S. bovis endocarditis**: Get a colonoscopy (colon cancer association)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Gram-positive cocci in chains, catalase-negative** → Streptococcus
2. **β-hemolytic, bacitracin-sensitive** → S. pyogenes (GAS)
3. **CAMP test positive** → S. agalactiae (GBS)
4. **Lancet-shaped diplococci, optochin-sensitive** → S. pneumoniae
5. **α-hemolytic, optochin-resistant** → Viridans streptococci
6. **Quellung reaction** → Pneumococcus (capsule swelling)
7. **M protein** → Major virulence factor of GAS; anti-phagocytic
8. **Rheumatic fever pathogenesis** → Molecular mimicry (M protein ~ cardiac myosin)
9. **Jones criteria for ARF** → J-O-N-E-S (joints, carditis, nodules, erythema marginatum, Sydenham chorea)
10. **#1 cause of neonatal meningitis** → Group B Strep (S. agalactiae)
11. **#1 cause of CAP** → S. pneumoniae
12. **#1 cause of dental caries** → S. mutans
13. **S. bovis association** → Colon cancer
14. **Post-splenectomy organisms** → SHiN (Strep pneumo, H. flu, Neisseria)
15. **ASO titer** → Evidence of recent GAS pharyngitis (not skin infection)
16. **Anti-DNase B** → Better for GAS skin infections
17. **Why treat GAS pharyngitis?** → Prevents rheumatic fever (not PSGN)
