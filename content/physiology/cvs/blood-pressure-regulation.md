# Blood Pressure Regulation

> 📚 **Sources:** Guyton & Hall 14th Ed (Ch 18-19), Ganong 26th Ed (Ch 32-33)
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

Blood pressure is regulated through a hierarchy of mechanisms operating at different time scales:
- **Immediate (seconds):** Neural - Baroreceptor reflex
- **Intermediate (minutes-hours):** Hormonal - RAAS, ADH, ANP
- **Long-term (days-weeks):** Renal - Pressure natriuresis

**MAP = CO × TPR** (Mean Arterial Pressure = Cardiac Output × Total Peripheral Resistance)

## Key Points

- Normal BP: **120/80 mmHg**, MAP ≈ **93 mmHg**
- Baroreceptors most sensitive between **80-150 mmHg**
- RAAS is the **most important** long-term regulator
- Kidney is the **ultimate determinant** of long-term BP
- Hypertension = sustained BP ≥ **140/90 mmHg**

## Detailed Content

### Immediate Regulation: Baroreceptor Reflex

```
↑ BP → Stretch baroreceptors (carotid sinus, aortic arch)
     → ↑ Afferent firing (CN IX, X)
     → NTS (medulla)
     → ↓ Sympathetic, ↑ Parasympathetic
     → ↓ HR, ↓ Contractility, Vasodilation
     → ↓ BP (negative feedback)
```

| Component | Location | Nerve | Function |
|-----------|----------|-------|----------|
| Carotid sinus | Carotid bifurcation | CN IX (Glossopharyngeal) | Senses stretch |
| Aortic arch | Aortic arch | CN X (Vagus) | Senses stretch |
| NTS | Medulla | - | Integration center |
| CVLM | Medulla | - | Inhibits sympathetic |
| RVLM | Medulla | - | Activates sympathetic |

**Clinical Pearl:** Carotid sinus massage → ↑ vagal tone → ↓ HR (used in SVT)

### Intermediate Regulation: Hormonal

#### RAAS (Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System)

```
↓ BP/↓ Na⁺/Sympathetic → JG cells (afferent arteriole)
                        → RENIN
                        → Angiotensinogen → Angiotensin I
                        → ACE (lung) → Angiotensin II
                        ↓
        ┌───────────────┼───────────────┐
        ↓               ↓               ↓
  Vasoconstriction   Aldosterone     ADH release
  (↑ TPR)            (Na⁺ retention) (H₂O retention)
        └───────────────┴───────────────┘
                        ↓
                      ↑ BP
```

#### Other Hormones

| Hormone | Source | Effect on BP | Mechanism |
|---------|--------|--------------|-----------|
| **ADH (Vasopressin)** | Posterior pituitary | ↑ | V1: Vasoconstriction, V2: H₂O retention |
| **ANP/BNP** | Atria/Ventricles | ↓ | Natriuresis, vasodilation, inhibits RAAS |
| **Epinephrine** | Adrenal medulla | ↑↑ | α1: Vasoconstriction, β1: ↑ HR & contractility |
| **Norepinephrine** | Sympathetic nerves | ↑ | α1: Potent vasoconstriction |
| **Dopamine** | - | Variable | Low dose: Renal vasodilation |

### Long-Term Regulation: Renal Pressure Natriuresis

```
↑ BP → ↑ Renal perfusion pressure
     → ↑ Na⁺ and H₂O excretion (pressure natriuresis)
     → ↓ Blood volume
     → ↓ BP

This is an INFINITE GAIN system - will restore BP to set point
```

**Key Concept:** The kidney sets the long-term BP "set point." Without kidneys, there is NO mechanism for permanent BP correction.

### Summary: Time Course of BP Regulation

| Time Frame | Mechanism | Gain |
|------------|-----------|------|
| Seconds | Baroreceptor reflex | High but adapts |
| Seconds | CNS ischemic response | Very high (emergency) |
| Minutes | Chemoreceptor reflex | Moderate |
| Minutes-Hours | Capillary fluid shift | Moderate |
| Hours | RAAS, ADH | High |
| Days-Weeks | Pressure natriuresis | Infinite |

## Clinical Correlations

### Essential Hypertension (95% of cases)
- **Mechanism:** ↑ TPR due to vascular remodeling, RAAS activation
- **Treatment targets:**
  - ACE inhibitors: Block Ang I → Ang II
  - ARBs: Block AT1 receptors
  - Diuretics: ↓ volume
  - β-blockers: ↓ HR, ↓ renin release
  - CCBs: Vasodilation

### Secondary Hypertension (5% of cases)

| Cause | Mechanism | Clue |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Renal artery stenosis** | ↑ Renin | Abdominal bruit, flash pulmonary edema |
| **Pheochromocytoma** | ↑ Catecholamines | Episodic HTN, headache, sweating, palpitations |
| **Conn's syndrome** | ↑ Aldosterone | Hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis |
| **Cushing's syndrome** | Cortisol excess | Moon facies, buffalo hump |
| **Coarctation of aorta** | Mechanical obstruction | Upper > lower limb BP |

### Orthostatic Hypotension
- ↓ BP > 20/10 mmHg on standing
- **Causes:** Autonomic failure, hypovolemia, drugs
- **Why?** Failed baroreceptor compensation

### Shock
- **Cardiogenic:** ↓ CO, ↑ TPR (compensatory)
- **Hypovolemic:** ↓ Preload, ↑ HR, ↑ TPR
- **Septic:** ↓↓ TPR (vasodilation), initially ↑ CO

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Baroreceptors adapt** over 1-2 days → Cannot prevent chronic HTN
2. **Cushing reflex:** ↑ ICP → ↑ BP + ↓ HR (brainstem ischemia response)
3. **Bezold-Jarisch reflex:** Coronary ischemia → Bradycardia, hypotension
4. **ACE inhibitors** cause cough (↑ bradykinin) and angioedema
5. **ARBs** don't cause cough (don't affect bradykinin)
6. **Hypertensive emergency:** BP > 180/120 + end-organ damage
7. **First-line antihypertensives:** ACEi/ARB, CCB, Thiazide diuretics
8. **ANP is released by atrial stretch** → Marker of volume overload
9. **BNP** elevated in heart failure (diagnostic and prognostic)
10. **α-blockers** (prazosin) cause first-dose hypotension

### Formulae
```
MAP = DBP + 1/3(PP) = DBP + 1/3(SBP - DBP)
MAP = CO × TPR
CO = SV × HR
TPR ∝ (Viscosity × Length) / r⁴ (Poiseuille's law)
```
