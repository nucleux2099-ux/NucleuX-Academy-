# Cardiac Cycle

> 📚 **Sources:** Guyton & Hall 14th Ed (Ch 9-10), Ganong 26th Ed (Ch 30)
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The cardiac cycle is the sequence of electrical and mechanical events that occur during one complete heartbeat. It consists of **systole** (contraction) and **diastole** (relaxation), with the entire cycle lasting approximately **0.8 seconds** at a heart rate of 75 bpm.

## Key Points

- Duration at 75 bpm: **0.8 seconds** (systole 0.3s, diastole 0.5s)
- Ventricular filling is **80% passive** (early diastole) and **20% atrial kick**
- Isovolumetric phases occur when **all valves are closed**
- Left ventricular pressure ranges from **0-120 mmHg**
- Aortic valve opens when LV pressure exceeds **80 mmHg** (diastolic BP)

## Detailed Content

### Phases of the Cardiac Cycle

| Phase | Duration | Valves | LV Pressure | LV Volume |
|-------|----------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Atrial Systole** | 0.1s | AV open, SL closed | 0→8 mmHg | ↑ 20% |
| **Isovolumetric Contraction** | 0.05s | All closed | 8→80 mmHg | Constant |
| **Rapid Ejection** | 0.15s | SL open, AV closed | 80→120 mmHg | ↓↓ |
| **Reduced Ejection** | 0.1s | SL open, AV closed | 120→100 mmHg | ↓ |
| **Isovolumetric Relaxation** | 0.08s | All closed | 100→0 mmHg | Constant |
| **Rapid Filling** | 0.12s | AV open, SL closed | ~0 mmHg | ↑↑ |
| **Diastasis** | 0.2s | AV open, SL closed | ~0 mmHg | ↑ |

### Pressure-Volume Loop (Wiggers Diagram)

```
           SYSTOLE                    DIASTOLE
    ┌─────────────────────┐    ┌─────────────────────────┐
    │  IVC  │ Ejection    │    │  IVR  │    Filling      │
    │       │             │    │       │                 │
    Mitral  Aortic      Aortic  Mitral               Atrial
    closes  opens       closes  opens                systole
```

### Key Volumes

- **End-Diastolic Volume (EDV):** 120-130 mL
- **End-Systolic Volume (ESV):** 50-60 mL
- **Stroke Volume (SV):** EDV - ESV = 70 mL
- **Ejection Fraction (EF):** SV/EDV × 100 = **55-70%**

### Heart Sounds Correlation

| Sound | Timing | Cause |
|-------|--------|-------|
| S1 | Start of systole | Mitral & Tricuspid closure |
| S2 | End of systole | Aortic & Pulmonary closure |
| S3 | Early diastole | Rapid ventricular filling (pathological in adults) |
| S4 | Late diastole | Atrial contraction against stiff ventricle |

## Clinical Correlations

### Heart Failure
- **Systolic HF (HFrEF):** ↓ EF (<40%), ↓ contractility
- **Diastolic HF (HFpEF):** Normal EF, impaired filling, prominent S4

### Valvular Disease
- **Mitral Stenosis:** Prolonged diastole, loud S1, opening snap
- **Aortic Stenosis:** Prolonged ejection, soft S2, ejection systolic murmur
- **Mitral Regurgitation:** Holosystolic murmur, ↑ preload

### Atrial Fibrillation
- Loss of atrial kick → ↓ cardiac output by 15-20%
- Critical in patients with diastolic dysfunction

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Longest phase** of cardiac cycle: Reduced ventricular filling (diastasis)
2. **Isovolumetric contraction** ends when LV pressure > aortic diastolic pressure
3. **S1 splitting** = Tricuspid closes slightly after Mitral
4. **Wide S2 splitting** on inspiration = Normal (↑ venous return delays pulmonic closure)
5. **Fixed S2 splitting** = Atrial Septal Defect
6. **Paradoxical S2 splitting** = LBBB, Aortic Stenosis
7. **a wave** in JVP corresponds to atrial contraction
8. **c wave** = Tricuspid bulging during isovolumetric contraction
9. **v wave** = Venous filling while tricuspid is closed
10. Tachycardia shortens **diastole more than systole** → compromises filling

### Quick Formula Box
```
Cardiac Output (CO) = SV × HR = 5 L/min at rest
Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) = DBP + 1/3(SBP - DBP)
Ejection Fraction (EF) = (SV/EDV) × 100
```
