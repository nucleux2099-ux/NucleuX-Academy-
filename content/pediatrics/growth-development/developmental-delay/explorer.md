# Developmental Delay

## Overview
Developmental delay refers to significant delay in one or more developmental domains: gross motor, fine motor, speech/language, cognitive, and social/adaptive. Global developmental delay (GDD) is defined as significant delay (>2 SD below mean) in ≥2 domains in a child <5 years. It affects 1-3% of children and is an important cause of childhood disability. Early identification and intervention improve outcomes.

## Key Points
- Significant delay = performance >2 SD below mean for age
- Global developmental delay (GDD): ≥2 domains delayed in child <5 years
- Intellectual disability (ID) replaces "mental retardation" for children >5 years (IQ <70)
- Most common cause: genetic/chromosomal (Down syndrome most common identifiable)
- Denver Developmental Screening Test (DDST-II) is the standard screening tool
- Early intervention is critical (brain plasticity highest in first 3 years)

## Definition & Classification

### Developmental Domains
1. **Gross motor**: Head control, sitting, walking, running
2. **Fine motor/adaptive**: Grasping, drawing, writing, self-care
3. **Language**: Receptive and expressive speech
4. **Social/personal**: Interaction, play, emotional development
5. **Cognitive**: Problem-solving, reasoning

### Severity (based on IQ for >5 years)
| Level | IQ Range | Approximate % |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| Mild | 50-70 | 85% |
| Moderate | 35-50 | 10% |
| Severe | 20-35 | 3-4% |
| Profound | <20 | 1-2% |

### Types of Delay
- **Isolated delay**: One domain only (e.g., speech delay alone)
- **Global developmental delay**: ≥2 domains
- **Developmental regression**: Loss of previously acquired milestones (neurodegenerative)

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Prenatal Causes (most common group)
- Chromosomal: Down syndrome (Trisomy 21), Fragile X syndrome, Turner, Klinefelter
- Structural brain anomalies: microcephaly, lissencephaly, holoprosencephaly
- TORCH infections: CMV (most common infectious cause), toxoplasma, rubella
- Maternal: alcohol (FAS), drugs, malnutrition, radiation
- Metabolic: PKU, hypothyroidism, galactosemia

### Perinatal Causes
- Birth asphyxia / HIE
- Prematurity, IVH, PVL
- Neonatal meningitis/sepsis
- Kernicterus

### Postnatal Causes
- Infections: meningitis, encephalitis
- Trauma: head injury, non-accidental injury
- Malnutrition, iron deficiency
- Environmental deprivation, neglect
- Lead poisoning
- Hypothyroidism

### Red Flags for Developmental Delay
- No social smile by 2 months
- No head control by 4 months
- No sitting by 9 months
- No standing by 12 months
- No walking by 18 months
- No words by 18 months
- No two-word phrases by 24 months
- Loss of milestones at any age (regression)

## Clinical Features

### By Domain
**Gross Motor Delay**: Floppy baby, delayed head control, late sitting/walking, abnormal gait
**Fine Motor Delay**: Poor grasp, clumsy, difficulty with buttons/writing
**Language Delay**: No babbling by 9 months, no words by 18 months, poor comprehension
**Social Delay**: Poor eye contact, no social smile, lack of stranger anxiety, poor play skills
**Cognitive Delay**: Poor problem-solving, difficulty following instructions, below-age learning

### Associated Features
- Dysmorphic features (suggest genetic cause)
- Microcephaly or macrocephaly
- Seizures
- Sensory impairment (vision, hearing)
- Behavioral problems (ADHD, autism spectrum)

## Diagnosis

### Screening Tools
- **Denver Developmental Screening Test (DDST-II)**: 0-6 years; 4 domains; pass/fail/caution
- **Ages and Stages Questionnaire (ASQ)**
- **Trivandrum Developmental Screening Chart (TDSC)**: Indian tool
- **Bayley Scales of Infant Development**: Gold standard diagnostic assessment

### Investigations (Stepwise)
1. **First-tier**: Hearing test (OAE/BERA), vision assessment, thyroid function, CBC, lead levels
2. **Second-tier**: Chromosomal analysis/karyotype, Fragile X testing, metabolic screen (TMS)
3. **Neuroimaging**: MRI brain (structural anomalies, myelination)
4. **EEG**: If seizures present
5. **Specific genetic testing**: Microarray, whole exome sequencing
6. **Metabolic workup**: Urine organic acids, plasma amino acids, lactate, ammonia

## Management

### Early Intervention (Most Important)
- **Physiotherapy**: Gross motor delay, hypotonia
- **Occupational therapy**: Fine motor, self-care skills
- **Speech therapy**: Language delay
- **Special education**: Cognitive delay
- **Behavioral therapy**: Social skills, autism spectrum

### Specific Treatment
- Hypothyroidism → levothyroxine
- PKU → phenylalanine-restricted diet
- Hearing loss → hearing aids/cochlear implant
- Vision impairment → correction
- Seizures → antiepileptics

### Family Support
- Parent counseling and education
- Support groups
- Government schemes (RBSK, Disability Certificate, UDID)
- Inclusive education

## Complications
- Academic failure
- Social isolation, bullying
- Behavioral disorders (ADHD, anxiety, depression)
- Caregiver burnout
- If unrecognized: preventable causes become irreversible (e.g., hypothyroidism, PKU)
