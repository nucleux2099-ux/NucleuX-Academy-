# Down Syndrome — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Trisomy 21. Most common chromosomal disorder. Free trisomy (95%), translocation (4%), mosaic (1%). Characteristic facies + hypotonia + simian crease. AV canal defect (40-50%). Karyotype = gold standard. Screen: quad test, NIPT.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Most common identifiable cause of intellectual disability
- ★ Free trisomy 21 (95%): non-disjunction, maternal age-related
- ★ AV canal (endocardial cushion) defect = most common cardiac lesion in Down syndrome
- ★ Quad screen: ↑β-hCG, ↑Inhibin A, ↓AFP, ↓uE3
- ★ Duodenal atresia: "double bubble" sign on X-ray, associated with Down syndrome
- ★ ALL risk 20× increased (NOT AML — though TMD/AMKL also associated)
- ★ Brushfield spots = white spots on iris (pathognomonic)
- ★ Atlantoaxial instability: 10-30%, screen with C-spine X-ray at 3-5 years
- ★ Hypothyroidism in 15-20%, screen TSH annually
- ★ Translocation type: MUST karyotype parents (carrier risk)
- ★ Early Alzheimer disease (age 40-50) due to APP gene on chromosome 21
