# Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21)

## Overview
Down syndrome is the most common chromosomal disorder and the most common identifiable cause of intellectual disability. It is caused by trisomy 21 (extra copy of chromosome 21). Incidence is ~1 in 700 live births and increases dramatically with maternal age (1 in 25 at age 45). It is characterized by distinctive facial features, intellectual disability, hypotonia, and multiple systemic associations.

## Key Points
- Trisomy 21 is the most common chromosomal abnormality
- Risk increases with maternal age (but most born to mothers <35 due to higher birth rate)
- Free trisomy 21 (95%), Robertsonian translocation (4%), Mosaicism (1%)
- Characteristic facies + hypotonia + single palmar crease + intellectual disability
- Associated with endocardial cushion defect (AV canal defect), hypothyroidism, atlantoaxial instability, ALL
- Life expectancy now 60+ years with proper care

## Definition & Classification

### Cytogenetic Types
| Type | Mechanism | Frequency | Recurrence Risk |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Free trisomy 21 | Non-disjunction (meiosis I/II) | 95% | 1% or age-related risk (whichever higher) |
| Robertsonian translocation | t(14;21) or t(21;21) | 4% | If parent carrier: mother 10-15%, father 3-5% |
| Mosaicism | Post-zygotic non-disjunction | 1% | Milder phenotype, lower recurrence |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Genetics
- **Non-disjunction**: Failure of chromosome 21 to separate during meiosis → 47,XX/XY,+21
- Maternal age is the strongest risk factor (related to oocyte aging)
- Translocation type: important to karyotype BOTH parents (one may be a balanced carrier)
- Mosaicism: two cell lines (46,XX and 47,XX,+21) → variable phenotype

### Prenatal Screening & Diagnosis
**First trimester screening (11-13.6 weeks):**
- Nuchal translucency (increased) + PAPP-A (decreased) + free β-hCG (increased)
- Combined detection rate: ~85%

**Second trimester (Quad screen, 15-20 weeks):**
- ↑ β-hCG, ↑ Inhibin A, ↓ AFP, ↓ Unconjugated estriol
- Mnemonic: "β-hCG and Inhibin go UP, AFP and uE3 go DOWN"

**Non-invasive prenatal testing (NIPT):**
- Cell-free fetal DNA in maternal blood; >99% detection rate

**Diagnostic (confirmatory):**
- Chorionic villus sampling (CVS): 10-12 weeks
- Amniocentesis: 15-18 weeks
- Karyotype is the gold standard diagnostic test

## Clinical Features

### Craniofacial
- Flat facial profile, flat nasal bridge
- Upslanting palpebral fissures
- Epicanthal folds
- Brushfield spots (white/gray spots on iris)
- Small ears, protruding tongue (relative macroglossia)
- Short neck with excess nuchal skin
- Brachycephaly (short head)

### Extremities
- Short, broad hands with single transverse palmar crease (simian crease)
- Clinodactyly (incurved 5th finger)
- Wide sandal gap (gap between 1st and 2nd toes)
- Hypotonia (universal finding in newborn)

### Systemic Associations
| System | Association | Frequency |
|--------|------------|-----------|
| Cardiac | Endocardial cushion defect (AV canal) | 40-50% |
| GI | Duodenal atresia, Hirschsprung, TE fistula | 12% |
| Hematologic | ALL (20× risk), transient myeloproliferative disorder | 1-2% |
| Endocrine | Hypothyroidism | 15-20% |
| Musculoskeletal | Atlantoaxial instability | 10-30% |
| Eyes | Strabismus, cataracts, refractive errors | 60% |
| Hearing | Conductive/sensorineural hearing loss | 75% |
| Neurological | Intellectual disability (mild-moderate), early Alzheimer's | 100% |
| Immune | Increased infections, autoimmune disorders | Common |

## Diagnosis
1. **Clinical**: Characteristic phenotype at birth
2. **Karyotype**: Gold standard (47,XX/XY,+21 or translocation)
3. **FISH**: Rapid preliminary result (24-48 hours)
4. **Echocardiography**: All Down syndrome babies (cardiac defects in 40-50%)
5. **Thyroid function**: At birth and annually
6. **Hearing screening**: OAE/BERA at birth
7. **Ophthalmologic exam**: By 6 months
8. **CBC**: Screening for hematologic abnormalities
9. **Cervical spine X-ray**: Before sports/anesthesia (atlantoaxial instability)

## Management

### Health Supervision (Preventive)
- **Cardiac**: Echo at birth; surgical repair of defects
- **Thyroid**: TSH at birth, 6 months, then annually
- **Hearing**: At birth, 6 months, annually
- **Vision**: By 6 months, then annually
- **Growth**: Plot on Down syndrome-specific growth charts
- **Cervical spine**: X-ray at 3-5 years (atlantoaxial instability screening)
- **Hematologic**: CBC at birth (TMD screening)
- **Sleep study**: Screen for obstructive sleep apnea

### Early Intervention
- Physiotherapy (hypotonia, motor delay)
- Speech therapy (language delay)
- Occupational therapy
- Special education → inclusive education
- Cognitive stimulation

### Associated Condition Management
- Cardiac surgery for CHD
- Levothyroxine for hypothyroidism
- ALL: standard chemotherapy protocols (good prognosis)
- Atlantoaxial instability: activity restriction, surgical stabilization if symptomatic

## Complications
- Congestive heart failure (from CHD)
- Recurrent infections (immune dysfunction)
- Obstructive sleep apnea
- Cervical cord compression (atlantoaxial instability)
- Early-onset Alzheimer disease (by age 40-50)
- Leukemia (ALL)
- Obesity, hypothyroidism
