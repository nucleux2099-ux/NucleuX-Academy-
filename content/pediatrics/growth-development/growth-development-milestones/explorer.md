# Growth and Development Milestones

## Overview
Growth refers to quantitative increase in size (weight, height, head circumference), while development refers to qualitative maturation of function (motor, language, cognitive, social). Understanding normal milestones is essential for early detection of developmental delay. Growth follows a cephalocaudal and proximodistal pattern. Development follows specific principles including cephalocaudal, proximodistal, and general-to-specific progression.

## Key Points
- Growth = quantitative (measurable); Development = qualitative (functional)
- Cephalocaudal (head to toe) and proximodistal (center to periphery) pattern
- Weight doubles by 5 months, triples by 1 year, quadruples by 2 years
- Birth length doubles by 4 years
- Head circumference: 35 cm at birth, 47 cm at 1 year
- Social smile at 2 months is the first social milestone

## Definition & Classification

### Growth Parameters
| Parameter | Birth | 6 months | 1 year | 2 years | 5 years |
|-----------|-------|----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Weight | 3 kg | 6 kg (×2) | 9 kg (×3) | 12 kg (×4) | 18 kg (×6) |
| Length/Height | 50 cm | 67 cm | 75 cm | 87 cm | 110 cm |
| Head circumference | 35 cm | 43 cm | 47 cm | 49 cm | 51 cm |

### Weight Gain Patterns
- First 3 months: 30 g/day (200 g/week)
- 3-6 months: 20 g/day
- 6-12 months: 10-12 g/day
- 1-3 years: 6-8 g/day
- Physiological weight loss: 5-10% in first 3-5 days, regained by 10-14 days

### Height Velocity
- First year: 25 cm/year
- Second year: 12.5 cm/year
- 3-5 years: 7-8 cm/year
- Prepubertal: 5-6 cm/year
- Pubertal: 8-12 cm/year (growth spurt)

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Principles of Development
1. **Cephalocaudal**: Head control → trunk → lower limbs
2. **Proximodistal**: Shoulder → arm → hand → fingers
3. **General to specific**: Mass activity → refined movements
4. **Continuous and sequential**: Predictable order (roll → sit → stand → walk)
5. **Individual variation**: Wide normal range

### Developmental Domains
1. Gross motor
2. Fine motor / vision
3. Language / hearing
4. Social / adaptive / personal

## Diagnosis
- **Growth monitoring**: Serial measurements plotted on WHO/IAP growth charts
- **Developmental assessment**: DDST-II, ASQ, Trivandrum Developmental Screening Chart
- **Developmental quotient (DQ)**: (Developmental age / Chronological age) × 100
- Normal DQ: 85-115; Delay: <70

## Management
- Regular growth monitoring at well-baby visits
- Anticipatory guidance to parents on expected milestones
- Early identification of delay → early intervention
- Referral for specialist evaluation if red flags present

## Complications
- Missed developmental delay if milestones not assessed
- Parental anxiety from normal variations
