# Short Stature in Children

## Overview
Short stature is defined as height below the 3rd percentile or below -2 SD for age and sex on appropriate growth charts. It affects 3% of children by definition. The two most common causes are familial (genetic) short stature and constitutional delay of growth and puberty (CDGP), both being normal variants. Pathological causes include growth hormone deficiency, hypothyroidism, Turner syndrome, chronic diseases, and skeletal dysplasias. Bone age assessment is the most informative single investigation.

## Key Points
- **Definition:** Height < -2 SD or <3rd percentile for age and sex
- **Most common causes:** Familial short stature and constitutional delay (both normal variants)
- **Bone age** — most useful single investigation
- **Growth velocity** — most important parameter for monitoring (normal >5 cm/year after age 4)
- **Turner syndrome** — consider in ALL short girls (karyotype essential)
- **GH deficiency:** Short stature + low growth velocity + delayed bone age + low IGF-1
- **Hypothyroidism** — most common treatable endocrine cause
- **Proportionate vs disproportionate** — key clinical distinction

## Definition & Classification

### Proportionate Short Stature (Normal Body Proportions)
| Category | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Familial (genetic)** | Short parents, normal growth velocity, bone age = chronological age, normal puberty |
| **Constitutional delay (CDGP)** | Family history of delayed puberty, delayed bone age, delayed puberty, catches up later |
| **GH deficiency** | Low growth velocity (<5 cm/year), delayed bone age, central adiposity, frontal bossing |
| **Hypothyroidism** | Coarse features, constipation, delayed reflexes, severely delayed bone age |
| **Chronic disease** | CKD, celiac, Crohn's, CF, CHD, chronic infections |
| **Malnutrition** | Most common cause worldwide |
| **Turner syndrome (45,X)** | Short stature + absent puberty + webbed neck + shield chest |
| **Psychosocial deprivation** | Emotional neglect → functional GH resistance |

### Disproportionate Short Stature (Abnormal Body Proportions)
| Category | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Achondroplasia** | Short limbs (rhizomelic), normal trunk, large head, AD, FGFR3 mutation |
| **Rickets** | Short limbs, widened wrists/ankles, bowed legs |
| **Mucopolysaccharidoses** | Short trunk, coarse features, hepatosplenomegaly |
| **Spondyloepiphyseal dysplasia** | Short trunk |

### Key Differentiation
| Feature | Familial SS | CDGP | GH Deficiency |
|---------|------------|------|---------------|
| Parents' height | Short | Normal/short | Normal |
| Growth velocity | Normal | Normal (slow early) | Low (<5 cm/year) |
| Bone age | = Chronological age | Delayed (= height age) | Delayed |
| Puberty | Normal timing | Delayed | Delayed |
| Final height | Short (within target) | Normal (delayed) | Short without treatment |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### GH-IGF-1 Axis
1. Hypothalamus: GHRH → anterior pituitary: GH release
2. GH → liver → IGF-1 (Insulin-like Growth Factor-1) → growth plate chondrocyte proliferation
3. GH also has direct effects on bone and cartilage
4. **GH deficiency** may be isolated or part of multiple pituitary hormone deficiency
5. Causes: idiopathic (most common), tumors (craniopharyngioma), post-radiation, genetic

### Target Height (Mid-Parental Height)
- **Boys:** (Father's height + Mother's height + 13) / 2 ± 8.5 cm
- **Girls:** (Father's height + Mother's height - 13) / 2 ± 8.5 cm

## Clinical Features

### History
- Birth weight and length, gestational age
- Growth pattern (when did deviation start?)
- Parental heights (target height calculation)
- Family history of delayed puberty (CDGP)
- Nutritional history
- Chronic disease symptoms
- Pubertal development
- Psychosocial history

### Examination
- Accurate height, weight, sitting height (upper:lower segment ratio)
- **Proportionality:** Upper segment:lower segment ratio (increased in short limb; decreased in short trunk)
- **Growth velocity** — plot serial measurements
- Pubertal staging (Tanner)
- Dysmorphic features: Turner (webbed neck, cubitus valgus, shield chest), achondroplasia (trident hand, frontal bossing)
- Thyroid examination
- Fundoscopy (papilledema — craniopharyngioma)

## Diagnosis

### Investigations
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Bone age (X-ray left hand)** | Most informative; delayed in CDGP, GH deficiency, hypothyroidism |
| **Thyroid function (TSH, fT4)** | Rule out hypothyroidism (most common treatable cause) |
| **IGF-1, IGFBP-3** | Screen for GH deficiency |
| **GH stimulation tests** | Confirm GH deficiency (clonidine, insulin, arginine test); GH peak <10 ng/mL = deficient |
| **Karyotype** | ALL short girls → Turner syndrome |
| **Celiac serology** | Anti-tTG IgA |
| **CBC, ESR, CRP** | Chronic disease screening |
| **Renal function** | CKD |
| **MRI brain** | If GH deficiency confirmed → pituitary anatomy (craniopharyngioma, empty sella) |

## Management

### GH Therapy (rhGH)
- **Indications:** GH deficiency, Turner syndrome, CKD, Prader-Willi, SGA without catch-up, idiopathic short stature
- **Dose:** 0.025-0.05 mg/kg/day SC injection (daily, usually at bedtime)
- **Monitor:** Growth velocity, IGF-1 levels, bone age, side effects
- **Continue until:** Growth velocity <2 cm/year or bone age maturity

### Specific Treatment
| Cause | Treatment |
|-------|-----------|
| Hypothyroidism | Levothyroxine |
| Celiac disease | Gluten-free diet |
| CDGP | Reassurance; short course testosterone/estrogen in adolescence if needed |
| Turner syndrome | GH + estrogen replacement at puberty |
| Chronic disease | Treat underlying condition |
| Psychosocial | Address emotional neglect |

## Complications
- **Psychosocial:** Low self-esteem, bullying, social isolation
- **Delayed puberty** and its psychological impact
- **Missed pathology** if normal variants assumed without investigation
- **GH therapy side effects:** Benign intracranial hypertension, slipped capital femoral epiphysis, scoliosis progression, rare malignancy concern
