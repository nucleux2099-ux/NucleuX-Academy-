# Short Stature — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Height < -2 SD or <3rd percentile. Most common: familial SS and CDGP (normal variants). Bone age = most useful investigation. Growth velocity <5 cm/year after age 4 = abnormal. GH deficiency: low velocity + delayed bone age + low IGF-1. Karyotype ALL short girls (Turner). Hypothyroidism = most common treatable cause. Target height from mid-parental formula.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Bone age** = most informative single investigation
- ★ **Growth velocity <5 cm/year** (after age 4) = pathological
- ★ **Familial SS:** Normal velocity, bone age = chronological age, short parents
- ★ **CDGP:** Delayed bone age, delayed puberty, normal final height
- ★ **GH stimulation test:** Peak GH <10 ng/mL = deficient
- ★ **Karyotype ALL short girls** → Turner syndrome (45,X)
- ★ **Hypothyroidism** = most common treatable endocrine cause
- ★ **Craniopharyngioma** = most common pituitary tumor causing GH deficiency in children
- ★ **Achondroplasia** = FGFR3 mutation, rhizomelic shortening, AD
- ★ **Turner features:** Webbed neck, shield chest, cubitus valgus, absent puberty
- ★ **Target height:** (Father + Mother ± 13) / 2

## Mnemonics

### **SHORT** — Causes
- **S**keletal dysplasia (achondroplasia)
- **H**ormone (GH, thyroid)
- **O**rgan disease (CKD, celiac, CF)
- **R**eally familial / constitutional
- **T**urner syndrome / chromosomal

### Bone Age Clue:
- Bone age = chronological → **Familial SS**
- Bone age = height age (delayed) → **CDGP** or **endocrine**

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most useful investigation** — Bone age (X-ray left hand)
2. **Normal variant causes** — Familial SS and CDGP
3. **Bone age in CDGP** — Delayed (= height age)
4. **GH deficiency confirmation** — GH stimulation test (<10 ng/mL)
5. **Karyotype in short girls** — Rule out Turner (45,X)
6. **Most common treatable cause** — Hypothyroidism
7. **Achondroplasia** — FGFR3, AD, rhizomelic limb shortening
8. **GH therapy indications** — GH deficiency, Turner, CKD, SGA, Prader-Willi
9. **Target height formula** — (F + M ± 13) / 2
10. **Craniopharyngioma** — Calcification on CT, visual field defects, GH deficiency
