# Pediatric Obesity — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
BMI ≥95th percentile = obese. Exogenous (>95%): tall for age, normal development. Endogenous (<5%): short for age (hypothyroidism, Cushing, Prader-Willi). Acanthosis nigricans = insulin resistance marker. NAFLD = most common liver disease. Screen: fasting glucose, lipids, ALT, TFT. Treatment: lifestyle modification (5-2-1-0 rule). Pharmacotherapy ≥12 years if severe.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Exogenous obesity = TALL** for age; **Endogenous = SHORT** for age
- ★ **Acanthosis nigricans** = insulin resistance marker (neck, axillae)
- ★ **NAFLD** = most common liver disease in obese children
- ★ **Prader-Willi:** Hyperphagia + hypotonia + hypogonadism + intellectual disability
- ★ **Purple striae** = Cushing syndrome; pink striae = simple obesity
- ★ **SCFE** = slipped capital femoral epiphysis — suspect in obese adolescent with hip/knee pain
- ★ **Metabolic syndrome** components measurable in children
- ★ **5-2-1-0 rule** — practical lifestyle prescription
- ★ **Type 2 DM** increasingly common in obese adolescents
- ★ **Family-based approach** = most effective behavioral intervention

## Mnemonics

### Exogenous vs Endogenous: "ExoTall, EndoShort"
- Exogenous: Tall, normal IQ, >95% of cases
- Endogenous: Short, may have ↓IQ, syndrome features

### **5-2-1-0** Rule
- **5** fruits/vegetables, **2** hours max screen, **1** hour exercise, **0** sugary drinks

### Prader-Willi: "H³I"
- **H**yperphagia, **H**ypotonia, **H**ypogonadism, **I**ntellectual disability

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **BMI criteria** — ≥95th = obese, 85-95th = overweight
2. **Tall vs short** — Exogenous (tall), Endogenous (short)
3. **Acanthosis nigricans** — Insulin resistance
4. **NAFLD screening** — ALT
5. **Prader-Willi** — Hyperphagia, chromosome 15
6. **Purple striae** — Cushing (not simple obesity)
7. **SCFE** — Obese adolescent with hip/knee pain
8. **First-line treatment** — Lifestyle modification
9. **Drug for ≥12 years** — Orlistat (lipase inhibitor)
10. **Metabolic syndrome** — Central obesity + dyslipidemia + HTN + hyperglycemia
