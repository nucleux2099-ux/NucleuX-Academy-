# Pediatric Obesity

## Overview
Pediatric obesity is a growing epidemic affecting 5-20% of children in India and is defined as BMI ≥95th percentile for age and sex. It is predominantly due to exogenous (dietary and lifestyle) factors in >95% of cases. Childhood obesity tracks into adulthood and is associated with metabolic syndrome, type 2 diabetes, NAFLD, PCOS, and cardiovascular disease. Prevention through lifestyle modification is the cornerstone; pharmacotherapy and bariatric surgery are reserved for severe cases.

## Key Points
- **Definition:** BMI ≥95th percentile for age and sex (2-18 years)
- **Overweight:** BMI 85th-95th percentile
- **Exogenous (simple) obesity:** >95% of cases — tall for age, normal development
- **Endogenous causes:** Hypothyroidism, Cushing, Prader-Willi, Laurence-Moon-Biedl — short stature
- **Key distinguishing feature:** Exogenous = tall; Endogenous = short
- **Metabolic syndrome** components present in obese children
- **Acanthosis nigricans** — marker of insulin resistance
- **NAFLD** — most common liver disease in obese children
- **Lifestyle modification** — first-line treatment at all severity levels

## Definition & Classification

### BMI Classification (2-18 years)
| Category | BMI Percentile | IAP Criteria |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Normal | 5th-85th | — |
| Overweight | 85th-95th | Adult equivalent BMI 23 |
| Obese | ≥95th | Adult equivalent BMI 27 |
| Severely obese | ≥99th or ≥120% of 95th | — |

### Exogenous vs Endogenous
| Feature | Exogenous (95%) | Endogenous (5%) |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Height | **Tall for age** | **Short for age** |
| Intelligence | Normal | May be impaired |
| Bone age | Advanced or normal | Delayed |
| Family history | Obese family | May have specific features |
| Development | Normal | May be delayed |
| Exam findings | Acanthosis nigricans | Specific syndrome features |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Causes
- **Exogenous:** Excess calorie intake, sedentary lifestyle, screen time, sugar-sweetened beverages
- **Genetic:** Polygenic (family predisposition); monogenic (leptin, MC4R mutations — rare)
- **Endocrine:** Hypothyroidism, Cushing syndrome, GH deficiency, pseudohypoparathyroidism
- **Syndromic:** Prader-Willi (hyperphagia, hypogonadism, hypotonia), Laurence-Moon-Biedl (retinitis pigmentosa, polydactyly), Beckwith-Wiedemann
- **Drugs:** Steroids, valproate, insulin, antipsychotics

### Pathophysiology
1. Energy imbalance: Intake > expenditure → adipose tissue expansion
2. Adipocyte hypertrophy → inflammation → insulin resistance
3. ↑Insulin resistance → hyperinsulinemia → metabolic syndrome
4. Leptin resistance (despite high leptin levels) → persistent appetite
5. Adipose tissue is endocrine organ: secretes adipokines (leptin, adiponectin, TNF-α, IL-6)

## Clinical Features

### Examination Findings
- BMI ≥95th percentile; weight significantly above ideal
- **Acanthosis nigricans** — dark, velvety skin (neck, axillae, groin) = insulin resistance
- **Striae** (stretch marks) — purple striae suggest Cushing; pink striae common in simple obesity
- **Gynecomastia** in boys
- **Hepatomegaly** (NAFLD)
- Advanced puberty or pseudoprecocious puberty (peripheral aromatization)
- Tall stature (exogenous) or short stature (endocrine/syndromic)

### Comorbidities to Screen For
- **Metabolic syndrome:** Central obesity + 2 of: ↑TG, ↓HDL, ↑BP, ↑fasting glucose
- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus** — increasingly common in obese adolescents
- **NAFLD** — elevated ALT; most common liver disease in obese children
- **Dyslipidemia** — ↑LDL, ↑TG, ↓HDL
- **Hypertension**
- **Obstructive sleep apnea** — snoring, daytime somnolence
- **Orthopedic:** SCFE (slipped capital femoral epiphysis), Blount disease (tibia vara)
- **PCOS** in adolescent girls
- **Depression, low self-esteem, bullying**
- **Pseudotumor cerebri** (idiopathic intracranial hypertension)

## Diagnosis

### Assessment
- BMI calculation and plotting on age/sex-specific growth charts
- Waist circumference (central obesity marker)
- Blood pressure measurement
- Detailed dietary and activity history
- Family history of obesity, diabetes, CVD

### Investigations
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Fasting glucose, HbA1c | Prediabetes, T2DM |
| Fasting lipid profile | Dyslipidemia |
| Liver function (ALT) | NAFLD screening |
| Thyroid function | Rule out hypothyroidism |
| Fasting insulin | Insulin resistance (HOMA-IR) |
| Cortisol | If Cushing suspected |
| Ultrasound liver | NAFLD (hepatic steatosis) |
| Sleep study | If OSA symptoms |

## Management

### Lifestyle Modification (Foundation for ALL)
- **5-2-1-0 Rule:**
  - **5** servings fruits/vegetables per day
  - **≤2** hours screen time per day
  - **≥1** hour physical activity per day
  - **0** sugar-sweetened beverages
- **Family-based approach** — entire family participates
- **Behavioral counseling** — motivational interviewing
- **Goal:** Weight maintenance (in growing child) or modest weight loss in severe cases

### Pharmacotherapy (Adjunct — Severe Obesity, >12 Years)
- **Orlistat** (lipase inhibitor) — approved for ≥12 years
- **Metformin** — for insulin resistance/prediabetes (off-label for obesity)
- **GLP-1 agonists (liraglutide)** — approved for ≥12 years with obesity

### Bariatric Surgery
- Consider in severely obese adolescents (BMI ≥40 or ≥35 with comorbidities)
- After failed lifestyle + pharmacotherapy
- Skeletal maturity (Tanner 4-5)
- Sleeve gastrectomy preferred

## Complications
- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus**
- **Cardiovascular disease** (atherosclerosis begins in childhood)
- **NAFLD → NASH → cirrhosis**
- **Hypertension**
- **Dyslipidemia**
- **PCOS**
- **Obstructive sleep apnea**
- **SCFE and Blount disease**
- **Pseudotumor cerebri**
- **Psychosocial:** Depression, poor self-esteem, bullying, eating disorders
