# Failure to Thrive (FTT)

## Overview
Failure to thrive (FTT) refers to inadequate physical growth in infants and young children, typically defined as weight below the 3rd percentile for age, weight-for-length below the 3rd percentile, or a downward crossing of ≥2 major percentile lines on the growth chart. It is a description, not a diagnosis. The most common cause worldwide is inadequate caloric intake, and in India, it is closely linked to poverty and malnutrition.

## Key Points
- Weight is affected FIRST, then length, then head circumference
- Most common cause: inadequate caloric intake (organic + non-organic)
- Non-organic (psychosocial) > organic causes
- Weight <3rd percentile or crossing ≥2 major percentile lines
- Detailed dietary history is the most important diagnostic tool
- Management: nutritional rehabilitation + address underlying cause

## Definition & Classification

### Criteria for FTT
1. Weight <3rd percentile for age on repeated measurements
2. Weight-for-length <3rd percentile
3. Weight deceleration crossing ≥2 major percentile lines
4. Weight <80% of ideal weight for age (Gomez classification overlap)

### Pattern of Growth Failure
| Pattern | Affected | Likely Cause |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Weight only | Weight ↓, Length N, HC N | Acute malnutrition / inadequate intake |
| Weight + Length | Weight ↓, Length ↓, HC N | Chronic malnutrition / endocrine |
| Weight + Length + HC | All ↓ | Genetic / chromosomal / TORCH / structural brain |

### Classification
- **Non-organic (psychosocial)**: 80% of cases — poverty, neglect, feeding difficulties, maternal depression
- **Organic**: 20% — underlying medical condition
- **Mixed**: Both components

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Non-Organic Causes
- Inadequate food availability (poverty)
- Incorrect formula preparation (over-dilution)
- Poor feeding practices, food fads
- Maternal depression, substance abuse
- Child neglect or abuse
- Dysfunctional parent-child interaction

### Organic Causes — By Mechanism
**Inadequate Intake:**
- Cleft palate, Pierre Robin sequence
- GERD, oral aversion
- CNS disease (cerebral palsy, mental retardation)

**Malabsorption:**
- Celiac disease, cystic fibrosis
- Cow's milk protein allergy
- Short bowel syndrome, chronic diarrhea

**Increased Demands:**
- Congenital heart disease
- Chronic lung disease (BPD)
- Chronic infections (TB, HIV)
- Malignancy
- Hyperthyroidism

**Impaired Utilization:**
- Inborn errors of metabolism
- Chronic renal failure
- Chronic liver disease

**Endocrine:**
- Hypothyroidism, growth hormone deficiency
- Diabetes mellitus

## Clinical Features
- Poor weight gain or weight loss
- Irritability or apathy
- Developmental delay (if prolonged)
- Decreased subcutaneous fat
- Muscle wasting
- Thin, sparse hair
- Signs of specific deficiencies (iron, zinc, vitamins)
- Signs of neglect: poor hygiene, diaper rash, flat occiput

### Red Flags for Organic Cause
- Vomiting, diarrhea, dysphagia
- Dysmorphic features
- Hepatosplenomegaly
- Recurrent infections
- Specific organ involvement

## Diagnosis
1. **Growth chart**: Plot weight, length, HC serially — the MOST important tool
2. **Dietary history**: Detailed 24-hour recall, feeding pattern, formula preparation
3. **Social history**: Family dynamics, poverty, maternal mental health
4. **Basic labs**: CBC, ESR, CRP, electrolytes, RFT, LFT, urine analysis, stool exam
5. **Targeted investigations**: Celiac screen, sweat chloride, thyroid, IGF-1 as indicated
6. **Bone age**: If short stature component
7. **Calorie count**: Calculate actual vs required caloric intake

### Caloric Requirements
- 0-6 months: 108 kcal/kg/day
- 6-12 months: 98 kcal/kg/day
- 1-3 years: ~100 kcal/kg/day
- For catch-up growth: 150% of expected calories for IDEAL weight (not current)

## Management

### Nutritional Rehabilitation
- Calculate calories for catch-up growth based on IDEAL weight for current length
- Catch-up calories = RDA × (ideal weight / actual weight) × 1.5
- Increase caloric density of feeds (add oil, sugar, milk powder)
- Frequent small meals (5-6/day)
- Micronutrient supplementation (iron, zinc, multivitamins)

### Non-Organic FTT
- Parental education on feeding practices
- Improve parent-child interaction
- Address maternal depression
- Social support, poverty alleviation programs
- Home visits, community health worker involvement

### Organic FTT
- Treat underlying condition
- Disease-specific nutrition (e.g., gluten-free for celiac)
- Specialist referral as needed

### Monitoring
- Weekly weight for first month
- Monthly weight, length, HC thereafter
- Developmental assessment
- Target: weight gain 20-30 g/day (infants), 10-15 g/day (1-3 years)

## Complications
- Growth stunting (irreversible if prolonged)
- Developmental delay, cognitive impairment
- Behavioral problems
- Immune dysfunction
- In severe cases: marasmus/kwashiorkor
- Long-term: adult chronic diseases (Barker hypothesis)
