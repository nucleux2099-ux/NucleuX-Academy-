# Failure to Thrive — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Weight <3rd percentile or crossing ≥2 percentile lines. Weight affected first, then length, then HC. Non-organic (80%) > organic. Most common cause: inadequate caloric intake. Management: nutritional rehabilitation with catch-up calories.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Weight affected FIRST → then length → then head circumference (last)
- ★ Non-organic causes (psychosocial) = 80% of FTT
- ★ Most common cause worldwide = inadequate caloric intake
- ★ Catch-up calories = 150% of RDA calculated for IDEAL weight (not actual)
- ★ All 3 parameters affected (W+L+HC) → think genetic/chromosomal/TORCH
- ★ Dietary history is the single most important diagnostic tool
- ★ Formula over-dilution is a common cause in formula-fed infants
- ★ Celiac disease: important organic cause to screen for
- ★ Target weight gain: 20-30 g/day in infants during catch-up
- ★ Maternal depression is an important non-organic cause

## Mnemonics
**Growth failure pattern — "WLH" (order of involvement):**
- **W**eight first (acute)
- **L**ength second (chronic)
- **H**ead circumference last (genetic/structural)

**Organic causes by mechanism — "IAMIE":**
- **I**nadequate intake (cleft, CNS)
- **A**bsorption poor (celiac, CF)
- **M**etabolic (IEM)
- **I**ncreased demands (CHD, chronic infection)
- **E**ndocrine (hypothyroid, GHD)
