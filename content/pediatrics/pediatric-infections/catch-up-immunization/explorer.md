# Catch-Up Immunization

## Overview
Catch-up immunization refers to the administration of missed or delayed vaccine doses to bring a child's immunization status up to date. It is crucial for children who missed vaccines due to illness, migration, conflict, natural disasters, or parental hesitancy. The fundamental principle is: "every dose counts" — an interrupted schedule should be resumed, never restarted. Minimum intervals between doses must be maintained, and maximum age limits for certain vaccines must be respected.

## Key Points
- **Never restart** an interrupted schedule — resume from where left off
- **Every dose counts** — previously administered valid doses are always counted
- **Minimum intervals** between doses must be maintained
- **Maximum age limits:** OPV booster ≤5 years; DPT ≤7 years; Rotavirus varies by brand
- **Multiple vaccines** can be given at the same visit (different sites)
- **Live-live interval:** ≥4 weeks if not given simultaneously
- **No maximum interval** — even if years have passed, resume (don't restart)
- **Use combination vaccines** when possible to reduce visits
- **Mission Indradhanush** — India's catch-up immunization drive

## Definition & Classification

### Types of Catch-Up Scenarios
| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|---------|
| **Partially immunized** | Identify missing doses; give as per catch-up schedule |
| **Unimmunized (no record)** | Assume no prior vaccination; start from current age-appropriate schedule |
| **Unknown status (lost card)** | If uncertain, revaccinate (better to give extra dose than miss one) |
| **Late start** | Begin immediately; accelerated schedule with minimum intervals |

### Key Minimum Intervals
| Vaccine | Minimum Interval Between Doses |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| Pentavalent/DPT | 4 weeks (between primary doses) |
| OPV/IPV | 4 weeks |
| Hepatitis B | 4 weeks (dose 1-2); 8 weeks (dose 2-3); 16 weeks (dose 1-3) |
| MR/MMR | 4 weeks between 2 doses |
| PCV | 4-8 weeks (depends on age at start) |

### Maximum Age Limits
| Vaccine | Maximum Age |
|---------|-------------|
| DPT (whole cell pertussis) | 7 years (switch to Td/Tdap after) |
| OPV booster | 5 years |
| Rotavirus | Brand-specific (last dose by 8 months for some) |
| BCG | Can be given up to 5 years (IAP); beyond 5 — do Mantoux first |
| Hib | Not needed after 5 years |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Why Children Miss Vaccines
- Migration/displacement
- Illness at time of scheduled visit
- Parental hesitancy/refusal
- Supply chain disruptions
- Limited health facility access
- Natural disasters, conflicts
- Incomplete awareness
- Side effects of previous dose (perceived or real)

### Immunological Basis
- Immunological memory persists despite delayed boosters
- Late boosters still produce anamnestic (memory) response
- Primary series provides foundational immunity even if boosters delayed
- Antibody levels may wane but memory B/T cells remain

## Clinical Features
Not applicable — catch-up immunization is a preventive intervention. Clinical features of vaccine-preventable diseases (measles, diphtheria, pertussis, etc.) serve as the reason for timely catch-up.

## Diagnosis

### Assessment of Immunization Status
1. Review immunization card/records
2. If no card: maternal recall (less reliable)
3. If no information: Consider unimmunized
4. Check: Which vaccines given, dates, number of doses, intervals
5. Compare with age-appropriate NIS schedule

### Tools
- **Immunization register** at health facility
- **MCTS/RCH portal** — electronic tracking
- **eVIN** — vaccine stock and temperature monitoring

## Management

### IAP Catch-Up Schedule (Principles)

#### For Previously Unimmunized Children (Starting Late)

**Age 0-12 months:** Follow regular NIS schedule from current age, maintaining minimum intervals

**Age 1-5 years (unimmunized):**
| Visit | Vaccines |
|-------|----------|
| Visit 1 | BCG, OPV-1, IPV-1, Pentavalent-1, PCV-1, MR-1 |
| Visit 2 (4 weeks later) | OPV-2, IPV-2, Pentavalent-2, PCV-2, Rotavirus (if <8 months) |
| Visit 3 (4 weeks later) | OPV-3, IPV-3, Pentavalent-3 |
| Visit 4 (4-8 weeks later) | MR-2, DPT booster (if ≥16 months and >6 months after Penta-3) |

**Age >7 years (unimmunized):**
| Visit | Vaccines |
|-------|----------|
| Visit 1 | Td-1, IPV-1, MR-1, HepB-1 |
| Visit 2 (4 weeks) | Td-2, IPV-2, MR-2, HepB-2 |
| Visit 3 (6 months) | Td-3, IPV-3, HepB-3 |
| Boosters | Td every 10 years |

### Key Rules
1. **Never restart** — count all valid previous doses
2. **Multiple vaccines same day** — give at different sites; no limit on number
3. **Live-live gap:** ≥4 weeks (if not given same day)
4. **No live-inactivated gap** requirement
5. **DPT after age 7** → use Td or Tdap (NO whole-cell pertussis)
6. **BCG after age 5** → Mantoux test first (IAP recommendation)
7. **Use catch-up opportunity** — give ALL due vaccines at every visit

### Mission Indradhanush
- Government of India initiative (2014)
- Targets underserved and hard-to-reach populations
- Intensified campaigns (IMI 2.0, 3.0, 4.0)
- Goal: ≥90% full immunization coverage

## Complications
- **Risk of vaccine-preventable disease** — main risk of delayed/missed vaccination
- **Increased AEFI** with multiple simultaneous vaccines — generally minor
- **Community risk** — reduced herd immunity with low coverage
- **Outbreak potential** — measles, diphtheria outbreaks in under-immunized populations
