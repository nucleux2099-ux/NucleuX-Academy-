# Scarlet Fever — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
GAS pharyngitis + erythrogenic toxin = scarlet fever. Age 5-15. Sandpaper rash (neck→generalizes, spares palms/soles). Pastia lines (petechiae in skin folds). Strawberry tongue (white→red). Circumoral pallor. Desquamation in recovery (fingertip peeling). Rx: Penicillin × 10 days. Prevents RF but NOT PSGN.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Sandpaper rash** = pathognomonic texture; fine erythematous blanching papules
- ★ **Pastia lines** = linear petechiae in skin folds (persist after rash fades)
- ★ **Strawberry tongue:** White → Red (as coating peels)
- ★ **Circumoral pallor** = flushed cheeks + pale perioral area
- ★ **Desquamation** = sheet-like peeling of fingertips in recovery phase
- ★ **Erythrogenic toxin** (superantigen) causes the rash
- ★ **Penicillin** = drug of choice (prevents RF, shortens illness)
- ★ **Antibiotics prevent RF but NOT PSGN**
- ★ **No cough/rhinorrhea** = GAS vs viral pharyngitis clue
- ★ **RADT** = rapid, specific test for GAS

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Pathognomonic rash** — Sandpaper texture
2. **Pastia lines** — Petechiae in skin folds
3. **Strawberry tongue** — White then red
4. **Circumoral pallor** — Present
5. **Desquamation timing** — 1-3 weeks after rash
6. **Drug of choice** — Penicillin V × 10 days
7. **RF prevention** — Yes (antibiotics work)
8. **PSGN prevention** — No (antibiotics don't prevent)
9. **Toxin type** — Erythrogenic/pyrogenic exotoxin (SPE)
10. **GAS clue** — NO cough, NO rhinorrhea
