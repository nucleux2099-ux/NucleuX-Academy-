# AEFI — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
AEFI = any adverse event temporally associated with immunization. 5 WHO categories: product-related, quality defect, error, anxiety, coincidental. Most common: local reactions + fever. Most serious: anaphylaxis. VAPP (OPV), BCG dissemination, intussusception (rotavirus) = important serious AEFIs. Anaphylaxis Rx: IM adrenaline 0.01 mg/kg. 30-min observation post-vaccination.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **IM Adrenaline** = first and most important drug for anaphylaxis
- ★ **Anaphylaxis dose:** 0.01 mL/kg of 1:1000 IM (max 0.3 mL child)
- ★ **VAPP** = 1 in 2.4 million OPV doses; onset 4-30 days
- ★ **Intussusception** from rotavirus vaccine — within 7 days of first dose
- ★ **BCG lymphadenitis** = most common serious BCG AEFI; aspirate, don't incise
- ★ **MMR:** Fever + rash at 5-12 days (expected); ITP 1 in 25,000
- ★ **DPT (whole cell):** Most reactogenic NIS vaccine
- ★ **30-minute observation** after every vaccination
- ★ **5 WHO AEFI categories:** Product, quality, error, anxiety, coincidental
- ★ **Report:** Death, hospitalization, clusters, significant public concern

## Mnemonics

### AEFI Categories: "PQ-EAC"
- **P**roduct-related
- **Q**uality defect
- **E**rror-related
- **A**nxiety-related
- **C**oincidental

### Anaphylaxis: "AAIO" (Adrenaline, Airway, IV fluids, Observe)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **First drug for anaphylaxis** — IM Adrenaline (epinephrine)
2. **Adrenaline dose** — 0.01 mg/kg of 1:1000 IM
3. **VAPP** — OPV complication, flaccid paralysis
4. **Intussusception** — Rotavirus vaccine
5. **BCG lymphadenitis management** — Aspirate, don't incise
6. **MMR reaction timing** — 5-12 days (fever, rash)
7. **Most reactogenic NIS vaccine** — DPT (whole cell pertussis)
8. **Observation time** — 30 minutes post-vaccination
9. **5 AEFI categories** — Know all five
10. **Route of adrenaline** — IM (NOT subcutaneous) in anterolateral thigh
