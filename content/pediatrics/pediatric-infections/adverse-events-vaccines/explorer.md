# Adverse Events Following Immunization (AEFI)

## Overview
An AEFI is any untoward medical occurrence following immunization that does not necessarily have a causal relationship with the vaccine. India's AEFI surveillance system monitors and classifies these events to maintain vaccine safety and public confidence. Most AEFIs are minor (local reactions, fever) and self-limiting. Serious AEFIs are rare but require prompt recognition, reporting, and management. Understanding AEFI classification, causality assessment, and management of anaphylaxis is critical.

## Key Points
- **AEFI** = any adverse event temporally associated with immunization
- **Most common AEFI:** Local reactions (pain, swelling) and fever
- **Most serious:** Anaphylaxis (any vaccine, rare — ~1 per million doses)
- **5 WHO categories:** Vaccine product-related, quality defect, immunization error, anxiety-related, coincidental
- **VAPP:** OPV — 1 in 2.4 million doses (reason for IPV inclusion)
- **BCG lymphadenitis:** Ipsilateral axillary, within 2-6 months — most common serious BCG AEFI
- **Intussusception:** Rotavirus vaccine — rare, within 7 days of first dose
- **Observation period:** 30 minutes post-vaccination recommended
- **Report all serious AEFIs** to district immunization officer

## Definition & Classification

### WHO Classification of AEFI
| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Vaccine product-related** | Inherent properties of vaccine | VAPP (OPV), BCG dissemination, febrile seizures (DPT) |
| **Vaccine quality defect** | Manufacturing/storage problem | Contamination, improper inactivation |
| **Immunization error** | Wrong preparation/handling/administration | Wrong dose, wrong route, unsterile needle, wrong reconstitution |
| **Immunization anxiety-related** | Stress response to injection | Vasovagal syncope, hyperventilation, group hysteria |
| **Coincidental** | Temporally associated but unrelated | SIDS, seizure disorder presenting near vaccination age |

### Severity Classification
| Severity | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| **Minor** | Local reaction, fever <39°C, irritability — self-limiting |
| **Severe** | High fever, large local reaction, persistent crying >3 hours |
| **Serious** | Death, hospitalization, disability, life-threatening (anaphylaxis) |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Common AEFIs by Vaccine
| Vaccine | Common Minor | Rare Serious |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **BCG** | Local ulcer, papule (normal) | Disseminated BCG, lymphadenitis, abscess |
| **DPT (whole cell)** | Fever, local pain, irritability | Encephalopathy, HHE (hypotonic-hyporesponsive episode) |
| **OPV** | Mild diarrhea | VAPP (1 in 2.4 million) |
| **MMR/MR** | Fever, rash (5-12 days) | Febrile seizures, ITP (1 in 25,000), anaphylaxis |
| **Hepatitis B** | Local pain | Anaphylaxis (rare) |
| **Rotavirus** | Irritability, mild diarrhea | Intussusception (1 in 20,000-100,000) |
| **PCV** | Local reactions, fever | — |
| **Pentavalent** | Fever, local reaction | Rare serious events (same as DPT components) |

### Anaphylaxis — Mechanism and Recognition
- **Type I hypersensitivity** — IgE mediated, within minutes
- Components causing allergy: egg protein (influenza, MMR), gelatin, neomycin, yeast (HepB)
- **Clinical:** Urticaria, angioedema, bronchospasm, hypotension, cardiovascular collapse
- **Timeline:** Usually within 15-30 minutes of vaccination

## Clinical Features

### Minor Reactions (Common)
- Local: Pain, erythema, swelling at injection site (24-48 hours)
- Systemic: Low-grade fever, irritability, poor feeding (24-72 hours)
- BCG: Expected papule → ulcer → scar over 6-8 weeks

### Moderate Reactions
- High fever (>39°C)
- Large local reaction (>50 mm)
- Persistent inconsolable crying >3 hours (DPT)
- Febrile seizures

### Serious Reactions
- **Anaphylaxis:** Onset within minutes; wheeze, stridor, hypotension, urticaria
- **VAPP:** Onset 4-30 days post-OPV; flaccid paralysis
- **BCG dissemination:** In severe immunodeficiency
- **Encephalopathy:** Within 72 hours of DPT (controversial; very rare)
- **Intussusception:** Within 7 days of rotavirus vaccine; colicky pain, bloody stools

## Diagnosis

### AEFI Reporting Criteria (India)
Report if:
- Death
- Hospitalization
- Cluster of events (2+ from same session)
- Cluster of injection site reactions
- Any event causing significant parental/public concern

### Causality Assessment (WHO)
- Consistent causal association (temporal + biological plausibility + no alternative)
- Indeterminate
- Inconsistent with causal association (coincidental)
- Unclassifiable

## Management

### Anaphylaxis Management (Emergency)
1. **IM Adrenaline (Epinephrine)** — FIRST and most important drug
   - Dose: 0.01 mL/kg of 1:1000 (0.01 mg/kg) IM anterolateral thigh
   - Max: 0.3 mL (child), 0.5 mL (adult)
   - Repeat every 5-15 minutes if needed
2. **Position:** Supine, legs elevated
3. **Oxygen** and airway support
4. **IV fluids** (NS 20 mL/kg bolus) for hypotension
5. **Adjuncts:** Antihistamines (chlorpheniramine), hydrocortisone (prevent biphasic reaction)
6. **Observe ≥4 hours** after treatment

### Minor AEFI Management
- Local reactions: Cold compress, paracetamol
- Fever: Paracetamol (NOT aspirin)
- Reassurance — these are expected and self-limiting

### BCG Lymphadenitis
- Small (<3 cm): Observe
- Large/fluctuant/abscess: Aspiration (do NOT incise); no anti-TB drugs needed

### Prevention
- **Screen for contraindications** before vaccination
- **30-minute observation** after every vaccination
- **Emergency kit** at every vaccination site (adrenaline, syringes)
- Proper cold chain maintenance
- Correct reconstitution and administration technique

## Complications
- **Death** from anaphylaxis (if untreated) — extremely rare
- **Permanent disability** — VAPP (paralysis)
- **Vaccine hesitancy** — social consequence of publicized AEFIs
- **Injection abscess** (immunization error — poor technique)
