# Travel Vaccines — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Pre-travel consultation 4-6 weeks before. First update routine vaccines. Hepatitis A = most common travel VPD. Yellow Fever certificate required for many countries (lifetime validity). Meningococcal ACWY required for Hajj. Rabies pre-exposure for dog-endemic areas. Typhoid for South Asia. JE for Asia-Pacific. Non-vaccine: malaria prophylaxis, food/water safety, insect prevention.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Hepatitis A** = most common travel-acquired VPD
- ★ **Yellow Fever vaccine** = required by IHR; certificate valid for life
- ★ **Yellow Fever age** = ≥9 months (live attenuated vaccine)
- ★ **Meningococcal ACWY** = required for Hajj/Umrah; meningitis belt of Africa
- ★ **Rabies pre-exposure:** 3 doses (0, 7, 21-28 days); still need post-exposure if bitten
- ★ **Typhoid conjugate vaccine** effective from ≥6 months (vs Vi polysaccharide ≥2 years)
- ★ **Consultation timing** = 4-6 weeks before travel
- ★ **Yellow Fever viscerotropic disease** = rare but potentially fatal complication
- ★ **Malaria** — no vaccine in routine use; chemoprophylaxis essential
- ★ **First step** = update routine immunizations

## Mnemonics

### Travel Vaccine Check: **"RHYTHM"**
- **R**abies (animal contact risk)

- **M**alaria prophylaxis (not vaccine)
