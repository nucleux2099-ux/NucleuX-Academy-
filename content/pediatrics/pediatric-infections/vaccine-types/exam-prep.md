# Vaccine Types — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Live attenuated (BCG, OPV, MMR, Rotavirus, Varicella): strong response, fewer doses, contraindicated in immunodeficiency. Inactivated (IPV, HepA, Rabies): safe in immunocompromised, needs boosters. Conjugate (PCV, Hib): T-dependent, effective <2 years. Polysaccharide: T-independent, poor <2 years. Toxoid: DPT. Recombinant: HepB. Live vaccines need 4-week gap.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Conjugate vs polysaccharide:** Conjugate works <2 years (T-dependent); polysaccharide doesn't
- ★ **Live vaccines contraindicated** in immunodeficiency
- ★ **Live vaccines: 4-week gap** if not given simultaneously
- ★ **Freeze-sensitive:** DPT, HepB, IPV, Pentavalent, Td (destroyed by freezing)
- ★ **OPV stored at -20°C** (only frozen vaccine)
- ★ **BCG** = only intradermal vaccine
- ★ **VAPP** = OPV complication (1 in 2.4 million) → reason for IPV
- ★ **DTwP vs DTaP:** Whole cell more reactogenic; acellular less so
- ★ **HepB** = recombinant (HBsAg) — NOT live, NOT killed whole virus
- ★ **Interrupted schedule:** Resume, do NOT restart

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Conjugate vs polysaccharide** — Conjugate effective in <2 years
2. **Live vaccine contraindication** — Immunodeficiency
3. **Freeze-sensitive vaccines** — DPT, HepB, IPV, Pentavalent
4. **OPV storage** — -20°C
5. **Only intradermal vaccine** — BCG
6. **VAPP** — OPV (live oral polio)
7. **HepB vaccine type** — Recombinant (HBsAg)
8. **DTwP vs DTaP** — Whole cell more side effects
9. **4-week gap rule** — Between 2 live vaccines (if not simultaneous)
10. **Interrupted schedule** — Resume, don't restart
