# Pneumonia (Infections) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
#1 infectious killer of <5 children. S. pneumoniae = top bacterial; RSV = top viral. WHO tachypnea: 60/50/40. Non-severe: oral amoxicillin. Severe: IV ampicillin + gentamicin. Mycoplasma >5y: azithromycin. PCP in HIV: cotrimoxazole. Prevention: PCV, Hib, breastfeeding, zinc. Empyema = most common complication.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **WHO tachypnea:** <2m: 60, 2-12m: 50, 1-5y: 40
- ★ **S. pneumoniae** = #1 bacterial cause all ages
- ★ **Amoxicillin** = first-line non-severe
- ★ **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** = first-line severe
- ★ **Pneumatoceles** = S. aureus
- ★ **Staccato cough + eosinophilia** = C. trachomatis infant
- ★ **SpO₂ <90%** = needs oxygen
- ★ **PCV** = most effective preventive intervention
- ★ **Breastfeeding** reduces pneumonia by 15-23%
- ★ **IMNCI danger signs:** Can't drink, vomiting, convulsions, lethargy, stridor

## Mnemonics

### Age-Pathogen: "R-S-P-M" (youngest to oldest)
- **R**SV (<2y)
- **S.** pneumoniae (all ages)
- **P**CP (HIV infants)
- **M**ycoplasma (>5y)
