# Tuberculosis in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Paucibacillary — diagnosis often clinical (contact + symptoms + Mantoux + CXR). Primary complex = Ghon focus + lymphadenopathy. Most common CXR finding = hilar lymphadenopathy. TB meningitis = most serious. Treatment: 2HRZE + 4HR (pulmonary 6 months); 12 months for meningitis/bone. Mantoux ≥10 mm positive. GeneXpert/CBNAAT for rapid diagnosis. BCG protects against severe forms.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Ghon complex** = Ghon focus + draining lymph node + lymphangitis
- ★ **Ranke complex** = calcified/healed Ghon complex
- ★ **Most common CXR finding** = hilar/paratracheal lymphadenopathy
- ★ **TB meningitis CSF:** Lymphocytes, ↓↓glucose, ↑↑protein, cobweb
- ★ **Choroidal tubercles** = pathognomonic of miliary TB (on fundoscopy)
- ★ **Mantoux:** Read at 48-72h; measure induration; ≥10 mm positive
- ★ **GeneXpert/CBNAAT** — rapid (2h), detects MTB + rifampicin resistance
- ★ **Gastric aspirate** — early morning × 3 days for AFB
- ★ **Steroids added** in TB meningitis and airway compression
- ★ **Isoniazid preventive therapy** — 6 months for <5 year contacts
- ★ **Pyridoxine** supplementation with INH (prevents peripheral neuropathy)

## Mnemonics

### Primary Complex: "GhON = Ghon focus + NOde"
### **HRZE** = "**H**uman **R**ace against **Z**oonotic **E**nemy"

### Drug Side Effects: "HIRE"
- **H**epatotoxicity (INH, Rif, PZA)
- **I**NH → peripheral neuropathy (give B6)
- **R**if → orange urine, hepatotoxicity
- **E**thambutol → optic neuritis (avoid <6y if possible)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common CXR finding** — Hilar lymphadenopathy
2. **Most serious form** — TB meningitis
3. **Pathognomonic sign of miliary TB** — Choroidal tubercles
4. **Mantoux reading** — 48-72 hours; measure induration
5. **Mantoux false negative** — Miliary TB, TB meningitis, HIV, measles, malnutrition
6. **Treatment duration** — 6 months (pulmonary), 12 months (meningitis)
7. **GeneXpert** — 2-hour result, detects rifampicin resistance
8. **BCG protection** — Meningitis and miliary TB (not pulmonary reliably)
9. **TB lymphadenitis** — Most common extrapulmonary TB in children
10. **Steroids in TB** — Meningitis (stage II/III), pericarditis, airway compression
