# HIV in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
>90% from MTCT (in utero, intrapartum, breastfeeding). Without PMTCT: 25-40% transmission → <2% with intervention. EID: DNA PCR at 6 weeks (antibody unreliable <18 months). ART for ALL regardless of CD4/stage. Cotrimoxazole from 6 weeks. PCP = most common OI in infants. First-line ART: ABC + 3TC + DTG (>4 weeks). NVP prophylaxis to infant.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **DNA PCR at 6 weeks** = gold standard for early infant diagnosis
- ★ **Antibody tests unreliable <18 months** (maternal IgG crosses placenta)
- ★ **ART for ALL** HIV-positive children regardless of age/CD4/stage
- ★ **Cotrimoxazole from 6 weeks** — prevents PCP (most common OI)
- ★ **PCP** = bilateral diffuse infiltrates, severe hypoxemia, peak 3-6 months
- ★ **LIP** = bilateral reticulonodular infiltrates + clubbing (chronic, not acute)
- ★ **Mixed feeding** increases MTCT risk more than exclusive breastfeeding
- ★ **NVP syrup** to infant for 6-12 weeks (PMTCT)
- ★ **Viral load** = best marker for treatment response
- ★ **DTG (Dolutegravir)** = preferred integrase inhibitor in children
- ★ **Without ART:** 50% mortality by age 5

## Mnemonics

### **PMTCT** Steps
- **P**regnant mother on ART (TDF+3TC+DTG)
- **M**ode of delivery (C-section if VL>1000)
- **T**reat baby with NVP syrup
- **C**otrimoxazole prophylaxis from 6 weeks
- **T**est at 6 weeks (DNA PCR)

### EID Timing: "6-6-18"
- **6** weeks: DNA PCR
- **6** months: Repeat PCR if breastfeeding
- **18** months: Antibody test (confirmatory)

1. **Infant diagnosis** — DNA PCR at 6 weeks
2. **Why not antibody <18 months** — Maternal IgG persists
3. **Most common OI in infants** — PCP
4. **PMTCT regimen** — Maternal ART + infant NVP
5. **ART initiation** — ALL children, regardless of CD4/stage
6. **Cotrimoxazole** — From 6 weeks; prevents PCP
7. **Best treatment response marker** — Viral load
8. **Mixed vs exclusive breastfeeding** — Mixed feeding increases risk
