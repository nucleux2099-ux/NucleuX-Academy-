# Nephrotic Syndrome — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Tetrad: massive proteinuria + hypoalbuminemia (<2.5) + edema + hyperlipidemia. MCD = 80% in children, 95% steroid-responsive. Peak age 2-6 years, M:F 2:1. ISKDC: prednisolone 2 mg/kg × 6 weeks → 1.5 mg/kg alternate day × 6 weeks. SBP (S. pneumoniae) = most common serious complication. Biopsy for: steroid-resistant, <1 year, hematuria, low C3, HTN.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **MCD** = 80% of childhood NS; steroid-sensitive in 95%
- ★ **Periorbital edema** = earliest sign (worse in morning)
- ★ **SBP by S. pneumoniae** = most common serious complication
- ★ **ISKDC protocol:** Pred 2 mg/kg/day × 6 wks → 1.5 mg/kg alt day × 6 wks
- ★ **Steroid resistance** = most common indication for renal biopsy; think FSGS
- ★ **Thromboembolism risk** — loss of AT-III, protein C/S
- ★ **C3/C4 normal** in MCD and FSGS; low in MPGN, lupus nephritis
- ★ **Hyperlipidemia** — ↑hepatic lipoprotein synthesis due to ↓albumin
- ★ **Levamisole** = first-line steroid-sparing agent for FRNS
- ★ **Albumin infusion** only for intravascular depletion (↑Hct, ↓BP), not routine
- ★ **Remission** = urine protein nil/trace × 3 consecutive days
- ★ **Relapse** = urine protein 3+ or more × 3 consecutive days

## Mnemonics

### **NEPHROTIC** — Features
- **N**o hematuria (in MCD)
- **E**dema (periorbital, pedal, anasarca)
- **P**roteinuria massive (>3.5 g/day)
- **H**yperlipidemia
- **R**enal vein thrombosis risk
- **O**val fat bodies in urine
- **T**reatment: steroids
- **I**nfections (SBP — S. pneumoniae)
- **C**omplement normal (MCD)

### Biopsy Indications: "CRASH"
- **C**omplement low
- **R**esistance to steroids
- **A**ge <1 or >12 years
- **S**econdary features (rash, joint pain)
- **H**ematuria or hypertension

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common histology** — MCD (80%)
2. **Most common complication** — SBP (S. pneumoniae)
3. **Biopsy indication** — Steroid resistance (most common)
4. **Steroid regimen** — ISKDC 6+6 weeks
5. **Normal C3** — MCD, FSGS; Low C3 — MPGN, post-strep GN, lupus
6. **Thrombosis mechanism** — Loss of AT-III
7. **Levamisole** — steroid-sparing agent for FRNS
8. **FSGS** = most common cause of steroid-resistant NS
9. **Periorbital edema** — earliest sign (morning predominance)
10. **Frothy urine** — due to heavy proteinuria
