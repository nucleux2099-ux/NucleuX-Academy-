# Immunization Schedule (NIS India) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
UIP covers 12 diseases. Birth: BCG + OPV-0 + HepB. 6/10/14 weeks: Pentavalent + OPV + IPV + Rotavirus + PCV. 9 months: MR-1 + Vit A. 16-24 months: MR-2 + DPT booster + OPV booster. BCG = only intradermal vaccine. DPT/HepB/IPV are freeze-sensitive. OPV stored at -20°C. Open vial policy for liquid killed vaccines (28 days). Mission Indradhanush (2014).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Birth vaccines:** BCG + OPV-0 + Hepatitis B (within 24 hours)
- ★ **BCG** = only intradermal vaccine; left upper arm; scar at 6-8 weeks
- ★ **Pentavalent** = DPT + HepB + Hib (3 doses at 6, 10, 14 weeks)
- ★ **OPV** = stored at -20°C (only frozen vaccine)
- ★ **DPT, HepB, IPV, Pentavalent** = freeze-sensitive (destroyed by freezing!)
- ★ **VAPP** = 1 in 2.4 million OPV doses → reason for IPV inclusion
- ★ **MR-1 at 9 months**, MR-2 at 16-24 months
- ★ **Vitamin A** starts at 9 months (1 lakh IU), then 2 lakh every 6 months
- ★ **Rotavirus vaccine (116E)** = Indian strain; live oral vaccine
- ★ **Open vial policy:** Killed liquid multidose vials for 28 days; live vaccines discarded same day
- ★ **VVM** = Vaccine Vial Monitor (time-temperature indicator)
- ★ **Mission Indradhanush** launched 2014 for full immunization coverage

## Mnemonics

### Birth Vaccines: "BOH" (Born On Holiday)
- **B**CG
- **O**PV-0
- **H**epatitis B birth dose

### 6-10-14 Weeks: "POP-R-P" (at each visit)
- **P**entavalent
- **O**PV
- **P**CV (6 & 14 weeks)
- **R**otavirus
- (IPV also given)

### Freeze-Sensitive: "DiPT-HeP-IPV" (all killed/subunit) — Think "killed = fragile to cold"
### NOT Freeze-Sensitive: BCG, OPV, MR (live vaccines more robust to freezing)
