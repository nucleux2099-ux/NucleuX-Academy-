# National Immunization Schedule (NIS) — India

## Overview
India's National Immunization Schedule (NIS) under the Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) is one of the largest immunization programs globally, targeting ~26 million infants annually. The schedule provides free vaccines against 12 vaccine-preventable diseases. The program has evolved significantly with recent additions of Rotavirus, PCV, IPV, and MR vaccine. Understanding the schedule, vaccine types, doses, routes, and cold chain requirements is essential for pediatric practice.

## Key Points
- **UIP** launched in 1985 (expanded from EPI, 1978)
- **12 diseases** covered: TB, Hepatitis B, Polio, Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, H. influenzae type b, Pneumococcus, Rotavirus, Measles, Rubella, JE (endemic areas)
- **Birth dose vaccines:** BCG, OPV-0, Hepatitis B birth dose
- **Mission Indradhanush** (2014) — to achieve full immunization coverage
- **Cold chain:** 2-8°C for most vaccines; OPV at -20°C (frozen)
- **Open vial policy** — applicable to certain vaccines
- **BCG** — only intradermal vaccine in the schedule

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Vaccine Types in NIS
| Vaccine | Type | Live/Killed |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **BCG** | Live attenuated (Bacillus Calmette-Guérin) | Live |
| **OPV** | Live attenuated (Sabin) | Live |
| **IPV** | Inactivated (Salk) | Killed |
| **Hepatitis B** | Recombinant (HBsAg) | Subunit |
| **Pentavalent** | DPT (toxoid+killed) + HepB (recombinant) + Hib (conjugate) | Killed/combination |
| **Rotavirus** | Live attenuated (116E strain in India) | Live |
| **PCV** | Conjugate | Killed |
| **MR** | Live attenuated | Live |
| **JE** | Live attenuated (SA-14-14-2) | Live |
| **Td** | Toxoid | Killed |

### Principles of Immunization
- **Active immunity:** Vaccine stimulates host immune response → memory
- **Primary series:** Initial doses to establish immunity
- **Booster:** Re-exposure to enhance and prolong immunity
- **Herd immunity:** When sufficient population is immunized, indirect protection to unvaccinated
- **Cold chain:** Maintaining 2-8°C from manufacture to administration (essential for potency)

## Clinical Features

### Expected Vaccine Reactions (Normal)
- **BCG:** Papule at 2 weeks → ulcer → scar at 6-8 weeks (Koch's phenomenon if TB-infected)
- **DPT:** Fever, local pain, swelling (whole-cell pertussis component — more reactogenic)
- **OPV:** Rarely VAPP (Vaccine-Associated Paralytic Polio) — 1 in 2.4 million doses
- **MR:** Fever, rash (5-12 days post-vaccination)
- **Rotavirus:** Mild diarrhea, irritability

### Contraindications
- **General:** Anaphylaxis to previous dose or vaccine component
- **BCG:** Symptomatic HIV, immunodeficiency
- **OPV:** Immunodeficiency (use IPV instead)
- **DPT:** Encephalopathy within 7 days of previous dose → switch to DTaP
- **Live vaccines (BCG, OPV, MR, Rotavirus):** Severe immunodeficiency

### NOT Contraindications (Common Misconceptions)
- Mild illness with low-grade fever
- Mild diarrhea
- Current antibiotic therapy
- Prematurity (vaccinate at chronological age)
- Malnutrition
- Family history of adverse events
- Breastfeeding

## Diagnosis

### Monitoring Immunization Coverage
- **Full immunization:** All doses of NIS vaccines by 12 months (BCG + 3 DPT + 3 OPV + MR-1 + HepB)
- **Dropout rate:** Started but didn't complete schedule
- **Coverage indicators:** BCG to MR-1 dropout rate
- **NFHS/HMIS data:** Track district-wise coverage

### Documentation
- Immunization card/Mother and Child Protection Card
- Electronic tracking (eVIN — Electronic Vaccine Intelligence Network)

## Management

### Administration Guidelines
- **Injection site:** Anterolateral thigh (<1 year); deltoid (>1 year)
- **BCG:** Intradermal (only ID vaccine); left upper arm; 0.1 mL (0.05 mL for <1 month)
- **Multiple vaccines:** Can give simultaneously at different sites
- **Minimum interval** between two live vaccines: 4 weeks (if not given simultaneously)

### Cold Chain Management
| Vaccine | Storage Temperature | Sensitivity |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| OPV | -20°C (frozen) | Heat-sensitive |
| BCG, MR | 2-8°C | Freeze-sensitive: NO (but avoid) |
| DPT, Pentavalent, TT, Td, HepB, IPV | 2-8°C | **Freeze-sensitive** (destroyed by freezing) |
| Diluents | 2-8°C | — |

### Cold Chain Equipment
- **Ice-Lined Refrigerator (ILR):** Store vaccines at PHC level
- **Deep Freezer:** Store OPV, prepare ice packs
- **Cold box, vaccine carrier:** Transport
- **VVM (Vaccine Vial Monitor):** Time-temperature indicator on vial

### Open Vial Policy
- **Can reuse** if opened during session: Hepatitis B, DPT, TT, Td, IPV, Pentavalent (liquid vaccines in multidose vials) — up to 28 days if stored properly
- **Cannot reuse:** BCG, MR, Rotavirus (discard at end of session)

## Complications

### Adverse Events Following Immunization (AEFI)
| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Vaccine product-related** | BCG lymphadenitis, VAPP (OPV), anaphylaxis |
| **Vaccine quality defect** | Contamination, improper inactivation |
| **Immunization error** | Wrong dose/route/site, contaminated needle |
| **Immunization anxiety** | Fainting, hyperventilation |
| **Coincidental** | Unrelated event temporally associated |

### Serious AEFI
- Anaphylaxis (immediate — any vaccine)
- VAPP (OPV — 1 in 2.4 million)
- BCG dissemination (immunocompromised)
- Intussusception (rotavirus — rare, 1 in 20,000-100,000)
- Encephalopathy (whole-cell pertussis — rare)
- Febrile seizures (DPT, MR)
