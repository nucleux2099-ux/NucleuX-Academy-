# IMNCI — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Indian adaptation of WHO IMCI + neonatal care. Primary care algorithm for 0-5 years. Color-coded: Pink (refer), Yellow (treat at facility), Green (home). No lab tests. Assess → Classify → Treat → Counsel. Tachypnea thresholds: 60/50/40. Five killers: pneumonia, diarrhea, malaria, measles, malnutrition. Four danger signs: can't drink, vomits everything, convulsions, lethargic.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Color coding:** Pink (urgent referral), Yellow (treat), Green (home)
- ★ **4 General danger signs:** Can't drink, vomits everything, convulsions, lethargic
- ★ **Tachypnea thresholds:** <2m: ≥60, 2-12m: ≥50, 1-5y: ≥40
- ★ **No laboratory tests** in basic algorithm
- ★ **Malnutrition** underlies 45% of under-5 deaths
- ★ **Young infant (0-2 months)** assessed separately
- ★ **Skin pinch** — key dehydration sign in IMNCI
- ★ **Palmar pallor** — anemia screening in IMNCI
- ★ **Pre-referral treatment** = first dose antibiotic, prevent hypoglycemia
- ★ **Integrated approach** — assess for ALL conditions at every visit

## Mnemonics

### IMNCI Steps: "ACTCC" — Assess, Classify, Treat, Counsel, follow-up Check
### Danger Signs: "DUCL" — Drink (can't), Unable to eat, Convulsions, Lethargic

### Color Code: "Pink=Panic, Yellow=Yes treat here, Green=Go home"
