# Adolescence — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
WHO: 10-19 years. Early (10-13), middle (14-16), late (17-19). First sign: thelarche (girls 8-13y), testicular enlargement >4mL (boys 9-14y). Growth spurt: girls peak Tanner 2-3, boys Tanner 3-4. Menarche at ~12.5y (Tanner 3-4). Precocious: <8 girls, <9 boys. HEADSS assessment. RKSK = India's adolescent health program. WIFS for girls.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **First sign girls** = Thelarche (breast bud, Tanner 2)
- ★ **First sign boys** = Testicular enlargement ≥4 mL (Tanner 2)
- ★ **Menarche** = ~2 years after thelarche; Tanner 3-4
- ★ **Peak height velocity:** Girls 11-12y (Tanner 2-3), Boys 13-14y (Tanner 3-4)
- ★ **Precocious puberty:** <8 girls, <9 boys; most common cause in girls = idiopathic
- ★ **Constitutional delay** = most common cause of delayed puberty
- ★ **HEADSS** = Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide
- ★ **RKSK** = Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram
- ★ **WIFS** = Weekly Iron Folic Acid Supplementation for adolescent girls
- ★ **Road traffic injuries** = leading cause of adolescent death globally

## Mnemonics
