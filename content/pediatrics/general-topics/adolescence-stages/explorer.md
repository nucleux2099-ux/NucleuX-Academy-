# Adolescence and Stages of Adolescence

## Overview
Adolescence is the transitional period between childhood and adulthood, characterized by rapid physical growth, sexual maturation (puberty), cognitive development, and psychosocial changes. WHO defines adolescence as ages 10-19 years. It is divided into early (10-13), middle (14-16), and late (17-19) adolescence. Understanding Tanner staging, growth spurt patterns, and the unique health challenges of this age group (HEADSS assessment) is essential for comprehensive adolescent care.

## Key Points
- **WHO definition:** 10-19 years
- **Puberty:** Girls start 8-13 years; Boys start 9-14 years
- **First sign of puberty:** Thelarche (breast buds) in girls; Testicular enlargement (>4 mL) in boys
- **Tanner staging** — 5 stages of sexual maturity rating
- **Growth spurt:** Girls peak at 11-12 years (Tanner 2-3); Boys peak at 13-14 years (Tanner 3-4)
- **Menarche:** Average 12.5 years (Tanner 3-4); ~2 years after thelarche
- **Precocious puberty:** Before 8 (girls) or 9 (boys)
- **HEADSS assessment:** Home, Education, Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide/depression
- **Leading causes of adolescent mortality:** Injuries, suicide, violence

## Definition & Classification

### WHO Age Classification
| Term | Age Range |
|------|-----------|
| **Early adolescence** | 10-13 years |
| **Middle adolescence** | 14-16 years |
| **Late adolescence** | 17-19 years |
| **Youth** | 15-24 years |

### Tanner Staging — Girls (Breast Development)
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Prepubertal; no breast development |
| 2 | Breast bud (thelarche); small mound; areola enlarges — **FIRST SIGN** |
| 3 | Further enlargement; no separation of contours |
| 4 | Areola and papilla form secondary mound |
| 5 | Adult; areola recesses to general contour |

### Tanner Staging — Boys (Genital Development)
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Prepubertal; testicular volume <4 mL |
| 2 | Testicular enlargement (≥4 mL) — **FIRST SIGN** |
| 3 | Penile lengthening; further testicular growth |
| 4 | Penile broadening; scrotal darkening |
| 5 | Adult genitalia |

### Pubic Hair Staging (Both Sexes)
| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No pubic hair |
| 2 | Sparse, lightly pigmented (labia/base of penis) |
| 3 | Darker, curlier, spreading |
| 4 | Adult-type but smaller area |
| 5 | Adult distribution (extends to medial thighs) |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Puberty — Hormonal Cascade
1. **GnRH pulse generator** reactivates in hypothalamus (dormant since infancy)
2. GnRH → Pituitary: LH + FSH release
3. **Girls:** FSH → ovarian follicle development → estrogen → breast development, fat distribution, growth spurt
4. **Boys:** LH → Leydig cells → testosterone → genital growth, muscle mass, voice deepening
5. **Adrenarche:** Adrenal androgens (DHEA) → pubic/axillary hair, body odor (may precede puberty by 1-2 years)
6. **Growth spurt:** GH + IGF-1 + sex steroids synergize; estrogen (both sexes) drives epiphyseal fusion

### Growth During Adolescence
- **Girls:** Peak height velocity 8-9 cm/year at Tanner 2-3 (before menarche)
- **Boys:** Peak height velocity 9-10 cm/year at Tanner 3-4
- Boys grow for ~2 years longer → adult height difference (~13 cm)
- 25% of final adult height gained during adolescent growth spurt
- 50% of adult bone mass acquired during adolescence

### Cognitive and Psychosocial Development
| Stage | Cognitive | Psychosocial |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Early (10-13)** | Concrete thinking; beginning abstract thought | Self-conscious; peer influence begins; body image concerns |
| **Middle (14-16)** | Abstract thinking develops; sense of invincibility | Peer group dominant; risk-taking behavior; identity exploration |
| **Late (17-19)** | Future-oriented; idealistic thinking | Independence; intimate relationships; career planning |

## Clinical Features

### Normal Pubertal Sequence
**Girls:** Thelarche → Growth spurt → Pubarche → Menarche (B2→B3→PH3→Menarche)
**Boys:** Testicular enlargement → Penile growth → Growth spurt → Voice change → Facial hair

### Pubertal Disorders
| Disorder | Definition | Common Causes |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| **Precocious puberty** | Before 8 (girls), 9 (boys) | Central (idiopathic, CNS tumors); Peripheral (CAH, tumors) |
| **Delayed puberty** | No signs by 13 (girls), 14 (boys) | Constitutional delay (most common); hypogonadism |

### Adolescent Health Issues (HEADSS Framework)
- **H**ome: Family dynamics, abuse, stability
- **E**ducation: Academic performance, school issues, bullying
- **A**ctivities: Peers, hobbies, screen time, exercise
- **D**rugs: Substance use (tobacco, alcohol, cannabis)
- **S**exuality: Sexual activity, contraception, STIs, gender identity
- **S**uicide/Depression: Mental health screening, self-harm, mood

### Common Adolescent Health Problems
- Acne, dysmenorrhea, PCOS
- Eating disorders (anorexia, bulimia)
- Obesity, metabolic syndrome
- Substance abuse (tobacco, alcohol, drugs)
- Depression, anxiety, suicide
- STIs, teenage pregnancy
- Injuries (road traffic accidents — leading cause of death)
- Sports injuries

## Diagnosis

### Assessment Tools
- **Tanner staging** — physical examination
- **Growth charts** — height, weight, BMI
- **Bone age** — if puberty timing concerns
- **HEADSS interview** — comprehensive psychosocial assessment
- **PHQ-A** — adolescent depression screening
- **CRAFFT** — substance abuse screening

### When to Investigate
- Precocious puberty: GnRH stimulation test, LH/FSH, estradiol/testosterone, bone age, MRI brain
- Delayed puberty: LH/FSH, testosterone/estradiol, karyotype (Turner/Klinefelter), bone age

## Management

### Adolescent-Friendly Health Services (AFHS)
- **Rashtriya Kishor Swasthya Karyakram (RKSK)** — India's national adolescent health program
- Confidential, non-judgmental, accessible
- Key areas: Nutrition, mental health, substance abuse, sexual/reproductive health, injuries/violence
- **WIFS** (Weekly Iron and Folic Acid Supplementation) — for adolescent girls
- **ARSH** (Adolescent Reproductive and Sexual Health) services

### Specific Interventions
- Iron supplementation (WIFS: IFA 100 mg iron + 500 μg folic acid weekly)
- HPV vaccination (9-14 years)
- Menstrual hygiene education
- Life skills education
- Mental health support
- Deworming (biannual albendazole)
- Tobacco/substance prevention

### Counseling Principles
- Respect confidentiality and autonomy
- Involve parents appropriately (with adolescent's consent)
- Non-judgmental approach
- Assess readiness for health behavior change

## Complications
- **Precocious puberty** → short adult height (early epiphyseal fusion), psychosocial difficulties
- **Eating disorders** → malnutrition, cardiac arrhythmias, death
- **Substance abuse** → addiction, academic failure, accidents
- **Teenage pregnancy** → maternal and neonatal complications
- **Mental health** → suicide (2nd-3rd leading cause of adolescent death)
- **Untreated STIs** → infertility, chronic disease
