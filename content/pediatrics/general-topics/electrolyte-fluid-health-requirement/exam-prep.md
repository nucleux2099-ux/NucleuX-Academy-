# Fluid & Electrolyte Requirements — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Holliday-Segar: 100/50/20 mL/kg/day (4/2/1 per hour). Maintenance Na: 2-3 mEq/kg/day, K: 1-2 mEq/kg/day. Neonates 80% TBW. Resuscitation: NS/RL 20 mL/kg bolus. Add K only after urine output. Never K bolus. Hyponatremia = most common electrolyte disturbance in hospitalized. Correct hypernatremia over 48-72h. SIADH = fluid restriction.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Holliday-Segar:** 100/50/20 mL/kg/day for 1st 10/next 10/each additional kg
- ★ **4-2-1 rule** (hourly version of above)
- ★ **Resuscitation bolus:** NS or RL 20 mL/kg over 20 min
- ★ **Never give K⁺ bolus** — cardiac arrest risk
- ★ **Add K⁺ only after urine output** confirmed
- ★ **Hypernatremia correction:** Slowly over 48-72h (<0.5 mEq/L/hour)
- ★ **Hyponatremia correction:** <10-12 mEq/L per 24 hours
- ★ **SIADH treatment** = fluid restriction (60-75% maintenance)
- ★ **Anion gap** = Na - (Cl + HCO₃); normal 8-12; elevated in DKA
- ★ **Isotonic dehydration** = most common type (80%)
- ★ **Neonatal TBW** = 75-80% (vs adult 60%)

## Mnemonics
### Holliday-Segar: "**100-50-20**" or "**4-2-1**"
### K⁺ Safety: "**No K without Pee**" (add K only after urine output)
### Correction Speed: "**Hyper = Slow (48-72h); Hypo = Not too fast (<12/day)**"
