# Meningitis in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Medical emergency. S. pneumoniae = most common bacterial (post-Hib era). Neonates: GBS + E. coli. CSF: bacterial (neutrophils, ↓glucose, ↑protein), viral (lymphocytes, normal glucose), TB (lymphocytes, ↓↓glucose, ↑↑protein). Empirical: Ceftriaxone ± vancomycin. Dexamethasone before/with antibiotics. Most common sequela: sensorineural hearing loss. Never delay antibiotics for LP.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **S. pneumoniae** = most common bacterial cause (post-Hib era)
- ★ **GBS + E. coli** = neonatal meningitis
- ★ **Enterovirus** = most common cause of viral/aseptic meningitis
- ★ **CSF glucose <40 mg/dL** (<50% blood glucose) = bacterial meningitis
- ★ **Petechial rash** = N. meningitidis (meningococcemia)
- ★ **Waterhouse-Friderichsen** = adrenal hemorrhage in meningococcemia
- ★ **Dexamethasone** before/with first antibiotic → reduces hearing loss (Hib)
- ★ **Sensorineural hearing loss** = most common long-term sequela → BERA test
- ★ **SIADH** = common complication → hyponatremia → fluid restriction
- ★ **Never delay antibiotics** for LP or imaging
- ★ **Chemoprophylaxis:** Rifampicin for meningococcal and Hib contacts
- ★ **CT before LP** if: papilledema, focal signs, GCS <12

## Mnemonics

### CSF: **"Bacterial = Bad Glucose"**
- Bacterial: ↓↓glucose, neutrophils, turbid
- Viral: Normal glucose, lymphocytes, clear
- TB: ↓↓glucose, lymphocytes, cobweb

### Neonatal Organisms: **"GEL"**
- **G**BS
- **E**. coli
- **L**isteria

### Meningococcal: **"Petechiae + Purpura = meNingococcus"**

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common bacterial cause** — S. pneumoniae (children >3 months)
2. **Neonatal meningitis** — GBS, E. coli; Rx: Ampicillin + Cefotaxime
3. **CSF in bacterial** — neutrophils, ↓glucose, ↑protein, turbid
4. **CSF in TB** — lymphocytes, ↓↓glucose, ↑↑protein, cobweb
5. **Petechial rash** — N. meningitidis
6. **Most common sequela** — Sensorineural hearing loss
7. **Dexamethasone timing** — Before or with first antibiotic dose
8. **SIADH** — hyponatremia, inappropriate ADH secretion
9. **CT before LP indication** — papilledema, focal signs, reduced consciousness
10. **Chemoprophylaxis** — Rifampicin (meningococcal, Hib contacts)
