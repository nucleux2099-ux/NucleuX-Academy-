# Neural Tube Defects — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Failure of neural tube closure (day 21-28). Anterior failure → anencephaly. Posterior failure → spina bifida/myelomeningocele. Folic acid 400 μg/day prevents 50-70%. MSAFP elevated in open NTDs. Myelomeningocele: paralysis below level + neurogenic bladder + hydrocephalus (90%) + Arnold-Chiari II. Valproate = most teratogenic AED for NTDs. Surgery within 24-72 hours.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Neural tube closes by day 28** — before most women know they're pregnant
- ★ **Folic acid 400 μg/day** prevents 50-70% of NTDs
- ★ **High-risk dose:** 4-5 mg/day (previous NTD, on valproate)
- ★ **Maternal serum AFP ↑** in open NTDs; normal in closed
- ★ **USS signs:** Lemon sign (skull), banana sign (cerebellum) = myelomeningocele
- ★ **Arnold-Chiari II** = cerebellar herniation through foramen magnum; 90% hydrocephalus
- ★ **Neurogenic bladder** — most common cause of renal damage in spina bifida; CIC essential
- ★ **Valproate** = most teratogenic common AED (1-2% NTD risk)
- ★ **Latex allergy** in 50% of myelomeningocele patients
- ★ **Level of lesion** determines functional outcome

## Mnemonics

### **FOLD** — Prevention of NTDs
- **F**olic acid (400 μg preconceptional)
- **O**mit valproate in pregnancy
- **L**ook at previous history (high-risk: 4-5 mg)
- **D**iagnose early (MSAFP, USS at 18-20 weeks)

### Types by Severity: "AMEMS" (least to most)
- Spina bifida **O**cculta → **M**eningocele → **M**yelomeningocele → **E**ncephalocele → **A**nencephaly
