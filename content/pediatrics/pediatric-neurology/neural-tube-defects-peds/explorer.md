# Neural Tube Defects

## Overview
Neural tube defects (NTDs) are the most common congenital anomalies of the central nervous system, resulting from failure of neural tube closure during the 3rd-4th week of gestation (days 21-28). They range from anencephaly (incompatible with life) to spina bifida occulta (often asymptomatic). The incidence is 1-2 per 1,000 births globally. Periconceptional folic acid supplementation (400 μg/day) prevents 50-70% of NTDs.

## Key Points
- **Neural tube closes by day 28** of gestation (often before pregnancy is known)
- **Folic acid 400 μg/day** periconceptionally prevents 50-70% of NTDs
- **High-risk women:** 4-5 mg/day folic acid (previous NTD, on AEDs)
- **Anencephaly** = failure of anterior closure; incompatible with life
- **Myelomeningocele** = most common clinically significant NTD
- **Maternal serum AFP ↑** in open NTDs (screening)
- **Valproate** is the most teratogenic AED for NTDs
- **Arnold-Chiari II malformation** — associated with myelomeningocele

## Definition & Classification

### Types of Neural Tube Defects
| Type | Description | Prognosis |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| **Anencephaly** | Absence of brain and calvarium (anterior closure failure) | Lethal |
| **Encephalocele** | Brain herniation through skull defect (occipital most common) | Variable; depends on content |
| **Myelomeningocele** | Spinal cord + meninges protrude through vertebral defect | Survivable; significant disability |
| **Meningocele** | Only meninges protrude; cord normal | Good prognosis |
| **Spina bifida occulta** | Vertebral arch defect only; no herniation | Usually asymptomatic |

### Classification by Closure
- **Open NTDs:** Exposed neural tissue (anencephaly, open myelomeningocele) — AFP elevated
- **Closed NTDs:** Skin-covered (meningocele, spina bifida occulta, lipomyelomeningocele) — AFP normal

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Risk Factors
- **Folic acid deficiency** — most important modifiable risk factor
- **Maternal AEDs:** Valproate (1-2% risk), carbamazepine
- **Maternal diabetes mellitus**
- **Previous NTD-affected pregnancy** (recurrence risk 2-5%)
- **Obesity**
- **Hyperthermia** in early pregnancy
- **Genetic factors:** Multifactorial inheritance
- **Ethnicity:** Higher in Sikh community in India, Celtic/Irish populations

### Embryology
1. Neural plate forms by day 18
2. Neural folds elevate and fuse → neural tube (day 21-28)
3. **Anterior neuropore** closes day 25 (failure → anencephaly, encephalocele)
4. **Posterior neuropore** closes day 28 (failure → spina bifida, myelomeningocele)
5. Folic acid is essential for DNA synthesis and methylation during rapid cell division

### Pathophysiology of Myelomeningocele
- Open spinal defect → exposed cord → secondary damage (amniotic fluid, trauma)
- Cord tethering → progressive neurological deficit
- Arnold-Chiari II malformation → hindbrain herniation → hydrocephalus (90%)
- Level of lesion determines motor/sensory deficit below

## Clinical Features

### Anencephaly
- Absent cranial vault and cerebral hemispheres
- Often detected on prenatal USS
- Incompatible with prolonged survival

### Myelomeningocele
- **Visible sac** on back (most common: lumbosacral)
- **Motor deficit:** Flaccid paralysis below lesion level
- **Sensory deficit:** Loss of sensation below level
- **Bladder dysfunction:** Neurogenic bladder (frequent UTIs, incontinence)
- **Bowel dysfunction:** Fecal incontinence, constipation
- **Orthopedic:** Talipes equinovarus (clubfoot), hip dislocation, scoliosis
- **Hydrocephalus:** 90% (Arnold-Chiari II) — increasing head circumference, bulging fontanelle
- **Arnold-Chiari II:** Cerebellar tonsils herniate through foramen magnum → stridor, apnea, swallowing difficulty
- **Cognitive:** Variable; many have normal intelligence; some have learning difficulties
- **Latex allergy:** High risk (50%) — avoid latex products

### Spina Bifida Occulta
- Often incidental finding on X-ray
- **Cutaneous markers:** Tuft of hair, dimple, lipoma, hemangioma over lumbosacral spine
- May present later with tethered cord syndrome: progressive weakness, bladder dysfunction, foot deformity

## Diagnosis

### Prenatal Screening
- **Maternal serum AFP (MSAFP):** ↑ in open NTDs (16-18 weeks); part of triple/quad screen
- **Ultrasound (18-20 weeks):** Lemon sign (skull), banana sign (cerebellum) — myelomeningocele
- **Amniocentesis:** ↑AFP + acetylcholinesterase in amniotic fluid (confirmatory)

### Postnatal
- Clinical examination of sac, neurological level
- **MRI spine:** Define anatomy, cord tethering
- **MRI brain:** Chiari malformation, hydrocephalus
- **USS head:** Serial monitoring for hydrocephalus
- **Urodynamic studies:** Bladder function assessment
- **Renal USS:** Monitor for reflux, hydronephrosis

## Management

### Prenatal
- **Folic acid supplementation:** 400 μg/day (all women); 4-5 mg/day (high-risk)
- **Prenatal counseling** based on diagnosis and severity
- **Fetal surgery:** In utero repair of myelomeningocele (MOMS trial) — reduces need for VP shunt

### Postnatal (Myelomeningocele)
1. **Emergency:** Keep sac moist (sterile saline dressing), prone positioning
2. **Surgical closure** within 24-72 hours to prevent infection
3. **Hydrocephalus:** VP shunt when symptomatic (80-90% need shunting)
4. **Bladder management:** Clean intermittent catheterization (CIC), anticholinergics
5. **Bowel program:** Regular evacuations, dietary fiber
6. **Orthopedic:** Bracing, correction of deformities, mobility aids
7. **Rehabilitation:** Physiotherapy, occupational therapy
8. **Multidisciplinary team:** Neurosurgery, urology, orthopedics, rehabilitation, psychology

### Prevention (Most Important!)
- **Universal folic acid supplementation** — all women of childbearing age: 400 μg/day
- **High-risk:** 4-5 mg/day starting 3 months before conception through first trimester
- **Food fortification** with folic acid (flour, rice)
- **Genetic counseling** for families with previous NTD

## Complications
- **Hydrocephalus** (90%) and VP shunt complications (infection, malfunction)
- **Chiari II symptoms:** Stridor, apnea, swallowing difficulty
- **Tethered cord syndrome** — progressive neurological deterioration
- **Recurrent UTIs** and renal damage (neurogenic bladder)
- **Pressure sores** (insensate skin)
- **Latex allergy** (50%)
- **Scoliosis, kyphosis** — progressive
- **Cognitive and learning difficulties**
- **Psychosocial challenges**
