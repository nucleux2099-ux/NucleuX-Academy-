# Epilepsy in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
≥2 unprovoked seizures >24h apart. ILAE: focal, generalized, unknown. CAE: 3 Hz spike-wave, ethosuximide. JME: morning myoclonic jerks, polyspike-wave, valproate lifelong. BECTS: centrotemporal spikes, self-limiting. West syndrome: infantile spasms + hypsarrhythmia + regression → ACTH. Focal: carbamazepine. Generalized: valproate. 2 years seizure-free before AED withdrawal.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **3 Hz spike-and-wave** = Childhood Absence Epilepsy; ethosuximide first-line
- ★ **Hypsarrhythmia** = West syndrome (infantile spasms); ACTH or vigabatrin
- ★ **Centrotemporal spikes** = BECTS (benign rolandic); often no treatment needed
- ★ **JME** = morning myoclonic jerks + GTCS; valproate; lifelong treatment (high relapse)
- ★ **Carbamazepine worsens** absence and myoclonic seizures
- ★ **Valproate** = broad-spectrum but avoid in girls (teratogenic — NTD)
- ★ **BECTS** = most common benign childhood epilepsy; self-resolving by puberty
- ★ **Dravet syndrome** = SCN1A mutation; avoid Na⁺ channel blockers (CBZ, PHT)
- ★ **Lennox-Gastaut** = slow spike-wave; multiple seizure types; poor prognosis
- ★ **Ketogenic diet** = effective in GLUT-1 deficiency and refractory epilepsy
- ★ **AED withdrawal** after 2 seizure-free years; taper over 3-6 months

## Mnemonics

### **WEST** Syndrome Triad
- **W**est syndrome
- **E**EG: hypsarrhythmia
- **S**pasms (infantile)
- **T**reatment: ACTH/vigabatrin

### Absence Epilepsy: "3-4-HV"
- **3** Hz spike-wave
- Age **4**-10 years
- Provoked by **H**yper**V**entilation

### Drugs That Worsen Absence: "Can't STOP absence with CBZ/PHT"
- **C**arbamazepine, **P**henytoin → worsen absence seizures

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **3 Hz spike-wave** — Childhood Absence Epilepsy
2. **Hypsarrhythmia** — West syndrome
3. **Drug for absence** — Ethosuximide (or valproate)
4. **Drug for focal** — Carbamazepine
5. **Morning myoclonic jerks** — JME
6. **Self-limiting epilepsy** — BECTS (benign rolandic)
7. **Infantile spasms treatment** — ACTH or vigabatrin
8. **CBZ worsens** — absence and myoclonic seizures
9. **Valproate teratogenicity** — Neural tube defects
