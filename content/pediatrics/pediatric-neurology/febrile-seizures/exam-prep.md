# Febrile Seizures — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Febrile seizures occur in children 6 months–5 years with fever ≥38°C, without CNS infection. **Simple FS** (80%): generalized, <15 min, single in 24h — benign, no workup needed. **Complex FS** (20%): focal, >15 min, or recurrent in 24h — needs evaluation. Recurrence ~30%. Epilepsy risk: 1-2% (simple) vs 4-6% (complex). Management: treat fever source, parental reassurance, benzodiazepines if prolonged. Continuous AED prophylaxis NOT recommended for simple FS.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Most common cause of seizures in children** — 2-5% prevalence
- ★ **HHV-6 (Roseola)** is the most common specific viral trigger
- ★ **Simple vs Complex** — duration 15 min, focal features, recurrence in 24h are the 3 differentiators
- ★ **LP indication:** <12 months with incomplete Hib/PCV vaccination, or signs of meningitis
- ★ **EEG and neuroimaging NOT indicated** for simple febrile seizures
- ★ **Antipyretics do NOT prevent** febrile seizure recurrence (very commonly asked!)
- ★ **Recurrence risk factors:** Age <18 months (strongest), lower temperature, shorter fever duration, family history
- ★ **Acute Rx:** Rectal diazepam 0.5 mg/kg or buccal midazolam 0.2 mg/kg if >5 min
- ★ **Intermittent prophylaxis:** Oral diazepam 0.33 mg/kg Q8h during febrile episodes
- ★ **Continuous prophylaxis:** Valproate or phenobarbital — rarely indicated, risks > benefits
- ★ **SCN1A mutation** links febrile seizures to Dravet syndrome (GEFS+ spectrum)
- ★ **No cognitive impairment** or brain damage from simple febrile seizures

## Mnemonics

### **FEBRILE** — Risk Factors for Recurrence
- **F** — Family history of febrile seizures
- **E** — Early age (<18 months) at first seizure
- **B** — Brief duration of fever before seizure
- **R** — Relatively low temperature at seizure
- **I** — Initial complex features (modest risk)
- **L** — Low threshold (genetic susceptibility)
- **E** — Episodes in daycare (frequent infections)

### Simple vs Complex — "Rule of 15-1-G"
- **15** minutes: <15 = simple, ≥15 = complex
- **1** seizure in 24h: 1 = simple, >1 = complex
- **G**eneralized: yes = simple, focal = complex

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common trigger** — HHV-6; most common cause = viral URTI
2. **Age range** — 6 months to 5 years (peak 12-18 months)
3. **When to do LP** — child <12 months, incomplete vaccination, signs of meningitis, pretreated with antibiotics
4. **Simple vs complex classification** — know all 3 criteria (duration, focality, recurrence)
5. **EEG in simple FS** — NOT recommended (does not predict recurrence or epilepsy)
6. **Antipyretics and prevention** — do NOT prevent recurrence (asked repeatedly)
7. **Drug for acute seizure >5 min** — rectal diazepam / buccal midazolam
8. **Intermittent vs continuous prophylaxis** — indications and drugs
9. **Risk of epilepsy** — 1-2% simple, 4-6% complex (know the numbers)
10. **Strongest risk factor for recurrence** — age <18 months at first episode
11. **Genetic link** — SCN1A mutation, GEFS+ spectrum, Dravet syndrome
12. **Most important management step** — parental counseling and reassurance
