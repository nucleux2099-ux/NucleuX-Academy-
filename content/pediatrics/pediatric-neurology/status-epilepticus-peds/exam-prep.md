# Status Epilepticus — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Seizure ≥5 minutes or recurrent without recovery. First-line: IV lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg or IM midazolam 0.2 mg/kg. Second-line: IV phenytoin 20 mg/kg or levetiracetam 40-60 mg/kg or valproate 20-40 mg/kg. Refractory: midazolam infusion, thiopentone. Always check glucose first. Max 2 doses of benzodiazepines. ABC before drugs. Mortality 3-5%.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Operational definition:** Seizure ≥5 minutes (start treatment)
- ★ **IV Lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg** = first-line if IV access available
- ★ **IM Midazolam 0.2 mg/kg** = as effective as IV lorazepam (RAMPART trial); best if no IV
- ★ **Max 2 doses** of benzodiazepines before second-line agents
- ★ **Phenytoin 20 mg/kg IV** over 20 min — monitor ECG (arrhythmia risk)
- ★ **Check blood glucose FIRST** — correct hypoglycemia (25% dextrose 2 mL/kg)
- ★ **GABA receptor internalization** — benzos less effective after 20-30 min
- ★ **Pyridoxine 100 mg IV** if <18 months and unknown cause
- ★ **Refractory SE** = fails 2 appropriate AEDs → ICU, midazolam infusion
- ★ **Most common cause in children** — Febrile status epilepticus
- ★ **Propofol** avoided in children (propofol infusion syndrome)

## Mnemonics

### SE Protocol: "BBS-PIV-R" (time-based)
- **B**enzos (5-15 min): Lorazepam/midazolam
- **B**enzos repeat (if needed)
- **S**econd-line (15-30 min): Phenytoin/levetiracetam/valproate
- **R**efractory (>30 min): Midazolam/thiopentone infusion

### First Check: "ABC-G" (Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Glucose)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **First-line drug** — IV Lorazepam (or IM Midazolam if no IV)
2. **Pre-hospital drug** — Rectal diazepam or buccal/IM midazolam
3. **Second-line** — Phenytoin/fosphenytoin 20 mg/kg
4. **Definition** — ≥5 minutes continuous seizure
5. **Refractory SE definition** — Fails 2 appropriate AEDs
6. **First bedside test** — Blood glucose
7. **RAMPART trial** — IM midazolam = IV lorazepam in efficacy
8. **Pyridoxine** — Give if <18 months, unknown cause
9. **Most common cause** — Febrile SE in young children
10. **Avoid propofol** — Propofol infusion syndrome risk in children
