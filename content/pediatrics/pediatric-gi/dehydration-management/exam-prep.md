# Dehydration Management — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common cause: diarrhea. Isotonic (80%) most common type. Hypernatremic most dangerous — correct over 48-72h. WHO Plan A (home/ORS), Plan B (ORS 75 mL/kg/4h), Plan C (IV RL). Skin pinch = most reliable sign. Holliday-Segar for maintenance. Ringer's Lactate preferred over NS. Add K⁺ only after urine output established.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Isotonic dehydration** = most common (80%); Na 130-150
- ★ **Hypernatremic** = most dangerous; correct slowly over 48-72h (Na drop <0.5/h)
- ★ **Plan B:** ORS 75 mL/kg over 4 hours
- ★ **Plan C:** IV RL — 30 mL/kg rapid + 70 mL/kg slower
- ★ **Ringer's Lactate** preferred IV fluid (contains Na, K, Ca, lactate)
- ★ **Skin pinch >2 seconds** = severe dehydration
- ★ **Holliday-Segar:** 100/50/20 mL/kg/day for first 10/next 10/each additional kg
- ★ **Rapid correction of hypernatremia → cerebral edema → seizures**
- ★ **3% NaCl** for symptomatic hyponatremia (Na <120 with seizures)
- ★ **Never give IV K⁺ bolus**; add to fluids only after urine output confirmed
- ★ **Weight change** = gold standard for assessing dehydration %
- ★ **Metabolic acidosis** = most common acid-base disturbance in diarrheal dehydration

## Mnemonics

### **DEHYDRATE** — Signs
- **D**ry mucous membranes
- **E**yes sunken
- **H**eart rate increased
- **Y**ellow/concentrated urine (oliguria)
- **D**ecreased skin turgor
- **R**estless → lethargic
- **A**nterior fontanelle sunken
- **T**ears absent
- **E**xtremities cool

### Holliday-Segar: "4-2-1 Rule" (hourly)
- 4 mL/kg/h for first 10 kg
- 2 mL/kg/h for next 10 kg
- 1 mL/kg/h for each additional kg

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common type** — Isotonic (isonatremic)
2. **Most dangerous type** — Hypernatremic
3. **Correction rate for hypernatremia** — <0.5 mEq/L/hour, <10-12/day
4. **Plan B ORS volume** — 75 mL/kg in 4 hours
5. **IV fluid of choice** — Ringer's Lactate
6. **Holliday-Segar formula** — 100/50/20 mL/kg/day
7. **Gold standard dehydration assessment** — Weight change
8. **Cerebral edema risk** — Too-rapid correction of hypernatremia
9. **CPM risk** — Too-rapid correction of hyponatremia
10. **When to add K⁺** — Only after urine output confirmed
