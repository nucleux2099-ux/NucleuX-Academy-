# Acute Diarrhea — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Second leading cause of under-5 mortality. Rotavirus = most common cause of severe dehydrating diarrhea. ORS + Zinc = cornerstone. Low-osmolarity ORS (245 mOsm/L). Plan A (no dehydration, home), Plan B (some, ORS 75 mL/kg/4h), Plan C (severe, IV RL). Antibiotics only for cholera, shigella, amoeba. Continue breastfeeding. No antidiarrheals in children.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Rotavirus** = most common cause of severe dehydrating diarrhea in children
- ★ **Low-osmolarity ORS** = 245 mOsm/L, Na 75 mEq/L, glucose 75 mmol/L
- ★ **Zinc 20 mg × 14 days** reduces duration and recurrence for 2-3 months
- ★ **Plan C IV fluids:** Ringer's Lactate preferred over NS
- ★ **Skin pinch >2 seconds** = severe dehydration
- ★ **Rice-water stools** = cholera
- ★ **Shigella** = bloody diarrhea + seizures (neurotoxin)
- ★ **HUS** = STEC O157:H7 (Shiga toxin) → microangiopathic hemolytic anemia + thrombocytopenia + renal failure
- ★ **NO loperamide** in children
- ★ **Continue feeding** during diarrhea (do NOT starve the gut)

## Mnemonics

### **ORS-Z** — Diarrhea Treatment Pillars
- **O**RS (oral rehydration solution)
- **R**ehydrate (Plan A/B/C)
- **S**upplemental zinc (14 days)
- **Z**ero unnecessary drugs (no antidiarrheals, no routine antibiotics)

### Plans: "ABC = Ambulatory, Bedside-ORS, Critical-IV"
- **A**: Home treatment, increase fluids
- **B**: ORS 75 mL/kg in 4 hours (supervised)
- **C**: IV RL — 30+70 mL/kg protocol

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common cause** — Rotavirus
2. **ORS osmolarity** — 245 mOsm/L (low-osmolarity)
3. **Zinc dose** — 20 mg/day × 14 days (>6 months); 10 mg (<6 months)
4. **IV fluid of choice** — Ringer's Lactate
5. **Plan B dose** — 75 mL/kg over 4 hours
6. **Antibiotic for Shigella** — Ciprofloxacin or Azithromycin
7. **Rice-water stools** — Cholera
8. **HUS organism** — E. coli O157:H7
9. **Skin pinch** — goes back slowly (some), very slowly (severe)
10. **ReSoMal** — for SAM children (lower Na, higher K — NOT standard ORS)
