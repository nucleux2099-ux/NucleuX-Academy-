# Celiac Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Autoimmune enteropathy triggered by gluten (wheat, barley, rye) in HLA-DQ2/DQ8 individuals. Anti-tTG IgA = best screening test. Gold standard = duodenal biopsy (villous atrophy, crypt hyperplasia, ↑IELs). Classical: chronic diarrhea + abdominal distension + FTT in 1-3 years. Treatment = lifelong gluten-free diet. Safe grains: rice, maize, millet. Check total IgA (IgA deficiency in 2-3%).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **HLA-DQ2** in 90-95% of celiacs — absence virtually excludes diagnosis
- ★ **Anti-tTG IgA** = best screening test; **EMA** = most specific
- ★ **Gold standard** = duodenal biopsy (while on gluten diet)
- ★ **Marsh 3c** = total villous atrophy (most severe)
- ★ **tTG enzyme** deamidates gliadin → enhances HLA binding → immune response
- ★ **Gluten sources:** Wheat (gliadin), Barley (hordein), Rye (secalin)
- ★ **Safe:** Rice, corn, millet, buckwheat, quinoa — NO gluten
- ★ **Dermatitis herpetiformis** = skin celiac; IgA deposits in dermal papillae
- ★ **Check total IgA** — 2-3% celiacs have IgA deficiency (false negative serology)
- ★ **Refractory to oral iron** = think celiac (common atypical presentation)
- ★ **Associated:** Type 1 DM, Down syndrome, autoimmune thyroiditis

## Mnemonics

### **CELIAC** — Features
- **C**hronic diarrhea, steatorrhea
- **E**MA/anti-tTG positive
- **L**ifelong GFD treatment
- **I**ron deficiency (refractory)
- **A**trophy of villi (biopsy)
- **C**hildren 1-3 years (classical onset)

### Gluten Grains: **"BRW"** = Barley, Rye, Wheat (avoid these)

### Biopsy Must Be Done **"On Gluten"** — not after starting GFD

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Best screening test** — Anti-tTG IgA
2. **Most specific test** — Anti-endomysial antibody (EMA)
3. **Gold standard** — Duodenal biopsy
4. **HLA association** — DQ2 (90-95%), DQ8
5. **Gluten-free grains** — Rice, corn, millet
6. **Skin manifestation** — Dermatitis herpetiformis
7. **Biopsy findings** — Villous atrophy, crypt hyperplasia, ↑IELs
8. **When biopsy not needed (ESPGHAN)** — tTG >10× ULN + EMA positive
9. **Marsh classification** — know grades 0-3c
10. **Malignancy risk** — EATL (enteropathy-associated T-cell lymphoma)
