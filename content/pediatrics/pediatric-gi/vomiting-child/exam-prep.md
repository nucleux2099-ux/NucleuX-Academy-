# Vomiting in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common cause: AGE. Bilious vomiting in neonate = surgical emergency (malrotation/volvulus). Pyloric stenosis: 3-6 weeks, projectile non-bilious, palpable olive, hypochloremic hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis, USS diagnosis, Ramstedt pyloromyotomy. Morning vomiting + headache = raised ICP. Ondansetron = safest antiemetic in children. Avoid metoclopramide (EPS).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Bilious vomiting in neonate** = surgical emergency (malrotation until proven otherwise)
- ★ **Pyloric stenosis:** Projectile, non-bilious, 3-6 weeks, M:F 4:1, palpable olive
- ★ **Metabolic alkalosis** (hypochloremic, hypokalemic) = pyloric stenosis
- ★ **USS pyloric stenosis:** Muscle thickness >3 mm, length >15 mm
- ★ **Ramstedt pyloromyotomy** = definitive treatment (after correcting electrolytes first!)
- ★ **Morning headache + vomiting** = raised ICP
- ★ **Ondansetron** = safe antiemetic in pediatric AGE
- ★ **Metoclopramide** = avoid (extrapyramidal side effects in children)
- ★ **Intussusception:** Colicky pain + currant-jelly stools + sausage-shaped mass
- ★ **UTI** = common cause of unexplained vomiting in infants

## Mnemonics

### **VOMIT** — Red Flags
- **V**olume of blood (hematemesis)
- **O**bstructive signs (bilious, distension)
- **M**orning vomiting (raised ICP)
- **I**nfant projectile (pyloric stenosis)
- **T**oxins/metabolic (DKA, IEM)

### Pyloric Stenosis ABCs
- **A**lkalosis (metabolic)
- **B**aby boy (M:F 4:1), 3-6 weeks
- **C**hloride low, K low, olive palpable

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Bilious vomiting neonate** — malrotation/volvulus (surgical emergency)
2. **Pyloric stenosis metabolic abnormality** — hypochloremic hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis
3. **Pyloric stenosis USS criteria** — thickness >3 mm, length >15 mm
4. **Treatment of pyloric stenosis** — Correct electrolytes FIRST, then Ramstedt
5. **Safest antiemetic** — Ondansetron (5-HT3 antagonist)
6. **Intussusception diagnosis** — USS target/donut sign
7. **Raised ICP vomiting** — projectile, morning, with headache and papilledema
8. **GERD in infants** — most common cause of recurrent vomiting in infancy
9. **Cyclical vomiting syndrome** — recurrent stereotypical episodes, migraine variant
10. **UTI presenting as vomiting** — always check urine in unexplained infant vomiting
