# Chronic Diarrhea — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Diarrhea >4 weeks. Classify by stool type: watery (osmotic/secretory), fatty (malabsorption), bloody (inflammatory). Growth failure = organic cause. Normal growth = functional (toddler's diarrhea). Common causes: celiac, CMPA, giardia, toddler's diarrhea, IBD. Workup: stool exam, celiac serology, sweat chloride if indicated. Treatment is cause-specific.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Toddler's diarrhea** = most common cause in 1-3 years; normal growth; no treatment needed
- ★ **Celiac disease** = most common cause of malabsorption in children
- ★ **Stool pH <5.5** = suggests carbohydrate malabsorption (lactose intolerance)
- ★ **Osmotic diarrhea stops with fasting**; secretory continues
- ★ **Perianal disease** (fistulae, tags) = think Crohn's disease
- ★ **Sweat chloride >60** = cystic fibrosis
- ★ **CMPA** — most common food allergy in infants; elimination diet is diagnostic
- ★ **Fecal calprotectin** — screening marker for intestinal inflammation (IBD)
- ★ **Giardia** = pear-shaped trophozoite, "falling leaf" motility
- ★ **Growth failure** differentiates organic from functional diarrhea

## Mnemonics

### Stool Type Workup: "WFB"
- **W**atery → osmotic gap, breath test (lactose), stool electrolytes
- **F**atty → celiac serology, sweat chloride, fecal fat
- **B**loody → stool culture, calprotectin, colonoscopy

### **CHRONIC** — Common Causes
- **C**eliac disease
- **H**ydrolyzed formula needed (CMPA)
- **R**ight age (toddler's diarrhea)
- **O**va and parasites (Giardia)
- **N**on-specific (post-infectious)
- **I**BD (Crohn's, UC)
- **C**ystic fibrosis

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common cause (toddlers)** — Toddler's diarrhea (functional)
2. **Most common malabsorption** — Celiac disease
3. **Osmotic vs secretory** — fasting test, stool osmotic gap
4. **Stool pH <5.5** — lactose intolerance
5. **Sweat chloride** — CF diagnosis (>60 mEq/L)
6. **Perianal disease** — Crohn's (not UC)
7. **CMPA diagnosis** — Elimination and rechallenge
8. **Giardia treatment** — Metronidazole
9. **IBD screening** — Fecal calprotectin
10. **Growth failure significance** — Differentiates organic from functional
