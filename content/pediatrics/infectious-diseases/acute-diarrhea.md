# Acute Diarrhea in Children

> 📚 **Sources:** Nelson's Pediatrics 21st Ed, OP Ghai 9th Ed, WHO/UNICEF Guidelines, IAP
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Acute diarrhea is the passage of **3 or more loose/watery stools in 24 hours** (or more frequent than normal) for **<14 days**. It's the second leading cause of death in children under 5 globally.

**Classification by Duration:**
- **Acute:** <14 days
- **Persistent:** 14-30 days
- **Chronic:** >30 days

**Key Principle:** Deaths from diarrhea are due to **dehydration**. Most can be prevented with ORS.

## Key Points

- **2nd leading cause** of under-5 mortality (after pneumonia)
- **Rotavirus** is #1 cause of severe diarrhea in children
- ORS + Zinc = Cornerstone of treatment
- **Antibiotics needed in <10%** of cases (bloody diarrhea, cholera)
- Continued feeding reduces duration and improves outcomes
- Hand hygiene and safe water prevent most cases

## Etiology

### Viral (Most Common - 70%)
| Virus | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Rotavirus** | #1 cause; peak 6-24 months; winter; watery profuse |
| Norovirus | Outbreaks, vomiting prominent, all ages |
| Adenovirus (40, 41) | Prolonged diarrhea, respiratory symptoms |
| Astrovirus | Milder, young children |

### Bacterial
| Organism | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **E. coli (ETEC)** | Travelers' diarrhea, watery |
| **E. coli (EPEC)** | Infantile diarrhea, persistent |
| **E. coli (EHEC/STEC)** | Bloody diarrhea, HUS (O157:H7) |
| **Shigella** | Dysentery, high fever, tenesmus |
| **Salmonella** | Food-borne, bloody, immunocompromised |
| **Vibrio cholerae** | Rice-water stools, severe dehydration |
| **Campylobacter** | Bloody, Guillain-Barré association |

### Parasitic
| Organism | Features |
|----------|----------|
| **Giardia lamblia** | Persistent, fatty stools, bloating |
| **Entamoeba histolytica** | Amoebic dysentery, liver abscess |
| **Cryptosporidium** | Persistent, immunocompromised |

## Clinical Assessment

### Dehydration Assessment (WHO)

| Sign | No Dehydration | Some Dehydration | Severe Dehydration |
|------|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **General condition** | Well, alert | Restless, irritable | Lethargic, unconscious |
| **Eyes** | Normal | Sunken | Very sunken, dry |
| **Thirst** | Drinks normally | Thirsty, drinks eagerly | Unable to drink |
| **Skin pinch** | Goes back quickly | Goes back slowly (<2 sec) | Goes back very slowly (>2 sec) |

**Classification:**
- **No dehydration:** 0-1 signs → Plan A
- **Some dehydration:** ≥2 signs → Plan B
- **Severe dehydration:** ≥2 signs including lethargy/unable to drink → Plan C

### Estimated Fluid Deficit
| Dehydration | % Body Weight Loss | Deficit (mL/kg) |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Mild | 3-5% | 30-50 |
| Moderate | 5-10% | 50-100 |
| Severe | >10% | >100 |

### Red Flags (Need Further Evaluation)
- Bloody stools
- High fever (>39°C)
- Age <2 months
- Severe malnutrition
- Immunocompromised
- Failed oral rehydration
- Severe dehydration

## Diagnosis

### Clinical
- History of acute onset watery/loose stools
- Associated vomiting, fever, abdominal pain
- Assess hydration status
- Look for dysentery (blood/mucus)

### Laboratory (When Indicated)
| Test | Indication |
|------|------------|
| Stool microscopy | Dysentery, persistent diarrhea |
| Stool culture | Dysentery, outbreaks, severe |
| Rotavirus antigen | Epidemiological, hospitalized |
| Electrolytes | Severe dehydration, IV fluids |
| Blood culture | Suspected bacteremia/sepsis |

### Stool Characteristics
| Type | Organism |
|------|----------|
| Watery, profuse | Rotavirus, ETEC, Cholera |
| Rice-water | Cholera |
| Mucoid, bloody | Shigella, EIEC, Amoeba |
| Foul-smelling, fatty | Giardia |

## Management

### WHO Treatment Plans

#### Plan A - No Dehydration (Home Treatment)
**4 Rules:**
1. **Give extra fluids** (ORS, home fluids)
   - <2 years: 50-100 mL after each loose stool
   - ≥2 years: 100-200 mL after each loose stool
2. **Give Zinc** for 10-14 days
3. **Continue feeding** (breastfeeding, age-appropriate diet)
4. **Return if:** Blood in stool, drinking poorly, becomes sicker, not better in 3 days

#### Plan B - Some Dehydration (ORS in Health Facility)
**ORS Amount (first 4 hours):**
```
= Weight (kg) × 75 mL

Or by age (approximate):
<4 months: 200-400 mL
4-11 months: 400-600 mL
12-23 months: 600-800 mL
2-4 years: 800-1200 mL
5-14 years: 1200-2200 mL
```

- Reassess after 4 hours
- If improved → Plan A
- If same → Repeat Plan B
- If worse → Plan C

#### Plan C - Severe Dehydration (IV Fluids)
**IV Rehydration:**
```
Ringer's Lactate or Normal Saline

Infants <12 months:
- First 30 mL/kg in 1 hour (repeat if pulse still weak)
- Then 70 mL/kg over 5 hours
- Total: 100 mL/kg over 6 hours

Children 1-5 years:
- First 30 mL/kg in 30 minutes
- Then 70 mL/kg over 2.5 hours
- Total: 100 mL/kg over 3 hours
```

- Start ORS as soon as child can drink
- Reassess every 15-30 minutes
- Once rehydrated → continue Plan A/B

### ORS Composition (Low-Osmolarity WHO-ORS)

| Component | Amount (per liter) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Sodium | 75 mmol/L |
| Chloride | 65 mmol/L |
| Glucose | 75 mmol/L |
| Potassium | 20 mmol/L |
| Citrate | 10 mmol/L |
| Osmolarity | 245 mOsm/L |

**Advantages of Low-Osmolarity ORS:**
- Reduces stool output by 25%
- Reduces vomiting by 30%
- Reduces need for IV fluids by 33%

### Zinc Supplementation (Essential!)

| Age | Dose | Duration |
|-----|------|----------|
| <6 months | 10 mg/day | 10-14 days |
| ≥6 months | 20 mg/day | 10-14 days |

**Benefits:**
- Reduces duration by 25%
- Reduces stool output by 30%
- Reduces recurrence for next 2-3 months

### Continued Feeding
- **Breastfeeding:** Continue, increase frequency
- **Formula-fed:** Continue regular formula (no dilution)
- **Older children:** Age-appropriate diet, avoid high-sugar foods
- **DO NOT starve the child**

### Antibiotics (Only When Indicated!)

| Indication | Antibiotic | Dose |
|------------|------------|------|
| **Bloody diarrhea (Shigella)** | Ciprofloxacin | 15 mg/kg BD × 3 days |
| Alternative | Azithromycin | 10 mg/kg/day × 3 days |
| **Cholera** | Azithromycin | 20 mg/kg single dose |
| Alternative | Doxycycline (>8 yrs) | 6 mg/kg single dose |
| **Amoebic dysentery** | Metronidazole | 10 mg/kg TDS × 5 days |
| **Giardiasis** | Metronidazole | 5 mg/kg TDS × 5 days |

**DO NOT give antibiotics for:**
- Watery diarrhea (viral, self-limiting)
- Mild bacterial diarrhea
- Routine ETEC

### What NOT to Give
- ❌ Anti-diarrheals (Loperamide) - Dangerous in children
- ❌ Antiemetics (Routine) - May mask dehydration
- ❌ Antibiotics (Routine)
- ❌ IV fluids for mild-moderate dehydration

## Prevention

### Proven Strategies
1. **Exclusive breastfeeding** (first 6 months)
2. **Rotavirus vaccination**
3. **Safe water and sanitation**
4. **Hand hygiene** (soap and water)
5. **Safe food handling**
6. **Zinc supplementation** (reduces future episodes)
7. **Vitamin A supplementation**

### Rotavirus Vaccine
| Vaccine | Doses | Schedule |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Rotavac (Indian) | 3 doses | 6, 10, 14 weeks |
| Rotarix | 2 doses | 6, 10 weeks |
| RotaTeq | 3 doses | 6, 10, 14 weeks |

**MCQ Pearls:**
- ❗ Low-osmolarity ORS reduces stool output by 25%
- ❗ Zinc reduces diarrhea duration AND future episodes
- ❗ Continued feeding is essential (DO NOT starve)
- ❗ Bloody diarrhea = Dysentery = Shigella until proven otherwise
- ❗ Rice-water stools + severe dehydration = Cholera
- ❗ E. coli O157:H7 → HUS (don't give antibiotics!)
- ❗ Rotavirus vaccine = Best prevention for severe diarrhea
- ❗ Skin pinch going back slowly = Some dehydration
- ❗ Loperamide is CONTRAINDICATED in children
