# Childhood Asthma — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common chronic disease in children. Triad: airway inflammation + reversible obstruction + hyperresponsiveness. ICS = mainstay controller. SABA = rescue. Spirometry: FEV1/FVC <80%, reversibility ≥12%. GINA stepwise approach. Spacer + MDI preferred over nebulizer. Acute: O₂ + SABA + ipratropium + systemic steroids. Silent chest = life-threatening. Montelukast = LTRA alternative.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **ICS (Inhaled Corticosteroids)** = mainstay of long-term control
- ★ **SABA reversibility ≥12%** in FEV1 = diagnostic
- ★ **Silent chest** = life-threatening (no air movement — impending respiratory failure)
- ★ **Spacer + MDI** = as effective as nebulizer (preferred in children)
- ★ **Montelukast** = leukotriene receptor antagonist; alternative to ICS in mild persistent
- ★ **Viral infections** = most common trigger in young children
- ★ **House dust mite** = most common allergen trigger
- ★ **Step up after 2-3 months** if uncontrolled; step down after 3 months well-controlled
- ★ **IV MgSO₄** = for severe/life-threatening acute asthma
- ★ **FeNO** = marker of eosinophilic airway inflammation
- ★ **Oral candidiasis** from ICS — prevent with spacer and mouth rinse

## Mnemonics

### **ASTHMA** — Triggers
- **A**llergens (dust mite, pollen, pets)
- **S**moke (tobacco, indoor pollution)
- **T**emperature (cold air)
- **H**ypersensitivity (exercise, drugs — NSAIDs, β-blockers)
- **M**icrobes (viral infections — RSV, rhinovirus)
- **A**ir pollution

### Acute Rx Steps: "O-SIPS"
- **O**xygen (SpO₂ 94-98%)
- **S**ABA (salbutamol nebulized/MDI)
- **I**pratropium (if moderate-severe)
- **P**rednisolone (systemic steroids)
- **S**evere → IV MgSO₄, aminophylline

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Mainstay controller** — Inhaled corticosteroids
2. **Rescue drug** — SABA (salbutamol)
3. **Spirometric diagnosis** — FEV1 reversibility ≥12% post-SABA
4. **Silent chest** — life-threatening sign
5. **Spacer vs nebulizer** — equally effective; spacer preferred
6. **Most common trigger in young children** — Viral infections
7. **LTRA** — Montelukast (alternative to ICS in mild)
8. **IV MgSO₄ indication** — Severe/life-threatening acute asthma
9. **ICS side effects** — Oral candidiasis, growth suppression (minimal at low dose)
10. **Methacholine challenge** — positive if PC20 <8 mg/mL (bronchial hyperresponsiveness)
