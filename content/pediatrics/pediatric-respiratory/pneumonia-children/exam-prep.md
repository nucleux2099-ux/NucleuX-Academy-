# Pneumonia in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Leading infectious cause of under-5 death. S. pneumoniae = most common bacterial cause. RSV = most common viral cause. WHO: tachypnea = most reliable sign. Non-severe: oral amoxicillin. Severe: IV ampicillin + gentamicin. Mycoplasma = atypical, >5 years, azithromycin. PCV + Hib vaccines = key prevention.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Tachypnea thresholds:** <2m: ≥60, 2-12m: ≥50, 1-5y: ≥40
- ★ **S. pneumoniae** = most common bacterial cause at ALL ages
- ★ **RSV** = most common cause overall in <2 years
- ★ **Amoxicillin** = first-line for non-severe pneumonia
- ★ **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** = WHO first-line for severe pneumonia
- ★ **Pneumatoceles** = S. aureus pneumonia (also empyema, pneumothorax)
- ★ **Mycoplasma** = >5 years, dry cough, "walking pneumonia," cold agglutinins
- ★ **Staccato cough + eosinophilia + afebrile** = Chlamydia trachomatis (infant)
- ★ **Round pneumonia** = S. pneumoniae in children (mimics mass on CXR)
- ★ **SpO₂ <90%** = severe pneumonia (needs oxygen)
- ★ **PCV vaccine** = most effective preventive intervention
