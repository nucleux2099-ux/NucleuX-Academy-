# Foreign Body Aspiration — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Peak age 1-3 years. Most common FB: peanuts. Most common site: right main bronchus. CXR: obstructive emphysema (most common finding); may be normal in 30-50%. Rigid bronchoscopy = gold standard (diagnostic + therapeutic). Choking episode in history = most important clue. <1 year: back blows + chest thrusts. >1 year: Heimlich maneuver.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Right main bronchus** = most common site (wider, shorter, more vertical)
- ★ **Peanuts** = most common aspirated FB in children
- ★ **Obstructive emphysema** = most common CXR finding (ball-valve mechanism)
- ★ **CXR normal in 30-50%** — do bronchoscopy if clinical suspicion high
- ★ **Expiratory film** more sensitive than inspiratory (shows air trapping)
- ★ **Rigid bronchoscopy** = gold standard for diagnosis AND removal
- ★ **3 phases:** Acute choking → asymptomatic interval → complications
- ★ **Unilateral wheeze** in a previously well child = think FBA
- ★ **Recurrent pneumonia** in same lobe = suspect retained FB
- ★ **Heimlich maneuver** for >1 year; back blows + chest thrusts for <1 year
- ★ **No blind finger sweeps** — may push FB deeper

## Mnemonics

### **RIGHT** = Right Main Bronchus
- **R**ight is wider, shorter, more vertical
- FB "falls right"

### **3 Phases:** "Choke → Quiet → Sick"
1. Acute choking (cough, gag)
2. Asymptomatic interval (dangerous — misleading)
3. Complications (pneumonia, abscess, bronchiectasis)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common site** — Right main bronchus
2. **Most common FB** — Peanut/organic material
3. **Most common CXR finding** — Obstructive emphysema
4. **Gold standard** — Rigid bronchoscopy
5. **Choking in <1 year** — Back blows + chest thrusts
6. **Choking in >1 year** — Heimlich maneuver
7. **Normal CXR** — Does NOT exclude FBA
8. **Ball-valve mechanism** — Air enters but can't exit → hyperinflation
9. **Organic FB** — Cause more inflammation, swell with moisture
10. **Recurrent same-lobe pneumonia** — Suspect foreign body
