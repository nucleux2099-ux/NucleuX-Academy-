# Upper Respiratory Infections — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common infection in children (6-8/year). Rhinovirus = most common cause of common cold. Most are viral — no antibiotics. GAS pharyngitis: only one needing antibiotics (prevent rheumatic fever). Croup: parainfluenza, barking cough, steeple sign, dexamethasone + nebulized epinephrine. Epiglottitis: Hib, drooling, thumb sign, airway emergency. AOM: amoxicillin first-line.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Rhinovirus** = most common cause of common cold
- ★ **Parainfluenza** = most common cause of croup
- ★ **GAS pharyngitis:** NO cough/rhinorrhea; tonsillar exudates; palatal petechiae
- ★ **Steeple sign** = croup (subglottic narrowing on AP neck X-ray)
- ★ **Thumb sign** = epiglottitis (lateral neck X-ray)
- ★ **Croup Rx:** Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg (single dose) ± nebulized epinephrine
- ★ **Epiglottitis:** Do NOT examine throat; secure airway first; IV ceftriaxone
- ★ **AOM organisms:** S. pneumoniae > H. influenzae > Moraxella
- ★ **AOM Rx:** Amoxicillin 80-90 mg/kg/day
- ★ **GAS Rx:** Penicillin V × 10 days (prevents RF; effective up to day 9)
- ★ **Yellow/green nasal discharge** does NOT mean bacterial infection

## Mnemonics

### Croup vs Epiglottitis: "Croup = Cool, Epiglottitis = Emergency"
- Croup: gradual, barking cough, viral, steeple sign
- Epiglottitis: sudden, drooling, bacterial (Hib), thumb sign, EMERGENCY

### GAS vs Viral: "GAS has NO Cough" (absence of cough favors GAS)

### AOM organisms: "SHaM" — S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, Moraxella
