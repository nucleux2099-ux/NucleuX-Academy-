# Wheezing in Children — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
50% of children wheeze by age 6. Most are transient viral wheezers. RSV bronchiolitis = most common cause <1 year. Asthma = most common cause of recurrent wheeze after infancy. Bronchiolitis: supportive only (NO bronchodilators, NO steroids). Unilateral wheeze = foreign body. mAPI predicts asthma persistence. ICS trial for likely asthma. Clubbing = NOT asthma (think CF).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **RSV** = most common cause of bronchiolitis (60-80%)
- ★ **Bronchiolitis management = SUPPORTIVE ONLY** (O₂, fluids, suctioning)
- ★ **NO bronchodilators, NO steroids** for bronchiolitis (evidence-based)
- ★ **Palivizumab** = RSV prophylaxis for high-risk infants
- ★ **Unilateral wheeze** = foreign body aspiration until proven otherwise
- ★ **mAPI positive** = ~80% chance of asthma at school age
- ★ **Transient wheezers** resolve by age 3-6 (no atopy, viral trigger only)
- ★ **Clubbing** = NOT asthma; think CF, bronchiectasis
- ★ **Bronchodilator reversibility** = diagnostic of asthma
- ★ **Response to SABA trial** differentiates asthma from other causes

## Mnemonics

### Wheezing NOT Asthma: "ABCDEF"
- **A**natomy (tracheomalacia, vascular ring)
- **B**ronchiolitis (RSV)
- **C**ystic fibrosis
- **D**rug/toxin aspiration, GERD
- **E**xternal compression (lymph nodes, tumor)
- **F**oreign body

### mAPI Major Criteria: "PAD"
- **P**arent with asthma
- **A**topic dermatitis
- **D**ust mite/aeroallergen sensitization
