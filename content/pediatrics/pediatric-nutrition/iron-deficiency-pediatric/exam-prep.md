# Iron Deficiency Anemia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common nutritional anemia worldwide. Microcytic hypochromic anemia. Serum ferritin = best single test (earliest to fall). TIBC elevated, transferrin saturation <16%. Peak age 6-24 months. Treatment: elemental iron 3-6 mg/kg/day, continue 3 months after Hb normalizes. Reticulocyte peak at day 5-7 = earliest sign of response. Differentiate from thalassemia trait by RDW (↑ in IDA) and Mentzer index (>13 in IDA).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Serum ferritin** = best single test for iron stores (first to decrease)
- ★ **Mentzer index (MCV/RBC):** >13 = IDA, <13 = thalassemia trait
- ★ **RDW elevated** in IDA, normal in thalassemia trait
- ★ **Reticulocyte peak at day 5-7** = earliest sign of treatment response
- ★ **Continue iron 3 months after Hb normalization** (replenish stores)
- ★ **Pica (pagophagia)** = characteristic of iron deficiency
- ★ **Koilonychia** = spoon-shaped nails
- ★ **Hepcidin** = master regulator of iron absorption
- ★ **Vitamin C enhances** iron absorption; tea/milk/phytates inhibit
- ★ **WIFS program** = Weekly Iron-Folic Acid for adolescents
- ★ **Delayed cord clamping** prevents neonatal iron deficiency
- ★ **Cognitive impairment** may be irreversible if IDA in first 2 years

## Mnemonics

### **IRON** — Lab Findings in IDA
- **I** — Iron (serum) decreased
- **R** — RDW raised (>14.5%)
- **O** — Overproduction of transferrin (TIBC ↑)
- **N** — No ferritin (stores depleted)

### Stages: "Store → Supply → Symptoms"
1. Storage depletion (↓ferritin only)
2. Iron-deficient erythropoiesis (↓iron, ↑TIBC)
3. Iron deficiency anemia (↓Hb, microcytic hypochromic)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Best single test** — Serum ferritin
2. **Most common cause of anemia in children** — Iron deficiency
3. **IDA vs thalassemia trait** — RDW, Mentzer index, HbA2, ferritin
4. **Peak reticulocyte response** — Day 5-7 of treatment
5. **Duration of treatment** — 3 months after Hb normalization
6. **Dose** — 3-6 mg/kg/day elemental iron
7. **Koilonychia, pica, angular stomatitis** — features of IDA
8. **Iron absorption enhancers/inhibitors** — Vit C enhances; phytates, tannins inhibit
9. **Plummer-Vinson syndrome** — dysphagia + web + IDA
10. **Blood transfusion** — Only Hb <4 or <7 with cardiac failure
