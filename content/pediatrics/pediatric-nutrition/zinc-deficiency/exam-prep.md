# Zinc Deficiency — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Zinc is essential for 300+ enzymes, immune function, and growth. Deficiency common in cereal-based diets (phytates inhibit absorption). Acrodermatitis enteropathica = AR, SLC39A4 mutation, triad: acral dermatitis + alopecia + diarrhea. WHO: Zinc 20 mg/day × 14 days with ORS for acute diarrhea (>6 months). Growth retardation is earliest sign of mild deficiency. Serum zinc <65 μg/dL = deficient. ALP decreased (zinc-dependent enzyme).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Acrodermatitis enteropathica triad:** Acral dermatitis + Alopecia + Diarrhea
- ★ **WHO diarrhea protocol:** ORS + Zinc 20 mg/day × 14 days (>6 months)
- ★ **Phytates** = main dietary inhibitor of zinc absorption
- ★ **Growth retardation** = earliest sign of mild zinc deficiency
- ★ **ALP decreased** in zinc deficiency (zinc-dependent enzyme)
- ★ **Oysters** = richest dietary source of zinc
- ★ **SLC39A4 gene** = acrodermatitis enteropathica
- ★ **Presents after weaning** in AE (breast milk has zinc-binding ligand)
- ★ **Periorificial + acral** distribution of skin lesions
- ★ **Zinc reduces diarrhea duration by ~25%** and recurrence for 2-3 months

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Acrodermatitis enteropathica** — triad, gene (SLC39A4), AR inheritance
2. **WHO zinc dose in diarrhea** — 20 mg × 14 days (>6 months), 10 mg (<6 months)
3. **Richest source** — Oysters/shellfish
4. **Main inhibitor** — Phytates in cereals
5. **Lab findings** — Low serum zinc, low ALP
6. **Skin lesion distribution** — Periorificial + acral
7. **Onset of AE symptoms** — After weaning from breast milk
8. **Growth retardation** — earliest sign of mild deficiency
9. **Zinc-Vitamin A interaction** — zinc needed for retinol-binding protein
10. **Treatment of AE** — Lifelong oral zinc supplementation
