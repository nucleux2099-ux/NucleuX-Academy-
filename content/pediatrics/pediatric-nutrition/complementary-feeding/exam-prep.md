# Complementary Feeding — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Start complementary foods at 6 months alongside continued breastfeeding (until 2 years). Iron-rich foods introduced early (fetal stores depleted by 6 months). Texture progression: mashed → chopped → family foods. Frequency: 2-3 meals (6-8m) → 3-4 meals (9-24m). Add fat (ghee/oil) to increase energy density. Minimum dietary diversity: ≥5 of 8 food groups. Avoid honey <12m (botulism), whole cow's milk <12m, fruit juice <12m.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **6 months** = WHO/IAP recommended age to start complementary feeding
- ★ **Iron is the most critical nutrient** gap at 6 months (fetal stores exhausted)
- ★ **Energy density** ≥0.8 kcal/g; add oil/ghee to traditional porridges
- ★ **Minimum dietary diversity** = ≥5 of 8 food groups in 24 hours
- ★ **Honey before 12 months** → infantile botulism (Clostridium botulinum)
- ★ **Cow's milk before 12 months** → iron-poor, high renal solute load, occult GI bleeding
- ★ **Extrusion reflex** disappears by 4-6 months → readiness for solids
- ★ **Responsive feeding** = feed based on hunger/satiety cues, not force-feeding
- ★ **Meal frequency:** 2-3 (6-8m), 3-4 (9-24m), 4+ (non-breastfed)
- ★ **Growth faltering** peaks at 6-18 months — complementary feeding window

## Mnemonics

### **TIMELY SAFE** — WHO CF Principles
- **T**imely (at 6 months)
- **A**dequate (meet energy/nutrient needs)
- **S**afe (hygienic preparation)
- **F**ed properly (responsive, right texture/frequency)

### **6-6-6 Rule**
- **6** months: start complementary feeding
- **6** months: fetal iron stores last this long
- By **6** months: extrusion reflex gone, GI tract mature

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **When to start** — 6 months (not 4, not 8)
2. **Most important nutrient to supplement** — Iron
3. **Honey contraindication** — <12 months (botulism)
4. **Cow's milk contraindication** — <12 months as main drink
5. **Energy density** — ≥0.8 kcal/g
6. **Extrusion reflex** — disappears by 4-6 months
7. **First food in India** — khichdi/mashed rice-dal with ghee
8. **Minimum dietary diversity** — ≥5 of 8 food groups
9. **Growth faltering window** — 6-18 months
10. **Signs of readiness** — head control, sitting, interest in food, loss of extrusion reflex
