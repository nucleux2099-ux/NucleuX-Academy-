# Vitamin D Deficiency Rickets — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Rickets = defective mineralization of growing bones due to Vitamin D deficiency. Earliest clinical sign: craniotabes. Earliest biochemical change: hypophosphatemia. Best marker of Vit D status: serum 25(OH)D. Most sensitive marker of activity: ALP. X-ray wrist: cupping, fraying, splaying. Treatment: Stoss therapy (60,000 IU/day × 10 days) + calcium. Active form: 1,25(OH)₂D (calcitriol) made in kidney.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Craniotabes** = earliest clinical sign (ping-pong ball sensation on skull)
- ★ **Hypophosphatemia** = earliest biochemical change
- ★ **Serum 25(OH)D** = best indicator of Vitamin D status
- ★ **ALP** = most sensitive marker of disease activity
- ★ **Rachitic rosary** = beading at costochondral junctions
- ★ **Harrison's sulcus** = diaphragmatic groove on softened ribs
- ★ **Wrist X-ray** — cupping, fraying, splaying of metaphysis
- ★ **Breast milk** has insufficient Vitamin D — supplement 400 IU/day
- ★ **Anticonvulsants** (phenytoin, phenobarbital) cause Vit D deficiency
- ★ **1α-hydroxylase** in kidney converts 25(OH)D → active 1,25(OH)₂D
- ★ **Hungry bone syndrome** — give calcium with Vit D to prevent hypocalcemia
- ★ **Bow legs** (<3 yrs) vs **Knock knees** (>3 yrs)

## Mnemonics

### **RICKETS** — Clinical Features
- **R** — Rachitic rosary
- **I** — Irritability, infections (recurrent)
- **C** — Craniotabes, carpopedal spasm
- **K** — Knock knees / bow legs
- **E** — Enlarged wrists and ankles
- **T** — Tetany (hypocalcemic seizures)
- **S** — Short stature, Harrison's sulcus

### Vitamin D Metabolism: "Skin → Liver → Kidney"
- Skin: UV-B → D3
- Liver: 25-hydroxylase → 25(OH)D (storage form, best marker)
- Kidney: 1α-hydroxylase → 1,25(OH)₂D (active form)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Earliest clinical sign** — Craniotabes
2. **Earliest biochemical change** — Hypophosphatemia
3. **Best marker of Vit D status** — Serum 25(OH)D
4. **Most sensitive marker** — Alkaline phosphatase (ALP)
5. **X-ray findings** — Cupping, fraying, splaying (wrist)
6. **Active form location** — 1,25(OH)₂D formed in kidney
7. **VDDR Type I** — 1α-hydroxylase deficiency (↓1,25(OH)₂D, normal 25(OH)D)
8. **VDDR Type II** — VDR receptor defect (↑↑1,25(OH)₂D, alopecia)
9. **Hypophosphatemic rickets** — X-linked, PHEX gene, normal Ca, low PO₄
10. **Anticonvulsant rickets** — Phenytoin induces hepatic enzymes → ↑Vit D catabolism
11. **Renal rickets** — CKD, high phosphate, low calcium, low 1,25(OH)₂D
12. **Stoss therapy** — 60,000 IU/day × 10 days OR single mega-dose
