# Vitamin D Deficiency Rickets

## Overview
Rickets is a disease of growing bones caused by defective mineralization of the growth plate and osteoid, most commonly due to Vitamin D deficiency. It remains prevalent in India despite abundant sunlight, due to dark skin pigmentation, limited sun exposure, and dietary deficiency. It presents in the first 2 years of life with skeletal deformities, growth failure, and hypocalcemic manifestations.

## Key Points
- **Most common metabolic bone disease** in children
- Affects growing bones — presents before epiphyseal closure
- **Active form:** 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D3 (calcitriol) — formed in kidneys
- **Earliest biochemical change:** ↓phosphate (hypophosphatemia)
- **Earliest radiological sign:** Fraying and cupping of metaphysis (wrist)
- **Craniotabes** — earliest clinical sign (softening of skull bones)
- **Rachitic rosary, Harrison's sulcus, bow legs** — classic skeletal signs
- **Serum 25(OH)D** — best indicator of Vitamin D status
- **Treatment:** Stoss therapy (single mega-dose) or daily supplementation

## Definition & Classification

### Types of Rickets
| Type | Cause | Calcium | Phosphate | ALP | PTH | 25(OH)D | 1,25(OH)₂D |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|-----|-----|---------|------------|
| **Nutritional (Calcipenic)** | Vit D deficiency | ↓/N | ↓ | ↑↑ | ↑↑ | ↓↓ | ↓/N/↑ |
| **Hypophosphatemic (XLH)** | PHEX gene mutation | N | ↓↓ | ↑ | N | N | Inappropriately N |
| **Vit D Dependent Type I** | 1α-hydroxylase deficiency | ↓ | ↓ | ↑↑ | ↑↑ | N | ↓↓ |
| **Vit D Dependent Type II** | VDR receptor defect | ↓ | ↓ | ↑↑ | ↑↑ | N | ↑↑ |
| **Renal Rickets** | CKD | ↓ | ↑ | ↑ | ↑↑ | N | ↓ |

### Vitamin D Levels
| Serum 25(OH)D | Status |
|---------------|--------|
| >30 ng/mL | Sufficient |
| 20-30 ng/mL | Insufficient |
| <20 ng/mL | Deficient |
| <10 ng/mL | Severely deficient |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Sources of Vitamin D
- **Sunlight (UV-B):** 7-dehydrocholesterol → Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol) in skin — 80% of requirement
- **Diet:** Fatty fish, egg yolk, liver, fortified milk/cereals
- **Vitamin D2 (ergocalciferol):** Plant/fungal sources
- **Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol):** Animal sources, skin synthesis

### Metabolism
1. Skin: 7-DHC → Vitamin D3 (UV-B radiation)
2. Liver: Vitamin D3 → **25(OH)D** (calcidiol) — by 25-hydroxylase — best marker of status
3. Kidney: 25(OH)D → **1,25(OH)₂D** (calcitriol) — by 1α-hydroxylase — active form
4. Calcitriol acts on intestine (↑Ca²⁺/PO₄ absorption), bone (mineralization), kidney (Ca²⁺ reabsorption)

### Risk Factors
- Dark skin pigmentation (melanin blocks UV-B)
- Exclusive breastfeeding without supplementation (breast milk has low Vit D)
- Limited sun exposure (indoor lifestyle, clothing habits, urban pollution)
- Maternal Vitamin D deficiency
- Vegetarian diet, no fortified foods
- Malabsorption (celiac, CF, biliary disease)
- Anticonvulsant therapy (phenytoin, phenobarbital — ↑Vit D catabolism)
- CKD (impaired 1α-hydroxylase)
- Prematurity

### Pathophysiology
1. ↓Vitamin D → ↓intestinal calcium absorption → **hypocalcemia**
2. Hypocalcemia → ↑PTH (secondary hyperparathyroidism)
3. PTH effects: ↑bone resorption, ↑renal Ca reabsorption, ↑phosphaturia → **hypophosphatemia**
4. ↓Calcium × Phosphate product → **defective mineralization** of growth plate
5. Growth plate: Proliferating cartilage cannot mineralize → widened, disorganized → **cupping, fraying**
6. Osteoid accumulates unmineralized → **osteomalacia** component → soft bones

## Clinical Features

### Skeletal Manifestations
- **Craniotabes:** Ping-pong ball sensation on pressing parietal/occipital bones (earliest sign, <1 year)
- **Frontal bossing:** Prominent forehead
- **Delayed fontanelle closure:** Anterior fontanelle open beyond 18 months
- **Rachitic rosary:** Beading at costochondral junctions (visible and palpable)
- **Harrison's sulcus:** Horizontal groove along lower chest (diaphragm pulls softened ribs)
- **Pigeon chest (pectus carinatum):** Sternum protrudes forward
- **Bow legs (genu varum):** <3 years; **Knock knees (genu valgum):** >3 years
- **Widening of wrists and ankles:** Metaphyseal expansion
- **Delayed dentition, enamel hypoplasia, caries**
- **Kyphoscoliosis**
- **Green-stick fractures**

### Non-Skeletal Manifestations
- **Hypocalcemic tetany/seizures:** Carpopedal spasm, laryngospasm, generalized seizures
- **Hypotonia and muscle weakness:** Pot belly, delayed motor milestones, waddling gait
- **Growth failure:** Short stature
- **Delayed milestones:** Motor delay (sitting, standing, walking)
- **Irritability**
- **Dental:** Delayed eruption, enamel defects
- **Anemia:** Associated iron/folate deficiency
- **Recurrent respiratory infections:** Due to chest wall deformity

### Signs of Latent Tetany
- **Chvostek's sign:** Tapping facial nerve → twitching of facial muscles
- **Trousseau's sign:** BP cuff inflation → carpopedal spasm
- **Peroneal sign:** Tapping peroneal nerve → dorsiflexion of foot

## Diagnosis

### Biochemical
| Parameter | Finding |
|-----------|---------|
| Serum 25(OH)D | ↓↓ (<20 ng/mL) — best marker |
| Serum calcium | ↓ or normal (maintained by PTH) |
| Serum phosphate | ↓ (earliest biochemical change) |
| Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) | ↑↑ (most sensitive marker of disease activity) |
| PTH | ↑↑ (secondary hyperparathyroidism) |
| Urinary calcium | ↓ |

### Radiological (Wrist X-ray — most informative)
- **Cupping:** Concave metaphyseal ends
- **Fraying:** Irregular, brush-like metaphyseal margin
- **Splaying:** Widened metaphysis
- **Widened growth plate:** Increased distance between metaphysis and epiphysis
- **Osteopenia:** Generalized decreased bone density
- **Green-stick fractures**
- **Looser zones (Milkman's pseudofractures):** In osteomalacia

### Healing Signs on X-ray
- Appearance of provisional zone of calcification (white line)
- Improvement in cupping and fraying (4-6 weeks after treatment)

## Management

### Treatment of Nutritional Rickets

#### Stoss Therapy (Single Mega-Dose)
- **Vitamin D3:** 60,000 IU/day × 10 days (oral) OR single dose 3-6 lakh IU
- **Alternative:** 1,000-2,000 IU/day for 3-6 months

#### Calcium Supplementation
- Oral calcium 50-75 mg/kg/day elemental calcium for 2-4 weeks
- Essential in early treatment to prevent **hungry bone syndrome** (hypocalcemia as bones remineralize)

#### Monitoring
- ALP — first to normalize (6-8 weeks)
- Radiological healing — 4-6 weeks (provisional zone of calcification appears)
- Complete radiological healing — 3-6 months
- Serum 25(OH)D — recheck at 3 months

### Prevention
- **Sunlight exposure:** 30 minutes/day (face and arms)
- **Vitamin D supplementation (AAP/IAP):**
  - All breastfed infants: 400 IU/day from birth
  - Preterm/LBW: 400-800 IU/day
- **Maternal supplementation** during pregnancy/lactation
- **Dietary:** Fortified milk, egg yolk, fish

### Treatment of Hypocalcemic Emergency
- **IV calcium gluconate 10%:** 1-2 mL/kg slow IV over 10 min (with cardiac monitoring)
- Follow with oral calcium + Vitamin D

## Complications
- **Skeletal deformities:** Permanent if untreated (bow legs, knock knees, short stature)
- **Pathological fractures**
- **Hypocalcemic seizures** — life-threatening
- **Laryngospasm** — acute airway emergency
- **Dilated cardiomyopathy** (chronic severe hypocalcemia)
- **Dental abnormalities**
- **Growth failure and short stature**
- **Hypervitaminosis D** (overtreatment): Hypercalcemia → nephrocalcinosis, renal failure
