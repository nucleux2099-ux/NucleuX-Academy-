# Iodine Deficiency Disorders

## Overview
Iodine Deficiency Disorders (IDD) encompass a spectrum of conditions resulting from inadequate iodine intake, ranging from goiter to cretinism. Iodine is essential for thyroid hormone synthesis, which is critical for brain development, growth, and metabolism. India is one of the most affected countries, with the sub-Himalayan belt being an endemic goiter zone. The National Iodine Deficiency Disorders Control Programme (NIDDCP) mandates universal salt iodization.

## Key Points
- **Most common preventable cause of mental retardation** worldwide
- **Critical period:** Fetal life and first 2 years — irreversible brain damage
- **Cretinism** = severe iodine deficiency in fetal/early life
- **Daily iodine requirement:** 90-150 μg/day (children), 250 μg (pregnancy)
- **Urinary iodine excretion** — best indicator of community iodine status
- **Universal Salt Iodization (USI)** — most effective prevention strategy
- **Iodized salt:** ≥15 ppm iodine at consumer level
- **NIDDCP** replaced NGOCP (National Goiter Control Programme) in 1992

## Definition & Classification

### Spectrum of IDD
| Stage of Life | Manifestation |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Fetus** | Abortions, stillbirths, congenital anomalies, cretinism |
| **Neonate** | Neonatal hypothyroidism, neonatal goiter |
| **Child** | Goiter, hypothyroidism, mental retardation, growth retardation |
| **Adolescent** | Goiter, impaired mental function, delayed puberty |
| **Adult** | Goiter, hypothyroidism, impaired mental function |

### Types of Cretinism
| Feature | Neurological Cretinism | Myxedematous Cretinism |
|---------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Mental retardation** | Severe | Severe |
| **Deaf-mutism** | Present | Absent/mild |
| **Spastic diplegia** | Present | Absent |
| **Squint** | Present | Absent |
| **Goiter** | Present/absent | Absent (atrophic thyroid) |
| **Growth retardation** | Moderate | Severe |
| **Myxedema** | Absent | Present |
| **TSH** | Normal/↑ | ↑↑ |
| **T4** | Normal/↓ | ↓↓ |
| **Mechanism** | Fetal brain damage (1st trimester) | Fetal + postnatal thyroid failure |

### Goiter Grading (WHO)
| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 0 | No palpable or visible goiter |
| 1 | Palpable but not visible with neck in normal position |
| 2 | Visible with neck in normal position |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Causes of Iodine Deficiency
- **Geographic:** Mountainous regions, flood-prone areas (iodine leached from soil)
- **Dietary:** Goitrogens — cabbage, cauliflower, cassava (thiocyanates), soybean
- **Water supply:** Low iodine content in drinking water
- **Soil depletion:** Sub-Himalayan belt, Indo-Gangetic plains

### Iodine Metabolism
1. Dietary iodine → absorbed from GI tract → taken up by thyroid (iodide trap)
2. Thyroid: Iodide → organification → MIT, DIT → T3, T4
3. 90% of body iodine is in thyroid gland
4. Excreted primarily in urine (>90%)

### Pathophysiology
1. ↓Iodine → ↓T3, T4 synthesis → ↑TSH (feedback)
2. ↑TSH → thyroid hyperplasia → **goiter**
3. Fetal brain development critically depends on maternal T4 (first trimester) then fetal thyroid
4. Severe deficiency in pregnancy → **cretinism** (irreversible brain damage)
5. Thyroid hormones essential for: myelination, neuronal migration, synaptogenesis, cerebellar development

### Daily Iodine Requirements (WHO)
| Group | RDA (μg/day) |
|-------|-------------|
| Children 0-5 years | 90 |
| Children 6-12 years | 120 |
| Adolescents/Adults | 150 |
| Pregnant/Lactating | 250 |

## Clinical Features

### Goiter
- Diffuse, non-tender thyroid enlargement
- Most common manifestation of IDD
- Grade 1 → Grade 2 progression
- May cause compressive symptoms (dysphagia, stridor) if large

### Hypothyroidism
- Lethargy, cold intolerance, constipation
- Dry skin, coarse hair
- Weight gain, growth retardation
- Delayed milestones, poor school performance
- Delayed puberty or precocious puberty

### Cretinism (Severe IDD)
- **Severe mental retardation** (IQ <70)
- **Short stature** (dwarfism)
- **Coarse facial features**
- **Large tongue (macroglossia)**
- **Umbilical hernia**
- **Deaf-mutism** (neurological type)
- **Spastic diplegia, squint** (neurological type)
- **Myxedema** (myxedematous type)
- **Delayed bone age, epiphyseal dysgenesis**

### Subclinical IDD
- Impaired cognitive function without overt hypothyroidism
- Lower IQ scores (10-15 points)
- Poor scholastic performance
- Impaired hearing

## Diagnosis

### Community Level (Epidemiological)
- **Urinary iodine excretion (UIE):** Best indicator of community iodine status
  - <100 μg/L: Insufficient
  - 100-199 μg/L: Adequate
  - 200-299 μg/L: More than adequate
  - ≥300 μg/L: Excessive
- **Total goiter rate (TGR):** ≥5% in school children = endemic area
- **Median UIE <100 μg/L** = iodine deficiency in population

### Individual Level
- **Thyroid function:** TSH (↑), T4 (↓), T3 (↓ or normal)
- **Neonatal TSH screening:** >5 mIU/L in >3% of neonates suggests community IDD
- **Thyroid ultrasound:** Thyroid volume (age/sex-specific)
- **Urinary iodine:** Spot sample (individual variation high — better for population)

### Salt Testing
- **Spot test kit:** Detects iodine in salt (starch-iodine reaction)
- **Iodometric titration:** Quantitative — ≥15 ppm at consumer level required

## Management

### Prevention (Most Important)
1. **Universal Salt Iodization (USI):**
   - Potassium iodate (KIO₃) added to salt
   - ≥30 ppm at production, ≥15 ppm at consumer level
   - Most cost-effective public health intervention for IDD
2. **Iodized oil:** Oral/IM in severely endemic areas (Lipiodol)
   - Oral: 200-400 mg iodine, lasts 1 year
   - IM: 480 mg, lasts 3-5 years
3. **Iodine supplementation in pregnancy:** 250 μg/day

### Treatment
- **Goiter (non-cretinous):** Iodine supplementation → regression over months
- **Hypothyroidism:** Levothyroxine replacement
- **Cretinism:** Early detection + levothyroxine (brain damage is irreversible, but growth/metabolic issues treatable)

### NIDDCP (National Iodine Deficiency Disorders Control Programme)
- Renamed from NGOCP in 1992
- Universal salt iodization strategy
- Ban on sale of non-iodized salt for human consumption
- Monitoring through urinary iodine surveys and salt testing

## Complications
- **Irreversible mental retardation** (cretinism)
- **Deaf-mutism**
- **Growth failure**
- **Increased perinatal mortality**
- **Stillbirths, abortions**
- **Iodine-induced hyperthyroidism** (Jod-Basedow phenomenon — in previously deficient individuals given excess iodine)
- **Goiter complications:** Tracheal compression, cosmetic disfigurement
