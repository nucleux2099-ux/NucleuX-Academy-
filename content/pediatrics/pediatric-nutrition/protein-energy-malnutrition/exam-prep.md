# Protein Energy Malnutrition — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
PEM is the most important nutritional disorder in developing countries. SAM defined by W/H < -3SD, MUAC <11.5cm, or bilateral edema. Marasmus = total calorie deficiency (emaciated, no edema, old man's face). Kwashiorkor = protein deficiency (edema, flaky-paint dermatosis, flag sign hair, fatty liver). Management: WHO 10-Step Protocol — F-75 (stabilization) → F-100 (rehabilitation). Use ReSoMal (NOT standard ORS). NO IV fluids, NO iron in Phase 1, NO diuretics for edema. Empirical antibiotics always.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **SAM criteria:** W/H < -3SD OR MUAC <11.5 cm OR bilateral pedal edema
- ★ **Old man's face** = marasmus (loss of buccal fat pad — last fat to go)
- ★ **Flag sign** = kwashiorkor (alternating bands of pigmented/depigmented hair)
- ★ **Flaky-paint dermatosis** = kwashiorkor; crazy pavement dermatosis
- ★ **F-75 (stabilization) → F-100 (rehabilitation)** — know the kcal and protein content
- ★ **ReSoMal:** Lower Na (45), higher K (40) than standard ORS — prevents heart failure
- ★ **NO iron in Phase 1** — promotes bacterial growth; add only in Phase 2
- ★ **NO IV fluids** in SAM (unless shock) — risk of heart failure
- ★ **Hypoglycemia** = most common cause of death in first 48 hours
- ★ **Refeeding syndrome** — hypophosphatemia, cardiac failure; why cautious feeding needed
- ★ **Edema in kwashiorkor** — due to hypoalbuminemia (↓oncotic pressure)
- ★ **Fatty liver** in kwashiorkor — due to ↓lipoprotein synthesis

## Mnemonics

### **MARASMUS** — Features
- **M** — Muscle wasting (severe)
- **A** — Alert, appetite good
- **R** — Ribs visible
- **A** — Absent edema & subcutaneous fat
- **S** — Skin loose and wrinkled
- **M** — Monkey-like face (old man)
- **U** — Under 1 year common
- **S** — Starvation (total calorie deficiency)

### **KWASH** — Kwashiorkor Features
- **K** — Keratomalacia (Vitamin A deficiency)
- **W** — Weeping skin (flaky-paint dermatosis)
- **A** — Apathetic, anorexic
- **S** — Swelling (edema) + silky hair (flag sign)
- **H** — Hepatomegaly (fatty liver)

### WHO 10 Steps — "Hypo-Hypo-Dehy, Electro-Infect-Micro, Feed-Grow-Stim-Home"
Steps 1-3: Treat hypoglycemia, hypothermia, dehydration
Steps 4-6: Electrolytes, infection, micronutrients
Steps 7-10: Feed (F-75), catch-up growth (F-100), stimulation, discharge
