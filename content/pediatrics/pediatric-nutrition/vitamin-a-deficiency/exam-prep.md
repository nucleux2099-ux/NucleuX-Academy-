# Vitamin A Deficiency — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
VAD is the leading cause of preventable childhood blindness. Earliest sign: night blindness (XN). Most common community finding: Bitot's spots (X1B). Keratomalacia (X3B) = irreversible blindness. Treatment: Vitamin A mega-dose (Day 1, Day 2, 2 weeks). Prophylaxis: 1 lakh IU at 9 months, then 2 lakh IU every 6 months till 5 years. Measles is the most important precipitating infection. Fat-soluble vitamin requiring dietary fat for absorption.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **WHO classification XN → X1A → X1B → X2 → X3A → X3B → XS** — know the sequence
- ★ **Bitot's spots** = foamy triangular patches on temporal conjunctiva (Corynebacterium xerosis)
- ★ **Night blindness** = earliest symptom; rhodopsin cycle impaired
- ★ **Keratomalacia (X3B)** = liquefaction of cornea — irreversible blindness
- ★ **Measles** is the most important infection precipitating xerophthalmia
- ★ **Treatment dose >12 months: 2 lakh IU** on Day 1, Day 2, and 2 weeks
- ★ **Prophylaxis starts at 9 months** with measles vaccine (1 lakh IU)
- ★ **Conjunctival impression cytology** — field test showing loss of goblet cells
- ★ **Phrynoderma (toad skin)** = follicular hyperkeratosis (extra-ocular sign)
- ★ **Hypervitaminosis A** → pseudotumor cerebri, hepatotoxicity, teratogenic
- ★ **Serum retinol <20 μg/dL** = deficient
- ★ **Never use topical steroids** in corneal ulceration from VAD

## Mnemonics

### **NIGHT** — Causes of Night Blindness
- **N** — Nutritional (Vitamin A deficiency — most common)
- **I** — Inherited (retinitis pigmentosa)
- **G** — Glaucoma (advanced)
- **H** — Hepatic disease (malabsorption)
- **T** — Toxic (chloroquine, quinine)

### WHO Classification Sequence: "Never X-ray A Cornea, Kids Scar Funny"
- **N**ight blindness (XN)
- Conjunctival **X**erosis (X1A)
- **B**itot's spots (X1B)
- **C**orneal xerosis (X2)
- **U**lceration (X3A, X3B/**K**eratomalacia)
- **S**car (XS)
- **F**undus (XF)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Earliest symptom** — Night blindness (XN)
2. **Most common community finding** — Bitot's spots (X1B)
3. **Pathognomonic sign** — Bitot's spots (foamy patches, temporal conjunctiva)
4. **Stage causing irreversible blindness** — X3B (keratomalacia)
5. **Precipitating infection** — Measles (most important)
6. **Treatment dose** — 2 lakh IU for >12 months (Day 1, Day 2, 2 weeks)
7. **Prophylaxis schedule** — 9 months (1 lakh), then 2 lakh every 6 months
8. **Richest dietary source** — Liver / cod liver oil
9. **Cheapest source in India** — Dark green leafy vegetables
10. **Hypervitaminosis A** — pseudotumor cerebri, teratogenicity
11. **Phrynoderma** — follicular hyperkeratosis (toad skin)
12. **Public health significance** — Bitot's spots >0.5% prevalence
