# RDS / Hyaline Membrane Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common respiratory cause in preterm. Surfactant deficiency → alveolar collapse. CXR: ground-glass + air bronchograms. Prevention: antenatal steroids. Treatment: CPAP + surfactant. Worsens over 48-72h then improves. BPD = chronic complication.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Surfactant produced by Type II pneumocytes from 24 weeks, mature by 35 weeks
- ★ DPPC (dipalmitoyl phosphatidylcholine/lecithin) = major surfactant component
- ★ L/S ratio ≥2 = lung maturity; PG presence = most reliable marker
- ★ CXR: reticulogranular (ground-glass) + air bronchograms + low volume
- ★ Grunting = infant's attempt to maintain PEEP
- ★ Antenatal betamethasone: 12 mg × 2 doses, 24h apart (24-34 weeks)
- ★ INSURE: Intubate-Surfactant-Extubate to CPAP
- ★ Risk increased: prematurity, male, C-section, maternal diabetes
- ★ Risk decreased: IUGR, PROM, maternal hypertension (chronic stress)
- ★ BPD: O₂ dependence at 36 weeks corrected age
- ★ Silverman-Anderson score: 0-10 (higher = worse distress)
- ★ Hyaline membranes = eosinophilic fibrin deposits (pathognomonic histology)

## Mnemonics
**RDS risk factors — "PM CD" (increased):**
- **P**rematurity
- **M**ale sex
- **C**esarean section (no labor)
- **D**iabetes (maternal)

**CXR grading — "1-2-3-4":**
- 1: Fine granularity
- 2: Air bronchograms
- 3: Cardiac border lost
- 4: Complete white-out

**Surfactant = "DPPC by Type II at 35"**
