# Neonatal Sepsis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Systemic infection in first 28 days. EOS (<72h): vertical, GBS/E. coli. LOS (>72h): nosocomial, Klebsiella/Staph. In India: Klebsiella most common. Sepsis screen: TLC, I:T ratio, CRP, micro-ESR. Blood culture = gold standard. Treat: Ampicillin + Gentamicin (EOS).

## High Yield Points
- ★ Blood culture = gold standard (minimum 1 mL blood)
- ★ In India: Klebsiella > E. coli > Staph aureus (NOT GBS like West)
- ★ Sepsis screen positive if ≥2 of 5 parameters abnormal
- ★ I:T ratio >0.2 = most sensitive single parameter
- ★ Sclerema neonatorum = board-like skin hardening → very poor prognosis
- ★ Hypothermia MORE common than fever in neonatal sepsis
- ★ EOS treatment: Ampicillin + Gentamicin
- ★ LOS treatment: Piperacillin-tazobactam + Amikacin (or add Vancomycin for MRSA)
- ★ CRP rises 6-8 hours after infection; PCT rises earlier (2-4 hours)
- ★ IVIG NOT recommended (INIS trial negative)
- ★ PROM >18 hours = major risk factor

## Mnemonics
**Sepsis Screen — "TIC-MP":**
- **T**LC (<5000 or >20000)
- **I**:T ratio (>0.2)
- **C**RP (>1 mg/dL)
- **M**icro-ESR (>15 mm/1st hour)
- **P**latelet (<100,000)

**EOS organisms — "GEL":**
- **G**BS
- **E**. coli
- **L**isteria

**"Not doing well" = Think sepsis in a neonate**
