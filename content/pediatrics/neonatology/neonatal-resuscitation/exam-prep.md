# Neonatal Resuscitation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
10% need help, 1% need extensive resuscitation. Golden minute = initial steps. PPV is the MOST important step. 3:1 compression:ventilation. Room air (21%) for term. Epinephrine 0.01-0.03 mg/kg IV if HR <60 despite PPV + compressions.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Effective ventilation = single most important step in neonatal resuscitation
- ★ Start with 21% O₂ (room air) for term; 21-30% for preterm <35 weeks
- ★ Compression:Ventilation = 3:1 (120 events/min = 90 compressions + 30 breaths)
- ★ Two-thumb encircling technique preferred for compressions
- ★ Epinephrine IV: 0.01-0.03 mg/kg (1:10,000); ET: 0.05-0.1 mg/kg
- ★ MR SOPA: corrective steps for ineffective PPV
- ★ Golden minute: initial steps completed in 60 seconds
- ★ FiO₂ increased to 100% ONLY during chest compressions
- ★ 3 assessment questions: Term? Tone? Crying?
- ★ PPV rate: 40-60 breaths/min
- ★ Congenital diaphragmatic hernia: intubate immediately, NO bag-mask
- ★ Preterm <32 weeks: plastic wrap to prevent heat loss

## Mnemonics
**MR SOPA — Corrective Ventilation Steps:**
- **M**ask adjustment
- **R**eposition airway
- **S**uction
- **O**pen mouth
- **P**ressure increase
- **A**lternative airway

**APGAR:**
- **A**ppearance, **P**ulse, **G**rimace, **A**ctivity, **R**espiration

**NRP Steps — "ABC + D":**
- **A**irway (initial steps)
- **B**reathing (PPV)
- **C**irculation (compressions)
- **D**rugs (epinephrine)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. Compression:ventilation ratio (3:1 in neonates vs 15:2 in children)
2. Initial FiO₂ for term vs preterm
3. Epinephrine dose and route
4. When to start compressions (HR <60 after 30s effective PPV)
5. MR SOPA corrective steps sequence
6. Two-thumb vs two-finger technique
7. Target SpO₂ at various time points post-birth
8. Golden minute concept
9. Volume expansion in NRP (10 mL/kg NS or O-neg blood)
10. Meconium management update (no routine suctioning)
