# Neonatal Resuscitation

## Overview
Approximately 10% of newborns require some assistance to begin breathing at birth, and about 1% need extensive resuscitation. The Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP) provides a systematic, evidence-based approach. Effective ventilation is the single most important step. The golden minute (first 60 seconds) focuses on initial steps and assessment.

## Key Points
- ~10% of newborns need help breathing; ~1% need extensive resuscitation
- Effective ventilation is the MOST important and effective step
- Golden minute: initial steps completed within 60 seconds
- Start with room air (21% O₂) for term; 21-30% for preterm
- Compression:ventilation ratio = 3:1
- Epinephrine: only drug routinely used in NRP

## Definition & Classification

### When to Resuscitate
Three assessment questions at birth:
1. Term gestation?
2. Good muscle tone?
3. Crying or breathing?

If YES to all → routine care (warmth, dry, skin-to-skin)
If NO to any → proceed to initial steps

### NRP Algorithm Steps
1. Initial steps (warmth, dry, stimulate, position, suction if needed)
2. PPV (positive pressure ventilation) — if apnea or HR <100
3. Chest compressions — if HR <60 despite 30 seconds effective PPV
4. Epinephrine/volume expansion — if HR <60 despite PPV + compressions

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Why Neonates Need Resuscitation
- Failure of transition from fetal to neonatal circulation
- Fetal lung fluid not cleared
- Inadequate respiratory drive (CNS depression, prematurity)
- Airway obstruction (meconium, secretions)
- Maternal drug effects (opioids, magnesium sulfate)

### Fetal to Neonatal Transition
- First breath → alveolar expansion → decrease in pulmonary vascular resistance → increased pulmonary blood flow → functional closure of foramen ovale and ductus arteriosus → transition from parallel to series circulation

## Clinical Features — Assessment Parameters

### Primary Apnea vs Secondary Apnea
| Feature | Primary | Secondary |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Response to stimulation | Resumes breathing | No response |
| Heart rate | Usually >60 | Often <60 |
| Treatment | Stimulation + PPV | Full resuscitation |

### Apgar Score (assessed at 1 and 5 minutes)
| Sign | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|------|---|---|---|
| **A**ppearance (color) | Blue/pale | Acrocyanosis | Pink |
| **P**ulse | Absent | <100 | ≥100 |
| **G**rimace (reflex) | No response | Grimace | Cry/cough |
| **A**ctivity (tone) | Limp | Some flexion | Active |
| **R**espiration | Absent | Weak/irregular | Strong cry |

**Note**: Apgar score should NOT be used to determine need for resuscitation (assessment should be ongoing)

## Diagnosis
- Clinical assessment at birth (tone, color, breathing, HR)
- Heart rate: best assessed by auscultation or pulse oximetry (right hand = pre-ductal)
- SpO₂ monitoring: pre-ductal (right hand)

### Target SpO₂ after Birth
| Time | Target SpO₂ |
|------|------------|
| 1 min | 60-65% |
| 2 min | 65-70% |
| 3 min | 70-75% |
| 4 min | 75-80% |
| 5 min | 80-85% |
| 10 min | 85-95% |

## Management — NRP Algorithm

### Step 1: Initial Steps (Golden Minute)
- **Warmth**: Radiant warmer, dry thoroughly, remove wet linen
- **Position**: Sniffing position (slight neck extension)
- **Clear airway**: Suction mouth then nose (only if obstructed)
- **Stimulate**: Flick soles, rub back
- **Preterm**: Plastic wrap (up to neck) if <32 weeks to prevent heat loss

### Step 2: Positive Pressure Ventilation (PPV)
- **Indication**: Apnea, gasping, or HR <100 after initial steps
- **Device**: T-piece resuscitator (preferred) or self-inflating bag with mask
- **Rate**: 40-60 breaths/minute
- **Pressure**: PIP 20-25 cmH₂O (term), 20 cmH₂O (preterm); PEEP 5 cmH₂O
- **FiO₂**: Start 21% for term, 21-30% for preterm (<35 weeks)
- **Assess**: Chest rise, HR response within 15 seconds
- **MR SOPA** corrective steps if not effective:
  - **M**ask adjustment
  - **R**eposition airway
  - **S**uction mouth/nose
  - **O**pen mouth
  - **P**ressure increase
  - **A**lternative airway (LMA/intubation)

### Step 3: Chest Compressions
- **Indication**: HR <60 despite 30 seconds of effective PPV
- **Technique**: Two-thumb encircling method (preferred) at lower third of sternum
- **Depth**: One-third AP diameter of chest
- **Ratio**: 3 compressions : 1 ventilation (90 compressions + 30 breaths = 120 events/min)
- **FiO₂**: Increase to 100% during compressions
- **Reassess**: Every 60 seconds

### Step 4: Medications
**Epinephrine:**
- Indication: HR <60 despite effective PPV + chest compressions
- IV dose: 0.01-0.03 mg/kg (1:10,000) via UVC
- ET dose: 0.05-0.1 mg/kg (if IV not available)
- May repeat every 3-5 minutes

**Volume Expansion:**
- Indication: Suspected blood loss (pale, weak pulses, poor response)
- NS or O Rh-negative blood: 10 mL/kg over 5-10 minutes

### Special Situations
- **Meconium**: No routine tracheal suctioning; intubate only if obstruction
- **Preterm <32 weeks**: Plastic wrap, lower O₂, gentle ventilation, consider CPAP
- **Congenital diaphragmatic hernia**: Immediate intubation (NO bag-mask ventilation)

## Complications
- Pneumothorax (from aggressive ventilation)
- Rib fractures (from compressions)
- Hepatic/splenic injury
- Hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (if delayed/ineffective resuscitation)
- Retinopathy of prematurity (excessive O₂ in preterm)
