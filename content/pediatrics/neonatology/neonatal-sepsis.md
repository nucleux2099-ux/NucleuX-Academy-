# Neonatal Sepsis

> 📚 **Sources:** Nelson's Pediatrics 21st Ed, OP Ghai 9th Ed, NNF Guidelines, WHO IMCI
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Neonatal sepsis is a **systemic bacterial infection** occurring in the first 28 days of life. It's a leading cause of neonatal mortality in developing countries.

**Why neonates are susceptible:**
1. Immature innate immunity (↓ complement, ↓ neutrophil function)
2. Deficient adaptive immunity (maternal IgG only; no IgM, IgA)
3. Thin skin barrier
4. Exposed umbilical stump (portal of entry)

**Classification by timing:**
- **Early-Onset Sepsis (EOS):** ≤72 hours (some say ≤7 days)
- **Late-Onset Sepsis (LOS):** >72 hours to 28 days

## Key Points

- **Leading cause of neonatal death** in India
- Blood culture = Gold standard (but sensitivity ~50-80%)
- "Sepsis screen" helps when culture unavailable/delayed
- **GBS** = #1 cause of EOS in developed countries
- **Klebsiella, E. coli, Staph** = Common in developing countries
- Clinical presentation is **non-specific** - "not doing well"

## Etiology

### Early-Onset Sepsis (Vertical transmission)
| Developed Countries | Developing Countries |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Group B Streptococcus (GBS) | Klebsiella pneumoniae |
| E. coli | E. coli |
| Listeria monocytogenes | Staphylococcus aureus |
| H. influenzae | GBS (less common) |

### Late-Onset Sepsis (Horizontal transmission)
- **Coagulase-negative Staphylococci** (CONS) - line-related
- Staphylococcus aureus
- Klebsiella, Pseudomonas, Acinetobacter
- Candida (prolonged antibiotics, TPN)

### Risk Factors
**Maternal:**
- Prolonged rupture of membranes (>18 hours)
- Maternal fever/chorioamnionitis
- Foul-smelling liquor
- GBS colonization
- Multiple per vaginum examinations
- Urinary tract infection

**Neonatal:**
- Prematurity, low birth weight
- Birth asphyxia
- Invasive procedures (IV lines, ETT)
- Prolonged NICU stay
- Formula feeding

## Clinical Features

**Cardinal Rule: "Neonate not doing well" = Think sepsis**

**Temperature Instability:**
- Fever (>37.5°C) or Hypothermia (<36°C)
- Hypothermia more common in preterm/LBW

**Respiratory:**
- Tachypnea, grunting, retractions
- Apnea (especially preterm)
- Cyanosis

**Cardiovascular:**
- Tachycardia or bradycardia
- Poor perfusion, prolonged CRT (>3 sec)
- Hypotension (late sign)

**Neurological:**
- Lethargy, irritability
- Poor feeding, weak cry
- Seizures, bulging fontanelle (meningitis)
- Hypotonia

**Gastrointestinal:**
- Feed intolerance, vomiting
- Abdominal distension
- Diarrhea

**Skin:**
- Jaundice (especially early)
- Petechiae, purpura (DIC)
- Sclerema (hardening - grave sign)
- Pustules, umbilical sepsis

**Metabolic:**
- Hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia
- Metabolic acidosis

## Diagnosis

### Sepsis Screen (≥2 positive = Probable sepsis)
| Test | Positive Value |
|------|----------------|
| TLC | <5000/mm³ or >20,000/mm³ |
| ANC | <1800/mm³ |
| I:T ratio | >0.2 (Immature:Total neutrophils) |
| Micro-ESR | >15 mm in 1st hour |
| CRP | >1 mg/dL (>10 mg/L) |

**Interpretation:**
- 2+ positive → Probable sepsis → Start antibiotics
- All negative → Sepsis unlikely (NPV >95%)

### Blood Culture (Gold Standard)
- **Volume:** Minimum 1 mL (ideally 2 mL)
- **Timing:** Before antibiotics if possible
- **Sites:** Peripheral vein (not from lines if possible)
- Positive in 50-80% of true sepsis

### Other Investigations
**Lumbar Puncture (for meningitis):**
- Indicated in: All LOS, EOS with positive blood culture, neurological signs
- CSF: ↑ cells, ↑ protein, ↓ glucose, Gram stain, culture

**Urine Culture:**
- LOS (especially >72 hours)
- Suprapubic aspiration or catheter (not bag specimen)

**Additional:**
- Chest X-ray (pneumonia)
- Procalcitonin (rising value, expensive)
- Acute phase reactants (serial CRP)

### Newer Markers
- **Procalcitonin:** Rises earlier than CRP
- **IL-6, IL-8:** Very early markers
- **Presepsin:** Promising
- **PCR-based detection:** Rapid, specific

## Management

### Empirical Antibiotics

**Early-Onset Sepsis:**
```
First-line: Ampicillin + Gentamicin
- Ampicillin: 50 mg/kg/dose IV q12h (meningitis: 100 mg/kg/dose)
- Gentamicin: 5 mg/kg/dose IV q24-36h (based on GA)
```

**Late-Onset Sepsis (Community):**
```
Same as EOS initially
OR Cefotaxime + Amikacin if Gram-negative predominant
```

**Late-Onset Sepsis (Nosocomial/NICU):**
```
Piperacillin-Tazobactam + Amikacin
OR Cefoperazone-Sulbactam + Amikacin
Add Vancomycin if MRSA/CONS suspected
Add Fluconazole if Candida risk
```

**Meningitis Doses:**
- Ampicillin: 100 mg/kg/dose q8h
- Cefotaxime: 50 mg/kg/dose q6h
- Gentamicin: Standard dose (poor CNS penetration)

### Duration of Therapy
| Condition | Duration |
|-----------|----------|
| Sepsis (blood culture negative) | 5-7 days |
| Sepsis (blood culture positive) | 10-14 days |
| Meningitis | 14-21 days (GBS: 14, Gram-neg: 21) |
| Osteomyelitis/Septic arthritis | 4-6 weeks |

### Supportive Care
1. **Respiratory:** O2, CPAP, ventilation as needed
2. **Cardiovascular:** Fluid bolus 10-20 mL/kg, inotropes (dopamine, dobutamine)
3. **Metabolic:** Correct hypoglycemia, acidosis
4. **Nutrition:** NPO initially → gradual feeds
5. **Temperature:** Neutral thermal environment

### Adjunctive Therapies
- **IVIg:** Not routinely recommended (Cochrane: no benefit)
- **G-CSF/GM-CSF:** Not routinely recommended
- **Exchange transfusion:** For sclerema/severe sepsis (anecdotal)

## Prevention

**Intrapartum Antibiotic Prophylaxis (IAP):**
- For GBS colonization: Penicillin G or Ampicillin IV during labor
- Reduces EOS by ~80%

**Infection Control:**
- Hand hygiene (most important!)
- Aseptic technique for procedures
- Limit invasive devices
- Early enteral feeding (breast milk)
- Kangaroo mother care

## Prognosis

**Mortality:**
- Developed countries: 5-10%
- Developing countries: 20-50%

**Morbidity:**
- Neurodevelopmental impairment (especially with meningitis)
- Hearing loss
- Cerebral palsy

## High-Yield for Exams

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| EOS timing | ≤72 hours (or ≤7 days) |
| LOS timing | >72 hours to 28 days |
| #1 EOS organism (West) | Group B Streptococcus |
| #1 EOS organism (India) | Klebsiella pneumoniae |
| Gold standard | Blood culture |
| Sepsis screen: I:T ratio | >0.2 = positive |
| CRP positive | >1 mg/dL |
| First-line antibiotics | Ampicillin + Gentamicin |
| LP indication | All LOS, positive BC, neuro signs |
| Sclerema | Hardening of skin, grave sign |

**MCQ Pearls:**
- ❗ Hypothermia is MORE common than fever in septic neonates
- ❗ Apnea may be the ONLY sign of sepsis in preterm
- ❗ Negative sepsis screen = High NPV (rules out sepsis)
- ❗ I:T ratio = Most sensitive screening test
- ❗ Band cells + toxic granules on smear = Suggestive of sepsis
- ❗ GBS prophylaxis = Intrapartum Penicillin/Ampicillin
- ❗ CONS = #1 cause of nosocomial sepsis in NICU
