# Low Birth Weight — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
LBW <2500g. Due to prematurity or IUGR. Asymmetric IUGR (3rd trimester, head sparing) vs Symmetric (1st trimester, proportionate). KMC reduces mortality by 40%. Key risks: hypothermia, hypoglycemia, sepsis.

## High Yield Points
- ★ LBW <2500g, VLBW <1500g, ELBW <1000g
- ★ India: ~30% of live births are LBW (highest globally)
- ★ Asymmetric IUGR: head sparing, low ponderal index, 3rd trimester insult (placental insufficiency)
- ★ Symmetric IUGR: proportionate, 1st trimester insult (TORCH, chromosomal), poor prognosis
- ★ KMC: skin-to-skin contact, reduces mortality by 40%, promotes breastfeeding
- ★ Feeding progression: gavage → paladai/cup → direct breastfeed
- ★ Polycythemia (PCV >65%) common in SGA babies
- ★ Hypoglycemia: monitor blood glucose within 1 hour of birth
- ★ Barker hypothesis: LBW → adult hypertension, diabetes, CVD
- ★ Iron supplementation: 2 mg/kg/day from 2 weeks of age for LBW

## Mnemonics
**LBW complications — "HANG RISK":**
- **H**ypothermia
- **A**pnea
- **N**EC
- **G**lucose low (hypoglycemia)
- **R**DS
- **I**nfection/sepsis
- **S**low feeding
- **K**ernicterus (jaundice)

**Asymmetric vs Symmetric — "A = Abdomen thin, S = Skull small"**
- Asymmetric: body thin, head spared
- Symmetric: skull and everything small
