# Low Birth Weight (LBW)

## Overview
Low birth weight (LBW) is defined as birth weight <2500 g regardless of gestational age. It is a major determinant of neonatal morbidity and mortality, particularly in developing countries. India contributes the largest number of LBW babies globally (~30% of all live births). LBW can result from prematurity, intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR), or both.

## Key Points
- LBW <2500g, VLBW <1500g, ELBW <1000g
- Two causes: preterm birth OR intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR)
- IUGR can be symmetric (type I) or asymmetric (type II)
- Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) is the cornerstone of LBW management
- Major risks: hypothermia, hypoglycemia, infections, feeding difficulties

## Definition & Classification

### By Birth Weight
| Category | Weight | Survival (India) |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| LBW | <2500 g | ~95% |
| VLBW | <1500 g | ~70-85% |
| ELBW | <1000 g | ~40-60% |

### IUGR Classification
| Feature | Symmetric (Type I) | Asymmetric (Type II) |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Timing of insult | Early (1st trimester) | Late (3rd trimester) |
| Head circumference | Small | Normal (head sparing) |
| Weight:Length ratio | Proportionate | Disproportionate |
| Ponderal index | Normal | Low |
| Causes | Chromosomal, TORCH, drugs | Placental insufficiency, PIH |
| Cell type | Hypoplasia (fewer cells) | Hypotrophy (smaller cells) |
| Prognosis | Poor catch-up | Better catch-up growth |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Causes of LBW
**Preterm Birth:**
- Preterm labor, PPROM, cervical incompetence
- Multiple pregnancy, polyhydramnios
- Maternal infections (UTI, chorioamnionitis)
- Placental abruption

**IUGR:**
- Maternal: malnutrition, anemia, PIH, chronic diseases, smoking, alcohol, drugs
- Fetal: chromosomal abnormalities, TORCH infections, multiple pregnancy
- Placental: insufficiency, infarction, abnormal implantation

### Indian Scenario
- ~30% of births are LBW (highest globally)
- Maternal malnutrition is the leading cause
- Short maternal stature, teenage pregnancy, close spacing of pregnancies
- Anemia (Hb <11 g/dL) in >50% of pregnant women

## Clinical Features

### LBW/Preterm Neonate
- Small size, thin skin, visible veins
- Poor subcutaneous fat
- Weak cry, poor suck and swallow
- Hypothermia prone (high surface area:volume ratio)
- Respiratory distress (surfactant deficiency in preterm)
- Abdominal distension, feeding intolerance

### SGA/IUGR Features
- **Asymmetric**: Large head relative to body, wasted appearance, loose skin folds, thin limbs
- **Symmetric**: Proportionately small in all parameters
- Alert, hungry appearance
- Dry, peeling skin (especially post-term SGA)
- Polycythemia (plethoric appearance)

## Diagnosis
1. **Accurate birth weight** on calibrated scale
2. **Gestational age assessment**: New Ballard Score
3. **Plot on growth charts**: Fenton (preterm) or WHO/IAP charts
4. **Ponderal index**: Weight (g) × 100 / Length³ (cm) — low in asymmetric IUGR
5. **Blood glucose**: Within 1 hour of birth (hypoglycemia screening)
6. **PCV/Hematocrit**: Polycythemia screening (PCV >65%)
7. **Prenatal USG**: Serial growth scans, Doppler (umbilical artery)

## Management

### Immediate Care — "WARM Chain"
1. **W**armth: Warm delivery room, immediate drying, skin-to-skin contact
2. **A**irway: Clear, position, suction if needed
3. **R**espiration: Stimulate, PPV if apneic
4. **M**onitoring: Temperature, glucose, feeding

### Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)
- **Position**: Baby placed prone, skin-to-skin on mother's chest, between breasts
- **Duration**: Continuous (24 hours) or intermittent (minimum 1 hour sessions)
- **Benefits**: Thermoregulation, breastfeeding promotion, bonding, reduced infection, reduced mortality
- **Criteria**: Stable baby, weight <2500g (ideally <2000g), mother willing
- **Evidence**: Reduces neonatal mortality by 40% in LBW babies

### Feeding
- **Breast milk** is ideal (expressed breast milk if unable to suck)
- **Feeding method progression**: Gavage (OG/NG tube) → Paladai/cup → Direct breastfeeding
- **Volume**: Start 60-80 mL/kg/day, increase by 15-20 mL/kg/day to 150-200 mL/kg/day
- **Fortification**: Human milk fortifier for VLBW babies
- **Supplements**: Iron (2 mg/kg/day from 2 weeks), Vitamin D (400 IU/day), Calcium, Phosphorus

### Thermoregulation
- Target axillary temperature: 36.5-37.5°C
- KMC, incubator, radiant warmer
- Warm IV fluids, warm transport

### Monitoring
- Daily weight, weekly length and head circumference
- Blood glucose monitoring (maintain >45 mg/dL)
- Infection surveillance
- Apnea monitoring (especially <34 weeks)

## Complications
- **Immediate**: Hypothermia, hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia, polycythemia, feeding difficulty, apnea, RDS, sepsis, NEC, IVH
- **Long-term**: Growth failure, neurodevelopmental delay, retinopathy of prematurity, chronic lung disease
- **Barker hypothesis**: LBW → increased risk of hypertension, diabetes, CVD in adult life
