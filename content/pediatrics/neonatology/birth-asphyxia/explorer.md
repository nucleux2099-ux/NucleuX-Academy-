# Birth Asphyxia

## Overview
Birth asphyxia is the failure to initiate and sustain breathing at birth, leading to hypoxia, hypercarbia, and acidosis. It is a major cause of neonatal mortality and long-term neurological morbidity worldwide. In India, it accounts for ~20% of neonatal deaths. Timely recognition and resuscitation are critical to prevent hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (HIE).

## Key Points
- Defined as failure to establish spontaneous respiration at birth
- Apgar score ≤3 at 5 minutes suggests severe asphyxia
- Cord blood pH <7.0 and base deficit >12 mmol/L are biochemical markers
- HIE is the major neurological consequence
- NNF/NRP resuscitation algorithm is the immediate management
- Therapeutic hypothermia (cooling) is the gold standard for moderate-severe HIE

## Definition & Classification

### WHO Definition
Failure to initiate and sustain breathing at birth.

### Clinical Criteria (all must be present for "significant" asphyxia)
1. Profound metabolic or mixed acidemia (pH <7.0) on umbilical cord blood
2. Apgar score 0-3 beyond 5 minutes
3. Neonatal neurological sequelae (seizures, coma, hypotonia)
4. Multiorgan dysfunction (cardiovascular, GI, hematologic, pulmonary, renal)

### Sarnat & Sarnat Staging of HIE
| Feature | Stage I (Mild) | Stage II (Moderate) | Stage III (Severe) |
|---------|---------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Consciousness | Hyperalert | Lethargic | Comatose |
| Muscle tone | Normal | Hypotonia | Flaccid |
| Reflexes | Exaggerated | Decreased | Absent |
| Seizures | None | Common | Decerebrate |
| Pupils | Dilated | Constricted | Fixed, dilated |
| EEG | Normal | Low voltage | Burst suppression |
| Duration | <24 hours | 2-14 days | Days to weeks |
| Prognosis | Good | Variable (20-30% sequelae) | Poor (50% mortality) |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Causes
**Antepartum (20%)**
- Maternal hypertension, diabetes, anemia
- Placental insufficiency, abruption
- IUGR, post-term pregnancy

**Intrapartum (35%)**
- Cord prolapse/compression
- Prolonged/obstructed labor
- Malpresentation
- Uterine rupture

**Neonatal (10%)**
- Prematurity, congenital anomalies
- Airway obstruction (meconium)
- Respiratory depression (maternal drugs)

### Pathophysiology
Hypoxia → anaerobic glycolysis → lactic acidosis → energy failure → cellular edema → excitotoxicity (glutamate release) → calcium influx → cell death (necrosis + apoptosis)

**Diving reflex**: Blood redistributed to brain, heart, adrenals at expense of kidneys, gut, skin, lungs.

## Clinical Features
- **Immediate**: Poor/absent cry, cyanosis, bradycardia, hypotonia, absent reflexes
- **CNS**: Seizures (within 6-12 hours), altered consciousness, bulging fontanelle
- **CVS**: Cardiogenic shock, tricuspid regurgitation, myocardial ischemia
- **Renal**: Oliguria/anuria, acute tubular necrosis
- **GI**: Necrotizing enterocolitis (NEC), feeding intolerance
- **Hematologic**: DIC, thrombocytopenia
- **Pulmonary**: Meconium aspiration, PPHN, pulmonary hemorrhage
- **Metabolic**: Hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia, SIADH/hyponatremia

## Diagnosis
1. **Apgar Score**: 1, 5, 10 minutes (≤3 at 5 min = severe)
2. **Cord blood gas**: pH <7.0, base deficit >12 mmol/L
3. **Blood investigations**: CBC, blood glucose, calcium, electrolytes, LFT, RFT, coagulation profile
4. **Neuroimaging**: Cranial ultrasound (early), MRI brain (day 5-7 — gold standard for HIE extent)
5. **EEG/aEEG**: Seizure detection, background activity, prognostication
6. **Echocardiography**: Myocardial dysfunction, PPHN assessment
7. **Biomarkers**: LDH, CK-MB, liver enzymes (AST/ALT)

## Management

### Immediate — Neonatal Resuscitation (NRP Algorithm)
1. **Warmth**: Radiant warmer, dry, stimulate
2. **Airway**: Position, suction if needed (MAS: intubate and suction if non-vigorous — *updated: routine tracheal suctioning no longer recommended*)
3. **Breathing**: PPV with room air (21% O₂ for term; 21-30% for preterm) if HR <100 or apnea
4. **Circulation**: Chest compressions if HR <60 despite 30 sec of effective PPV (3:1 ratio)
5. **Drugs**: Epinephrine 0.01-0.03 mg/kg IV if HR <60 despite compressions

### Supportive Care
- **Fluid restriction**: 60 mL/kg/day (cerebral edema risk)
- **Glucose monitoring**: Maintain 50-110 mg/dL
- **Seizure control**: Phenobarbitone (20 mg/kg loading, max 40 mg/kg)
- **Ventilation**: As needed for respiratory failure
- **Inotropes**: Dopamine/dobutamine for cardiogenic shock
- **Avoid hyperthermia**: Worsens brain injury

### Therapeutic Hypothermia (Cooling)
- **Indication**: Moderate to severe HIE (Sarnat Stage II/III)
- **Criteria**: ≥36 weeks gestation, within 6 hours of birth
- **Target**: Core temperature 33.5°C ± 0.5°C for 72 hours
- **Method**: Whole body cooling or selective head cooling
- **Rewarming**: Gradual, 0.5°C per hour over 6-12 hours
- **Evidence**: Reduces death/disability by ~25% (NNT = 7)

## Complications
- **Short-term**: Seizures, multi-organ failure, death
- **Long-term**: Cerebral palsy (spastic quadriplegia), intellectual disability, epilepsy, cortical visual impairment, hearing loss, learning disabilities

## Prognosis
- Stage I HIE: 100% normal outcome
- Stage II HIE: 70-80% normal, 20-30% neurological sequelae
- Stage III HIE: ~50% mortality, survivors often have severe disability
