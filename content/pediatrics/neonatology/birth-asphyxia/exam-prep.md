# Birth Asphyxia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Birth asphyxia = failure to breathe at birth → hypoxia → HIE. Sarnat staging guides severity. Immediate NRP resuscitation + therapeutic hypothermia (33.5°C × 72h) for moderate-severe HIE. MCD accounts for major neonatal mortality in India.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Apgar ≤3 at 5 minutes = severe asphyxia
- ★ Cord pH <7.0 + base deficit >12 = biochemical definition
- ★ Sarnat Stage II (moderate): lethargic, hypotonia, seizures common, constricted pupils
- ★ Sarnat Stage III (severe): comatose, flaccid, absent reflexes, fixed dilated pupils
- ★ Therapeutic hypothermia: 33.5°C for 72 hours, within 6 hours of birth, ≥36 weeks
- ★ First drug in NRP: Epinephrine 0.01-0.03 mg/kg IV (if HR <60 after PPV + compressions)
- ★ Compression:ventilation ratio = 3:1 in neonates
- ★ Resuscitation starts with room air (21% O₂) for term neonates
- ★ Seizures in HIE: Phenobarbitone is first-line (20 mg/kg loading)
- ★ MRI brain on day 5-7 = gold standard for extent of injury
- ★ Multi-organ injury: kidneys (ATN), heart (TR), liver, gut (NEC), blood (DIC)
- ★ Diving reflex: blood shunted to brain, heart, adrenals

## Mnemonics
**"MR SOAP" — Resuscitation Steps:**
- **M**econium (assess vigor)
- **R**adiant warmer (warmth)
- **S**uction, **S**timulate
- **O**xygen (PPV if needed)
- **A**uscultate heart rate
- **P**ressure (chest compressions if HR <60)

**Sarnat staging — "HAF" (progressively worse):**
- Stage I: **H**yperalert
- Stage II: **A**ltered (lethargic)
- Stage III: **F**laccid (comatose)
