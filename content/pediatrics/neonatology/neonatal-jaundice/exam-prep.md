# Neonatal Jaundice — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common neonatal condition. Physiological: >24h onset, peaks day 3-5, resolves <2 weeks. Pathological: <24h onset, rapid rise, prolonged. Unconjugated → kernicterus risk. Treatment: phototherapy (lumirubin formation) and exchange transfusion.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Jaundice within 24 hours = ALWAYS pathological (think hemolysis: Rh/ABO)
- ★ Physiological jaundice peaks day 3-5 in term (day 5-7 in preterm)
- ★ Kernicterus: deposits in globus pallidus, subthalamic nuclei → choreoathetoid CP + hearing loss
- ★ Phototherapy converts bilirubin to lumirubin (water-soluble, excreted without conjugation)
- ★ Exchange transfusion: double volume (160-180 mL/kg), removes 85% of bilirubin
- ★ Kramer's zone 1 (face) = ~5 mg/dL; zone 5 (palms/soles) = >12 mg/dL
- ★ Conjugated bilirubin >2 mg/dL = ALWAYS pathological (think biliary atresia)
- ★ Breast milk jaundice: prolonged but benign; continue breastfeeding
- ★ Crigler-Najjar Type I: absent UGT, severe, needs liver transplant
- ★ IVIG reduces exchange transfusion need in immune hemolytic jaundice
- ★ Bronze baby syndrome: phototherapy in conjugated hyperbilirubinemia

## Mnemonics
**Kernicterus tetrad — "CUDS":**
- **C**horeoathetoid cerebral palsy
- **U**pward gaze palsy
- **D**eafness (sensorineural)
- **S**tained teeth (enamel dysplasia)

**Pathological jaundice — "RAPID":**
- **R**ise >5 mg/dL/day
- **A**ppears within 24 hours
- **P**rolonged (>2 weeks term, >3 weeks preterm)
- **I**ncreased direct bilirubin
- **D**ark urine / pale stools (conjugated)
