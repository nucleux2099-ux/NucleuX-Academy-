# Prematurity

> 📚 **Sources:** Nelson's Pediatrics 21st Ed, OP Ghai 9th Ed, WHO Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Prematurity is birth before **37 completed weeks** of gestation. It is the leading cause of neonatal mortality and the second leading cause of death in children under 5 years globally.

**Survival improves with each additional week:**
- 24 weeks: 40-50% survival
- 28 weeks: 80-90% survival
- 32 weeks: 95% survival
- 34 weeks: >98% survival

## Key Points

- **15 million** preterm births annually worldwide
- Leading cause of neonatal mortality
- Limit of viability: **22-24 weeks** (with aggressive NICU care)
- Corrected/Adjusted age used until 2-3 years for developmental milestones
- Major complications: RDS, IVH, NEC, ROP, BPD

## Classification

### By Gestational Age
| Category | Gestational Age | % of Preterm |
|----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Extremely Preterm | <28 weeks | ~5% |
| Very Preterm | 28-<32 weeks | ~10% |
| Moderate Preterm | 32-<34 weeks | ~15% |
| Late Preterm | 34-<37 weeks | ~70% |

### By Birth Weight
| Category | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Low Birth Weight (LBW) | <2500g |
| Very Low Birth Weight (VLBW) | <1500g |
| Extremely Low Birth Weight (ELBW) | <1000g |

**Note:** LBW can be due to prematurity OR intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR)

## Etiology

### Maternal Factors
- Multiple pregnancy (30% of preterm births)
- Cervical incompetence
- Uterine anomalies
- Previous preterm birth
- Short interpregnancy interval (<6 months)
- Extremes of maternal age (<17, >40)
- Infections: UTI, bacterial vaginosis, chorioamnionitis
- Pre-eclampsia, chronic hypertension
- Diabetes mellitus
- Smoking, substance abuse

### Fetal Factors
- Multiple gestation
- Congenital anomalies
- Polyhydramnios
- PPROM (Preterm Premature Rupture of Membranes)

### Placental Factors
- Placenta previa
- Placental abruption

### Idiopathic
- 40-50% have no identifiable cause

## Clinical Features

### Physical Characteristics of Preterm Baby
**Skin:**
- Thin, gelatinous (especially <28 weeks)
- Visible veins
- Lanugo present
- Vernix caseosa abundant
- Edema common

**Head:**
- Relatively large (1/3 of body length)
- Soft skull bones, wide sutures
- Fontanelles may be large

**Ears:**
- Soft, flat pinna
- Slow recoil when folded

**Genitalia:**
- Male: Undescended testes, poorly rugated scrotum
- Female: Prominent labia minora, clitoris

**Posture:**
- Hypotonic, frog-leg posture
- Decreased flexion

**Neurological:**
- Weak/absent suck and swallow (develops ~34 weeks)
- Absent/weak primitive reflexes
- Periodic breathing and apnea

### Assessment of Gestational Age
**New Ballard Score:**
- 6 Physical + 6 Neuromuscular criteria
- Score -10 to +50 → corresponds to 20-44 weeks
- Accurate within ±2 weeks

## Complications

### Respiratory
| Condition | Timing | Features |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| RDS | Hours to days | Surfactant deficiency |
| Apnea of Prematurity | First week | Cessation >20 sec or with bradycardia |
| BPD | Weeks | O2 dependency at 36 weeks PMA |

### Cardiovascular
- **Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA):** Persistent connection, L→R shunt
  - Bounding pulses, wide pulse pressure, machinery murmur
  - Treatment: Fluid restriction, Indomethacin/Ibuprofen, surgical ligation

### Neurological
- **Intraventricular Hemorrhage (IVH):**
  - Bleeding from germinal matrix
  - Grading (Papile):
    - Grade I: Germinal matrix only
    - Grade II: IVH without ventricular dilatation
    - Grade III: IVH with ventricular dilatation
    - Grade IV: IVH with parenchymal involvement
  - Prevention: Antenatal steroids, gentle handling, avoid fluctuations

- **Periventricular Leukomalacia (PVL):**
  - White matter injury → spastic diplegia (legs > arms)

### Gastrointestinal
- **Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC):**
  - Ischemic-inflammatory bowel injury
  - Triad: Abdominal distension + bilious vomiting + bloody stools
  - X-ray: Pneumatosis intestinalis, portal venous gas, pneumoperitoneum

- **Feeding Intolerance:**
  - Immature GI motility
  - Requires slow advancement of feeds

### Ophthalmological
- **Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP):**
  - Abnormal retinal vascularization due to hyperoxia
  - Screening: All <32 weeks or <1500g at 4 weeks postnatal age
  - Treatment: Laser photocoagulation, anti-VEGF

### Hematological
- **Anemia of Prematurity:**
  - Low erythropoietin, frequent blood draws
  - May need transfusion

### Metabolic
- Hypoglycemia (low glycogen stores)
- Hypocalcemia
- Hypothermia (high surface area:volume, less brown fat)
- Hyperbilirubinemia

### Immunological
- Increased susceptibility to infections
- Low immunoglobulin levels

## Management

### Immediate Resuscitation
- Warm delivery room, pre-warmed radiant warmer
- Plastic wrap/bag for <32 weeks (prevents heat loss)
- Gentle handling to prevent IVH
- CPAP early if required

### Temperature Regulation
- **Neutral Thermal Environment:**
  - 34-35°C for ELBW
  - 32-34°C for VLBW
  - 30-32°C for larger preterm
- Incubator or radiant warmer
- Kangaroo Mother Care (skin-to-skin)

### Respiratory Support
- Oxygen: Target SpO2 91-95%
- CPAP: First-line for RDS
- Caffeine: For apnea of prematurity (loading 20 mg/kg, maintenance 5 mg/kg)
- Surfactant: For RDS
- Mechanical ventilation: If CPAP fails

### Nutrition
**Goals:** Match intrauterine growth (15-20 g/kg/day)

**Enteral:**
- Mother's own milk preferred (anti-infective, promotes gut maturation)
- Donor human milk if unavailable
- Fortification with HMF after 100 mL/kg/day
- Trophic feeds start Day 1-2 (10-20 mL/kg/day)
- Advance 10-20 mL/kg/day as tolerated

**Parenteral:**
- Start Day 1 with amino acids + glucose
- Add lipids by Day 2-3
- Transition to full enteral over 2-4 weeks

### Infection Prevention
- Strict hand hygiene
- Minimize invasive procedures
- Antibiotic stewardship
- Breast milk feeding

### Developmental Care
- NIDCAP (Newborn Individualized Developmental Care)
- Minimal handling
- Cycled lighting
- Noise reduction
- Family involvement

### Follow-up
- High-risk follow-up clinic
- Monitor growth with corrected age
- Developmental surveillance
- Vision and hearing screening
- Immunizations (chronological age, not corrected)

## Prevention

- Progesterone supplementation (history of preterm birth)
- Cervical cerclage (cervical incompetence)
- Treatment of infections
- Lifestyle modifications
- Adequate interpregnancy interval
- Antenatal steroids if preterm delivery anticipated

**MCQ Pearls:**
- ❗ Leading cause of neonatal mortality = Prematurity
- ❗ Late preterm = 70% of all preterm births
- ❗ PDA in preterm: Continuous/Machinery murmur + bounding pulses
- ❗ IVH timing: Usually first 72 hours (bleeding from germinal matrix)
- ❗ PVL → Spastic diplegia (affects lower limbs)
- ❗ Antenatal steroids reduce RDS, IVH, NEC, and mortality
- ❗ Kangaroo care reduces mortality, sepsis, and improves growth
