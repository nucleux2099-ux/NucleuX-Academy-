# Hirschsprung Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Congenital absence of ganglion cells in distal bowel → functional obstruction. Rectosigmoid (80%). Failure to pass meconium in 48h. Diagnosis: rectal suction biopsy (absent ganglion cells + increased AChE). Treatment: pull-through surgery.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Failure to pass meconium within 48 hours = hallmark presentation
- ★ Rectal suction biopsy = GOLD STANDARD (absent ganglion cells, hypertrophied nerves, ↑AChE)
- ★ Aganglionic segment is NARROW; proximal normal bowel is DILATED
- ★ Rectosigmoid = most common location (80%)
- ★ Barium enema: transition zone + delayed 24-hour evacuation (do UNPREPARED)
- ★ Absent RAIR (recto-anal inhibitory reflex) on anorectal manometry
- ★ RET proto-oncogene mutation is the most common genetic cause
- ★ Male:Female = 4:1; associated with Down syndrome
- ★ HAEC (enterocolitis) = most dangerous complication / cause of death
- ★ Empty rectum on DRE (vs functional constipation where rectum is loaded)
- ★ "Blast sign" = explosive gush of stool on DRE

## Mnemonics
**"HIRSCH" — Features:**
- **H**ypertrophied nerve trunks
- **I**ntestinal obstruction (functional)
- **R**ET gene mutation
- **S**uction biopsy = gold standard
- **C**raniocaudal migration failure (neural crest)
- **H**AEC = dreaded complication

**Narrow = No ganglion cells** (the narrow segment is the diseased one)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. Gold standard diagnosis (rectal suction biopsy)
2. Most common location (rectosigmoid)
3. Barium enema findings (transition zone)
4. Differentiation from functional constipation (empty vs loaded rectum)
