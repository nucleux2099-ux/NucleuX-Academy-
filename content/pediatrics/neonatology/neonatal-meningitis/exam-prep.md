# Neonatal Meningitis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Serious CNS infection in first 28 days. GBS + E. coli K1 most common. Nonspecific presentation (neck stiffness usually ABSENT). CSF: ↑WBC, ↑protein, ↓glucose. Treat: Ampicillin + Cefotaxime × 21 days. High mortality/morbidity. Hydrocephalus = most common chronic complication.

## High Yield Points
- ★ GBS = most common cause (developed countries); E. coli K1 = most common gram-negative
- ★ In India: Klebsiella and E. coli more common than GBS
- ★ Neck stiffness / Kernig / Brudzinski usually ABSENT in neonates
- ★ Seizures present in 40-50% of neonatal meningitis
- ★ CSF: WBC >30, protein >150 mg/dL, glucose <40 mg/dL
- ★ Ampicillin + Cefotaxime (NOT ceftriaxone — displaces bilirubin)
- ★ Duration: 21 days (GBS/Listeria), 21-28 days (gram-negative)
- ★ Dexamethasone NOT recommended in neonatal meningitis
- ★ Hydrocephalus = most common long-term complication
- ★ Repeat LP at 48-72 hours to confirm CSF sterilization
- ★ Bulging fontanelle is a LATE sign
