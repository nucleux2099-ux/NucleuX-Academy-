# Gestational Age Assessment — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
GA assessed prenatally (LMP, USG) and postnatally (New Ballard Score). NBS has 6 physical + 6 neuromuscular criteria. Classify by GA (preterm/term/post-term) and weight (SGA/AGA/LGA). First trimester CRL is most accurate prenatal method.

## High Yield Points
- ★ New Ballard Score: 6 neuromuscular + 6 physical maturity = total score → GA
- ★ Best done within 12-48 hours of birth
- ★ First trimester CRL = most accurate prenatal GA assessment (±5 days)
- ★ Preterm: <37 weeks; Term: 37-42 weeks; Post-term: >42 weeks
- ★ SGA: <10th percentile; LGA: >90th percentile
- ★ LBW <2500g, VLBW <1500g, ELBW <1000g
- ★ SGA risks: hypoglycemia, polycythemia, hypothermia
- ★ LGA risks: birth injuries, hypoglycemia (especially infant of diabetic mother)
- ★ Post-term: parchment skin, long nails, meconium staining, alert appearance
- ★ Preterm: thin skin, lanugo, poor ear recoil, extended posture

## Mnemonics
**NBS Physical maturity — "SLBEG" (6 criteria):**
- **S**kin
- **L**anugo
- **B**reast
- **E**ye/Ear
- **G**enitals
- Plantar surface

**NBS Neuromuscular — "PASS-SH":**
- **P**osture
- **A**rm recoil
- **S**quare window
- **S**carf sign
- Popliteal angle (**S**traight leg)
- **H**eel to ear

**Weight classification — "2500-1500-1000":**
- LBW < 2500, VLBW < 1500, ELBW < 1000
