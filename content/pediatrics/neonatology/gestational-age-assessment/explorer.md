# Gestational Age Assessment

## Overview
Gestational age (GA) assessment is fundamental in neonatology for classifying newborns, predicting complications, and guiding management. It can be determined prenatally (LMP, ultrasound) or postnatally (New Ballard Score). Classification by GA and birth weight determines the risk profile of each neonate.

## Key Points
- New Ballard Score (NBS) is the standard postnatal GA assessment tool
- NBS assesses 6 neuromuscular + 6 physical maturity criteria
- Accurate to ±2 weeks; best done within 48 hours of birth
- Classification: Preterm (<37 weeks), Term (37-42 weeks), Post-term (>42 weeks)
- Weight classification: SGA (<10th percentile), AGA (10th-90th), LGA (>90th)

## Definition & Classification

### By Gestational Age
| Category | Gestational Age | Key Risks |
|----------|----------------|-----------|
| Extremely preterm | <28 weeks | RDS, IVH, NEC, ROP, BPD |
| Very preterm | 28-32 weeks | RDS, sepsis, IVH |
| Moderate preterm | 32-34 weeks | Feeding issues, jaundice |
| Late preterm | 34-37 weeks | Hypothermia, hypoglycemia |
| Term | 37-42 weeks | Lowest risk |
| Post-term | >42 weeks | Meconium aspiration, macrosomia |

### By Birth Weight
| Category | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Normal birth weight | ≥2500 g |
| Low birth weight (LBW) | <2500 g |
| Very low birth weight (VLBW) | <1500 g |
| Extremely low birth weight (ELBW) | <1000 g |

### By Weight for GA
- **SGA** (Small for Gestational Age): <10th percentile — risk of hypoglycemia, polycythemia
- **AGA** (Appropriate for GA): 10th-90th percentile
- **LGA** (Large for GA): >90th percentile — risk of birth injuries, hypoglycemia (IDM)

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Determinants of Gestational Age
**Prenatal Assessment:**
- Last menstrual period (LMP): Naegele's rule
- First trimester USG: Crown-rump length (CRL) — most accurate (±5 days)
- Second trimester: Biparietal diameter (BPD), femur length
- Third trimester: Least accurate

**Postnatal Assessment:**
- **New Ballard Score (NBS)**: Modified from Dubowitz; valid from 20-44 weeks
- Best performed within 12-48 hours of birth
- Overestimates GA in extremely premature, underestimates in post-term

### New Ballard Score Components
**Physical Maturity (6 criteria):**
1. Skin (gelatinous → leathery/cracked)
2. Lanugo (none → sparse → bald areas)
3. Plantar surface (no creases → creases over entire sole)
4. Breast (imperceptible → full areola, 5-10 mm bud)
5. Eye/ear (fused lids → thick cartilage, instant recoil)
6. Genitals: Male (scrotum flat → testes pendulous, deep rugae) / Female (clitoris prominent → labia majora cover)

**Neuromuscular Maturity (6 criteria):**
1. Posture (extended → fully flexed)
2. Square window (wrist flexion angle: >90° → 0°)
3. Arm recoil (180° → <90°)
4. Popliteal angle (180° → <90°)
5. Scarf sign (elbow past opposite axillary line → doesn't reach midline)
6. Heel to ear

**Scoring**: Each item scored -1 to 5; total score mapped to GA (e.g., 35 = 38 weeks)

## Clinical Features

### Preterm Features
- Skin: Thin, translucent, visible veins, abundant lanugo
- Ears: Soft, flat, poor recoil
- Breast: No palpable tissue
- Genitals: Undescended testes (male), prominent clitoris (female)
- Neurological: Hypotonia, poor suck/swallow, absent/weak reflexes
- Posture: Extended

### Term Features
- Skin: Pale pink, few veins, minimal lanugo
- Ears: Well-curved, firm, instant recoil
- Breast: Raised areola, 3-4 mm bud
- Genitals: Testes in scrotum (male), labia majora cover minora (female)
- Neurological: Good tone, active reflexes, strong suck
- Posture: Flexed

### Post-term Features
- Skin: Parchment-like, peeling, cracked, meconium staining
- Decreased vernix caseosa and lanugo
- Long nails, alert appearance
- Plantar creases covering entire sole

## Diagnosis
1. **Prenatal**: LMP + early USG (CRL in first trimester is gold standard)
2. **Postnatal**: New Ballard Score within 48 hours
3. **Plotting**: Weight, length, head circumference on Fenton/Lubchenco charts
4. **Classification**: Determine preterm/term/post-term AND SGA/AGA/LGA

## Management

### Based on Classification
**Preterm + LBW/VLBW:**
- Thermoregulation (KMC, incubator)
- Feeding support (gavage → cup → direct breastfeeding)
- RDS prevention (antenatal steroids, surfactant)
- Apnea monitoring
- Infection surveillance
- ROP screening

**SGA Neonate:**
- Blood glucose monitoring (risk of hypoglycemia)
- Polycythemia screening (PCV)
- Temperature monitoring
- Early feeding initiation

**LGA/IDM:**
- Blood glucose monitoring (rebound hypoglycemia)
- Calcium monitoring
- Polycythemia, jaundice screening
- Echocardiography (HOCM, congenital heart defects)

**Post-term:**
- Meconium aspiration risk
- Hypoglycemia monitoring (depleted glycogen)
- Polycythemia screening

## Complications
- **Preterm**: RDS, NEC, IVH, PDA, sepsis, ROP, BPD, developmental delay
- **SGA**: Hypoglycemia, polycythemia, hypothermia, metabolic syndrome later in life
- **LGA**: Birth injuries, hypoglycemia, respiratory distress
- **Post-term**: Meconium aspiration, stillbirth, macrosomia
