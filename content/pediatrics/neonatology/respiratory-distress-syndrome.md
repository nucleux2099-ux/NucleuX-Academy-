# Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS)

> 📚 **Sources:** Nelson's Pediatrics 21st Ed, OP Ghai 9th Ed, NNF Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

RDS (formerly Hyaline Membrane Disease) is caused by **surfactant deficiency** in premature lungs. Surfactant reduces alveolar surface tension, preventing alveolar collapse. Without it, progressive atelectasis → hypoxemia → respiratory failure.

**Surfactant = Phospholipids (90%) + Proteins (10%)**
- Dipalmitoyl phosphatidylcholine (DPPC) = main component
- Produced by Type II pneumocytes from **24 weeks**, adequate by **35 weeks**

## Key Points

- **Inverse relationship** with gestational age: <28 weeks = 80% risk, >35 weeks = rare
- Onset: Within **6 hours of birth** (usually within minutes)
- Lecithin/Sphingomyelin (L/S) ratio ≥2 = lung maturity
- Phosphatidylglycerol (PG) presence = mature lungs
- **Antenatal steroids reduce risk by 50%**
- More common in: Males, Diabetic mothers, C-section, Second twin

## Clinical Features

**Classic Triad (appears within 6 hours):**
1. **Tachypnea** (RR >60/min)
2. **Grunting** (expiratory grunt = glottis closure to maintain PEEP)
3. **Retractions** (subcostal, intercostal, suprasternal)

**Additional Signs:**
- Nasal flaring
- Cyanosis (central)
- Decreased air entry bilaterally
- Silverman-Anderson score for severity (0-10, higher = worse)

**Silverman-Anderson Score Components:**
| Finding | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---------|---|---|---|
| Chest movement | Synchronized | Lag | See-saw |
| Intercostal retractions | None | Just visible | Marked |
| Xiphoid retractions | None | Just visible | Marked |
| Nasal flaring | None | Minimal | Marked |
| Grunting | None | With stethoscope | Audible |

## Diagnosis

**Clinical Criteria:**
- Preterm baby + respiratory distress within 6 hours + characteristic CXR

**Chest X-ray (Pathognomonic):**
- **Reticulogranular/Ground glass** appearance (diffuse)
- **Air bronchograms** (air-filled bronchi visible against opaque lung)
- **Low lung volumes**
- Grading (I-IV): IV = white-out lungs, cardiac borders obscured

**Blood Gas:**
- Hypoxemia (↓PaO2)
- Respiratory acidosis (↓pH, ↑PaCO2)
- Later: Metabolic acidosis

**L/S Ratio (Amniotic fluid):**
- ≥2.0 = mature
- 1.5-2.0 = transitional
- <1.5 = immature

## Management

### Prevention (Most Important!)
**Antenatal Corticosteroids:**
- **Betamethasone 12mg IM × 2 doses, 24 hours apart** (preferred)
- OR Dexamethasone 6mg IM × 4 doses, 12 hours apart
- Given: 24-34 weeks gestation with risk of preterm delivery
- Effective if delivered >24 hours and <7 days after completion

### Supportive Care
1. **Thermoregulation** - Neutral thermal environment
2. **Fluid management** - Start 60-80 mL/kg/day D10W
3. **Monitoring** - SpO2, vitals, blood gases

### Respiratory Support (Stepwise)
1. **Oxygen therapy** - Target SpO2: 91-95%
2. **CPAP** (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) - 5-8 cm H2O
3. **Mechanical ventilation** - If CPAP fails

### Surfactant Therapy
**Types:**
- Natural (bovine/porcine): Survanta, Curosurf, Alveofact
- Synthetic: Lucinactant

**Dose:**
- Survanta: 4 mL/kg (100 mg phospholipid/kg)
- Curosurf: 2.5 mL/kg (200 mg/kg)

**Administration:**
- Via ET tube, 4 aliquots with position changes
- "INSURE" technique: Intubate → Surfactant → Extubate to CPAP

**Timing:**
- Prophylactic (<2 hours) for very preterm (<28 weeks)
- Rescue (after diagnosis) for others

## Complications

- **Acute:** Pneumothorax, pulmonary hemorrhage, PDA
- **Chronic:** Bronchopulmonary Dysplasia (BPD) - O2 dependency at 36 weeks PMA
- **Systemic:** IVH, ROP, NEC

**MCQ Pearls:**
- ❗ Diabetes increases RDS risk (insulin inhibits surfactant)
- ❗ PROM decreases RDS risk (stress → steroids)
- ❗ Female babies have lower RDS risk
- ❗ Surfactant reduces SURFACE TENSION, not viscosity
- ❗ Ground glass = RDS; White-out = Severe RDS or pneumonia
