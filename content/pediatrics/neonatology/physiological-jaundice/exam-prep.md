# Physiological Jaundice — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Benign unconjugated jaundice in 60% term, 80% preterm neonates. Appears >24h, peaks day 3-5, resolves <2 weeks. Due to high RBC mass + immature UGT + increased enterohepatic circulation. Baby is WELL. Usually no treatment needed.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Appears AFTER 24 hours (before 24h = pathological)
- ★ Peak: day 3-5 (term), day 5-7 (preterm)
- ★ Maximum TSB: <15 mg/dL (term), <12 mg/dL (preterm)
- ★ Rate of rise <5 mg/dL/day
- ★ Duration: <14 days (term), <21 days (preterm)
- ★ UGT activity at birth = 1% of adult levels
- ★ Neonatal RBC lifespan = 70-90 days (vs 120 in adults)
- ★ Breastfeeding jaundice (day 2-4, inadequate intake) vs Breast milk jaundice (day 4-7, β-glucuronidase)
- ★ Continue breastfeeding in both breastfeeding and breast milk jaundice
- ★ Sun exposure NOT recommended for jaundice treatment
- ★ Sterile gut → no urobilinogen → increased enterohepatic circulation

## Mnemonics
**Why neonates get jaundice — "PILE":**
- **P**roduction increased (high RBC mass, short lifespan)
- **I**mmature conjugation (low UGT)
- **L**igandin low (decreased hepatic uptake)
- **E**nterohepatic circulation increased (sterile gut, β-glucuronidase)

**Physiological jaundice criteria — "After 24, Less than 5, Under 15, Gone by 14":**
- Appears AFTER 24 hours
- Rise LESS THAN 5 mg/dL/day
- Peak UNDER 15 mg/dL
- GONE BY 14 days
