# Meconium Aspiration Syndrome — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
MAS = aspiration of MSAF → ball-valve obstruction + chemical pneumonitis + surfactant inactivation → respiratory distress. Term/post-term babies. CXR: coarse patchy opacities + hyperinflation. PPHN = most feared complication. Updated NRP: no routine tracheal suctioning.

## High Yield Points
- ★ MAS is almost exclusively a disease of term/post-term babies
- ★ Ball-valve mechanism: partial obstruction → air trapping → pneumothorax
- ★ CXR: coarse patchy opacities + hyperinflation + air leaks
- ★ PPHN: severe cyanosis + pre-post ductal SpO₂ difference >10%
- ★ NRP 2020: NO routine intubation/tracheal suctioning even in non-vigorous babies
- ★ Pneumothorax occurs in 15-30% of MAS cases
- ★ Inhaled nitric oxide (iNO) = treatment for PPHN
- ★ Meconium is rarely passed before 34 weeks (GI tract immaturity)
- ★ Barrel-shaped chest = air trapping sign
- ★ Antibiotics given because meconium promotes bacterial growth

## Mnemonics
**MAS Pathophysiology — "BASIC":**
- **B**all-valve obstruction
- **A**telectasis (complete obstruction)
- **S**urfactant inactivation
- **I**nflammation (chemical pneumonitis)
- **C**onstriction of pulmonary vessels (PPHN)

**PPHN treatment — "NO SilE":**
- **N**itric **O**xide (inhaled)
- **Sil**denafil
- **E**CMO (last resort)
