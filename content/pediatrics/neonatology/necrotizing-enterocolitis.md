# Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC)

> 📚 **Sources:** Nelson's Pediatrics 21st Ed, OP Ghai 9th Ed, Bell's Staging
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

NEC is an **acute inflammatory bowel necrosis** primarily affecting preterm infants. It's the most common surgical emergency in neonates and has high mortality (20-30%).

**Pathophysiology Triad:**
1. **Intestinal immaturity** (barrier dysfunction)
2. **Abnormal bacterial colonization** (dysbiosis)
3. **Ischemia-reperfusion injury** (hypoxic insult)

Formula feeding, hypoxia, and infection trigger inflammatory cascade → mucosal necrosis → transmural necrosis → perforation.

## Key Points

- Affects **5-10% of VLBW infants**
- Most common: 30-32 weeks gestation, 2-3 weeks postnatal age
- **Terminal ileum and ascending colon** most commonly affected
- Pathognomonic finding: **Pneumatosis intestinalis** (gas in bowel wall)
- Breast milk is **protective**
- Bell's staging guides management

## Risk Factors

### Major
- **Prematurity** (strongest risk factor)
- Very low birth weight (<1500g)
- Formula feeding (vs breast milk)

### Contributing
- Perinatal asphyxia
- Hypotension, shock
- Polycythemia (hyperviscosity)
- Patent ductus arteriosus
- Umbilical catheterization
- Exchange transfusion
- Rapid advancement of feeds
- Hypertonic formula or medications
- Bacterial colonization (abnormal flora)

### Protective Factors
- **Breast milk** (human milk oligosaccharides, IgA)
- Probiotics (evidence for prevention)
- Antenatal steroids
- Slow feed advancement

## Clinical Features

**Classic Triad:**
1. **Abdominal distension**
2. **Bilious vomiting/gastric aspirates**
3. **Bloody stools**

**Systemic Signs:**
- Temperature instability
- Lethargy, apnea
- Hypotension, poor perfusion
- Metabolic acidosis
- Thrombocytopenia

**Abdominal Signs:**
- Distension (early sign)
- Tenderness, guarding
- Visible bowel loops
- Abdominal wall erythema/discoloration (late - suggests gangrene)
- Absent bowel sounds

**Progression:**
- Mild: Non-specific, feeding intolerance
- Moderate: Definite abdominal findings, systemic illness
- Severe: Shock, DIC, peritonitis, perforation

## Bell's Staging

| Stage | Clinical | Radiological | Treatment |
|-------|----------|--------------|-----------|
| **IA - Suspected** | Mild distension, gastric residuals, guaiac-positive stools | Normal or mild ileus | NPO, antibiotics × 3 days |
| **IB - Suspected** | Same + gross blood in stool | Same | NPO, antibiotics × 3 days |
| **IIA - Definite, Mild** | Moderate distension, absent bowel sounds, abdominal tenderness | Ileus, pneumatosis intestinalis | NPO × 7-10 days, antibiotics |
| **IIB - Definite, Moderate** | Marked distension, abdominal wall cellulitis, palpable mass | Pneumatosis, portal venous gas | NPO × 14 days, antibiotics |
| **IIIA - Advanced, Severe** | Deterioration, shock, DIC, acidosis | Same + ascites | Intensive care, consider surgery |
| **IIIB - Advanced, Perforation** | Same + peritonitis | **Pneumoperitoneum** | SURGERY |

## Diagnosis

### Clinical
- High index of suspicion in preterm with feeding intolerance + abdominal distension

### Laboratory
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| CBC | Thrombocytopenia, neutropenia/leukocytosis |
| ABG | Metabolic acidosis |
| CRP | Elevated |
| Coagulation | Prolonged PT/aPTT (DIC) |
| Blood culture | May be positive (50%) |

### Radiological (Abdominal X-ray)
**Diagnostic Findings:**
- **Pneumatosis intestinalis:** Air in bowel wall (bubbly/linear) - PATHOGNOMONIC
- **Portal venous gas:** Linear lucencies over liver - indicates severity
- **Pneumoperitoneum:** Free air - indicates perforation → SURGERY

**Other Findings:**
- Dilated bowel loops
- Thickened bowel walls
- Ascites (gasless abdomen)
- Persistent single dilated loop (sentinel loop)

**Frequency:**
- Every 6-8 hours initially if suspected/confirmed
- Look for: Increasing pneumatosis, portal gas, fixed loop, perforation

### Paracentesis
- Brown or green turbid fluid = bowel necrosis/perforation
- May be diagnostic when X-ray inconclusive

## Management

### Medical Management (Stage I-II, IIIA)

**1. Bowel Rest:**
- NPO (Nil per os)
- Nasogastric decompression
- Duration: 7-14 days based on stage

**2. IV Fluids and Nutrition:**
- TPN for nutritional support
- Correct fluid and electrolyte imbalances

**3. Antibiotics:**
```
Standard regimen:
- Ampicillin + Gentamicin + Metronidazole
OR
- Ampicillin + Cefotaxime + Metronidazole (if gentamicin toxicity concern)

Duration:
- Stage I: 3 days
- Stage II: 7-14 days
- Stage III: 14 days or more
```

**4. Supportive Care:**
- Respiratory support (mechanical ventilation if needed)
- Cardiovascular support (inotropes for shock)
- Correction of acidosis, coagulopathy
- Transfusions (platelets, FFP, pRBC as needed)

**5. Serial Monitoring:**
- Abdominal examination every 4-6 hours
- Abdominal X-ray every 6-8 hours
- Laboratory monitoring

### Surgical Management

**Indications:**
- **Absolute:** Pneumoperitoneum (perforation)
- **Relative:**
  - Clinical deterioration despite medical therapy
  - Fixed dilated loop on serial X-rays
  - Abdominal wall erythema
  - Palpable mass
  - Portal venous gas with clinical worsening
  - Positive paracentesis

**Procedures:**
1. **Primary peritoneal drainage:**
   - For very unstable ELBW infants
   - Temporizing measure or definitive in some cases

2. **Exploratory laparotomy:**
   - Resection of necrotic bowel
   - May require: Ostomy creation, "clip and drop" (second look), primary anastomosis

**Post-operative Complications:**
- Short bowel syndrome
- Strictures (most common late complication)
- Intestinal failure
- Recurrent NEC

## Prevention

**Proven Strategies:**
1. **Breast milk feeding** - Most effective single intervention
2. **Probiotics** - Evidence supports in VLBW infants
3. **Antenatal steroids** - Reduce overall mortality and NEC
4. **Standardized feeding protocols** - Slow advancement, delayed fortification
5. **Avoidance of unnecessary antibiotics** - Preserve gut microbiome

**Not Proven:**
- Oral antibiotics prophylaxis
- Oral immunoglobulins

## Prognosis

| Stage | Survival |
|-------|----------|
| Stage I-II | >90% |
| Stage IIIA | 70-80% |
| Stage IIIB (surgical) | 50-70% |

**Long-term Complications:**
- Strictures (10-30%) - Present weeks to months later
- Short bowel syndrome (if extensive resection)
- Neurodevelopmental impairment
- Cholestasis (from prolonged TPN)
- Failure to thrive

**MCQ Pearls:**
- ❗ Pneumatosis intestinalis = Air in bowel wall = NEC until proven otherwise
- ❗ Portal venous gas = More severe, NOT absolute surgical indication
- ❗ Pneumoperitoneum = ABSOLUTE surgical indication
- ❗ Breast milk reduces NEC by 50-80%
- ❗ Probiotics reduce NEC in VLBW (NNT ~25-30)
- ❗ Formula feeding = Major modifiable risk factor
- ❗ Thrombocytopenia = Marker of severity in NEC
- ❗ Late strictures can present weeks to months later - need contrast study before ostomy closure
