# Neonatal Jaundice

> 📚 **Sources:** Nelson's Pediatrics 21st Ed, OP Ghai 9th Ed, AAP Guidelines 2022
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Neonatal jaundice (hyperbilirubinemia) is **visible yellow discoloration** when serum bilirubin exceeds **5 mg/dL**. It results from an imbalance between bilirubin production and conjugation/excretion.

**Why common in neonates?**
1. ↑ RBC mass + shorter RBC lifespan (90 days vs 120)
2. ↓ Hepatic uptake (low ligandin)
3. ↓ UGT1A1 (glucuronyl transferase) activity
4. ↑ Enterohepatic circulation (high β-glucuronidase)

**Bilirubin pathway:** Heme → Biliverdin → **Unconjugated bilirubin** → (liver) → **Conjugated bilirubin** → Gut → Stercobilin

## Key Points

- **Physiological jaundice** = Normal, self-limiting, NEVER pathological
- **Pathological jaundice** = Requires investigation and treatment
- Unconjugated = Lipid soluble = **Crosses BBB** → Kernicterus risk
- Conjugated = Water soluble = Safe but indicates liver/biliary disease
- **Kramer's rule** for clinical estimation (cephalocaudal progression)

## Classification

### Physiological Jaundice
| Feature | Term Baby | Preterm Baby |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Appears | Day 2-3 | Day 3-5 |
| Peaks | Day 3-5 (12-15 mg/dL) | Day 5-7 (15-17 mg/dL) |
| Disappears | Day 7-10 | Day 14 |
| Rate of rise | <5 mg/dL/day | <5 mg/dL/day |

### Pathological Jaundice (Any ONE = Pathological)
1. Jaundice within **24 hours**
2. Bilirubin rising **>5 mg/dL/day** (or >0.5 mg/dL/hour)
3. Peak bilirubin **>15 mg/dL** (term) or **>12 mg/dL** (preterm)
4. **Conjugated bilirubin** >2 mg/dL or >20% of total
5. Jaundice persisting **>2 weeks** (term) or **>3 weeks** (preterm)
6. Clinical signs of illness

## Clinical Features

**Kramer's Rule (Zone = Bilirubin Level):**
| Zone | Area | Approximate TSB |
|------|------|-----------------|
| 1 | Face, neck | 5-7 mg/dL |
| 2 | Upper trunk to umbilicus | 7-10 mg/dL |
| 3 | Lower trunk to thighs | 10-13 mg/dL |
| 4 | Arms, legs below knee | 13-17 mg/dL |
| 5 | Palms, soles | >17 mg/dL |

**Kernicterus (Bilirubin Encephalopathy):**
- **Acute:** Lethargy, hypotonia, poor suck → Hypertonia, retrocollis, opisthotonus, fever, high-pitched cry, seizures
- **Chronic:** Choreoathetoid CP, upward gaze palsy, sensorineural deafness, dental enamel hypoplasia

## Etiology by Timing

### Day 1 (Always Pathological!)
- **Hemolytic:** Rh incompatibility, ABO incompatibility, G6PD deficiency
- Sepsis, TORCH infections

### Day 2-7
- Physiological (most common)
- Breastfeeding jaundice (inadequate intake)
- Hemolysis, polycythemia, sepsis
- Cephalohematoma, bruising

### >2 Weeks (Prolonged)
- **Breast milk jaundice** (inhibits UGT)
- Hypothyroidism
- Biliary atresia (conjugated)
- Gilbert, Crigler-Najjar syndromes
- Metabolic: Galactosemia

## Diagnosis

**First-line:**
- Total Serum Bilirubin (TSB)
- Direct (conjugated) bilirubin
- Blood group (mother + baby) + Coombs test (DCT)
- Complete blood count + reticulocyte count + peripheral smear

**Second-line (if indicated):**
- G6PD assay
- Sepsis workup
- Thyroid function tests
- TORCH screening

**Transcutaneous Bilirubinometry (TcB):**
- Non-invasive screening
- Correlates with TSB up to 15 mg/dL
- Useful for screening, confirm with TSB if elevated

## Management

### Phototherapy

**Mechanism:** Converts unconjugated bilirubin to water-soluble photoisomers (lumirubin) → excreted in bile/urine without conjugation

**Indications:** Based on Bhutani nomogram (TSB vs hours of age)
- Term healthy: Usually >15-18 mg/dL
- With risk factors: Lower threshold

**Types:**
- Conventional: Single overhead lights
- Intensive: Multiple light sources + reflective surfaces (doubles irradiance)
- Fiberoptic blankets: For supplemental use

**Effective Phototherapy:**
- Blue-green spectrum (460-490 nm)
- Irradiance >30 μW/cm²/nm
- Maximum skin exposure
- Distance: 20-30 cm from baby

**Nursing Care During Phototherapy:**
- Eyes covered (risk of retinal damage)
- Genitals covered (theoretical gonadal damage)
- Monitor temperature (hyperthermia/hypothermia)
- Extra fluids (insensible losses ↑ 20-40%)
- Check bilirubin every 6-12 hours

**Side Effects:**
- Bronze baby syndrome (with conjugated hyperbilirubinemia)
- Loose green stools (photoproducts)
- Skin rash
- Dehydration

### Exchange Transfusion

**Mechanism:** Removes bilirubin-laden blood + antibody-coated RBCs, replaces with fresh blood

**Volume:** Double volume exchange = 2 × 80-90 mL/kg = **160-180 mL/kg**

**Indications:**
- TSB approaching 25-30 mg/dL (term, healthy)
- Signs of acute bilirubin encephalopathy
- TSB rising despite intensive phototherapy
- Lower thresholds for preterm/sick

**Blood Used:**
- Fresh (<7 days), irradiated, CMV-negative
- Rh incompatibility: O negative, compatible with mother's serum
- ABO incompatibility: O cells with AB plasma

**Complications:**
- Thrombocytopenia, hypocalcemia
- Infection, NEC, portal vein thrombosis
- Air embolism, cardiac arrhythmias
- Mortality: ~1%

### Other Treatments

**IVIg:** For isoimmune hemolysis (Rh, ABO) - 0.5-1 g/kg over 2 hours

**Phenobarbitone:** Induces UGT (rarely used now; for Crigler-Najjar)

## Special Conditions

### ABO Incompatibility
- Mother O, Baby A or B
- Milder than Rh; may occur in first pregnancy
- Direct Coombs: Weakly positive or negative
- Spherocytes on smear

### Rh Isoimmunization
- Rh negative mother, Rh positive baby
- Not in first pregnancy (unless prior sensitization)
- Direct Coombs: Strongly positive
- Hydrops fetalis if severe

### Breast Milk Jaundice
- Appears Day 4-7, peaks Week 2-3
- May persist 3-12 weeks
- Baby is well, gaining weight
- Interruption of breastfeeding for 24-48 hours → ↓ bilirubin confirms diagnosis
- Continue breastfeeding (benefits outweigh risks)

### Breastfeeding Jaundice
- Due to inadequate intake → ↑ enterohepatic circulation
- Day 2-5, baby appears dehydrated
- Treatment: Improve breastfeeding, supplement if needed

## High-Yield for Exams

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Visible jaundice | Bilirubin >5 mg/dL |
| Physiological peak (term) | Day 3-5, up to 12-15 mg/dL |
| Pathological: Day 1 | Always investigate |
| Kramer Zone 5 | Palms/soles, >17 mg/dL |
| Kernicterus triad | CP + Deafness + Upward gaze palsy |
| Phototherapy wavelength | Blue-green, 460-490 nm |
| Exchange volume | 160-180 mL/kg (double volume) |
| Breast milk jaundice | Peaks week 2-3, baby well |
| Breastfeeding jaundice | Early, poor intake, dehydrated |
| Direct Coombs | Positive in isoimmune hemolysis |

**MCQ Pearls:**
- ❗ Jaundice in first 24 hours = ALWAYS pathological
- ❗ Conjugated hyperbilirubinemia = NEVER physiological
- ❗ Breast milk jaundice → Continue breastfeeding
- ❗ Kernicterus risk = Unconjugated bilirubin only
- ❗ Bronze baby = Phototherapy + Conjugated bilirubin
- ❗ G6PD deficiency = Most common enzyme deficiency causing jaundice
