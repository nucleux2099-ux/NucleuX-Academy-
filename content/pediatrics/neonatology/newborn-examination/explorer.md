# Newborn Examination

## Overview
A systematic newborn examination is essential within the first 24 hours of life and before discharge. It identifies congenital anomalies, birth injuries, and conditions requiring immediate intervention. The exam includes assessment of vital signs, anthropometry, general appearance, and a detailed head-to-toe system examination. It also includes screening tests and assessment of gestational age.

## Key Points
- Perform within first 24 hours and before discharge
- Includes anthropometry: weight, length, head circumference
- Vital signs: HR 120-160/min, RR 30-60/min, Temp 36.5-37.5°C
- Normal birth weight: 2500-4000g; length: 50 cm; HC: 35 cm
- Red reflex, hip examination (Barlow/Ortolani), and cardiovascular assessment are critical
- Assess for congenital anomalies and birth injuries

## Definition & Classification

### Timing of Examinations
1. **Immediate (at birth)**: Apgar, need for resuscitation, gross anomalies
2. **Detailed (within 24 hours)**: Complete head-to-toe examination
3. **Pre-discharge**: Repeat examination, feeding assessment, screening tests
4. **Follow-up**: Within 48-72 hours after early discharge

### Normal Neonatal Parameters
| Parameter | Normal Value |
|-----------|-------------|
| Weight | 2500-4000 g (avg 3000 g) |
| Length | 47-53 cm (avg 50 cm) |
| Head circumference | 33-37 cm (avg 35 cm) |
| Chest circumference | 30.5-33 cm (1-2 cm < HC) |
| Heart rate | 120-160 bpm |
| Respiratory rate | 30-60/min |
| Temperature | 36.5-37.5°C (axillary) |
| Blood pressure | 60-80/40-50 mmHg |

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Purpose of Newborn Examination
- Detect life-threatening conditions (cyanotic CHD, TEF, diaphragmatic hernia)
- Identify congenital anomalies (cleft lip/palate, DDH, neural tube defects)
- Assess gestational age and appropriateness of growth
- Screen for birth injuries
- Establish baseline parameters
- Counsel parents

## Clinical Features — Systematic Examination

### General Assessment
- Color (pink, jaundice, cyanosis, pallor)
- Activity, tone, posture (term = flexed)
- Cry (lusty vs weak vs high-pitched)
- Dysmorphic features

### Head
- **Fontanelles**: Anterior (2.5 × 2.5 cm, diamond-shaped, closes 12-18 months); Posterior (0.5 cm, triangular, closes 2-3 months)
- Sutures: Patent but not widely separated
- Molding, caput succedaneum, cephalhematoma
- Head circumference (occipitofrontal)

### Eyes
- **Red reflex**: MUST be checked (absence → retinoblastoma, cataract, glaucoma)
- Subconjunctival hemorrhage (common, benign)
- Pupillary response
- Epicanthal folds (Down syndrome)

### Ears
- Position: upper ear attachment above line from outer canthus of eye
- Low-set ears → chromosomal abnormalities, renal anomalies
- Preauricular tags/pits

### Mouth
- Palate: intact (palpate for submucous cleft)
- Epstein pearls (white keratin cysts on palate — normal)
- Ranula (cyst under tongue), tongue-tie
- Natal teeth

### Cardiovascular
- Precordial impulse, heart sounds
- Murmurs (most in first 24h are transitional — PDA closing)
- Femoral pulses: MUST be checked (absent/weak → coarctation of aorta)
- Four-limb BP if coarctation suspected
- Pre-post ductal SpO₂ screening for critical CHD

### Respiratory
- Rate, pattern, work of breathing
- Chest symmetry, breath sounds
- Grunting, nasal flaring, retractions

### Abdomen
- Soft, slightly distended
- Liver palpable 1-2 cm below costal margin (normal)
- Spleen tip may be palpable (normal)
- Kidneys: palpable in neonates (especially left)
- Umbilicus: 2 arteries + 1 vein (single umbilical artery → renal anomalies)
- Anal patency (passage of meconium within 48 hours)

### Genitalia
- **Male**: Testes descended, meatus position (hypospadias/epispadias), hydrocele
- **Female**: Pseudomenstruation (maternal estrogen withdrawal — normal), vaginal tags
- Ambiguous genitalia → urgent endocrine evaluation

### Musculoskeletal
- **Spine**: Intact, no dimples/tufts/sinuses (lumbosacral → spina bifida occulta)
- **Hips**: Barlow test (dislocatable?) and Ortolani test (reducible?) for DDH
- **Limbs**: Symmetry, digits (polydactyly, syndactyly), talipes
- **Clavicles**: Palpate for fractures

### Neurological
- Tone, posture, spontaneous movements
- **Primitive reflexes**: Moro (0-4 mo), grasp (0-3 mo), rooting (0-4 mo), sucking, stepping, ATNR (tonic neck reflex, 0-6 mo)
- Cry quality

### Skin
- Vernix caseosa, lanugo (normal)
- Erythema toxicum (benign, eosinophils — "flea-bite" rash)
- Mongolian spots (blue-gray, sacral area — normal in Indian babies)
- Milia (white papules on nose/face — keratin cysts)
- Hemangiomas (strawberry, port-wine stain → Sturge-Weber if V1 distribution)
- Harlequin color change (transient vasomotor phenomenon)

## Diagnosis — Screening Tests
1. **Newborn metabolic screen**: TSH (congenital hypothyroidism), PKU, G6PD
2. **Pulse oximetry**: Critical CHD screening (pre + post-ductal SpO₂)
3. **OAE/BERA**: Universal newborn hearing screening
4. **Red reflex**: Retinoblastoma, congenital cataract
5. **Blood glucose**: If LGA, SGA, IDM, or symptomatic
6. **Blood group + Coombs**: If Rh-negative mother

## Management
- Maintain warmth (skin-to-skin contact)
- Initiate breastfeeding within 1 hour
- Vitamin K 1 mg IM (prevent VKDB)
- Eye prophylaxis (where applicable)
- Cord care (clean and dry)
- Immunizations: BCG + OPV-0 + Hepatitis B birth dose
- Parental counseling on feeding, danger signs, follow-up

## Complications
- Missed congenital anomalies if examination incomplete
- DDH → late diagnosis → avascular necrosis
- Critical CHD → sudden collapse if missed
- Retinoblastoma → delayed diagnosis if red reflex not checked
