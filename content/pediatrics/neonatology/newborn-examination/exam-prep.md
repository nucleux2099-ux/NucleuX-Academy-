# Newborn Examination — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Systematic head-to-toe exam within 24 hours. Normal: weight 3kg, length 50cm, HC 35cm, HR 120-160, RR 30-60. Check red reflex, femoral pulses, hips (Barlow/Ortolani), anal patency. Vitamin K 1mg IM at birth. BCG + OPV-0 + HepB birth dose.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Anterior fontanelle: 2.5cm, diamond-shaped, closes 12-18 months
- ★ Posterior fontanelle: 0.5cm, triangular, closes 2-3 months
- ★ Absent red reflex → retinoblastoma or congenital cataract
- ★ Absent femoral pulses → coarctation of aorta
- ★ Single umbilical artery → associated with renal anomalies
- ★ Barlow = dislocate; Ortolani = reduce (DDH screening)
- ★ Erythema toxicum: benign, eosinophils on smear, "flea-bite" rash
- ★ Mongolian spots: blue-gray sacral, normal in Indian babies
- ★ Vitamin K 1mg IM at birth prevents hemorrhagic disease of newborn (VKDB)
- ★ Epstein pearls = keratin cysts on palate (normal)
- ★ Pulse oximetry screening for critical CHD (pre + post-ductal)

## Mnemonics
**Birth vaccines — "BOH":**
- **B**CG
- **O**PV-0
- **H**epatitis B birth dose

**DDH tests — "BAR-O":**
- **BAR**low = push Back (dislocate)
- **O**rtolani = pull Out (reduce) → "clunk"

**Primitive reflexes disappearance:**
- Moro: **4** months
- Grasp: **3** months (palmar); **9** months (plantar)
- Rooting: **4** months
- ATNR: **6** months
- Parachute: appears at **9** months (never disappears)
