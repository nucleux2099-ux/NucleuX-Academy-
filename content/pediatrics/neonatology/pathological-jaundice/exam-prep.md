# Pathological Jaundice — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Jaundice within 24h = always pathological. Most common cause: hemolytic (Rh > ABO). Rh: needs prior sensitization, DCT positive, can cause hydrops. ABO: can affect first pregnancy, milder. Treatment: phototherapy, exchange transfusion, IVIG. Prevention: Anti-D immunoglobulin.

## High Yield Points
- ★ Jaundice <24 hours = hemolysis until proven otherwise
- ★ Rh disease: requires prior sensitization, anti-D IgG crosses placenta, DCT positive
- ★ ABO incompatibility: Mother O + Baby A/B, CAN affect first pregnancy, spherocytes on smear
- ★ Anti-D prophylaxis: 300 μg at 28 weeks + within 72 hours post-delivery
- ★ Hydrops fetalis: generalized edema + ascites + pleural effusion (severe Rh disease)
- ★ G6PD: X-linked, Heinz bodies, bite cells, avoid oxidant stress
- ★ Conjugated bilirubin >2 mg/dL = always pathological (biliary atresia, TORCH)
- ★ Biliary atresia: Kasai portoenterostomy before 60 days
- ★ Crigler-Najjar I: absent UGT, severe; Type II: responds to phenobarbitone
- ★ IVIG reduces exchange transfusion need in immune hemolysis
- ★ Bhutani nomogram: plot TSB vs age in hours for risk stratification

## Mnemonics
**Hemolytic causes — "RAGS":**
- **R**h incompatibility
- **A**BO incompatibility
- **G**6PD deficiency
- **S**pherocytosis

**Rh vs ABO:**
| Feature | Rh | ABO |
|---------|----|----|
| First pregnancy | No | Yes |
| Coombs | Strongly + | Weak/- |
| Severity | Severe | Mild |
| Smear | Normal | Spherocytes |
| Hydrops | Yes | Rare |
