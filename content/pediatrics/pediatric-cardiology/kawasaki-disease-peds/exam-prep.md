# Kawasaki Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Acute febrile vasculitis, <5 years. Fever ≥5 days + 4/5 criteria (conjunctivitis, oral changes, extremity changes, rash, lymphadenopathy). Leading cause of acquired heart disease in developed countries. Coronary aneurysm = most feared complication (25% untreated → <5% with IVIG). Treatment: IVIG 2 g/kg single dose within 10 days + aspirin. Thrombocytosis in subacute phase. Diagnosis is clinical — no specific test.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **IVIG 2 g/kg within 10 days** reduces coronary aneurysm from 25% → <5%
- ★ **Coronary artery aneurysm** = most serious complication
- ★ **Thrombocytosis** in week 2-3 (may exceed 1 million) — highest aneurysm risk period
- ★ **Conjunctivitis is limbal-sparing** and non-exudative
- ★ **Periungual desquamation** = hallmark of subacute phase
- ★ **Cervical lymphadenopathy** = least common diagnostic criterion
- ★ **Incomplete KD** — <4 criteria; infants <6 months at higher risk
- ★ **BCG scar reactivation** — useful diagnostic clue
- ★ **Aspirin** — one of few pediatric indications (high then low dose)
- ★ **IVIG-resistant** (~15%): second IVIG, methylprednisolone, infliximab
- ★ **Giant aneurysm >8 mm** — rarely regresses, needs anticoagulation
- ★ **Extreme irritability** — very characteristic feature

## Mnemonics

### **CRASH and Burn** — 5 Criteria
- **C**onjunctivitis (bilateral, non-exudative)
- **R**ash (polymorphous)
- **A**denopathy (cervical, ≥1.5 cm, unilateral)
- **S**trawberry tongue + lip changes
- **H**ands and feet changes (edema, erythema, peeling)
- **Burn** = Fever ≥5 days

### Phases: "ASC" — Acute (1-2 wk), Subacute (2-4 wk), Convalescent (4-8 wk)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Leading cause of acquired heart disease** in developed countries — KD
2. **Most feared complication** — Coronary artery aneurysm
3. **Treatment** — IVIG 2 g/kg + aspirin
4. **Least common criterion** — Cervical lymphadenopathy
5. **Limbal-sparing conjunctivitis** — differentiates from other causes
6. **Thrombocytosis timing** — Week 2-3 (subacute phase)
7. **Periungual peeling** — subacute phase hallmark
8. **Incomplete KD** — more common in infants; higher coronary risk
9. **IVIG-resistant management** — second IVIG, steroids, infliximab
10. **Aspirin and Reye syndrome** — switch to clopidogrel if influenza/varicella
