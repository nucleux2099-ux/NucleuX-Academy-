# Rheumatic Fever

## Overview
Acute Rheumatic Fever (ARF) is a systemic inflammatory disease occurring as a delayed, non-suppurative sequel to Group A β-hemolytic Streptococcal (GAS) pharyngitis. It primarily affects children 5-15 years and is the leading cause of acquired heart disease in children in developing countries. The hallmark is carditis, which can lead to chronic Rheumatic Heart Disease (RHD). India has one of the highest burdens globally.

## Key Points
- **Delayed sequel to GAS pharyngitis** (NOT skin infection)
- **Latent period:** 2-4 weeks after pharyngitis
- **Age:** 5-15 years (peak 8-12 years)
- **Jones Criteria** (2015 revised) for diagnosis
- **Carditis** — most serious manifestation; determines prognosis
- **Mitral valve** most commonly affected; mitral regurgitation most common lesion
- **Sydenham chorea** — may be the sole manifestation; occurs late (1-6 months)
- **ASO titer** — most commonly used evidence of recent strep infection
- **Penicillin** — drug of choice for treatment and prophylaxis

## Definition & Classification

### Jones Criteria (2015 AHA Revision)
**Diagnosis requires:** Evidence of preceding GAS infection + 2 Major OR 1 Major + 2 Minor criteria

#### Low-Risk Populations (ARF incidence <2/100,000)
| Major Criteria | Minor Criteria |
|---------------|----------------|
| Carditis (clinical/subclinical) | Arthralgia (polyarthralgia) |
| Polyarthritis | Fever ≥38.5°C |
| Chorea | ESR ≥60 mm/h or CRP ≥3 mg/dL |
| Erythema marginatum | Prolonged PR interval |
| Subcutaneous nodules | |

#### Moderate-High Risk Populations (India)
| Major Criteria | Minor Criteria |
|---------------|----------------|
| Carditis (clinical/subclinical echo) | Monoarthralgia |
| Arthritis (polyarthritis, monoarthritis, polyarthralgia) | Fever ≥38°C |
| Chorea | ESR ≥30 mm/h or CRP ≥3 mg/dL |
| Erythema marginatum | Prolonged PR interval |
| Subcutaneous nodules | |

### Evidence of Preceding GAS Infection
- Positive throat culture or Rapid Antigen Detection Test
- Elevated/rising streptococcal antibodies (ASO, Anti-DNase B)
- Recent scarlet fever

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Etiology
- **Causative agent:** Group A β-hemolytic Streptococcus (Streptococcus pyogenes)
- Only **pharyngeal** strep infection triggers ARF (NOT skin)
- **M-protein** on streptococcal cell wall — major virulence factor
- Rheumatogenic strains: M types 1, 3, 5, 6, 14, 18, 19, 24

### Pathophysiology — Molecular Mimicry
1. GAS pharyngitis → immune response against streptococcal antigens
2. **Molecular mimicry:** Streptococcal M-protein shares epitopes with human tissues
   - Cardiac myosin and valve glycoprotein → **carditis**
   - Neuronal tissue (basal ganglia) → **chorea**
   - Joint synovium → **arthritis**
3. Cross-reactive antibodies and T-cells attack host tissues
4. Type II and Type IV hypersensitivity reactions
5. **Aschoff bodies** — pathognomonic histological lesion (granulomatous inflammation with giant cells)
6. **Anitschkow cells** (owl-eye/caterpillar cells) — modified macrophages in Aschoff body

### Cardiac Pathology
- **Pancarditis:** Endocarditis + myocarditis + pericarditis
- **Mitral valve** most commonly affected (MC lesion: mitral regurgitation)
- Verrucous endocarditis → valve scarring → chronic RHD (mitral stenosis)
- Valve involvement: Mitral > Aortic > Tricuspid > Pulmonary

## Clinical Features

### Major Manifestations

#### 1. Carditis (50-70%)
- Most important manifestation — determines long-term prognosis
- **Valvulitis:** New murmur — apical pansystolic (MR), mid-diastolic (Carey Coombs murmur)
- **Myocarditis:** Tachycardia disproportionate to fever, CCF
- **Pericarditis:** Chest pain, friction rub, pericardial effusion
- **Subclinical carditis:** Detected only by echocardiography (Doppler MR/AR)

#### 2. Arthritis (60-75%)
- **Migratory polyarthritis** of large joints (knees, ankles, elbows, wrists)
- Extremely painful, exquisitely tender
- Responds dramatically to aspirin within 24-48 hours
- **Never causes permanent joint damage** (unlike RA)
- Each joint affected for 1-2 weeks

#### 3. Sydenham Chorea (10-30%)
- Involuntary, purposeless, jerky movements of face and limbs
- Emotional lability, muscle weakness
- **Late manifestation** (1-6 months after pharyngitis)
- May be the SOLE manifestation (no need for evidence of recent strep)
- Self-limiting (2-6 months); may recur
- More common in girls

#### 4. Erythema Marginatum (<5%)
- Evanescent, non-pruritic, pink-red rings/arcs with clear center
- Trunk and proximal limbs (never face)
- Comes and goes; worsened by heat
- Highly specific but rare

#### 5. Subcutaneous Nodules (<5%)
- Firm, painless, pea-sized nodules
- Over bony prominences and extensor tendons (elbows, knees, wrists, occiput)
- Associated with severe carditis
- Late manifestation

### Minor Manifestations
- **Fever** (high-grade, >38.5°C)
- **Arthralgia** (without overt arthritis)
- **Elevated acute phase reactants:** ESR, CRP
- **Prolonged PR interval** on ECG (first-degree AV block)

## Diagnosis

### Laboratory
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **ASO titer** | ↑ (>200 IU/mL in adults, >333 in children) — most commonly used |
| **Anti-DNase B** | ↑ (more specific, useful when ASO is negative) |
| **ESR** | ↑ (markedly elevated) |
| **CRP** | ↑ (useful for monitoring) |
| **Throat culture** | May be negative by time ARF presents |
| **CBC** | Leukocytosis, anemia of chronic disease |

### Investigations
- **ECG:** Prolonged PR interval (first-degree AV block); not diagnostic
- **Echocardiography:** ESSENTIAL — detects subclinical carditis (Doppler MR/AR)
- **CXR:** Cardiomegaly if significant carditis/CCF
- **Blood culture:** To rule out infective endocarditis

## Management

### Acute Treatment
1. **Eradicate GAS:** Benzathine Penicillin G 6-12 lakh IU IM single dose (or Penicillin V oral × 10 days)
2. **Anti-inflammatory:**
   - **Arthritis without carditis:** Aspirin 80-100 mg/kg/day × 4-6 weeks (tapered)
   - **Carditis:** Prednisolone 1-2 mg/kg/day × 2-3 weeks then taper + Aspirin overlap
3. **Chorea:** Haloperidol, valproate, or carbamazepine; bed rest
4. **CCF:** Diuretics, ACE inhibitors, digoxin; bed rest

### Secondary Prophylaxis (Prevention of Recurrence) — ⭐ Critical
| Category | Duration |
|----------|----------|
| ARF without carditis | 5 years or until age 21 (whichever is longer) |
| ARF with carditis, no residual disease | 10 years or until age 21 |
| ARF with residual heart disease | 10 years or until age 40 (lifelong if severe) |
| After valve surgery | Lifelong |

**Drug:** Benzathine Penicillin G 12 lakh IU IM every 3 weeks (preferred; every 4 weeks in low-risk)

### Primary Prophylaxis (Prevention of First Attack)
- Treat all GAS pharyngitis with penicillin
- Benzathine Penicillin G IM single dose OR Oral Penicillin V × 10 days
- Penicillin allergy: Azithromycin × 5 days

## Complications
- **Chronic RHD** — mitral stenosis (most common chronic lesion), MR, AR
- **CCF** — from severe carditis or chronic valve disease
- **Infective endocarditis** — on damaged valves
- **Atrial fibrillation** — from LA dilatation in mitral stenosis
- **Thromboembolism/stroke** — from AF and dilated LA
- **Recurrence** — with each strep reinfection if prophylaxis not maintained
- **Pulmonary hypertension** — from chronic mitral stenosis
