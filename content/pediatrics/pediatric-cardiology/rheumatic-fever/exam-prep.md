# Rheumatic Fever — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
ARF = delayed non-suppurative sequel to GAS pharyngitis (2-4 weeks). Age 5-15. Jones Criteria: 2 major OR 1 major + 2 minor + evidence of strep infection. Carditis = most serious (determines prognosis). Migratory polyarthritis = most common. Mitral valve most affected. Sydenham chorea may be sole manifestation. Treatment: eradicate strep (Benzathine Pen G) + aspirin/steroids. Secondary prophylaxis: Benzathine Pen G every 3 weeks for years.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Jones Criteria** — 2 Major or 1 Major + 2 Minor + strep evidence
- ★ **Carditis** = most serious; **Arthritis** = most common manifestation
- ★ **Mitral valve** most commonly affected; MR = most common acute lesion; MS = chronic
- ★ **Aschoff body** = pathognomonic histological lesion; **Anitschkow cells** (owl-eye)
- ★ **Molecular mimicry** = M-protein cross-reacts with cardiac myosin
- ★ **Only pharyngeal strep** causes ARF (NOT skin strep)
- ★ **ASO titer** = most commonly used evidence of recent strep
- ★ **Arthritis responds dramatically to aspirin** within 24-48 hours; never causes joint damage
- ★ **Chorea** = late (1-6 months), may be sole manifestation, no need for strep evidence
- ★ **Erythema marginatum** = evanescent rings on trunk, never face
- ★ **Secondary prophylaxis** = Benzathine Pen G IM every 3 weeks
- ★ **Carey Coombs murmur** = mid-diastolic murmur of acute rheumatic valvulitis

## Mnemonics

### **JONES** — Major Criteria
- **J**oints (migratory polyarthritis)
- **♥** Carditis
- **N**odules (subcutaneous)
- **E**rythema marginatum
- **S**ydenham chorea

### **CAFE PAL** — Minor Criteria
- **C**RP elevated
- **A**rthralgia
- **F**ever
- **E**SR elevated
- **P**R prolonged
- **A**cute phase reactants
- **L**eukocytosis

### Secondary Prophylaxis Duration: "5-10-Life"
- No carditis: **5** years or age 21
- Carditis, healed: **10** years or age 21
- Residual RHD: **10** years or age 40 / **Life**long if severe

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Pathognomonic lesion** — Aschoff body
2. **Most common manifestation** — Arthritis (60-75%)
3. **Most serious manifestation** — Carditis
4. **Sole manifestation possible** — Chorea (no strep evidence needed)
5. **Valve most affected** — Mitral; chronic lesion = mitral stenosis
6. **Jones criteria change in high-risk** — monoarthritis/polyarthralgia accepted as major
7. **Drug for prophylaxis** — Benzathine Penicillin G IM every 3 weeks
8. **Erythema marginatum** — never on face, evanescent rings
9. **Subcutaneous nodules** — associated with severe carditis
10. **ASO titer** — most commonly used strep marker
