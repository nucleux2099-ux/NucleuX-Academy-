# Congenital Heart Disease — Classification

## Overview
Congenital heart disease (CHD) is the most common congenital anomaly, affecting 8-10 per 1,000 live births. It encompasses structural abnormalities of the heart and great vessels present at birth. Classification into acyanotic (left-to-right shunts) and cyanotic (right-to-left shunts) CHD is fundamental. VSD is the most common CHD overall, while Tetralogy of Fallot is the most common cyanotic CHD.

## Key Points
- **Incidence:** 8-10 per 1,000 live births
- **Most common CHD overall:** VSD (25-30%)
- **Most common cyanotic CHD:** Tetralogy of Fallot (TOF)
- **Most common CHD in Down syndrome:** AVSD (Endocardial Cushion Defect)
- **Most common CHD in Turner syndrome:** Coarctation of aorta, bicuspid aortic valve
- **Acyanotic (L→R shunt):** VSD, ASD, PDA — increased pulmonary blood flow
- **Cyanotic (R→L shunt):** TOF, TGA, Tricuspid atresia — decreased/abnormal pulmonary flow
- **Eisenmenger syndrome:** Irreversible pulmonary hypertension from chronic L→R shunt

## Definition & Classification

### Major Classification
| Category | Mechanism | Examples |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Acyanotic — L→R Shunt** | ↑ Pulmonary blood flow | VSD, ASD, PDA, AVSD |
| **Acyanotic — Obstructive** | No shunt; obstruction to flow | PS, AS, Coarctation of aorta |
| **Cyanotic — ↓ Pulmonary flow** | R→L shunt, ↓ lung flow | TOF, Tricuspid atresia, Ebstein anomaly |
| **Cyanotic — ↑ Pulmonary flow** | Mixing lesions | TGA, TAPVR, Truncus arteriosus, Single ventricle |

### Individual Defects — Quick Summary

#### VSD (Ventricular Septal Defect)
- Most common CHD (25-30%)
- Types: Perimembranous (80%), muscular, inlet, outlet
- Small VSD: loud pansystolic murmur (Maladie de Roger), often closes spontaneously
- Large VSD: CCF by 6-8 weeks, failure to thrive, recurrent LRTI
- Complications: Eisenmenger syndrome, infective endocarditis, AR (outlet VSD)

#### ASD (Atrial Septal Defect)
- Second most common CHD
- Types: Ostium secundum (70%), ostium primum, sinus venosus
- Wide fixed splitting of S2 (hallmark)
- Ejection systolic murmur at pulmonary area (relative PS from increased flow)
- Usually asymptomatic in childhood; CCF in 3rd-4th decade if untreated

#### PDA (Patent Ductus Arteriosus)
- Common in premature infants
- Continuous "machinery" murmur (Gibson murmur) — best heard in left infraclavicular area
- Wide pulse pressure, bounding pulses
- Medical closure: Indomethacin/Ibuprofen (inhibit prostaglandin)
- PGE1 keeps ductus open in duct-dependent lesions (TGA, critical PS/AS, coarctation)

#### TOF (Tetralogy of Fallot)
- Most common cyanotic CHD
- **4 components:** VSD + Overriding aorta + RVOT obstruction (PS) + RVH
- **Boot-shaped heart** on CXR
- **Tet spells (hypercyanotic spells):** ↑cyanosis, squatting position
- Squatting ↑SVR → ↑L→R shunt → ↑pulmonary flow → relief
- Tet spell Rx: Knee-chest position, O₂, morphine, IV fluids, phenylephrine

#### TGA (Transposition of Great Arteries)
- Most common cyanotic CHD presenting in neonates
- Aorta arises from RV, PA from LV — parallel circulations
- **Egg-on-string** appearance on CXR
- Survival depends on mixing (ASD, VSD, PDA)
- **Balloon atrial septostomy (Rashkind procedure)** — emergency palliation
- Definitive: **Arterial switch operation (Jatene)** within first 2 weeks

#### Coarctation of Aorta
- Narrowing of aorta, usually at/near ductus arteriosus (juxtaductal)
- **Upper limb hypertension, weak femoral pulses**
- Radio-femoral delay
- Rib notching on CXR (>5 years — collateral intercostal arteries)
- Associated: Bicuspid aortic valve, Turner syndrome, Berry aneurysm

## Etiology & Pathophysiology

### Etiology
- **Multifactorial** (genetic + environmental) in most cases
- **Chromosomal:** Down (AVSD), Turner (CoA), DiGeorge/22q11 (Truncus, TOF), Williams (supravalvar AS)
- **Teratogens:** Rubella (PDA, PS), alcohol (VSD, ASD), lithium (Ebstein), maternal DM (TGA, VSD), phenytoin
- **Single gene:** Noonan (PS), Marfan (MVP, aortic root), Holt-Oram (ASD)

### Pathophysiology Principles
- **L→R shunt:** Blood flows from high-pressure left to low-pressure right → ↑pulmonary blood flow → pulmonary overcirculation → CCF
- **R→L shunt:** Deoxygenated blood enters systemic circulation → cyanosis
- **Eisenmenger syndrome:** Chronic ↑pulmonary flow → pulmonary vascular remodeling → irreversible pulmonary HTN → shunt reversal (L→R becomes R→L)
- **Duct-dependent lesions:** Survival depends on PDA remaining open; PGE1 infusion to maintain

## Clinical Features

### Acyanotic CHD Presentation
- **CCF symptoms:** Tachypnea, diaphoresis during feeds, poor weight gain, recurrent LRTI
- **Murmurs:** Characteristic for each defect
- **Failure to thrive**
- **Hepatomegaly** (right heart failure)

### Cyanotic CHD Presentation
- **Central cyanosis** — not responsive to O₂ (hyperoxia test negative)
- **Clubbing** (chronic cyanosis)
- **Polycythemia** → hyperviscosity → stroke risk
- **Growth failure**
- **Tet spells** (TOF) — squatting relieves
- **Brain abscess** (R→L shunt → paradoxical embolism)

### Hyperoxia Test (Critical for Neonates)
- Give 100% O₂ for 10 minutes
- PaO₂ >150 mmHg → pulmonary cause
- PaO₂ <100 mmHg → cyanotic CHD (shunt physiology)

## Diagnosis

### Non-Invasive
- **CXR:** Heart size, shape, pulmonary vascularity
  - Boot-shaped: TOF
  - Egg-on-string: TGA
  - Snowman/figure-of-8: TAPVR
  - Rib notching: Coarctation
- **ECG:** Chamber hypertrophy, axis
- **Echocardiography:** Gold standard for diagnosis — anatomy, function, hemodynamics
- **Pulse oximetry screening:** Pre-ductal (right hand) and post-ductal (foot) SpO₂

### Invasive
- **Cardiac catheterization:** Hemodynamics, angiography, interventional procedures
- **CT/MRI:** Complex anatomy, vascular rings

## Management

### Medical
- **CCF management:** Diuretics (furosemide), ACE inhibitors (enalapril), digoxin
- **PGE1 infusion:** Keep PDA open in duct-dependent lesions
- **Indomethacin/Ibuprofen:** Close PDA in preterm
- **Tet spell:** Knee-chest, O₂, morphine, fluids, phenylephrine, propranolol

### Surgical
- **Corrective:** Definitive repair (VSD closure, TOF repair, arterial switch)
- **Palliative:** BT shunt (systemic-to-pulmonary), PA banding (limit pulmonary flow)
- **Interventional catheterization:** Device closure (ASD, VSD, PDA), balloon valvuloplasty

### Infective Endocarditis Prophylaxis
- Required for prosthetic valves, previous IE, unrepaired cyanotic CHD, repaired CHD with residual defects
- Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg before dental procedures

## Complications
- **CCF** — most common complication of large L→R shunts
- **Eisenmenger syndrome** — irreversible; contraindication to surgery
- **Infective endocarditis**
- **Brain abscess** — cyanotic CHD (R→L shunt)
- **Polycythemia/hyperviscosity** → stroke
- **Growth failure**
- **Arrhythmias**
- **Pulmonary hypertension**
- **Paradoxical embolism** → stroke
