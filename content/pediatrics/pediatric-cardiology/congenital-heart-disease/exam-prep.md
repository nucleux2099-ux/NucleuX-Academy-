# Congenital Heart Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
CHD: 8-10/1000 live births. Most common overall: VSD. Most common cyanotic: TOF. Acyanotic = L→R shunt (VSD, ASD, PDA) → ↑pulmonary flow → CCF. Cyanotic = R→L shunt (TOF, TGA) → cyanosis. TOF = VSD + overriding aorta + RVOT obstruction + RVH; boot-shaped heart. TGA = egg-on-string; Rashkind septostomy → arterial switch. Hyperoxia test differentiates cardiac from pulmonary cyanosis.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Most common CHD:** VSD (25-30%)
- ★ **Most common cyanotic CHD:** TOF
- ★ **Down syndrome CHD:** AVSD (Endocardial Cushion Defect)
- ★ **Turner syndrome:** Coarctation of aorta + bicuspid aortic valve
- ★ **DiGeorge (22q11):** Truncus arteriosus, TOF
- ★ **Machinery murmur:** PDA (Gibson murmur)
- ★ **Wide fixed split S2:** ASD
- ★ **Boot-shaped heart:** TOF; **Egg-on-string:** TGA; **Snowman:** TAPVR
- ★ **PGE1** keeps PDA open in duct-dependent lesions
- ★ **Indomethacin** closes PDA in preterm
- ★ **Tet spell Rx:** Knee-chest, O₂, morphine, phenylephrine
- ★ **Eisenmenger** = irreversible pulmonary HTN; contraindicates surgery

## Mnemonics

### **5 T's of Cyanotic CHD**
- **T**etralogy of Fallot
- **T**ransposition of Great Arteries
- **T**runcus Arteriosus
- **T**otal Anomalous Pulmonary Venous Return
- **T**ricuspid Atresia

### TOF Components: "PROVe"
- **P**ulmonary stenosis (RVOT obstruction)
- **R**VH (right ventricular hypertrophy)
- **O**verriding aorta
- **V**SD

### Maternal Rubella: "PDA-PS" (PDA + Peripheral PS)

## Common MCQ Topics
1. **Most common CHD** — VSD
2. **Most common cyanotic** — TOF (beyond neonatal period)
3. **Most common neonatal cyanotic** — TGA
4. **CHD in Down syndrome** — AVSD
5. **PDA murmur** — Continuous machinery murmur
6. **ASD finding** — Wide fixed split S2
7. **Boot-shaped heart** — TOF
8. **Egg-on-string** — TGA
9. **Rib notching** — Coarctation of aorta
10. **Hyperoxia test** — PaO₂ <100 = cardiac; >150 = pulmonary
11. **Rashkind procedure** — Balloon atrial septostomy for TGA
12. **Eisenmenger** — chronic L→R shunt → irreversible pulmonary HTN → R→L shunt
