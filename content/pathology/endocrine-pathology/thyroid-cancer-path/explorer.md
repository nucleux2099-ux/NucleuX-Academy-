# Thyroid Cancer — Pathology

## Overview
Thyroid carcinomas are the most common endocrine malignancies. They arise from follicular epithelium (papillary, follicular, anaplastic) or parafollicular C cells (medullary). Papillary carcinoma is by far the most common (80%) and has an excellent prognosis.

## Key Points
- Most common endocrine malignancy
- Papillary > Follicular > Medullary > Anaplastic (in frequency)
- Radiation exposure is the most important risk factor for papillary carcinoma
- Prognosis inversely related to differentiation: Papillary (best) → Anaplastic (worst)
- RET/PTC rearrangement (papillary), RAS mutation (follicular), RET point mutation (medullary)

## Definition & Classification

| Type | Origin | Frequency | Prognosis |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Papillary** | Follicular epithelium | 80% | Excellent (>95% 10-yr survival) |
| **Follicular** | Follicular epithelium | 10-15% | Good |
| **Medullary** | Parafollicular C cells | 5% | Intermediate |
| **Anaplastic** | Undifferentiated | <5% | Dismal (<1 yr) |
| **Lymphoma** | MALT lymphoma | Rare | Variable |

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Risk Factors
- **Radiation exposure** — most important (especially in childhood); papillary type
- **Iodine deficiency** — follicular carcinoma (via goiter → hyperplasia)
- **Hashimoto's thyroiditis** — risk for thyroid lymphoma
- **MEN 2A/2B** — medullary carcinoma
- **Family history, female sex**

### Molecular Pathways
| Type | Key Mutations |
|------|--------------|
| Papillary | **RET/PTC rearrangement**, **BRAF V600E** (most common), RAS |
| Follicular | **RAS**, **PAX8-PPARγ** fusion |
| Medullary | **RET point mutations** (germline in MEN, somatic in sporadic) |
| Anaplastic | **TP53**, **BRAF**, **RAS** (often arises from pre-existing differentiated carcinoma) |

## Morphology

### Papillary Carcinoma
**Gross:** Infiltrative, firm, white; often cystic; may be multifocal; calcification common
**Microscopic — HIGH YIELD:**
- **Papillary architecture** with fibrovascular cores
- **Orphan Annie eye nuclei** — optically clear, ground-glass nuclei (due to chromatin dispersion)
- **Nuclear grooves** — longitudinal lines in nuclei
- **Intranuclear pseudoinclusions** — cytoplasmic invaginations into nucleus
- **Psammoma bodies** — concentric laminated calcifications (present in 50%)
- **Meningothelial-like whorls**
- Spread: **lymphatic** → cervical lymph nodes (but doesn't worsen prognosis much)

### Follicular Carcinoma
**Gross:** Solitary, encapsulated, well-circumscribed
**Microscopic:**
- Uniform follicles lined by atypical cells
- **Capsular invasion** and/or **vascular invasion** = KEY diagnostic criteria (distinguishes from adenoma)
- Cannot diagnose on FNAC — need histology to assess capsule/vascular invasion
- Spread: **hematogenous** → bone, lung (unlike papillary which is lymphatic)

### Medullary Carcinoma
**Gross:** Firm, white-gray; bilateral in MEN 2 (unilateral in sporadic)
**Microscopic:**
- Sheets/nests of polygonal to spindle cells
- **Amyloid deposits** in stroma (Congo red positive, apple-green birefringence)
- Amyloid derived from **calcitonin**
- **Calcitonin immunohistochemistry** positive
- C-cell hyperplasia = precursor lesion (especially in MEN 2)

### Anaplastic Carcinoma
**Gross:** Large, bulky, infiltrative mass; areas of necrosis, hemorrhage
**Microscopic:**
- Highly pleomorphic — spindle cells, giant cells, squamoid cells
- Frequent mitoses, necrosis
- May show areas of differentiated carcinoma (suggesting origin from pre-existing papillary/follicular)
- Rapidly invasive — extrathyroidal extension

## Clinical Features
- **Thyroid nodule** — painless, firm, fixed; solitary cold nodule on scan
- **Cervical lymphadenopathy** (papillary)
- **Hoarseness** (recurrent laryngeal nerve invasion)
- **Dysphagia, dyspnea** (local compression)
- **Medullary:** Diarrhea (calcitonin effect), flushing
- **Anaplastic:** Rapidly enlarging neck mass in elderly; stridor, dysphagia

## Diagnosis
- **FNAC** — first-line investigation for thyroid nodule
  - Diagnostic for papillary (nuclear features)
  - CANNOT distinguish follicular adenoma from carcinoma
- **Thyroid function tests:** Usually euthyroid
- **Calcitonin:** Elevated in medullary carcinoma
- **Thyroglobulin:** Tumor marker for follow-up of differentiated thyroid cancer
- **Ultrasound:** Solid, hypoechoic, microcalcifications → suspicious
- **Radioiodine scan:** Cold nodule = suspicious for malignancy
- **RET mutation analysis:** For medullary carcinoma / MEN screening

## Management (Brief)
- **Papillary/Follicular:** Total thyroidectomy ± lymph node dissection → radioactive iodine ablation → levothyroxine suppression
- **Medullary:** Total thyroidectomy + central LN dissection (no RAI — C cells don't take up iodine)
- **Anaplastic:** Palliative; surgery rarely curative; chemoradiation

## Complications
1. Recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy (surgical or tumor invasion)
2. Hypoparathyroidism (post-surgical)
3. Metastatic disease (bone, lung)
4. Tracheal compression/invasion
5. Hypothyroidism (post-treatment)
6. Transformation to anaplastic (from long-standing differentiated carcinoma)
