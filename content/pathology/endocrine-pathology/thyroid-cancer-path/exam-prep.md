# Thyroid Cancer — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common endocrine malignancy. Papillary (80%) = Orphan Annie nuclei + psammoma bodies + lymphatic spread. Follicular = capsular/vascular invasion + hematogenous spread. Medullary = C cells + amyloid + calcitonin + RET. Anaplastic = worst prognosis, elderly.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Orphan Annie eye nuclei** (ground glass) + **nuclear grooves** + **intranuclear inclusions** = papillary carcinoma
- ★ **Psammoma bodies** = concentric calcifications; seen in papillary thyroid, serous ovarian, meningioma, mesothelioma
- ★ **BRAF V600E** = most common mutation in papillary carcinoma
- ★ **Follicular carcinoma** CANNOT be diagnosed on FNAC (need capsular/vascular invasion on histology)
- ★ **Papillary = lymphatic spread; Follicular = hematogenous spread** (bone, lung)
- ★ **Amyloid in stroma** = medullary carcinoma (calcitonin-derived amyloid)
- ★ **RET proto-oncogene**: rearrangement in papillary; point mutation in medullary
- ★ **Cold nodule** on radioiodine scan = suspicious for malignancy
- ★ **Radiation exposure** = most important risk factor (papillary)
- ★ **Anaplastic** = may arise from pre-existing differentiated carcinoma; TP53 mutation
