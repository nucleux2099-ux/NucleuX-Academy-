# Graves Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Most common cause of hyperthyroidism. Autoimmune — TSI (IgG) binds TSH receptor → cAMP activation → diffuse thyroid hyperplasia. Triad: hyperthyroidism + exophthalmos + pretibial myxedema. Histology: tall columnar epithelium, papillary infoldings, scalloped colloid, lymphoid infiltrates.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **TSI (thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulin)** = Type II hypersensitivity (stimulatory)
- ★ **Scalloped colloid** + **tall columnar epithelium** + **pseudopapillae** = histologic hallmarks
- ★ **HLA-DR3** association
- ★ **Exophthalmos** = due to GAG deposition + lymphocytic infiltration in retroorbital tissue
- ★ **Pretibial myxedema** = unique to Graves (NOT other causes of hyperthyroidism)
- ★ **RAIU** = diffusely increased (vs hot nodule in toxic adenoma)
- ★ **TSH suppressed, T3/T4 elevated** = classic biochemistry
- ★ **Thyroid storm** = medical emergency; precipitated by surgery/infection/trauma
- ★ Lymphoid aggregates with germinal centers in thyroid stroma
- ★ **PTU** preferred in first trimester pregnancy (methimazole is teratogenic — aplasia cutis)

## Mnemonics
**Graves Triad: "GET"**
- **G**oiter (diffuse)
- **E**xophthalmos
- **T**oxicosis (hyperthyroidism)

**Hyperthyroidism symptoms: "SWEATING"**
- **S**weating
- **W**eight loss
- **E**motional lability
- **A**trial fibrillation
- **T**remor
- **I**ntolerance to heat
- **N**ervousness
- **G**I hypermotility (diarrhea)
