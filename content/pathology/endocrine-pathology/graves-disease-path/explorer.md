# Graves Disease — Pathology

## Overview
Graves disease is the most common cause of hyperthyroidism, accounting for 60-80% of cases. It is an autoimmune disorder characterized by thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulins (TSI) that activate TSH receptors, causing diffuse thyroid hyperplasia and hyperthyroidism. Classic triad: hyperthyroidism, ophthalmopathy, dermopathy.

## Key Points
- Most common cause of endogenous hyperthyroidism
- Autoimmune: IgG antibodies (TSI) mimic TSH → stimulate thyroid
- Female predominance (5-10:1); peak 20-40 years
- Diffuse, symmetric thyroid enlargement
- Unique features: exophthalmos, pretibial myxedema (not seen in other causes of hyperthyroidism)

## Definition & Classification
**Graves disease** = organ-specific autoimmune disease producing antibodies against TSH receptor, causing diffuse toxic goiter with hyperthyroidism.

### Antibody Types
| Antibody | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **TSI (thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulin)** | Binds TSH-R → stimulates → hyperthyroidism |
| **TGI (thyroid growth immunoglobulin)** | Stimulates thyroid growth → goiter |
| **TBII (TSH-binding inhibitory immunoglobulin)** | Blocks TSH binding |

## Etiology & Pathogenesis
### Autoimmune Basis
- **HLA-DR3** and **HLA-B8** association
- Loss of self-tolerance → CD4+ T-helper cells → B-cell activation → TSI production
- TSI binds TSH receptor → activates cAMP pathway → ↑thyroid hormone synthesis
- Positive feedback loop (but TSH is suppressed due to high T3/T4 negative feedback on pituitary)

### Ophthalmopathy
- Autoimmune inflammation of retroorbital tissues
- TSH-R expressed on orbital fibroblasts/adipocytes → targeted by autoantibodies
- → Glycosaminoglycan deposition + edema + T-cell infiltration → proptosis

### Dermopathy (Pretibial Myxedema)
- Accumulation of GAGs (glycosaminoglycans) in dermis
- Thickened, indurated skin over pretibial region

## Morphology

### Gross Appearance
- **Diffusely enlarged** thyroid (usually symmetric)
- Soft, meaty, beefy-red appearance
- ↑ Vascularity — may hear bruit/thrill on auscultation

### Microscopic Features
- **Diffuse hyperplasia:** Tall columnar follicular epithelium (normally cuboidal)
- **Papillary infoldings** projecting into follicular lumen (pseudopapillae — no fibrovascular core)
- **Scalloped colloid** — pale, watery colloid with scalloping at periphery (due to active resorption)
- **Lymphoid infiltration** — lymphoid aggregates/germinal centers in stroma
- **Decreased colloid** storage (hyperactive gland using up colloid)

### After Treatment with Iodine
- Involution of hyperplastic changes
- ↑ Colloid storage, flattening of epithelium

## Clinical Features
### Hyperthyroidism Symptoms
- Weight loss despite ↑ appetite, heat intolerance, sweating
- Tremor, anxiety, irritability, insomnia
- Tachycardia, palpitations, atrial fibrillation
- Diarrhea, ↑ bowel movements
- Warm, moist skin; fine hair
- Proximal myopathy

### Graves-Specific Features
- **Exophthalmos/proptosis** — bilateral in Graves (unilateral = think tumor)
- **Lid lag, lid retraction** (due to sympathetic stimulation of Müller's muscle)
- **Pretibial myxedema** — waxy, non-pitting edema over shins
- **Thyroid acropachy** — clubbing (rare)
- **Diffuse goiter** with bruit

## Diagnosis
- **TSH:** Suppressed (very low/undetectable)
- **Free T4, Free T3:** Elevated
- **TSI/TRAb:** Positive (confirmatory)
- **Radioactive iodine uptake (RAIU):** Diffusely increased (vs toxic nodule = hot nodule)
- **Thyroid scan:** Diffuse homogeneous uptake
- **Anti-TPO antibodies:** Often positive (non-specific)

## Management (Brief)
- **Anti-thyroid drugs:** Methimazole (first-line), PTU (first trimester pregnancy)
- **Beta-blockers:** Propranolol for symptomatic relief
- **Radioactive iodine (I-131):** Definitive treatment (commonly used in USA)
- **Surgery:** Subtotal/total thyroidectomy (for large goiters, compressive symptoms)
- **Ophthalmopathy:** Corticosteroids, orbital decompression if severe

## Complications
1. **Thyroid storm** — life-threatening hyperthyroid crisis (fever, tachycardia, delirium)
2. **Atrial fibrillation** → embolic stroke
3. **Osteoporosis** (↑ bone resorption)
4. **Heart failure** (high-output cardiac failure)
5. **Ophthalmopathy** — exposure keratitis, optic nerve compression
6. **Post-treatment hypothyroidism** (after RAI or surgery)
