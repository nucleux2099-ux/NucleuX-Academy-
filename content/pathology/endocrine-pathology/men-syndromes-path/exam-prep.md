# MEN Syndromes — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Autosomal dominant syndromes with multi-organ endocrine neoplasia. MEN 1 (Menin, 11q13) = 3 P's. MEN 2A/2B (RET, 10q11.2) = medullary thyroid carcinoma ± pheochromocytoma ± parathyroid/neuromas. Prophylactic thyroidectomy for RET mutation carriers.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **MEN 1 = 3 P's:** Parathyroid (95%) + Pancreas (40%) + Pituitary (30%)
- ★ **MEN 1 gene = Menin** (tumor suppressor, 11q13) — two-hit hypothesis
- ★ **MEN 2 gene = RET** (proto-oncogene, 10q11.2) — gain-of-function
- ★ **Gastrinoma** = most common pancreatic tumor in MEN 1
- ★ **Medullary thyroid carcinoma** = bilateral & multicentric in MEN 2 (100% penetrance)
- ★ **Amyloid** in MTC stroma = derived from calcitonin
- ★ **Always remove pheochromocytoma BEFORE thyroidectomy** (avoid hypertensive crisis)
- ★ **MEN 2B** = most aggressive; mucosal neuromas + Marfanoid habitus (NO lens subluxation)
- ★ **Prophylactic thyroidectomy** — within 1st year for MEN 2B; by age 5 for high-risk MEN 2A
- ★ **Calcitonin** = tumor marker for MTC; used for screening and follow-up

## Mnemonics
**MEN 1 = "3 P's":** Pituitary, Parathyroid, Pancreas
**MEN 2A = "MPC":** MTC, Pheo, Calcium (parathyroid)
**MEN 2B = "MPN":** MTC, Pheo, Neuromas (+ Marfanoid)

**Menin vs RET:**
- Me**N**in = MEN **1** (N=1 letter looks like 1)
- **RET**urn of the **2** = MEN **2**
