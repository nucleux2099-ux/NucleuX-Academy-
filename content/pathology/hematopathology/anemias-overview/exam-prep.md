# Anemias Overview — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
↓Hb below normal. Classify by MCV: microcytic (<80), normocytic (80-100), macrocytic (>100). Reticulocyte count: low = production defect, high = hemolysis/blood loss. Iron deficiency = most common worldwide. Peripheral smear is the single most informative test.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Iron deficiency:** ↓ferritin (most sensitive), ↓iron, ↑TIBC, ↓saturation; microcytic hypochromic
- ★ **Peripheral smear findings** table = EXTREMELY HIGH YIELD (see explorer.md)
- ★ **Hypersegmented neutrophils (≥5 lobes)** = megaloblastic anemia (B12/folate)
- ★ **Reticulocyte count** = first test to classify anemia mechanism
- ★ **↑LDH + ↓haptoglobin + ↑unconjugated bilirubin** = hemolysis triad
- ★ **Schistocytes** = microangiopathic hemolytic anemia (DIC, TTP, HUS)
- ★ **Rouleaux** = myeloma; **Teardrop** = myelofibrosis; **Bite cells** = G6PD
- ★ **Koilonychia** (spoon nails) + **Plummer-Vinson** (dysphagia + web) = iron deficiency
- ★ **Ring sideroblasts** = iron-laden mitochondria around nucleus (Prussian blue stain)
- ★ **RDW elevated** = iron deficiency; normal RDW = thalassemia trait (both microcytic)

## Mnemonics
**Microcytic anemias: "TAILS"**
- **T**halassemia, **A**nemia of chronic disease, **I**ron deficiency, **L**ead poisoning, **S**ideroblastic

**Hemolysis markers: "LUH"**
- **L**DH ↑, **U**nconjugated bilirubin ↑, **H**aptoglobin ↓

**Iron studies in IDA vs ACD:**
- IDA: Ferritin ↓, TIBC ↑
- ACD: Ferritin ↑ (or normal), TIBC ↓
