# Bleeding Disorders — Pathology

## Overview
Bleeding disorders result from defects in one or more components of hemostasis: blood vessels, platelets, or coagulation factors. They are broadly classified into platelet/vascular disorders (mucocutaneous bleeding) and coagulation factor disorders (deep tissue/joint bleeding).

## Key Points
- Primary hemostasis (platelet plug) vs secondary hemostasis (coagulation cascade)
- Platelet disorders → petechiae, purpura, mucosal bleeding; prolonged BT
- Coagulation disorders → hemarthrosis, deep hematomas; prolonged PT/aPTT
- vWD = most common inherited bleeding disorder overall
- Hemophilia A (Factor VIII) = most common severe inherited bleeding disorder

## Definition & Classification

### By Mechanism
| Type | Defect | Bleeding Pattern | Lab |
|------|--------|-----------------|-----|
| **Platelet/vascular** | ↓platelets or dysfunction | Petechiae, purpura, mucosal | ↑BT, normal PT/aPTT |
| **Coagulation** | Factor deficiency | Hemarthrosis, deep hematomas | ↑PT and/or ↑aPTT |
| **Mixed** | DIC, vWD | Both patterns | Variable |

### Key Bleeding Disorders
| Disorder | Defect | Inheritance |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| vWD | vWF deficiency/dysfunction | AD (most types) |
| Hemophilia A | Factor VIII deficiency | X-linked recessive |
| Hemophilia B | Factor IX deficiency | X-linked recessive |
| ITP | Autoimmune platelet destruction | Acquired |
| TTP | ADAMTS13 deficiency | Acquired/inherited |
| HUS | Shiga toxin | Acquired |
| DIC | Widespread activation | Acquired |

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### von Willebrand Disease (vWD)
- **Most common inherited bleeding disorder** (1% of population)
- vWF functions: platelet adhesion (GP Ib receptor) + carrier for Factor VIII
- Type 1 (80%): Quantitative ↓vWF (partial); mild; AD
- Type 2: Qualitative defect; subtypes (2A, 2B, 2M, 2N)
- Type 3: Complete absence; severe; AR

### DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation)
- Widespread activation of coagulation → microthrombi → consumption of platelets + factors → bleeding
- Triggers: sepsis, obstetric complications, malignancy (APL), trauma, snake bites
- "Consumption coagulopathy" — both thrombosis AND bleeding simultaneously

### ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura)
- Autoantibodies (IgG) against GPIIb/IIIa → splenic destruction of opsonized platelets
- Children: acute, post-viral, self-limiting
- Adults: chronic, insidious

## Morphology
### DIC
- **Microthrombi** in small vessels (kidney glomeruli, brain, lungs, adrenals)
- Schistocytes on peripheral smear (microangiopathic hemolytic anemia)
- Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome: bilateral adrenal hemorrhagic necrosis (meningococcemia + DIC)

### ITP
- **Bone marrow:** ↑Megakaryocytes (compensatory; marrow tries to make more platelets)
- **Spleen:** Normal size (NOT enlarged — important distinguishing feature)
- Peripheral blood: ↓platelets, otherwise normal

### TTP
- Pentad: thrombocytopenia, microangiopathic hemolytic anemia, neurological symptoms, renal dysfunction, fever
- Hyaline microthrombi (platelet-rich, fibrin-poor) in arterioles
- ADAMTS13 deficiency → uncleaved ultra-large vWF multimers → platelet aggregation

## Clinical Features
- **Platelet disorders:** Petechiae, purpura, epistaxis, menorrhagia, GI bleeding, easy bruising
- **Coagulation disorders:** Hemarthrosis (hemophilia), deep muscle hematomas, delayed bleeding after trauma
- **DIC:** Simultaneous bleeding AND thrombosis; organ failure
- **TTP:** Pentad (above) — medical emergency

## Diagnosis
| Test | What it Measures | Abnormal in |
|------|-----------------|-------------|
| **BT** | Platelet function | Platelet disorders, vWD |
| **PT** | Extrinsic (VII) + common | Warfarin, liver disease, DIC, VII deficiency |
| **aPTT** | Intrinsic (VIII, IX, XI, XII) + common | Hemophilia, heparin, vWD, DIC |
| **Platelet count** | Number | ITP, TTP, DIC |
| **D-dimer** | Fibrin degradation | DIC (elevated) |
| **Fibrinogen** | Clotting factor | DIC (decreased) |
| **Mixing study** | Corrects? | Factor deficiency (corrects) vs inhibitor (doesn't correct) |

## Management (Brief)
- **vWD:** Desmopressin (DDAVP) — releases stored vWF; vWF concentrates for severe
- **Hemophilia A:** Factor VIII replacement; desmopressin for mild
- **ITP:** Observation (children); steroids, IVIG, splenectomy, rituximab (adults)
- **TTP:** Plasma exchange (URGENT); do NOT give platelets (worsens)
- **DIC:** Treat underlying cause; supportive (FFP, platelets, cryoprecipitate)

## Complications
1. Life-threatening hemorrhage (any bleeding disorder)
2. Hemarthrosis → chronic arthropathy (hemophilia)
3. Organ failure (DIC — renal, adrenal, brain)
4. Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome (DIC + meningococcemia)
5. Inhibitor development (antibodies against replacement factors)
6. Transfusion-transmitted infections
