# Aplastic Anemia — Pathology

## Overview
Aplastic anemia is a syndrome of bone marrow failure characterized by pancytopenia (anemia + leukopenia + thrombocytopenia) with a hypocellular/fatty bone marrow. It results from damage to or suppression of hematopoietic stem cells.

## Key Points
- Pancytopenia + hypocellular (fatty) marrow = hallmark
- Most cases are idiopathic/autoimmune (T-cell mediated stem cell destruction)
- Drugs: chloramphenicol, benzene, alkylating agents, gold
- Fanconi anemia = most common inherited form (AR, DNA repair defect)
- Must distinguish from myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and aleukemic leukemia

## Definition & Classification
### By Cause
| Type | Examples |
|------|---------|
| **Idiopathic** (65%) | Autoimmune T-cell mediated |
| **Drugs/chemicals** | Chloramphenicol, benzene, alkylating agents, phenylbutazone |
| **Infections** | Hepatitis (non-A non-B non-C), parvovirus B19, EBV, HIV |
| **Inherited** | Fanconi anemia, dyskeratosis congenita |
| **Radiation** | Dose-dependent marrow suppression |
| **Others** | Pregnancy, PNH overlap |

### Severity
- **Severe:** Marrow cellularity <25%; ≥2 of: neutrophils <500, platelets <20,000, reticulocytes <1%
- **Non-severe:** Does not meet severe criteria

## Etiology & Pathogenesis
- **Autoimmune (most common mechanism):** Activated CD8+ cytotoxic T-cells → IFN-γ, TNF-α → apoptosis of HSCs
- **Direct stem cell damage:** Drugs, chemicals, radiation
- **Intrinsic stem cell defects:** Fanconi anemia (mutations in DNA repair genes — FANC pathway), telomere shortening

### Fanconi Anemia
- Autosomal recessive; defective DNA repair (interstrand crosslink repair)
- Presents in childhood (5-10 years) with pancytopenia
- Congenital anomalies: short stature, thumb/radial defects, café-au-lait spots, microcephaly
- ↑Risk of AML and squamous cell carcinomas
- Diagnosis: **Chromosomal breakage test** (DEB/MMC test)

## Morphology
### Bone Marrow — KEY FINDING
**Gross/Low power:** Markedly hypocellular; >75% fat (normally 50% cellular in adults)
**Micro:**
- Fat cells replace hematopoietic elements
- Residual lymphocytes, plasma cells, mast cells, stromal cells
- Absence of fibrosis (unlike myelofibrosis)
- No dysplastic changes (unlike MDS)
- No increase in blasts (unlike leukemia)

### Peripheral Blood
- **Pancytopenia:** ↓RBC (normocytic normochromic), ↓WBC (especially neutrophils), ↓platelets
- **↓Reticulocyte count** (marrow can't respond)
- No abnormal cells (no blasts, no dysplasia)

## Clinical Features
- **Anemia:** Fatigue, pallor, dyspnea
- **Thrombocytopenia:** Petechiae, purpura, mucosal bleeding, epistaxis
- **Neutropenia:** Recurrent infections (most common cause of death)
- **Insidious onset** in most; can be acute (post-hepatitis)
- **NO hepatosplenomegaly** (unlike leukemia) — important distinguishing feature
- Fanconi: Above + congenital anomalies

## Diagnosis
- **CBC:** Pancytopenia with low reticulocyte count
- **Peripheral smear:** Normocytic normochromic; no blasts or dysplastic cells
- **Bone marrow biopsy (essential):** Hypocellular (<25%), fatty replacement; no fibrosis, no blasts
- **Rule out:** MDS (dysplasia), PNH (flow cytometry for CD55/59), leukemia (blasts)
- **Fanconi:** DEB (diepoxybutane) chromosomal breakage test
- **Flow cytometry:** PNH screen (loss of GPI-anchored proteins)

## Management (Brief)
- **Young + severe + matched donor:** Allogeneic bone marrow transplant (curative)
- **No donor / older:** Immunosuppressive therapy (ATG + cyclosporine)
- **Supportive:** RBC/platelet transfusions, antibiotics/antifungals, growth factors (G-CSF)
- **Fanconi:** BMT (reduced intensity conditioning); genetic counseling
- **Eltrombopag:** TPO agonist — now used in severe aplastic anemia

## Complications
1. Severe infections (neutropenia — #1 cause of death)
2. Hemorrhage (thrombocytopenia)
3. Transfusion-related iron overload
4. Clonal evolution → MDS or AML (10-15%)
5. PNH overlap/development
6. Graft failure or GVHD (post-BMT)
