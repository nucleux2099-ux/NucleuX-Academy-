# Aplastic Anemia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Pancytopenia + hypocellular fatty marrow. Usually autoimmune (CD8+ T-cells destroy HSCs). Drugs: chloramphenicol, benzene. Fanconi = inherited (AR, DNA repair defect, congenital anomalies). Treatment: BMT (young) or ATG+cyclosporine (older).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Hypocellular marrow (<25%) with >75% fat** = diagnostic hallmark
- ★ **Chloramphenicol** = most classic drug cause (dose-independent idiosyncratic)
- ★ **Fanconi anemia** = AR, FANC gene, ↑chromosomal breakage (DEB test)
- ★ **NO hepatosplenomegaly** (unlike leukemia/lymphoma)
- ★ **Reticulocyte count LOW** (marrow failure — can't respond)
- ★ **Neutropenia** = most common cause of death (infections)
- ★ **PNH** can evolve from or overlap with aplastic anemia
- ★ **Post-hepatitis aplastic anemia** = seronegative hepatitis → aplasia (2-3 months later)
- ★ **10-15%** may evolve to MDS or AML (clonal evolution)
- ★ Fanconi: thumb/radial anomalies + café-au-lait + short stature + pancytopenia

## Mnemonics
**Causes: "ABC"**
- **A**utoimmune (idiopathic)
- **B**enzene, drugs (chloramphenicol)
- **C**ongenital (Fanconi)

**Fanconi features: "SHORT FANC"**
- **S**hort stature, **H**yperpigmentation, **O**ptic/ear anomalies, **R**adial/Thumb defects, **T**ranslocation risk (cancer), **FANC** gene
