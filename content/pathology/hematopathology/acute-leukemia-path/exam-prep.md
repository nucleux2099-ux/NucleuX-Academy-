# Acute Leukemia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
≥20% blasts in marrow. ALL = children (peak 2-5), TdT+, MPO−, PAS block+. AML = adults, MPO+, Auer rods. APL (M3) = t(15;17), DIC, ATRA cure. ALL has >90% cure in kids.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Auer rods** = pathognomonic for AML; crystallized MPO
- ★ **t(15;17) PML-RARA** = APL (M3); DIC at presentation; ATRA + arsenic trioxide = cure
- ★ **TdT** = positive in ALL (both B and T); NEGATIVE in AML and mature lymphocytes
- ★ **MPO** = positive in AML; negative in ALL (key differentiator)
- ★ **t(9;22) BCR-ABL (Philadelphia chromosome)** in ALL = POOR prognosis (vs good in CML)
- ★ **Down syndrome** → ↑risk ALL and AML-M7 (megakaryoblastic)
- ★ **"Faggot cells"** = bundles of Auer rods in APL
- ★ **Gum hypertrophy** = AML-M4, M5 (monocytic)
- ★ **T-ALL** = adolescent males, mediastinal (thymic) mass
- ★ **Tumor lysis syndrome** — prevent with allopurinol/rasburicase, hydration
- ★ **CD10 (CALLA)** positivity in B-ALL = good prognosis marker

## Mnemonics
**ALL vs AML staining:**
- ALL = **PAS** (block), **TdT** (+)
- AML = **MPO** (+), **Sudan Black B** (+), **Auer rods**

**AML-M3 (APL): "PML = Promyelocytic = t(15;17) = DIC = ATRA"**
