# Acute Leukemia — Pathology

## Overview
Acute leukemias are malignant clonal proliferations of immature hematopoietic cells (blasts) in the bone marrow and blood. Classified into Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL) and Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML). ALL is the most common childhood cancer; AML predominates in adults.

## Key Points
- ≥20% blasts in bone marrow = diagnostic criterion (WHO)
- ALL = most common cancer in children (peak 2-5 years); excellent prognosis with treatment
- AML = most common acute leukemia in adults; worse prognosis
- Auer rods = pathognomonic for AML (especially APL — M3)
- APL (M3) = t(15;17) PML-RARA; DIC; responds to ATRA (all-trans retinoic acid)

## Definition & Classification

### ALL (FAB: L1-L3; WHO preferred)
| Type | Features |
|------|---------|
| B-ALL (85%) | CD10 (CALLA)+, CD19+, CD20+, TdT+ |
| T-ALL (15%) | CD3+, CD7+, TdT+; thymic mass common; adolescent males |

### AML (FAB: M0-M7)
| FAB | Name | Key Features |
|-----|------|-------------|
| M0 | Minimally differentiated | MPO−; diagnosis by IHC/flow |
| M1 | Without maturation | MPO+ |
| M2 | With maturation | t(8;21); Auer rods; good prognosis |
| M3 | Acute Promyelocytic (APL) | t(15;17) PML-RARA; **DIC**; Auer rods; **ATRA** |
| M4 | Myelomonocytic | inv(16); eosinophilia in M4Eo |
| M5 | Monocytic | Gum infiltration, skin involvement |
| M6 | Erythroleukemia | PAS+ erythroblasts |
| M7 | Megakaryoblastic | Down syndrome association; CD41/CD61+ |

## Etiology & Pathogenesis
- **Genetic:** Down syndrome (↑ALL and AML-M7), Fanconi anemia, Bloom syndrome
- **Radiation, benzene, alkylating agents** (therapy-related AML)
- **Clonal expansion** of blasts with blocked differentiation
- Key translocations: t(12;21) TEL-AML1 (B-ALL, good prognosis), t(9;22) BCR-ABL (Ph+ ALL, poor), t(15;17) (APL), t(8;21) (AML-M2)

## Morphology
### Bone Marrow
- **Hypercellular** marrow packed with blasts (≥20%)
- Normal hematopoiesis suppressed → pancytopenia

### ALL Blasts
- Small to medium, high N:C ratio, scant cytoplasm, inconspicuous nucleoli
- **PAS positive** (block positivity)
- **TdT positive** (terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase)
- **MPO negative** (distinguishes from AML)

### AML Blasts
- Large cells, abundant cytoplasm, prominent nucleoli
- **Auer rods** — crystallized MPO; pink, needle-shaped inclusions; pathognomonic
- **"Faggot cells"** in APL — cells with bundles of Auer rods
- **MPO positive** (myeloperoxidase)
- Sudan Black B positive

## Clinical Features
- **Bone marrow failure:** Anemia (fatigue, pallor), neutropenia (infections), thrombocytopenia (bleeding, petechiae, purpura)
- **Organ infiltration:** Hepatosplenomegaly, lymphadenopathy
- **Bone pain/tenderness** (marrow expansion — especially children with ALL)
- **CNS involvement** (ALL > AML): headache, cranial nerve palsies
- **Gum hypertrophy** (AML-M4, M5)
- **Mediastinal mass** (T-ALL — thymic)
- **DIC** (APL — M3)
- **Testicular infiltration** (ALL — sanctuary site)

## Diagnosis
- **CBC:** Pancytopenia with circulating blasts; ↑WBC (leukemic) or normal/low (aleukemic)
- **Bone marrow biopsy:** ≥20% blasts
- **Flow cytometry:** Immunophenotyping (CD markers)
- **Cytogenetics/FISH:** Translocations
- **MPO stain:** Positive = AML; Negative = ALL
- **TdT:** Positive = ALL (both B and T)
- **Sudan Black B:** Positive = AML
- **PAS:** Block positivity = ALL

## Management (Brief)
- **ALL:** Induction (vincristine, prednisone, L-asparaginase, daunorubicin) → consolidation → maintenance → CNS prophylaxis; >90% cure in children
- **AML:** Induction (7+3: cytarabine + daunorubicin) → consolidation; BMT for high-risk
- **APL:** ATRA + arsenic trioxide (revolutionary cure; >90% cure rate)

## Complications
1. Tumor lysis syndrome (↑K⁺, ↑PO₄, ↑uric acid, ↓Ca²⁺)
2. DIC (especially APL)
3. Infections (neutropenia)
4. Bleeding (thrombocytopenia)
5. CNS relapse (ALL)
6. Treatment-related secondary malignancies
