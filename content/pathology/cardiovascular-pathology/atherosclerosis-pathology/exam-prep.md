# Atherosclerosis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Chronic inflammatory disease of large/medium arteries. Endothelial injury → LDL accumulation → oxidized LDL → foam cells → fibrofatty plaque. Complications: rupture, thrombosis, MI, stroke. Leading cause of death globally.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Response to injury hypothesis** — endothelial dysfunction is the initiating event
- ★ **Fatty streak** = earliest visible lesion (even in children); reversible
- ★ **Foam cells** = macrophages engorged with oxidized LDL via scavenger receptors
- ★ **Vulnerable plaque** = thin cap, large lipid core, heavy inflammation → rupture risk
- ★ **Cholesterol clefts** (needle-shaped spaces) on histology = pathognomonic
- ★ **LDL is atherogenic; HDL is protective** — most important modifiable risk factor
- ★ **Complicated plaque** = ulceration + thrombosis + hemorrhage + calcification
- ★ Most common site: **abdominal aorta** > coronary > popliteal > carotid
- ★ **Shoulder region** of plaque = site of maximum inflammation & rupture
- ★ Calcification = **dystrophic** (in damaged tissue with normal calcium levels)
- ★ **Monckeberg medial calcific sclerosis** — do NOT confuse with atherosclerosis (media vs intima)
