# Myocardial Infarction — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Ischemic necrosis of myocardium due to coronary artery occlusion (>90% from atherosclerotic plaque rupture + thrombosis). LAD territory most common. Sequential morphological changes from wavy fibers (minutes) → coagulative necrosis → granulation tissue → fibrotic scar (weeks). Troponin = most specific marker.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **LAD** supplies anterior wall/septum/apex — most commonly affected (40-50%)
- ★ **Subendocardium** is most vulnerable (wavefront phenomenon) — watershed zone
- ★ **Wavy fibers** = earliest histologic change; **contraction bands** = reperfusion injury
- ★ **3-7 days = WEAKEST** → highest risk of myocardial rupture → tamponade
- ★ **Coagulative necrosis** is the type (except brain = liquefactive)
- ★ **Troponin I/T** = most specific marker; CK-MB useful for re-infarction detection
- ★ **VF (ventricular fibrillation)** = most common cause of death in first 1-2 hours
- ★ **Dressler syndrome** = autoimmune pericarditis, 2-10 weeks post-MI
- ★ **Cardiogenic shock** = >40% LV mass infarcted
- ★ **Neutrophils peak** at 1-3 days; **macrophages** begin cleanup by day 3-7
- ★ **TTC (triphenyltetrazolium chloride)** stain — viable tissue stains brick red; infarcted area remains pale

## Mnemonics
**Timeline of MI changes: "0-1-3-7-14"**
- 0-4h: Nothing gross; wavy fibers
- 1 day: Yellow tan + neutrophils
- 3-7 days: SOFT (rupture risk) + macrophages
- 7-14 days: Granulation tissue (red-gray)
- >2 weeks: Scar (white, firm)

**Complications by time — "ARMD"**
- **A**rrhythmias (hours)
- **R**upture (3-7 days)
- **M**ural thrombus (weeks)
- **D**ressler syndrome (2-10 weeks)
