# Pericardial Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Pericardial diseases include pericarditis (serous, fibrinous, purulent, hemorrhagic, constrictive), effusion, and tamponade. "Bread and butter" = fibrinous pericarditis. TB = most common cause of constrictive pericarditis. Tamponade = Beck's triad. Rate of fluid accumulation matters more than volume.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **"Bread and butter" pericarditis** = fibrinous type; seen in uremia, Dressler, rheumatic fever
- ★ **Beck's triad** = hypotension + ↑JVP + muffled heart sounds (tamponade)
- ★ **Pulsus paradoxus** = >10 mmHg fall in SBP during inspiration
- ★ **TB** = most common cause of constrictive pericarditis in India
- ★ **Rate** of accumulation determines tamponade, not volume (slow = 2L tolerated; rapid = 200 mL → tamponade)
- ★ **Dressler syndrome** = autoimmune pericarditis 2-10 weeks post-MI
- ★ **Kussmaul sign** = paradoxical ↑JVP on inspiration (constrictive pericarditis)
- ★ **Electrical alternans** on ECG = swinging heart in large effusion
- ★ **Pericardial knock** = early diastolic sound in constrictive pericarditis
- ★ **Armor heart** = heavily calcified constrictive pericarditis

## Mnemonics
**Causes of Pericarditis — "CARDIAC RIND"**
- **C**ollagen vascular disease (SLE)
- **A**ortic dissection
- **R**adiation
- **D**rugs (procainamide, hydralazine)
- **I**nfections (viral, TB, bacterial)
- **A**cute MI / Dressler
- **C**ancer
- **R**enal failure (uremia)
- **I**diopathic
- **N**eighboring inflammation
- **D**ressler syndrome

**Beck's Triad: "3 D's"**
- **D**istended neck veins
- **D**istant heart sounds
- **D**ecreased BP
