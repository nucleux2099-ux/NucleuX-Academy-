# Lung Cancer — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Leading cause of cancer death. Smoking = 85% of cases. Adenocarcinoma (most common overall, peripheral), squamous (central, smokers, keratin pearls, PTHrP), small cell (central, neuroendocrine, ACTH/ADH, worst prognosis), large cell (exclusion diagnosis). EGFR/ALK mutations = targetable in adenocarcinoma.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Adenocarcinoma** = most common type overall and in non-smokers; peripheral location
- ★ **Squamous cell** = central, keratin pearls, cavitation, PTHrP → hypercalcemia
- ★ **Small cell** = central, neuroendocrine, ACTH (Cushing), ADH (SIADH), Eaton-Lambert; Rb + p53 mutations
- ★ **EGFR mutation** = non-smoker, Asian, female, adenocarcinoma — responds to tyrosine kinase inhibitors
- ★ **Pancoast tumor** = apex → Horner syndrome (miosis, ptosis, anhidrosis, enophthalmos)
- ★ **SVC syndrome** = face/arm edema, dilated veins — most commonly from lung cancer
- ★ **Psammoma bodies** = NOT in lung cancer (common wrong answer)
- ★ **Asbestos + smoking** = multiplicative risk (not just additive)
- ★ **TTF-1, Napsin A** = adenocarcinoma markers; **p40, CK5/6** = squamous markers
- ★ **Sputum cytology** most useful for central squamous cell carcinoma
- ★ **Small cell** = chemo-sensitive but NOT surgical (widespread at diagnosis)

## Mnemonics
**Central tumors: "SS" — Squamous, Small cell**
**Peripheral tumors: "AL" — Adenocarcinoma, Large cell**

**Paraneoplastic syndromes: "CASH"**
- **C**ushing (ACTH) — Small cell
- **A**DH (SIADH) — Small cell
- **S**quamous — hyperCalcemia (PTHrP)
- **H**ypertrophic osteoarthropathy — Adenocarcinoma

**Small cell = "Everything Small is Bad"** — worst prognosis, no surgery, most paraneoplastic
