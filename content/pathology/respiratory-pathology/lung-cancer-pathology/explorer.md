# Lung Cancer — Pathology

## Overview
Lung cancer (bronchogenic carcinoma) is the leading cause of cancer-related deaths worldwide in both men and women. Smoking is the single most important risk factor, responsible for ~85% of cases. Classified into non-small cell lung carcinoma (NSCLC, 80%) and small cell lung carcinoma (SCLC, 20%).

## Key Points
- Leading cause of cancer mortality worldwide
- Smoking = #1 risk factor; dose-dependent (pack-years)
- NSCLC (80%): Adenocarcinoma, Squamous cell, Large cell
- SCLC (20%): Neuroendocrine origin, most aggressive, paraneoplastic syndromes
- Adenocarcinoma = most common type overall (and in non-smokers)
- Squamous cell = most common in smokers (central location)

## Definition & Classification

### WHO Classification
| Type | % | Location | Key Features |
|------|---|----------|-------------|
| **Adenocarcinoma** | 40% | Peripheral | Most common overall; non-smokers; glandular/mucin |
| **Squamous cell** | 25-30% | Central | Smokers; keratin pearls; cavitation |
| **Small cell (oat cell)** | 15-20% | Central | Neuroendocrine; most aggressive; paraneoplastic |
| **Large cell** | 10% | Peripheral | Undifferentiated; diagnosis of exclusion |

### Precursor Lesions
- Squamous: Squamous metaplasia → dysplasia → carcinoma in situ → invasive
- Adenocarcinoma: Atypical adenomatous hyperplasia → adenocarcinoma in situ → minimally invasive → invasive

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Risk Factors
- **Smoking** (85%): 10-30× risk; squamous and small cell most strongly linked
- **Asbestos** — synergistic with smoking (multiplicative risk)
- **Radon, arsenic, chromium, nickel**
- **Air pollution**
- **Pulmonary fibrosis/scarring** — "scar carcinoma" (adenocarcinoma)
- **Genetic:** EGFR, KRAS, ALK, p53, Rb

### Molecular Pathways
| Mutation | Type |
|----------|------|
| **KRAS** | Adenocarcinoma (smokers) |
| **EGFR** | Adenocarcinoma (non-smokers, Asian women) — targetable |
| **ALK rearrangement** | Adenocarcinoma (young non-smokers) — targetable |
| **p53** | All types |
| **Rb, p53** | Small cell (nearly 100%) |
| **p16/CDKN2A** | Squamous cell |

## Morphology

### Squamous Cell Carcinoma
**Gross:** Central, hilar mass; endobronchial growth; cavitation common
**Micro:** Keratinization, **keratin pearls**, intercellular bridges; ranges from well to poorly differentiated

### Adenocarcinoma
**Gross:** Peripheral, subpleural nodule; may have central scar
**Micro:** Glandular/acinar/papillary/lepidic patterns; **mucin positive**; TTF-1 and Napsin A positive
- **Lepidic pattern** (BAC-like): Growth along alveolar walls without invasion

### Small Cell Carcinoma
**Gross:** Central, perihilar; bulky mediastinal lymphadenopathy; widespread at diagnosis
**Micro:** Small round blue cells; scant cytoplasm; "crush artifact"; nuclear molding; high mitotic rate; necrosis
- **Neuroendocrine markers:** Chromogranin, synaptophysin, CD56, NSE
- Azzopardi effect: DNA encrustation of blood vessel walls

### Large Cell Carcinoma
**Gross:** Large, peripheral, necrotic mass
**Micro:** Large anaplastic cells; no squamous or glandular differentiation; diagnosis of exclusion

## Clinical Features
- **Cough** (most common symptom), hemoptysis, dyspnea, chest pain
- **Weight loss, anorexia, fatigue**
- **Recurrent pneumonia** (obstructive, in central tumors)
- **Hoarseness** (recurrent laryngeal nerve invasion)
- **SVC syndrome** (SVC obstruction — facial/upper limb edema, distended veins)
- **Pancoast tumor** (apex) → Horner syndrome (miosis, ptosis, anhidrosis) + brachial plexus invasion

### Paraneoplastic Syndromes — HIGH YIELD
| Syndrome | Substance | Tumor Type |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Cushing syndrome** | ACTH | Small cell |
| **SIADH** | ADH | Small cell |
| **Hypercalcemia** | PTHrP | Squamous cell |
| **Eaton-Lambert** | Anti-Ca²⁺ channel Ab | Small cell |
| **Hypertrophic osteoarthropathy** | — | Adenocarcinoma |
| **Carcinoid syndrome** | Serotonin | Carcinoid |

## Diagnosis
- **CXR:** Mass, hilar enlargement, pleural effusion
- **CT chest:** Characterize lesion, staging
- **Tissue diagnosis:** Bronchoscopy + biopsy (central); CT-guided biopsy (peripheral); FNAC
- **Sputum cytology:** Especially for central squamous cell
- **PET-CT:** Staging, metastasis detection
- **IHC:** TTF-1, Napsin A (adeno); p40, CK5/6 (squamous); chromogranin, synaptophysin (small cell)
- **Molecular testing:** EGFR, ALK, ROS1, PD-L1

## Management (Brief)
- **NSCLC (early):** Surgical resection (lobectomy) ± adjuvant chemo
- **NSCLC (advanced):** Chemo + immunotherapy (PD-L1 inhibitors); targeted therapy (EGFR/ALK+)
- **SCLC:** Chemotherapy + radiation (limited stage); chemo ± immunotherapy (extensive); NO surgery (widespread at diagnosis)

## Complications
1. Metastasis: Brain, bone, liver, adrenals (most common distant sites)
2. SVC syndrome
3. Pancoast syndrome / Horner syndrome
4. Paraneoplastic syndromes
5. Pleural effusion (malignant)
6. Recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy
7. Pericardial involvement → tamponade
