# Lung Tumors — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Beyond bronchogenic carcinoma: carcinoid (neuroendocrine, NOT smoking-related, central, vascular), hamartoma (most common benign, popcorn calcification), mesothelioma (asbestos, pleural encasement, calretinin+). Lung is most common site for metastases (cannonball lesions).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Hamartoma** = most common benign lung tumor; "popcorn calcification" on CT
- ★ **Bronchial carcinoid** = NOT related to smoking; Kulchitsky cell origin; "salt and pepper" chromatin
- ★ **Mesothelioma markers:** Calretinin+, CK5/6+, WT-1+ vs Adenocarcinoma: CEA+, TTF-1+
- ★ **Asbestos** → mesothelioma (amphibole > serpentine); latency 20-40 years
- ★ **Cannonball metastases** = multiple bilateral well-defined lung nodules from distant primary
- ★ **Typical carcinoid** = <2 mitoses/10 HPF, no necrosis; **Atypical** = 2-10, focal necrosis
- ★ **Carcinoid syndrome** rare with bronchial carcinoid (<5%); common only with hepatic metastases
- ★ **Asbestos bodies** = golden-brown, dumbbell-shaped, iron-coated fibers (Prussian blue+)
- ★ **Mesothelioma** ≠ bronchogenic carcinoma; asbestos + smoking → synergistic for lung CANCER, not mesothelioma
- ★ **Solitary fibrous tumor** of pleura = benign; CD34 positive

## Mnemonics
**Mesothelioma vs Adenocarcinoma IHC:**
- **"CaW"** = Calretinin, WT-1 → Mesothelioma
- **"CT"** = CEA, TTF-1 → Adenocarcinoma

**Asbestos complications: "5 P's"**
- **P**leural plaques (most common)
- **P**leural effusion
- **P**ulmonary fibrosis (asbestosis)
- **P**rimary lung carcinoma
- **P**leural mesothelioma
