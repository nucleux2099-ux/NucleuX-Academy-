# Acute Pancreatitis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Autodigestion of pancreas by activated enzymes. Gallstones (#1) and alcohol (#2) = 80% of causes. Trypsin is the key activator. Fat necrosis with saponification (chalky white) = hallmark. Lipase > amylase for diagnosis. Grey Turner's and Cullen's signs = hemorrhagic pancreatitis.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Trypsin** = master enzyme that activates all others
- ★ **Fat necrosis + saponification** (Ca²⁺ soaps) = pathognomonic gross finding
- ★ **Lipase** = more specific than amylase for diagnosis
- ★ **Grey Turner's sign** = flank bruising; **Cullen's sign** = periumbilical bruising
- ★ **Pseudocyst** = NO epithelial lining (unlike true cyst); encapsulated necrotic debris
- ★ **Hypocalcemia** = calcium consumed in saponification (important complication)
- ★ **Elastase** destroys vessel walls → hemorrhage
- ★ **Phospholipase A2** → destroys surfactant → ARDS
- ★ **Ranson's criteria ≥3** = severe pancreatitis
- ★ **Gallstone pancreatitis** → ERCP + later cholecystectomy
- ★ **Avoid morphine** (causes sphincter of Oddi spasm)

## Mnemonics
**Causes: "I GET SMASHED"**
- Idiopathic, Gallstones, Ethanol, Trauma, Steroids, Mumps, Autoimmune, Scorpion/Spider, Hyperlipidemia/Hypercalcemia, ERCP, Drugs

**Signs: "CG = Cullen-Grey"**
- **C**ullen = **C**entral (periumbilical)
- **G**rey Turner = **G**oing to flanks
