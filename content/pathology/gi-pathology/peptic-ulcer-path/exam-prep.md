# Peptic Ulcer Disease — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Mucosal ulceration from imbalance between acid/pepsin and mucosal defenses. H. pylori (#1) and NSAIDs (#2). DU = ↑acid, anterior wall (perforation), posterior wall (bleeding). GU = lesser curvature, ↓defense. 4 histological layers. Complications: PHOB.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **H. pylori** urease test = CLO test; CagA and VacA = virulence factors
- ★ **4 microscopic layers:** Necrotic debris → neutrophils → granulation tissue → fibrosis
- ★ **Anterior DU** → perforation; **Posterior DU** → hemorrhage (gastroduodenal artery)
- ★ **Curling's** = burns (duodenal); **Cushing's** = CNS (gastric, ↑vagal → ↑acid)
- ★ **NSAIDs** → ↓PGE2 → ↓mucus, ↓bicarbonate, ↓blood flow
- ★ **Benign vs malignant ulcer:** Clean/punched-out vs raised/irregular/rolled margins
- ★ **DU almost NEVER becomes malignant**; GU has 1-3% risk
- ★ **Zollinger-Ellison** = gastrinoma → multiple refractory ulcers; check serum gastrin
- ★ **Hemorrhage** = most common complication of PUD
- ★ **Perforation** = most common indication for emergency surgery

## Mnemonics
**Complications: "PHOB"**
- **P**erforation
- **H**emorrhage (most common)
- **O**bstruction (pyloric stenosis)
- **B**ecoming malignant (GU only)

**Curling vs Cushing:**
- Cur**L**ing = burns (L = Lower = duodenum)
- Cush**ING** = brain (ING = Increased Neural = ↑vagal)

**DU vs GU pain:**
- DU = "Decreases with food" (hunger pain)
- GU = "Gets worse with food"
