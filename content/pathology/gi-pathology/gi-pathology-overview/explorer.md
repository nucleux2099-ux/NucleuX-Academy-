# GI Pathology — Overview

## Overview
Gastrointestinal pathology encompasses diseases of the esophagus, stomach, small intestine, large intestine, and appendix. This overview covers the most important conditions across the GI tract with emphasis on inflammatory and neoplastic conditions.

## Key Points
- Barrett's esophagus = intestinal metaplasia of lower esophagus (risk for adenocarcinoma)
- Celiac disease = gluten sensitivity → villous atrophy + crypt hyperplasia
- IBD: Crohn's (transmural, skip lesions, granulomas) vs UC (mucosal, continuous, from rectum)
- Colorectal carcinoma = adenoma-carcinoma sequence (APC → KRAS → p53)
- Appendicitis = most common surgical emergency

## Definition & Classification

### By Organ
| Organ | Key Conditions |
|-------|---------------|
| Esophagus | GERD, Barrett's, squamous cell carcinoma, adenocarcinoma |
| Stomach | Gastritis, peptic ulcer, gastric carcinoma, GIST, MALT lymphoma |
| Small intestine | Celiac disease, Crohn's, carcinoid |
| Large intestine | UC, Crohn's, adenomatous polyps, colorectal carcinoma |
| Appendix | Acute appendicitis, carcinoid tumor |

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Barrett's Esophagus
- Chronic GERD → squamous → **intestinal metaplasia** (goblet cells)
- Pre-malignant: risk of esophageal adenocarcinoma (0.5% per year)
- Sequence: GERD → metaplasia → dysplasia → adenocarcinoma

### Celiac Disease
- Immune reaction to **gliadin** (gluten component) in genetically susceptible individuals
- HLA-DQ2 (90%) / HLA-DQ8
- Anti-tTG (tissue transglutaminase), anti-endomysial antibodies
- Villous atrophy + crypt hyperplasia + intraepithelial lymphocytes

### Inflammatory Bowel Disease

| Feature | Crohn's Disease | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---------|----------------|-------------------|
| Location | Mouth to anus (ileum most common) | Rectum → proximal (colon only) |
| Distribution | Skip lesions | Continuous |
| Depth | Transmural | Mucosa/submucosa only |
| Granulomas | Non-caseating (60%) | Absent |
| Fistulae | Common | Rare |
| Gross | Cobblestone mucosa, creeping fat | Pseudopolyps, lead-pipe colon |
| Cancer risk | Slight increase | Significant increase |
| Smoking | Worsens | Protective |

### Colorectal Carcinoma
- **Adenoma-carcinoma sequence** (70-80%): APC → KRAS → SMAD4 → p53
- **Microsatellite instability (MSI) pathway** (15%): DNA mismatch repair defects (MLH1, MSH2)
- Lynch syndrome (HNPCC) = hereditary MSI pathway
- FAP (Familial Adenomatous Polyposis) = APC gene mutation → thousands of polyps

## Morphology

### Barrett's Esophagus
- **Gross:** Red, velvety mucosa replacing white squamous mucosa in lower esophagus
- **Micro:** Intestinal metaplasia with **goblet cells** (diagnostic); may show dysplasia

### Celiac Disease
- **Micro:** Villous atrophy (flat mucosa), crypt hyperplasia, ↑ intraepithelial lymphocytes (>30/100 enterocytes), Marsh classification

### Crohn's Disease
- **Gross:** Skip lesions, cobblestone mucosa, strictures, fistulae, creeping fat, string sign
- **Micro:** Transmural inflammation, **non-caseating granulomas**, lymphoid aggregates, fissure ulcers

### Ulcerative Colitis
- **Gross:** Continuous involvement from rectum; pseudopolyps; lead-pipe colon (chronic)
- **Micro:** Mucosal/submucosal inflammation only; **crypt abscesses** (neutrophils in crypts); crypt distortion; goblet cell depletion

### Colorectal Carcinoma
- **Left side:** Annular, "napkin ring" constriction; obstruction; altered bowel habits
- **Right side:** Polypoid, fungating mass; iron deficiency anemia; occult blood
- **Micro:** Adenocarcinoma — glandular differentiation, desmoplastic stroma; Duke's/TNM staging

## Clinical Features
- **GERD/Barrett's:** Heartburn, dysphagia, endoscopic surveillance needed
- **Celiac:** Malabsorption, steatorrhea, dermatitis herpetiformis, iron deficiency
- **IBD:** Chronic diarrhea ± blood, abdominal pain, weight loss; extraintestinal manifestations
- **CRC:** Change in bowel habits, rectal bleeding, iron deficiency anemia (right-sided), obstruction (left-sided)

## Diagnosis
- **Endoscopy + biopsy** — gold standard for most GI conditions
- **Celiac:** Anti-tTG IgA, anti-endomysial Ab; duodenal biopsy
- **IBD:** Colonoscopy + biopsy; p-ANCA (UC), ASCA (Crohn's)
- **CRC screening:** Colonoscopy, fecal occult blood test, CT colonography
- **Barrett's:** Endoscopy with Seattle protocol biopsies

## Management (Brief)
- **Barrett's:** PPI + surveillance; ablation/resection for high-grade dysplasia
- **Celiac:** Lifelong gluten-free diet
- **IBD:** 5-ASA (UC), steroids, immunomodulators, biologics (anti-TNF)
- **CRC:** Surgical resection + adjuvant chemotherapy (stage-dependent)

## Complications
1. Barrett's → esophageal adenocarcinoma
2. Celiac → T-cell lymphoma (enteropathy-associated), osteoporosis
3. Crohn's → fistulae, strictures, abscess, B12 deficiency (ileal disease)
4. UC → toxic megacolon, colorectal carcinoma
5. CRC → obstruction, perforation, metastasis (liver most common)
