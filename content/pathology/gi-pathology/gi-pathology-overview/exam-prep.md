# GI Pathology Overview — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Key GI conditions: Barrett's (goblet cells, adenocarcinoma risk), Celiac (villous atrophy, anti-tTG, HLA-DQ2), Crohn's vs UC (transmural/skip/granulomas vs mucosal/continuous/crypt abscesses), CRC (adenoma-carcinoma sequence: APC→KRAS→p53).

## High Yield Points
- ★ **Barrett's esophagus** = goblet cells (intestinal metaplasia) = pre-malignant for adenocarcinoma
- ★ **Crohn's vs UC** table = MOST ASKED comparison in pathology
- ★ **Non-caseating granulomas** = Crohn's (not always present, ~60%)
- ★ **Crypt abscesses** = UC (neutrophils in crypts); also Crohn's but less characteristic
- ★ **APC → KRAS → SMAD4 → p53** = adenoma-carcinoma sequence
- ★ **Left-sided CRC** = obstruction, napkin ring; **Right-sided** = anemia, occult blood
- ★ **Celiac:** Villous atrophy + crypt hyperplasia + ↑IELs; HLA-DQ2 (90%)
- ★ **FAP** = APC mutation → >100 polyps → 100% cancer risk if untreated
- ★ **Lynch syndrome (HNPCC)** = MSI pathway; MLH1, MSH2 mutations
- ★ **Dermatitis herpetiformis** = skin manifestation of celiac disease
- ★ **p-ANCA** = UC; **ASCA** = Crohn's
