# Liver Pathology — Overview

## Overview
The liver is subject to a wide range of pathological processes including cirrhosis, hepatocellular carcinoma, metabolic liver diseases (Wilson disease, hemochromatosis, α1-antitrypsin deficiency), and biliary diseases. This overview covers cirrhosis and key liver conditions beyond hepatitis and ALD.

## Key Points
- Cirrhosis = irreversible; diffuse fibrosis + regenerative nodules = end-stage of many liver diseases
- HCC = most common primary liver malignancy; AFP is tumor marker
- Hepatic encephalopathy = ammonia + other toxins → cerebral edema
- Wilson disease = copper overload (ceruloplasmin ↓); Kayser-Fleischer rings
- Hemochromatosis = iron overload; "bronze diabetes"

## Definition & Classification

### Cirrhosis — Classification by Etiology
| Cause | Key Features |
|-------|-------------|
| Alcoholic | Micronodular initially |
| Post-hepatitic (HBV/HCV) | Macronodular |
| Biliary (PBC, PSC) | Greenish liver |
| Metabolic (Wilson, hemochromatosis) | Specific deposits |
| Cryptogenic | No identifiable cause |

### Primary Liver Tumors
- **Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC):** Most common primary malignancy
- **Cholangiocarcinoma:** From bile duct epithelium
- **Hepatic adenoma:** Benign; OCP-associated
- **Hemangioma:** Most common benign liver tumor

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Cirrhosis
- Common pathway: chronic liver injury → stellate cell activation → collagen deposition → regenerative nodules
- Hepatic stellate cells (Ito cells) transform into myofibroblasts → produce collagen I and III
- Loss of fenestrations in sinusoidal endothelium → "capillarization of sinusoids"
- Portal hypertension: ↑ resistance to portal flow → portosystemic shunts

### HCC
- Arises in cirrhotic liver in 80-90% cases
- Risk: HBV, HCV, alcohol, aflatoxin B1, hemochromatosis
- **Aflatoxin B1** (Aspergillus flavus) → p53 mutation (codon 249, Arg→Ser)
- Fibrolamellar variant: young patients, no cirrhosis, better prognosis

### Wilson Disease
- AR; ATP7B gene (chromosome 13); copper transport defect
- ↓ Ceruloplasmin, ↑ free copper → deposits in liver, brain (basal ganglia), cornea
- Liver: fatty change → chronic hepatitis → cirrhosis

### Hemochromatosis
- Primary (hereditary): HFE gene (C282Y mutation); AR
- ↑ Iron absorption → hemosiderin deposits → organ damage
- Triad: cirrhosis + diabetes + skin pigmentation ("bronze diabetes")

## Morphology

### Cirrhosis
**Gross:** Shrunken, hard, nodular liver; yellow-tan (if fatty) or brown-green (if biliary)
**Micro:** Regenerative nodules enclosed by fibrous septa; loss of normal lobular architecture; bile ductular proliferation at margins

### HCC
**Gross:** Single large mass, multifocal, or diffusely infiltrative; greenish if bile-producing
**Micro:** Trabecular pattern (most common); tumor cells resemble hepatocytes; bile production; Mallory bodies possible
**IHC:** HepPar1+, AFP+, glypican-3+

### Wilson Disease
**Liver:** Fatty change, Mallory bodies, copper granules (rhodanine/rubeanic acid stain)
**Brain:** Cavitary necrosis in basal ganglia (lenticular degeneration)
**Eye:** Kayser-Fleischer rings (copper in Descemet's membrane)

### Hemochromatosis
**Liver:** Golden-brown hemosiderin granules; Prussian blue stain positive; cirrhosis
**Pancreas:** Hemosiderin → "bronze diabetes"
**Heart:** Hemosiderin → restrictive cardiomyopathy
**Skin:** Melanin + hemosiderin → bronze color

## Clinical Features
### Cirrhosis
- **Portal hypertension:** Esophageal varices, caput medusae, splenomegaly, ascites, hemorrhoids
- **Hepatocellular failure:** Jaundice, coagulopathy, hypoalbuminemia
- **Hepatic encephalopathy:** Asterixis, confusion, coma
- **Hepatorenal syndrome:** Renal failure in advanced cirrhosis
- Spider angiomas, palmar erythema, gynecomastia, testicular atrophy

### HCC
- Rapidly enlarging liver, RUQ pain, weight loss; ↑AFP; Budd-Chiari (hepatic vein thrombosis)

### Wilson Disease
- Liver disease (any form), neuropsychiatric symptoms, Kayser-Fleischer rings; age 5-40

### Hemochromatosis
- "Bronze diabetes" + cirrhosis + cardiomyopathy; arthropathy; hypogonadism; middle-aged males

## Diagnosis
- **Cirrhosis:** LFTs, albumin, PT; biopsy; FibroScan; imaging
- **HCC:** AFP (>400 ng/mL suggestive); CT/MRI (arterial enhancement + washout); biopsy
- **Wilson:** ↓Ceruloplasmin, ↑24hr urinary copper, Kayser-Fleischer ring (slit lamp), liver biopsy (copper quantification)
- **Hemochromatosis:** ↑Serum ferritin, ↑transferrin saturation (>45%), HFE gene testing, liver biopsy (Prussian blue)

## Management (Brief)
- **Cirrhosis:** Treat underlying cause; manage complications; liver transplant for end-stage
- **HCC:** Resection, liver transplant (Milan criteria), sorafenib, TACE
- **Wilson:** D-penicillamine (copper chelation), zinc (blocks absorption), liver transplant
- **Hemochromatosis:** Phlebotomy (first-line), deferoxamine (chelation)

## Complications
1. Cirrhosis → HCC (especially HBV, HCV, hemochromatosis)
2. Variceal hemorrhage (life-threatening)
3. Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis
4. Hepatorenal syndrome
5. Hepatopulmonary syndrome
6. Wilson → fulminant hepatic failure, hemolytic anemia (copper release)
