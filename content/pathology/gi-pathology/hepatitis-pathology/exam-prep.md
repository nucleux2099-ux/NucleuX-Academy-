# Hepatitis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
5 hepatitis viruses: HAV/HEV (fecal-oral, no chronicity), HBV (DNA, immune-mediated damage, ground glass hepatocytes), HCV (RNA, 80% chronic, lymphoid follicles), HDV (needs HBV). Councilman bodies = apoptotic hepatocytes. HBV serology and window period = must-know.

## High Yield Points
- ★ **HBV = only DNA virus** among hepatitis viruses
- ★ **HBV serology window period:** HBsAg−, Anti-HBs−, only Anti-HBc IgM+
- ★ **Ground glass hepatocytes** = HBsAg accumulation in SER (orcein stain+)
- ★ **Councilman bodies** = apoptotic hepatocytes (eosinophilic, shrunken)
- ★ **HCV → 80% chronicity** (high mutation rate escapes immunity)
- ★ **HBV liver damage is immune-mediated** (CD8+ CTLs), NOT direct cytopathic
- ★ **HEV in pregnancy** = 20% mortality in 3rd trimester (fulminant hepatitis)
- ★ **Interface hepatitis** (piecemeal necrosis) = hallmark of chronic hepatitis
- ★ **HBV can cause HCC even WITHOUT cirrhosis** (DNA integration)
- ★ **HCV** → mixed cryoglobulinemia, MPGN (extrahepatic)
- ★ **Neonatal HBV** → 90% become chronic carriers (immature immune system)

## Mnemonics
**Fecal-oral: "Vowels = Fecal"** → HAV, HEV
**Parenteral: "Consonants = Blood"** → HBV, HCV, HDV

**HBV Serology: "The Surface tells the Story"**
- HBsAg = infection present
- Anti-HBs = immune/recovered
- HBeAg = replicating (e = extra infectious)

**Chronicity rates: "HCV Chronically Vexes" (80%); HBV in adults = 5-10%; HAV/HEV = 0%**
