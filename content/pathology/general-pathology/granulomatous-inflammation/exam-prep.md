# Granulomatous Inflammation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Granuloma = aggregate of epithelioid macrophages ± giant cells ± necrosis. Caseating (TB, fungi) vs non-caseating (sarcoidosis, Crohn). Formed by Th1/IFN-γ–mediated macrophage activation. Langhans giant cells (horseshoe nuclei) in TB. Anti-TNF therapy → TB reactivation.

## High Yield Points

★ Epithelioid cells = activated macrophages with epithelial-like appearance
★ Langhans giant cells: Nuclei in horseshoe/peripheral pattern (TB) — NOT Langerhans cells
★ Foreign body giant cells: Nuclei scattered haphazardly
★ Caseating granuloma = TB until proven otherwise
★ Sarcoidosis: Non-caseating granulomas + Schaumann bodies + asteroid bodies
★ IFN-γ = key cytokine for macrophage activation and granuloma formation
★ Anti-TNF therapy can reactivate latent TB (TNF essential for granuloma maintenance)
★ Cat-scratch disease: Stellate (star-shaped) necrotizing granulomas
★ Crohn disease granulomas: Non-caseating, found in any layer of bowel wall
★ Schaumann bodies and asteroid bodies are seen in sarcoidosis but NOT pathognomonic

## Mnemonics

- **Causes of granulomatous inflammation:** "TB-SCFL" — TB, Sarcoidosis, Crohn, Fungal, Leprosy (+ Foreign body, Wegener, Cat-scratch)
- **Caseating vs Non-caseating:** "TB CASEates; Sarcoid does NOT"
- **Giant cell types:** "Langhans = horseshoe (L = Lined up); Foreign body = scattered (F = Free)"
- **Sarcoidosis features:** "GRUELING" — Granulomas, Raised ACE, Uveitis, Erythema nodosum, Lymphadenopathy (bilateral hilar), Interstitial fibrosis, Non-caseating, Gammaglobulinemia (hypergammaglobulinemia)
