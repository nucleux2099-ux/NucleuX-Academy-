# Neoplasia — Overview

## Overview

Neoplasia ("new growth") is a fundamental disorder of cell growth regulation in which a clonal population of cells proliferates autonomously, exceeding and uncoordinated with normal tissues. Neoplasms (tumors) are classified as benign or malignant based on their behavior. Cancer (malignant neoplasm) is among the leading causes of death worldwide.

## Key Points

- **Neoplasia = autonomous, uncoordinated new growth** that persists after stimulus removal
- **Benign vs Malignant:** Differentiation, rate of growth, invasion, metastasis
- **Carcinoma:** Malignant epithelial tumor; **Sarcoma:** Malignant mesenchymal tumor
- **Cancer hallmarks (Hanahan-Weinberg):** 8 hallmarks of cancer
- **Metastasis = defining feature of malignancy**

## Definition & Classification

### Benign vs Malignant

| Feature | Benign | Malignant |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Differentiation | Well-differentiated | Variable (well to undifferentiated) |
| Growth rate | Slow | Fast (variable) |
| Capsule | Usually encapsulated | Usually unencapsulated |
| Borders | Well-defined | Irregular, infiltrative |
| Invasion | No (expansile growth) | Yes (local invasion) |
| Metastasis | No | Yes (defining feature) |
| Necrosis/hemorrhage | Rare | Common |
| Mitoses | Few, normal | Many, abnormal |

### Nomenclature
- **Benign epithelial:** -oma (adenoma, papilloma, polyp)
- **Benign mesenchymal:** -oma (fibroma, lipoma, chondroma, osteoma)
- **Malignant epithelial:** Carcinoma (adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma)
- **Malignant mesenchymal:** Sarcoma (fibrosarcoma, liposarcoma, osteosarcoma)
- **Exceptions to rules:** Lymphoma, melanoma, mesothelioma, seminoma = malignant despite "-oma"
- **Mixed tumors:** Pleomorphic adenoma (salivary), Wilms tumor (nephroblastoma)
- **Teratoma:** From totipotent cells; can be benign or malignant

### Hamartoma vs Choristoma
- Hamartoma: Disorganized mass of tissue native to the site (e.g., pulmonary hamartoma)
- Choristoma: Normal tissue in an abnormal location (e.g., pancreatic tissue in stomach wall)

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Hallmarks of Cancer (Hanahan-Weinberg)
1. **Self-sufficiency in growth signals** (oncogenes — RAS, MYC, HER2)
2. **Insensitivity to growth-inhibitory signals** (loss of tumor suppressors — RB, p53)
3. **Evasion of apoptosis** (BCL2 overexpression, p53 loss)
4. **Limitless replicative potential** (telomerase activation)
5. **Sustained angiogenesis** (VEGF)
6. **Invasion and metastasis** (loss of E-cadherin, MMPs)
7. **Reprogramming of energy metabolism** (Warburg effect — aerobic glycolysis)
8. **Evasion of immune destruction** (PD-L1, CTLA-4 pathways)

### Enabling Characteristics
- Genomic instability and mutation
- Tumor-promoting inflammation

### Carcinogenesis — Multi-Step Process
- Initiation → Promotion → Progression
- Accumulation of driver mutations in proto-oncogenes and tumor suppressor genes
- Clonal expansion of mutated cells

## Morphology

### Gross Characteristics
- **Benign:** Well-circumscribed, encapsulated, smooth, homogeneous cut surface
- **Malignant:** Irregular, infiltrative margins, necrosis, hemorrhage, firm/hard (desmoplasia)
- **Fungating:** Exophytic growth (colon, stomach)
- **Ulcerative:** Crater-like (gastric cancer, oral cancer)
- **Infiltrative/scirrhous:** Hard, contracted (linitis plastica of stomach)

### Microscopic Characteristics
- **Differentiation:** How closely tumor resembles tissue of origin
  - Well-differentiated → Low grade
  - Poorly differentiated/undifferentiated (anaplastic) → High grade
- **Anaplasia features:** Pleomorphism, hyperchromasia, high N:C ratio, abnormal mitoses, tumor giant cells, loss of polarity
- **Dysplasia:** Pre-malignant disordered growth
- **Carcinoma in situ:** Full-thickness dysplasia WITHOUT basement membrane invasion
- **Desmoplasia:** Dense fibrous stroma around tumor (scirrhous)

## Clinical Features

- **Local effects:** Mass/swelling, obstruction, compression, ulceration, bleeding
- **Hormonal:** Functioning tumors (insulinoma → hypoglycemia, pheochromocytoma → hypertension)
- **Paraneoplastic syndromes:** Remote effects not due to invasion/metastasis
- **Cachexia:** Progressive wasting (TNF-α, IL-1, IL-6, PIF)
- **Fever, night sweats** (lymphoma)
- **Pain:** Late feature in most cancers

## Diagnosis

- **Biopsy and histopathology:** Gold standard
- **Cytology:** FNAC, Pap smear, fluid cytology
- **IHC:** Tumor markers (cytokeratin for carcinoma, vimentin for sarcoma, CD markers for lymphoma)
- **Molecular:** FISH, PCR, NGS for mutations
- **Tumor markers:** PSA (prostate), AFP (HCC, yolk sac), CEA (colon), CA-125 (ovarian), β-hCG (choriocarcinoma)
- **Imaging:** CT, MRI, PET-CT for staging

## Management

- **Surgery:** Primary modality for solid tumors
- **Chemotherapy:** Systemic treatment
- **Radiation:** Local control
- **Targeted therapy:** Imatinib (BCR-ABL), trastuzumab (HER2)
- **Immunotherapy:** Checkpoint inhibitors (anti-PD-1, anti-CTLA-4)
- **Hormonal therapy:** Tamoxifen (breast), GnRH agonists (prostate)
- **Staging (TNM)** guides treatment decisions

## Complications

- Metastasis (most important)
- Local invasion and destruction
- Obstruction (bowel, airway, urinary)
- Paraneoplastic syndromes
- Cachexia and malnutrition
- Treatment-related complications (chemo toxicity, radiation injury)
