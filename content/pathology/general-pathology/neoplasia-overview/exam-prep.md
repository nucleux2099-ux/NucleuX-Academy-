# Neoplasia Overview — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Neoplasia = autonomous new growth. Benign (encapsulated, no metastasis) vs Malignant (invasive, metastatic). Carcinoma = epithelial; Sarcoma = mesenchymal. 8 hallmarks of cancer (Hanahan-Weinberg). Anaplasia = undifferentiated. CIS = full-thickness dysplasia without BM invasion. Metastasis = defining feature of malignancy.

## High Yield Points

★ Metastasis = single most reliable feature distinguishing malignant from benign
★ Exceptions: Lymphoma, melanoma, mesothelioma, seminoma = malignant despite "-oma" suffix
★ Hallmarks of cancer: Self-sufficient growth, evade inhibitors, resist apoptosis, telomerase, angiogenesis, invasion, Warburg effect, immune evasion
★ Carcinoma in situ: Dysplastic cells occupy full thickness but DON'T invade basement membrane
★ Anaplasia features: Pleomorphism, abnormal mitoses, hyperchromasia, high N:C ratio, giant cells
★ Hamartoma = native tissue, disorganized; Choristoma = normal tissue, wrong location
★ Warburg effect = aerobic glycolysis (cancer cells use glycolysis even with O₂)
★ Grading = differentiation (histology); Staging = extent of spread (TNM) — staging is more important prognostically
★ Most common route of metastasis: Lymphatic (carcinomas); Hematogenous (sarcomas)
★ Desmoplasia = dense fibrous stroma induced by tumor (scirrhous carcinoma)

## Mnemonics

- **Benign vs Malignant:** "DRIM" — Differentiation, Rate of growth, Invasion, Metastasis
- **Hallmarks of cancer:** "SEVEN + 1: Self-growth, Evade inhibitors, Vascularize, Evade apoptosis, Nonlimited replication, Invade/metastasize, Energy reprogram, No immune kill"
- **Malignant "-oma" exceptions:** "LyMMS" — Lymphoma, Melanoma, Mesothelioma, Seminoma
- **Tumor markers:** "AFP-HCC, CEA-Colon, CA125-Ovary, PSA-Prostate, hCG-Chorio"
