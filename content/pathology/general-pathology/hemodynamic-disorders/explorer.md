# Hemodynamic Disorders

## Overview

Hemodynamic disorders encompass disturbances in normal blood flow and fluid balance, including hyperemia, congestion, edema, hemorrhage, thrombosis, embolism, infarction, and shock. These conditions are interrelated and represent a spectrum from mild circulatory disturbances to life-threatening emergencies. Normal hemodynamics depends on cardiac output, vascular integrity, blood volume, and coagulation balance.

## Key Points

- **Virchow triad (thrombosis):** Endothelial injury, stasis/turbulence, hypercoagulability
- **Thrombosis → embolism → infarction** is a key pathological sequence
- **Edema:** Increased hydrostatic pressure, decreased oncotic pressure, lymphatic obstruction, increased permeability
- **Hemorrhage:** Can be external or internal (petechiae, purpura, ecchymoses, hematoma)
- **Shock:** Final common pathway of many hemodynamic catastrophes

## Definition & Classification

### Components of Hemodynamic Disorders
1. **Hyperemia & Congestion** — increased blood in tissues
2. **Edema** — excess fluid in interstitium
3. **Hemorrhage** — escape of blood from vessels
4. **Thrombosis** — pathologic clot in vasculature
5. **Embolism** — intravascular mass carried to distant site
6. **Infarction** — tissue necrosis due to ischemia
7. **Shock** — systemic hypoperfusion

### Hemorrhage Classification
- **By size:** Petechiae (1-2 mm), Purpura (3-5 mm), Ecchymoses (>1-2 cm)
- **Large collections:** Hematoma (tissue), Hemothorax (pleural), Hemopericardium (pericardial), Hemoperitoneum (peritoneal), Hemarthrosis (joint)
- **By etiology:** Traumatic, coagulopathy, vascular defect, platelet disorder

### Thrombosis — Virchow Triad
1. **Endothelial injury** — most important; atherosclerosis, trauma, inflammation
2. **Stasis or turbulence** — aneurysms, atrial fibrillation, immobilization
3. **Hypercoagulability** — Factor V Leiden, antiphospholipid syndrome, malignancy, OCP use

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Normal Hemostasis Sequence
1. Vasoconstriction (endothelin)
2. Primary hemostasis: Platelet adhesion (vWF/GpIb) → activation → aggregation (fibrinogen/GpIIb-IIIa)
3. Secondary hemostasis: Coagulation cascade → fibrin mesh
4. Fibrinolysis: Plasmin dissolves clot (tPA → plasminogen → plasmin)

### Thrombosis Pathogenesis
- Endothelial injury exposes subendothelial collagen → platelet adhesion and tissue factor release
- Stasis allows coagulation factors to accumulate and prevents dilution
- Hypercoagulable states: Primary (genetic — Factor V Leiden, prothrombin mutation, AT-III/Protein C/S deficiency) or Secondary (acquired — malignancy, pregnancy, OCP, heparin-induced thrombocytopenia, nephrotic syndrome)

### DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation)
- Widespread activation of coagulation → microthrombi in small vessels
- Consumption of clotting factors and platelets → paradoxical bleeding
- Causes: Sepsis, obstetric complications, malignancy, massive trauma
- Lab: ↑ PT/aPTT, ↓ platelets, ↓ fibrinogen, ↑ D-dimer, schistocytes on smear

## Morphology

### Thrombosis
- **Arterial thrombi:** Gray-white, adherent, occur at sites of endothelial injury
- **Venous thrombi:** Red (stasis), propagate in direction of blood flow
- **Lines of Zahn:** Alternating pale (platelets/fibrin) and dark (RBC) layers — indicates antemortem thrombus
- **Postmortem clot:** No lines of Zahn, rubbery, not attached ("chicken fat" yellow layer over "currant jelly" red)

### Congestion
- **Active hyperemia:** Arteriolar dilation → red, warm tissue (inflammation, exercise)
- **Passive congestion:** Impaired venous outflow → blue-red, swollen
  - Chronic pulmonary congestion: Brown induration, hemosiderin-laden macrophages (heart failure cells)
  - Chronic hepatic congestion: Nutmeg liver (alternating red congested and yellow fatty areas), centrilobular necrosis

## Clinical Features

- **Hemorrhage:** Depends on volume and rate — <20% usually tolerated; >40% → hemorrhagic shock
- **Thrombosis:** DVT (leg swelling, Homan sign), coronary thrombosis (MI), cerebral thrombosis (stroke)
- **DIC:** Simultaneous bleeding and thrombosis, petechiae, organ failure
- **Congestion:** Hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, pulmonary crackles

## Diagnosis

- **Coagulation studies:** PT, aPTT, fibrinogen, D-dimer, platelet count
- **Imaging:** Doppler ultrasound (DVT), CT angiography (PE), echocardiography
- **Blood smear:** Schistocytes (DIC/TTP), platelet estimate
- **Thrombophilia screen:** Factor V Leiden, protein C/S, AT-III, antiphospholipid antibodies
- **Histopathology:** Lines of Zahn, nutmeg liver, heart failure cells

## Management

- **Anticoagulation:** Heparin, warfarin, DOACs for thrombosis/PE
- **Antiplatelet:** Aspirin, clopidogrel for arterial thrombosis
- **Thrombolysis:** tPA for acute MI, PE, stroke
- **DIC:** Treat underlying cause; supportive (FFP, platelets, cryoprecipitate)
- **Hemorrhage:** Volume resuscitation, blood products, surgical control
- **Congestion:** Treat underlying heart/liver disease, diuretics

## Complications

- Thrombosis → embolism → infarction
- Hemorrhagic shock → multi-organ failure
- DIC → organ failure and death
- Chronic congestion → fibrosis (cardiac cirrhosis)
- Post-thrombotic syndrome (chronic venous insufficiency)
