# Hemodynamic Disorders — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Hemodynamic disorders include hyperemia, congestion, edema, hemorrhage, thrombosis, embolism, infarction, and shock. Virchow triad governs thrombosis. Lines of Zahn distinguish antemortem thrombus from postmortem clot. DIC = simultaneous thrombosis + bleeding. Nutmeg liver = chronic hepatic congestion.

## High Yield Points

★ Virchow triad: Endothelial injury (most important), stasis, hypercoagulability
★ Lines of Zahn = alternating pale/dark layers = antemortem thrombus
★ Postmortem clot: No Lines of Zahn, "chicken fat" + "currant jelly," not attached
★ Nutmeg liver = chronic hepatic congestion (centrilobular necrosis + periportal fatty change)
★ Heart failure cells = hemosiderin-laden macrophages in lung (chronic pulmonary congestion)
★ DIC: ↑ PT/aPTT, ↓ fibrinogen, ↓ platelets, ↑ D-dimer, schistocytes
★ Petechiae (<3mm) = platelet/vascular disorder; Ecchymoses (>1cm) = coagulation disorder
★ Factor V Leiden = most common inherited thrombophilia (resistance to activated protein C)
★ Arterial thrombi = white, at sites of injury; Venous thrombi = red, in areas of stasis
★ Primary hemostasis: Platelet adhesion via vWF-GpIb; Aggregation via fibrinogen-GpIIb/IIIa

## Mnemonics

- **Virchow triad:** "SET" — Stasis, Endothelial injury, Thrombophilia
- **Hemorrhage sizes:** "PPE" — Petechiae (1-2mm), Purpura (3-5mm), Ecchymoses (>1cm)
- **DIC causes:** "STOP Making Thrombi" — Sepsis, Trauma, Obstetric, Pancreatitis, Malignancy, Transfusion
- **Hypercoagulable states:** "PLAN" — Protein C/S deficiency, Leiden (Factor V), AT-III deficiency, Nephrotic
