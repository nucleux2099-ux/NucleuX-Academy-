# Genetic Disorders — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Three categories: Mendelian (single-gene), chromosomal, multifactorial. AD = structural proteins (Marfan, NF1, FH); AR = enzyme deficiencies (CF, storage diseases); XR = males affected (hemophilia, DMD). Down (trisomy 21) = most common chromosomal disorder. Turner (45,X) = primary amenorrhea. Klinefelter (47,XXY) = male hypogonadism.

## High Yield Points

★ CF: CFTR mutation (ΔF508 most common), sweat chloride >60, thick secretions, Pseudomonas infections
★ Marfan: FBN1, lens subluxation UPWARD (vs DOWN in homocystinuria), aortic dissection
★ Down syndrome: 95% trisomy 21 (nondisjunction), ↑ maternal age; ALL risk, duodenal atresia, ASD/VSD
★ Gaucher = most common lysosomal storage disease; crumpled tissue paper macrophages
★ Tay-Sachs: Cherry red spot on macula, hexosaminidase A deficiency, Ashkenazi Jews
★ NF1: Café-au-lait spots ≥6, Lisch nodules, neurofibromin (chromosome 17)
★ Familial hypercholesterolemia: LDL receptor defect, tendon xanthomas, premature atherosclerosis
★ Turner: Webbed neck, coarctation, streak gonads, 45X
★ Klinefelter: 47XXY, tall, gynecomastia, small testes, Barr body positive
★ Trinucleotide repeat: Fragile X (CGG), Huntington (CAG), Myotonic dystrophy (CTG)

## Mnemonics

- **AD disorders:** "MANHATTAN" — Marfan, Achondroplasia, NF, Huntington, Adult PCKD, Tuberous sclerosis, Hereditary spherocytosis, AIP, NF
- **AR disorders:** "CF-GAT-SHiP" — CF, Gaucher, Albinism, Tay-Sachs, Sickle cell, Hemochromatosis, PKU
- **X-linked recessive:** "Be Wise, Fool's GOLD Heals DMD" — Bruton, Wiskott-Aldrich, Fabry, G6PD, Ocular albinism, Lesch-Nyhan, Duchenne, Hunter
- **Trinucleotide repeats:** "X-FraGile CGG, Huntington CAGs, Myotonic CTGs, Friedreich GAAs"
