# Edema

## Overview

Edema is the accumulation of excess fluid in the interstitial tissue spaces or body cavities. It can be localized or generalized (anasarca). Understanding Starling forces and the distinction between transudate and exudate is fundamental. Edema is a clinical manifestation of underlying diseases affecting the heart, kidneys, liver, or vasculature.

## Key Points

- **Transudate:** Low protein (<3 g/dL), low specific gravity (<1.012) — hemodynamic cause
- **Exudate:** High protein (>3 g/dL), high specific gravity (>1.020) — inflammatory cause
- **Most common cause of generalized edema:** Congestive heart failure
- **Dependent edema:** Pedal (ambulatory), sacral (bedridden)
- **Anasarca:** Severe generalized edema

## Definition & Classification

### Starling Forces (Normal Fluid Balance)
- **Forces favoring filtration:** Capillary hydrostatic pressure, interstitial oncotic pressure
- **Forces opposing filtration:** Plasma oncotic pressure, interstitial hydrostatic pressure
- Edema occurs when net filtration exceeds lymphatic drainage

### Classification by Mechanism
1. **Increased hydrostatic pressure:** CHF, DVT, portal hypertension
2. **Decreased plasma oncotic pressure:** Nephrotic syndrome, liver cirrhosis, malnutrition (kwashiorkor)
3. **Increased vascular permeability:** Inflammation, burns, allergic reactions
4. **Lymphatic obstruction:** Filariasis (elephantiasis), post-surgical, tumor infiltration
5. **Sodium/water retention:** Renal failure, excess aldosterone

### Classification by Location
- **Localized:** DVT (unilateral leg edema), lymphedema, angioedema
- **Generalized:** CHF, nephrotic syndrome, cirrhosis
- **Body cavities:** Ascites (peritoneal), pleural effusion, pericardial effusion, hydrocele
- **Pulmonary edema:** Cardiogenic (LVF) or non-cardiogenic (ARDS)
- **Cerebral edema:** Vasogenic, cytotoxic, interstitial

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Increased Hydrostatic Pressure
- CHF → ↑ venous pressure → right-sided → peripheral edema; left-sided → pulmonary edema
- Portal hypertension (cirrhosis) → ascites
- DVT → localized edema of affected limb

### Decreased Oncotic Pressure
- Nephrotic syndrome: Loss of albumin in urine → hypoalbuminemia
- Cirrhosis: ↓ Albumin synthesis by liver
- Malnutrition: Inadequate protein intake (kwashiorkor)
- Protein-losing enteropathy

### Increased Vascular Permeability
- Inflammation: Histamine, bradykinin → endothelial gaps
- Burns: Direct endothelial injury
- Anaphylaxis/angioedema: Mast cell degranulation

### Lymphatic Obstruction
- Filariasis (Wuchereria bancrofti) → elephantiasis
- Post-mastectomy (lymph node removal) → arm lymphedema
- Tumor infiltration of lymph nodes

### Sodium/Water Retention
- Reduced renal perfusion (CHF, cirrhosis) → RAAS activation → Na⁺/H₂O retention
- Primary renal disease (acute GN, renal failure)

## Morphology

### Gross Appearance
- **Subcutaneous:** Pitting edema (finger impression remains), dependent distribution
- **Pulmonary:** Heavy, wet lungs (2-3x normal weight); fluid in alveoli
- **Cerebral:** Swollen, flattened gyri, narrowed sulci; risk of herniation
- **Ascites:** Distended abdomen, fluid wave, shifting dullness

### Microscopic Appearance
- **Subcutaneous:** Separation of collagen fibers by pale eosinophilic fluid; no inflammatory cells in transudate
- **Pulmonary:** Alveoli filled with pale pink fluid (transudate) or proteinaceous fluid with hyaline membranes (ARDS); hemosiderin-laden macrophages in chronic congestion ("heart failure cells")
- **Cerebral:** Perivascular and pericellular clear spaces; neuronal ischemic changes if severe

## Clinical Features

- **Peripheral edema:** Swollen ankles/legs, sacral edema in bedridden, pitting on pressure
- **Pulmonary edema:** Dyspnea, orthopnea, PND, pink frothy sputum, bilateral crackles
- **Cerebral edema:** Headache, vomiting, papilledema, altered consciousness, herniation
- **Ascites:** Abdominal distension, fluid thrill, shifting dullness
- **Pleural effusion:** Dyspnea, decreased breath sounds, stony dull percussion
- **Periorbital edema:** Characteristic of nephrotic syndrome (loose tissue)

## Diagnosis

- **Clinical examination:** Pitting test, distribution pattern
- **Lab:** Serum albumin, renal function, liver function, urinalysis (proteinuria)
- **Imaging:** CXR (pulmonary edema, effusion), echo (CHF), USG abdomen (ascites)
- **Fluid analysis:** Transudate vs exudate (Light's criteria for pleural fluid), SAAG for ascites
- **BNP/NT-proBNP:** Elevated in cardiac edema

## Management

- **Treat underlying cause** (CHF, nephrotic syndrome, cirrhosis)
- **Diuretics:** Loop diuretics (furosemide), spironolactone (ascites)
- **Salt and fluid restriction**
- **Albumin infusion** (if severe hypoalbuminemia)
- **Elevation of affected limb** (peripheral edema)
- **Compression stockings** (lymphedema)
- **Paracentesis/thoracentesis** for symptomatic collections

## Complications

- Pulmonary edema → respiratory failure
- Cerebral edema → brain herniation → death
- Skin breakdown and cellulitis in chronic peripheral edema
- Impaired wound healing
- Spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (in cirrhotic ascites)
- Nutritional impairment from gut edema
