# Edema — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Edema = excess interstitial fluid. Caused by ↑ hydrostatic pressure (CHF), ↓ oncotic pressure (nephrotic/cirrhosis), ↑ permeability (inflammation), lymphatic obstruction, or Na⁺/H₂O retention. Transudate = low protein, hemodynamic; Exudate = high protein, inflammatory. Anasarca = generalized edema.

## High Yield Points

★ Transudate: Sp. gravity <1.012, protein <3 g/dL; Exudate: Sp. gravity >1.020, protein >3 g/dL
★ Most common cause of generalized edema = CHF
★ Periorbital edema = nephrotic syndrome (loose periorbital tissue)
★ Dependent edema: Pedal (ambulatory), Sacral (bedridden)
★ Heart failure cells = hemosiderin-laden macrophages in lungs (chronic pulmonary congestion)
★ Elephantiasis = lymphatic obstruction by Wuchereria bancrofti
★ SAAG ≥1.1 = portal hypertension (transudative ascites); SAAG <1.1 = exudative
★ Cerebral edema types: Vasogenic (BBB disruption), Cytotoxic (cell swelling), Interstitial (↑ CSF)
★ Kwashiorkor → hypoalbuminemia → generalized edema
★ Starling forces govern fluid movement across capillaries

## Mnemonics

- **Causes of edema:** "HOPLNS" — Hydrostatic ↑, Oncotic ↓, Permeability ↑, Lymphatic obstruction, Na⁺ retention, Secondary
- **Transudate vs Exudate:** "Transudate = Thin (low protein); Exudate = Extra protein"
- **Heart failure cells:** "HF Cells = Hemosiderin-Filled Cells in lungs"
