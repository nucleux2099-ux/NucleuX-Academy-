# Amyloidosis

## Overview

Amyloidosis is a group of diseases characterized by extracellular deposition of abnormal fibrillar proteins (amyloid) in various tissues and organs. These misfolded proteins adopt a β-pleated sheet configuration, are resistant to proteolysis, and progressively impair organ function. It can be systemic or localized, and primary or secondary.

## Key Points

- **Defining feature:** Extracellular deposition of β-pleated sheet fibrils
- **Staining:** Congo red → apple-green birefringence under polarized light
- **Major types:** AL (primary), AA (secondary), ATTR (transthyretin-related)
- **Most commonly affected organs:** Kidney, heart, liver, spleen, nerves
- **Prognosis:** Depends on type and organ involvement; cardiac amyloid carries worst prognosis

## Definition & Classification

### Definition
Amyloidosis = extracellular deposition of insoluble fibrillar proteins with β-pleated sheet structure that stain with Congo red and show apple-green birefringence.

### Classification

| Type | Precursor Protein | Associated Condition |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| AL (Primary) | Immunoglobulin light chains | Multiple myeloma, B-cell dyscrasias |
| AA (Secondary/Reactive) | Serum amyloid A (SAA) | Chronic inflammation (RA, TB, FMF, bronchiectasis) |
| ATTR | Transthyretin | Familial amyloid polyneuropathy; senile cardiac amyloidosis |
| Aβ2M | β2-microglobulin | Long-term dialysis |
| Aβ | Amyloid precursor protein (APP) | Alzheimer disease (cerebral) |
| AIAPP | Islet amyloid polypeptide | Type 2 diabetes (localized to islets) |
| Localized | Various | Medullary carcinoma thyroid (calcitonin-derived) |

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Mechanism of Amyloid Formation
1. Overproduction of amyloidogenic proteins (AL — excess light chains)
2. Increased production of acute phase protein (AA — chronic inflammation ↑ SAA)
3. Mutations causing misfolding (ATTR — transthyretin mutations)
4. Failure of clearance mechanisms

### Pathogenetic Sequence
- Precursor protein → misfolding → β-pleated sheet aggregation → fibril deposition
- Serum amyloid P (SAP) component stabilizes fibrils and inhibits proteolysis
- Progressive accumulation → pressure atrophy → organ dysfunction

### AL Amyloidosis
- Monoclonal plasma cell proliferation → excess light chains (usually λ)
- ~15% of myeloma patients develop AL amyloidosis

### AA Amyloidosis
- Chronic inflammatory conditions → persistent ↑ SAA (liver-derived)
- Common causes: Rheumatoid arthritis, TB, osteomyelitis, FMF, bronchiectasis, IBD

## Morphology

### Gross Appearance
- Affected organs are enlarged, firm, waxy ("lardaceous")
- **Spleen:**
  - Sago spleen — amyloid in splenic follicles (white pulp) → tapioca-like nodules
  - Lardaceous spleen — diffuse involvement of red pulp → waxy, enlarged
- **Kidney:** Enlarged, pale, waxy; later contracted and shrunken
- **Liver:** Enlarged, pale, firm, waxy cut surface
- **Heart:** Enlarged, firm, rubbery, "dropped on floor" consistency

### Microscopic Appearance
- **Amorphous, eosinophilic, acellular** material in extracellular spaces
- Congo red staining → salmon pink in ordinary light → **apple-green birefringence** under polarized light
- Kidney: Mesangial, subendothelial, and tubular basement membrane deposits
- Heart: Interstitial deposits between myocardial fibers → restrictive cardiomyopathy pattern
- Liver: Space of Disse deposits, portal tracts
- Nerve: Endoneurial deposits → peripheral neuropathy
- Electron microscopy: Non-branching fibrils, 7.5–10 nm diameter

## Clinical Features

### Systemic Manifestations
- **Kidney (most common organ in AA):** Nephrotic syndrome → progressive renal failure
- **Heart (most common in AL):** Restrictive cardiomyopathy, arrhythmias, heart failure
  - Low-voltage ECG with pseudo-infarct pattern
  - "Sparkling" granular myocardium on echocardiogram
- **Liver:** Hepatomegaly, elevated ALP, mild transaminase elevation
- **GI tract:** Macroglossia (pathognomonic for AL), malabsorption, diarrhea
- **Nervous system:** Peripheral neuropathy, carpal tunnel syndrome, autonomic neuropathy
- **Skin:** Waxy papules, purpura (periorbital "raccoon eyes" — characteristic of AL)
- **Joints:** Shoulder pad sign, carpal tunnel

### Localized Amyloidosis
- Alzheimer disease: Cerebral plaques (Aβ)
- Type 2 DM: Islet amyloid (AIAPP)
- Medullary carcinoma thyroid: Calcitonin-derived amyloid in stroma

## Diagnosis

- **Gold standard:** Tissue biopsy with Congo red staining → apple-green birefringence
- **Biopsy sites:** Abdominal fat pad aspirate (least invasive), rectal biopsy, gingival biopsy, or affected organ
- **Typing:** Immunohistochemistry, mass spectrometry, immunofluorescence
- **Serum/urine:** Free light chain assay, serum protein electrophoresis (AL)
- **SAP scintigraphy:** Nuclear imaging with radiolabeled SAP
- **Echocardiography:** Granular sparkling, thickened walls, restrictive physiology
- **Cardiac MRI:** Late gadolinium enhancement pattern

## Management

- **AL:** Chemotherapy targeting plasma cells (bortezomib, melphalan, dexamethasone); stem cell transplant in eligible patients
- **AA:** Treat underlying chronic inflammatory disease; colchicine for FMF
- **ATTR:** Tafamidis (TTR stabilizer) for cardiac ATTR; liver transplant for familial ATTR; patisiran/inotersen (gene silencing)
- **Supportive:** Diuretics for heart failure, ACE inhibitors for proteinuria (caution in cardiac amyloid), dialysis for renal failure

## Complications

- Progressive renal failure → ESRD
- Restrictive cardiomyopathy → intractable heart failure
- Arrhythmias and sudden cardiac death
- Malabsorption and GI hemorrhage
- Peripheral and autonomic neuropathy
- Recurrent infections (functional asplenia)
