# Amyloidosis — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Amyloidosis = extracellular deposition of misfolded β-pleated sheet proteins. Congo red stain → apple-green birefringence (polarized light). AL (light chains, myeloma) → heart; AA (SAA, chronic inflammation) → kidney. Diagnosis by fat pad/rectal biopsy. Non-branching fibrils 7.5–10 nm on EM.

## High Yield Points

★ Congo red → apple-green birefringence under polarized light (DIAGNOSTIC)
★ AL amyloid = immunoglobulin light chains (usually λ) — associated with multiple myeloma
★ AA amyloid = serum amyloid A — secondary to chronic inflammation (RA, TB, FMF)
★ Most common organ: Kidney (AA) and Heart (AL)
★ Macroglossia = pathognomonic for AL amyloidosis
★ Sago spleen = follicular involvement; Lardaceous spleen = diffuse red pulp involvement
★ Periorbital purpura ("raccoon eyes") = characteristic of AL
★ EM: Non-branching fibrils, 7.5–10 nm diameter
★ Abdominal fat pad aspirate = least invasive biopsy for diagnosis
★ Cardiac amyloid: Restrictive cardiomyopathy + low-voltage ECG + sparkling echo
★ FMF: Autosomal recessive; colchicine prevents AA amyloidosis
★ Tafamidis = TTR stabilizer for ATTR cardiac amyloidosis

## Mnemonics

- **AL = Light chains, Amyloid of Loners** (plasma cell neoplasm — monoclonal)
- **AA = Always secondary to Another disease** (chronic inflammation)
- **Organs in AL:** "Heart Loves Tongue Nerves" — Heart, Liver, Tongue (macroglossia), Nerves
- **Causes of AA:** "Really Terrible Bones Feel Inflamed" — RA, TB, Bronchiectasis, FMF, IBD
