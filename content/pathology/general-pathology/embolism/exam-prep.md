# Embolism — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Embolus = intravascular mass carried to distant site. >99% are thromboemboli. PE source: DVT of lower limb. Systemic emboli source: left heart. Special types: fat (fractures, triad), air (>100mL), amniotic fluid (labor, DIC, high mortality). Saddle embolus → sudden death.

## High Yield Points

★ >95% of PE arise from deep veins of lower extremity (iliac, femoral, popliteal)
★ Saddle embolus straddles pulmonary artery bifurcation → sudden death
★ Pulmonary infarcts are hemorrhagic (red) and wedge-shaped (dual blood supply)
★ Fat embolism triad: Respiratory distress + Neurologic symptoms + Petechial rash (1-3 days post-fracture)
★ Amniotic fluid embolism: Fetal squamous cells + lanugo in maternal pulmonary vessels; 80% mortality
★ Air embolism: >100 mL air needed; frothy blood in right ventricle at autopsy
★ Decompression sickness (Caisson disease) = nitrogen gas bubbles → "the bends"
★ Paradoxical embolism: Venous → arterial via PFO/ASD
★ Hampton hump (wedge opacity) and Westermark sign (oligemia) on CXR in PE
★ D-dimer: High sensitivity, low specificity for PE; CT-PA = gold standard

## Mnemonics

- **Fat embolism triad:** "FAN" — Fat (respiratory), Axillary petechiae, Neurologic
- **Virchow triad (DVT risk):** "SHE" — Stasis, Hypercoagulability, Endothelial injury
- **Types of emboli:** "FAST-P" — Fat, Air, Septic, Thrombus, Paradoxical
