# Inflammation

> 📚 **Sources:** Robbins & Cotran Pathologic Basis of Disease 10th Ed, Harsh Mohan Textbook of Pathology 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Inflammation is a **protective response** to eliminate the initial cause of cell injury (microbes, toxins, necrotic tissue) and initiate tissue repair. It involves **vascular changes**, **leukocyte recruitment**, and **mediator release**. Can be **acute** (rapid, neutrophils) or **chronic** (prolonged, macrophages/lymphocytes).

**Cardinal signs (Celsus)**: Rubor (redness), Tumor (swelling), Calor (heat), Dolor (pain), Functio laesa (loss of function - Virchow)

## Key Points

- Acute inflammation: **Neutrophils** predominate, vascular changes, short duration
- Chronic inflammation: **Macrophages/lymphocytes**, tissue destruction + repair
- **Exudate**: High protein, inflammatory; **Transudate**: Low protein, non-inflammatory
- Key mediators: Histamine, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, cytokines, complement
- Outcomes: Resolution, scarring, chronic inflammation, abscess

## Detailed Content

### Acute Inflammation

#### Stimuli
- Infections (bacteria, viruses, fungi)
- Tissue necrosis (ischemia, trauma)
- Foreign bodies
- Immune reactions

#### Vascular Changes

| Phase | Mechanism | Effect |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Transient vasoconstriction** | Neurogenic | Seconds |
| **Vasodilation** | Histamine, NO, PGI₂ | ↑ Blood flow (redness, warmth) |
| **↑ Vascular permeability** | Endothelial contraction, injury | Exudate formation (swelling) |
| **Stasis** | ↑ Viscosity, fluid loss | Leukocyte margination |

#### Mechanisms of Increased Permeability

| Mechanism | Mediator | Timing | Vessels |
|-----------|----------|--------|---------|
| **Endothelial contraction** | Histamine, leukotrienes | Immediate (15-30 min) | Venules |
| **Direct endothelial injury** | Burns, toxins | Immediate, prolonged | All vessels |
| **Leukocyte-mediated injury** | ROS, enzymes | Delayed | Venules, capillaries |
| **Increased transcytosis** | VEGF | Delayed | Venules |
| **New vessel leakage** | Angiogenesis | Repair phase | New capillaries |

#### Leukocyte Recruitment (Multi-step process)

**Mnemonic:MART-ED**
1. **M**argination → slowing in vessel
2. **A**dhesion → rolling (selectins) → firm adhesion (integrins)
3. **R**olling → Selectins (E-selectin, P-selectin) bind Sialyl-Lewis X
4. **T**ight adhesion → Integrins (LFA-1, Mac-1) bind ICAM-1
5. **E**migration (diapedesis) → through endothelium (PECAM-1/CD31)
6. **D**irected migration (chemotaxis) → toward chemoattractants

#### Adhesion Molecules

| Family | Molecule | Location | Ligand |
|--------|----------|----------|--------|
| **Selectins** | P-selectin | Endothelium (Weibel-Palade) | Sialyl-Lewis X |
| | E-selectin | Endothelium (induced) | Sialyl-Lewis X |
| | L-selectin | Leukocytes | GlyCAM-1 |
| **Integrins** | LFA-1 (CD11a/CD18) | Leukocytes | ICAM-1 |
| | Mac-1 (CD11b/CD18) | Leukocytes | ICAM-1, iC3b |
| | VLA-4 | Lymphocytes | VCAM-1 |
| **Ig superfamily** | ICAM-1 | Endothelium | LFA-1, Mac-1 |
| | VCAM-1 | Endothelium | VLA-4 |
| | PECAM-1 (CD31) | Endothelium, leukocytes | PECAM-1 |

**Clinical Pearl**: **Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency (LAD)**
- LAD-I: Defect in CD18 (β₂ integrin) → recurrent bacterial infections, delayed cord separation, no pus formation
- LAD-II: Defect in fucose metabolism (no Sialyl-Lewis X) → recurrent infections + mental retardation

#### Chemotaxis

**Chemoattractants:**
- Bacterial products: N-formyl peptides (fMLP)
- Complement: **C5a** (most potent)
- Lipids: **Leukotriene B₄** (LTB₄)
- Chemokines: IL-8 (neutrophils), MCP-1 (monocytes)

#### Phagocytosis

**Three steps: Recognition → Engulfment → Killing**

1. **Recognition & Attachment**
   - **Opsonins**: IgG (Fc receptor), C3b (CR1 receptor), Collectins
   - **Pattern recognition**: TLRs, mannose receptors

2. **Engulfment**
   - Pseudopod extension → phagosome formation
   - Phagosome + lysosome → **phagolysosome**

3. **Killing & Degradation**

| Mechanism | Agents | Targets |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Oxygen-dependent (Respiratory burst)** | O₂⁻, H₂O₂, HOCl, OH• | Most potent bactericidal |
| **NADPH oxidase** | Superoxide → H₂O₂ | Key enzyme |
| **Myeloperoxidase (MPO)** | H₂O₂ + Cl⁻ → HOCl | Most efficient killing |
| **Oxygen-independent** | Lysozyme, lactoferrin, defensins, BPI | Complement killing |

**Clinical Pearl**: **Chronic Granulomatous Disease (CGD)**
- Defect in **NADPH oxidase** (most common: gp91phox)
- Cannot produce superoxide → recurrent infections with **catalase-positive** organisms
- Organisms: Staph aureus, Serratia, Nocardia, Aspergillus, Candida
- **NBT test**: Negative (no blue color) | **DHR flow cytometry**: Confirmatory

### Chemical Mediators of Inflammation

#### Cell-Derived Mediators

| Mediator | Source | Actions |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **Histamine** | Mast cells, basophils, platelets | Vasodilation, ↑ permeability, itching |
| **Serotonin** | Platelets | Vasodilation |
| **Prostaglandins** | All cells (COX pathway) | Pain, fever, vasodilation |
| **Leukotrienes** | Leukocytes (LOX pathway) | Vasoconstriction, ↑ permeability, chemotaxis |
| **PAF** | Leukocytes, endothelium | Platelet aggregation, vasodilation, bronchoconstriction |
| **Cytokines** | Macrophages, lymphocytes | Systemic effects (fever, acute phase response) |
| **NO** | Endothelium, macrophages | Vasodilation, microbicidal |
| **ROS** | Leukocytes | Microbicidal, tissue damage |

#### Arachidonic Acid Metabolites (Eicosanoids)

```
Membrane Phospholipids
         ↓ Phospholipase A₂ (inhibited by corticosteroids)
   Arachidonic Acid
    ↙            ↘
COX pathway      LOX pathway
(inhibited by    (not affected by
NSAIDs, aspirin) NSAIDs)
    ↓                ↓
Prostaglandins    Leukotrienes
Thromboxanes
```

| Product | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **PGI₂ (prostacyclin)** | Vasodilation, inhibits platelet aggregation |
| **TXA₂ (thromboxane)** | Vasoconstriction, platelet aggregation |
| **PGD₂, PGE₂** | Vasodilation, pain, fever |
| **LTB₄** | Chemotaxis (neutrophils) |
| **LTC₄, LTD₄, LTE₄** | Bronchoconstriction, ↑ permeability (SRS-A) |

#### Cytokines

| Cytokine | Source | Actions |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **TNF-α** | Macrophages | Fever, acute phase proteins, cachexia, septic shock |
| **IL-1** | Macrophages | Fever, acute phase proteins |
| **IL-6** | Macrophages, T cells | Acute phase proteins, B cell activation |
| **IL-8** | Macrophages | Neutrophil chemotaxis |
| **IL-12** | Macrophages, DCs | Th1 differentiation, NK activation |

#### Plasma-Derived Mediators

**Complement System:**
| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **C3a, C5a** | Anaphylatoxins (mast cell degranulation), C5a = chemotaxis |
| **C3b** | Opsonin |
| **C5b-9 (MAC)** | Cell lysis |

**Kinin System:**
- Factor XII → Prekallikrein → **Kallikrein**
- Kallikrein + HMWK → **Bradykinin**
- Bradykinin: Vasodilation, ↑ permeability, pain, smooth muscle contraction

**Coagulation/Fibrinolytic System:**
- Factor XII activates both coagulation and kinin systems
- Fibrinopeptides: ↑ permeability, chemotaxis
- Plasmin: Activates complement, generates fibrin degradation products

### Morphologic Patterns of Acute Inflammation

| Pattern | Characteristics | Examples |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| **Serous** | Clear, protein-poor fluid | Skin blister, pleural effusion |
| **Fibrinous** | Fibrin-rich exudate | Pericarditis ("bread and butter"), pleuritis |
| **Purulent/Suppurative** | Pus (neutrophils + liquefactive necrosis) | Abscess, empyema |
| **Ulcerative** | Mucosal necrosis + excavation | Peptic ulcer, aphthous ulcer |

### Outcomes of Acute Inflammation

1. **Resolution** → Complete restoration (if minimal damage)
2. **Scarring/Fibrosis** → If significant tissue destruction
3. **Chronic inflammation** → Persistent stimulus
4. **Abscess formation** → Walled-off pus collection

---

### Chronic Inflammation

#### Causes
- Persistent infections (TB, viruses, fungi)
- Autoimmune diseases (RA, SLE, IBD)
- Prolonged toxic exposure (silica, asbestos)
- Foreign bodies

#### Features
- **Mononuclear infiltrate**: Macrophages, lymphocytes, plasma cells
- **Tissue destruction**: By inflammatory cells
- **Repair**: Angiogenesis, fibrosis

#### Macrophage Activation

| Pathway | Stimulus | Function |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **M1 (Classical)** | IFN-γ, TLR ligands | Microbicidal, inflammation, ROS/NO |
| **M2 (Alternative)** | IL-4, IL-13 | Tissue repair, anti-inflammatory, fibrosis |

### Granulomatous Inflammation

**Granuloma**: Collection of activated macrophages (**epithelioid cells**) ± multinucleated giant cells, surrounded by lymphocytes

#### Types of Giant Cells

| Type | Nuclei Arrangement | Associated With |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Langhans giant cell** | Peripheral horseshoe | TB, sarcoidosis |
| **Foreign body giant cell** | Random/central | Foreign material |
| **Touton giant cell** | Ring around central lipid | Xanthomas, fat necrosis |

#### Causes of Granulomatous Inflammation

**Mnemonic: TB SINGS F**
- **T**uberculosis
- **B**erylliosis
- **S**arcoidosis
- **I**nflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's)
- **N**ocardia
- **G**ranulomatosis with polyangiitis (Wegener's)
- **S**yphilis
- **F**oreign bodies, **F**ungi

#### Caseating vs Non-Caseating Granulomas

| Feature | Caseating | Non-Caseating |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Central necrosis** | Present (cheesy) | Absent |
| **Causes** | TB, fungi | Sarcoidosis, Crohn's, foreign body |

### Systemic Effects of Inflammation

**Acute Phase Response:**
- Mediated by IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α
- **Fever**: IL-1, TNF → hypothalamus → PGE₂
- **Acute phase proteins**: CRP, fibrinogen, SAA, hepcidin
- **Leukocytosis**: IL-1, TNF → bone marrow release
- **↑ ESR**: Due to fibrinogen (RBC rouleaux formation)

## Clinical Applications

1. **Sepsis/SIRS**: Excessive TNF-α, IL-1 → vasodilation, ↑ permeability → shock
2. **Rheumatoid arthritis**: Chronic synovial inflammation → pannus → joint destruction
3. **Tuberculosis**: Granulomatous inflammation → caseation → cavitation
4. **NSAIDs mechanism**: COX inhibition → ↓ prostaglandins → ↓ pain, fever, inflammation
5. **Corticosteroids**: Inhibit phospholipase A₂ → ↓ all eicosanoids

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **First cells in acute inflammation** → Neutrophils (6-24 hours)
2. **Predominant cells in chronic inflammation** → Macrophages
3. **Most potent chemotactic factor** → C5a
4. **Most potent vasodilator** → NO (nitric oxide)
5. **Fever mediator** → PGE₂ (produced in hypothalamus via IL-1/TNF)
6. **"Bread and butter" pericarditis** → Fibrinous inflammation
7. **Leukocyte Adhesion Deficiency Type I** → CD18 deficiency → delayed cord separation
8. **Chronic Granulomatous Disease** → NADPH oxidase deficiency → catalase-positive infections
9. **Chédiak-Higashi syndrome** → Microtubule defect → giant granules, impaired phagolysosome fusion
10. **Non-caseating granulomas** → Sarcoidosis (think "naked granulomas")
11. **Caseating granulomas** → TB until proven otherwise
12. **Langhans giant cells** → TB (horseshoe nuclei)
13. **SRS-A (Slow Reacting Substance of Anaphylaxis)** → LTC₄, LTD₄, LTE₄
14. **Aspirin-sensitive asthma** → COX inhibition shunts AA to LOX pathway → ↑ leukotrienes
