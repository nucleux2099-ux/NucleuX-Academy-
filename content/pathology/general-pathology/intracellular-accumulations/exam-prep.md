# Intracellular Accumulations — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Cells accumulate lipids (steatosis), proteins (Russell bodies, Mallory-Denk), glycogen (DM, storage diseases), or pigments (lipofuscin, hemosiderin, melanin, carbon). Dystrophic calcification = dead tissue, normal calcium. Metastatic calcification = normal tissue, high calcium. Psammoma bodies in papillary thyroid, meningioma, serous ovarian tumors.

## High Yield Points

★ Fatty liver: Alcohol = most common cause; macrovesicular (large droplet) vs microvesicular
★ Lipofuscin = "wear and tear" pigment; golden-brown, perinuclear; indicates atrophy, NOT injury
★ Hemosiderin: Prussian blue (Perl's) stain positive; indicates iron overload
★ Dystrophic calcification = DEAD tissue, NORMAL calcium; Metastatic = NORMAL tissue, HIGH calcium
★ Metastatic calcification sites: Lung, kidney, stomach, blood vessels (acid-secreting = alkaline interstitium)
★ Psammoma bodies: Papillary thyroid carcinoma, Meningioma, Papillary serous ovarian carcinoma
★ α1-antitrypsin: PAS+, diastase-resistant globules in hepatocytes
★ Mallory-Denk bodies = damaged cytokeratin intermediate filaments (alcoholic hepatitis, also NASH, Wilson, HCC)
★ Carbon (anthracosis) = most common exogenous pigment
★ Russell bodies = excess immunoglobulin in plasma cells (Mott cells = multiple Russell bodies)

## Mnemonics

- **Psammoma bodies:** "PSaMMOma" — Papillary thyroid, Serous ovarian, Meningioma, Mesothelioma (Occasional)
- **Dystrophic vs Metastatic:** "DysTrophic = Dead Tissue, Normal calcium; MeTastatic = Moving calcium to Normal tissue"
- **Fatty liver causes:** "ADOPT" — Alcohol, Diabetes/Drugs, Obesity, Protein malnutrition, Toxins (CCl₄)
- **Endogenous pigments:** "LHMB" — Lipofuscin, Hemosiderin, Melanin, Bilirubin
