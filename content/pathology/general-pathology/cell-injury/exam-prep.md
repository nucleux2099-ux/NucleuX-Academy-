# Cell Injury — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Cell injury results when stress exceeds adaptive capacity. Most common cause = hypoxia. Reversible injury: cell swelling, fatty change. Irreversible injury: necrosis (pyknosis → karyorrhexis → karyolysis). Key mechanisms: ATP depletion, Ca²⁺ influx, ROS, membrane damage, mitochondrial dysfunction.

## High Yield Points

★ Most common cause of cell injury = **Hypoxia** (ischemia most common type)
★ Earliest morphologic change in reversible injury = **Cell swelling** (hydropic change)
★ Point of no return = **Mitochondrial dysfunction + membrane damage**
★ Nuclear changes in necrosis: Pyknosis → Karyorrhexis → Karyolysis
★ ATP depletion → ↓ Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase → cell swelling
★ ↑ Cytosolic Ca²⁺ activates phospholipases, proteases, endonucleases
★ ROS defense: SOD (O₂⁻ → H₂O₂), Catalase (H₂O₂ → H₂O), Glutathione peroxidase
★ Fatty change most commonly seen in **liver** (also heart, kidney)
★ Reperfusion injury: ROS burst + complement + neutrophil infiltration
★ Cyanide causes histotoxic hypoxia (blocks cytochrome oxidase)
★ Fenton reaction: Fe²⁺ + H₂O₂ → OH· (most reactive ROS)

## Mnemonics

- **Causes of cell injury:** "CING PHN" — Chemical, Immunologic, Nutritional, Genetic, Physical, Hypoxia, iNfectious
- **Mechanisms:** "AMMO CD" — ATP depletion, Mitochondrial damage, Membrane damage, Oxidative stress, Calcium influx, DNA/protein damage
- **Nuclear changes:** "Pyknosis Karyorrhexis Karyolysis = PKK" — shrink, fragment, dissolve
- **Free radical defense:** "SOC-G-VCE" — SOD, Catalase, Glutathione peroxidase, Vitamins C & E
