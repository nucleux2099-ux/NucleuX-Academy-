# Acute Inflammation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Acute inflammation is a rapid, short-lived vascular and cellular response to injury, dominated by **neutrophils**. Key events: vasodilation → increased permeability → neutrophil recruitment → phagocytosis. Mediated by histamine, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, complement, and cytokines. Outcomes: resolution, abscess, chronicity, or scarring.

## High Yield Points

★ Cardinal signs: Rubor, Tumor, Calor, Dolor, Functio laesa
★ Hallmark cell = **Neutrophil** (PMN)
★ Histamine → immediate transient increase in vascular permeability (endothelial contraction in venules)
★ C5a = most potent chemotactic complement fragment
★ LTB4 = potent chemotactic agent; LTC4/D4/E4 = bronchospasm + permeability
★ Opsonins: IgG Fc, C3b, collectins
★ Oxygen-dependent killing: Respiratory burst → O₂⁻ → H₂O₂ → HOCl (MPO system — most efficient bactericidal mechanism)
★ Rolling = selectins; Adhesion = integrins; Transmigration = PECAM-1 (CD31)
★ "Bread and butter" appearance = fibrinous pericarditis
★ Leukocyte adhesion deficiency (LAD): defective integrins → recurrent infections, no pus, delayed cord separation
★ Acute phase reactants: CRP, fibrinogen, SAA, hepcidin, ferritin (↑); albumin, transferrin (↓ — negative)

## Mnemonics

- **Cardinal signs:** "Really Tired? Can't Do Fun things" — Rubor, Tumor, Calor, Dolor, Functio laesa
- **Selectins for Rolling:** "SeLect to RoLL" — Selectins → Rolling
- **Chemotactic agents:** "Come Let's Be 8riends" — C5a, LTB4, Bacterial products, IL-8
