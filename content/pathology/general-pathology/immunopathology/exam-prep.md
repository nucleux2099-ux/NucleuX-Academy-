# Immunopathology — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Four types of hypersensitivity: I (IgE, immediate), II (IgG/IgM, cytotoxic), III (immune complex), IV (T cell, delayed). SLE = prototype systemic autoimmune disease with anti-dsDNA and immune complex nephritis. Primary immunodeficiencies are genetic; secondary = HIV/AIDS. Transplant rejection involves T cell-mediated and antibody-mediated mechanisms.

## High Yield Points

★ Type I: IgE + mast cells; anaphylaxis treatment = EPINEPHRINE
★ Type II: IgG/IgM against cell surface; Linear IF (Goodpasture); Direct Coombs (AIHA)
★ Type III: Immune complexes; Granular IF ("lumpy bumpy"); SLE, serum sickness, PSGN
★ Type IV: T cell-mediated; 24-72 hrs; TB skin test, contact dermatitis, transplant rejection
★ SLE antibodies: ANA (sensitive), anti-dsDNA (specific, correlates with disease activity), anti-Smith (most specific)
★ Wire-loop lesion = Class IV lupus nephritis (diffuse proliferative — worst prognosis)
★ "Full house" IF = IgG + IgA + IgM + C3 + C1q = SLE nephritis
★ Libman-Sacks endocarditis = sterile vegetations on BOTH sides of valve (SLE)
★ Bruton (X-linked agammaglobulinemia): BTK mutation, absent B cells, recurrent bacterial infections after 6 months
★ SCID: Absent T and B cells; bubble boy; bone marrow transplant curative
★ HLA-B27: Ankylosing spondylitis, reactive arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, IBD-associated arthritis

## Mnemonics

- **Hypersensitivity types:** "ACID" — Anaphylactic, Cytotoxic, Immune complex, Delayed
- **SLE criteria (selected):** "SOAP BRAIN MD" — Serositis, Oral ulcers, Arthritis, Photosensitivity, Blood (cytopenias), Renal, ANA, Immunologic (anti-dsDNA/Smith), Neurologic, Malar rash, Discoid rash
- **Type II diseases:** "ABCG" — AIHA, Blood transfusion, Coombs positive, Goodpasture
- **Primary immunodeficiencies:** "BASIC-W" — Bruton, Adenosine deaminase (SCID), Selective IgA, IgA/Common variable, Wiskott-Aldrich
