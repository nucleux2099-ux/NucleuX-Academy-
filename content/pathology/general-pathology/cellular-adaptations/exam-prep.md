# Cellular Adaptations — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Four main adaptations: Hypertrophy (↑ size), Hyperplasia (↑ number), Atrophy (↓ size), Metaplasia (cell type change). All are reversible. Metaplasia → dysplasia → neoplasia is a key progression. Dysplasia is pre-neoplastic (not a true adaptation). Adaptations are driven by altered demand, hormones, or chronic irritation.

## High Yield Points

★ Hypertrophy = ↑ cell size (non-dividing cells: cardiac, skeletal muscle)
★ Hyperplasia = ↑ cell number (dividing cells only)
★ Cardiac myocytes undergo ONLY hypertrophy (not hyperplasia)
★ Barrett esophagus = squamous → intestinal columnar metaplasia (premalignant → adenocarcinoma)
★ Brown atrophy = lipofuscin accumulation in atrophic cells
★ Atrophy mechanism: ubiquitin-proteasome pathway + autophagy
★ Endometrial hyperplasia (unopposed estrogen) → risk of endometrial carcinoma
★ Metaplasia = stem cell reprogramming (NOT direct cell transformation)
★ Dysplasia features: pleomorphism, hyperchromasia, ↑ mitoses, loss of polarity
★ Physiologic hypertrophy: Uterus in pregnancy; Pathologic: LVH in HTN

## Mnemonics

- **Adaptations:** "HHAM" — Hypertrophy, Hyperplasia, Atrophy, Metaplasia
- **Causes of atrophy:** "DINE-PS" — Disuse, Ischemia, Nutritional, Endocrine loss, Pressure, denervation (S = Senile)
- **Metaplasia → Cancer sequence:** "MET → DYS → NEO" (Metaplasia → Dysplasia → Neoplasia)
- **Hypertrophy vs Hyperplasia:** "Non-dividers Hypertrophy, Dividers Hyperplase"
