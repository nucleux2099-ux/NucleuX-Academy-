# Chronic Inflammation — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Chronic inflammation = prolonged (weeks-years) inflammation with macrophages, lymphocytes, and plasma cells. Triad: inflammation + tissue destruction + fibrosis. Macrophage is the central cell (M1 = pro-inflammatory, M2 = repair). Causes: persistent infections, autoimmune diseases, toxic exposures. Complications include fibrosis, amyloidosis, and cancer.

## High Yield Points

★ Hallmark cells: **Macrophages**, lymphocytes, plasma cells (NOT neutrophils)
★ M1 macrophages (classical, IFN-γ) = kill microbes; M2 (alternative, IL-4/IL-13) = repair/fibrosis
★ Chronic inflammation = simultaneous destruction + inflammation + repair
★ Tissue macrophage names: Kupffer (liver), Microglia (brain), Alveolar (lung), Osteoclast (bone)
★ Chronic inflammation → AA amyloidosis (via ↑ SAA)
★ Chronic inflammation → ↑ cancer risk (HBV/HCV → HCC, H. pylori → gastric cancer, IBD → CRC)
★ Anemia of chronic disease: ↑ hepcidin → ↓ iron availability, ↓ serum iron, ↓ TIBC, ↑ ferritin
★ TGF-β = master regulator of fibrosis
★ Russell bodies = eosinophilic immunoglobulin inclusions in plasma cells
★ Th1 (IFN-γ, IL-2) vs Th2 (IL-4, IL-5, IL-13) paradigm in chronic inflammation

## Mnemonics

- **Cells of chronic inflammation:** "MLP" — Macrophages, Lymphocytes, Plasma cells
- **Macrophage activation:** "M1 = Militant (kill), M2 = Mender (repair)"
- **Chronic inflammation → Cancer:** "HHH-UC" — Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, H. pylori, UC/Crohn
- **Tissue macrophages:** "KLAMO" — Kupffer, Langerhans, Alveolar, Microglia, Osteoclast
