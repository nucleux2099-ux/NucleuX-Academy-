# Chronic Inflammation

## Overview

Chronic inflammation is a prolonged inflammatory response (weeks to years) characterized by simultaneous tissue destruction, inflammation, and repair. Unlike acute inflammation dominated by neutrophils, chronic inflammation features mononuclear cell infiltrates (macrophages, lymphocytes, plasma cells), tissue destruction, and fibrosis. It may follow acute inflammation or begin insidiously.

## Key Points

- **Duration:** Weeks to months to years
- **Hallmark cells:** Macrophages, lymphocytes, plasma cells
- **Features triad:** Inflammation + tissue destruction + repair (fibrosis)
- **Can arise:** Following acute inflammation, de novo, or as low-grade smoldering response
- **End result:** Often fibrosis and scarring

## Definition & Classification

### Definition
Chronic inflammation = prolonged inflammatory process in which active inflammation, tissue destruction, and attempts at repair proceed simultaneously.

### Causes
1. **Persistent infections:** TB, syphilis, fungi, certain viruses (HBV, HCV)
2. **Prolonged exposure to toxic agents:** Silicosis, asbestosis, atherosclerosis (oxidized lipids)
3. **Autoimmune diseases:** Rheumatoid arthritis, SLE, IBD, MS
4. **Following acute inflammation** that fails to resolve

### Patterns
- **Non-specific chronic inflammation:** Diffuse mononuclear infiltrate + fibrosis
- **Granulomatous inflammation** (a specific subtype — discussed separately)

## Etiology & Pathogenesis

### Role of Macrophages (Central Cell)
- Derived from blood monocytes → tissue macrophages (within 24-48 hrs)
- Tissue-specific names: Kupffer cells (liver), alveolar macrophages (lung), microglia (brain), osteoclasts (bone), mesangial cells (kidney)
- **Activation pathways:**
  - **Classical (M1):** IFN-γ activated → microbicidal, pro-inflammatory, ROS/NO production, IL-1/TNF secretion
  - **Alternative (M2):** IL-4/IL-13 activated → anti-inflammatory, tissue repair, fibrosis, TGF-β/IL-10
- Functions: Phagocytosis, antigen presentation, cytokine secretion, tissue remodeling

### Role of Lymphocytes
- **T cells:** CD4+ (Th1 → IFN-γ; Th2 → IL-4, IL-5, IL-13; Th17 → IL-17)
- **B cells/Plasma cells:** Antibody production
- Bidirectional macrophage-lymphocyte interaction sustains chronic inflammation

### Other Cells
- **Eosinophils:** Parasitic infections, allergic reactions; major basic protein (MBP) — toxic to parasites and host tissue
- **Mast cells:** IgE-mediated reactions, fibrosis
- **Fibroblasts:** Collagen synthesis → fibrosis (end stage)

### Mediators
- Cytokines: TNF, IL-1, IFN-γ, IL-12, IL-17
- Growth factors: PDGF, FGF, TGF-β (fibrosis)
- ROS and NO from activated macrophages
- Proteases: MMPs (tissue destruction and remodeling)

## Morphology

### Gross Appearance
- Thickened, fibrotic tissues
- Organ may be shrunken and scarred (cirrhotic liver, contracted kidney)
- Ulceration may be present (chronic peptic ulcer, IBD)
- Abscess cavities may persist

### Microscopic Appearance
- **Mononuclear cell infiltrate:** Macrophages, lymphocytes, plasma cells (Russell bodies in plasma cells)
- **Tissue destruction:** Caused by inflammatory cells (MMPs, ROS)
- **Fibrosis:** Proliferation of fibroblasts, collagen deposition, angiogenesis (granulation tissue)
- Epithelioid macrophages and giant cells (in granulomatous type)
- Lymphoid aggregates or follicles may form (tertiary lymphoid organs)

## Clinical Features

- **Insidious onset** — may be asymptomatic initially
- Low-grade fever, malaise, weight loss, fatigue
- Organ-specific: Joint stiffness (RA), diarrhea (IBD), breathlessness (pulmonary fibrosis)
- **Systemic:** Anemia of chronic disease, elevated ESR, ↑ immunoglobulins
- Secondary amyloidosis (AA) in longstanding cases

## Diagnosis

- **Histopathology:** Mononuclear infiltrate + fibrosis + tissue destruction
- **Lab:** ↑ ESR, ↑ CRP, anemia of chronic disease, ↑ globulins
- **Autoimmune markers:** ANA, RF, anti-CCP, ANCA (depending on disease)
- **Imaging:** Fibrotic changes on CT/MRI
- **Special stains:** AFB stain (TB), PAS (fungi)

## Management

- **Treat underlying cause** (antibiotics, antifungals, immunosuppressants)
- **Anti-inflammatory:** Corticosteroids, DMARDs (methotrexate, azathioprine)
- **Biologics:** Anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab), anti-IL-6, anti-CD20
- **Supportive:** Nutrition, physiotherapy, organ-specific care
- **Surgery:** For complications (strictures, fibrosis, abscesses)

## Complications

- Progressive organ fibrosis and dysfunction
- Secondary amyloidosis (AA)
- Anemia of chronic disease
- Increased cancer risk (chronic inflammation → neoplasia: chronic hepatitis → HCC, chronic gastritis → gastric cancer, IBD → colorectal cancer)
- Cachexia and wasting
- Autoimmune flares
