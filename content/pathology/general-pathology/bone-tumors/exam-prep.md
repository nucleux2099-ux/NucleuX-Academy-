# Bone Tumors — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Bone tumors classified by tissue of origin (bone, cartilage, marrow, fibrous). Most common benign = osteochondroma; most common malignant (adolescents) = osteosarcoma. Ewing sarcoma = small round blue cell tumor with t(11;22) and CD99+. Diagnosis requires clinical-radiological-pathological correlation. Metastatic disease > primary tumors in adults.

## High Yield Points

★ Osteosarcoma: Metaphysis, 10-20 yrs, Codman triangle + sunburst pattern, malignant osteoid production
★ Ewing sarcoma: Diaphysis, 5-15 yrs, onion-skin periosteal reaction, t(11;22) EWS-FLI1, CD99+, small round blue cells
★ Giant cell tumor: Epiphysis, 20-40 yrs, soap bubble appearance, multinucleated giant cells
★ Osteochondroma: Most common benign bone tumor; cartilage cap >2 cm = suspect malignancy
★ Chondrosarcoma: 40-60 yrs, pelvis/proximal femur, chemo/radio-RESISTANT
★ Osteoid osteoma: Night pain relieved by aspirin; <2 cm; nidus on CT
★ Osteosarcoma associated with RB gene mutations, Paget disease, Li-Fraumeni
★ Most common site of bone tumor metastasis = LUNG
★ Ewing sarcoma rare in Black populations
★ Codman triangle = periosteal elevation (NOT pathognomonic for osteosarcoma)

## Mnemonics

- **Bone tumor age:** "5-15 Ewing, 10-20 Osteo, 20-40 GCT, 40-60 Chondro"
- **Location:** "Ewing = Diaphysis (D for D), Osteo = Metaphysis (M for M), GCT = Epiphysis (E for E)"
- **Small round blue cell tumors:** "LEMON" — Lymphoma, Ewing, Medulloblastoma/Merkel, Oat cell (small cell lung), Neuroblastoma/Nephroblastoma
- **Osteosarcoma chemo:** "MAP" — Methotrexate, Adriamycin, cisPlatin
