# Hyperemia & Congestion — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Hyperemia = active (arteriolar dilation, red tissue). Congestion = passive (impaired venous outflow, blue-red). Chronic pulmonary congestion → heart failure cells + brown induration. Chronic hepatic congestion → nutmeg liver + centrilobular necrosis. Chronic splenic congestion → Gamna-Gandy bodies.

## High Yield Points

★ Hyperemia = ACTIVE (arteriolar dilation); Congestion = PASSIVE (impaired venous outflow)
★ Heart failure cells = hemosiderin-laden macrophages in lungs (Prussian blue positive)
★ Brown induration = chronic pulmonary congestion (hemosiderin + fibrosis)
★ Nutmeg liver = chronic hepatic congestion (red centrilobular + yellow periportal zones)
★ Centrilobular necrosis = zone 3 (around central vein) — most vulnerable to hypoxia
★ Gamna-Gandy bodies = iron + calcium encrusted fibrotic nodules in chronically congested spleen
★ Left heart failure → pulmonary congestion; Right heart failure → systemic congestion
★ Cardiac cirrhosis = end-stage chronic hepatic congestion (rare)
★ Congestive splenomegaly → hypersplenism → pancytopenia

## Mnemonics

- **Hyperemia vs Congestion:** "Hyperemia = Hot Red Active; Congestion = Cold Blue Passive"
- **Nutmeg liver zones:** "Central Red, Peripheral Yellow" (centrilobular congestion + periportal fatty change)
- **Heart failure cells:** "HFC = Hemosiderin-Filled Cells from Heart Failure"
