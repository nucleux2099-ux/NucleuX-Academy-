# Meninges — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Three layers: dura (tough, 2 cranial layers), arachnoid (web-like, granulations absorb CSF), pia (adherent to brain). Spaces: epidural (potential — MMA rupture), subdural (potential — bridging veins), subarachnoid (real — CSF, arteries). Dural folds: falx cerebri, tentorium cerebelli. CSF: choroid plexus → ventricles → subarachnoid → arachnoid granulations → SSS.

## High Yield Points

★ Epidural haematoma: middle meningeal artery rupture; biconvex on CT; lucid interval
★ Subdural haematoma: bridging vein rupture; crescent on CT; crosses suture lines
★ Subarachnoid haemorrhage: berry aneurysm rupture; thunderclap headache; blood in CSF
★ Falx cerebri: superior sagittal sinus (upper border), inferior sagittal sinus (lower border)
★ Tentorium cerebelli: separates cerebrum from cerebellum; tentorial notch = CN III at risk
★ Arachnoid granulations absorb CSF into superior sagittal sinus
★ Middle meningeal artery enters via foramen spinosum; runs at pterion
★ Supratentorial dura: trigeminal nerve; infratentorial: C1-3 and vagus
★ Uncal herniation through tentorial notch → CN III palsy → ipsilateral mydriasis
★ Pia dips into sulci; arachnoid does NOT

## Mnemonics

- **Layers (outside in):** "DAP" — Dura, Arachnoid, Pia
- **Epidural vs Subdural:** "Epidural = artery = biconvEx (lens); Subdural = vein = creSCent"
- **Dural folds:** "FaTFaD" — Falx cerebri, Tentorium cerebelli, Falx cerebelli, Diaphragma sellae
- **MMA entry:** "Foramen Spinosum = Spindle-shaped MMA"
