# Brachial Plexus — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Brachial plexus: ventral rami C5–T1 → Roots → Trunks (upper C5C6, middle C7, lower C8T1) → Divisions → Cords (lateral, posterior, medial — named by relation to 2nd part of axillary artery) → Terminal branches. Supraclavicular part in posterior triangle; infraclavicular in axilla.

## High Yield Points

★ Roots between scalenus anterior and medius
★ Erb's point = junction of C5+C6 forming upper trunk
★ Cords named by relation to 2nd part of axillary artery
★ Long thoracic nerve (C5,6,7) → serratus anterior; injury → medial winging
★ Suprascapular nerve from upper trunk → supraspinatus + infraspinatus
★ Erb-Duchenne palsy (C5,C6): policeman's tip position
★ Klumpke's palsy (C8,T1): claw hand ± Horner's syndrome
★ Lateral cord = musculocutaneous + lateral root of median + lateral pectoral nerve
★ Posterior cord = ULTRA: Upper subscapular, Lower subscapular, Thoracodorsal, Radial, Axillary
★ Medial cord = ulnar + medial root of median + medial pectoral + medial cutaneous nerves of arm & forearm
★ Dorsal scapular nerve (C5) → rhomboids + levator scapulae

## Mnemonics

- **Parts order:** "Robert Taylor Drinks Cold Beer" — Roots, Trunks, Divisions, Cords, Branches
- **Posterior cord branches:** "ULTRA" — Upper subscapular, Lower subscapular, Thoracodorsal, Radial, Axillary
- **Long thoracic nerve roots:** "C5, 6, 7 — bells of heaven" (WINGS of serratus anterior)
- **Erb's palsy muscles:** "5 Ds + 2Bs" — Deltoid, supraspinatus (Dorsal rotators), infraspinatus; Biceps, Brachialis, Brachioradialis
