# Ear Anatomy

## Overview

The ear is the organ of hearing and balance, divided into three parts: external ear (auricle and external acoustic meatus), middle ear (tympanic cavity with ossicles), and internal ear (bony and membranous labyrinth). The external and middle ear are concerned with sound conduction, while the internal ear contains receptors for hearing (organ of Corti in cochlea) and balance (maculae and cristae in vestibular apparatus).

## Key Points

- **External ear:** Auricle (elastic cartilage except lobule) + external acoustic meatus (lateral 1/3 cartilaginous, medial 2/3 bony)
- **Middle ear (tympanic cavity):** Air-filled, contains 3 ossicles (malleus, incus, stapes), 2 muscles (tensor tympani, stapedius), chorda tympani nerve
- **Internal ear:** Bony labyrinth (cochlea, vestibule, semicircular canals) and membranous labyrinth within it
- **Tympanic membrane:** Obliquely placed; pars tensa (large) and pars flaccida (Shrapnell's membrane)
- **Nerve supply of TM:** Lateral surface — auriculotemporal (V₃); medial surface — tympanic plexus (IX)

## Gross Anatomy

### External Ear
- **Auricle:** Helix, antihelix, tragus, antitragus, concha, lobule
  - Nerve supply: great auricular (C2,3), auriculotemporal (V₃), auricular branch of vagus (Arnold's nerve), lesser occipital (C2)
- **External acoustic meatus:** ~2.5 cm long; lateral 1/3 cartilaginous (has ceruminous glands, hair), medial 2/3 bony
  - Direction: curves anteriorly and inferiorly then medially — pull pinna up, back, and laterally for otoscopy in adults
  - Relations: parotid gland (anterior), mastoid process (posterior), middle cranial fossa (superior), TMJ (anterior)

### Middle Ear (Tympanic Cavity)
- **Shape:** Air-filled box with 6 walls
- **Roof (Tegmental wall):** Tegmen tympani — separates from middle cranial fossa/temporal lobe
- **Floor (Jugular wall):** Thin bone over jugular bulb
- **Lateral wall:** Tympanic membrane + bony rim
- **Medial wall:** Promontory (basal turn of cochlea), oval window (stapes footplate), round window (secondary tympanic membrane), prominence of facial nerve canal
- **Anterior wall:** Eustachian tube opening, tensor tympani canal, internal carotid artery
- **Posterior wall:** Aditus to mastoid antrum, pyramid (stapedius muscle), facial nerve canal (descending part)

#### Ossicles
- **Malleus:** Handle attached to TM; head in epitympanum
- **Incus:** Body articulates with malleus head; long process → lenticular process → stapes
- **Stapes:** Footplate in oval window; smallest bone in body

#### Muscles
- **Tensor tympani:** Supplied by V₃ (mandibular nerve); tenses TM by pulling malleus medially
- **Stapedius:** Supplied by CN VII (facial nerve); dampens stapes vibration — protective reflex

### Internal Ear
- **Bony labyrinth:** Cochlea (2.5 turns), vestibule, 3 semicircular canals (anterior, posterior, lateral)
  - Filled with perilymph (similar to CSF/ECF — high Na⁺)
- **Membranous labyrinth:** Within bony labyrinth
  - Cochlear duct (scala media) — contains organ of Corti (hearing receptor)
  - Utricle and saccule in vestibule — contain maculae (linear acceleration, gravity)
  - 3 semicircular ducts — contain cristae ampullares (rotational acceleration)
  - Filled with endolymph (similar to ICF — high K⁺)

## Relations & Surface Markings

- **Mastoid antrum:** 1.5 cm deep to suprameatal triangle of MacEwen (posterosuperior to external meatus)
- **Facial nerve:** Runs in medial wall, then posterior wall of middle ear (descending part)
- **Middle cranial fossa:** Superior to tegmen tympani
- **Jugular vein bulb:** Inferior to floor of middle ear
- **Internal carotid artery:** Anterior to middle ear (in carotid canal)

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Blood Supply
- **External ear:** Superficial temporal artery, posterior auricular artery
- **Middle ear:** Anterior tympanic artery (from maxillary), stylomastoid artery (from posterior auricular)
- **Internal ear:** Labyrinthine artery (from basilar artery or AICA) — end artery

### Nerve Supply
- **Hearing:** CN VIII (cochlear part) — spiral ganglion
- **Balance:** CN VIII (vestibular part) — vestibular ganglion (Scarpa's)
- **Middle ear sensation:** Tympanic plexus (CN IX — Jacobson's nerve)
- **External ear:** V₃ (auriculotemporal), great auricular (C2,3), Arnold's nerve (X)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Otitis Media
- Middle ear infection → can spread to mastoid (mastoiditis) or through tegmen tympani → meningitis/brain abscess
- Cholesteatoma can erode ossicles → conductive hearing loss; erode facial canal → facial nerve palsy

### Facial Nerve in Middle Ear
- Runs in the medial then posterior wall; vulnerable during middle ear surgery
- Bell's palsy: LMN facial palsy (idiopathic inflammation of facial nerve in its canal)

### Arnold's Nerve Reflex (Ear-Cough Reflex)
- Stimulation of auricular branch of vagus in external meatus → cough reflex
- Important during ear syringing

### Hearing Loss
- **Conductive:** External/middle ear pathology (wax, TM perforation, otosclerosis)
- **Sensorineural:** Inner ear or CN VIII pathology (presbycusis, acoustic neuroma)
- **Rinne test:** Positive (AC>BC) in normal/SNHL; Negative (BC>AC) in conductive loss
- **Weber test:** Lateralises to affected ear in conductive loss; to normal ear in SNHL
