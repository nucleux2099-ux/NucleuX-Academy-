# Ear Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

External ear (auricle + meatus: lateral 1/3 cartilaginous, medial 2/3 bony), middle ear (tympanic cavity: 6 walls, 3 ossicles, 2 muscles), internal ear (bony labyrinth with perilymph, membranous labyrinth with endolymph; cochlea for hearing, vestibular apparatus for balance). Labyrinthine artery = end artery from basilar/AICA.

## High Yield Points

★ Tympanic membrane: pars tensa + pars flaccida (Shrapnell's); lateral surface = V₃, medial = IX
★ Middle ear roof (tegmen tympani) separates it from middle cranial fossa
★ Stapes = smallest bone in body; footplate in oval window
★ Tensor tympani = V₃; Stapedius = VII (facial nerve)
★ Labyrinthine artery = end artery → sudden SNHL if occluded
★ Perilymph = high Na⁺ (ECF-like); Endolymph = high K⁺ (ICF-like)
★ Organ of Corti in cochlear duct = hearing receptor
★ Arnold's nerve (auricular branch of vagus) → ear-cough reflex
★ Facial nerve runs in medial and posterior walls of middle ear
★ MacEwen's triangle (suprameatal) = surface landmark for mastoid antrum

## Mnemonics

- **Ossicles (lateral to medial):** "MIS" — Malleus, Incus, Stapes
- **Semicircular canals:** "ASL" — Anterior, Superior (same), Lateral (horizontal) — anterior = superior
- **Walls of middle ear:** "TRAMPL" — Tegmental (roof), Roof=tegmen, Anterior, Medial (labyrinthine), Posterior (mastoid), Lateral (membranous), Lower (jugular)
- **Arnold's nerve:** "Arnold makes you cough" (vagus branch in ear)
