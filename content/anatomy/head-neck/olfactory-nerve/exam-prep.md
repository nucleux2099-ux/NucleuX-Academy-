# Olfactory Nerve (CN I) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

CN I: pure sensory (SVA — smell). ~20 olfactory fila through cribriform plate → olfactory bulb → tract → primary olfactory cortex (uncus, piriform cortex). Only CN that doesn't relay through thalamus. Only neurons that regenerate. Anosmia from cribriform plate fracture, olfactory groove meningioma, or infections.

## High Yield Points

★ Not a true peripheral nerve — extension of telencephalon (brain)
★ Olfactory neurons are bipolar; only neurons that regenerate throughout life
★ Passes through cribriform plate (anterior cranial fossa floor)
★ Does NOT relay through thalamus — unique among sensory pathways
★ Primary olfactory cortex: uncus, piriform cortex, entorhinal area (temporal lobe)
★ Olfactory area: roof, superior concha, upper septum of nasal cavity
★ Cribriform plate fracture → anosmia + CSF rhinorrhoea
★ Foster Kennedy syndrome: ipsilateral anosmia + optic atrophy + contralateral papilloedema
★ Uncinate fits: temporal lobe epilepsy → olfactory hallucinations
★ Olfactory fila are unmyelinated → slow conduction

## Mnemonics

- **CN I is unique:** "1st nerve, 1st to smell, 1st to regenerate, 1st to skip thalamus"
- **Foster Kennedy syndrome:** "FKS = Front (olfactory groove) → Kills Sight (optic atrophy ipsilateral) + Swells (papilloedema contralateral)"
- **Olfactory area:** "RSS" — Roof, Superior concha, upper Septum
