# Cranial Nerves Overview — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

12 pairs: I–XII. Sensory only: I, II, VIII. Motor only: III, IV, VI, XI, XII. Mixed: V, VII, IX, X. Parasympathetic: III (ciliary), VII (pterygopalatine, submandibular), IX (otic), X (organ ganglia). CN IV = only nerve from dorsal brainstem. CN VI = longest intracranial course.

## High Yield Points

★ CN IV: only cranial nerve emerging from posterior surface of brainstem; supplies superior oblique
★ CN VI: longest intracranial course → false localising sign in raised ICP
★ Parasympathetic ganglia: ciliary (III), pterygopalatine & submandibular (VII), otic (IX)
★ Superior orbital fissure: III, IV, V₁, VI
★ Jugular foramen: IX, X, XI
★ CN VII UMN vs LMN: forehead spared in UMN (bilateral cortical supply)
★ CN XII LMN lesion: tongue deviates to side of lesion
★ CN III palsy: eye down and out, ptosis, dilated pupil
★ CN I and II are not true peripheral nerves (extensions of brain)
★ Taste: anterior 2/3 = VII (chorda tympani), posterior 1/3 = IX, epiglottic = X
★ All cranial nerves from pontomedullary junction: VI, VII, VIII
