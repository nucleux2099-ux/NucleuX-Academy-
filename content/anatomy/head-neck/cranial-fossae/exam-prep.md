# Cranial Fossae — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Three fossae: ACF (frontal lobes, cribriform plate), MCF (temporal lobes, pituitary fossa, key foramina), PCF (cerebellum, brainstem, foramen magnum). ACF→MCF boundary = lesser wing of sphenoid. MCF→PCF = petrous temporal ridge + dorsum sellae.

## High Yield Points

★ Superior orbital fissure: CN III, IV, V₁, VI + ophthalmic veins
★ Foramen rotundum: V₂ (maxillary); Foramen ovale: V₃ (mandibular); Foramen spinosum: middle meningeal artery
★ Jugular foramen: CN IX, X, XI + sigmoid sinus→IJV
★ Internal acoustic meatus: CN VII + VIII + labyrinthine artery
★ Hypoglossal canal: CN XII
★ Foramen magnum: medulla, vertebral arteries, spinal accessory nerve (ascending)
★ Pterion fracture → middle meningeal artery injury → epidural haematoma
★ Cribriform plate fracture → CSF rhinorrhoea + anosmia
★ Pituitary tumour → bitemporal hemianopia (optic chiasma compression)
★ Middle meningeal artery enters skull via foramen spinosum
★ Foramen lacerum: internal carotid artery crosses its upper part (does NOT pass through it)

## Mnemonics

- **Superior orbital fissure:** "Lazy French Tarts Sit Naked In Anticipation" — Lacrimal (V₁), Frontal (V₁), Trochlear (IV), Superior division of III, Nasociliary (V₁), Inferior division of III, Abducens (VI)
- **Jugular foramen:** "9, 10, 11" — CN IX, X, XI
- **Foramen ovale contents:** "OVALE" — Otic ganglion (below), V₃, Accessory meningeal artery, Lesser petrosal nerve, Emissary veins
