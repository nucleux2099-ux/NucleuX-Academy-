# Cranial Fossae

## Overview

The interior of the base of the skull is divided into three cranial fossae — anterior, middle, and posterior — arranged in a step-like fashion from front to back, each at a progressively lower level. These fossae house the frontal lobes, temporal lobes, and cerebellum/brainstem respectively. Knowledge of the foramina and structures passing through them is essential for understanding cranial nerve pathology and skull base surgery.

## Key Points

- **Anterior cranial fossa:** Houses frontal lobes; floor formed by orbital plates of frontal bone, cribriform plate of ethmoid, lesser wing of sphenoid
- **Middle cranial fossa:** Houses temporal lobes and pituitary; sella turcica, superior orbital fissure, foramen rotundum, ovale, spinosum
- **Posterior cranial fossa:** Houses cerebellum, pons, medulla; foramen magnum, jugular foramen, internal acoustic meatus, hypoglossal canal
- **Boundary ACF–MCF:** Posterior border of lesser wing of sphenoid + anterior clinoid processes
- **Boundary MCF–PCF:** Superior border of petrous temporal bone + dorsum sellae

## Gross Anatomy

### Anterior Cranial Fossa
- **Bones:** Orbital plate of frontal, cribriform plate of ethmoid, lesser wing of sphenoid
- **Key features:** Crista galli (attachment of falx cerebri), cribriform plate (olfactory nerve fibres), foramen caecum (emissary vein)
- **Contents:** Frontal lobes, olfactory bulbs and tracts
- **Floor:** Roof of orbit and nasal cavity

### Middle Cranial Fossa
- **Median part:** Body of sphenoid — sella turcica (pituitary fossa), tuberculum sellae, dorsum sellae, anterior and posterior clinoid processes, chiasmatic groove (optic chiasma)
- **Lateral part (each side):** Greater wing of sphenoid, squamous and petrous temporal bone

#### Foramina and Contents (Lateral Part)
| Foramen | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| Superior orbital fissure | CN III, IV, V₁ (ophthalmic), VI, ophthalmic veins, sympathetic fibres |
| Foramen rotundum | CN V₂ (maxillary nerve) |
| Foramen ovale | CN V₃ (mandibular nerve), accessory meningeal artery, lesser petrosal nerve |
| Foramen spinosum | Middle meningeal artery, meningeal branch of V₃ |
| Foramen lacerum | Internal carotid artery (crosses upper part), greater petrosal nerve |
| Hiatus for greater petrosal nerve | Greater petrosal nerve (from CN VII) |
| Hiatus for lesser petrosal nerve | Lesser petrosal nerve (from CN IX) |

### Posterior Cranial Fossa
- **Bones:** Occipital bone, petrous and mastoid temporal, body of sphenoid (dorsum sellae)
- **Key features:** Foramen magnum, internal occipital protuberance, clivus

#### Foramina and Contents
| Foramen | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| Foramen magnum | Medulla oblongata, vertebral arteries, spinal accessory nerve (ascending), meninges |
| Jugular foramen | CN IX, X, XI; sigmoid sinus → IJV; inferior petrosal sinus |
| Internal acoustic meatus | CN VII, CN VIII, labyrinthine artery |
| Hypoglossal canal | CN XII (hypoglossal nerve) |

- **Internal occipital crest:** Attachment of falx cerebelli
- **Grooves:** Transverse sinus, sigmoid sinus, superior petrosal sinus

## Relations & Surface Markings

- **Pterion:** Meeting of frontal, parietal, temporal, greater wing of sphenoid — overlies anterior branch of middle meningeal artery (MCF)
- **Asterion:** Meeting of parietal, temporal, occipital — overlies transverse–sigmoid sinus junction (PCF)
- **Anterior cranial fossa floor:** Roof of orbit — fracture can cause CSF rhinorrhoea, raccoon eyes
- **Middle cranial fossa floor:** Thin — fracture can cause epidural haematoma from middle meningeal artery

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Meningeal Arteries
- **Anterior cranial fossa:** Anterior meningeal arteries (from anterior ethmoidal artery)
- **Middle cranial fossa:** Middle meningeal artery (from maxillary artery, enters via foramen spinosum) — most important
- **Posterior cranial fossa:** Posterior meningeal artery (from vertebral artery)

### Dural Venous Sinuses
- Superior sagittal sinus, inferior sagittal sinus, straight sinus, transverse sinus, sigmoid sinus, cavernous sinus, superior and inferior petrosal sinuses

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Anterior Cranial Fossa Fracture
- CSF rhinorrhoea (CSF leak through nose via cribriform plate)
- Anosmia (olfactory nerve damage)
- Raccoon eyes (bilateral periorbital ecchymosis)

### Middle Cranial Fossa Fracture
- Epidural haematoma (middle meningeal artery rupture)
- CSF otorrhoea (leak through ear)
- Battle's sign (mastoid ecchymosis)
- CN VII and VIII damage (petrous temporal fracture)

### Posterior Cranial Fossa
- Foramen magnum herniation (tonsillar herniation) → life-threatening brainstem compression
- Jugular foramen syndrome (Vernet's syndrome): CN IX, X, XI palsy
- Cerebellopontine angle tumour (acoustic neuroma): CN VII, VIII involvement

### Pituitary Tumours
- Expand in sella turcica → compress optic chiasma → bitemporal hemianopia
- Lateral expansion into cavernous sinus → CN III, IV, V₁, VI palsies
