# Brain Surface Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Brain: cerebrum (2 hemispheres), cerebellum, brainstem. Each hemisphere has 3 surfaces, 3 poles, 5 lobes (frontal, parietal, temporal, occipital, insula). Central sulcus separates frontal from parietal; lateral sulcus separates temporal. Key functional areas: motor (area 4, precentral), sensory (3,1,2, postcentral), visual (17, calcarine), auditory (41,42, Heschl's), Broca's (44,45), Wernicke's (22).

## High Yield Points

★ Central sulcus separates precentral (motor) from postcentral (sensory) gyrus
★ Lateral sulcus: temporal lobe below, frontal & parietal above
★ Paracentral lobule (medial surface): motor & sensory for lower limb + bladder control
★ Calcarine sulcus walls = primary visual cortex (area 17) — supplied by posterior cerebral artery
★ Middle cerebral artery supplies largest area (superolateral surface)
★ Motor homunculus: face near lateral sulcus, lower limb on medial surface
★ Broca's area (44,45) = expressive speech; Wernicke's (22) = receptive speech
★ Insula = hidden lobe deep to lateral sulcus; gustatory cortex
★ Uncal herniation → CN III compression → ipsilateral mydriasis
★ Corpus callosum parts: rostrum, genu, body, splenium

## Mnemonics

- **Lobes:** "FTPO + I" — Frontal, Temporal, Parietal, Occipital + Insula
- **Corpus callosum parts (ant→post):** "Really Good Body Shape" — Rostrum, Genu, Body, Splenium
- **Brodmann's areas:** "4 (motor), 3-1-2 (sensory), 17 (visual), 41-42 (auditory), 44-45 (Broca), 22 (Wernicke)"
- **MCA territory:** "MCA = Most of the Cortical Area" (superolateral surface)
