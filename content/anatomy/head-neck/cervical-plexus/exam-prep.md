# Cervical Plexus — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Ventral rami C1–C4. Cutaneous branches (lesser occipital, great auricular, transverse cervical, supraclavicular) emerge at Erb's point. Deep branches: ansa cervicalis (infrahyoid muscles except thyrohyoid), phrenic nerve (C3,4,5 — diaphragm). Thyrohyoid supplied by C1 via hypoglossal nerve.

## High Yield Points

★ Erb's point = middle of posterior border of SCM — all cutaneous branches emerge here
★ Great auricular nerve (C2,C3) = largest cutaneous branch of cervical plexus
★ Phrenic nerve (C3,4,5) = sole motor supply to diaphragm; on scalenus anterior
★ "C3,4,5 keeps the diaphragm alive"
★ Ansa cervicalis: superior root (C1), inferior root (C2,3) — supplies sternohyoid, sternothyroid, omohyoid
★ Thyrohyoid supplied by C1 via hypoglossal nerve (NOT ansa cervicalis)
★ Shoulder tip pain = referred from diaphragm via C3,4 (phrenic) = same dermatome as supraclavicular nerves
★ Kehr's sign = left shoulder pain from splenic rupture (diaphragmatic irritation)
★ Phrenic nerve passes between subclavian artery and vein to enter thorax
★ Cervical plexus block at Erb's point for carotid endarterectomy

## Mnemonics

- **Cutaneous branches (superior to inferior):** "LGTS" — Lesser occipital, Great auricular, Transverse cervical, Supraclavicular
- **Phrenic nerve roots:** "C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive"
- **Ansa cervicalis muscles:** "SSTO" — Sternohyoid, Sternothyroid, Thyrohyoid (NO! — by C1 directly), Omohyoid
