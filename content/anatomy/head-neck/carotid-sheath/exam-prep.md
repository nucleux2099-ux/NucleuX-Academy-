# Carotid Sheath — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Tubular condensation of all three layers of deep cervical fascia. Extends from base of skull to root of neck. Contains: common/internal carotid artery (medial), IJV (lateral), vagus nerve (posterior, between the two), deep cervical lymph nodes, ansa cervicalis (anterior wall).

## High Yield Points

★ Formed by contributions from all 3 layers of deep cervical fascia
★ Contents: carotid artery (medial), IJV (lateral), vagus nerve (posterior between them)
★ Thin wall between artery and vein allows IJV expansion
★ Sympathetic trunk lies POSTERIOR to sheath, NOT inside it
★ Ansa cervicalis superior root (C1) lies on the anterior wall
★ Carotid bifurcation at C3-C4 level (upper border of thyroid cartilage)
★ Carotid body = chemoreceptor; carotid sinus = baroreceptor
★ Sheath communicates with posterior mediastinum → route for infection spread
★ Surface marking: along anterior border of SCM
★ IJV cannulation: vein lateral to carotid pulse within the sheath

## Mnemonics

- **Contents (medial to lateral):** "I See A Very Valuable Jewel" — Internal carotid Artery, Vagus, Vein (IJV)
- **"AVN"** — Artery (medial), Vein (lateral), Nerve (posterior)
- **Carotid body vs sinus:** "Body = Chemistry, Sinus = presSure" (chemoreceptor vs baroreceptor)
