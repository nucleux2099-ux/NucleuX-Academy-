# Carotid Sheath

## Overview

The carotid sheath is a condensation of deep cervical fascia that forms a tubular investment around the major neurovascular structures of the neck. It extends from the base of the skull to the root of the neck, running the entire length of the neck. It contains the common carotid artery (and then internal carotid artery), internal jugular vein, vagus nerve, and part of the ansa cervicalis.

## Key Points

- **Composition:** Contributions from all three layers of deep cervical fascia (investing, pretracheal, prevertebral)
- **Extent:** Base of skull (carotid canal and jugular foramen) to the root of the neck (arch of aorta on left, brachiocephalic trunk on right)
- **Contents:** Common/internal carotid artery, internal jugular vein, vagus nerve, deep cervical lymph nodes, ansa cervicalis (part)
- **Arrangement:** Artery medially, vein laterally, vagus nerve posteriorly (between the two)
- **Clinical significance:** Landmark for surgery; related to carotid body and carotid sinus

## Gross Anatomy

### Formation
- The investing layer of deep cervical fascia contributes to the outer covering
- Pretracheal fascia blends anteriorly
- Prevertebral fascia blends posteriorly
- Thin between artery and vein to allow expansion of the IJV

### Contents (from Medial to Lateral)
1. **Common carotid artery** (below) / **Internal carotid artery** (above)
2. **Internal jugular vein** — lateral to the artery
3. **Vagus nerve (CN X)** — in the posterior groove between artery and vein
4. **Deep cervical lymph nodes** — along the IJV
5. **Ansa cervicalis** — superior root embedded in the anterior wall
6. **Sympathetic fibres** — carotid sympathetic plexus on the artery wall

### Extent
- **Superiorly:** Attached to the margins of the carotid canal and jugular foramen at the base of the skull
- **Inferiorly:** Blends with the adventitia of the aortic arch and subclavian vessels

## Relations & Surface Markings

### Relations
- **Anteriorly:** SCM, investing fascia, infrahyoid muscles
- **Posteriorly:** Prevertebral muscles, prevertebral fascia, sympathetic trunk
- **Medially:** Thyroid gland, trachea, oesophagus, recurrent laryngeal nerve, pharynx, larynx
- **Laterally:** SCM

### Surface Marking
- Along the anterior border of SCM from the sternoclavicular joint to the ear lobule
- Corresponds to a line from the sternoclavicular joint to a point midway between the tip of the mastoid process and the angle of the mandible

### At Different Levels
- **At C3-C4:** Common carotid bifurcation into internal and external carotid arteries
- **Carotid sinus and body:** At the bifurcation level

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Structures Supplying the Sheath
- Vasa vasorum of the carotid artery and IJV supply the sheath walls
- Sympathetic trunk lies just posterior to the sheath

### Important Neural Relations
- **Vagus nerve:** Within the sheath
- **Sympathetic trunk:** Just posterior to the sheath (not inside)
- **Ansa cervicalis:** Superior root (C1) runs on the anterior wall of the sheath
- **Hypoglossal nerve:** Crosses the internal and external carotid arteries superficially

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Carotid Body and Sinus
- **Carotid body:** Chemoreceptor at the bifurcation; senses O₂, CO₂, pH
- **Carotid sinus:** Baroreceptor in the wall of the internal carotid artery; senses blood pressure
- **Carotid sinus massage:** Stimulates baroreceptors → bradycardia (vagal reflex); used to terminate SVT

### Carotid Endarterectomy
- Surgical removal of atherosclerotic plaque from the common/internal carotid artery
- Requires careful identification of structures within the sheath

### Internal Jugular Vein Cannulation
- Landmark-based or ultrasound-guided
- IJV lies lateral to the carotid artery within the sheath
- Complication: carotid artery puncture, pneumothorax, vagus nerve injury

### Spread of Infection
- The carotid sheath communicates with the posterior mediastinum
- Infection in the neck can track down along the sheath into the mediastinum → mediastinitis

### Carotid Sheath Block
- Local anaesthetic injected around the sheath for vagal and glossopharyngeal blocks
