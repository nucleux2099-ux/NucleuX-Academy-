# Cervical Fascia

## Overview

The cervical fascia is the connective tissue framework of the neck that encloses muscles, vessels, nerves, and viscera. It is divided into superficial cervical fascia (subcutaneous tissue) and deep cervical fascia. The deep cervical fascia has three layers: investing layer, pretracheal layer, and prevertebral layer. These fascial layers create compartments that determine the spread of infection and guide surgical planes.

## Key Points

- **Superficial cervical fascia:** Subcutaneous tissue containing platysma, cutaneous nerves, superficial veins, superficial lymph nodes
- **Deep cervical fascia:** Three layers — investing, pretracheal, prevertebral
- **Investing layer:** Encloses SCM and trapezius; forms roof of posterior triangle
- **Pretracheal layer:** Has muscular and visceral parts; encloses thyroid gland (forming false capsule)
- **Prevertebral layer:** Covers prevertebral muscles; extends laterally as axillary sheath
- **Carotid sheath:** Receives contributions from all three layers

## Gross Anatomy

### Superficial Cervical Fascia
- Thin layer of subcutaneous connective tissue
- Contains platysma muscle, external jugular vein, cutaneous nerves, superficial lymph nodes
- Continuous with superficial fascia of face, thorax, and axilla

### Deep Cervical Fascia

#### 1. Investing Layer (Most Superficial)
- **Attachments:** Superiorly — superior nuchal line, mastoid process, zygomatic arch, lower border of mandible; Inferiorly — spine of scapula, acromion, clavicle, manubrium
- **Encloses:** SCM, trapezius, parotid gland, submandibular gland
- **Forms:** Roof of posterior triangle, roof of anterior triangle
- **Splits to form:** Suprasternal space of Burns (between sternal heads of SCM), contains sternal heads of SCM, jugular venous arch, lymph node

#### 2. Pretracheal Layer (Middle Layer)
- **Muscular part:** Encloses infrahyoid muscles (sternohyoid, sternothyroid, thyrohyoid, omohyoid)
- **Visceral part:** Encloses thyroid gland, trachea, oesophagus, pharynx
  - Forms the **false capsule of thyroid** (distinct from true capsule)
  - Ligament of Berry — thickened pretracheal fascia attaching thyroid to tracheal rings
- **Extent:** Hyoid bone → superior mediastinum (blends with fibrous pericardium)
- **Buccopharyngeal fascia:** Covers the posterior surface of pharynx and oesophagus

#### 3. Prevertebral Layer (Deepest)
- **Covers:** Prevertebral muscles (longus colli, longus capitis, scalenus anterior, medius, posterior), vertebral column
- **Extends laterally:** As the axillary sheath (enclosing brachial plexus and axillary vessels)
- **Alar fascia:** Thin fascial plane between the prevertebral fascia and buccopharyngeal fascia; extends from base of skull to T2 vertebra
- **Danger space:** Between alar fascia and prevertebral fascia proper; extends to the diaphragm

### Fascial Spaces
- **Retropharyngeal space:** Between buccopharyngeal fascia and alar fascia; base of skull to T2 (upper mediastinum)
- **Danger space (space 4):** Between alar fascia and prevertebral fascia; extends to diaphragm — route for spread of infection to posterior mediastinum
- **Suprasternal space of Burns:** Between the two layers of investing fascia at the suprasternal notch

## Relations & Surface Markings

- Investing layer palpable as taut fascia in the posterior triangle
- Pretracheal fascia attached to oblique line of thyroid cartilage superiorly
- All layers can be identified during surgical dissection of the neck
- Fascial planes guide the surgeon during thyroidectomy, tracheostomy, and neck exploration

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

- Fascial layers are relatively avascular — important for surgical dissection
- Cutaneous nerves of the cervical plexus pierce the investing fascia at Erb's point (posterior border of SCM at junction of upper 1/3 and lower 2/3)
- External jugular vein pierces the investing layer above the clavicle

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Spread of Infection
- **Retropharyngeal abscess:** Pus between pharynx and prevertebral fascia; can track to superior mediastinum → bulging of posterior pharyngeal wall, dysphagia, stridor
- **Via danger space:** Infection can extend from neck to posterior mediastinum → mediastinitis
- **Ludwig's angina:** Infection in submandibular space → bilateral sublingual and submandibular space involvement → airway compromise

### False Capsule of Thyroid
- Formed by pretracheal fascia
- Contains parathyroid glands between true and false capsules
- Surgeon must go between true and false capsule during thyroidectomy to avoid injury to parathyroids

### Ligament of Berry
- Attaches thyroid to 2nd–3rd tracheal rings
- Recurrent laryngeal nerve passes close to or through it — risk of injury during thyroidectomy

### Investing Layer and Surgery
- Must be incised to access the posterior triangle
- Prevents spread of superficial infections into the deep neck
