# Cervical Fascia — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Deep cervical fascia has 3 layers: investing (encloses SCM, trapezius), pretracheal (muscular + visceral; encloses thyroid — false capsule), prevertebral (covers prevertebral muscles; extends as axillary sheath). Carotid sheath formed by all 3. Key spaces: retropharyngeal, danger space (to posterior mediastinum).

## High Yield Points

★ Investing layer encloses SCM and trapezius; forms roof of posterior triangle
★ Pretracheal fascia forms false capsule of thyroid gland
★ Parathyroids lie between true and false capsules of thyroid
★ Ligament of Berry: pretracheal fascia attaching thyroid to tracheal rings; RLN at risk
★ Prevertebral fascia extends laterally as axillary sheath
★ Retropharyngeal space: between buccopharyngeal and alar fascia — to upper mediastinum
★ Danger space: between alar and prevertebral fascia — to posterior mediastinum (to diaphragm)
★ Suprasternal space of Burns: between investing layer splits at manubrium
★ Carotid sheath receives contributions from all 3 deep layers
★ Erb's point: cutaneous nerves pierce investing layer at posterior border of SCM

## Mnemonics

- **Layers (superficial to deep):** "I Put Patients first" — Investing, Pretracheal, Prevertebral
- **Investing layer encloses:** "SPIT" — SCM, Parotid, (Investing layer), Trapezius
- **Retropharyngeal vs Danger space:** "Retro stops at T2; Danger goes to Diaphragm"
