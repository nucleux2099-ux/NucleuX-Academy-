# Eye Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Three coats: fibrous (sclera+cornea), vascular/uveal (choroid, ciliary body, iris), nervous (retina). Three chambers: anterior, posterior (aqueous humour), vitreous. Central retinal artery = end artery. Aqueous: produced by ciliary body → posterior chamber → anterior chamber → canal of Schlemm. Fovea = maximum acuity (cones only).

## High Yield Points

★ Cornea is avascular; most powerful refracting medium (~43D); supplied by nasociliary nerve (V₁)
★ Central retinal artery = END ARTERY; occlusion → sudden painless blindness + cherry-red spot
★ Sphincter pupillae = parasympathetic (III) = miosis; Dilator pupillae = sympathetic = mydriasis
★ Aqueous humour drainage: trabecular meshwork → canal of Schlemm; block → glaucoma
★ Fovea centralis: thinnest retina, cones only, maximum visual acuity
★ Optic disc = blind spot; no photoreceptors; where CRA enters and CRV exits
★ Ciliary muscle contraction (parasympathetic) → lens becomes convex → accommodation
★ Rods = dim light (scotopic); Cones = colour/bright light (photopic)
★ Pupillary light reflex: afferent CN II, efferent CN III
★ Papilloedema = raised ICP → optic disc swelling (CRV compression)

## Mnemonics

- **Extraocular muscles by CN:** "LR₆SO₄R₃" — Lateral Rectus = VI, Superior Oblique = IV, Rest = III
- **Corneal layers:** "EBSDE" — Epithelium, Bowman's, Stroma, Descemet's, Endothelium
- **Aqueous flow:** "Ciliary body → Posterior chamber → Pupil → Anterior chamber → Canal of Schlemm"
- **"RAPID" for CRAO:** Retinal Artery Pale, Infarction, cherry-reD spot
