# Accessory Nerve (CN XI)

## Overview

The accessory nerve (cranial nerve XI) is a unique cranial nerve with both cranial and spinal roots. It is primarily a motor nerve that supplies the sternocleidomastoid (SCM) and trapezius muscles. The spinal root arises from the upper five cervical segments of the spinal cord, while the cranial root arises from the nucleus ambiguus in the medulla oblongata. The cranial root joins the vagus nerve and is distributed through its pharyngeal and recurrent laryngeal branches.

## Key Points

- **Type:** Motor nerve (branchiomotor — spinal part; special visceral efferent — cranial part)
- **Cranial Root:** Arises from nucleus ambiguus; joins the vagus nerve
- **Spinal Root:** Arises from the lateral aspect of the upper 5 cervical spinal cord segments
- **Entry into Skull:** Spinal root enters through the foramen magnum
- **Exit from Skull:** Jugular foramen (middle compartment)
- **Motor Supply:** SCM and trapezius (spinal root); laryngeal muscles via vagus (cranial root)
- **Testing:** Shrug shoulders (trapezius) and turn head against resistance (SCM)

## Gross Anatomy

### Origin
- **Cranial root:** 4–5 rootlets from the lateral surface of the medulla, below the vagus rootlets; nucleus ambiguus
- **Spinal root:** Rootlets from the lateral aspect of C1–C5 spinal cord segments; spinal nucleus of accessory nerve (in lateral horn)

### Course
1. Spinal rootlets ascend through the foramen magnum behind the vertebral artery
2. Cranial and spinal roots unite briefly near the jugular foramen
3. Exit through the jugular foramen (between vagus and spinal root)
4. Cranial root separates and joins the vagus nerve (internal ramus)
5. Spinal root (external ramus) descends, crosses the internal jugular vein, enters SCM
6. Crosses the posterior triangle superficially on levator scapulae
7. Passes deep to the anterior border of trapezius

### Parts
- **Internal ramus (cranial root):** Joins vagus → pharyngeal and recurrent laryngeal branches
- **External ramus (spinal root):** Supplies SCM and trapezius

## Relations & Surface Markings

### In the Posterior Triangle
- Superficial to levator scapulae and the prevertebral fascia
- Deep to the investing layer of deep cervical fascia
- Crosses the triangle from the posterior border of SCM (junction of upper 1/3 and lower 2/3) to the anterior border of trapezius (junction of upper 1/3 and lower 2/3)

### Surface Marking
- A line from the middle of the posterior border of SCM to the junction of the upper 1/3 and lower 2/3 of the anterior border of trapezius

### Important Relations
- At jugular foramen: between vagus and spinal root, near IJV
- Posterior triangle: crossed by superficial cervical artery and suprascapular artery
- Related to the occipital artery at the apex of the posterior triangle

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Blood Supply
- Branches of the occipital artery and ascending cervical artery accompany the nerve in the posterior triangle

### Nerve Supply (to accessory nerve itself)
- Proprioceptive fibres from C2, C3, C4 join the spinal accessory nerve in the posterior triangle
- C2 fibres to SCM; C3, C4 fibres to trapezius

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Accessory Nerve Palsy
- **Cause:** Surgical injury during lymph node biopsy in posterior triangle, radical neck dissection
- **Effects:**
  - Weakness of SCM — difficulty turning head to opposite side
  - Trapezius paralysis — drooping of shoulder, winging of scapula (lateral winging), difficulty in abduction beyond 90°
  - Shoulder asymmetry, difficulty combing hair

### Torticollis (Wry Neck)
- Spasm or contracture of SCM → head tilted to same side, chin rotated to opposite side
- Congenital torticollis due to fibrosis of SCM from birth trauma

### Spinal Accessory Nerve in Surgery
- Most commonly injured nerve in posterior triangle surgery
- Must be identified and preserved during lymph node biopsy, radical neck dissection
- Nerve is superficial and vulnerable in the posterior triangle

### Testing CN XI
- **SCM:** Turn head against resistance to opposite side; palpate SCM
- **Trapezius:** Shrug shoulders against resistance
