# Accessory Nerve (CN XI) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

CN XI has cranial and spinal roots. Cranial root joins vagus; spinal root (C1–C5) enters skull via foramen magnum, exits via jugular foramen, supplies SCM and trapezius. Most vulnerable nerve in posterior triangle surgery.

## High Yield Points

★ Spinal root arises from C1–C5 lateral spinal cord; enters skull via foramen magnum
★ Exits through jugular foramen (middle compartment)
★ Cranial root joins vagus → supplies laryngeal muscles via recurrent laryngeal nerve
★ Spinal root (external ramus) supplies SCM then trapezius
★ Crosses posterior triangle superficially — most commonly injured nerve here
★ Surface marking: posterior border of SCM (middle) → anterior border of trapezius (junction upper 1/3 & lower 2/3)
★ Injury → shoulder drop, lateral winging of scapula, difficulty abducting arm >90°
★ SCM testing: turn head to opposite side against resistance
★ Trapezius testing: shrug shoulders against resistance
★ C2 proprioceptive fibres to SCM; C3, C4 to trapezius
★ Torticollis = SCM spasm/contracture → head tilted ipsilaterally, chin to opposite side

## Mnemonics

- **"Some Tough nerves Cross Posteriorly"** — Spinal accessory, Trapezius, Crosses Posterior triangle
- **Jugular foramen contents:** "JIGSAW" — Jugular bulb (IJV), IX (glossopharyngeal), X (vagus), Spinal Accessory (XI), Sigmoid → IJV
- **SCM actions:** "SCROLL" — SCM Contralateral Rotation, ipsiLateral Lateral flexion
