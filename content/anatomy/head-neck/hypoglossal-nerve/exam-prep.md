# Hypoglossal Nerve (CN XII) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Pure motor nerve (GSE). Supplies all tongue muscles except palatoglossus (vagus). Exits via hypoglossal canal. Crosses superficial to hyoglossus. C1 fibres hitchhike with it (to thyrohyoid, geniohyoid, superior root of ansa cervicalis). LMN lesion: tongue deviates TO side of lesion with atrophy/fasciculations.

## High Yield Points

★ Supplies all intrinsic + all extrinsic muscles of tongue EXCEPT palatoglossus
★ Palatoglossus supplied by vagus via pharyngeal plexus
★ Exits through hypoglossal canal (occipital bone)
★ Emerges between pyramid and olive (anterolateral sulcus of medulla)
★ LMN lesion: tongue deviates TO side of lesion ("licks the wound")
★ UMN lesion: tongue deviates AWAY from cortical lesion (to contralateral side)
★ C1 fibres travel with CN XII → superior root of ansa cervicalis, thyrohyoid, geniohyoid
★ Genioglossus = main protruder of tongue; unopposed action causes deviation
★ Crosses superficial to hyoglossus (lingual artery deep to hyoglossus)
★ At risk during carotid endarterectomy and neck dissection

## Mnemonics

- **Tongue deviation (LMN):** "The tongue Licks the Lesion" — deviates to same side
- **Muscles of tongue (extrinsic):** "She Goes Places Happily" — Styloglossus, Genioglossus, Palatoglossus (NOT XII), Hyoglossus
- **C1 hitchhikers on XII:** "THyroHyoid, GenioHyoid, Superior root of Ansa" = "Those Grateful Servants"
- **Intrinsic muscles:** "SITS" — Superior longitudinal, Inferior longitudinal, Transverse, Vertical (S for shape-change)
