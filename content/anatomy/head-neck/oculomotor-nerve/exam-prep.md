# Oculomotor Nerve (CN III) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

CN III: motor to SR, IR, MR, IO, LPS + parasympathetic (Edinger-Westphal → ciliary ganglion → sphincter pupillae + ciliary muscle). Emerges at interpeduncular fossa of midbrain. Passes between PCA and SCA. In lateral wall of cavernous sinus (most superior). Complete palsy: ptosis, eye down and out, fixed dilated pupil.

## High Yield Points

★ Supplies all extraocular muscles EXCEPT lateral rectus (VI) and superior oblique (IV) — "LR₆SO₄R₃"
★ Parasympathetic fibres are SUPERFICIAL in the nerve → compressed first in surgical palsy (pupil involved early)
★ Medical/ischaemic CN III palsy: pupil SPARED (diabetes, HTN)
★ Surgical/compressive palsy: pupil involved FIRST (aneurysm, uncal herniation)
★ Posterior communicating artery aneurysm = commonest cause of compressive CN III palsy
★ Emerges from interpeduncular fossa; passes between PCA (above) and SCA (below)
★ Superior division: SR + LPS; Inferior division: MR, IR, IO + parasympathetic branch
★ Weber's syndrome: ipsilateral CN III palsy + contralateral hemiplegia (midbrain)
★ Edinger-Westphal nucleus = parasympathetic nucleus for CN III
★ CN III palsy → eye "down and out" (LR pulls laterally, SO pulls down)

## Mnemonics

- **"LR₆SO₄R₃"** — Lateral Rectus = VI, Superior Oblique = IV, Rest = III
- **CN III palsy position:** "Down and Out, Ptosis, Pupil dilated" = "DOP"
- **Cavernous sinus wall order:** "O TOM CAT" — III (Oculomotor), IV (Trochlear), V₁ (Ophthalmic), V₂ (Maxillary); VI (Carotid, Abducens, together inside)
- **Surgical vs Medical:** "Surgical = Surface (pupil first); Medical = Middle (pupil spared)"
