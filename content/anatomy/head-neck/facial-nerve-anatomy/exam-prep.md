# Facial Nerve Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

CN VII: motor (2nd arch muscles), taste (anterior 2/3 tongue via chorda tympani), parasympathetic (submandibular, sublingual, lacrimal glands). Course: pons → IAM → facial canal (labyrinthine→tympanic→mastoid) → stylomastoid foramen → parotid → 5 branches. Greater petrosal nerve from geniculate ganglion; chorda tympani from mastoid segment.

## High Yield Points

★ Facial nerve loops around abducens nucleus forming facial colliculus in 4th ventricle floor
★ Geniculate ganglion = sensory ganglion at the bend; gives off greater petrosal nerve
★ Greater petrosal nerve → pterygopalatine ganglion → lacrimal, nasal, palatal glands
★ Chorda tympani: taste anterior 2/3 tongue + parasympathetic to submandibular & sublingual glands
★ Chorda tympani joins lingual nerve (V₃) in infratemporal fossa
★ CN VII does NOT supply the parotid gland (CN IX does via otic ganglion)
★ 5 terminal branches: Temporal, Zygomatic, Buccal, Marginal mandibular, Cervical
★ Labyrinthine segment = narrowest → most common site of Bell's palsy
★ Localise lesion by which functions are lost (lacrimation, stapedius reflex, taste, motor)
★ Crocodile tears = aberrant regeneration of parasympathetic fibres to lacrimal gland

## Mnemonics

- **Branches:** "To Zanzibar By Motor Car" — Temporal, Zygomatic, Buccal, Marginal mandibular, Cervical
- **Facial canal segments:** "LTM" — Labyrinthine, Tympanic, Mastoid
- **Chorda tympani carries:** "Taste and Salivation" (SVA + GVE)
- **Bell's palsy site:** "Bell rings in the LAByrinth" (labyrinthine segment)
