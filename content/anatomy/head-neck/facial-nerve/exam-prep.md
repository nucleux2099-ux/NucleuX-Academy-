# Facial Nerve — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

CN VII: 2nd pharyngeal arch nerve. Motor root (muscles of facial expression) + nervus intermedius (taste anterior 2/3 tongue + parasympathetic). Course: CPA → IAM → facial canal (labyrinthine/tympanic/mastoid) → stylomastoid foramen → parotid → 5 branches. Key branches in canal: greater petrosal nerve, nerve to stapedius, chorda tympani.

## High Yield Points

★ Nervus intermedius carries taste (SVA) and parasympathetic (GVE) fibres
★ Greater petrosal nerve: from geniculate ganglion → pterygopalatine ganglion → lacrimal gland
★ Chorda tympani: taste (anterior 2/3 tongue) + parasympathetic (submandibular & sublingual glands)
★ Facial nerve divides parotid into superficial and deep lobes but does NOT supply it
★ Bell's palsy: idiopathic LMN facial palsy; labyrinthine segment (narrowest) most affected
★ Ramsay Hunt syndrome: herpes zoster of geniculate ganglion
★ Tympanic segment often dehiscent → risk during middle ear surgery
★ UMN palsy spares forehead; LMN palsy affects entire ipsilateral face
★ Stapedius paralysis → hyperacusis (sensitivity to loud sounds)
★ Marginal mandibular nerve at risk in submandibular surgery

## Mnemonics

- **Branches:** "To Zanzibar By Motor Car" — T, Z, B, M, C
- **Canal branches (proximal to distal):** "Greater petrosal → Stapedius → Chorda tympani" = "Great Singers Can perform"
- **Chorda tympani path:** "Chorda → Crosses middle ear → Joins Lingual nerve"
- **Bell's palsy recovery:** "85% recover spontaneously"
