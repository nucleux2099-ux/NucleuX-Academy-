# Facial Muscles — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

All muscles of facial expression arise from 2nd pharyngeal arch, supplied by CN VII. Insert into skin (not bone). Key muscles: orbicularis oculi (eye sphincter), orbicularis oris (mouth sphincter), buccinator (cheek — keeps food between teeth). No deep fascia over face. UMN facial palsy spares forehead; LMN affects entire ipsilateral face.

## High Yield Points

★ ALL muscles of facial expression supplied by facial nerve (CN VII)
★ Buccinator: muscle of facial expression, NOT mastication; pierced by parotid duct
★ Buccal branch of VII (motor) ≠ buccal nerve of V₃ (sensory)
★ Orbicularis oculi: orbital part = forceful closure; palpebral part = blinking
★ UMN facial palsy: lower face only (contralateral); LMN: entire face (ipsilateral)
★ Bell's phenomenon: upward rolling of eyeball on attempted closure (LMN palsy)
★ No deep fascia over face — aids spread of infection
★ Dangerous area of face: angular vein → ophthalmic veins → cavernous sinus (no valves)
★ Marginal mandibular nerve at risk in submandibular surgery
★ Platysma supplied by cervical branch of VII

## Mnemonics

- **Branches of facial nerve (parotid):** "To Zanzibar By Motor Car" — Temporal, Zygomatic, Buccal, Marginal mandibular, Cervical
- **Buccinator facts:** "Buccinator = Bugle player muscle; pierced by parotid duct; NOT a muscle of mastication"
- **UMN vs LMN:** "Upper spares Upper (forehead); Lower affects Lower face only in UMN"
