# Facial Muscles (Muscles of Facial Expression)

## Overview

The muscles of facial expression are subcutaneous muscles that arise from bone or fascia of the skull and insert into the skin of the face. They are all derived from the second pharyngeal (hyoid) arch and are therefore supplied by the facial nerve (CN VII). They are grouped around the orifices of the face — orbit, nose, mouth, and ear — and function in facial expression, eye closure, and oral competence. The platysma, although in the neck, is also a muscle of facial expression.

## Key Points

- **Embryological origin:** Second pharyngeal arch mesoderm
- **Nerve supply:** ALL supplied by facial nerve (CN VII) — temporal, zygomatic, buccal, marginal mandibular, cervical branches
- **Insertion:** Into skin (not bone) — hence move skin to produce expressions
- **No deep fascia** over the face (unlike rest of the body)
- **Key muscles:** Orbicularis oculi, orbicularis oris, buccinator, frontalis, platysma

## Gross Anatomy

### Muscles Around the Eye (Orbital Group)
- **Orbicularis oculi:**
  - Orbital part: forceful closure (tight eye closure)
  - Palpebral part: gentle closure (blinking)
  - Lacrimal part (Horner's muscle): aids lacrimal drainage
  - Sphincter of the eye; supplied by temporal and zygomatic branches of VII
- **Corrugator supercilii:** Draws eyebrow medially and downward (frowning)

### Muscles Around the Nose (Nasal Group)
- **Nasalis:** Compressor and dilator parts; flaring of nostrils
- **Procerus:** Pulls skin between eyebrows downward (transverse wrinkles on nose bridge)

### Muscles Around the Mouth (Oral Group)
- **Orbicularis oris:** Sphincter of the mouth; closes lips, purses lips
- **Buccinator:** Deep muscle of cheek; keeps food between teeth during chewing; forms the basis of cheek
  - Pierced by parotid duct (Stensen's duct)
  - Nerve supply: buccal branch of CN VII (NOT buccal nerve of V₃ which is sensory)
- **Levator labii superioris, zygomaticus major and minor:** Elevate upper lip, smile
- **Depressor labii inferioris, depressor anguli oris:** Depress lower lip
- **Mentalis:** Elevates chin skin, protrudes lower lip (pouting)
- **Risorius:** Retracts angle of mouth (grinning)
- **Levator labii superioris alaeque nasi:** Elevates upper lip and dilates nostril

### Muscles of Scalp and Ear
- **Occipitofrontalis (epicranius):** Frontal belly raises eyebrows (surprise); occipital belly retracts scalp
  - Connected by galea aponeurotica
- **Auricular muscles:** Anterior, superior, posterior — vestigial in humans

### Platysma
- Broad thin sheet in superficial fascia of neck
- Tenses skin of neck; depresses mandible and angle of mouth
- Supplied by cervical branch of CN VII

## Relations & Surface Markings

- Facial muscles lie in the superficial fascia (subcutaneous plane)
- No deep fascia over the face — hence facial infections spread easily
- Buccinator lies deep to other oral muscles, directly on the buccal mucosa
- Parotid duct pierces buccinator opposite the upper 2nd molar tooth
- Facial nerve branches emerge from the parotid gland and fan across the face

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Blood Supply
- Facial artery (main supply) — submental, inferior and superior labial, lateral nasal, angular branches
- Superficial temporal artery — scalp and temporal region
- Transverse facial artery — cheek region

### Nerve Supply
- **Motor:** Facial nerve (CN VII) — ALL muscles of facial expression
  - 5 terminal branches: Temporal, Zygomatic, Buccal, Marginal mandibular, Cervical
- **Sensory:** Trigeminal nerve (V) provides cutaneous sensation to overlying skin (NOT motor to these muscles)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Facial Nerve Palsy
- **LMN (Bell's palsy):** Entire ipsilateral face paralysed; cannot close eye, wrinkle forehead, whistle, blow
- **UMN (stroke):** Contralateral lower face affected; forehead spared (bilateral cortical supply to frontalis)
- **Bell's phenomenon:** On attempted eye closure, eyeball rolls upward (visible in LMN palsy)

### Buccinator
- Paralysis → food collects in vestibule of mouth (between teeth and cheek)
- Often tested in viva as "muscle of mastication" — it is NOT; it is a muscle of facial expression

### Marginal Mandibular Nerve
- Vulnerable during submandibular gland surgery — runs below the mandible, superficial to facial artery
- Injury → inability to depress lower lip on that side

### Dangerous Area of Face
- Veins of the face (angular vein → ophthalmic veins → cavernous sinus) have no valves
- Infections of the nose/upper lip can spread to cavernous sinus → cavernous sinus thrombosis
