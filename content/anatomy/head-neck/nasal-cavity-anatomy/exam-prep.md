# Nasal Cavity Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary

Nasal cavity: roof (cribriform plate), floor (hard palate), medial wall (septum: septal cartilage, perpendicular plate of ethmoid, vomer), lateral wall (3 conchae + meatuses). Most sinuses drain into middle meatus. Kiesselbach's plexus (Little's area) on anteroinferior septum = commonest epistaxis site. Sphenopalatine artery = main arterial supply.

## High Yield Points

★ Nasal septum: septal cartilage + perpendicular plate of ethmoid + vomer
★ Little's area (Kiesselbach's plexus): anteroinferior septum; 90% epistaxis here
★ Kiesselbach's plexus: 4 arteries — anterior ethmoidal, sphenopalatine, greater palatine, superior labial
★ Middle meatus: drainage of maxillary, frontal, anterior ethmoidal sinuses
★ Nasolacrimal duct opens into inferior meatus
★ Sphenoid sinus opens into sphenoethmoidal recess (above superior concha)
★ Cribriform plate fracture → CSF rhinorrhoea + anosmia
★ Maxillary sinus drains via hiatus semilunaris in middle meatus
★ Sphenopalatine artery = main arterial supply of nasal cavity
★ Olfactory area: upper part (superior concha, upper septum, roof)

## Mnemonics

- **Septum components:** "Very Perpendicular Septum" — Vomer, Perpendicular plate of ethmoid, Septal cartilage
- **Kiesselbach's arteries:** "SAGE" — Sphenopalatine, Anterior ethmoidal, Greater palatine, External carotid (superior labial from facial)
- **Middle meatus drains:** "MFA" — Maxillary, Frontal, Anterior ethmoidal
- **Inferior meatus:** "NLD" — Nasolacrimal Duct (only thing that opens here)
