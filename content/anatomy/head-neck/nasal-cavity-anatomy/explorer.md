# Nasal Cavity Anatomy

## Overview

The nasal cavity is a large air-filled space above and behind the nose, divided into right and left halves by the nasal septum. It extends from the nostrils (nares) anteriorly to the choanae (posterior nasal apertures) posteriorly, where it opens into the nasopharynx. The nasal cavity functions in respiration (warming, humidifying, filtering air), olfaction, and resonance of voice. The lateral wall is characterised by three conchae (turbinates) with corresponding meatuses beneath them.

## Key Points

- **Boundaries:** Roof (cribriform plate), floor (hard palate), medial wall (nasal septum), lateral wall (3 conchae + meatuses)
- **Nasal septum:** Septal cartilage, perpendicular plate of ethmoid, vomer
- **Conchae:** Superior, middle (both parts of ethmoid), inferior (separate bone)
- **Paranasal sinuses:** Maxillary, frontal, anterior ethmoidal → drain into middle meatus; posterior ethmoidal → superior meatus; sphenoid → sphenoethmoidal recess
- **Blood supply:** Kiesselbach's plexus (Little's area) on anteroinferior septum — commonest site of epistaxis

## Gross Anatomy

### Walls of the Nasal Cavity

#### Roof (narrow)
- Nasal bone, cribriform plate of ethmoid (olfactory nerve fibres pass through), body of sphenoid

#### Floor (wider)
- Hard palate: palatine process of maxilla (anterior 3/4) + horizontal plate of palatine bone (posterior 1/4)
- Incisive canal transmits nasopalatine nerve and greater palatine artery

#### Medial Wall (Nasal Septum)
- **Bony part:** Perpendicular plate of ethmoid (upper), vomer (posteroinferior)
- **Cartilaginous part:** Septal (quadrangular) cartilage (anteroinferior)
- **Membranous part:** Between columella and septal cartilage (anteroinferior)

#### Lateral Wall (Most Complex)
- **Three conchae (turbinates):** Superior, middle (ethmoid), inferior (separate bone)
- **Three meatuses** (spaces below each concha):
  - **Inferior meatus:** Nasolacrimal duct opening
  - **Middle meatus:** Contains:
    - Hiatus semilunaris (crescentic groove)
    - Ethmoidal bulla (bulge of middle ethmoidal air cells)
    - Ethmoidal infundibulum (drains frontal sinus)
    - Openings of: maxillary sinus, frontal sinus (via infundibulum), anterior ethmoidal sinuses
  - **Superior meatus:** Posterior ethmoidal sinus opening
- **Sphenoethmoidal recess:** Above superior concha; sphenoid sinus opens here

### Paranasal Sinuses Drainage Summary
| Sinus | Drains Into |
|-------|-------------|
| Maxillary | Middle meatus (hiatus semilunaris) |
| Frontal | Middle meatus (via ethmoidal infundibulum) |
| Anterior ethmoidal | Middle meatus (into/on ethmoidal bulla) |
| Middle ethmoidal | Middle meatus (on ethmoidal bulla) |
| Posterior ethmoidal | Superior meatus |
| Sphenoid | Sphenoethmoidal recess |

### Nasal Septum Blood Supply (Kiesselbach's Plexus / Little's Area)
Located on anteroinferior part of septum. Anastomosis of:
1. Anterior ethmoidal artery (from ophthalmic artery)
2. Sphenopalatine artery (from maxillary artery) — main arterial supply
3. Greater palatine artery (from maxillary artery)
4. Superior labial artery (from facial artery)
5. Septal branch of anterior ethmoidal

## Relations & Surface Markings

- Cribriform plate: floor of anterior cranial fossa — fracture → CSF rhinorrhoea
- Floor: roof of oral cavity
- Lateral wall: maxillary sinus (below middle concha), ethmoid sinuses (between orbits)
- Nasopharynx communicates posteriorly through choanae
- Eustachian tube opening in lateral wall of nasopharynx (behind posterior end of inferior concha)

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Arterial Supply
- **Sphenopalatine artery** (from 3rd part of maxillary artery): main supply to lateral wall and septum
- **Anterior and posterior ethmoidal arteries** (from ophthalmic artery): supply upper lateral wall and septum
- **Greater palatine artery** and **superior labial artery**: supplement septal supply

### Venous Drainage
- Sphenopalatine vein → pterygoid venous plexus → cavernous sinus (infection route)
- Anterior facial vein; ophthalmic veins

### Nerve Supply
- **Olfactory (CN I):** Olfactory area (upper part — superior concha, upper septum, roof)
- **General sensation:**
  - Anterosuperior: anterior ethmoidal nerve (V₁)
  - Posteroinferior: nasopalatine nerve, greater and lesser palatine nerves, posterior superior nasal nerves (all from V₂ via pterygopalatine ganglion)
- **Secretomotor (parasympathetic):** CN VII → greater petrosal nerve → pterygopalatine ganglion → nasal glands

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Epistaxis (Nosebleed)
- **90% anterior:** From Little's area (Kiesselbach's plexus) — managed by pressure/cautery/packing
- **10% posterior:** From sphenopalatine artery — severe, may need posterior packing or arterial ligation/embolisation

### CSF Rhinorrhoea
- Fracture of cribriform plate → CSF leaks through nose
- Risk of ascending meningitis

### Deviated Nasal Septum
- Common; may obstruct nasal airway → septoplasty

### Sinusitis
- Maxillary sinus most commonly infected (ostium is high on medial wall → poor gravity drainage)
- Middle meatus is the key area — ostiomeatal complex

### Nasal Polyps
- Arise from mucosa of middle meatus/ethmoidal region
- Cause nasal obstruction; associated with aspirin sensitivity and asthma (Samter's triad)
