# Lungs and Bronchial Tree

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Moore's Clinically Oriented Anatomy 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The lungs are paired organs of respiration located in the thoracic cavity. The **right lung has 3 lobes** (10 segments), and the **left lung has 2 lobes** (8-10 segments). Understanding bronchopulmonary segments is crucial for localized lung disease and surgical resection.

## Key Points

- **Right lung:** 3 lobes (upper, middle, lower), 10 segments
- **Left lung:** 2 lobes (upper, lower), 8-10 segments, cardiac notch
- **Hilum contents (anterior to posterior):** Vein, Artery, Bronchus (VAB) on left; Bronchus highest on right
- **Bronchopulmonary segment:** Functionally independent unit
- **Right main bronchus:** Wider, shorter, more vertical (foreign body aspiration site)

| Feature | Right Lung | Left Lung |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| **Lobes** | 3 (upper, middle, lower) | 2 (upper, lower) |
| **Fissures** | 2 (oblique + horizontal) | 1 (oblique only) |
| **Weight** | ~600g | ~500g (smaller due to heart) |
| **Segments** | 10 | 8-10 |
| **Cardiac notch** | Absent | Present (anterior border) |
| **Lingula** | Absent | Present (analogue of middle lobe) |

### Surfaces and Borders

- **Costal surface:** Largest, related to ribs and intercostals
- **Mediastinal surface:** Contains hilum, related to mediastinal structures
- **Diaphragmatic surface (base):** Related to diaphragm
- **Borders:** Anterior, Posterior, Inferior

### Hilum (Root of Lung)

**Mnemonic for arrangement (superior to inferior):**
- **Right:** "BAPV" - Bronchus, Artery, Pulmonary veins
- **Left:** "ABPV" - Artery, Bronchus, Pulmonary veins

| Structure | Right Hilum | Left Hilum |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Most superior** | Eparterial bronchus | Pulmonary artery |
| **Anterior** | Pulmonary veins | Pulmonary veins |
| **Posterior** | Bronchus | Bronchus |

### Bronchopulmonary Segments

**Right Lung (10 segments):**

| Lobe | Segments |
|------|----------|
| **Upper** | 1. Apical, 2. Posterior, 3. Anterior |
| **Middle** | 4. Lateral, 5. Medial |
| **Lower** | 6. Superior, 7. Medial basal, 8. Anterior basal, 9. Lateral basal, 10. Posterior basal |

**Left Lung (8-10 segments):**

| Lobe | Segments |
|------|----------|
| **Upper** | 1+2. Apicoposterior (fused), 3. Anterior, 4. Superior lingular, 5. Inferior lingular |
| **Lower** | 6. Superior, 7+8. Anteromedial basal (may be fused), 9. Lateral basal, 10. Posterior basal |

### Blood Supply

**Dual supply:**
1. **Pulmonary circulation (functional):**
   - Pulmonary arteries → Capillaries → Pulmonary veins
   - Gas exchange

2. **Bronchial circulation (nutritional):**
   - Bronchial arteries (from thoracic aorta)
   - Supply bronchi, visceral pleura
   - Drain to pulmonary veins (some to azygos)

### Lymphatic Drainage

```
Lung parenchyma → Pulmonary nodes (in lung)
                        ↓
              Bronchopulmonary nodes (at hilum)
                        ↓
              Tracheobronchial nodes
                        ↓
              Paratracheal nodes
                        ↓
           Bronchomediastinal trunk
```

### Nerve Supply

- **Pulmonary plexus:** Anterior and posterior to hilum
- **Parasympathetic:** Vagus (bronchoconstriction, secretion)
- **Sympathetic:** T2-T5 (bronchodilation)
- **Visceral afferents:** Travel with both (pain, stretch)

### Pleura

- **Visceral pleura:** Covers lung, insensitive to pain
- **Parietal pleura:** Lines thoracic wall, sensitive to pain (intercostal + phrenic nerves)
- **Pleural cavity:** Potential space with ~10 mL serous fluid
- **Pleural recesses:**
  - Costodiaphragmatic (posterior, lower during expiration)
  - Costomediastinal (anterior)

## Clinical Correlations

### Foreign Body Aspiration
- **Most common site:** Right main bronchus (then right lower lobe)
- **Why?** Right bronchus is wider, shorter, more vertical

### Pneumothorax
- **Tension pneumothorax:** Mediastinal shift to opposite side, emergency
- **Treatment:** Needle decompression (2nd ICS MCL), then chest tube

### Lung Cancer
- **Central tumors:** Squamous cell, Small cell (smokers)
- **Peripheral tumors:** Adenocarcinoma (non-smokers)
- **Pancoast tumor:** Apex - invades brachial plexus, stellate ganglion

### Pleural Effusion
- **Thoracentesis:** 7th ICS, posterior axillary line (above rib to avoid neurovascular bundle)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Right main bronchus:** Wider, shorter, more vertical → foreign body site
2. **Eparterial bronchus:** Only on right (upper lobe bronchus above pulmonary artery)
3. **Lingula:** Left lung analogue of right middle lobe
4. **Bronchopulmonary segment:** Smallest surgically resectable unit
5. **Pleural reflection:** 2 ribs below lung border
6. **Pulmonary ligament:** Below hilum, allows vessel expansion
7. **Visceral pleura:** Insensitive to pain
8. **Parietal pleura:** Sensitive to pain (intercostal nerves for costal, phrenic for mediastinal/diaphragmatic)
9. **Bronchial arteries:** Nutritional supply (from aorta)
10. **Costodiaphragmatic recess:** Lowest extent of pleural cavity

### Surface Anatomy
- **Apex:** 2.5 cm above medial 1/3 of clavicle
- **Anterior border:** 2nd-6th costal cartilage
- **Lower border (lung):** 6th rib MCL, 8th rib MAL, 10th rib at back
- **Lower border (pleura):** 8th rib MCL, 10th rib MAL, 12th rib at back
