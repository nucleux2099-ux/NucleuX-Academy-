# Heart Anatomy

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Moore's Clinically Oriented Anatomy 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The heart is a **muscular pump** located in the middle mediastinum, enclosed within the pericardium. It has four chambers, four valves, and its own blood supply via coronary arteries. Understanding cardiac anatomy is essential for ECG interpretation, catheterization, and cardiac surgery.

## Key Points

- **Location:** Middle mediastinum, 2/3 to left of midline
- **Size:** Approximately the size of owner's fist (~300g in males)
- **Apex:** 5th intercostal space, midclavicular line (left)
- **Base:** Posterior surface (primarily left atrium)
- **Coronary blood supply:** Right and Left coronary arteries (from ascending aorta)

- **Aortic:** 2nd right intercostal space

```
                    ASCENDING AORTA
                          │
            ┌─────────────┴─────────────┐
            │                           │
       LEFT CORONARY                RIGHT CORONARY
       ARTERY (LCA)                 ARTERY (RCA)
            │                           │
    ┌───────┴───────┐                   │
    │               │               ┌───┴───────┐
   LAD           LCx               │           │
(Anterior IV)  (Circumflex)    Marginal    Posterior IV
    │               │                      (in 80%)
    │               │

```

| Artery | Territory | Clinical |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| **LAD** | Anterior wall, Apex, Anterior 2/3 IVS | "Widow maker" - anterior MI |
| **LCx** | Lateral wall, Posterior LA | Lateral MI |
| **RCA** | RA, RV, Posterior wall, SA node (60%), AV node (90%) | Inferior MI, bradycardia |
| **PDA** | Posterior 1/3 IVS, Posterior wall | From RCA (80%) or LCx (20%) |

**Dominance:**
- **Right dominant (80%):** RCA gives PDA
- **Left dominant (10%):** LCx gives PDA
- **Codominant (10%):** Both contribute

### Pericardium

- **Fibrous pericardium:** Tough outer layer, fused to central tendon of diaphragm
- **Serous pericardium:**
  - Parietal layer (lines fibrous pericardium)
  - Visceral layer (epicardium, covers heart)
- **Pericardial cavity:** Between parietal and visceral layers (15-50 mL fluid)

**Pericardial Sinuses:**
- **Transverse sinus:** Between arterial and venous vessels (posterior to ascending aorta)
- **Oblique sinus:** Behind left atrium (cul-de-sac)

## Clinical Correlations

### Myocardial Infarction
- **LAD occlusion:** Anterior wall MI - most lethal
- **RCA occlusion:** Inferior MI + heart block (AV node supplied by RCA)
- **LCx occlusion:** Lateral/posterior MI

### Cardiac Tamponade
- Beck's triad: Hypotension, Distended neck veins, Muffled heart sounds
- **Pericardiocentesis:** Subxiphoid approach, aim toward left shoulder

### Valvular Disease
- **Mitral stenosis:** Rheumatic heart disease → Enlarged LA → AFib
- **Aortic stenosis:** Calcification, Bicuspid valve → Syncope, Angina, Heart failure

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **SA node** is the natural pacemaker (60-100 bpm)
2. **AV node** blood supply: RCA (90%)
3. **LAD** supplies anterior 2/3 of interventricular septum
4. **Moderator band** carries right bundle branch to anterior papillary muscle
5. **Apex beat:** 5th ICS, midclavicular line
6. **Right dominant circulation:** 80% of population
7. **Cardiac tamponade:** Even 200 mL can cause tamponade if rapid
8. **Transverse sinus** is used for surgical clamping of great vessels
9. **Mitral valve** has 2 cusps (bicuspid); Tricuspid has 3
10. **Coronary sinus** drains into right atrium

### Surface Anatomy
- **Right border:** SVC, RA (3rd-6th costal cartilages)
- **Inferior border:** RV (from 6th CC right to apex)
- **Left border:** LV (from apex to 2nd ICS left)
- **Superior border:** Great vessels (2nd ICS)
