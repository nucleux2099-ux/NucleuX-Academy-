# Lung Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Right lung: 3 lobes, 2 fissures, wider/shorter. Left lung: 2 lobes, 1 fissure, cardiac notch, lingula. Hilum contains pulmonary artery, veins, bronchus, bronchial vessels, nerves. Right bronchus wider, shorter, more vertical → foreign body site. Apex 2.5 cm above medial 1/3 clavicle.

## High Yield Points

★ Right bronchus: **wider, shorter, more vertical** → foreign body enters right lung
★ Right lung has **3 lobes** (upper, middle, lower) and **2 fissures** (oblique + horizontal)
★ Left lung: **cardiac notch** + **lingula** (equivalent of right middle lobe)
★ **Eparterial bronchus**: right upper lobe bronchus (above pulmonary artery)
★ Over left hilum: **aortic arch**; Over right hilum: **azygos vein arch**
★ Lung apex related to **subclavian artery**, cervical pleura, stellate ganglion
★ Bronchial arteries: left (2 from aorta), right (1 from intercostal/aorta)
★ Inferior lung border: **6-8-10** rule (MCL, MAL, posterior)
★ **Pancoast tumour**: apex → Horner's syndrome, T1 wasting, subclavian compression
★ Surfactant from **type II pneumocytes** at ~24 weeks

## Mnemonics

**Right hilum (top to bottom):** "**BAPV**" — (upper lobe) Bronchus, Artery, (lower lobe) Bronchus, Pulmonary Veins

**Left hilum (top to bottom):** "**ABV**" — Artery, Bronchus, Veins

**Lung borders (inferior):** "**6-8-10**" — 6th rib MCL, 8th MAL, 10th posterior (scapular line)
