# Lung Anatomy

## Overview
The lungs are paired organs of respiration occupying the pleural cavities in the thorax. The right lung has three lobes and ten segments; the left lung has two lobes and eight–ten segments. Each lung has an apex, base, two surfaces (costal, mediastinal), and three borders (anterior, posterior, inferior).

## Key Points
- Right lung: 3 lobes, 2 fissures (oblique + horizontal), wider and shorter (liver below)
- Left lung: 2 lobes, 1 fissure (oblique), cardiac notch on anterior border, lingula
- Weight: right ~600 g, left ~500 g
- Root of lung at T5–T7, structures arranged as RALS (rib → artery, lymph, superior vein arrangement)
- Hilum: pulmonary artery, pulmonary veins (2), bronchus, bronchial vessels, nerves, lymphatics

## Gross Anatomy

### External Features
- **Apex:** Projects 2.5 cm above medial 1/3 of clavicle into root of neck
- **Base:** Concave, rests on diaphragm, reaches 10th rib posteriorly
- **Costal surface:** Convex, related to ribs and intercostal muscles
- **Mediastinal surface:** Contains hilum and impressions of mediastinal structures

### Lobes and Fissures
| Feature | Right Lung | Left Lung |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Lobes | Upper, Middle, Lower | Upper, Lower |
| Fissures | Oblique + Horizontal | Oblique only |
| Lingula | Absent | Present (equivalent of middle lobe) |
| Cardiac notch | Absent | Present (anterior border) |

### Hilum / Root

**Right Lung (from above down):** Upper lobe bronchus (eparterial), pulmonary artery, middle + lower lobe bronchi, pulmonary veins
**Left Lung (from above down):** Pulmonary artery (highest), left main bronchus, pulmonary veins (lowest)

### Root Relations
- Anterior: phrenic nerve, anterior pulmonary plexus
- Posterior: vagus nerve, posterior pulmonary plexus
- Superior: azygos vein arch (right), aortic arch (left)
- Inferior: pulmonary ligament

### Mediastinal Surface Impressions
**Right lung:** SVC, azygos vein, oesophagus, cardiac impression (RA, RV)
**Left lung:** Aortic arch, descending aorta, cardiac impression (LV), oesophagus

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Apex: 2.5 cm above medial 1/3 of clavicle
- Anterior border: from behind sternoclavicular joint to 6th costal cartilage
- Left anterior border: cardiac notch at 4th costal cartilage
- Inferior border: 6th rib (MCL), 8th rib (midaxillary), 10th rib (posterior)
- Oblique fissure: T3 spine → 6th costal cartilage
- Horizontal fissure (right): 4th costal cartilage to oblique fissure

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Functional
- Pulmonary arteries (deoxygenated blood from RV)
- Pulmonary veins (oxygenated blood to LA)

### Nutritive
- Bronchial arteries: left (2) from thoracic aorta; right (1) from 3rd posterior intercostal or aorta
- Bronchial veins drain into azygos (right) and hemiazygos (left)

### Lymphatic
- Pulmonary → bronchopulmonary (hilar) → tracheobronchial → paratracheal → bronchomediastinal trunk

### Nerve Supply
- Pulmonary plexus (anterior and posterior) — vagus + sympathetic
- Vagus: bronchoconstriction, secretion
- Sympathetic: bronchodilation

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Right Main Bronchus
- Wider, shorter, more vertical → foreign bodies tend to lodge in right lung (especially right lower lobe)

### Pancoast Tumour
- Apex of lung tumour → compresses sympathetic trunk (Horner's syndrome), brachial plexus (C8-T1), subclavian vessels

### Lung Cancer
- Most common site: right upper lobe
- Left hilar mass may compress left recurrent laryngeal nerve → hoarseness

### Pleural Effusion
- Fluid in pleural cavity; examined by posterior percussion; aspirated at 8th–9th ICS posterior to midaxillary line

### Pneumonectomy / Lobectomy
- Requires knowledge of segmental anatomy and hilar structures

## Development
- Lung bud from foregut (ventral) at week 4
- Stages: embryonic → pseudoglandular → canalicular → terminal saccular → alveolar
- Surfactant production begins at ~24 weeks (type II pneumocytes)
- Respiratory distress syndrome in premature infants (surfactant deficiency)
