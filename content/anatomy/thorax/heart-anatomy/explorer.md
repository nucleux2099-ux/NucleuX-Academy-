# Heart Anatomy

## Overview
The heart is a muscular pump located in the middle mediastinum, enclosed within the pericardium. It is roughly the size of a closed fist, weighs ~300 g in males and ~250 g in females. It has four chambers (two atria, two ventricles), four valves, and its own coronary circulation. It lies obliquely with 2/3 to the left and 1/3 to the right of the midline.

## Key Points
- Located in middle mediastinum, between lungs
- 2/3 to the left of midline
- Four borders: right (RA), left (LV), inferior (RV), superior (great vessels)
- Four surfaces: sternocostal (anterior — mainly RV), diaphragmatic (inferior — mainly RV + LV), left pulmonary, base (posterior — mainly LA)
- Apex formed by LV — in 5th left ICS, midclavicular line

## Gross Anatomy

### External Features
- **Apex:** Left 5th ICS, midclavicular line (apex beat)
- **Base:** Posterior, formed mainly by LA, faces vertebral column
- **Surfaces:** Sternocostal (anterior), diaphragmatic (inferior), left pulmonary, base
- **Sulci:** Coronary sulcus (atrioventricular), anterior and posterior interventricular sulci

### Chambers

#### Right Atrium
- Receives: SVC (no valve), IVC (Eustachian valve), coronary sinus (Thebesian valve)
- Crista terminalis: muscular ridge separating smooth (sinus venarum) from rough (pectinate muscles)
- Fossa ovalis: remnant of foramen ovale
- SA node: in upper part near SVC opening

#### Right Ventricle
- Inflow tract: trabeculae carneae, tricuspid valve
- Outflow tract (infundibulum/conus arteriosus): smooth-walled, leads to pulmonary trunk
- Moderator band (septomarginal trabecula): carries right bundle branch
- Tricuspid valve: 3 cusps (anterior, posterior, septal)

#### Left Atrium
- Smoothest chamber; receives 4 pulmonary veins
- Small atrial appendage (auricle)
- Forms most of the base (posterior surface)
- Lies anterior to oesophagus → enlarged LA compresses oesophagus

#### Left Ventricle
- Thickest wall (~3× RV), forms apex and left border
- Mitral (bicuspid) valve: 2 cusps (anterior/aortic, posterior/mural)
- Aortic vestibule: smooth outflow tract
- Papillary muscles: anterolateral, posteromedial
- Chordae tendineae prevent valve prolapse

### Valves
| Valve | Cusps | Position |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Tricuspid | 3 (anterior, posterior, septal) | Right AV orifice |
| Mitral | 2 (anterior, posterior) | Left AV orifice |
| Pulmonary | 3 semilunar | Pulmonary trunk origin |
| Aortic | 3 semilunar (R coronary, L coronary, non-coronary) | Aortic root |

### Fibrous Skeleton
- Four fibrous rings around valve orifices
- Provides attachment for valves and muscle
- Acts as electrical insulator between atria and ventricles

## Relations & Surface Markings
- **Apex:** 5th left ICS, 9 cm from midline (midclavicular line)
- **Right border:** 3rd–6th right costal cartilages, 1 cm from sternum
- **Left border:** 2nd left ICS to apex
- **Superior border:** Lower border of 2nd costal cartilages
- **Inferior border:** Right 6th costal cartilage to apex

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Coronary Arteries
- **Right coronary (RCA):** SA node (60%), AV node (80%), RV, inferior LV
- **Left coronary (LCA):** Divides into LAD + LCx
  - LAD: anterior interventricular, anterior 2/3 septum, apex
  - LCx: left atrium, posterolateral LV

### Venous Drainage
- Great cardiac vein → coronary sinus → RA
- Small cardiac vein, middle cardiac vein → coronary sinus
- Anterior cardiac veins → directly into RA
- Venae cordis minimae (Thebesian veins) → all chambers

### Nerve Supply
- Sympathetic: T1–T5 (cardiac accelerator nerves) — increases rate and force
- Parasympathetic: vagus nerve — decreases rate (SA and AV nodes)
- Cardiac plexus (superficial and deep) behind aortic arch

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Referred Pain
- Heart pain referred to T1–T5 dermatomes: chest, left arm, jaw (same dermatomes as cardiac sympathetic)

### Valve Auscultation Areas
- Mitral: apex (5th ICS, MCL)
- Tricuspid: lower left sternal border
- Aortic: right 2nd ICS
- Pulmonary: left 2nd ICS

### Pericardiocentesis
- Left xiphocostal angle, needle directed towards left shoulder

### Atrial Septal Defect
- Patent foramen ovale (most common — ostium secundum type)

## Development
- From cardiogenic mesoderm (splanchnic mesoderm)
- Heart tube: truncus arteriosus → bulbus cordis → ventricle → atrium → sinus venosus
- Septation occurs during weeks 4–7
- Foramen ovale closes at birth with increased left atrial pressure
