# Heart Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Four-chambered muscular pump in middle mediastinum. 2/3 left of midline. Apex (LV) at 5th left ICS MCL. Sternocostal surface = mainly RV. Base = mainly LA. Coronary arteries from aortic sinuses. SA node supplied by RCA (60%). Fibrous skeleton insulates atria from ventricles.

## High Yield Points

★ Sternocostal (anterior) surface: mainly **RV** — stab wounds affect RV
★ Base of heart = **left atrium** (posterior, faces oesophagus)
★ Apex = **left ventricle**, 5th ICS midclavicular line
★ **SA node** supplied by RCA (60%) or LCx (40%)
★ **AV node** supplied by RCA (80%)
★ **Moderator band** in RV carries right bundle branch
★ Fossa ovalis in RA = remnant of **foramen ovale**
★ Aortic valve cusps: right coronary, left coronary, **non-coronary**
★ LA lies anterior to **oesophagus** — enlargement causes dysphagia
★ Mitral valve has **2 cusps** (anterior/aortic, posterior/mural)

## Mnemonics

**Valve auscultation areas:** "**All Patients Take Medicine**" — Aortic (R 2nd ICS), Pulmonary (L 2nd ICS), Tricuspid (L lower sternal border), Mitral (apex)

**Heart borders:** "**SIR-L**" — Superior (great vessels), Inferior (RV), Right (RA), Left (LV)

**Coronary sinus tributaries:** "**Great, Middle, Small + Left posterior**"
