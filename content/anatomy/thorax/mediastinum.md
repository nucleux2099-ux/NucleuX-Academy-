# Mediastinum

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Moore's Clinically Oriented Anatomy 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The mediastinum is the **central compartment of the thoracic cavity** between the two pleural cavities. It contains the heart, great vessels, trachea, esophagus, thymus, nerves, and lymph nodes. Division into superior and inferior (anterior, middle, posterior) mediastinum helps localize pathology.

## Key Points

- **Boundaries:** Sternum (anterior), Vertebrae (posterior), Thoracic inlet (superior), Diaphragm (inferior)
- **Divisions:** Superior and Inferior (anterior, middle, posterior)
- **Sternal angle (T4-5):** Divides superior from inferior mediastinum
- **Middle mediastinum:** Contains heart and pericardium
- **Posterior mediastinum:** Contains esophagus and descending aorta

### Superior Mediastinum

**Boundaries:**
- Superior: Thoracic inlet
- Inferior: Transverse plane through sternal angle (T4-5)
- Anterior: Manubrium
- Posterior: T1-T4 vertebrae

**Contents (anterior to posterior):**

| Layer | Contents |
|-------|----------|
| **Anterior** | Thymus, SVC, Brachiocephalic veins |
| **Middle** | Aortic arch, Brachiocephalic trunk, Left CCA, Left subclavian |
| **Posterior** | Trachea, Esophagus, Thoracic duct, Vagus nerves, Recurrent laryngeal (left) |

### Inferior Mediastinum

#### Anterior Mediastinum
- **Location:** Between sternum and pericardium
- **Contents:** Fat, Lymph nodes, Thymic remnants, Internal thoracic vessels

#### Middle Mediastinum
- **Contents:**
  - Heart and pericardium
  - Ascending aorta (beginning)
  - Pulmonary trunk
  - SVC (terminal), IVC
  - Main bronchi
  - Pulmonary arteries and veins
  - Phrenic nerves

#### Posterior Mediastinum
- **Location:** Between pericardium and vertebrae
- **Contents:**
  - Esophagus
  - Descending thoracic aorta
  - Azygos and hemiazygos veins
  - Thoracic duct
  - Vagus nerves
  - Splanchnic nerves
  - Sympathetic chains

```
               Ascending       Descending
                aorta           aorta
```

#### Trachea
- Begins: C6 (cricoid cartilage level)
- Bifurcates: T4-5 (sternal angle) into main bronchi
- Carina: Ridge at bifurcation

#### Esophagus
- Begins: C6 (continuous with pharynx)
- Passes through: Esophageal hiatus (T10)
- Three constrictions:
  1. Cricopharyngeal (C6) - 15 cm from incisors
  2. Aortic arch/Left bronchus (T4-5) - 25 cm
  3. Diaphragmatic (T10) - 40 cm

### Nerves

| Nerve | Course | Function |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Vagus (CN X)** | Posterior to hilum | Parasympathetic to thorax, abdomen |
| **Phrenic (C3,4,5)** | Anterior to hilum | Motor to diaphragm |
| **Left recurrent laryngeal** | Loops under aortic arch | Voice, swallowing |
| **Sympathetic trunk** | Alongside vertebral bodies | Sympathetic supply |

### Thoracic Duct
- **Origin:** Cisterna chyli (L1-2)
- **Course:** Through aortic hiatus (T12), ascends in posterior mediastinum
- **Drainage:** Left venous angle (junction of left IJV and subclavian)
- **Drains:** All body EXCEPT right head, neck, thorax, and right upper limb

## Clinical Correlations

### Mediastinal Masses

| Location | Common Masses | "4 T's" |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **Anterior** | Thymoma, Teratoma, Thyroid (retrosternal), Terrible lymphoma | Yes |
| **Middle** | Lymphoma, Pericardial cyst, Bronchogenic cyst | - |
| **Posterior** | Neurogenic tumors, Esophageal tumors, Aneurysms | - |

### Superior Vena Cava Syndrome
- **Cause:** Compression/invasion by malignancy (lung cancer, lymphoma)
- **Signs:** Facial swelling, Distended neck veins, Dyspnea, Headache
- **Pemberton's sign:** Facial plethora with arm elevation

### Aortic Aneurysm/Dissection
- **Thoracic aneurysm:** May compress trachea, esophagus, recurrent laryngeal
- **Dissection:** Severe "tearing" chest pain radiating to back

### Left Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve Palsy
- **Causes:** Aortic aneurysm, Lung cancer (left hilum), Mediastinal surgery
- **Presentation:** Hoarseness, bovine cough

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Sternal angle (T4-5):** Divides superior from inferior mediastinum
2. **"4 T's"** of anterior mediastinal masses: Thymoma, Teratoma, Thyroid, Terrible lymphoma
3. **Left recurrent laryngeal nerve** loops under aortic arch
4. **Right recurrent laryngeal** loops under right subclavian artery
5. **Thoracic duct** drains into left venous angle
6. **Esophagus:** 3 constrictions (C6, T4-5, T10)
7. **Phrenic nerve:** Anterior to hilum, "C3-4-5 keeps diaphragm alive"
8. **Vagus nerve:** Posterior to hilum
9. **Azygos vein** arches over right main bronchus to join SVC
10. **Middle mediastinum** contains heart - widened in cardiomegaly

### Surface Anatomy
- **Sternal angle:** Opposite T4-5 disc
- **Aortic arch:** Behind manubrium
- **Tracheal bifurcation:** T4-5 (sternal angle)
