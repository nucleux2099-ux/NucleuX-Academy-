# Duodenum — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
C-shaped, 25 cm, mostly retroperitoneal. Four parts: D1 (5 cm, at L1), D2 (7.5 cm, L1–L3, major papilla here), D3 (10 cm, L3, crossed by SMA), D4 (2.5 cm, to L2, ends at DJ flexure). Dual blood supply: superior pancreaticoduodenal (coeliac) and inferior pancreaticoduodenal (SMA).

## High Yield Points

★ Only first **2.5 cm of D1** (duodenal cap) is intraperitoneal — rest retroperitoneal
★ Major duodenal papilla in **D2** — foregut-midgut junction
★ Anterior duodenal ulcer → **perforation**; Posterior → **haemorrhage** (gastroduodenal artery)
★ **D3** crossed anteriorly by SMA — SMA syndrome
★ **Ligament of Treitz** at DJ flexure — attached to right crus of diaphragm
★ Dual blood supply watershed at major papilla: coeliac (above) + SMA (below)
★ Duodenal atresia: **double bubble sign**, associated with **Down syndrome**
★ Gastroduodenal artery passes **behind D1** — eroded by posterior ulcer
★ D2 is related medially to **head of pancreas**
★ Portal vein, CBD, and IVC are posterior to D1

## Mnemonics

**Duodenal parts length:** "**Counting 5-7-10-2.5**" (short-long-longest-shortest)

**Posterior relations of D1:** "**PIG-C**" — Portal vein, IVC, Gastroduodenal artery, CBD

**Duodenal ulcer bleeding vs perforation:** "Anterior = Air (perforation), Posterior = Pour (haemorrhage)"
