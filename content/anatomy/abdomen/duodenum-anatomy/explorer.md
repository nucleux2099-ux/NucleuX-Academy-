# Duodenum

## Overview
The duodenum is the first, shortest (25 cm / 12 finger-breadths), widest, and most fixed part of the small intestine. It forms a C-shaped loop around the head of the pancreas and is mostly retroperitoneal. It receives the bile duct and pancreatic duct at the major duodenal papilla.

## Key Points
- C-shaped, 25 cm long, mostly retroperitoneal (except first 2.5 cm of D1)
- Four parts: superior (D1), descending (D2), horizontal (D3), ascending (D4)
- Major duodenal papilla in D2 — opening of CBD + main pancreatic duct
- Blood supply: coeliac trunk (foregut) + SMA (midgut) — watershed
- Duodenojejunal flexure supported by ligament of Treitz

## Gross Anatomy

### Parts

#### D1 — Superior (5 cm)
- Begins at pylorus (L1), passes right to left and posteriorly
- First 2.5 cm (duodenal cap) is intraperitoneal, mobile — common ulcer site
- **Relations:** Anterior — liver, gallbladder; Posterior — CBD, portal vein, IVC, gastroduodenal artery

#### D2 — Descending (7.5 cm)
- Descends from L1 to L3 along right side of vertebral column
- Entirely retroperitoneal
- **Major duodenal papilla** (of Vater) on posteromedial wall — opening of hepatopancreatic ampulla
- **Minor duodenal papilla** — 2 cm above, opening of accessory pancreatic duct
- **Relations:** Medial — head of pancreas; Lateral — right kidney, right colic flexure; Anterior — transverse colon, transverse mesocolon

#### D3 — Horizontal/Inferior (10 cm)
- Crosses L3 from right to left
- Crossed anteriorly by SMA and SMV (SMA syndrome)
- **Relations:** Posterior — right ureter, right psoas, IVC, aorta; Anterior — SMA, SMV, root of mesentery

#### D4 — Ascending (2.5 cm)
- Ascends on left side of aorta to L2
- Ends at duodenojejunal flexure
- **Ligament of Treitz** (suspensory muscle of duodenum) — attached to right crus of diaphragm

## Relations & Surface Markings
- D1: transpyloric plane (L1)
- D2: right paravertebral L1–L3
- D3: subcostal plane (L3), crossed by SMA
- DJ flexure: 2.5 cm left of midline at L2
- Surface: C-shaped, 3 cm from midline on right

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Arterial Supply
- **Above major papilla (foregut):** Superior pancreaticoduodenal artery (from gastroduodenal → coeliac trunk)
- **Below major papilla (midgut):** Inferior pancreaticoduodenal artery (from SMA)
- These arteries form anterior and posterior arterial arcades on the head of pancreas

### Venous Drainage
- Pancreaticoduodenal veins → SMV → portal vein

### Lymphatic Drainage
- Pancreaticoduodenal nodes → coeliac and superior mesenteric nodes

### Nerve Supply
- Sympathetic: T6–T9 (coeliac plexus)
- Parasympathetic: vagus nerve

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Duodenal Ulcer
- Most common in first part (duodenal cap) — anterior wall
- Anterior ulcer → perforation → pneumoperitoneum
- Posterior ulcer → haemorrhage (erodes gastroduodenal artery)

### SMA Syndrome (Wilkie's Syndrome)
- Compression of D3 between SMA and aorta
- Occurs with loss of fat pad (weight loss, body cast, burns)

### Paraduodenal Hernia
- Near DJ flexure — related to peritoneal fossae (Landzert, Waldeyer)

### Duodenal Atresia
- Congenital; "double bubble" sign on X-ray
- Associated with Down syndrome
- Failure of recanalization of duodenal lumen

### Whipple's Procedure (Pancreaticoduodenectomy)
- Removal of duodenum with head of pancreas for periampullary carcinoma

## Development
- D1 and proximal D2 (above major papilla): foregut
- Distal D2, D3, D4: midgut
- Major papilla marks the foregut-midgut junction
- Duodenum rotates to become retroperitoneal with rotation of midgut loop
