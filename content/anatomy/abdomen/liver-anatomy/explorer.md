# Liver Anatomy

## Overview
The liver is the largest gland in the body (~1.5 kg), located in the right hypochondrium, epigastrium, and extending into the left hypochondrium. It is both an exocrine (bile) and endocrine gland. It has a dual blood supply: hepatic artery (30%) and portal vein (70%). The functional unit is the hepatic lobule.

## Key Points
- Largest solid organ, occupies right hypochondrium and epigastrium
- Anatomical lobes: right, left, caudate, quadrate (by surface markings)
- Functional lobes: right and left (Cantlie's line — gallbladder fossa to IVC)
- Dual blood supply: hepatic artery proper (30%) + portal vein (70%)
- Couinaud's 8 segments based on hepatic vein and portal vein branching
- Bare area: directly attached to diaphragm, no peritoneal covering

## Gross Anatomy

### Surfaces
1. **Diaphragmatic surface:** Smooth, convex; divided into superior, anterior, posterior, right areas
2. **Visceral surface:** Irregular, concave; has H-shaped arrangement of fossae and fissures

### H-shaped Arrangement (Visceral Surface)
- **Left limb (anterior):** Fissure for ligamentum teres (round ligament)
- **Left limb (posterior):** Fissure for ligamentum venosum
- **Right limb (anterior):** Gallbladder fossa
- **Right limb (posterior):** Groove for IVC
- **Crossbar:** Porta hepatis (hilum)

### Porta Hepatis (Hilum)
Contents (from anterior to posterior):
- Hepatic ducts (right and left) — most anterior
- Hepatic artery proper — middle
- Portal vein — most posterior and largest

### Peritoneal Relations
- Almost entirely covered by peritoneum except **bare area**
- **Bare area:** on posterior surface, bounded by coronary ligaments (superior and inferior layers), directly attached to diaphragm
- Ligaments: falciform, coronary, right and left triangular, lesser omentum (hepatogastric + hepatoduodenal)

### Lobes
| Lobe | Boundaries |
|------|-----------|
| Right | Largest; right of falciform ligament (anatomical) |
| Left | Left of falciform ligament |
| Caudate | Between IVC and ligamentum venosum; has its own portal and hepatic venous drainage |
| Quadrate | Between gallbladder fossa and ligamentum teres, anterior to porta hepatis |

### Couinaud's Segments (I–VIII)
- Based on portal vein branches (portal pedicles) and hepatic veins
- Segment I = caudate lobe (independent drainage)
- Segments II–IV = left functional lobe
- Segments V–VIII = right functional lobe

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Upper border: 5th intercostal space in right midclavicular line
- Lower border: right costal margin (descends with respiration)
- Can be percussed: liver dullness from 5th ICS to costal margin

### Visceral Surface Relations
- Right kidney, right suprarenal, right colic flexure, D1, D2, stomach, oesophagus, gallbladder

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Arterial
- Common hepatic artery (from coeliac trunk) → hepatic artery proper → right and left hepatic arteries
- Cystic artery (usually from right hepatic) — within Calot's triangle
- Supplies 30% of blood but 50% of oxygen

### Portal Venous
- Portal vein (from SMV + splenic vein confluence behind pancreatic neck)
- Supplies 70% of blood, 50% of oxygen
- Carries nutrient-rich blood from GIT

### Hepatic Veins
- Right, middle, and left hepatic veins → IVC (just below diaphragm)
- No extrahepatic course — difficult surgical access
- Middle hepatic vein in Cantlie's line

### Lymphatic
- Hepatic lymph nodes → coeliac nodes
- Some lymph passes through bare area to posterior mediastinal nodes

### Nerve Supply
- Hepatic plexus (sympathetic from coeliac plexus + parasympathetic from vagus)
- Right phrenic nerve (C3, C4, C5) — referred pain to right shoulder tip

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Liver Segments in Surgery
- Couinaud's classification allows segmental resection
- Cantlie's line: from gallbladder fossa to IVC — divides liver into right and left functional lobes

### Portal Hypertension
- Obstruction to portal flow → portosystemic anastomoses open up
- Sites: oesophageal, rectal, paraumbilical, retroperitoneal, bare area

### Liver Biopsy
- Percutaneous: through 8th–9th intercostal space, right midaxillary line
- Patient holds breath in expiration to minimize risk of lung injury

### Referred Pain
- Diaphragmatic irritation → right shoulder tip pain (C3, 4, 5 — phrenic nerve)
- Liver abscess, subphrenic abscess

### Calot's Triangle
- Boundaries: cystic duct, common hepatic duct, inferior surface of liver
- Contains cystic artery (and sometimes right hepatic artery)

### Bare Area
- Liver abscess can spread to thorax through bare area
- Amoebic liver abscess → right pleural effusion

## Development
- Develops from hepatic diverticulum (ventral mesogastrium outgrowth) at 3rd week
- Liver bud from foregut endoderm → hepatocytes, bile duct epithelium
- Septum transversum mesenchyme → Kupffer cells, connective tissue
- Haematopoietic organ in fetal life (2nd–7th month)
