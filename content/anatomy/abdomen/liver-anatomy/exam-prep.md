# Liver Anatomy — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Largest gland (~1.5 kg). Dual blood supply: hepatic artery (30%) + portal vein (70%). Anatomical lobes: right, left, caudate, quadrate. Functional division by Cantlie's line (gallbladder fossa to IVC). Couinaud's 8 segments. Bare area on posterior surface. Porta hepatis contains hepatic ducts, hepatic artery, portal vein.

## High Yield Points

★ **Cantlie's line**: gallbladder fossa to IVC — functional right/left division
★ Portal vein = **70% blood**, hepatic artery = **30% blood** but 50% oxygen each
★ **Couinaud segment I** = caudate lobe — independent portal + hepatic venous drainage
★ **Bare area**: no peritoneum, bounded by coronary ligaments, abuts diaphragm
★ Porta hepatis (ant→post): **Duct, Artery, Vein** (DAV)
★ **Calot's triangle**: cystic duct, common hepatic duct, liver — contains cystic artery
★ Referred pain to **right shoulder** via phrenic nerve (C3, 4, 5)
★ Hepatic veins have **no extrahepatic course** — drain directly into IVC
★ **H-shaped fissures** on visceral surface define caudate and quadrate lobes
★ Liver biopsy: **8th–9th ICS**, right midaxillary line, in **expiration**

## Mnemonics

**Porta hepatis (anterior to posterior):** "**DAV**" — Duct, Artery, Vein

**Couinaud segments (clockwise on visceral surface):** I (caudate), II, III (left lateral), IV (left medial/quadrate), V, VI, VII, VIII (right)

**Phrenic nerve (referred shoulder pain):** "**C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive**" — irritation → shoulder tip
