# Rectus Sheath — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Formed by aponeuroses of EO, IO, TA. Above arcuate line: anterior wall (EO + anterior IO), posterior wall (posterior IO + TA). Below arcuate line: ALL pass anterior, posterior wall is only transversalis fascia. Contains epigastric vessels and T7–T12 nerves.

## High Yield Points

★ Above arcuate line: IO aponeurosis **splits** to enclose rectus
★ Below arcuate line: **all aponeuroses anterior** — posterior wall deficient
★ Arcuate line: midway between **umbilicus and pubic symphysis**
★ **Inferior epigastric artery** enters sheath at arcuate line
★ Superior and inferior epigastric arteries **anastomose** — collateral in aortic coarctation
★ Tendinous intersections attached to **anterior wall only** (not posterior)
★ **Fothergill's sign**: mass doesn't cross midline, more prominent on tensing
★ **Linea alba**: avascular midline raphe — preferred for midline incision
★ Intercostal nerves enter from **lateral side** → at risk in paramedian incision
★ Spigelian hernia: near arcuate line along **linea semilunaris**

## Mnemonics

**Rectus sheath above arcuate line:**
"**Split the IO**" — EO anterior, IO splits (anterior + posterior), TA posterior

**Below arcuate line:** "**All Go Anterior**" — EO + IO + TA all pass in front of rectus

**Contents:** "**PENIS**" — Pyramidalis, Epigastric vessels (superior + inferior), Nerves (T7–T12), Intercostal (lower 6), Sheath fluid
