# Stomach — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
J-shaped, entirely intraperitoneal. Four parts: cardia (T11), fundus, body, pylorus (L1). Blood supply from all three branches of coeliac trunk. Three muscle layers (unique inner oblique). Vagal supply: anterior trunk (left vagus) and posterior trunk (right vagus). Nerve of Latarjet along lesser curvature.

## High Yield Points

★ Stomach has **three muscle layers** — inner oblique is unique to stomach
★ Blood supply entirely from **coeliac trunk** branches
★ **Left gastric artery** = most common origin of aberrant left hepatic artery
★ Gastric ulcer most common on **lesser curvature**; posterior ulcer erodes **splenic artery**
★ After rotation: **left vagus → anterior**, right vagus → posterior ("**LARP**")
★ **Nerve of Latarjet**: continuation of vagal trunks along lesser curvature
★ Pyloric sphincter at **L1** (transpyloric plane)
★ Fundus related to **left dome of diaphragm** — gas bubble on X-ray
★ **Congenital hypertrophic pyloric stenosis**: projectile non-bilious vomiting, Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy
★ Highly selective vagotomy preserves **nerve of Latarjet** → no drainage needed

## Mnemonics

**Stomach blood supply:** "**Left and Right, Gastric and Gastroepiploic + Short gastric**"

**Vagal rotation:** "**LARP**" — Left vagus → Anterior, Right vagus → Posterior

**Stomach bed:** "**SLAP-DK**" — Splenic artery, Left suprarenal, Aorta, Pancreas, Diaphragm, Kidney (left)
