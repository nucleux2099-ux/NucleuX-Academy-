# Liver Anatomy

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Blumgart's Surgery of the Liver 6th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The liver is the **largest solid organ** in the body (1.5 kg), located in the right upper quadrant. It has a unique **dual blood supply** (hepatic artery + portal vein) and performs over 500 metabolic functions. Understanding its segmental anatomy (Couinaud classification) is essential for hepatic surgery.

## Key Points

- **Weight:** 1400-1600 g (2% body weight, receives 25% cardiac output)
- **Location:** Right hypochondrium, epigastrium (protected by ribs 7-11)
- **Blood supply:** 75% portal vein, 25% hepatic artery
- **Venous drainage:** Hepatic veins → IVC
- **Bile drainage:** Right + Left hepatic ducts → Common hepatic duct
- **Couinaud segments:** 8 functionally independent segments

**Traditional (Morphological) Lobes:**
- **Right lobe:** Largest (separated by falciform ligament)
- **Left lobe:** Smaller, extends to left hypochondrium
- **Caudate lobe:** Posterior, between IVC and ligamentum venosum
- **Quadrate lobe:** Inferior, between gallbladder fossa and ligamentum teres

### Porta Hepatis ("Gateway to the Liver")

Located on visceral surface, contains (from anterior to posterior): **"DAV"**
- **D:** Bile Duct (Common hepatic duct) - most anterior and lateral
- **A:** Hepatic Artery (Proper) - anterior and medial
- **V:** Portal Vein - posterior and largest

### Peritoneal Attachments

| Ligament | Attachment | Contents |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **Falciform ligament** | Anterior abdominal wall | Ligamentum teres (obliterated umbilical v.) |
| **Coronary ligament** | Diaphragm | Creates bare area |
| **Triangular ligaments (R/L)** | Lateral extensions of coronary | - |
| **Lesser omentum** | Porta hepatis → Lesser curvature | Contains portal triad |
| **Ligamentum venosum** | Fissure between caudate and left lobe | Obliterated ductus venosus |

**Bare Area:** Portion without peritoneum, direct contact with diaphragm (between coronary ligament layers)

| Segment | Location | Key Feature |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| I | Caudate lobe | Independent drainage to IVC |
| II | Left lateral superior | - |
| III | Left lateral inferior | - |
| IVa | Left medial superior | Quadrate (upper) |
| IVb | Left medial inferior | Quadrate (lower) |
| V | Right anterior inferior | - |
| VI | Right posterior inferior | - |
| VII | Right posterior superior | - |
| VIII | Right anterior superior | - |

**Surgical Division:**
- **Right liver (R hemihepatectomy):** Segments V, VI, VII, VIII
- **Left liver (L hemihepatectomy):** Segments II, III, IV
- **Right lobe + Caudate:** Most common in living donor transplant

### Blood Supply

#### Portal Vein (75% of blood, 50% of oxygen)
```
                SMV + Splenic vein
                        │
                   PORTAL VEIN
                  (10-12 cm long)
                        │
              ┌─────────┴─────────┐
              │                   │
         Right branch        Left branch
         (shorter, wider)    (longer)
              │                   │
         R anterior         Segments II,III,IV
         R posterior
```

#### Hepatic Artery (25% of blood, 50% of oxygen)

```
        CELIAC TRUNK
              │
        Common hepatic a.
              │
    ┌─────────┴─────────┐
    │                   │
Gastroduodenal a.   Proper hepatic a.
                         │
               ┌─────────┴─────────┐
               │                   │
          Right hepatic a.    Left hepatic a.
               │
          Cystic artery
          (usually from R hepatic)
```

**Anatomical Variations (IMPORTANT for surgery):**
- Replaced right hepatic from SMA (15-20%)
- Replaced left hepatic from left gastric (10%)
- Accessory arteries common

#### Hepatic Veins (Drainage)

```
        RIGHT  MIDDLE  LEFT
          │      │      │
          └──────┼──────┘
                 │
                IVC
                 │
        (enters IVC just below diaphragm)
```

**Note:** Caudate lobe (Segment I) drains directly to IVC via small hepatic veins

### Lymphatic Drainage

- **Superficial:** To diaphragmatic, mediastinal nodes
- **Deep:** To hepatic nodes at porta → Celiac nodes

### Nerve Supply

- **Sympathetic:** Celiac plexus (T7-T10)
- **Parasympathetic:** Vagus (anterior trunk)
- **Sensory:** Right phrenic nerve (referred pain to right shoulder)

### Relations

| Surface | Structures |
|---------|------------|
| **Superior** | Diaphragm (right dome), Pericardium |
| **Anterior** | Anterior abdominal wall, Diaphragm |
| **Posterior** | IVC, Right adrenal, Right kidney, Bare area |
| **Inferior** | Stomach, Duodenum (D1), Hepatic flexure, Gallbladder |

### Hepatic Histology (Lobule Models)

| Model | Central Structure | Peripheral Structure |
|-------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Classic lobule** | Central vein | Portal triads at corners |
| **Portal lobule** | Portal triad | Central veins at corners |
| **Hepatic acinus** | Portal triad | Central vein (Zone 3) |

**Zones of Hepatic Acinus:**
- **Zone 1:** Periportal - highest O₂, first to regenerate, last to die
- **Zone 2:** Intermediate
- **Zone 3:** Pericentral - lowest O₂, first to die in hypoxia, acetaminophen toxicity

## Clinical Correlations

### Liver Cirrhosis
- **Portal hypertension:** >10 mmHg gradient
- **Collaterals:** Esophageal varices, caput medusae, hemorrhoids
- **Ascites:** Low albumin, high portal pressure

### Hepatocellular Carcinoma
- **Risk:** Cirrhosis (any cause), Hep B/C, Aflatoxin
- **Screening:** AFP + Ultrasound every 6 months in high-risk
- **Treatment:** Resection, Transplant, TACE, Ablation

### Liver Resections
- **Segmentectomy:** Single segment
- **Right hepatectomy:** Segments V-VIII
- **Left hepatectomy:** Segments II-IV
- **Extended right:** Right + Segment IV
- **Future liver remnant:** Must be >25-30% of total liver volume

### Hepatic Trauma
- **Most injured abdominal organ** in blunt trauma
- **Management:** Hemodynamically stable → CT + conservative
- **Pringle maneuver:** Compress portal triad to control hemorrhage

### Budd-Chiari Syndrome
- **Obstruction:** Hepatic veins or IVC
- **Triad:** Abdominal pain, Hepatomegaly, Ascites
- **Caudate hypertrophy:** Because segment I drains directly to IVC

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Portal vein** forms behind neck of pancreas (SMV + Splenic)
2. **Right hepatic artery** crosses BEHIND common hepatic duct
3. **Bare area** is between layers of coronary ligament
4. **Caudate lobe (Segment I)** has independent blood supply AND venous drainage
5. **Calot's triangle:** Cystic duct, Common hepatic duct, Liver edge (contains cystic artery)
6. **Pringle maneuver** controls hepatic artery and portal vein bleeding
7. **Referred pain** to right shoulder = Phrenic nerve (C3,4,5)
8. **Zone 3** (pericentral) most susceptible to hypoxia and toxins
9. **Replaced right hepatic** from SMA - look for it in cholecystectomy!
10. **Living donor transplant:** Usually right lobe (segments V-VIII) or left lateral (II-III)

### Surface Anatomy
- **Upper border:** 5th rib in MCL (right dome of diaphragm)
- **Lower border:** Right costal margin (may be palpable 1-2 cm below)
- **Span in MCL:** 12-15 cm (percussion)
