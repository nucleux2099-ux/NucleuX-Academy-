# Autonomic Nerves of Abdomen — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Sympathetic supply to abdomen via splanchnic nerves (T5–L2) synapsing in prevertebral ganglia (coeliac, superior/inferior mesenteric). Parasympathetic via vagus (foregut/midgut) and pelvic splanchnics S2–S4 (hindgut). Coeliac plexus is the largest autonomic plexus at L1.

## High Yield Points

★ Greater splanchnic nerve: **T5–T9** → coeliac ganglion → foregut
★ Lesser splanchnic: **T10–T11** → aorticorenal ganglion
★ Vagus supplies up to **left 2/3 of transverse colon** (splenic flexure)
★ Pelvic splanchnics (**S2–S4**) supply hindgut distal to splenic flexure
★ Referred pain: foregut = **epigastric**, midgut = **periumbilical**, hindgut = **suprapubic**
★ Coeliac plexus at **L1** — block for pancreatic cancer pain
★ **Hirschsprung's disease**: absent parasympathetic ganglia (neural crest migration failure)
★ Lumbar sympathectomy: **L2–L3** ganglia for PVD
★ Right sympathetic trunk lies behind **IVC**
★ White rami communicantes only from **L1–L2** in lumbar region

## Mnemonics

**Splanchnic nerves:** "**5-9 Greater, 10-11 Lesser, 12 Least**" — descending order of size

**Vagal territory cutoff:** "**V for Vagus, V for left 2/3 transVerse colon**"

**Referred pain levels:** "**5-9 Epi, 10-11 Umbi, 12-L2 Hypo**"
