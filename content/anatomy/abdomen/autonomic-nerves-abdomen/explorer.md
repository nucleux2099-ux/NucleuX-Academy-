# Autonomic Nerves of the Abdomen

## Overview
The autonomic nervous system in the abdomen controls visceral functions including gut motility, secretion, blood vessel tone, and glandular activity. It comprises the sympathetic chain, prevertebral plexuses and ganglia, and parasympathetic fibres from the vagus nerve (foregut and midgut) and pelvic splanchnic nerves (hindgut).

## Key Points
- Sympathetic supply: T5–L2 (splanchnic nerves → prevertebral ganglia)
- Parasympathetic: vagus (foregut + midgut), pelvic splanchnic S2–S4 (hindgut)
- Major prevertebral ganglia: coeliac, aorticorenal, superior & inferior mesenteric
- Pain from viscera travels with sympathetic fibres (referred pain)
- Parasympathetic = secretomotor and motor to gut wall

## Gross Anatomy

### Sympathetic Supply

#### Lumbar Sympathetic Trunk
- Continuation of thoracic sympathetic trunk behind the medial arcuate ligament
- 4 ganglia on anterolateral surface of lumbar vertebral bodies
- Right trunk lies behind IVC; left trunk lies on left side of aorta
- Gives grey rami communicantes to all lumbar spinal nerves
- Receives white rami communicantes from L1–L2 only

#### Splanchnic Nerves
| Nerve | Origin | Synapse | Distribution |
|-------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Greater splanchnic | T5–T9 | Coeliac ganglion | Foregut |
| Lesser splanchnic | T10–T11 | Aorticorenal ganglion | Midgut/kidney |
| Least splanchnic | T12 | Renal plexus | Kidney |
| Lumbar splanchnic | L1–L2 | Inferior mesenteric & superior hypogastric plexus | Hindgut, pelvis |

### Parasympathetic Supply
- **Vagus nerve:** Provides parasympathetic fibres to foregut and midgut (up to splenic flexure/left 2/3 of transverse colon)
- **Pelvic splanchnic nerves (S2–S4):** Supply hindgut (distal 1/3 transverse colon to upper anal canal)

### Prevertebral Ganglia & Plexuses

#### Coeliac Plexus (Solar Plexus)
- Largest autonomic plexus
- Located around the origin of coeliac trunk at L1
- Composed of coeliac ganglia + contributions from greater and lesser splanchnic nerves + vagus
- Gives subsidiary plexuses: hepatic, splenic, gastric, renal, suprarenal

#### Superior Mesenteric Plexus
- Around the origin of SMA
- Supplies midgut derivatives

#### Inferior Mesenteric Plexus
- Around the origin of IMA
- Supplies hindgut derivatives

#### Intermesenteric Plexus
- Connects superior and inferior mesenteric plexuses along the aorta

#### Superior Hypogastric Plexus
- Below aortic bifurcation (presacral nerve)
- Continuation into pelvis

### Aorticorenal Ganglia
- Located at origin of renal arteries
- Lesser and least splanchnic nerves synapse here

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Coeliac plexus: deep to stomach and pancreas, at L1 level
- Lumbar sympathetic trunk: medial to psoas major
- Right trunk lies behind IVC; left trunk at left border of aorta

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply
- The ganglia and plexuses are supplied by small branches from adjacent arteries
- Nerve supply is intrinsic (autonomic ganglia contain postganglionic cell bodies)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Coeliac Plexus Block
- Used for intractable pain from upper abdominal malignancies (especially pancreatic cancer)
- Needle guided under fluoroscopy/CT to L1 level

### Lumbar Sympathectomy
- For peripheral vascular disease of the lower limb
- Surgical or chemical destruction of L2–L3 ganglia
- Improves skin blood flow (not muscle blood flow)

### Vagotomy
- Truncal, selective, or highly selective (parietal cell vagotomy)
- For peptic ulcer disease (historical, before PPIs)
- Truncal vagotomy → gastric stasis, diarrhoea (denervation of entire abdomen)

### Hirschsprung's Disease
- Absence of parasympathetic ganglia in the myenteric and submucosal plexuses
- Most commonly in rectosigmoid region
- Functional obstruction — aganglionic segment cannot relax

### Referred Pain Patterns
- Foregut (T5–T9): epigastric region
- Midgut (T10–T11): periumbilical region
- Hindgut (T12–L2): hypogastric/suprapubic region

## Development
- Sympathetic ganglia derived from neural crest cells
- Parasympathetic ganglia in gut wall (enteric nervous system) also from neural crest
- Failure of neural crest migration → Hirschsprung's disease
