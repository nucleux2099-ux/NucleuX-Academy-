# Appendix — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
The vermiform appendix arises from the posteromedial wall of the caecum at the convergence of three taeniae coli. It has a constant base (McBurney's point) but variable tip position (retrocaecal 65%). Supplied by the appendicular artery (end artery, branch of ileocolic). Referred pain at umbilicus (T10).

## High Yield Points

★ Most common position of appendix tip: **retrocaecal** (65%)
★ McBurney's point: junction of **lateral 1/3 and medial 2/3** of line from ASIS to umbilicus
★ Appendicular artery is an **end artery** — thrombosis → gangrene
★ Three **taeniae coli converge** at the base of appendix — surgical landmark
★ Referred pain: **periumbilical (T10)** → shifts to RIF (somatic)
★ Psoas sign: pain on hip extension → **retrocaecal** appendicitis
★ Obturator sign: pain on internal rotation → **pelvic** appendicitis
★ Most common site of **carcinoid tumour** in GIT
★ Rich in **lymphoid tissue** — "abdominal tonsil"
★ Blood supply: appendicular artery → ileocolic → SMA

## Mnemonics

**Positions of appendix tip:** "**Really Pleased Surgeon Packs It**" — Retrocaecal, Pelvic, Subcaecal, Preileal, Postileal

**Appendicitis signs:** "**ROMP**" — Rovsing's, Obturator, McBurney's, Psoas
