# Kidney & Retroperitoneum — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Kidneys are retroperitoneal, right lower than left. Hilum at L1: vein, artery, pelvis (anterior to posterior). Five segments, all end arteries. Coverings: fibrous capsule → perirenal fat → renal fascia (Gerota's) → pararenal fat. Left renal vein crosses anterior to aorta.

## High Yield Points

★ Right kidney: **T12–L3**; Left kidney: **T11–L2** (left is higher)
★ Hilum structures (anterior → posterior): **Vein, Artery, Pelvis** (VAP)
★ Renal arteries are **end arteries** — occlusion causes segmental infarction
★ **Brödel's bloodless line**: lateral border, between anterior and posterior segments
★ Left renal vein receives **left gonadal + left suprarenal** veins
★ **Horseshoe kidney**: lower poles fused, arrested at IMA (L3)
★ Coverings outside-in: **pararenal fat → Gerota's → perirenal fat → fibrous capsule**
★ Posterior relations include **12th rib, diaphragm, psoas, quadratus lumborum**
★ Referred pain from kidney: **T10–L1** (loin to groin)
★ Development from **metanephros** (ureteric bud + metanephric mesoderm)

## Mnemonics

**Hilum structures (ant → post):** "**VAP**" — Vein, Artery, Pelvis

**Coverings outside-in:** "**PG-PF**" — Pararenal fat, Gerota's fascia, Perirenal fat, Fibrous capsule

**Anterior relations of left kidney:** "**SSS-PCJ**" — Suprarenal, Spleen, Stomach, Pancreas, Colic flexure, Jejunum
