# Kidney & Retroperitoneum

## Overview
The kidneys are paired retroperitoneal organs that lie on the posterior abdominal wall, one on each side of the vertebral column (T12–L3). They are responsible for urine formation, electrolyte balance, and endocrine functions. The right kidney is slightly lower than the left due to the liver. Each kidney is enclosed in a fibrous capsule, surrounded by perirenal fat, renal fascia (Gerota's), and pararenal fat.

## Key Points
- Retroperitoneal, bean-shaped, ~11 × 6 × 3 cm
- Right kidney lower than left (T12–L3 vs T11–L2)
- Hilum at L1 (transpyloric plane) — structures from anterior to posterior: Renal Vein, Artery, Pelvis (VAP)
- Renal artery is an end artery — segmental branches are also end arteries
- Five renal segments: apical, upper, middle, lower, posterior

## Gross Anatomy

### External Features
- Convex lateral border, concave medial border with hilum
- Upper pole capped by suprarenal gland
- Two surfaces (anterior, posterior), two borders (lateral, medial), two poles

### Coverings (Outside In)
1. Pararenal fat
2. Renal fascia (Gerota's fascia) — anterior and posterior layers
3. Perirenal fat (perinephric fat)
4. Fibrous capsule (true capsule)

### Relations

**Right Kidney:**
| Surface | Relations |
|---------|-----------|
| Anterior | Suprarenal, liver (large area), D2, right colic flexure, small intestine |
| Posterior | Diaphragm, 12th rib, psoas, quadratus lumborum, transversus abdominis, subcostal (T12), iliohypogastric, ilioinguinal nerves |

**Left Kidney:**
| Surface | Relations |
|---------|-----------|
| Anterior | Suprarenal, spleen, stomach, pancreas (body/tail), left colic flexure, jejunum |
| Posterior | Same as right + 11th rib (left kidney is higher) |

### Internal Structure
- Cortex: outer, contains glomeruli and convoluted tubules, cortical columns of Bertin
- Medulla: inner, contains 8–18 renal pyramids; apices = renal papillae
- Minor calyces (8–18) → major calyces (2–3) → renal pelvis → ureter

### Renal Segments (Brödel's)
1. Apical (superior)
2. Upper anterior
3. Middle anterior (largest)
4. Lower
5. Posterior

- **Brödel's bloodless line:** between anterior and posterior segments on lateral border — optimal site for nephrotomy

## Relations & Surface Markings
- **Morris' quadrilateral:** surface marking of kidney on posterior abdominal wall
  - Medial border: 2.5 cm from midline
  - Lateral border: 9 cm from midline
  - Upper pole: 12th thoracic spine
  - Lower pole: L3 spine
- **Hilum:** transpyloric plane (L1)

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Arterial
- Renal artery (from aorta at L1–L2) → segmental arteries (5) → lobar → interlobar → arcuate → interlobular → afferent arterioles → glomeruli
- Left renal artery shorter course; Right renal artery longer, passes behind IVC
- **End arteries** — occlusion → segmental infarction

### Venous
- Follows arteries in reverse: interlobular → arcuate → interlobar → renal vein → IVC
- Left renal vein crosses anterior to aorta, receives left gonadal + left suprarenal veins

### Lymphatic
- Para-aortic lymph nodes (lateral aortic nodes)

### Nerve Supply
- Renal plexus: T10–L1 (sympathetic), vagus (parasympathetic)
- Referred pain: T10–L1 dermatome (loin, groin)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Renal Angle Tenderness
- Angle between 12th rib and erector spinae — tender in pyelonephritis

### Polycystic Kidney Disease
- ADPKD: bilateral enlarged kidneys with multiple cysts

### Horseshoe Kidney
- Fusion of lower poles across midline
- Arrested ascent at IMA (L3) — cannot pass above it
- Increased risk of obstruction at PUJ

### Ectopic Kidney
- Usually pelvic (fails to ascend)
- Blood supply from local vessels (iliac arteries)

### Nephrectomy
- Approach: loin incision (12th rib bed) or transperitoneal
- Renal pedicle: vein anterior, artery behind, pelvis most posterior
- Ligate renal artery before vein to allow drainage

### Renal Transplant
- Placed in iliac fossa (heterotopic)
- Renal artery anastomosed to external/internal iliac artery
- Ureter implanted into bladder

## Development
- Develops from metanephros (definitive kidney)
- Ureteric bud (from mesonephric duct) → collecting system
- Metanephric mesoderm → nephrons
- Ascends from pelvis to lumbar region during development
- Fetal lobulation may persist
