# Abdominal Aorta

## Overview
The abdominal aorta is the largest artery in the abdominal cavity, extending from the aortic hiatus of the diaphragm (T12) to its bifurcation at the level of L4 vertebra into the two common iliac arteries. It supplies the abdominal viscera, walls, and lower limbs.

## Key Points
- Enters abdomen through aortic hiatus at T12 level
- Lies on the anterior surface of the vertebral bodies (L1–L4)
- Bifurcates at L4 (level of umbilicus / iliac crest)
- Gives off paired and unpaired visceral branches, and parietal branches
- Surrounded by the aortic plexus of sympathetic nerves

## Gross Anatomy

### Course & Extent
- **Beginning:** Aortic hiatus of diaphragm at T12 (midline)
- **Termination:** Bifurcation at L4 into right and left common iliac arteries
- **Length:** Approximately 13 cm
- **Caliber:** ~2 cm at origin, narrows to ~1.75 cm at bifurcation

### Relations

| Aspect | Structures |
|--------|-----------|
| **Anterior** | Lesser omentum, stomach, pancreas (body), splenic vein, left renal vein, third part of duodenum, coils of small intestine, peritoneum |
| **Posterior** | Vertebral bodies L1–L4, anterior longitudinal ligament, left lumbar veins |
| **Right** | Right crus of diaphragm (above), IVC, cisterna chyli, azygos vein, right coeliac ganglion |
| **Left** | Left crus of diaphragm, left coeliac ganglion, left sympathetic trunk |

### Branches

#### Unpaired Visceral Branches (Ventral)
1. **Coeliac trunk** (T12) — supplies foregut
2. **Superior mesenteric artery** (L1) — supplies midgut
3. **Inferior mesenteric artery** (L3) — supplies hindgut

#### Paired Visceral Branches (Lateral)
1. **Middle suprarenal arteries** (L1)
2. **Renal arteries** (L1–L2)
3. **Gonadal arteries** (testicular/ovarian) (L2)

#### Parietal Branches
1. **Inferior phrenic arteries** (paired) — first branches
2. **Lumbar arteries** (4 pairs) — segmental
3. **Median sacral artery** — from posterior surface just above bifurcation

## Surface Marking
- A line from a point slightly left of midline at the transpyloric plane (L1) to a point 1.25 cm below and to the left of the umbilicus (L4)
- Bifurcation: at the level of the highest point of the iliac crest (supracristal plane)

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply
- **Vasa vasorum** supply the aortic wall
- **Aortic plexus** (sympathetic) surrounds the vessel — derived from coeliac, superior and inferior mesenteric, and intermesenteric plexuses

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA)
- Most common below the renal arteries (infrarenal)
- Risk factors: atherosclerosis, hypertension, smoking, male sex, age > 65
- Pulsatile expansile mass in the abdomen
- Rupture → catastrophic retroperitoneal haemorrhage
- Screening: ultrasound in males > 65 years

### Aortic Coarctation
- Rare in abdominal aorta; usually thoracic

### Leriche Syndrome
- Aortoiliac occlusive disease
- Triad: claudication, absent femoral pulses, erectile dysfunction

### Surgical Anatomy
- Left renal vein crosses anterior to aorta — important during surgery
- Third part of duodenum crosses anterior — landmark for SMA syndrome
- IVC lies to the right — risk during retroperitoneal dissection

## Development
- Derived from the dorsal aorta (paired → fused)
- Ventral branches (coeliac, SMA, IMA) represent persistent vitelline artery roots
- Lateral branches (renal, gonadal) arise from lateral mesonephric arteries
- Posterior branches (lumbar) from intersegmental arteries
