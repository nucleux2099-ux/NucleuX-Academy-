# Abdominal Aorta — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
The abdominal aorta extends from the aortic hiatus (T12) to its bifurcation at L4 into common iliac arteries. It gives unpaired ventral branches (coeliac, SMA, IMA) to the gut, paired lateral branches (renal, gonadal, suprarenal), and parietal branches (inferior phrenic, lumbar, median sacral).

## High Yield Points

★ Aortic hiatus is at **T12** level — transmits aorta, thoracic duct, azygos vein
★ Bifurcation at **L4** — level of iliac crest / umbilicus
★ **Left renal vein** crosses anterior to aorta (nutcracker position)
★ **Third part of duodenum** crosses anterior — SMA syndrome
★ Unpaired ventral branches: Coeliac (T12), SMA (L1), IMA (L3)
★ First branches = **inferior phrenic arteries**
★ AAA most common **infrarenal** — pulsatile expansile mass
★ **Leriche syndrome**: aortoiliac occlusion → claudication, impotence, absent femoral pulses
★ IVC lies to the **right** of the aorta
★ Median sacral artery arises from **posterior** surface just above bifurcation
★ Aorta lies on **anterior longitudinal ligament** over vertebral bodies
