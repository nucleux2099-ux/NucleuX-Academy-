# Superior Mesenteric Artery — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Arises at L1 from aorta, supplies midgut (lower D2 to proximal 2/3 transverse colon). Branches: inferior pancreaticoduodenal, jejunal, ileal, ileocolic, right colic, middle colic. Crosses D3 anteriorly. SMV lies to its right.

## High Yield Points

★ Arises at **L1**, 1 cm below coeliac trunk
★ Crosses **D3** anteriorly → SMA syndrome
★ **Ileocolic artery** gives appendicular artery (end artery)
★ Jejunum: **long vasa recta, few arcades**; Ileum: **short vasa recta, many arcades**
★ SMV lies to **right** of SMA → join splenic vein → portal vein
★ Root of mesentery: DJ flexure to **right sacroiliac joint** (15 cm)
★ SMA embolism: most common cause of **acute mesenteric ischaemia** (from AF)
★ Midgut rotates **270° counterclockwise** around SMA axis
★ Left renal vein passes **between SMA and aorta** posteriorly (nutcracker)
★ Marginal artery connects SMA + IMA; watershed at **splenic flexure**

## Mnemonics

**SMA branches:** "**I Just Immediately Ran into My Internist**" — Inferior pancreaticoduodenal, Jejunal, Ileal, Right colic, (middle) colic, Ileocolic

**Jejunum vs Ileum arcades:** "**Jejunum = Just a few arcades; Ileum = Incredibly many**"
