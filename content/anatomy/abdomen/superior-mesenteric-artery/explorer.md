# Superior Mesenteric Artery (SMA)

## Overview
The SMA is the second unpaired ventral branch of the abdominal aorta, arising at L1 level. It supplies the midgut derivatives — from the lower duodenum (below major papilla) to the proximal two-thirds of the transverse colon. It is a major artery with important branches and clinical significance.

## Key Points
- Arises from anterior surface of aorta at L1, behind neck of pancreas
- Supplies midgut: lower D2 to proximal 2/3 transverse colon
- Major branches: inferior pancreaticoduodenal, jejunal, ileal, ileocolic, right colic, middle colic
- Crosses D3 of duodenum anteriorly (SMA syndrome)
- SMV lies to its right; together they form the root of mesentery

## Gross Anatomy

### Origin & Course
- Arises from aorta at L1, 1 cm below coeliac trunk
- Passes behind the neck of pancreas and splenic vein
- Crosses anterior to D3 (and left renal vein, uncinate process)
- Enters the root of mesentery, descending to right iliac fossa

### Branches
| Branch | Supply |
|--------|--------|
| Inferior pancreaticoduodenal | Head of pancreas, D2–D3 |
| Jejunal arteries (4–5) | Jejunum — long vasa recta, few arcades |
| Ileal arteries (12+) | Ileum — short vasa recta, many arcades |
| Ileocolic | Terminal ileum, caecum, appendix (appendicular artery) |
| Right colic | Ascending colon |
| Middle colic | Transverse colon (proximal 2/3) |

### Relations
| Aspect | Structures |
|--------|-----------|
| Anterior | Neck of pancreas, splenic vein (at origin); small intestine mesentery |
| Posterior | Left renal vein, uncinate process of pancreas, D3 |
| Right | SMV |
| Left | Aorta |

### Root of Mesentery
- 15 cm long, runs from DJ flexure (L2) to right sacroiliac joint
- Contains SMA, SMV, lymph nodes, nerves, fat
- Crosses: D3, aorta, IVC, right ureter, right psoas, right gonadal vessels

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Origin at L1, 1 cm below coeliac trunk
- Crosses D3 at L3 level
- Root of mesentery runs obliquely from left upper to right lower

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply
- SMA itself is a major artery (supplies midgut)
- **Venous:** SMV lies to the right; drains same territory → joins splenic vein → portal vein
- **Nerve supply:** Superior mesenteric plexus (sympathetic T10–T11, parasympathetic vagus)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### SMA Syndrome (Wilkie's Syndrome)
- Compression of D3 between SMA and aorta
- Reduced aortomesenteric angle (<20°) or distance (<8 mm)
- Occurs in rapid weight loss (loss of mesenteric fat pad)
- Symptoms: postprandial pain, bilious vomiting

### Midgut Volvulus
- Rotation of midgut around SMA axis
- Associated with malrotation (failure of 270° rotation)
- Surgical emergency — can cause total midgut ischaemia

### Mesenteric Ischaemia
- SMA embolism (most common cause of acute mesenteric ischaemia)
- Atrial fibrillation → embolus lodges in SMA
- "Pain out of proportion to findings"

### Difference: Jejunum vs Ileum
| Feature | Jejunum | Ileum |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Vasa recta | Long | Short |
| Arcades | Few (1–2) | Many (4–5) |
| Wall | Thicker | Thinner |
| Fat in mesentery | Less | More |
| Peyer's patches | Absent/few | Many |

### Marginal Artery of Drummond
- Anastomotic arcade connecting SMA and IMA along colon
- Watershed at splenic flexure (Griffith's point)

## Development
- SMA represents the artery of the midgut
- Midgut loop herniation (week 6) and rotation (270° counterclockwise) around SMA axis
- Failure of rotation → malrotation → Ladd's bands → volvulus
