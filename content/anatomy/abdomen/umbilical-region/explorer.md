# Umbilical Region

## Overview
The umbilical region is the central of the nine regions of the abdomen, centered on the umbilicus. The umbilicus is the scar of the detached umbilical cord, located at the level of L3–L4 vertebra (variable). It is an important surface landmark, dermatome reference (T10), and site of several clinical conditions including umbilical hernia and Sister Mary Joseph nodule.

## Key Points
- Umbilicus at L3–L4 (varies with body habitus)
- T10 dermatome
- Deep structures: median umbilical ligament (urachus), medial umbilical ligaments (obliterated umbilical arteries), round ligament of liver (obliterated umbilical vein)
- Surrounded by the umbilical ring — weak area, site of umbilical hernia
- Paraumbilical veins connect portal system to systemic veins (caput medusae in portal hypertension)

## Gross Anatomy

### Surface Anatomy
- Central landmark of anterior abdominal wall
- Level: L3–L4 (at or near transtubercular plane)
- Dermatome: T10

### Structures Deep to Umbilicus

| Structure | Adult Remnant | Fetal Structure |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Median umbilical ligament | In median umbilical fold | Urachus (allantois) |
| Medial umbilical ligament (paired) | In medial umbilical folds | Umbilical arteries |
| Round ligament of liver (ligamentum teres) | In falciform ligament | Umbilical vein |
| Paraumbilical veins | Along round ligament | Periumbilical venous connections |

### Peritoneal Folds on Inner Surface of Anterior Abdominal Wall
- **Median umbilical fold:** Over median umbilical ligament (urachus)
- **Medial umbilical fold (paired):** Over obliterated umbilical arteries
- **Lateral umbilical fold (paired):** Over inferior epigastric vessels
- Between these folds: supravesical, medial inguinal, and lateral inguinal fossae

### Umbilical Ring
- Fibrous ring in the linea alba
- Weak spot; covered only by skin, fascia, and peritoneum (no muscle layer)
- Site of umbilical hernia

### Nine Regions of Abdomen
The abdomen is divided by two vertical (midclavicular) and two horizontal (subcostal, transtubercular) lines:
1. Right hypochondrium, Epigastrium, Left hypochondrium
2. Right lumbar, Umbilical, Left lumbar
3. Right iliac, Hypogastrium, Left iliac

### Viscera in Umbilical Region
- Transverse colon, omentum, coils of jejunum and ileum, aorta bifurcation (below)

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Transpyloric plane (L1): midway between jugular notch and pubic symphysis
- Transtubercular plane (L5): between iliac tubercles
- Subcostal plane (L3): lower border of 10th costal cartilage
- Umbilicus marks T10 dermatome and approximately L3–L4 vertebral level

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply
- **Periumbilical skin:** Superficial epigastric arteries, branches of intercostal arteries
- **Nerve supply:** T10 intercostal nerve (dermatome)
- **Paraumbilical veins:** Along round ligament of liver, connect portal to systemic venous system

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Umbilical Hernia
- **Infantile:** Through umbilical ring, common in premature babies, usually resolves by age 3
- **Adult (paraumbilical):** Through linea alba near umbilicus, more common in obese multiparous women
- Risk of strangulation higher in adults

### Sister Mary Joseph Nodule
- Metastatic nodule at the umbilicus
- Most commonly from gastric or ovarian cancer
- Spreads via peritoneal or lymphatic route along round ligament

### Caput Medusae
- Dilated paraumbilical veins radiating from umbilicus
- Seen in portal hypertension
- Flow direction: away from umbilicus

### Patent Urachus
- Persistent connection between bladder and umbilicus
- Urine discharge from umbilicus
- Urachal cyst if partially patent

### Omphalitis
- Infection of umbilical stump in neonates
- Can lead to portal pyaemia → portal vein thrombosis

### Laparoscopic Surgery
- Umbilicus is common site for primary port insertion
- Thinnest part of abdominal wall (no muscle layer)

## Development
- Umbilical cord contains: two umbilical arteries, one umbilical vein, allantois (urachus), vitellointestinal duct, Wharton's jelly
- After birth: arteries → medial umbilical ligaments, vein → round ligament, urachus → median umbilical ligament
- Physiological herniation of midgut through umbilicus (weeks 6–10) → returns by week 10
