# Umbilical Region — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Umbilicus at L3–L4, T10 dermatome. Deep structures are remnants of fetal structures: median umbilical ligament (urachus), medial umbilical ligaments (umbilical arteries), round ligament of liver (umbilical vein). Paraumbilical veins = portosystemic anastomosis site. Umbilical ring = weak spot for hernia.

## High Yield Points

★ Umbilicus dermatome: **T10**
★ Median umbilical ligament = remnant of **urachus** (allantois)
★ Medial umbilical ligaments = remnant of **umbilical arteries**
★ Round ligament of liver = remnant of **umbilical vein** (in falciform ligament)
★ **Caput medusae**: dilated paraumbilical veins in portal hypertension
★ **Sister Mary Joseph nodule**: metastatic deposit at umbilicus (gastric/ovarian cancer)
★ Lateral umbilical fold contains **inferior epigastric vessels**
★ Between medial and lateral folds: **medial inguinal fossa** (direct hernia site)
★ Umbilical ring has **no muscle layer** — only skin, fascia, peritoneum
★ Patent urachus → **urine discharge** from umbilicus

## Mnemonics

**Fetal remnants at umbilicus:** "**MUM**" — Median = Urachus, Medial = Umbilical arteries, (round ligament = umbilical vein)

**Peritoneal folds (medial to lateral):** "**Median, Medial, Lateral**" — Urachus, Umbilical arteries, Epigastric vessels

**Umbilical cord contents:** "**2 Arteries, 1 Vein, Urachus, Vitellointestinal duct, Wharton's jelly**"
