# Lumbar Region

## Overview
The lumbar region refers to the posterior abdominal wall between the 12th rib and the iliac crest. It contains important muscles (psoas major, quadratus lumborum, iliacus), the lumbar plexus, lumbar sympathetic trunk, and is traversed by the kidneys and ureters. It is clinically significant for lumbar puncture, kidney surgery, and understanding back pain.

## Key Points
- Posterior abdominal wall muscles: psoas major, psoas minor, quadratus lumborum, iliacus, transversus abdominis
- Lumbar plexus (L1–L4) formed within psoas major
- Lumbar triangle (of Petit) and superior lumbar triangle (of Grynfeltt) — sites of lumbar hernia
- Lumbar vertebrae: largest, body is kidney-shaped, no costal facets, no transverse foramina
- Five lumbar vertebrae (L1–L5); spinal cord ends at L1–L2 (conus medullaris)

## Gross Anatomy

### Muscles of Posterior Abdominal Wall

| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Action | Nerve Supply |
|--------|--------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Psoas major | T12–L5 bodies, transverse processes, intervertebral discs | Lesser trochanter (with iliacus) | Hip flexion, lateral flexion of trunk | L1–L3 ventral rami |
| Iliacus | Iliac fossa, ala of sacrum | Lesser trochanter (with psoas) | Hip flexion | Femoral nerve (L2–L4) |
| Quadratus lumborum | Iliac crest, iliolumbar ligament | 12th rib, L1–L4 transverse processes | Fixes 12th rib in respiration, lateral flexion | T12–L4 |
| Psoas minor | T12–L1 bodies | Pectineal line | Weak trunk flexor | L1 |

### Fasciae
- **Psoas fascia:** Covers psoas major, thickened superiorly as medial arcuate ligament
- **Quadratus lumborum fascia:** Covered by anterior layer of thoracolumbar fascia
- **Thoracolumbar fascia:** Three layers — anterior (covers QL), middle (between QL and erector spinae), posterior (covers erector spinae)

### Lumbar Triangles

**Inferior Lumbar Triangle (of Petit):**
- Boundaries: iliac crest (base), external oblique (anterior), latissimus dorsi (posterior)
- Floor: internal oblique
- Site of inferior lumbar hernia

**Superior Lumbar Triangle (of Grynfeltt):**
- Boundaries: 12th rib (superior), internal oblique (anterior), erector spinae (posterior)
- Floor: transversus abdominis aponeurosis
- More common site of lumbar hernia

### Lumbar Plexus (within psoas major)
Formed by ventral rami of L1–L4 (with contribution from T12):
- **Iliohypogastric (T12, L1)**
- **Ilioinguinal (L1)**
- **Genitofemoral (L1, L2)**
- **Lateral cutaneous nerve of thigh (L2, L3)**
- **Femoral nerve (L2, L3, L4)** — largest branch
- **Obturator nerve (L2, L3, L4)**
- **Accessory obturator (L3, L4)** — inconstant

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Kidneys lie on the posterior abdominal wall, related to psoas and quadratus lumborum
- Renal angle: between 12th rib and erector spinae — tender in pyelonephritis
- Lumbar puncture performed at L3–L4 or L4–L5 (below conus medullaris)

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply
- **Lumbar arteries (4 pairs):** from abdominal aorta, segmental
- **Subcostal artery:** runs below 12th rib
- **Iliolumbar artery:** from internal iliac
- **Lumbar veins:** drain into IVC (or ascending lumbar vein → azygos system)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Lumbar Puncture
- Performed at L3–L4 interspace (spinal cord ends at L1–L2)
- Layers pierced: skin → superficial fascia → supraspinous ligament → interspinous ligament → ligamentum flavum → epidural space → dura → arachnoid → subarachnoid space

### Psoas Abscess
- Tuberculous abscess of lumbar vertebrae tracks along psoas sheath
- Presents as swelling in the groin (below inguinal ligament in femoral triangle)
- "Cold abscess" — no signs of acute inflammation

### Lumbar Hernia
- Through lumbar triangles (Petit or Grynfeltt)
- Superior triangle hernia more common

### Kidney Surgery
- Loin approach: incision along 12th rib
- Structures at risk: subcostal nerve (T12), iliohypogastric and ilioinguinal nerves

### Psoas Sign
- Pain on extension of hip (stretching inflamed psoas)
- Positive in retrocaecal appendicitis, psoas abscess

## Development
- Lumbar vertebrae develop from sclerotomes of somites
- Muscles from myotomes of lumbar somites
- Lumbar plexus from ventral rami of lumbar spinal nerves
