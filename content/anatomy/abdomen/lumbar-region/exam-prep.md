# Lumbar Region — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Posterior abdominal wall muscles: psoas major (contains lumbar plexus), quadratus lumborum, iliacus. Lumbar plexus (L1–L4) gives femoral and obturator nerves. Two lumbar triangles (Petit, Grynfeltt) — sites of hernia. Lumbar puncture at L3–L4 (below conus at L1–L2).

## High Yield Points

★ Lumbar plexus forms **within psoas major** muscle
★ **Femoral nerve (L2,3,4)**: largest branch of lumbar plexus, emerges lateral to psoas
★ **Obturator nerve (L2,3,4)**: emerges medial border of psoas
★ Conus medullaris ends at **L1–L2** — LP done at L3–L4
★ **Quadratus lumborum** fixes 12th rib during inspiration
★ **Psoas abscess** (TB spine) tracks along psoas → presents in femoral triangle
★ Superior lumbar triangle (Grynfeltt) — **more common** site of lumbar hernia
★ Thoracolumbar fascia has **3 layers** — anterior, middle, posterior
★ Psoas + iliacus = **iliopsoas** — chief hip flexor, inserts on lesser trochanter
★ LP layers: skin → supraspinous → interspinous → **ligamentum flavum** → dura → arachnoid

## Mnemonics

**Lumbar plexus branches (T12–L4):** "**I I Get Lots of Females On Fridays**" — Iliohypogastric, Ilioinguinal, Genitofemoral, Lateral cutaneous of thigh, Femoral, Obturator

**Petit's triangle:** "**ELI**" — External oblique (anterior), Latissimus dorsi (posterior), Iliac crest (inferior)
