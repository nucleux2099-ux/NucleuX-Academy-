# Portal Vein — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Formed behind neck of pancreas (L2) by SMV + splenic vein. Ascends in hepatoduodenal ligament (most posterior in portal triad). No valves. Divides at porta hepatis. Five sites of portosystemic anastomosis open in portal hypertension.

## High Yield Points

★ Formed by **SMV + splenic vein** behind neck of pancreas
★ **No valves** — bidirectional flow possible
★ In hepatoduodenal ligament: portal vein is **most posterior**, CBD on right, hepatic artery on left
★ **5 sites of portosystemic anastomosis**: oesophagus, rectum, umbilicus, retroperitoneum, bare area
★ Oesophageal varices: left gastric vein ↔ **azygos system** — most dangerous
★ **Caput medusae**: dilated paraumbilical veins around umbilicus
★ IMV usually drains into **splenic vein**
★ Portal hypertension threshold: **>12 mmHg**
★ **Left gastric vein** (coronary vein) — direct tributary of portal vein
★ Develops from **vitelline veins**

## Mnemonics

**Portosystemic anastomoses:** "**RABEL**" — Rectum, (around) Abdomen/umbilicus, Bare area of liver, Esophagus, (retro)peritoLumbar

**Portal triad (right to left):** "**DCA**" — Duct (CBD) right, Central (hepatic artery), (portal vein) Aft (posterior)
