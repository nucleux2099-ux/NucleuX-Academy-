# Anterior Abdominal Wall — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
The anterior abdominal wall has skin, superficial fascia (Camper's + Scarpa's), three flat muscles (EO, IO, TA), two vertical muscles (rectus abdominis, pyramidalis), transversalis fascia, extraperitoneal fat, and parietal peritoneum. Supplied by T7–T12, L1. Blood supply from superior and inferior epigastric arteries.

## High Yield Points

★ Superficial fascia: **Camper's** (fatty) + **Scarpa's** (membranous) → continues as Colles' fascia in perineum
★ **T10 dermatome** = umbilicus level
★ Arcuate line (of Douglas): below this, ALL aponeuroses pass ANTERIOR to rectus
★ **Conjoint tendon** = fused aponeuroses of internal oblique + transversus abdominis
★ Superior and inferior epigastric arteries anastomose behind rectus — collateral pathway in aortic coarctation
★ Inferior epigastric artery: landmark for **direct vs indirect** inguinal hernia
★ **McBurney's point**: 1/3 from ASIS on line to umbilicus — appendix base
★ Linea alba is relatively **avascular** — preferred midline incision site
★ **Scarpa's fascia** fused to fascia lata below inguinal ligament → limits urine spread to thigh
★ Gridiron incision: muscle-splitting, preserves nerve supply

## Mnemonics

**Layers of abdominal wall:** "**Skin, Superficial fascia, External oblique, Internal oblique, Transversus, Transversalis fascia, Extraperitoneal fat, Peritoneum**" — **S-S-E-I-T-T-E-P**

**Dermatomes:** "**7 Up at Xiphoid, X (10) at umbilicus**"

**Flat muscles direction of fibres:** EO = hands in pockets (downward-medial), IO = opposite, TA = horizontal
