# Inferior Vena Cava — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Formed at L5 by union of common iliac veins. Ascends right of aorta, passes through caval opening at T8. Left renal vein crosses anterior to aorta and receives left gonadal + left suprarenal veins. Hepatic veins enter just below diaphragm. Complex embryological development.

## High Yield Points

★ Formed at **L5**, passes through diaphragm at **T8** (tendinous part)
★ Left gonadal and left suprarenal veins drain into **left renal vein** (not directly into IVC)
★ Left renal vein crosses **anterior to aorta, posterior to SMA** — nutcracker position
★ **Hepatic veins** are the last tributaries before IVC enters right atrium
★ IVC forms **posterior boundary** of epiploic foramen
★ Caval opening at T8 transmits IVC + right phrenic nerve
★ **Renal cell carcinoma** invades renal vein → IVC extension → left varicocele
★ IVC filter placed **below renal veins**
★ **No valves** in IVC except Eustachian valve at termination
★ Development: subcardinal (renal), supracardinal (infrarenal), vitelline (hepatic)

## Mnemonics

**Diaphragmatic openings:** "**I 8 (ate) 10 Eggs At 12**" — IVC at T8, Oesophagus at T10, Aorta at T12

**Left renal vein tributaries:** "Left renal vein is **Longer** and receives **Left gonadal + Left suprarenal**"

**IVC tributaries (bottom to top):** "**Common, Lumbar, Right Gonadal, Renal, Right Suprarenal, Inferior phrenic, Hepatic**"
