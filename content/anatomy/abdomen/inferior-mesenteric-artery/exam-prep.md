# Inferior Mesenteric Artery — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
IMA arises from aorta at L3, supplies hindgut (left 1/3 transverse colon to upper anal canal). Three branches: left colic, sigmoid arteries, superior rectal. Watershed areas at splenic flexure (Griffith's point) and rectosigmoid junction (Sudeck's point).

## High Yield Points

★ Arises at **L3** — smallest unpaired ventral branch of aorta
★ Supplies **hindgut**: left 1/3 transverse colon to upper anal canal
★ Terminal branch = **superior rectal artery**
★ **Griffith's point** (splenic flexure) — watershed, prone to ischaemic colitis
★ **Sudeck's critical point** — rectosigmoid junction, poor anastomosis
★ **Marginal artery of Drummond** connects SMA and IMA territories
★ IMV usually joins **splenic vein** (not SMV)
★ IMV passes **behind pancreas** — surgical landmark
★ Parasympathetic supply from **pelvic splanchnics S2–S4** (not vagus)
★ Arc of Riolan — inconstant collateral between SMA and IMA

## Mnemonics

**IMA branches:** "**L-S-S**" — Left colic, Sigmoid, Superior rectal

**Watershed areas:** "**Griffith's at the Flexure, Sudeck's at the Sigmoid**"

**IMV drainage:** "IMV joins Splenic Vein" — **I Meet Splenic Vein**
