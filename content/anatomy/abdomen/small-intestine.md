# Small Intestine Anatomy

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Moore's Clinically Oriented Anatomy 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The small intestine is the **longest part of the GI tract** (6-7 meters), extending from the pylorus to the ileocecal junction. It comprises the **duodenum** (fixed, retroperitoneal), **jejunum**, and **ileum** (mobile, intraperitoneal). It is the primary site of digestion and absorption, with anatomical features that maximize surface area.

## Key Points

- Total length: **6-7 meters** (Duodenum 25 cm, Jejunum 2.5m, Ileum 3.5m)
- Surface area increased by: Plicae circulares, Villi, Microvilli
- **Duodenum:** C-shaped, mostly retroperitoneal, surrounds pancreatic head
- **Jejunum vs Ileum:** Jejunum has thicker wall, larger villi, less fat in mesentery
- Blood supply: SMA (midgut derivative)

**Duodenal Relations:**

| Part | Anterior | Posterior |
|------|----------|-----------|

| D2 | Transverse colon, Right lobe liver | Right kidney, IVC |
| D3 | SMA & SMV, Root of mesentery | Aorta, IVC, Right psoas |
| D4 | Beginning of mesentery | Left psoas, Left renal vessels |

### Ligament of Treitz (Suspensory Ligament of Duodenum)

- **Structure:** Fibromuscular band from right crus of diaphragm to DJ flexure
- **Contains:** Skeletal and smooth muscle (suspensory muscle of duodenum)
- **Landmark:** Divides GI tract into "upper" and "lower" for bleeding classification

### Jejunum and Ileum

**Both are intraperitoneal, suspended by mesentery**

| Feature | Jejunum | Ileum |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **Location** | Upper left abdomen | Lower right abdomen |
| **Length** | ~2.5 m (40%) | ~3.5 m (60%) |
| **Diameter** | Wider (4 cm) | Narrower (3 cm) |
| **Wall thickness** | Thicker | Thinner |
| **Plicae circulares** | Large, numerous, tall | Small, sparse, low |
| **Villi** | Long, leaf-like | Short, finger-like |
| **Mesenteric fat** | Less, "windows" visible | More, obscures vessels |
| **Arterial arcades** | Few (1-2), long vasa recta | Many (4-5), short vasa recta |
| **Peyer's patches** | Few/absent | Numerous (antimesenteric border) |

### Mesentery

- **Attachment:** Root runs from DJ flexure (L2) to ileocecal junction (right iliac fossa)
- **Root length:** 15 cm (diagonal, crosses D3, aorta, IVC, right ureter)
- **Contains:** SMA & branches, SMV & tributaries, lymph nodes, autonomic nerves, fat

### Venous Drainage

- **Jejunal & Ileal veins** → SMV → Portal vein
- SMV runs to right of SMA

### Lymphatic Drainage

1. Lymphoid follicles in wall (Peyer's patches in ileum)
2. **Lacteals** (absorb fat) → **Mesenteric nodes** (100-150)
3. → Superior mesenteric nodes (at SMA origin)
4. → Cisterna chyli → Thoracic duct

### Nerve Supply

- **Sympathetic:** Greater and lesser splanchnic nerves → Celiac/SMA plexus
- **Parasympathetic:** Vagus (until 2/3 of transverse colon)
- **Function:** Parasympathetic ↑ motility, ↓ sphincter tone; Sympathetic opposite

### Histology Highlights

| Layer | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Mucosa** | Villi, Crypts of Lieberkühn, Goblet cells, Paneth cells |
| **Submucosa** | Brunner's glands (D1 only), Meissner's plexus |
| **Muscularis** | Inner circular, Outer longitudinal, Myenteric plexus |
| **Serosa** | Visceral peritoneum |

**Special cells:**
- **Enterocytes:** Absorption (brush border enzymes)
- **Goblet cells:** Mucus secretion (increase distally)
- **Paneth cells:** Antimicrobial peptides (defensins)
- **Enteroendocrine cells:** Hormones (CCK, Secretin, GIP)
- **M cells:** Overlie Peyer's patches, antigen sampling

## Clinical Correlations

### Duodenal Ulcer
- **Location:** D1 (duodenal cap) - anterior wall (perforation), posterior wall (bleeding from GDA)
- **Association:** H. pylori, NSAIDs
- **Perforation:** Air under diaphragm on erect X-ray

### Meckel's Diverticulum
- **"Rule of 2s":** 2% prevalence, 2 feet from ileocecal valve, 2 inches long, 2 types of ectopic tissue (gastric, pancreatic), symptomatic before 2 years
- **Origin:** Remnant of vitellointestinal (omphalomesenteric) duct
- **Complication:** Painless rectal bleeding (ectopic gastric mucosa → ulceration)

### Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO)
- **Causes:** Adhesions (60%), Hernias (15%), Malignancy (15%)
- **Signs:** Colicky pain, Vomiting (bilious), Distension, Obstipation
- **Imaging:** Dilated small bowel (>3 cm), Air-fluid levels, Decompressed colon

### Superior Mesenteric Artery Syndrome
- **Compression of D3** between SMA and Aorta
- **Risk factors:** Rapid weight loss, burns, prolonged bed rest
- **Symptoms:** Postprandial epigastric pain, vomiting, relief with prone position

### Crohn's Disease
- **Most common site:** Terminal ileum
- **Features:** Skip lesions, Transmural inflammation, Cobblestone mucosa
- **Complications:** Strictures, Fistulas, Abscesses

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Duodenum length:** 25 cm = 12 fingerbreadths (hence "duodenum")
2. **D1 anterior ulcer** → Perforation; **Posterior ulcer** → GDA bleeding
3. **Ligament of Treitz:** Landmark for upper vs lower GI bleeding
4. **Major papilla:** D2 posteromedial, 10 cm from pylorus
5. **Jejunum:** Thick wall, large plicae, few arcades, long vasa recta
6. **Ileum:** Thin wall, Peyer's patches, many arcades, short vasa recta
7. **Peyer's patches:** Antimesenteric border of ileum (important for typhoid)
8. **Meckel's diverticulum:** Antimesenteric, true diverticulum
9. **SMA syndrome:** D3 compressed between SMA and aorta
10. **Root of mesentery:** Crosses D3, aorta, IVC, right ureter

### Surface Anatomy
- **Umbilicus:** Overlies L3-L4 disc (D3 level)
- **DJ flexure:** L2, 2.5 cm left of midline
- **McBurney's point:** 1/3 from ASIS to umbilicus (appendix base, overlies terminal ileum)
