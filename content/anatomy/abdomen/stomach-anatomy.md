# Stomach Anatomy

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Moore's Clinically Oriented Anatomy 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The stomach is a **J-shaped muscular organ** located in the left upper quadrant of the abdomen. It serves as a reservoir for food, initiates protein digestion with pepsin and HCl, and regulates gastric emptying. Its rich blood supply from the celiac trunk and extensive lymphatic drainage have major surgical implications.

## Key Points

- **Location:** Left hypochondrium and epigastrium, levels T10-L1
- **Capacity:** 50 mL (empty) to 1500 mL (full)
- **Blood supply:** Entirely from **celiac trunk** (foregut)
- **Venous drainage:** Portal system
- **Nerve supply:** Vagus (parasympathetic) + Celiac plexus (sympathetic)
- **Lymph:** Drains to celiac nodes ultimately

### Peritoneal Relations

**Stomach is INTRAPERITONEAL** (completely covered by visceral peritoneum)

| Structure | Attachment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Lesser omentum** | Lesser curvature → Liver |
| **Greater omentum** | Greater curvature → Transverse colon |
| **Gastrophrenic ligament** | Fundus → Diaphragm |
| **Gastrosplenic ligament** | Greater curvature → Spleen (contains short gastrics) |

### Venous Drainage

**All drain to PORTAL VEIN**

| Vein | Drains to |
|------|-----------|
| Left gastric (coronary) vein | Portal vein directly |
| Right gastric vein | Portal vein directly |
| Short gastric veins | Splenic vein |
| Left gastro-omental | Splenic vein |
| Right gastro-omental | SMV |

**Clinical:** Left gastric vein connects to esophageal veins → **Esophageal varices** in portal hypertension

### Lymphatic Drainage

```
All gastric lymph → CELIAC NODES ultimately

Zones:
- Upper 2/3 lesser curvature → Left gastric nodes
- Lower 1/3 lesser curvature → Right gastric/Hepatic nodes  
- Upper left greater curvature → Pancreaticosplenic nodes
- Lower right greater curvature → Right gastro-omental nodes
```

**Surgical relevance:** Gastric cancer requires extensive lymphadenectomy (D2 dissection)

### Nerve Supply

| Type | Source | Function |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Parasympathetic** | Vagus (CN X) | ↑ Motility, ↑ Secretion |
| **Sympathetic** | T6-T9 via celiac plexus | ↓ Motility, Vasoconstriction |

**Vagal Trunks:**
- **Anterior vagal trunk** (mainly left vagus) → Hepatic branch, anterior gastric branches
- **Posterior vagal trunk** (mainly right vagus) → Celiac branch, posterior gastric branches

**Nerves of Latarjet:** Terminal branches along lesser curvature → "Crow's foot" at antrum

### Histology Overview

| Layer | Features |
|-------|----------|
| **Mucosa** | Rugae, gastric pits, glands |
| **Submucosa** | Meissner's plexus |
| **Muscularis externa** | 3 layers: Inner oblique, Middle circular, Outer longitudinal |
| **Serosa** | Visceral peritoneum |

**Note:** Stomach has **3 muscle layers** (unique) - oblique layer prevents reflux

### Gastric Glands Cell Types

| Cell Type | Location | Secretion |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Parietal cells** | Body, fundus | HCl, Intrinsic factor |
| **Chief cells** | Body, fundus | Pepsinogen |
| **G cells** | Antrum | Gastrin |
| **D cells** | Antrum | Somatostatin |
| **Mucous cells** | Throughout | Mucus, HCO₃⁻ |
| **Enterochromaffin-like** | Body | Histamine |

## Clinical Correlations

### Peptic Ulcer Disease
- **Gastric ulcers:** Lesser curvature (watershed area)
- **Duodenal ulcers:** Anterior wall (perforation), Posterior wall (bleeding - GDA)

### Gastric Cancer
- **Most common site:** Pyloric antrum
- **Linitis plastica:** Diffuse signet ring carcinoma, "leather bottle" stomach
- **Krukenberg tumor:** Metastasis to ovary
- **Virchow's node:** Left supraclavicular node metastasis

### Hiatal Hernia
- **Sliding (95%):** GE junction herniates above diaphragm
- **Rolling (paraesophageal):** Fundus herniates, GEJ in place

### Congenital Pyloric Stenosis
- **Presentation:** 2-8 week old male, projectile non-bilious vomiting
- **Sign:** Olive-shaped mass in epigastrium
- **Treatment:** Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy

### Surgical Procedures
- **Billroth I:** Gastroduodenostomy (antrectomy + D1 anastomosis)
- **Billroth II:** Gastrojejunostomy (antrectomy + jejunal anastomosis)
- **Truncal vagotomy:** Cuts both vagi at esophageal hiatus
- **Highly selective vagotomy:** Preserves antral innervation (nerves of Latarjet)

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Stomach blood supply:** ALL from celiac trunk (foregut derivative)
2. **Angle of His:** Acute angle at GE junction, prevents reflux
3. **Left gastric artery:** Most important for lesser curvature, bleeds in ulcers
4. **Short gastric arteries:** Supply fundus, arise from splenic artery
5. **Left gastric vein:** Portal-systemic anastomosis (esophageal varices)
6. **Stomach bed:** Pancreas is most important posterior relation
7. **Vagotomy** denervates stomach → ↓ acid secretion
8. **Three muscle layers** in stomach (unique in GI tract)
9. **Parietal cells** make HCl AND intrinsic factor (B12 absorption)
10. **Gastric ulcers** on lesser curvature; **duodenal ulcers** 4× more common

### Surface Anatomy
- **Cardiac orifice:** T11, 2.5 cm from midline (left)
- **Pylorus:** L1, 1.2 cm right of midline (transpyloric plane)
- **Fundus:** 5th intercostal space in midclavicular line (gas bubble)
