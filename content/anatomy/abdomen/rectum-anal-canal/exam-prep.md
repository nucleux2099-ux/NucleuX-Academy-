# Rectum & Anal Canal — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Rectum (12 cm, S3 to coccyx) has no taeniae/haustra/appendices epiploicae. Anal canal (3.8 cm) divided by pectinate line: above = columnar, portal, autonomic, internal haemorrhoids; below = squamous, systemic, somatic, external haemorrhoids. Continence by puborectalis + sphincters.

## High Yield Points

★ **Pectinate line**: THE watershed — epithelium, blood supply, drainage, innervation all change
★ Internal haemorrhoids at **3, 7, 11 o'clock** (superior rectal artery branches)
★ Above pectinate: **painless** (autonomic); Below: **painful** (somatic/pudendal)
★ **Puborectalis** maintains anorectal angle (~80°) — key to continence
★ Lymph below pectinate → **superficial inguinal nodes**
★ Rectum peritoneum: upper 1/3 front+sides, middle front only, lower **none**
★ **Pouch of Douglas**: lowest peritoneal point, palpable on PR exam
★ Posterior anal fissure: **watershed** area of blood supply
★ Anal canal = **portosystemic anastomosis** site (superior rectal ↔ middle/inferior rectal)
★ External sphincter: **pudendal nerve S2,3,4** — voluntary

## Mnemonics

**Internal haemorrhoid positions:** "**3, 7, 11 o'clock**" — think of a clock in lithotomy position

**Above vs below pectinate:** "**PAIL above**" — Portal, Autonomic, Internal haemorrhoids, Lymph to internal iliac

**Rectal peritoneum:** "**1/3 rule**" — upper (front+sides), middle (front), lower (none)
