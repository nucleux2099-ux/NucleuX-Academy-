# Rectum & Anal Canal

## Overview
The rectum is the terminal part of the large intestine (~12 cm), extending from S3 to the tip of the coccyx, where it becomes the anal canal (~3.8 cm). The rectum follows the sacral curvature. The anal canal is the final segment, passing through the pelvic floor, and is divided by the pectinate (dentate) line into upper and lower halves with distinct embryological origin, blood supply, innervation, and lymphatic drainage.

## Key Points
- Rectum: S3 to coccyx tip, 12 cm, follows sacral curve
- Anal canal: anorectal junction to anus, 3.8 cm, angled posteriorly
- Pectinate line: crucial watershed dividing endoderm (above) from ectoderm (below)
- Rectum has no mesentery, no taeniae coli, no appendices epiploicae, no haustra
- Puborectalis sling maintains anorectal angle — key to continence

## Gross Anatomy

### Rectum
- **Peritoneal covering:** Upper 1/3 = front and sides; Middle 1/3 = front only; Lower 1/3 = none (below peritoneal reflection)
- **Houston's valves:** Three transverse folds (two on left, one on right); middle fold at level of peritoneal reflection
- **Ampulla:** Dilated lower part, stores faeces before defecation
- **No taeniae coli** — longitudinal muscle forms a complete coat

### Relations of Rectum
**Male:**
- Anterior: rectovesical pouch (peritoneum), bladder base, seminal vesicles, prostate, vas deferens (Denonvilliers' fascia separates)
- Posterior: sacrum, coccyx, piriformis, sacral plexus, median sacral vessels

**Female:**
- Anterior: rectouterine pouch (of Douglas), posterior vaginal wall
- Posterior: same as male

### Anal Canal (3.8 cm)
**Surgical anal canal:** From anorectal ring (puborectalis) to anal verge
**Anatomical anal canal:** From pectinate line to anal verge

#### Pectinate (Dentate) Line
| Feature | Above Pectinate | Below Pectinate |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Epithelium | Columnar | Stratified squamous |
| Embryology | Endoderm (hindgut) | Ectoderm (proctodeum) |
| Arterial supply | Superior rectal (IMA) | Inferior rectal (internal pudendal) |
| Venous drainage | Superior rectal → portal | Inferior rectal → systemic (IVC) |
| Lymphatics | Internal iliac nodes | Superficial inguinal nodes |
| Nerve supply | Autonomic (painless) | Somatic/pudendal (painful) |
| Haemorrhoids | Internal | External |

#### Anal Sphincters
- **Internal sphincter:** Involuntary, thickened circular smooth muscle, autonomic supply
- **External sphincter:** Voluntary, striated muscle, pudendal nerve (S2,3,4)
  - Three parts: subcutaneous, superficial, deep
- **Puborectalis:** Part of levator ani, maintains anorectal angle (~80°)

#### Columns of Morgagni
- 6–10 vertical folds in upper anal canal above pectinate line
- Anal valves at their lower ends; anal sinuses between columns

### Spaces Around Anal Canal
- Ischiorectal fossa: lateral to anal canal, contains pudendal (Alcock's) canal
- Perianal space: subcutaneous, around anal verge
- Submucosal space: between mucosa and internal sphincter

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Arterial
- **Superior rectal artery** (terminal branch of IMA) — main supply to rectum
- **Middle rectal artery** (from internal iliac) — lateral wall of lower rectum
- **Inferior rectal artery** (from internal pudendal) — anal canal below pectinate line

### Venous
- Superior rectal vein → IMV → portal system
- Middle and inferior rectal veins → internal iliac → systemic
- Internal rectal venous plexus → portosystemic anastomosis site

### Lymphatic
- Upper rectum: pararectal → inferior mesenteric nodes
- Lower rectum: internal iliac nodes
- Below pectinate: superficial inguinal nodes

### Nerve Supply
- Rectum and above pectinate: sympathetic (L1–L2) + parasympathetic (S2–S4)
- Below pectinate: pudendal nerve (S2–S4) — somatic, very sensitive

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy

### Haemorrhoids
- Internal: above pectinate, at 3, 7, 11 o'clock (branches of superior rectal artery)
- External: below pectinate, painful (somatic innervation)

### Per Rectal Examination
- Structures palpable: prostate/cervix, rectovesical/rectouterine pouch, ischial spines
- Pouch of Douglas (lowest point of peritoneum) — can be drained per rectum

### Anal Fissure
- Most common in posterior midline (poor blood supply — watershed area)

### Anorectal Abscess
- Perianal, ischiorectal, intersphincteric, supralevator

### Continence
- Puborectalis sling (anorectal angle) + internal + external sphincters
- Damage to puborectalis → incontinence

## Development
- Rectum: from hindgut (endoderm)
- Upper anal canal: from hindgut (above pectinate)
- Lower anal canal: from proctodeum/ectoderm (below pectinate)
- Urorectal septum divides cloaca into rectum and urogenital sinus
