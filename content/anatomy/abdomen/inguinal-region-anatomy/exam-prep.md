# Inguinal Region — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Inguinal canal is 4 cm long, from deep ring (transversalis fascia, lateral to inferior epigastric artery) to superficial ring (EO aponeurosis). Transmits spermatic cord (males)/round ligament (females). Hesselbach's triangle: site of direct hernia. Indirect hernia through deep ring, lateral to inferior epigastric.

## High Yield Points

★ Deep ring: **lateral** to inferior epigastric artery; Direct hernia: **medial** to it
★ Anterior wall: **external oblique** aponeurosis (whole length) + internal oblique (lateral 1/3)
★ Posterior wall: **transversalis fascia** (whole length) + conjoint tendon (medial 1/3)
★ **Hesselbach's triangle**: inf. epigastric (lateral), rectus (medial), inguinal ligament (inferior)
★ Indirect hernia: **congenital** (patent processus vaginalis) or acquired; more common
★ Direct hernia: always **acquired**, weakness of posterior wall
★ Mid-inguinal point (ASIS to pubic symphysis midpoint) = **femoral artery**
★ Midpoint of inguinal ligament (ASIS to pubic tubercle) = **deep ring**
★ Cremasteric reflex: afferent + efferent = **genitofemoral nerve (L1, L2)**
★ Femoral hernia: **below and lateral** to pubic tubercle

## Mnemonics

**Walls of inguinal canal:** "**MALT**" — M (muscles form roof), A (anterior = EO aponeurosis), L (ligament = floor), T (transversalis fascia = posterior)

**Hesselbach's triangle:** "**RIL**" — Rectus, Inferior epigastric, (inguinal) Ligament

**Direct vs Indirect:** "**MD**s are Direct" — Medial to inferior epigastric = Direct
