# Greater Omentum — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Largest peritoneal fold, four-layered, hangs from greater curvature of stomach like an apron over transverse colon and small intestine. Contains gastroepiploic vessels, fat, macrophages. "Policeman of the abdomen" — walls off infections. Develops from dorsal mesogastrium.

## High Yield Points

★ **Four-layered** peritoneal fold (fused in adults)
★ "**Policeman of the abdomen**" — migrates to sites of infection
★ Contains **right and left gastroepiploic arteries** (anastomose)
★ Gastrocolic ligament is part of greater omentum — contains gastroepiploic vessels
★ Develops from **dorsal mesogastrium**
★ **Milky spots** — lymphoid aggregates with macrophages
★ Used in **Graham patch** repair for perforated duodenal ulcer
★ Common site of **metastatic deposits** in ovarian cancer → omentectomy
★ Gastrosplenic ligament contains **short gastric + left gastroepiploic** vessels
★ Can obstruct peritoneal dialysis catheter

## Mnemonics

**Greater omentum attachments:** "**GCG**" — Greater curvature → Colon (transverse) → hangs like a Ghost curtain

**Contents of gastrosplenic ligament:** "**Short Left**" — Short gastric + Left gastroepiploic arteries
