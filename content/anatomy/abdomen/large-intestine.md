# Large Intestine Anatomy

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed, Moore's Clinically Oriented Anatomy 8th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The large intestine extends from the **ileocecal junction to the anus** (~1.5 m). Its main functions are water/electrolyte absorption and feces formation. Key distinguishing features include **teniae coli, haustra, and appendices epiploicae**. Understanding the blood supply (SMA vs IMA territories) and the "watershed" areas is critical for surgical planning.

## Key Points

- **Length:** ~1.5 m; **Diameter:** 6-7 cm (widest at cecum)
- **Parts:** Cecum, Appendix, Ascending colon, Transverse colon, Descending colon, Sigmoid, Rectum, Anal canal
- **Features:** Teniae coli, Haustra, Appendices epiploicae (absent in rectum)
- **Blood supply:** SMA (cecum to splenic flexure), IMA (splenic flexure to rectum)
- **Watershed areas:** Splenic flexure (Griffiths point), Rectosigmoid (Sudeck's point)

### Cecum and Appendix

**Cecum:**
- **Location:** Right iliac fossa, below ileocecal valve
- **Size:** 6-8 cm long, 7-8 cm wide
- **Peritoneum:** Intraperitoneal (but variable mobility)
- **Ileocecal valve:** Prevents reflux from cecum to ileum

**Appendix (Vermiform Appendix):**
- **Length:** 6-10 cm (average 8 cm)
- **Base:** Posteromedial cecum, where teniae converge
- **Position (variable):**
  - Retrocecal (65%) - most common
  - Pelvic (30%)
  - Others: Pre-ileal, Post-ileal, Paracecal

| Position | Clinical Presentation |
|----------|----------------------|
| Retrocecal | Psoas sign, flank pain |
| Pelvic | Suprapubic pain, urinary symptoms |
| Pre-ileal | Typical RLQ pain |

**Appendicular Artery:** From ileocolic artery (SMA branch), runs in mesoappendix

### Ascending Colon

- **Length:** ~15 cm
- **Peritoneum:** Retroperitoneal (secondarily)
- **Relations:** Right kidney, Right ureter, Iliacus muscle posteriorly

### Hepatic Flexure (Right Colic Flexure)

- **Location:** Below liver, anterior to right kidney
- **Peritoneum:** Has phrenicocolic ligament

### Transverse Colon

- **Length:** ~45 cm (most variable, can reach pelvis)
- **Peritoneum:** Intraperitoneal (suspended by transverse mesocolon)
- **Relations:** Greater omentum attached, Stomach and Spleen above
- **Blood supply:** Middle colic artery (SMA)

### Splenic Flexure (Left Colic Flexure)

- **Location:** Higher than hepatic flexure, near spleen
- **Attachment:** Phrenicocolic ligament (to diaphragm) - "sustentaculum lienis"
- **Watershed area:** Junction of SMA and IMA territories (Griffiths point)

### Descending Colon

- **Length:** ~25 cm
- **Peritoneum:** Retroperitoneal (secondarily)
- **Blood supply:** Left colic artery (IMA)

### Sigmoid Colon

- **Length:** ~40 cm (variable)
- **Shape:** S-shaped (or Ω-shaped)
- **Peritoneum:** Intraperitoneal (sigmoid mesocolon)
- **Characteristic:** Mobile, can twist (volvulus)
- **Blood supply:** Sigmoidal arteries (IMA)

#### Superior Mesenteric Artery Territory (Midgut)

#### Inferior Mesenteric Artery Territory (Hindgut)

```
               IMA (arises at L3)
                │
    ┌───────────┼───────────┐
    │           │           │
Left colic  Sigmoidal   Superior rectal
    │         (2-4)          │
    │           │            │
    ├───────────┴────────────┤
    │                        │
    Descending,         Rectum
    Sigmoid               (upper)
```

#### Marginal Artery (of Drummond)

- **Course:** Continuous arterial arcade along mesenteric border
- **Significance:** Connects SMA and IMA territories
- **Arc of Riolan:** Inconstant, connects middle colic to left colic

### Watershed Areas (Critical!)

| Area | Name | Location | Vulnerable to |
|------|------|----------|--------------|
| **Splenic flexure** | Griffiths point | SMA-IMA junction | Ischemic colitis |
| **Rectosigmoid** | Sudeck's point | Last sigmoid-Superior rectal | Anastomotic leak |

### Venous Drainage

- **SMV:** Drains cecum to splenic flexure → Portal vein
- **IMV:** Drains splenic flexure to rectum → Splenic vein → Portal vein
- **IMV joins** splenic vein (or SMV-splenic junction)

### Lymphatic Drainage

```
Wall → Epicolic nodes → Paracolic nodes
                            │
                     Intermediate colic nodes
                            │
                     Principal nodes (SMA/IMA)
                            │
                      Cisterna chyli
```

### Nerve Supply

- **Parasympathetic:**
  - Vagus → Cecum to 2/3 transverse colon
  - Pelvic splanchnic (S2-4) → Distal 1/3 transverse to rectum
- **Sympathetic:** L1-L2 via SMA/IMA plexus
- **Watershed for autonomics:** Also at splenic flexure!

## Clinical Correlations

### Appendicitis

- **McBurney's point:** 1/3 distance from ASIS to umbilicus
- **Signs:** Rovsing's sign, Psoas sign, Obturator sign
- **Complication:** Perforation → Peritonitis, Abscess
- **Treatment:** Appendectomy (open or laparoscopic)

### Colorectal Cancer

- **Most common site:** Rectosigmoid (55%)
- **Right-sided:** Occult bleeding, anemia, mass
- **Left-sided:** Obstruction, change in bowel habits
- **Staging:** Duke's / TNM
- **Screening:** Colonoscopy at 45-50 years

### Diverticular Disease

- **Location:** Most common in sigmoid (95%)
- **Type:** Pseudodiverticula (mucosa herniates through muscle at vasa recta entry)
- **Complications:** Diverticulitis, Bleeding, Perforation, Fistula

### Volvulus

- **Sigmoid volvulus:** "Coffee bean" sign on X-ray, elderly/institutionalized
- **Cecal volvulus:** Younger patients, mobile cecum
- **Treatment:** Sigmoidoscopic decompression (sigmoid), Surgery

### Ischemic Colitis

- **Location:** Splenic flexure most vulnerable (watershed)
- **Presentation:** Sudden abdominal pain, bloody diarrhea
- **Causes:** Low-flow states, Atherosclerosis, Embolism

### Ulcerative Colitis

- **Pattern:** Continuous, starts at rectum, extends proximally
- **Layers:** Mucosal (superficial)
- **Complication:** Toxic megacolon, Colorectal cancer

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Teniae coli converge** at the base of appendix (surgical landmark)
2. **Retrocecal** is the most common appendix position (65%)
3. **Splenic flexure** = Watershed between SMA and IMA
4. **IMV drains into splenic vein** (not SMV directly)
5. **Sigmoid colon** is the most common site for diverticulosis
6. **Rectum has NO teniae, haustra, or appendices epiploicae**
7. **Pelvic splanchnic nerves (S2-4)** supply distal colon
8. **Marginal artery of Drummond:** Anastomosis along colonic border
9. **Coffee bean sign:** Sigmoid volvulus
10. **Duke's C:** Lymph node involvement in colorectal cancer

### Surface Anatomy

- **Cecum:** Right iliac fossa
- **Appendix base:** McBurney's point
- **Transverse colon:** Umbilical region (variable)
- **Sigmoid colon:** Left iliac fossa, S-shaped
