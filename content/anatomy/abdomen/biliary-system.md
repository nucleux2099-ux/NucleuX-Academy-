# Biliary System Anatomy

> 📚 **Sources:** Gray's Anatomy 42nd Ed, Blumgart's Surgery of the Liver 6th Ed, Netter Atlas 7th Ed
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

The biliary system consists of the **gallbladder** (storage) and **bile ducts** (conduits) that transport bile from the liver to the duodenum. The **extrahepatic biliary tree** (CHD, CBD, cystic duct) is surgically critical. Understanding the anatomical variations of the cystic artery and biliary anatomy is essential to prevent iatrogenic injury during cholecystectomy.

## Key Points

- Gallbladder capacity: **30-50 mL**
- CBD length: **7-8 cm**, diameter: **<6 mm** (≤8 mm post-cholecystectomy)
- Bile drainage: Right + Left hepatic duct → CHD → (+ cystic duct) → CBD → Ampulla
- **Calot's triangle:** Critical surgical landmark
- **Sphincter of Oddi:** Controls bile/pancreatic juice flow into duodenum
- CBD joins pancreatic duct in 70% to form ampulla of Vater

## Detailed Content

### Gallbladder

```
            Fundus (projects beyond liver edge)
               │
               │
              Body (main portion)
               │
               │
         Infundibulum (Hartmann's pouch)
               │
              Neck
               │
          Cystic duct (spiral valve of Heister)
               │
              CBD
```

| Part | Features | Clinical |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Fundus** | Blind end, projects at 9th costal cartilage (MCL) | Palpable in distension |
| **Body** | Main portion, contact with liver | Porcelain gallbladder (calcified) |
| **Infundibulum** | Dilated area near neck (Hartmann's pouch) | Gallstones impact here |
| **Neck** | S-shaped, continues as cystic duct | Contains spiral valve |

**Relations:**
- **Superior:** Liver (gallbladder fossa between segments IVb and V)
- **Inferior:** Transverse colon, Duodenum (D1)
- **Anterior:** Anterior abdominal wall

### Segments of CBD

| Segment | Location | Length |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Supraduodenal** | From cystic duct junction to duodenum | 2-3 cm |
| **Retroduodenal** | Behind D1 | ~2 cm |
| **Pancreatic** | Within/behind pancreatic head | 2-3 cm |
| **Intraduodenal** | Through duodenal wall | ~1 cm |

### Calot's Triangle (Hepatocystic Triangle)

**Critical Surgical Anatomy for Safe Cholecystectomy**

```
              LIVER
                │
        ┌───────┴───────┐
        │               │
    Cystic duct    Common hepatic
        │           duct
        │               │
        └───────┬───────┘
                │
         Contents:
         - Cystic artery (usually)
         - Cystic lymph node
         - Connective tissue
         - Possible accessory structures
```

**Boundaries:**
- **Superior:** Inferior surface of liver (Segment IVb/V junction)
- **Medial:** Common hepatic duct
- **Inferior:** Cystic duct

**Critical View of Safety (CVS):** Laparoscopic cholecystectomy landmark
1. Calot's triangle cleared of fat/fibrous tissue
2. Lowest part of gallbladder separated from liver bed
3. Two and ONLY two structures entering gallbladder (cystic duct + cystic artery)

### Blood Supply

#### Cystic Artery

**Classic origin (75%):** Right hepatic artery, passes through Calot's triangle

```
    Common hepatic artery
           │
    Proper hepatic artery
           │
    ┌──────┴──────┐
    │             │
 Left hepatic  Right hepatic
                  │
             Cystic artery ──→ Gallbladder
           (in Calot's triangle)
```

**Variations (IMPORTANT):**
- From right hepatic passing ANTERIOR to CHD (15%)
- From left hepatic, GDA, SMA, or celiac trunk
- Double cystic artery (15%)
- Accessory cystic artery

#### Biliary Blood Supply

| Structure | Blood Supply |
|-----------|--------------|
| CHD & upper CBD | Cystic artery, Right hepatic artery |
| Mid CBD | Multiple small branches (9 o'clock, 3 o'clock arteries) |
| Lower CBD | Posterior superior pancreaticoduodenal artery |

**Clinical:** CBD blood supply is AXIAL (along the duct) - excessive dissection can cause ischemic stricture

### Sphincter of Oddi Complex

```
        CBD                    Main pancreatic duct
         │                           │
    Sphincter choledochus    Sphincter pancreaticus
         │                           │
         └───────────┬───────────────┘
                     │
            Sphincter ampullae
                     │
              Ampulla of Vater
                     │
           Major duodenal papilla
              (D2, posteromedial)
```

**Functions:**
- Prevents reflux of duodenal contents into bile duct
- Regulates bile flow into duodenum
- Coordinates with gallbladder contraction

### Lymphatic Drainage

**Gallbladder:**
1. **Cystic lymph node (Lund's node)** - in Calot's triangle
2. → Hepatic nodes (at porta hepatis)
3. → Celiac nodes
4. → Cisterna chyli

### Nerve Supply

- **Sympathetic:** Celiac plexus (inhibits contraction)
- **Parasympathetic:** Vagus (stimulates contraction, relaxes Oddi)
- **Sensory:** Right phrenic nerve (referred pain to right shoulder tip)

### Bile Flow Physiology

```
Fasting: Sphincter of Oddi CONTRACTED → Bile to gallbladder
         Gallbladder relaxed → Storage & concentration

Fed:     CCK released (from duodenal I cells)
         → Gallbladder CONTRACTS
         → Sphincter of Oddi RELAXES
         → Bile flows into duodenum
```

### Anatomical Variations (High-Yield!)

#### Cystic Duct Variations
1. Normal (70%): Joins CHD at acute angle, lateral
2. Low insertion into CBD
3. Medial insertion (crosses behind CHD)
4. Parallel course with CHD (may share wall)
5. Absent cystic duct (gallbladder drains directly into CHD)

#### Biliary Tree Variations
1. Accessory hepatic ducts
2. Aberrant right sectoral duct draining into CHD or cystic duct
3. Triple confluence (Right anterior, Right posterior, Left hepatic)

## Clinical Correlations

### Cholelithiasis (Gallstones)
- **Risk factors:** "5 F's" - Fat, Female, Fertile, Forty, Fair
- **Composition:** Cholesterol (80%), Pigment (20%)
- **Complications:** Biliary colic, Acute cholecystitis, Choledocholithiasis, Pancreatitis

### Acute Cholecystitis
- **Murphy's sign:** Inspiratory arrest during RUQ palpation
- **Imaging:** Ultrasound (gallstones, wall thickening >3mm, pericholecystic fluid)
- **Treatment:** Antibiotics + Cholecystectomy (within 72 hours optimal)

### Choledocholithiasis
- **Presentation:** Jaundice, RUQ pain, elevated ALP/bilirubin
- **CBD diameter:** >6 mm concerning (>8 mm post-cholecystectomy normal)
- **Treatment:** ERCP with sphincterotomy, then cholecystectomy

### Bile Duct Injury (BDI)
- **Incidence:** 0.3-0.5% in laparoscopic cholecystectomy
- **Most common mechanism:** Misidentification of CHD/CBD as cystic duct
- **Strasberg Classification:** Type A (cystic duct leak) to Type E (complete transection)
- **Prevention:** Critical View of Safety, Cholangiography if unclear

### Mirizzi Syndrome
- **Definition:** Impacted stone in Hartmann's pouch compressing CHD
- **Result:** Obstructive jaundice despite patent CBD
- **Types:** Type I (external compression) to Type IV (cholecysto-choledochal fistula)

### Gallbladder Cancer
- **Association:** Porcelain gallbladder, large polyps (>1 cm)
- **Spread:** Direct invasion of liver, lymphatic to hepatic/celiac nodes
- **If incidental after cholecystectomy:** Re-resection if T1b or higher

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Calot's triangle boundaries:** Liver, Cystic duct, Common hepatic duct
2. **Cystic artery origin:** Usually from right hepatic (75%)
3. **CBD normal diameter:** ≤6 mm (add 1 mm per decade >50, or ≤8 mm post-chole)
4. **Ampulla of Vater location:** Posteromedial wall of D2
5. **Murphy's sign:** Pathognomonic for acute cholecystitis
6. **"Critical View of Safety":** Must see ONLY 2 structures entering gallbladder
7. **Courvoisier's law:** Palpable gallbladder + jaundice = Unlikely gallstones (think malignancy)
8. **Charcot's triad:** Fever, Jaundice, RUQ pain (cholangitis)
9. **Reynold's pentad:** Charcot's + Hypotension + Altered mental status (severe cholangitis)
10. **Gallbladder referred pain:** Right shoulder/scapula (phrenic nerve C3-5)

### Surface Anatomy
- **Fundus of gallbladder:** Where lateral border of rectus abdominis meets 9th costal cartilage
- **Murphy's point:** Intersection of MCL with right costal margin
- **Major papilla:** D2, 10 cm from pylorus
