# Lesser Sac (Omental Bursa) — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Peritoneal space behind stomach and lesser omentum, communicating with greater sac via epiploic foramen. Bounded by stomach bed posteriorly (pancreas, left kidney, splenic artery). Epiploic foramen boundaries: anterior (free edge of lesser omentum), posterior (IVC), superior (caudate lobe), inferior (D1).

## High Yield Points

★ Communicates with greater sac via **epiploic foramen (of Winslow)**
★ Epiploic foramen anterior boundary: **free edge of lesser omentum** (portal triad: portal vein, hepatic artery, CBD)
★ Epiploic foramen posterior boundary: **IVC**
★ **Pringle manoeuvre**: compress free edge of lesser omentum → controls hepatic bleeding
★ **Pancreatic pseudocyst** commonly collects in lesser sac
★ Posterior gastric ulcer perforation → fluid collects in **lesser sac** (contained)
★ Stomach bed: **diaphragm, left suprarenal, left kidney, splenic artery, pancreas body/tail, transverse mesocolon**
★ Surgical access: through **gastrocolic ligament** (most common)
★ Superior recess behind **caudate lobe** of liver
★ Develops from right side of **dorsal mesogastrium**

## Mnemonics

**Epiploic foramen boundaries:** "**FLOP**" (like a door flap) — Free edge of lesser omentum (anterior), Liver caudate lobe (superior), Oral part of duodenum/D1 (inferior), Posterior = IVC

**Stomach bed:** "**SLAP-DK**" — Splenic artery, Left suprarenal, Aorta, Pancreas, Diaphragm (left crus), Kidney (left)
