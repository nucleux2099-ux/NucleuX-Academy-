# Large Intestine & Rectum

## Overview
The large intestine extends from the ileocaecal junction to the anus, approximately 1.5 m long. It comprises the caecum, appendix, ascending colon, transverse colon, descending colon, sigmoid colon, rectum, and anal canal. Its primary functions are water absorption, formation and storage of faeces, and bacterial fermentation.

## Key Points
- Total length ~1.5 m, diameter ~6 cm (caecum widest)
- Distinguishing features: taeniae coli, haustra, appendices epiploicae
- Ascending and descending colon are retroperitoneal; transverse and sigmoid are intraperitoneal
- Blood supply: SMA (midgut — caecum to proximal 2/3 transverse colon), IMA (hindgut — distal 1/3 transverse to rectum)
- Watershed areas: splenic flexure (Griffith's point), rectosigmoid (Sudeck's point)

## Gross Anatomy

### Distinguishing Features of Large Intestine
1. **Taeniae coli:** Three longitudinal muscle bands (taenia libera, taenia omentalis, taenia mesocolica)
2. **Haustra:** Sacculations between taeniae
3. **Appendices epiploicae:** Fat-filled peritoneal tags
4. **Larger caliber** than small intestine
5. **Fixed position** (ascending and descending colon)

### Parts

#### Caecum
- Blind-ended pouch in right iliac fossa, ~6 cm
- Completely covered by peritoneum (intraperitoneal) but no mesentery (usually)
- Appendix arises from its posteromedial wall
- Ileocaecal valve (papilla): two horizontal folds, prevents reflux

#### Ascending Colon (15 cm)
- Right iliac fossa to right colic (hepatic) flexure
- Retroperitoneal, covered by peritoneum on front and sides only
- Paracolic gutter lateral to it

#### Transverse Colon (45 cm)
- Hepatic flexure to splenic flexure
- Intraperitoneal with transverse mesocolon
- Most mobile part; hangs down in a loop
- Splenic flexure higher than hepatic flexure, attached to diaphragm by phrenicocolic ligament

#### Descending Colon (25 cm)
- Splenic flexure to left iliac fossa
- Retroperitoneal, narrower than ascending colon

#### Sigmoid Colon (40 cm)
- S-shaped, intraperitoneal with sigmoid mesocolon
- Lies in left iliac fossa, enters pelvis at S3
- Sigmoid mesocolon: inverted V attachment, left ureter lies at apex

#### Rectum (12 cm)
- S3 to coccyx tip, follows sacral curvature
- Upper 1/3: peritoneum on front and sides
- Middle 1/3: peritoneum on front only
- Lower 1/3: below peritoneal reflection (no peritoneum)
- Three lateral curves (Houston's valves) internally
- Ampulla: dilated lower part for storage

#### Anal Canal (3.8 cm)
- From anorectal junction to anus
- Pectinate (dentate) line: important watershed line
- Above pectinate: columnar epithelium, portal drainage, autonomic supply, internal haemorrhoids
- Below pectinate: squamous epithelium, systemic drainage, somatic supply, external haemorrhoids

## Relations & Surface Markings
- Caecum: right iliac fossa
- Hepatic flexure: right hypochondrium
- Splenic flexure: left hypochondrium (higher)
- McBurney's point: base of appendix

## Blood Supply & Nerve Supply

### Arterial (SMA territory — midgut)
- Ileocolic → caecum, appendix, terminal ileum
- Right colic → ascending colon
- Middle colic → transverse colon (proximal 2/3)

### Arterial (IMA territory — hindgut)
- Left colic → descending colon, distal 1/3 transverse
- Sigmoid arteries → sigmoid colon
- Superior rectal → upper rectum

### Venous
- SMV (midgut) and IMV (hindgut) → portal vein

### Lymphatic
- Epicolic → paracolic → intermediate → principal (SMA/IMA) nodes

### Nerve Supply
- Midgut: sympathetic (T10–T12), parasympathetic (vagus)
- Hindgut: sympathetic (L1–L2), parasympathetic (S2–S4 pelvic splanchnic)

## Applied / Clinical Anatomy
- **Volvulus:** Most common in sigmoid (long mesocolon) and caecum
- **Diverticulosis:** Most common in sigmoid (highest intraluminal pressure)
- **Colorectal cancer:** Most common in rectosigmoid region
- **Ischaemic colitis:** Splenic flexure watershed
- **Taeniae coli converge** at base of appendix — surgical landmark
- **Pectinate line:** crucial anatomical landmark for haemorrhoids, fissures, cancer spread

## Development
- Caecum to proximal 2/3 transverse colon: midgut
- Distal 1/3 transverse to upper anal canal: hindgut
- Rotation of midgut loop: 270° counterclockwise
