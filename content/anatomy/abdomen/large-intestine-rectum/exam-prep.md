# Large Intestine & Rectum — Exam Prep

## Quick Summary
Large intestine (~1.5 m): caecum, ascending (retroperitoneal), transverse (intraperitoneal), descending (retroperitoneal), sigmoid (intraperitoneal), rectum, anal canal. Distinguished by taeniae coli, haustra, appendices epiploicae. Pectinate line divides anal canal into upper (portal, autonomic) and lower (systemic, somatic) halves.

## High Yield Points

★ **Taeniae coli, haustra, appendices epiploicae** — distinguish large from small intestine
★ Ascending & descending colon: **retroperitoneal**; Transverse & sigmoid: **intraperitoneal**
★ Splenic flexure is **higher** than hepatic; attached by **phrenicocolic ligament**
★ **Pectinate line**: above = columnar, portal, autonomic, internal haemorrhoids; below = squamous, systemic, somatic, external haemorrhoids
★ Sigmoid volvulus: **most common** volvulus (long mesocolon)
★ **Watershed areas**: Griffith's (splenic flexure), Sudeck's (rectosigmoid)
★ SMA supplies up to **proximal 2/3 transverse colon**; IMA supplies rest
★ Rectum: upper 1/3 peritoneum front+sides; middle front only; lower none
★ Left ureter at **apex of sigmoid mesocolon** — at risk during surgery
★ Midgut rotation: **270° counterclockwise**

## Mnemonics

**Features of large intestine:** "**THE**" — Taeniae coli, Haustra, Epiploicae (appendices)

**Retroperitoneal parts:** "**SAD DUCK**" — Second part of duodenum, Ascending colon, Descending colon (+ Duodenum, Ureters, Colon, Kidneys)

**Pectinate line — above vs below:** "Above = **PAIL**" — Portal drainage, Autonomic nerves, Internal haemorrhoids, Lymph to internal iliac
