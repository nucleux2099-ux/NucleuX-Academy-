# Mechanical Injuries

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 12-14), Parikh's Textbook, Apurba Nandy
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

**Mechanical injuries** are caused by physical force applied to the body. They are classified based on the mechanism of injury and the type of wound produced. Understanding wound characteristics helps determine:
- Type of weapon used
- Manner of causation (accident/suicide/homicide)
- Direction and force of impact

## Key Points

### Classification of Mechanical Injuries

| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Abrasion** | Friction/scraping | Graze, scratch |
| **Contusion (Bruise)** | Blunt force → extravasation | Black eye, ecchymosis |
| **Laceration** | Blunt force → tearing | Torn wound |
| **Incised wound** | Sharp cutting force | Cut, slash, stab |
| **Stab wound** | Sharp penetrating force | Knife wound |
| **Chop wound** | Heavy sharp weapon | Axe wound |

### Abrasions

**Types:**
1. **Scratch** — Fingernail marks (linear)
2. **Graze/Sliding** — Tangential friction (road rash)
3. **Pressure** — Pattern (ligature, teeth)
4. **Impact** — Blow imprint

**Characteristics:**
- Superficial (epidermis only)
- Red-brown discoloration
- May show pattern of causative agent
- Heal without scar if superficial
- **MLI:** Direction of force (tag points toward direction)

### Contusions (Bruises)

**Formation:** Blunt force → vessel rupture → blood extravasation into tissues

**Color Changes (Age Estimation):**
| Color | Time |
|-------|------|
| Red | Fresh (<24 hours) |
| Blue/Purple | 1-3 days |
| Bluish-green | 3-6 days |
| Greenish-yellow | 5-7 days |
| Yellow/Brown | 1-2 weeks |
| Disappears | 2-3 weeks |

**Note:** Colors overlap; age estimation is approximate

**Factors Affecting Contusion:**
- **Tissue type** — Loose tissue (eyelids) bruises easily
- **Vessel fragility** — Elderly, anticoagulants
- **Skin color** — Difficult to see on dark skin
- **Force and area** — Greater force = larger bruise

### Lacerations

**Characteristics:**
- **Irregular margins** (torn, not cut)
- **Tissue bridges** (intact strands crossing wound)
- **Underlying tissue visible**
- **Bruising around edges**
- **Hair bulbs intact** (crushed, not cut)

**Common Sites:** Scalp (over bone), shins, eyebrows

### Incised Wounds

**Characteristics:**
- **Clean-cut margins** (sharp)
- **No tissue bridges**
- **Length > depth** (cutting)
- **Hair cleanly cut**
- **Tails** at ends (where blade entered/exited)
- **Minimal bruising**

**Types:**
1. **Slash/Cut** — Longer than deep
2. **Chop** — Deep, heavy weapon (axe, sword)

### Stab Wounds

**Characteristics:**
- **Depth > length** (penetrating)
- **Reflects weapon shape** (single/double-edged)
- **Entry wound only** OR entry + exit

**Weapon Identification:**
| Knife Type | Wound Shape |
|------------|-------------|
| Single-edged | One angle acute, one squared |
| Double-edged | Both angles acute (spindle-shaped) |
| Screwdriver | Round/square with abrasion ring |

**Depth may exceed blade length** — Skin compression during thrust

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Documentation Requirements
For each wound, record:
1. Type of wound
2. Size (L × B × D in cm)
3. Shape
4. Location (anatomical landmarks)
5. Margins (clean/irregular)
6. Floor/depth
7. Direction
8. Age (fresh/healing)
9. Underlying damage

### Differentiating Suicide vs Homicide

| Feature | Suicide | Homicide |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Location** | Accessible (wrist, neck) | Any site |
| **Number** | Often multiple attempts | Variable |
| **Tentative cuts** | Present | Absent |
| **Defense wounds** | Absent | Often present |
| **Clothes** | Often removed at site | Damaged |
| **Wound direction** | Self-accessible | Variable |

### Defense Wounds
- Injuries from warding off attack
- **Active defense:** Hands, forearms (palmar surface)
- **Passive defense:** Back of hands, outer forearms
- Strongly suggest **homicide**

## Examination Findings

### Weapon Identification from Wounds

| Wound Type | Possible Weapon |
|------------|-----------------|
| Parallel scratch marks | Fingernails |
| Curved bruise | Rod, stick |
| Tram-line bruise | Narrow rod (double lines) |
| Pattern bruise | Specific object (belt buckle) |
| Clean incised wound | Sharp blade |
| Chop wound | Axe, heavy blade |

### Antemortem vs Postmortem Wounds

| Feature | Antemortem | Postmortem |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Bleeding | Present, profuse | Minimal |
| Wound edges | Swollen, everted | Dry, no swelling |
| Blood clot | Present | Minimal/absent |
| Vital reaction | Inflammation present | Absent |
| Color | Red, hemorrhagic | Pale, yellow |

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Tissue bridges present in:** Laceration (NOT incised wound)
2. **Tram-line bruise caused by:** Narrow rod (parallel lines with pale center)
3. **Defense wounds suggest:** Homicide
4. **Tentative/hesitation cuts suggest:** Suicide
5. **Spindle-shaped stab wound indicates:** Double-edged blade
6. **Contusion color yellow at:** 5-7 days (hemosiderin)
7. **Bruise appears fastest in:** Loose tissue (eyelids, scrotum)
8. **Direction of abrasion shown by:** Tag end (epithelial flap)

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: How to differentiate laceration from incised wound?
  - A: Laceration has irregular margins, tissue bridges, bruised edges; Incised wound has clean margins, no bridges

- Q: Can bruises appear postmortem?
  - A: No — need circulation for extravasation; PM lividity is NOT a bruise

### IPC Sections

| Section | Type of Harm |
|---------|--------------|
| **319** | Hurt (bodily pain/disease/infirmity) |
| **320** | Grievous hurt (8 types listed) |
| **321** | Voluntarily causing hurt |
| **322** | Voluntarily causing grievous hurt |

### Eight Types of Grievous Hurt (Section 320 IPC)
1. Emasculation
2. Permanent loss of sight (either eye)
3. Permanent loss of hearing (either ear)
4. Loss of any member or joint
5. Destruction of any member or joint
6. Permanent disfiguration of head/face
7. Fracture or dislocation of bone/tooth
8. Hurt endangering life or causing severe pain for >20 days
