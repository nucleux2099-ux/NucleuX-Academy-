# Regional Injuries

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 16-17), Parikh's Textbook, Apurba Nandy
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

Different body regions have unique vulnerabilities and injury patterns. Understanding regional anatomy helps in:
- Interpreting injury mechanisms
- Predicting complications
- Determining severity and prognosis
- Reconstructing events

## Key Points

### Head Injuries

**Types:**
1. **Scalp injuries:** Abrasion, contusion, laceration
2. **Skull fractures:** Linear, depressed, comminuted, base
3. **Intracranial hemorrhage:** Extradural, subdural, subarachnoid, intracerebral
4. **Brain injuries:** Concussion, contusion, laceration

**Scalp Layers (SCALP):**
- **S**kin
- **C**onnective tissue (dense)
- **A**poneurosis (galea)
- **L**oose areolar tissue
- **P**ericranium

**Skull Fractures:**
| Type | Characteristics | Cause |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Linear | Simple crack | Fall, blow |
| Depressed | Bone pushed inward | Focal impact |
| Comminuted | Multiple fragments | Severe force |
| Diastatic | Along suture lines | Children |
| Ring (base) | Around foramen magnum | Fall on feet/buttocks |

**Intracranial Hemorrhages:**
| Type | Location | Cause | Classic Feature |
|------|----------|-------|-----------------|
| Extradural | Between skull and dura | Middle meningeal artery tear | Lucid interval |
| Subdural | Between dura and arachnoid | Bridging vein tear | Crescent on CT |
| Subarachnoid | Subarachnoid space | Berry aneurysm, trauma | Blood in CSF |
| Intracerebral | Within brain parenchyma | Hypertension, trauma | Focal deficit |

### Chest Injuries

**Rib Fractures:**
- Single rib: Simple injury
- Multiple ribs: Serious, look for complications
- **Flail chest:** ≥3 consecutive ribs fractured in ≥2 places
- First rib fracture → High-energy injury, check great vessels

**Complications:**
- Pneumothorax
- Hemothorax
- Cardiac contusion
- Aortic injury (deceleration)

### Abdominal Injuries

**Solid Organs (vulnerable to blunt trauma):**
- Spleen (most common in blunt trauma)
- Liver
- Kidney

**Hollow Organs (vulnerable to compression):**
- Stomach (when full)
- Bladder (when full)
- Intestines (especially fixed points)

**Seatbelt Injury Pattern:**
- Abdominal wall bruise
- Lumbar spine fracture (Chance fracture)
- Mesenteric tear
- Bowel injury

### Spinal Injuries

**Level and Consequence:**
| Level | Common Cause | Consequence |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Cervical (C1-4) | Hyperflexion, diving | Quadriplegia, death |
| Cervical (C5-7) | Whiplash, falls | Upper limb weakness |
| Thoracic | Direct trauma, falls | Paraplegia |
| Lumbar | Falls, seatbelt | Lower limb weakness |

**Jefferson Fracture:** C1 burst (diving injury)
**Hangman's Fracture:** C2 bilateral pars (hyperextension)

### Extremity Injuries

**Fracture Patterns:**
| Type | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| Spiral | Twisting |
| Transverse | Direct blow |
| Oblique | Angled force |
| Comminuted | High energy |
| Greenstick | Pediatric, incomplete |

**Defense Wounds:**
- Forearm (ulna): Parrying injury
- Palm: Grasping weapon
- Fingers: Holding/blocking

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Head Injuries — MLI
- Common in assault, accidents, falls
- Minimal external injury may hide severe internal injury
- Always document level of consciousness (GCS)
- Imaging (CT) essential before opinion

**Contre-coup Injury:**
- Brain injury opposite to impact site
- Deceleration mechanism
- MLI: May confuse site of impact

### Traffic Accident Pattern Injuries
| Victim Type | Common Injuries |
|-------------|-----------------|
| **Pedestrian** | Primary (bumper), secondary (fall), tertiary (run over) |
| **Driver** | Steering wheel (chest), dashboard (knees), seatbelt |
| **Rear passenger** | Head injury, cervical spine |
| **Motorcyclist** | Head, long bones, degloving |

### Fall from Height
| Surface | Injuries |
|---------|----------|
| Feet first | Calcaneal fractures, vertebral compression |
| Head first | Skull fractures, cervical spine |
| Side | Rib fractures, pelvic injury |

**Estimating Height:** Severity increases with height; fatal injuries common above 3 floors

## Examination Findings

### Head Injury Assessment
- GCS score documentation
- Pupil size and reactivity
- Battle's sign (mastoid bruising) → Base of skull fracture
- Raccoon eyes (periorbital ecchymosis) → Anterior cranial fossa fracture
- CSF rhinorrhea/otorrhea → Base of skull fracture
- Scalp palpation (deformity, crepitus)

### Abdominal Assessment
- Seatbelt sign (abdominal wall bruise)
- Guarding, rigidity
- Cullen's sign (periumbilical bruising) → Retroperitoneal bleed
- Grey Turner's sign (flank bruising) → Retroperitoneal bleed

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Lucid interval characteristic of:** Extradural hemorrhage
2. **Crescent-shaped hemorrhage:** Subdural hemorrhage
3. **Talk and die:** Extradural hemorrhage (lucid interval then deterioration)
4. **Contre-coup injury:** Brain injury opposite to impact site
5. **Flail chest:** ≥3 consecutive ribs fractured at ≥2 places
6. **Most common solid organ injured in blunt trauma:** Spleen
7. **Battle's sign indicates:** Base of skull fracture
8. **Ring fracture of skull base:** Fall on feet/buttocks

### Classic Patterns
| Pattern | Suggests |
|---------|----------|
| Bumper fracture (tibia) | Pedestrian hit by vehicle |
| Contre-coup | Deceleration injury |
| Defense wounds | Homicide |
| Hesitation marks | Suicide |
| Patterned bruise | Specific weapon |

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: What is a lucid interval?
  - A: Period of consciousness between initial injury and deterioration in extradural hemorrhage
  
- Q: What is flail chest?
  - A: ≥3 consecutive ribs fractured in ≥2 places, causing paradoxical movement

### IPC Application
- **Head injury with subdural hematoma requiring surgery:** Grievous (Sec 320 Clause 8 — endangering life)
- **Femur fracture:** Grievous (Sec 320 Clause 7 — fracture)
- **Abdominal injury with splenectomy:** Grievous (Sec 320 Clause 4/5 — loss of organ)
