# Thermal Injuries

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 18-19), Parikh's Textbook, Apurba Nandy
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

**Thermal injuries** include damage from heat (burns, scalds), cold (frostbite, hypothermia), and electricity. These injuries are common in accidents, suicide, and homicide, making their forensic evaluation crucial.

## Key Points

### Burns Classification

**By Depth:**
| Degree | Layers Involved | Appearance | Healing |
|--------|-----------------|------------|---------|
| **First (Superficial)** | Epidermis only | Red, painful, no blisters | Few days, no scar |
| **Second (Partial)** | Epidermis + dermis | Blisters, painful | 2-3 weeks, possible scar |
| **Third (Full)** | All skin layers | White/charred, painless | Grafting needed |
| **Fourth (Deep)** | Muscle, bone | Charred, necrotic | Amputation may be needed |

**By Surface Area — Rule of 9s (Wallace):**
| Region | Adult % | Child % |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Head/Neck | 9% | 18% |
| Each arm | 9% | 9% |
| Anterior trunk | 18% | 18% |
| Posterior trunk | 18% | 18% |
| Each leg | 18% | 14% |
| Perineum | 1% | 1% |

**Palm Rule:** Patient's palm ≈ 1% BSA (for scattered burns)

### Causes of Death in Burns
1. **Immediate:** Neurogenic shock (pain), asphyxia (fire)
2. **Early (hours-days):** Hypovolemic shock, ARDS
3. **Late (days-weeks):** Sepsis, renal failure, DIC

### Antemortem vs Postmortem Burns

| Feature | Antemortem | Postmortem |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Vital reaction | Present (inflammation, redness) | Absent |
| Blistering | Clear fluid, high protein | Air, low protein |
| Soot in airways | Present if fire | Absent |
| COHb levels | Elevated (>10%) | Normal |
| Red line at margin | Present | Absent |

### Fire Deaths — Key Findings

**Signs of Antemortem Burns:**
1. **Soot in trachea/bronchi** — Person was breathing
2. **Elevated COHb** — CO poisoning (cherry pink color)
3. **Pugilistic attitude** — Heat coagulation of muscles (also occurs postmortem)
4. **Heat ruptures** — Clean margins, no vital reaction
5. **Cherry pink lividity** — CO in blood

**Heat Hematoma (Postmortem):**
- Epidural blood collection from skull heating
- Chocolate brown color (not red)
- Distinguishable from antemortem epidural hematoma

### Scalds (Hot Liquid Burns)

**Characteristics:**
- Defined margins (liquid boundary)
- Flow pattern visible
- No singeing of hair (unlike flame)
- Clothing protection pattern

**Differentiation:**
| Accidental | Intentional |
|------------|-------------|
| Splash pattern | Immersion pattern (stocking-glove) |
| Upper body (spill) | Lower body (dipping) |
| Irregular margins | Sharp waterline |

### Electrocution

**Factors Affecting Severity:**
- Voltage and current type (AC more dangerous)
- Duration of contact
- Pathway through body (heart involvement)
- Resistance of skin (wet = lower resistance)

**Electric Mark (Joule Burn):**
- Pale, depressed, firm lesion
- Crater-like appearance
- May show metallization (conductor pattern)
- Location indicates entry point

**Cause of Death:**
- Ventricular fibrillation (low voltage, AC)
- Respiratory arrest (high voltage)
- Burns (high voltage)

### Lightning Injury

**Features:**
- **Lichtenberg figures** — Arborescent (tree-like) skin marks
- **Keraunographic markings** — Pattern of clothing
- **Magnetization** of metal objects
- **Clothing explosion** (steam from sweat)

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Suicide vs Homicide vs Accident in Burns

| Feature | Suicide | Homicide | Accident |
|---------|---------|----------|----------|
| **Accelerant use** | Often | Often | Rare |
| **Pour pattern** | Over self | On body | Splash |
| **Site** | Self-accessible | Variable | Variable |
| **Other injuries** | Absent | May be present | Absent |
| **Scene evidence** | Container near body | May be hidden | Hazard present |

### Dowry Death (Section 304B IPC)
- Death of woman within 7 years of marriage
- Burns or bodily injury under abnormal circumstances
- Cruelty/harassment by husband or relatives
- Presumption of dowry death if harassment proven

### Documentation
- Percentage BSA involved
- Depth of burns
- Distribution pattern
- Soot/inhalation signs
- Other injuries
- Scene photographs
- Toxicology (CO, drugs)

## Examination Findings

### Burn Examination Protocol
1. **Calculate BSA** (Rule of 9s)
2. **Assess depth** at each site
3. **Document pattern:**
   - Pour pattern (accelerant)
   - Splash pattern (accident)
   - Protected areas (clothing, folded skin)
4. **Airways:** Soot, singed nasal hair
5. **Blood for COHb**
6. **Photography** with scale

### Cold Injuries

**Frostbite Stages:**
| Stage | Features |
|-------|----------|
| First | Numbness, pallor, recovery |
| Second | Blisters, edema |
| Third | Necrosis, eschar |
| Fourth | Deep tissue/bone necrosis |

**Hypothermia:**
- Core temp <35°C
- Paradoxical undressing (confusion)
- Wischnewsky spots (gastric mucosal hemorrhages)
- Postmortem pink coloration

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Rule of 9s — adult leg:** 18%
2. **Rule of 9s — child head:** 18%
3. **Soot in airways indicates:** Alive during fire (antemortem)
4. **COHb level in fire deaths:** Usually >30%
5. **Pugilistic attitude caused by:** Heat coagulation of muscles
6. **Heat hematoma color:** Chocolate brown (not red)
7. **Electric mark features:** Pale, depressed, firm, crater-like
8. **Lichtenberg figure seen in:** Lightning strike

### Cherry Pink Coloration Causes
- CO poisoning
- Cyanide poisoning
- Cold/refrigeration
- Postmortem in cold weather

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: How to differentiate antemortem from postmortem burns?
  - A: Antemortem shows vital reaction, soot in airways, elevated COHb
  
- Q: What is pugilistic attitude?
  - A: Boxer-like posture (flexed limbs) due to heat coagulation — occurs both ante- and postmortem

- Q: What is Joule burn?
  - A: Electric mark at contact point — pale, depressed, firm
