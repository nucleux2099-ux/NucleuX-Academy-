# Injury Classification - IPC Framework

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 11), Indian Penal Code, Parikh's Textbook
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

In medico-legal practice, injuries are classified according to the **Indian Penal Code (IPC)** to determine the severity of harm and corresponding legal consequences. This classification guides charging decisions and sentencing.

**Note:** IPC 1860 has been replaced by **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023**, but IPC framework remains relevant for understanding concepts.

## Key Points

### IPC Classification of Injuries

| Section | Type | Definition |
|---------|------|------------|
| **319** | Hurt | Causing bodily pain, disease, or infirmity |
| **320** | Grievous Hurt | Eight specified serious injuries |
| **321** | Voluntarily causing hurt | Intentional hurt |
| **322** | Voluntarily causing grievous hurt | Intentional GH |
| **323** | Punishment for hurt | Up to 1 year |
| **324** | Hurt by dangerous weapon | Up to 3 years |
| **325** | Punishment for grievous hurt | Up to 7 years |
| **326** | GH by dangerous weapon | Up to life |

### Eight Types of Grievous Hurt (Section 320 IPC)

| # | Type | Explanation |
|---|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Emasculation** | Depriving of masculine vigor |
| 2 | **Permanent loss of sight** | Either eye |
| 3 | **Permanent loss of hearing** | Either ear |
| 4 | **Privation of any member** | Loss of limb/organ |
| 5 | **Destruction/permanent impairing** | Member/joint useless |
| 6 | **Permanent disfiguration** | Head or face |
| 7 | **Fracture/dislocation** | Any bone or tooth |
| 8 | **Hurt endangering life** | OR severe bodily pain for ≥20 days |

### Simple vs Grievous Hurt

| Feature | Simple Hurt | Grievous Hurt |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Definition | Sec 319 | Sec 320 |
| Examples | Minor cuts, bruises | Fractures, permanent injury |
| Healing | Usually <20 days | ≥20 days or permanent |
| Punishment | Up to 1 year | Up to 7 years |

### Dangerous Weapons (Section 324, 326)
- Any instrument for shooting, stabbing, cutting
- Fire or heated substance
- Poison or corrosive substance
- Any explosive substance
- Any substance deleterious to human body

### Other Relevant IPC Sections

| Section | Offense |
|---------|---------|
| **307** | Attempt to murder |
| **308** | Attempt to culpable homicide |
| **326A** | Voluntarily causing GH by acid attack |
| **326B** | Throwing/attempting to throw acid |
| **334** | Hurt on provocation |
| **335** | GH on provocation |

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Classification Criteria for Doctor
When examining injuries, consider:
1. **Endangering to life?** → Grievous (Type 8)
2. **Likely to cause ≥20 days incapacity?** → Grievous (Type 8)
3. **Involves fracture/dislocation?** → Grievous (Type 7)
4. **Permanent loss of function?** → Grievous (Type 4, 5)
5. **Permanent disfigurement of head/face?** → Grievous (Type 6)
6. **None of above?** → Simple hurt

### Writing Opinion
**Format in Wound Certificate:**

> "The injury is simple/grievous in nature and was caused by hard and blunt/sharp/pointed object."

OR

> "The injury is grievous in nature as it falls under Section 320 IPC, Clause [number] — [description]."

**Example:**
> "The fracture of right radius is grievous in nature falling under Section 320 IPC, Clause 7 (fracture of bone)."

### Age of Injury
| Stage | Duration | Features |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Fresh** | <12 hours | Bleeding, no healing |
| **Recent** | 12-24 hours | Clot, early inflammation |
| **Healing** | Days-weeks | Scab, granulation |
| **Healed** | Weeks-months | Scar formation |

### Duration of Disability Assessment
| Injury Type | Typical Duration |
|-------------|------------------|
| Simple abrasions | 3-5 days |
| Contusions | 1-2 weeks |
| Simple fractures | 4-8 weeks |
| Compound fractures | 8-12+ weeks |
| Lacerations | 1-3 weeks |

## Examination Findings

### Wound Certificate Contents
1. MLC number, date, time
2. Patient identification
3. History (patient's words)
4. Description of each injury:
   - Type, size, shape
   - Location, direction
   - Age (fresh/healing)
5. Opinion on:
   - Simple or grievous
   - Weapon type
   - Dangerous to life
6. Doctor's signature and seal

### Dangerous to Life Assessment
Consider danger if:
- Hemorrhage (significant blood loss)
- Shock
- Injury to vital organs
- Septicemia risk
- Respiratory compromise
- Requires urgent intervention

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Simple hurt defined in:** Section 319 IPC
2. **Grievous hurt defined in:** Section 320 IPC
3. **Number of types of grievous hurt:** 8 (eight)
4. **Fracture/dislocation is:** Grievous hurt (Clause 7)
5. **Duration criterion for GH:** ≥20 days of severe pain/incapacity
6. **Acid attack specific section:** 326A (voluntarily), 326B (throwing)
7. **Punishment for hurt:** Up to 1 year (Sec 323)
8. **Punishment for grievous hurt:** Up to 7 years (Sec 325)

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: What makes an injury "grievous"?
  - A: Falls under any of the 8 clauses of Section 320 IPC
  
- Q: Is fracture of tooth grievous?
  - A: Yes — Section 320, Clause 7 includes "tooth"

- Q: Who decides if injury is simple or grievous?
  - A: Doctor gives opinion in wound certificate; court makes final decision

### Common Scenarios

| Injury | Classification | Clause |
