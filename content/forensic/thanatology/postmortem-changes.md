# Postmortem Changes

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 8-10), Parikh's Textbook of Medical Jurisprudence
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

Postmortem changes are physical and chemical alterations occurring in the body after death. They are classified into:
- **Immediate changes** (somatic death signs)
- **Early changes** (within 24-48 hours)
- **Late changes** (after 48 hours)

Understanding PM changes is crucial for **estimating time since death** and differentiating natural from unnatural deaths.

## Key Points

### Immediate Changes (Somatic Death Signs)
- **Insensibility** - Loss of sensations
- **Loss of voluntary movements**
- **Cessation of respiration**
- **Cessation of circulation**
- **Changes in eyes:**
  - Loss of corneal reflex
  - Pupils dilated and fixed
  - Loss of intraocular tension (flaccidity of eyeball)
  - Tache noire (black spot) - drying of sclera

### Early Postmortem Changes

| Change | Onset | Duration |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Cooling (Algor mortis) | Immediately | Until ambient temp |
| Lividity (Livor mortis) | 1-2 hours | Fixed by 6-12 hours |
| Rigidity (Rigor mortis) | 1-2 hours | Disappears 24-48 hours |
| Cadaveric spasm | Instantaneous | Until rigor starts |

### Late Postmortem Changes
- **Decomposition** (autolysis + putrefaction)
- **Adipocere formation** (saponification)
- **Mummification** (desiccation)
- **Maceration** (dead fetus in utero)

## Medico-Legal Aspects

1. **Time since death estimation** - Critical in criminal investigations
2. **Position of body after death** - Livor mortis pattern
3. **Disturbance of body** - Shifting of lividity
4. **Cause of death clues:**
   - Cherry pink lividity → CO poisoning, cold
   - Brownish lividity → Nitrites, aniline poisoning
5. **Documentation required:**
   - Exact time of examination
   - Ambient temperature
   - Clothing and body position
   - All PM changes observed with measurements

## Examination Findings

### Algor Mortis (Cooling)
- **Rate:** ~1.5°F (0.8°C) per hour initially
- **Newton's law of cooling** modified by Henssge nomogram
- **Factors affecting:**
  - Body build (obese cools slower)
  - Clothing and covering
  - Ambient temperature
  - Cause of death (sepsis = higher initial temp)

### Livor Mortis (Postmortem Lividity)
- Purple-red discoloration in dependent parts
- **Appears:** 1-2 hours after death
- **Becomes fixed:** 6-12 hours (blood coagulates in capillaries)
- **Blanches on pressure** until fixation
- **Absent in:** Anemia, exsanguination, pressure points

### Rigor Mortis (Postmortem Rigidity)
- **Nysten's Rule:** Starts in face → neck → trunk → limbs
- **Disappears in same order** (face first, legs last)
- **Primary flaccidity → Rigor → Secondary flaccidity**
- **Heat rigor** - Immediate stiffening in fire deaths (heat coagulation)
- **Cold stiffening** - Frozen bodies appear rigid

### Cadaveric Spasm
- Instantaneous rigor in muscles actively used at death
- Indicates **last act before death**
- Cannot be reproduced artificially
- Common in: Suicide (gripping weapon), drowning (clutching weeds)

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Rigor mortis first appears in:** Eyelids/face (involuntary muscles)
2. **Rigor mortis lasts:** 24-48 hours in Indian climate
3. **Livor mortis fixed by:** 6-12 hours
4. **Cherry pink lividity indicates:** CO poisoning or cold exposure
5. **Cadaveric spasm has MLI because:** It indicates last voluntary act
6. **Primary relaxation:** Immediately after death (muscles flaccid)
7. **Nysten's rule:** Face → neck → trunk → upper limbs → lower limbs

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: Difference between rigor mortis and cadaveric spasm?
  - A: Cadaveric spasm is instantaneous, involves specific muscle groups, cannot be reproduced; rigor develops gradually in all muscles
  
- Q: Why does shifting of lividity occur?
  - A: Before fixation (6-12 hours), blood is still fluid and settles by gravity
