# Time Since Death Estimation

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 10), Parikh's Textbook, Apurba Nandy
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

**Time Since Death (TSD)** or **Postmortem Interval (PMI)** is the time elapsed between death and examination. This is one of the most important medico-legal determinations in forensic practice.

Multiple parameters must be considered together — **no single finding is reliable in isolation**.

## Key Points

### Early Period (0-24 hours)
| Finding | Time Estimation |
|---------|-----------------|
| Warm, no rigor, no lividity | <2 hours |
| Warm, rigor developing, lividity present | 2-6 hours |
| Cold, rigor complete, lividity fixed | 6-12 hours |
| Cold, rigor passes off | 24-36 hours |

### Algor Mortis (Body Cooling)
- **Standard formula:** 
  - First 6 hours: 1.5°F/hour
  - After 6 hours: 0.75°F/hour
- **Henssge Nomogram:** Most accurate method
- **Rectal temperature** preferred (core temp)
- **Factors modifying rate:**
  - Obesity (slower cooling)
  - Thin body (faster cooling)
  - High ambient temp (slower)
  - Water immersion (faster)
  - Clothing/covering (slower)

### Rigor Mortis Timeline
- **Onset:** 1-2 hours (starts in face)
- **Complete:** 12 hours
- **Persists:** 12-24 hours
- **Passes off:** 24-36 hours
- In **hot climate (India):** Faster onset and resolution
- In **cold climate:** Delayed and prolonged

### Livor Mortis Timeline
- **Appears:** 1-2 hours
- **Well-developed:** 4-6 hours
- **Fixed:** 6-12 hours
- **Becomes fixed** when blood coagulates in vessels

### Other Parameters

**Stomach Contents:**
| Finding | Time Since Last Meal |
|---------|---------------------|
| Stomach full, food recognizable | <2 hours |
| Stomach half-empty | 2-4 hours |
| Stomach empty | >4-6 hours |

**Vitreous Humor Analysis:**
- Potassium rises after death: 0.14 mEq/L/hour
- **Formula:** TSD (hours) = 7.14 × [K+] - 39.1
- Most reliable biochemical method

**Entomology (Insect Evidence):**
- Blowflies arrive within minutes
- Maggots (larvae) in 12-24 hours
- Beetle larvae after 1 week
- Lifecycle can estimate TSD in days to weeks

## Medico-Legal Aspects

1. **Court testimony** often hinges on TSD
2. **Alibi verification** of accused depends on accurate TSD
3. Must give **range, not exact time** (e.g., "between 6-12 hours")
4. **Document all parameters** examined
5. Multiple methods should be used together
6. **Environmental factors** must be recorded:
   - Room temperature
   - Ventilation
   - Body covering
   - Season

## Examination Findings

### Systematic Approach to TSD
1. **Note ambient conditions:**
   - Room/outdoor temperature
   - Humidity, ventilation
   - Body position and clothing

2. **Examine algor mortis:**
   - Rectal temperature (thermometer)
   - Compare with ambient temp
   - Apply Henssge nomogram

3. **Examine rigor mortis:**
   - Check all joints systematically
   - Note if developing, complete, or passing

4. **Examine livor mortis:**
   - Color and distribution
   - Blanching on pressure (fixed vs unfixed)
   - Position correlation

5. **Eye changes:**
   - Corneal opacity (begins 2-3 hours)
   - Tache noire (exposed sclera dries)

6. **Decomposition signs** (if late)

### Rule of Thumb Summary
| Hours After Death | Typical Findings |
|-------------------|------------------|
| 0-2 | Warm, flaccid, no lividity |
| 2-6 | Warm, rigor developing, lividity appears |
| 6-12 | Cold, rigor complete, lividity fixed |
| 12-24 | Cold, rigor complete to passing |
| 24-48 | Cold, rigor passes, decomposition starts |
| >48 | Decomposition evident |

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Most reliable method for TSD:** Multiple parameters combined
2. **Most reliable biochemical marker:** Vitreous potassium
3. **Henssge nomogram uses:** Body weight and rectal temperature
4. **Rigor mortis complete by:** 12 hours
5. **Rigor passes off by:** 24-36 hours
6. **Livor becomes fixed by:** 6-12 hours
7. **Gastric emptying complete:** 4-6 hours for average meal
8. **First insect to arrive:** Blowfly (within minutes)

### Common Errors in TSD Estimation
- Relying on single parameter
- Ignoring environmental factors
- Not recording ambient temperature
- Giving exact time instead of range

### Practical Points
- In court, always give **range** with conditions stated
- "Based on the examination findings and environmental factors, death occurred approximately 6-12 hours before examination"
- Never be overconfident about TSD — too many variables!
