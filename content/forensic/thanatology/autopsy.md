# Autopsy (Postmortem Examination)

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 3-4), Parikh's Textbook, Apurba Nandy
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

**Autopsy** (Greek: *autos* = self, *opsis* = view) is the systematic examination of a dead body to determine the **cause, manner, and mode of death**.

Two types:
1. **Medico-legal autopsy** — Ordered by law for suspicious/unnatural deaths
2. **Clinical/Pathological autopsy** — Academic purpose with consent

## Key Points

### Types of Autopsy

| Feature | Medico-Legal | Clinical |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Authority | Police/Magistrate | Hospital authority |
| Consent | Not required | Required from relatives |
| Purpose | Cause of death, criminal investigation | Academic, research |
| Conducted by | Forensic expert | Pathologist |
| Legal validity | Admissible in court | Not for legal purposes |

### Indications for Medico-Legal Autopsy
- All unnatural deaths
- Deaths in custody (police/judicial)
- Deaths within 24 hours of hospital admission
- Sudden unexpected deaths
- Deaths under anesthesia or surgery
- Homicide, suicide, accident (unclear)
- Unclaimed bodies
- Deaths in suspicious circumstances

### Objectives of Autopsy
1. **Establish identity** of deceased
2. Determine **cause of death**
3. Determine **manner of death** (natural/accident/suicide/homicide)
4. Estimate **time since death**
5. Collect evidence for investigation
6. Determine if **treatment was proper** (medical negligence)

## Procedure of Medico-Legal Autopsy

### 1. External Examination
- **Identification markers:** Height, weight, scars, tattoos
- **Clothing and belongings**
- **Postmortem changes:** Rigor, livor, decomposition
- **Injuries:** Describe each with:
  - Type, size, shape
  - Location (anatomical landmarks, distance from fixed points)
  - Direction, margins, depth
  - Age of injury (fresh/healing)
- **Natural disease signs:** Pallor, jaundice, edema
- **Evidence:** Fibers, soil, bloodstains

### 2. Internal Examination
**Standard technique: Virchow method** (organ-by-organ removal)

**Head:**
- Scalp reflection, skull examination
- Subdural, extradural hemorrhage
- Brain (weight: ~1400g)
- Intracranial vessels

**Neck:**
- Layer-by-layer dissection
- Hyoid bone, thyroid cartilage
- Great vessels, airways

**Thorax:**
- Rib fractures, hemothorax
- Lungs (weights: R ~600g, L ~550g)
- Heart (weight: ~300g)
- Great vessels

**Abdomen:**
- Stomach contents (note volume, nature)
- Liver (~1500g), spleen (~150g), kidneys
- Intestines, mesentery
- Uterus (pregnancy), bladder

**Spine:**
- If indicated (spinal injury, asphyxia)

### 3. Collection of Samples
| Sample | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Blood (preservative) | Alcohol, drugs, toxicology |
| Stomach contents | Poison detection |
| Liver, kidney | Toxicology, heavy metals |
| Urine | Drugs of abuse |
| Vitreous humor | TSD, biochemistry |
| Vaginal/anal swabs | Sexual assault |
| Hair, nails | Arsenic, drugs |

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Legal Framework
- **Section 174 CrPC:** Police inquiry into unnatural death
- **Section 176 CrPC:** Magistrate's inquest
- **Section 293 CrPC:** Expert evidence admissible

### Autopsy Report Contents
1. **Preamble:** Authority, date, time, place
2. **Identification particulars**
3. **External examination findings**
4. **Internal examination findings**
5. **Opinion as to cause of death**
6. **Manner of death** (if determinable)
7. **Time since death** (approximation)
8. **Signature and seal**

### Key Points for Report Writing
- Be **objective and factual**
- Describe findings, not opinions (except in final opinion)
- Use **anatomical terminology**
- Mention what was **NOT found** (relevant negatives)
- Photographs should accompany report

## Examination Findings

### Artifacts (False Findings)
- **Agonal changes:** Blood in airways (agonal aspiration)
- **Postmortem changes:** Decomposition, insect activity
- **Handling artifacts:** Injuries during transport
- **Resuscitation injuries:** Rib fractures from CPR

### Preserved Specimens
All specimens must be:
- Properly labeled
- Sealed with seal
- Preserved (formalin/saturated NaCl)
- Chain of custody maintained
- Sent with forwarding letter

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Authority for ML autopsy:** Police (Section 174 CrPC) or Magistrate (Section 176)
2. **Consent needed for ML autopsy:** NO
3. **Who can conduct autopsy:** Any registered medical practitioner
4. **Second autopsy ordered by:** Magistrate/Court
5. **Exhumation ordered by:** Executive Magistrate or Court
6. **Minimum number of doctors for ML autopsy:** One (legal requirement is one)
7. **Bloodless field autopsy done in:** Drowning suspected

### Virchow vs Rokitansky Technique
| Virchow | Rokitansky |
|---------|------------|
| Organ-by-organ removal | En-masse removal |
| More time consuming | Faster |
| Better for teaching | Better for experienced |

### Special Autopsies
- **Virtual autopsy (Virtopsy):** CT/MRI based, non-invasive
- **Exhumation:** Autopsy after burial (ordered by magistrate)
- **Negative autopsy:** No cause found (up to 5% cases)

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: Can relatives refuse ML autopsy?
  - A: No, consent not required for ML autopsy
  
- Q: Who can order exhumation?
  - A: Executive Magistrate (SDM or higher) or Court
