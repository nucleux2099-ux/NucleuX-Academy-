# Death Certification & Declaration

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 7), Parikh's Textbook, Legal Medicine Guidelines
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

**Death certification** is the formal documentation of death by a registered medical practitioner. It serves legal, statistical, and administrative purposes.

Two documents:
1. **Death Certificate** — For registration (Form 4/4A under RBD Act)
2. **Cause of Death Certificate** — Medical certification of cause

## Key Points

### Types of Death
1. **Somatic/Clinical death:** Cessation of vital functions (brain, heart, respiration)
2. **Molecular/Cellular death:** Death of individual cells (2-3 hours after somatic death)
3. **Brain death:** Irreversible cessation of all brain functions (including brainstem)

### Brain Death Criteria (India - THOA 1994)
Required for organ transplantation:

**Prerequisites:**
- Known cause of coma
- Core temperature >35°C
- No CNS depressants
- No metabolic cause

**Clinical Tests (performed twice, 6 hours apart):**
1. No pupillary response to light
2. Absent corneal reflex
3. Absent vestibulo-ocular reflex (cold caloric)
4. No motor response to pain
5. Absent gag reflex
6. Absent cough reflex
7. Apnea test positive (no respiratory effort)

**Certification by:**
- RMP in-charge of hospital
- RMP nominated by hospital
- Neurologist/Neurosurgeon
- Specialist nominated by hospital

### Death Certificate (Form 4)
Issued for deaths where doctor attended during illness.

**Contents:**
- Name, age, sex of deceased
- Father's/Husband's name
- Residence
- Date, time, place of death
- Cause of death (disease/condition)
- Duration of illness
- Doctor's registration number and signature

### Cause of Death Certificate (Form 4A)
For deaths not attended by doctor OR requiring ML investigation.

**WHO Format:**

| Part | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **I(a)** | Immediate cause | Cardiopulmonary arrest |
| **I(b)** | Antecedent cause | Acute myocardial infarction |
| **I(c)** | Underlying cause | Coronary artery disease |
| **II** | Contributing factors | Diabetes mellitus, Hypertension |

The **underlying cause** (lowest line in Part I) is used for mortality statistics.

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Legal Requirements
- **Registration of Births and Deaths Act, 1969**
- Death must be registered within **21 days**
- Penalty for non-registration: Fine up to ₹50
- **Section 46 IEA:** Death certificate is public document

### When NOT to Issue Death Certificate
1. Death due to unnatural causes
2. Suspicious circumstances
3. Death within 24 hours of admission
4. Unknown identity
5. Deaths in custody
6. Doctor not familiar with illness

### Inform Police When
- Apparent suicide, homicide, accident
- Sudden/unexpected death
- Death in suspicious circumstances
- Body found dead
- Unidentified body
- Deaths under anesthesia/operation (suspicious)

### Presumption of Death
- Under **Indian Evidence Act, Section 108:**
- If person not heard of for **7 years** by those who would have heard, presumed dead

### Presumption of Survivorship
- Under **Indian Succession Act:**
- When two persons die in same accident and it's unclear who died first
- **Younger person presumed to survive** the older

## Examination Findings

### Signs of Death to Confirm
**Immediate:**
- Cessation of circulation (no pulse, no heart sounds for 5 minutes)
- Cessation of respiration (no chest movement, no breath sounds)
- Loss of reflexes (corneal, pupillary)

**Early Changes:**
- Corneal opacity (1-2 hours)
- Muscular flaccidity
- Cooling of body

**Tests to Confirm Death:**
| Test | Finding |
|------|---------|
| Magnus test | No pulsation in tied finger |
| Diaphanous test | No red glow in fingers with light |
| Icard's test | Fluorescein injection doesn't cause green discoloration |
| ECG | Flat line for >5 minutes |

### Documentation Checklist
- ☐ Full identification confirmed
- ☐ Time of death recorded
- ☐ Cause of death established
- ☐ Whether natural or unnatural
- ☐ Police information if required
- ☐ Relatives informed
- ☐ Hospital records updated

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **Form 4:** Death attended by doctor
2. **Form 4A:** Death NOT attended by doctor
3. **Registration deadline:** 21 days
4. **Underlying cause of death:** Lowest entry in Part I
5. **Presumption of death:** After 7 years (Section 108 IEA)
6. **Brain death certification:** 4 doctors (as per THOA 1994)
7. **Interval between brain death tests:** 6 hours
8. **Apnea test:** PaCO2 must rise to 60 mmHg

### Suspended Animation
- Condition mimicking death (very feeble vital signs)
- Causes:
  - Drowning (cold water)
  - Electrocution
  - Drug overdose (barbiturates)
  - Hypothermia
  - Cholera
  - Newborns
- **Never certify death** without proper examination!

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: Difference between immediate and underlying cause of death?
  - A: Immediate is final event (arrest); underlying is initiating disease
  
- Q: Who can declare brain death?
  - A: Panel of 4 doctors including neurologist

### IPC Sections Related
- **Section 304A IPC:** Death by negligence
- **Section 201 IPC:** Causing disappearance of evidence of death
