# Medical Documentation

> 📚 **Sources:** Reddy's Forensic Medicine (Ch. 2), Parikh's Textbook, MCI Guidelines
> **Level:** UG

## Core Concept

Medical documentation is the systematic recording of patient information, treatment, and clinical events. It serves clinical, legal, administrative, and research purposes.

**Golden Rule:** "If it's not documented, it didn't happen."

Medical records are **legal documents** and may be used as evidence in court.

## Key Points

### Types of Medical Records

| Record Type | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| **Case sheet/OPD record** | Outpatient visits |
| **Admission record** | Inpatient stay |
| **Operative notes** | Surgical procedures |
| **Anesthesia record** | Anesthesia details |
| **Nursing notes** | Daily patient care |
| **Consent forms** | Permission documentation |
| **Discharge summary** | Summary of admission |
| **Death summary** | Details at death |
| **MLR (Medico-Legal Register)** | Medico-legal cases |

### Medico-Legal Register (MLR)
Maintained for medico-legal cases:

**Entries include:**
- MLC number and date
- Patient identification
- Date/time of incident
- Brought by (police/relatives)
- History (in patient's words)
- Examination findings
- Opinion on nature of injuries
- Treatment given
- Intimation to police

**Cases requiring MLC:**
- All injuries (assault, accident, burns)
- Poisoning/intoxication
- Sexual offenses
- Attempted suicide
- Unconscious unknown persons
- Deaths under suspicious circumstances

### Principles of Good Documentation

**Essential Elements:**
1. **Patient identification** on every page
2. **Date and time** of every entry
3. **Legible** handwriting (or typed)
4. **Chronological** order
5. **Signed** by treating doctor
6. **Black ink** (permanent, photocopy-friendly)
7. **No blank spaces** (draw line if unused)
8. **Corrections:** Single line, date, initial (no overwriting/erasing)

**Content Standards:**
- **Objective** — Document what you see, not assume
- **Specific** — Exact measurements, anatomical landmarks
- **Complete** — Include relevant negatives
- **Accurate** — Don't exaggerate or minimize
- **Timely** — Record as soon as possible

### Injury Documentation (MLR)

**For each injury, record:**
1. **Type** (abrasion, laceration, contusion, etc.)
2. **Size** (length × breadth × depth in cm)
3. **Shape** (oval, linear, irregular)
4. **Location** (anatomical landmark, distance from fixed points)
5. **Direction/axis**
6. **Margins** (clean, irregular, undermined)
7. **Floor** (tissue visible)
8. **Surrounding area** (swelling, discoloration)
9. **Age of injury** (fresh, healing, healed)

**Example Documentation:**
> "Lacerated wound, 4 cm × 1 cm × 0.5 cm, obliquely placed, located 5 cm above right eyebrow and 3 cm to right of midline, irregular margins, floor showing subcutaneous tissue, surrounded by ecchymosis 6 cm in diameter, fresh, actively bleeding."

## Medico-Legal Aspects

### Legal Status of Medical Records

**Indian Evidence Act:**
- **Section 35:** Entries in public records made by public servant = relevant
- **Section 79:** Presumption of genuineness of certified copies
- **Section 80:** Presumption as to documents produced from proper custody

**Medical records are:**
- Primary evidence of treatment given
- Can be summoned by court
- Must be preserved (retention period varies)

### Retention Period
| Record Type | Retention |
|-------------|-----------|
| OPD records | 3 years |
| IPD records | 5 years |
| ML cases | Indefinitely (or as per court direction) |
| X-rays, scans | 3 years |
| Operation notes | Permanently |

### Who Can Access Records
- Patient (has right to copies)
- Legal guardian (for minors/incapacitated)
- Court (on summons)
- Police (with patient consent or court order)
- Insurance (with patient consent)
- Other doctors (for continuity of care)

### Certificates Issued by Doctors

| Certificate | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| **Fitness certificate** | Fitness for job, travel |
| **Medical certificate** | Illness, leave |
| **Wound certificate** | Nature of injuries (for police) |
| **Age certificate** | Age estimation |
| **Death certificate** | Cause of death |
| **Insanity certificate** | Mental status |

**Issuing false certificate:**
- **Section 197 IPC:** Issuing false certificate
- **Section 199 IPC:** Making false statement in certificate
- MCI disciplinary action

## Examination Findings

### Good Documentation Checklist
- ☐ Patient identified (3 identifiers)
- ☐ Date and time on every entry
- ☐ Chief complaints in patient's words
- ☐ Relevant history documented
- ☐ Vital signs recorded
- ☐ Examination findings (positive and negative)
- ☐ Provisional diagnosis
- ☐ Investigations with results
- ☐ Treatment plan and medications
- ☐ Informed consent documented
- ☐ Advice given
- ☐ Follow-up plan
- ☐ Signature with name and designation

### Common Errors to Avoid
- Illegible handwriting
- Undated/unsigned entries
- Using pencil or erasable ink
- Blank spaces left
- Overwriting/erasing
- Using abbreviations not universally known
- Documenting hearsay as fact
- Late entries without notation

## High-Yield for Exams

### Must-Know MCQ Points
1. **MLR maintained for:** Medico-legal cases (injuries, poisoning, assault)
2. **Black ink used because:** Permanent, good for photocopies
3. **Corrections in records:** Single line, date, initial (no erasing)
4. **False certificate IPC:** Section 197
5. **Medical records are:** Legal documents admissible in court
6. **Wound certificate issued to:** Police (not patient)
7. **Dying declaration is:** Oral/written statement before death (Section 32 IEA)
8. **Age certificate required for:** Employment, marriage, prosecution

### Dying Declaration
- Statement by person expecting death
- About cause of death or circumstances
- Admissible under **Section 32(1) IEA**
- Can be oral, written, gestures, nods
- **Can be recorded by:** Anyone (doctor, magistrate, police)
- Preferably before magistrate
- If patient expected to survive → not dying declaration

### Common Viva Questions
- Q: Who can demand medical records?
  - A: Patient (copies), court (original), police (with consent/court order)
  
- Q: How to correct an error in medical record?
  - A: Single line through error, date, initial, correct entry nearby

- Q: What is a wound certificate?
  - A: Certificate describing nature of injuries, issued to police for legal purposes

### Documentation in Specific Situations
| Situation | Special Requirements |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **Assault** | Detailed injury description, photo if possible |
| **Sexual offense** | Use proforma, chain of custody for samples |
| **Poisoning** | Preserve vomitus, stomach wash |
| **Burns** | Surface area %, depth, distribution |
| **Deaths** | Detailed documentation before body leaves |
