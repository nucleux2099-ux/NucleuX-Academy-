# Colles Fracture

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Campbell's Operative Orthopaedics 14th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Colles fracture is a **fracture of the distal radius** occurring within 2.5 cm of the wrist joint with characteristic **dorsal displacement and angulation** of the distal fragment. First described by Abraham Colles in 1814.

**Definition:** Extra-articular fracture of distal radius with:
- Dorsal displacement
- Dorsal angulation (apex volar)
- Radial deviation
- Supination of distal fragment
- Proximal impaction (shortening)

## Key Points

- **Most common fracture** in adults over 50 years
- **Bimodal distribution**: Young males (high-energy trauma), Elderly females (osteoporotic, low-energy fall)
- **Mechanism**: Fall On Outstretched Hand (FOOSH) with wrist in dorsiflexion
- **Dinner fork deformity** is pathognomonic
- Always assess for associated injuries (scaphoid, DRUJ, median nerve)

## Clinical Features

### History
- Fall on outstretched hand
- Wrist dorsiflexed at impact
- Immediate pain, swelling, deformity
- Inability to grip or move wrist

### Examination Findings

**Inspection:**
- **Dinner fork deformity** (lateral view) - dorsal prominence
- **Garden spade deformity** (AP view) - radial deviation
- Swelling, bruising
- Possible skin tenting

**Palpation:**
- Tenderness over distal radius
- Dorsal prominence of distal fragment
- Step deformity
- Check DRUJ stability

**Neurovascular Assessment (Critical!):**
- **Median nerve** - most commonly injured (10%)
  - Acute carpal tunnel syndrome
  - Check thumb opposition, sensation over thenar eminence
- Radial and ulnar pulses
- Capillary refill

### Associated Injuries
- Ulnar styloid fracture (60%)
- DRUJ disruption
- Scaphoid fracture
- Median nerve injury
- TFCC injury

## Imaging

### X-ray (AP + Lateral - Mandatory)

**Normal Parameters:**
| Parameter | Normal Value |
|-----------|--------------|
| Radial inclination | 22-23° |
| Radial height | 11-12 mm |
| Volar tilt | 11-12° (palmar) |

**Colles Fracture Findings:**
- **Radial shortening** (loss of radial height)
- **Dorsal tilt** (reversal of normal volar tilt)
- **Loss of radial inclination**
- **Dorsal displacement** of distal fragment
- ± Comminution
- ± Intra-articular extension

### CT Scan
- Indicated for intra-articular fractures
- Pre-operative planning
- Assess articular step-off

## Classification

### Frykman Classification (Based on involvement of DRUJ and radiocarpal joint)
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| I | Extra-articular |
| II | Extra-articular + ulnar styloid fracture |
| III | Intra-articular (radiocarpal joint) |
| IV | Radiocarpal + ulnar styloid |
| V | Intra-articular (DRUJ) |
| VI | DRUJ + ulnar styloid |
| VII | Both joints involved |
| VIII | Both joints + ulnar styloid |

*Higher number = worse prognosis*

### AO/OTA Classification
- Type A: Extra-articular
- Type B: Partial articular
- Type C: Complete articular

## Management

### Indications for Conservative Treatment
- Stable, extra-articular fractures
- Acceptable reduction maintained
- Low functional demand patients

### Conservative Treatment
1. **Closed reduction under hematoma block/Bier's block**
   - Traction, volar flexion, ulnar deviation
   - Reduce dorsal displacement
   
2. **Immobilization**
   - Below-elbow cast (Colles cast)
   - Wrist in slight flexion and ulnar deviation
   - Duration: 6 weeks
   
3. **Follow-up X-rays**
   - 1 week, 2 weeks, 6 weeks
   - Watch for re-displacement

### Indications for Surgery
- Intra-articular fractures with step-off >2mm
- Unstable fractures (re-displacement after reduction)
- Dorsal angulation >10°
- Radial shortening >3mm
- Open fractures
- Associated carpal injuries
- Polytrauma patients (early mobilization needed)

### Surgical Options
1. **Volar locking plate** (most common, gold standard)
   - Allows early mobilization
   - Better maintenance of reduction

2. **External fixation**
   - Severely comminuted fractures
   - Open fractures

3. **K-wire fixation**
   - Less stable, needs cast support
   - Used in some extra-articular patterns

4. **Fragment-specific fixation**
   - Multiple small plates for complex patterns

## Complications

### Early
- Median nerve compression (acute carpal tunnel)
- Compartment syndrome (rare)
- Skin necrosis from tight cast/swelling

### Late
- **Malunion** (most common) - dinner fork deformity persists
- Stiffness (shoulder-hand syndrome)
- **Sudeck's osteodystrophy** (Complex Regional Pain Syndrome Type 1)
- Carpal tunnel syndrome (delayed)
- Rupture of EPL tendon (Lister's tubercle irritation)
- DRUJ instability
- Post-traumatic arthritis

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Abraham Colles** described in 1814, before X-rays existed - purely clinical description
2. **Dinner fork deformity** - lateral view, **Garden spade** - AP view
3. **Smith's fracture** is "reverse Colles" - volar displacement (fall on dorsiflexed wrist)
4. **Barton's fracture** - intra-articular, marginal (volar or dorsal)
5. **Chauffeur's fracture** - radial styloid fracture
6. **EPL rupture** occurs 6-8 weeks post-injury (attritional rupture over Lister's tubercle)
7. **Acceptable reduction parameters:**
   - Radial shortening <3mm
   - Dorsal angulation <10°
   - Intra-articular step <2mm
8. **Most common complication** = Malunion
9. **Frykman VIII** is the worst type (both joints + ulnar styloid)
10. **Colles cast position**: Slight flexion + ulnar deviation (Cotton-Loder position)

## Comparison Table

| Feature | Colles | Smith | Barton |
|---------|--------|-------|--------|
| Displacement | Dorsal | Volar | Volar/Dorsal |
| Mechanism | FOOSH (extension) | FOOSH (flexion) | Direct blow |
| Deformity | Dinner fork | Garden spade (reversed) | - |
| Joint | Extra-articular | Extra-articular | Intra-articular |
| Treatment | Cast/Volar plate | Volar plate | Plate (always surgical) |

## Mnemonics

**"DORSAL" - Features of Colles Fracture:**
- **D**orsal displacement
- **O**lder patients (osteoporosis)
- **R**adial deviation
- **S**upination
- **A**ngulation (dorsal)
- **L**ength shortened
