# Fracture Classification Systems

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Campbell's Operative Orthopaedics 14th Ed, AO Foundation
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Fracture classification systems provide a **common language** for describing injuries, guiding treatment decisions, and predicting prognosis. A good classification should be:
- **Reproducible** (consistent between observers)
- **Prognostic** (predict outcomes)
- **Guide treatment**

**The AO/OTA System** is the most comprehensive and universally accepted classification, based on anatomical location and fracture complexity.

## Key Points

- Every fracture should be described systematically: **Location, Type, Displacement, Associated injuries**
- Open fractures always need separate classification (**Gustilo-Anderson**)
- Soft tissue injury classification increasingly important
- Know the **commonly examined classifications** for each region

## General Fracture Descriptors

### By Fracture Line
| Type | Description | Stability |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| Transverse | Perpendicular to long axis | Stable if reduced |
| Oblique | Angled to long axis | Tends to shorten |
| Spiral | Twisting fracture line | Often long, may displace |
| Comminuted | >2 fragments | Unstable |
| Segmental | Two separate fractures | Very unstable |
| Butterfly | Triangular fragment | Unstable |

### By Displacement
- **Angulation** - Measured by distal fragment direction (varus/valgus, apex anterior/posterior)
- **Translation** - Shift of fragments (% bone width)
- **Shortening** - Overlap of fragments (mm)
- **Rotation** - Rotational malignment

### By Relationship to Joint
- **Extra-articular** - Does not involve joint surface
- **Partial articular** - Part of articular surface involved
- **Complete articular** - Entire articular surface separated from shaft

## AO/OTA Classification

**Universal Alphanumeric System**

### Bone Numbering
| Number | Bone |
|--------|------|
| 1 | Humerus |
| 2 | Radius/Ulna |
| 3 | Femur |
| 4 | Tibia/Fibula |

### Segment Numbering
| Number | Location |
|--------|----------|
| 1 | Proximal |
| 2 | Diaphysis (shaft) |
| 3 | Distal |

### Type Classification
| Type | Diaphysis | Metaphysis/Epiphysis |
|------|-----------|---------------------|
| A | Simple | Extra-articular |
| B | Wedge | Partial articular |
| C | Complex | Complete articular |

**Example:** 31B2 = Femur (3), Proximal (1), Partial articular (B), Subgroup 2

### Severity Grading (1-3)
Within each type, severity increases: A1 < A2 < A3

## Open Fracture Classification

### Gustilo-Anderson Classification (Most Important!)

| Grade | Wound Size | Contamination | Soft Tissue | Bone Injury |
|-------|------------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| **I** | <1 cm | Clean | Minimal damage | Simple pattern |
| **II** | 1-10 cm | Moderate | Moderate, no flaps | Moderate comminution |
| **III** | >10 cm | Extensive | Severe damage | Severe comminution |

**Grade III Subgroups:**
| Subtype | Features |
|---------|----------|
| **IIIA** | Adequate soft tissue coverage possible |
| **IIIB** | Requires flap coverage (bone exposure) |
| **IIIC** | Arterial injury requiring repair |

**Key Treatment Principles:**
- **All open fractures** need urgent antibiotics, debridement, tetanus
- Grade I: Cefazolin
- Grade II: Cefazolin + Aminoglycoside
- Grade III: Add Penicillin (for anaerobes), consider wound culture
- IIIB/IIIC: Early plastic surgery consultation

## Region-Specific Classifications

### Proximal Femur

| Classification | For | Grades |
|----------------|-----|--------|
| **Garden** | Femoral neck | I-IV (displacement) |
| **Pauwels** | Femoral neck | I-III (angle of fracture) |
| **Boyd & Griffin** | Intertrochanteric | I-IV (complexity) |
| **Fielding** | Subtrochanteric | I-III (level below LT) |

### Distal Radius

| Classification | Basis | Grades |
|----------------|-------|--------|
| **Frykman** | Joint involvement + ulna | I-VIII |
| **Fernandez** | Mechanism | I-V |
| **AO** | Standard alphanumeric | A-C, 1-3 |

### Ankle

**Weber Classification (Fibula):**
| Type | Level | Syndesmosis | Stability |
|------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| A | Below syndesmosis | Intact | Stable |
| B | At syndesmosis | May be injured | Variable |
| C | Above syndesmosis | Disrupted | Unstable |

**Lauge-Hansen Classification:** Based on mechanism (complex, less used clinically)

### Tibial Plateau

**Schatzker Classification:**
| Type | Description | Treatment |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| I | Lateral split | ORIF if displaced |
| II | Lateral split-depression | ORIF + bone graft |
| III | Pure depression (lateral) | ORIF + bone graft |
| IV | Medial plateau | ORIF (high energy) |
| V | Bicondylar | ORIF both |
| VI | Plateau + metaphysis separation | Complex reconstruction |

### Pelvis

**Young-Burgess Classification:**
| Type | Mechanism | Direction |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **APC** | Anterior-Posterior Compression | Opens pelvis |
| **LC** | Lateral Compression | Closes pelvis |
| **VS** | Vertical Shear | Hemipelvis displaced vertically |
| **CM** | Combined Mechanism | Mixed pattern |

**Tile Classification:** A (stable), B (rotationally unstable), C (rotationally + vertically unstable)

### Supracondylar Humerus (Children)

**Gartland Classification:**
| Type | Displacement | Treatment |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| I | Undisplaced | Casting |
| II | Displaced, posterior cortex intact | Closed reduction + K-wires |
| III | Completely displaced | Closed/Open reduction + K-wires |
| IV | Multi-directional instability | Open reduction + K-wires |

### Spine

| Region | Classification | Basis |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Cervical | Allen-Ferguson | Mechanism |
| Odontoid | Anderson-D'Alonzo | Location (I-III) |
| Thoracolumbar | Denis | Columns involved |
| Thoracolumbar | TLICS | Morphology + PLC + Neuro (scoring) |

## Soft Tissue Injury Classification

### Tscherne Classification (Closed Fractures)
| Grade | Soft Tissue Injury |
|-------|-------------------|
| 0 | Minimal soft tissue injury |
| 1 | Superficial abrasion, mild contusion |
| 2 | Deep contaminated abrasion, muscle contusion, impending compartment syndrome |
| 3 | Extensive skin contusion, crush injury, severe muscle damage |

### OTA Open Fracture Classification
More detailed than Gustilo, includes:
- Skin injury
- Muscle injury
- Arterial injury
- Contamination
- Bone loss

## Pediatric Fracture Classifications

### Salter-Harris (Physeal Injuries)
| Type | Involvement | Mnemonic | Prognosis |
|------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| I | Through physis only | **S**lipped | Excellent |
| II | Physis + metaphysis | **A**bove | Good |
| III | Physis + epiphysis | **L**ower | Fair (articular) |
| IV | Through all three | **T**hrough | Poor |
| V | Crush injury to physis | e**R**ased | Worst |

**SALTR** mnemonic: Same, Above, Lower, Through, Ruined

### Rang Classification
Type VI added - injury to perichondral ring

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Gustilo IIIB** - Requires flap coverage, **IIIC** - Has arterial injury
2. **Garden I & II** = Undisplaced, **Garden III & IV** = Displaced (neck of femur)
3. **Pauwels III** (>50°) has worst prognosis - vertical shear forces
4. **Schatzker I-III** = Lateral plateau, **IV** = Medial, **V-VI** = Both
5. **Weber C** fibula fracture = Syndesmosis disruption = Unstable
6. **Salter-Harris II** is most common physeal injury
7. **Salter-Harris IV & V** have worst prognosis (cross physis or crush it)
8. **Gartland III** supracondylar = Most common pattern needing surgery
9. **AO Type C** = Complete articular involvement (worst prognosis)
10. **TLICS ≥5** = Surgical indication for thoracolumbar fractures
11. **Tscherne Grade 3** - Severe soft tissue injury, may need staged treatment
12. **Frykman VIII** - Worst distal radius (both joints + ulnar styloid)

## Classification Pearls

- **Document both Gustilo AND anatomical classification** for open fractures
- **Photography** helps with classification agreement
- **CT scan** may upgrade classification (reveals more comminution)
- When in doubt, **treat for the worse grade** (better to be conservative)
- **Classification drives treatment protocol** - know the decision points
