# Spine Trauma

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Campbell's Operative Orthopaedics 14th Ed, ATLS Guidelines
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Spinal trauma encompasses injuries to the vertebral column and/or spinal cord. The **primary goal** is to prevent secondary neurological injury. Until proven otherwise, **every trauma patient has a spinal injury**.

**Key Principle:** An unstable spine can cause or worsen spinal cord injury. Stability assessment is paramount.

**Denis Three-Column Concept (Thoracolumbar Spine):**
- **Anterior column:** Anterior longitudinal ligament + anterior 2/3 of vertebral body
- **Middle column:** Posterior 1/3 of body + posterior longitudinal ligament
- **Posterior column:** Pedicles, facets, laminae, spinous processes, posterior ligaments

**Two-column disruption = Unstable**

## Key Points

- **C-spine immobilization** until cleared clinically or radiologically
- **Most common level of injury:** C5-C6 (cervical), T12-L1 (thoracolumbar junction)
- **Neurological level** = Most caudal segment with normal motor and sensory function
- **Complete vs Incomplete injury:** Presence of sacral sparing (S4-S5) determines prognosis
- **Spinal shock** - transient loss of function below injury level (areflexia, flaccid paralysis)
- **Neurogenic shock** - hypotension + bradycardia (loss of sympathetic tone)

## Classification

### Cervical Spine Injuries

#### Upper Cervical (C0-C2)

| Injury | Mechanism | Features | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Atlanto-occipital dislocation** | Hyperextension/distraction | Usually fatal | Posterior fusion |
| **Atlas fracture (Jefferson)** | Axial load | Burst of C1 ring, 4 fracture points | Halo vest/fusion |
| **Odontoid fracture** | Flexion/extension | Most common C2 injury | See below |
| **Hangman's fracture** | Hyperextension | Bilateral C2 pars fracture | Halo/collar |
| **Atlantoaxial rotatory subluxation** | Rotation | Torticollis | Reduction, collar |

**Odontoid (Dens) Fracture - Anderson & D'Alonzo Classification:**
| Type | Location | Stability | Treatment |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| I | Tip (rare) | Stable | Collar |
| II | Base (most common) | Unstable, high non-union | Screw fixation or fusion |
| III | Body of C2 | Stable | Halo vest |

#### Lower Cervical (C3-C7)

**Allen-Ferguson Classification:** Based on mechanism
- Compressive Flexion
- Vertical Compression
- Distractive Flexion (most unstable)
- Compressive Extension
- Distractive Extension
- Lateral Flexion

**Common Patterns:**
| Injury | Mechanism | Features | Stability |
|--------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Burst fracture | Axial load | Retropulsed fragments | May be unstable |
| Facet dislocation (unilateral) | Flexion-rotation | 25% subluxation | Unstable |
| Facet dislocation (bilateral) | Flexion | 50% subluxation, locked | Very unstable |
| Clay shoveler's | Flexion | C7 spinous process avulsion | Stable |

### Thoracolumbar Injuries

**Denis Classification:**
| Type | Description | Columns Involved | Stability |
|------|-------------|------------------|-----------|
| Compression | Anterior wedging | Anterior only | Stable |
| Burst | Axial load, retropulsion | Anterior + Middle | Unstable |
| Flexion-distraction (Chance) | Lap belt injury | Middle + Posterior | Unstable |
| Fracture-dislocation | Multiple mechanisms | All three | Very unstable |

**Thoracolumbar Injury Classification & Severity Score (TLICS):**

| Parameter | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| **Morphology** | |
| Compression | 1 |
| Burst | 2 |
| Translation/Rotation | 3 |
| Distraction | 4 |
| **PLC Integrity** | |
| Intact | 0 |
| Suspected injury | 2 |
| Injured | 3 |
| **Neurology** | |
| Intact | 0 |
| Root injury | 2 |
| Complete cord injury | 2 |
| Incomplete cord injury | 3 |
| Cauda equina | 3 |

**TLICS Score:**
- ≤3: Non-operative
- 4: Surgeon discretion
- ≥5: Surgical

## Clinical Features

### History (AMPLE)
- Mechanism of injury
- Pain localization
- Neurological symptoms (weakness, numbness, bladder/bowel)
- Associated injuries

### Examination

**Inspection:**
- Spinal deformity (kyphosis, step-off)
- Bruising, abrasions
- Priapism (sign of spinal cord injury)

**Palpation (with log-roll):**
- Tenderness
- Interspinous gap widening
- Step deformity

**Neurological Examination (ASIA Scale):**

| Grade | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| A | Complete - No motor or sensory function in S4-S5 |
| B | Sensory incomplete - Sensation but no motor below level |
| C | Motor incomplete - Motor present, <50% key muscles grade ≥3 |
| D | Motor incomplete - Motor present, ≥50% key muscles grade ≥3 |
| E | Normal |

**Key Muscles to Test:**
| Level | Muscle | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| C5 | Biceps | Elbow flexion |
| C6 | Wrist extensors | Wrist extension |
| C7 | Triceps | Elbow extension |
| C8 | Finger flexors | Finger flexion |
| T1 | Intrinsics | Finger abduction |
| L2 | Iliopsoas | Hip flexion |
| L3 | Quadriceps | Knee extension |
| L4 | Tibialis anterior | Ankle dorsiflexion |
| L5 | EHL | Great toe extension |
| S1 | Gastrocnemius | Ankle plantarflexion |

**Sacral Sparing (Critical):**
- Perianal sensation
- Anal sphincter tone
- Toe flexion
- If present → Incomplete injury → Better prognosis

## Imaging

### Plain X-ray
**Lateral cervical spine must show C7-T1 junction!**

**ABCS Approach:**
- **A**lignment - Anterior, posterior, spinolaminar lines
- **B**one - Vertebral body heights, fractures
- **C**artilage - Disc spaces
- **S**oft tissue - Prevertebral swelling >7mm at C2, >22mm at C6

### CT Scan
- **Gold standard** for bony injury
- All trauma patients with altered consciousness
- When X-ray inadequate

### MRI
- **Gold standard for cord, ligaments, discs**
- All patients with neurological deficit
- Assess PLC integrity (for TLICS)
- Shows cord edema, hemorrhage, compression

## Management

### Pre-hospital & Emergency
1. **Immobilization** - Rigid collar, spine board
2. **ABC** with C-spine protection
3. **Avoid hypoxia and hypotension** (worsens secondary injury)
4. **Log-roll** for examination
5. **Foley catheter** (neurogenic bladder)

### Medical Management
- **Maintain MAP >85 mmHg** for 7 days (optimize cord perfusion)
- **High-dose steroids** - No longer recommended (NASCIS controversy)
- DVT prophylaxis
- Stress ulcer prophylaxis
- Skin care (prevent pressure sores)

### Surgical Indications
- Progressive neurological deficit
- Incomplete SCI with ongoing compression
- Unstable injuries
- Open injuries
- Polytrauma (early stabilization)

### Surgical Timing
- **Incomplete SCI with compression → Urgent decompression (<24h)**
- **Complete SCI → Early surgery (within 72h) may improve outcomes**
- **Unstable without neuro deficit → Semi-elective (within 72h)**

### Surgical Approaches
- **Anterior:** Corpectomy, fusion, plating (for anterior compression)
- **Posterior:** Pedicle screws, rods, fusion (for posterior instability)
- **Combined:** Severe injuries requiring 360° stabilization

## Spinal Cord Injury Syndromes

| Syndrome | Cause | Features | Prognosis |
|----------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Central cord** | Hyperextension (elderly with stenosis) | Arms > Legs weakness, "man in barrel" | Good |
| **Anterior cord** | Flexion injury, anterior artery | Loss of motor + pain/temp, preserved proprioception | Poor |
| **Posterior cord** | Rare | Loss of proprioception, preserved motor | Moderate |
| **Brown-Séquard** | Penetrating injury | Ipsilateral motor + proprioception loss, contralateral pain/temp loss | Good |
| **Cauda equina** | Below L1-L2 | LMN signs, bladder/bowel dysfunction | Variable |
| **Conus medullaris** | At L1-L2 | Mixed UMN/LMN, early bladder/bowel | Poor |

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Jefferson fracture** - Axial load on C1, "burst" fracture, rule of Spence (>7mm overhang = transverse ligament rupture)
2. **Hangman's fracture** - Bilateral C2 pars fracture, usually neurologically intact (large canal at C2)
3. **Odontoid Type II** - Highest non-union rate (poor blood supply at base)
4. **Chance fracture** - "Lap belt injury," horizontal fracture through vertebra, associated with abdominal injuries
5. **SCIWORA** - Spinal Cord Injury Without Radiographic Abnormality (children)
6. **Central cord syndrome** - Most common incomplete syndrome, elderly + hyperextension + stenosis
7. **Spinal shock** - Areflexia, ends when bulbocavernosus reflex returns
8. **Neurogenic shock** - Hypotension + Bradycardia (loss of sympathetic tone T1-L2)
9. **ASIA A** = Complete injury, **ASIA E** = Normal
10. **Sacral sparing** - Perianal sensation/tone = incomplete injury = better prognosis
11. **Atlanto-dental interval (ADI)** - >3mm in adults indicates C1-C2 instability
12. **Flexion teardrop fracture** - Very unstable, often with anterior cord syndrome

## Special Considerations

### Clearing the C-spine
**NEXUS Criteria (if all met, no imaging needed):**
- No posterior midline tenderness
- No focal neurological deficit
- Normal alertness
- No intoxication
- No distracting injury

**Canadian C-spine Rules** - More sensitive, uses age >65, dangerous mechanism, paresthesias

### Ankylosing Spondylitis
- **Chalk-stick fractures** - Through fused spine
- Very unstable
- High rate of epidural hematoma
- Often need long segment fixation
