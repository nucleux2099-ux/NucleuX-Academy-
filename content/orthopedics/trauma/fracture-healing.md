# Fracture Healing

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Campbell's Operative Orthopaedics 14th Ed
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Fracture healing is a complex regenerative process that restores bone to its original structure and function. Unlike soft tissue healing (which heals by scar), bone heals by **regeneration** - forming new bone identical to the original.

**Two Types of Healing:**
1. **Primary (Direct) Healing** - Requires absolute stability, occurs via Haversian remodeling
2. **Secondary (Indirect) Healing** - More common, occurs via callus formation through endochondral ossification

## Key Points

- Bone is the **only tissue that heals without scar formation**
- Fracture hematoma is **essential** for healing - don't evacuate it!
- Movement at fracture site determines healing type: **Motion → Callus, No motion → Direct**
- Average healing time: Upper limb 6-8 weeks, Lower limb 12-16 weeks
- Children heal faster (abundant periosteum, better blood supply)
- **Wolff's Law**: Bone remodels according to stress placed upon it

## Stages of Secondary (Callus) Healing

### Stage 1: Inflammation & Hematoma (Day 1-7)
- Fracture disrupts blood vessels → hematoma formation
- Inflammatory cells arrive (macrophages, PMNs)
- Cytokines released: IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α
- Hematoma organizes, forms fibrin mesh

### Stage 2: Soft Callus Formation (Week 1-3)
- **Granulation tissue** replaces hematoma
- Fibroblasts and chondroblasts proliferate
- Cartilaginous (soft) callus forms
- Provides **provisional stability**
- Visible on X-ray by week 2-3

### Stage 3: Hard Callus Formation (Week 3-12)
- **Endochondral ossification** - cartilage replaced by woven bone
- Osteoblasts deposit osteoid on cartilage scaffold
- Hard callus provides mechanical stability
- Clinical union achieved (no movement at fracture site)

### Stage 4: Remodeling (Months to Years)
- Woven bone replaced by **lamellar bone**
- Excess callus resorbed by osteoclasts
- Medullary canal reconstituted
- Bone regains original shape and strength
- Guided by **Wolff's Law**

## Clinical Features

### Signs of Fracture Union
| Clinical Union | Radiological Union |
|----------------|-------------------|
| No tenderness at fracture site | Callus bridging ≥3 cortices |
| No abnormal mobility | Obliteration of fracture line |
| No pain on stressing | Trabecular continuity |

### Time to Union (Average)
| Bone | Time |
|------|------|
| Clavicle | 6 weeks |
| Humerus shaft | 8-12 weeks |
| Radius/Ulna | 8-12 weeks |
| Femur shaft | 12-16 weeks |
| Tibia shaft | 16-20 weeks |
| Scaphoid | 8-12 weeks |

## Imaging

**X-ray findings in healing:**
- Week 2-3: Periosteal reaction, haziness at fracture line
- Week 4-6: Visible callus formation
- Week 8-12: Bridging callus, fracture line less distinct
- Months later: Remodeling, restoration of normal architecture

**CT scan:** Better for assessing union in complex fractures

## Factors Affecting Fracture Healing

### Local Factors
| Favorable | Unfavorable |
|-----------|-------------|
| Good blood supply | Poor vascularity |
| Adequate immobilization | Excessive movement |
| Bone contact | Gap/distraction |
| Healthy periosteum | Periosteal stripping |
| Transverse fracture | Comminuted fracture |
| Closed fracture | Open fracture with infection |

### Systemic Factors
| Favorable | Unfavorable |
|-----------|-------------|
| Young age | Old age |
| Good nutrition | Malnutrition, Vitamin C/D deficiency |
| Normal hormones | Diabetes, hypothyroidism |
| Non-smoker | Smoking (↓ blood supply) |
| - | NSAIDs (inhibit COX-2) |
| - | Steroids, Cytotoxic drugs |

## Complications of Fracture Healing

### Delayed Union
- Healing slower than expected but progressing
- Causes: Inadequate immobilization, infection, poor blood supply
- Treatment: Optimize factors, bone stimulators

### Non-Union
- **No healing by 6-9 months**, no radiological progression for 3 months
- Types:
  - **Hypertrophic** (elephant foot): Good blood supply, inadequate stability → Needs fixation
  - **Atrophic**: Poor blood supply → Needs bone graft + fixation
  - **Infected**: Requires debridement, antibiotics, staged reconstruction

### Malunion
- Heals in abnormal position (angulation, rotation, shortening)
- May need corrective osteotomy

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Primary healing** occurs with rigid internal fixation (compression plating) - no callus visible on X-ray
2. **Secondary healing** is the natural process - callus visible
3. **Fracture hematoma** contains growth factors (PDGF, TGF-β, BMP) - essential for healing
4. **Most important factor** for fracture healing = Blood supply
5. **Smoking** reduces healing by 40% - vasoconstriction, reduced oxygenation
6. **NSAIDs** delay healing by inhibiting prostaglandins (COX-2 pathway)
7. **Absolute stability** (compression plating) → Primary healing
8. **Relative stability** (intramedullary nail, external fixator) → Secondary healing
9. **Electrical stimulation** may help non-unions (mechanism: mimics piezoelectric effect of bone)
10. **Weber-Cech classification** of non-union: Viable (hypertrophic) vs Non-viable (atrophic)
