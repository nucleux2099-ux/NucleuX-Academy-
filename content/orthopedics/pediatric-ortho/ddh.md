# Developmental Dysplasia of the Hip (DDH)

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Tachdjian's Pediatric Orthopaedics, Campbell's Operative
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

DDH is a spectrum of hip abnormalities ranging from **mild acetabular dysplasia** to **complete dislocation** of the femoral head from the acetabulum. The term "developmental" replaced "congenital" because many cases develop postnatally and the condition evolves over time.

**The Spectrum:**
```
Dysplasia → Subluxation → Dislocatable → Dislocated (Reducible) → Dislocated (Irreducible)
```

**Key Principle:** Early detection and treatment (<6 months) leads to normal hip development. Delayed treatment leads to permanent disability and early arthritis.

## Key Points

- **Incidence:** 1-3 per 1000 live births (frank dislocation)
- **Female:Male = 6:1** (ligamentous laxity from maternal estrogen)
- **Left hip** more common (85%) - Left OA position against mother's sacrum
- **Bilateral** in 20%
- **NOT a true congenital malformation** - can develop/resolve after birth
- **Screening is critical** - clinical exam at birth, 6 weeks, and all well-baby checks

## Risk Factors

| Risk Factor | Odds Ratio |
|-------------|------------|
| **Breech presentation** | 5-10x |
| **Family history** | 10x (if sibling affected), 30x (if parent affected) |
| **Female sex** | 6x |
| **First-born** | 2x |
| **Oligohydramnios** | 2x |
| **Swaddling with legs extended** | High |

**Associated Conditions:**
- Torticollis (20%)
- Metatarsus adductus
- Foot deformities
- Other "packaging disorders"

## Clinical Features

### Newborn Examination (0-3 months)

**Barlow Test** - Provocative test for instability
- Hip flexed 90°, adducted
- Apply posterior pressure on femur
- Positive = Hip **dislocates** out of acetabulum
- "Dislocatable hip"

**Ortolani Test** - Reduction test
- Start with hip flexed and adducted
- Abduct while lifting greater trochanter anteriorly
- Positive = "Clunk" of relocation
- "Dislocated but reducible hip"

**Note:** Clicks ≠ Clunks. Clicks are common and benign (ligaments snapping). Clunks indicate instability.

### Older Infant (3-12 months)

- Ortolani/Barlow become less reliable (soft tissue contractures)
- **Asymmetric skin folds** (thigh, gluteal)
- **Apparent limb shortening** (Galeazzi sign)
- **Limited abduction** (<60° when hips flexed) - Most reliable sign
- **Widened perineum** (bilateral DDH)

### Walking Child (>1 year)

- **Limp** (painless in child)
- **Trendelenburg gait** (abductor weakness)
- **Positive Trendelenburg test**
- **Short limb** (real, not apparent)
- **Hyperlordosis** (bilateral DDH)
- **Waddling gait** (bilateral)
- **Toe walking** on affected side

## Imaging

### Ultrasound (Birth to 4-6 months)
**Gold standard for infants** (femoral head not ossified yet)

**Graf Classification:**
| Type | Alpha Angle | Beta Angle | Description |
|------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| I | >60° | <55° | Normal |
| IIa | 50-59° | <55° | Immature (normal <3mo) |
| IIb | 50-59° | <55° | Delayed ossification (>3mo) |
| IIc | 43-49° | <77° | Critical instability |
| D | 43-49° | >77° | Decentered hip |
| III | <43° | >77° | Dislocated |
| IV | <43° | >77° | Dislocated, inverted labrum |

**Alpha angle** = Bony acetabular coverage
**Beta angle** = Cartilaginous roof coverage

### X-ray (>4-6 months)
When femoral head ossifies

**Key Lines:**
| Line | Description | Finding in DDH |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Hilgenreiner's** | Horizontal through both triradiate cartilages | Reference line |
| **Perkins'** | Vertical through lateral acetabular edge | Femoral head should be in inferomedial quadrant |
| **Shenton's** | Arc from femoral neck to obturator | Broken in subluxation/dislocation |
| **Acetabular index** | Angle of acetabular roof to Hilgenreiner's | >30° = Dysplasia |

**Other X-ray Signs:**
- Delayed ossification of femoral head
- Femoral head in superolateral quadrant (vs inferomedial normal)
- Increased acetabular index (>30°)

### Radiographic Classification (Older Child)

**Tonnis Classification (Severity of Dislocation):**
| Grade | Femoral Head Position |
|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | Below Hilgenreiner's line |
| 2 | At Hilgenreiner's line |
| 3 | Above Hilgenreiner's line |
| 4 | Above + lateral to acetabulum |

## Management

### Treatment Algorithm by Age

```
0-6 months      → Pavlik harness (90% success)
6-18 months     → Closed reduction + Spica cast
18-36 months    → Open reduction + pelvic osteotomy
>3 years        → Open reduction + pelvic + femoral osteotomy
>8 years        → Salvage procedures (Chiari, shelf)
```

### Pavlik Harness (0-6 months)
- **Most important treatment** - 90-95% success if started early
- Maintains hip in flexion (90-110°) and abduction (50-70°)
- Allows active movement within safe zone
- Wear 23-24 hours/day initially
- Duration: Until hip stable (usually 6-12 weeks)

**Complications of Pavlik:**
- **Pavlik harness disease** - AVN from excessive abduction/forced reduction
- Femoral nerve palsy (from excessive flexion)
- Skin problems

**Contraindications:**
- Age >6 months
- Irreducible hip (Ortolani negative)
- Established dislocation (Tonnis 3-4)
- Neuromuscular conditions

### Closed Reduction + Spica Cast (6-18 months)
1. **Arthrogram** under anesthesia - assess reducibility
2. **Gentle closed reduction**
3. **Spica cast** in "human position" (100° flexion, 40-50° abduction)
4. Cast for 3-6 months
5. If fails → Open reduction

**Safe Zone (Ramsey):** Range of abduction where hip is stable
- Too much abduction → AVN
- Too little → Redislocation

### Open Reduction (If closed fails or age >18 months)
- **Medial approach** (younger children) - Less invasive
- **Anterior approach** (older children) - Address all obstacles

**Obstacles to Reduction:**
| Obstacle | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Iliopsoas | Constricts capsule (hourglass) |
| Labrum | Inverted, blocks entry |
| Ligamentum teres | Hypertrophied |
| Transverse acetabular ligament | Tightened |
| Pulvinar | Fibrofatty tissue filling acetabulum |
| Capsule | Contracted, hourglass deformity |

### Pelvic Osteotomies

| Osteotomy | Indication | Principle |
|-----------|------------|-----------|
| **Salter** | Age 2-6, shallow acetabulum | Innominate rotation |
| **Pemberton** | Age 2-8, anterior deficiency | Periacetabular |
| **Dega** | Neuromuscular, posterior deficiency | Incomplete periacetabular |
| **Triple** (Steel) | Older child, triradiate closed | Cuts all 3 bones |
| **Periacetabular (Ganz)** | Adolescent/adult dysplasia | Preserves blood supply |
| **Chiari** | Salvage, severe dysplasia | Medial displacement |
| **Shelf** | Salvage, augmentation | Add bone to rim |

### Femoral Osteotomies
- **Varus derotation osteotomy** - Correct excessive anteversion
- **Shortening osteotomy** - Reduce pressure on femoral head (older children)

## Complications

| Complication | Cause | Prevention |
|--------------|-------|------------|
| **AVN** | Excessive pressure on femoral head | Gentle reduction, safe zone |
| **Redislocation** | Inadequate treatment | Proper immobilization |
| **Residual dysplasia** | Incomplete remodeling | Osteotomy if needed |
| **Leg length discrepancy** | AVN, growth arrest | Monitor, equalize later |
| **Early OA** | Any residual incongruency | Aim for concentric reduction |

**AVN Risk Factors:**
- Forced reduction
- Excessive abduction
- Age at reduction >6 months
- Multiple reduction attempts

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Barlow = Dislocate**, **Ortolani = Relocate** ("O" for Out then in = Ortolani)
2. **Left hip** more common (LOA position in utero)
3. **Breech** is the strongest risk factor
4. **Graf Type I** = Normal (alpha >60°)
5. **Pavlik harness** = Treatment of choice <6 months (>90% success)
6. **Most reliable sign in older infant** = Limited abduction
7. **Acetabular index >30°** = Dysplasia on X-ray
8. **Shenton's line disruption** = Subluxation/dislocation
9. **Perkins' line** - Normal femoral head in inferomedial quadrant
10. **Pavlik harness disease** = AVN from improper use
11. **Salter osteotomy** - Rotates entire innominate bone
12. **Goal of treatment** = Concentric reduction and maintaining it until acetabular remodeling occurs

## Screening Guidelines

| Organization | Recommendation |
|--------------|----------------|
| AAP | Clinical exam at birth and all well-baby visits; US if high risk or positive exam |
| AAOS | Clinical exam; US for equivocal exam or high risk |
| Graf (Europe) | Universal US screening at 6 weeks |

**High-risk criteria for ultrasound:**
- Breech presentation
- Family history
