# Congenital Talipes Equinovarus (CTEV) - Clubfoot

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Ponseti Method Manual, Tachdjian's Pediatric Orthopaedics
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

CTEV (Clubfoot) is a **complex 3-dimensional deformity** of the foot present at birth, characterized by four components:
1. **Cavus** (high arch)
2. **Adductus** (forefoot adduction)
3. **Varus** (heel inversion)
4. **Equinus** (plantarflexion)

**Mnemonic: CAVE** - Cavus, Adductus, Varus, Equinus

The deformity is **rigid** (cannot be passively corrected to neutral), distinguishing it from positional clubfoot.

## Key Points

- **Incidence:** 1-2 per 1000 live births
- **Male:Female = 2:1**
- **Bilateral** in 50%
- **Idiopathic** in 80% (no underlying cause)
- **Ponseti method** is the gold standard treatment (>95% success)
- If untreated → Permanent disability, walks on dorsum of foot
- **Always examine for associated conditions** (neurological, syndromes)

## Etiology & Classification

### Types of Clubfoot

| Type | Characteristics | % |
|------|-----------------|---|
| **Idiopathic** | Isolated, otherwise normal child | 80% |
| **Neurogenic** | Spina bifida, myelomeningocele | 10% |
| **Syndromic** | Arthrogryposis, diastrophic dysplasia | 5% |
| **Positional** | Flexible, corrects passively | 5% |

### Associated Conditions to Rule Out
- Myelomeningocele (check spine!)
- Arthrogryposis
- Larsen syndrome
- Diastrophic dysplasia
- Congenital constriction band syndrome

## Pathoanatomy

**The Deformity is Centered at the Talus**

| Structure | Abnormality |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Talus** | Plantarflexed, neck deviated medially |
| **Navicular** | Medially displaced, articulates with medial talus |
| **Calcaneus** | Inverted (varus), rotated under talus |
| **Cuboid** | Medially displaced |
| **Metatarsals** | Adducted |
| **Soft tissues** | Contracted medially and posteriorly |

**Contracted Structures:**
- Medial: Tibialis posterior, toe flexors, talonavicular capsule
- Posterior: Achilles tendon, posterior ankle capsule
- Plantar: Plantar fascia

## Clinical Features

### Examination at Birth

**Inspection:**
- Foot turned inward and downward
- Sole faces medially and posteriorly
- Cannot see sole from front (normally visible)
- High medial arch (cavus)
- Deep creases - medial and posterior
- Small, atrophic calf ("stork leg")
- Short foot

**Palpation:**
- Rigid deformity (vs positional which is flexible)
- Head of talus palpable laterally (uncovered)
- Prominent navicular tuberosity medially
- Achilles tendon tight

**Important Assessments:**
1. **Spine examination** - Rule out myelomeningocele
2. **Hip examination** - DDH association
3. **Overall neuromuscular exam** - Exclude syndromic causes

### Pirani Scoring System (Most Commonly Used)

**Total score: 0-6 (0 = normal, 6 = severe)**

| Sign | 0 | 0.5 | 1 |
|------|---|-----|---|
| **Midfoot** | | | |
| Medial crease | None | ≥2 fine creases | ≥1 deep crease |
| Curved lateral border | None | Mild | Severe |
| Talar head coverage | Fully covered | Partially covered | Not covered |
| **Hindfoot** | | | |
| Posterior crease | None | ≥2 fine creases | ≥1 deep crease |
| Rigid equinus | None | Mild | Severe |
| Empty heel | Heel well formed | Tuberosity hard to feel | Tuberosity not palpable |

**Midfoot score:** 0-3
**Hindfoot score:** 0-3

### Dimeglio Classification

| Grade | Score | Severity | Reducibility |
|-------|-------|----------|--------------|
| I | <5 | Benign | >90% reducible |
| II | 5-10 | Moderate | >50% reducible |
| III | 10-15 | Severe | <50% reducible |
| IV | 15-20 | Very severe | Minimal reducibility |

## Imaging

### X-ray (AP and Lateral)
Usually not needed for diagnosis but useful for:
- Assessing correction
- Surgical planning
- Research

**Normal Relationships Disrupted:**
| View | Finding in CTEV |
|------|-----------------|
| AP | Talus-calcaneus angle <20° (normal 20-40°) - Parallelism |
| AP | Negative talus-first metatarsal angle |
| Lateral | Talus-calcaneus angle <25° (normal 25-50°) |
| Lateral | All bones overlap (ladder pattern) |

**Kite's Angle (Talocalcaneal Angle):**
- Normal AP: 20-40°
- Normal Lateral: 35-50°
- In CTEV: Both decreased (parallelism of talus and calcaneus)

## Management

### Ponseti Method (Gold Standard)

**Developed by Ignacio Ponseti at University of Iowa**

#### Principles
1. **Correct deformities in sequence**
2. **Gentle manipulation** (no force)
3. **Serial casting** (weekly)
4. **Achilles tenotomy** for residual equinus
5. **Bracing** to prevent relapse

#### Correction Sequence (CAVE in Reverse)
1. **Cavus** - Corrected first (supinate forefoot to align with hindfoot)
2. **Adductus + Varus** - Corrected together by abducting foot around head of talus (talus as fulcrum)
3. **Equinus** - Corrected last (after adductus/varus corrected), usually needs tenotomy

**Key Point:** Never pronate the foot! Pronation locks the calcaneus under talus.

#### Casting Technique
- Weekly cast changes
- Long leg cast with knee at 90°
- Hold forefoot, abduct around talus (index finger on talar head)
- **Average 5-7 casts** for correction

#### Percutaneous Achilles Tenotomy
- Needed in **80-90%** of cases
- Performed when adductus/varus corrected, equinus persists
- Done under local anesthesia in clinic
- Complete transection of tendon
- Cast for 3 weeks post-tenotomy
- Tendon regenerates at proper length

#### Bracing (Foot Abduction Orthosis - FAO)
**Critical for preventing relapse!**

| Phase | Duration | Hours/Day |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| First 3 months | Full-time | 23 hours |
| Until age 2 | Nights + naps | 14-16 hours |
| Age 2-4/5 years | Nights only | 10-12 hours |

**Brace Position:**
- Affected foot: 60-70° external rotation
- Unaffected foot: 30-40° external rotation
- Both feet: 10-15° dorsiflexion

**Relapse Rate:**
- With bracing compliance: <5%
- Without bracing: 80%!

### Surgical Treatment

**Indications:**
- Failed Ponseti method (rare if done correctly)
- Late presentation (>2 years)
- Complex/atypical clubfoot
- Relapsed clubfoot

**Procedures:**

| Procedure | Indication |
|-----------|------------|
| **Anterior tibialis transfer (TATT)** | Relapse >2.5 years, dynamic supination |
| **Posterior release** | Residual equinus |
| **Medial release** | Residual adductus/varus |
| **Posteromedial release (PMR)** | Combined deformities (less used now) |
| **Lateral column shortening** | Overcorrected/residual adductus |
| **Triple arthrodesis** | Salvage in older child/adult |

### French Method (Functional Method)
- Daily manipulation and taping
- More intensive (daily visits)
- Results comparable to Ponseti
- Less popular due to resource intensity

## Complications

### Of Untreated CTEV
- Walks on dorsum of foot
- Painful callosities
- Secondary bony changes
- Severe disability

### Of Treatment

| Complication | Cause | Prevention |
|--------------|-------|------------|
| **Relapse** | Non-compliance with brace | Education, follow-up |
| **Rocker-bottom foot** | Forcing dorsiflexion before correcting midfoot | Correct sequence! |
| **Complex clubfoot** | Excessive manipulation | Gentle technique |
| **Overcorrection** | Excessive treatment | Monitor carefully |
| **Flat-top talus** | AVN/repeated reduction | Gentle manipulation |

### Relapse Patterns
- **Dynamic supination** - Tibialis anterior overpull → TATT
- **Recurrent equinus** - Tight Achilles → Repeat tenotomy or lengthening
- **Midfoot adductus** - Medial soft tissues → Release or osteotomy

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **CAVE** - Cavus, Adductus, Varus, Equinus
2. **Ponseti method** = Gold standard (>95% success)
3. **First deformity to correct** = Cavus (supinate forefoot)
4. **Never pronate** the foot (worsens deformity)
5. **Talus is the fulcrum** for correction
6. **Achilles tenotomy** needed in 80-90%
7. **Bracing compliance** is the key to preventing relapse
8. **Relapse without brace** = 80%
9. **Pirani score** used to track correction (0 = fully corrected)
10. **Bilateral** in 50%, **Male** predominant (2:1)
11. **Always check spine** for myelomeningocele
12. **Kite's angle** (talocalcaneal angle) is decreased (parallelism)

## Comparison: CTEV vs Positional Clubfoot

| Feature | CTEV (True Clubfoot) | Positional Clubfoot |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Rigidity | Rigid, cannot passively correct | Flexible, corrects passively |
| Calf | Atrophic, small | Normal |
| Creases | Deep medial and posterior | Absent or minimal |
| Treatment | Ponseti (casts, tenotomy, brace) | Stretching, resolves spontaneously |
| Prognosis | Requires treatment | Excellent, self-limiting |

## Surgical Anatomy (For Advanced Understanding)

**Cincinnati Incision:** Circumferential incision from medial malleolus around heel to lateral malleolus - provides excellent exposure for extensile release.

**Structures Released in Posteromedial Release:**
- Z-lengthening of Achilles tendon
- Posterior ankle capsule
- Posterior subtalar capsule
- Calcaneofibular ligament
- Tibialis posterior lengthening
- Toe flexor lengthening
- Talonavicular capsule
- Spring ligament
- Abductor hallucis release
