# Pediatric Bone Tumors

> 📚 **Sources:** Maheshwari Orthopedics 7th Ed, Campbell's Operative Orthopaedics, Enneking Staging System
> **Level:** UG | PG

## Core Concept

Bone tumors in children differ significantly from adult tumors. **Primary malignancies** (osteosarcoma, Ewing sarcoma) are more common than metastases (unlike adults). Early recognition is critical as these tumors are aggressive but often curable with multimodal treatment.

**Key Principle:** Any bone pain in a child that:
- Wakes them at night
- Persists >2 weeks
- Has associated mass or swelling
→ Needs X-ray and further workup!

## Key Points

- **Most common benign tumor:** Osteochondroma
- **Most common malignant tumor:** Osteosarcoma
- **Most common in children overall:** Osteochondroma > Simple bone cyst > Osteosarcoma
- **Peak age for malignancies:** Adolescent growth spurt (10-20 years)
- **Location matters:** Metaphysis (most tumors), Diaphysis (Ewing), Epiphysis (chondroblastoma, GCT)
- **Night pain** - Concerning for malignancy or osteoid osteoma

## Benign Bone Tumors

### Osteochondroma (Most Common Benign Tumor)
- **Location:** Metaphysis, points away from joint
- **Features:** Cartilage cap, continuous with medullary canal
- **X-ray:** Pedunculated or sessile bony projection
- **Malignant transformation:** <1% (solitary), 5-25% (multiple hereditary)
- **Treatment:** Observation; excision if symptomatic

### Simple Bone Cyst (Unicameral Bone Cyst)
- **Age:** 5-15 years, M>F
- **Location:** Proximal humerus (50%), proximal femur (25%)
- **Features:** "Fallen fragment sign" on X-ray (pathognomonic)
- **X-ray:** Central, well-defined lytic lesion in metaphysis
- **Treatment:** Observation, steroid injection, curettage + bone graft

### Aneurysmal Bone Cyst (ABC)
- **Age:** 10-20 years
- **Features:** Expansile, "blown out" appearance
- **X-ray:** Eccentric, multi-septated, "soap bubble" appearance
- **Fluid-fluid levels on MRI** (diagnostic!)
- **Treatment:** Curettage + bone graft; sclerotherapy; embolization

### Osteoid Osteoma
- **Age:** 10-25 years, M:F = 3:1
- **Location:** Femur, tibia (cortex of diaphysis)
- **Classic presentation:** Night pain relieved by NSAIDs (aspirin/ibuprofen)
- **X-ray:** <2cm lucent nidus with surrounding sclerosis
- **CT:** Best to visualize nidus
- **Treatment:** NSAIDs (can observe if tolerable), radiofrequency ablation, surgical excision

### Osteoblastoma
- **"Large osteoid osteoma"** (>2cm)
- **Location:** Spine (posterior elements) most common
- **Less responsive to NSAIDs**
- **Treatment:** Curettage or excision

### Non-Ossifying Fibroma / Fibrous Cortical Defect
- **Most common incidental finding** on pediatric X-rays
- **Location:** Metaphysis of long bones (distal femur, proximal tibia)
- **X-ray:** Eccentric, well-defined, scalloped lytic lesion with sclerotic rim
- **Natural history:** Spontaneous resolution
- **Treatment:** Observation (unless >50% of bone width → pathological fracture risk)

## Malignant Bone Tumors

### Osteosarcoma (Most Common Primary Malignant Bone Tumor)

**Epidemiology:**
- Bimodal: Peak 10-20 years; second peak >65 years (Paget's)
- **Male > Female**
- **Risk factors:** Retinoblastoma (RB gene), Li-Fraumeni (p53), Paget's disease, radiation

**Location:**
- Metaphysis of long bones
- Distal femur (40%) > Proximal tibia (20%) > Proximal humerus (10%)
- **"Around the knee"** - most common site

**Clinical Features:**
- Pain (worse at night, progressive)
- Swelling, mass
- Pathological fracture (10%)
- Systemic symptoms late

**Imaging:**

| Modality | Findings |
|----------|----------|
| **X-ray** | Mixed lytic/scite, "sunburst" periosteal reaction, Codman triangle |
| **MRI** | Extent of marrow involvement, soft tissue mass, skip lesions |
| **CT chest** | Lung metastases (most common site) |
| **Bone scan** | Whole body for skip lesions and metastases |

**Classic X-ray Signs:**
- **Sunburst/Sunray periosteal reaction** - New bone radiating from cortex
- **Codman triangle** - Raised periosteum at tumor margin
- **Moth-eaten destruction** - Permeative pattern

**Treatment:**
1. **Neoadjuvant chemotherapy** (3-4 cycles)
2. **Wide surgical excision** (limb salvage or amputation)
3. **Adjuvant chemotherapy** (additional cycles)

**Prognosis:**
- With modern treatment: 60-70% 5-year survival (localized)
- Metastatic at diagnosis: 20-30% survival
- **Chemotherapy response (>90% necrosis)** = Good prognosis

### Ewing Sarcoma (Second Most Common Malignant Bone Tumor in Children)

**Epidemiology:**
- Age: 5-25 years (peak 10-15)
- **Very rare in African/Asian populations** (EWS-FLI1 translocation)
- **t(11;22)** translocation - EWS-FLI1 fusion (diagnostic)

**Location:**
- **Diaphysis** of long bones
- Flat bones (pelvis, ribs, scapula) - 40%
- Femur most common long bone

**Clinical Features:**
- Pain, swelling
- **Systemic symptoms common** (fever, anemia, elevated ESR/LDH) - Can mimic osteomyelitis!
- Mass, warmth, redness
- Pathological fracture

**Imaging:**

| Modality | Findings |
|----------|----------|
| **X-ray** | Permeative destruction, "onion skin" periosteal reaction |
| **MRI** | Large soft tissue mass (bigger than bone lesion often) |
| **CT chest** | Lung metastases |
| **Bone scan/PET** | Metastases (25% at diagnosis) |

**Classic X-ray Sign:**
- **Onion skin periosteal reaction** - Lamellated periosteal new bone
- Permeative "moth-eaten" destruction
- Large soft tissue mass

**Treatment:**
1. **Neoadjuvant chemotherapy** (vincristine, doxorubicin, cyclophosphamide, ifosfamide, etoposide)
2. **Local control:** Surgery (preferred) or Radiation
3. **Adjuvant chemotherapy**

**Prognosis:**
- Localized: 70% 5-year survival
- Metastatic: 20-30%
- Pelvic tumors: Worst prognosis

## Staging Systems

### Enneking Staging (Musculoskeletal Tumor Society)

**Benign Tumors:**
| Stage | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Latent | Fibrous cortical defect |
| 2 | Active | ABC, osteochondroma |
| 3 | Aggressive | GCT |

**Malignant Tumors:**
| Stage | Grade | Compartment | Metastasis |
|-------|-------|-------------|------------|
| IA | Low | Intracompartmental | No |
| IB | Low | Extracompartmental | No |
| IIA | High | Intracompartmental | No |
| IIB | High | Extracompartmental | No |
| III | Any | Any | Yes |

**Most osteosarcomas present as Stage IIB** (high grade, extracompartmental)

### Surgical Margins (Enneking)

| Margin | Description | Local Recurrence |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| Intralesional | Through tumor | High |
| Marginal | Through reactive zone | Moderate |
| Wide | Through normal tissue | Low |
| Radical | Entire compartment | Lowest |

## Diagnostic Approach

### When to Suspect Bone Tumor
- Pain worse at night
- Persistent pain >2 weeks
- Pain not related to activity
- Palpable mass
- Pathological fracture

### Workup Algorithm
1. **X-ray** - First test
2. **MRI** - Local staging, soft tissue extent
3. **CT chest** - Lung metastases
4. **Bone scan/PET** - Whole body staging
5. **Biopsy** - Definitive diagnosis (must be done by treating surgeon!)

### Biopsy Principles
- **Performed by the treating surgeon** (biopsy tract excised with tumor)
- Longitudinal incision in line with limb
- Avoid contaminating additional compartments
- **Needle biopsy** preferred when possible
- Send for histology, cytogenetics (Ewing), molecular studies

## High-Yield for Exams

1. **Osteochondroma** - Most common benign bone tumor
2. **Osteosarcoma** - Most common primary malignant bone tumor in children
3. **Location:** Metaphysis (osteosarcoma), Diaphysis (Ewing), Epiphysis (chondroblastoma)
4. **Sunburst + Codman triangle** = Osteosarcoma
5. **Onion skin periosteal reaction** = Ewing sarcoma
6. **t(11;22) translocation** = Ewing sarcoma (EWS-FLI1)
7. **Night pain relieved by aspirin** = Osteoid osteoma
8. **Fluid-fluid levels on MRI** = ABC (also can be in GCT, telangiectatic osteosarcoma)
9. **Fallen fragment sign** = Simple bone cyst
10. **"Around the knee"** - Most common site for osteosarcoma
11. **Chemotherapy response >90% necrosis** = Good prognosis in osteosarcoma
12. **Fever + bone lesion in child** = Ewing vs osteomyelitis (DDx)

| Feature | Osteosarcoma | Ewing Sarcoma | Osteomyelitis |

| Age | 10-20 | 5-15 | Any |

| Fever | Late | Common | Common |
